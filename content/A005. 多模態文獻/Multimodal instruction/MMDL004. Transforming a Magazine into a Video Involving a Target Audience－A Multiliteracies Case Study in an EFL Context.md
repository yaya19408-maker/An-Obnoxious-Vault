1. Hafner (2014) discovered six steps in video making: 
	1. Reading
	2. Data collection
	3. Scripting and storyboarding
	4. Filming
	5. Editing
	6. Sharing
	7. So as to identify “a wide range of semiotic resources, including 
		1. Moving images and animation
		2. Charts and tables for scientific data, subtitles
		3. Different camera angles and lighting, background music
		4. Sound effects
		5. Interesting locations
		6. Interesting participants
		7. Facial expression (p. 669).
		8. Concerned if “over-relying on multimedia” or “excess use of technique may annoy the audience.” 
		9. Another challenge was “how to write an interesting script for narration for the documentary” because they viewed “language as an equally important resource” (p. 668).
