5. Complexity #5 : **Transglobalism and relations of power 跨全球化論與權力關係** (**政治和地位的差異化。** Kress (2010: 3)說也許很多時候交流不是問題，而是權力關係，但文獻中卻鮮少探討政治權力地位關係。此外經濟資本、社會、文化和政治資本也會影響模式的使用傾向、創建何種信息及如何傳播，還有各種行為者/參與者的相互定位。Sherris & Adami (2019: 2) 說如果符號創造本質上是關係性的、糾纏的和動態的，那麼它也不會少了政治性。)
##### Thinking about Multi (or Trans-) Modality, and Trans (or Multi-) Linguality: Power, Ideology, and Emerging Questions
- By Bruce Horner, Cynthia Selfe, Tim Lockridge on October 6, 2014
A potential synergy arises from the confluence of multi- (or trans-) modality, and trans- (or multi-) linguality. These areas of concern have emerged contemporaneously—at least within the context of modern composition studies—partially, in response to changes in the means and identities of people in communication practices worldwide. In important ways, these changes challenge compositionists to rethink what composition entails.

However, despite the common points of origination, discussions of modality have remained largely separate from discussions of translinguality, to the impoverishment of both. We find this situation to be most interesting and worthy of exploration. In our preliminary observations—which will be explored in more detail in a later publication—we make the following observations:

1. Recent world-order changes in communication practices and environments—especially, but not solely those that are digital in their nature—call attention to ideologies elided by the supposed “norm” of a single, uniform (“standard”) language or mode (hereafter referenced as “SLMN”).

2. These same changes bring to awareness the presence of communicative practices in the past that SLMN ideology has suppressed.

3. Emergent communicative practices are themselves materially different from past, and other, communicative practices in ways that challenge both “SLMN“ ideologies and the practices now identified (ideologically) as SLMN.

We resist any understanding that statistically standard language practices are singular either in their linguistic or modal forms, and we resist the understanding that _statistically standard_ is the linguistic or modal equivalent of _normal_. This ideological formation is two sided and doubly dangerous.

As we explore these observations, it seems ultimately problematic to distinguish between language and modality. Dominant conceptions of language offer a highly attenuated, restricted sense of all that goes on in the activity of “language acts” (a.k.a. communicative acts). Kress (2000) acknowledges this in calling language multimodal (p. 186), vs. thinking of language as itself a discrete mode. Conversely, it seems appropriate to recognize modalities as a feature of language. Given this, it no longer makes sense to treat language, whether as writing or speech or both, as apart from the “multimodal” (see Calvet 21-22).

We need to be wary of the power of monolingual-ist, monomodal-ist, dispositions to distort our sense of the practices under consideration. This danger manifests in two ways: the tendency to view practices not _marked_ as either multimodal or multilingual as SLMN; conversely, the tendency to conflate practices _marked_ as either multimodal or translingual with multimodal/translingual dispositions, when their non-SLMN character may be more apparent than real.

Second, and paradoxically, we also need to recognize the effect of specific material social environments on dispositions toward language(s) and modalities. As we’ve already suggested, the emergence of changes to communicative practices—most obviously, the development of digital communication technologies and global communicative networks, and less obviously, the increasing traffic of (exchanges and changes to) peoples and language practices—have  contributed to the increasing visibility of, and questions about, language and modality.

The “new” communicative practices, as they are often described—those that dominant dispositions lead us to recognize as different—also force a re-evaluation of and change to those communicative practices that we see and experience as simply _natural_, the _norm._

In light of all this, rather than understanding modality and linguality in terms of fixed (“defined”) categories and practices, we pose the following questions of definition as more productive in bringing out the dialectical relations between dispositions and practices with language and modality:

- What are the material social conditions of composing possibility for the deployment of language and modality (including available and competing dispositions toward and training with these)?

- How are modality and language deployed (or might they be deployed) in this composition? To what end? Demanding, or expecting, what kinds of work? How does such deployment work on and with the conditions of its composition, distribution, and reception?

- In what ways do our current analytical categories of modality and language need to be revised to accommodate differences in the ways this composition engages these?

#### **Interculturality** 文化間性
The term interculturality is often used to express the right to difference in relations of a dualistic nature between minorities or marginalized cultures and the majority or dominant cultures (not always the majority demographically speaking, in the Latin American case, for instance), which have historically tended to be tense or conflictive.

#### **Supramodality** [神經超模態性](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pmc/articles/PMC5099160/) & **Cross-modal plasticity** 跨模態可塑性
大腦特定區域處理特定信息而不限於感官的具體模態 (也就是信息處理並不是絕大多數依賴視覺，那只是我們以為)。盲人與正常視力者對形狀/運動/空間的識別能力在反應上都相同。
supramodality是指與生俱來的跨感官處理能力，而cross-modal plasticity是失去視覺後大腦的重新分配和適應力。
Supramodal processing within the “visual” extrastriate system has been studied in both sighted and congenitally blind individuals. In particular, research has been conducted on form recognition, motion discrimination, spatial and navigational processing, using visual and non-visual sensory tasks in both congenitally blind and sighted individuals. These studies have demonstrated that neural responses in “visual” areas during non-visual processing are not merely related to visual imagery, and that visual experience is not a mandatory prerequisite for the functional specialization within the visual system.

#### **Multimodal metaphor** [多模態隱喻](聯覺和多模態隱喻.pdf)
*(1) Metaphor 隱喻:*
在Lackoff & Johnson (1980)的Metaphors We Live By這本書裡提到了概念隱喻理論 (Conceptual Metaphor Theory)，其中認為隱喻有普遍性 (universality)、系統性 (systemicity)和概念性 (conceptuality)。
*(2) Source domain, target domain, mapping, and image schemas:*
隱喻是以一種**較熟悉且易理解的事物 (即source domain 源域)**去**理解 (mapping映射)**到一個**較不熟悉且難理解的事物 (即target domain 目標域)**，而該流程的**心理基礎就是抽象的意象圖式 (image schemas)**
Forceville & Urios-Aparisi (2009)稱源域和目標域可以是具象的實體 (concrete entities)
*(3) Definition:*

2. 聽覺或聲波模態

#### **Transsensory work 超感官作品** 
###### **in [[A003. literate 字; 文]]**
- ⼤⼩的調整以影響誦讀速度至超圖像作品， 並非僅存在於字母符號視為視覺元素的視覺滿⾜︔還有關於詩歌與聲⾳還原的⽤意， 隨著⽂字間距⽽影響閱讀時「默語」的停留時間，這樣不存在的聲⾳也透過⾴⾯編排產⽣念誦之間的空⽩與節奏，也將「⽂字—圖像」之間的轉換關係往「聲⾳—圖像」。從這條思索軌跡，來理解超圖像作為⼀種 **「超感官」（transsensory）** 作品， 不只是形式上**混合⼩說、繪畫（tableaux）、插圖（déssin）、出版品（publication）** 等形式，也涵蓋了在**作品內部，聲⾳代替圖像、圖像代替聲⾳**，甚或是**其他感知與視覺、聽覺的替代關係**。換句話說，超圖像不僅是**實踐視覺元素的可讀、語⾔符號的可⾒之意圖**，亦是需要在⾴⾯載體實現「**不存在聲⾳的可聽**」之可能。
- 字母派在超圖像的詩歌作品承接著第⼀節對聯覺的描述，**聯覺所欲造成感官的聯通，實際上仍然存在各種以視覺為中⼼、服務於視覺的其他感官**︔然⽽**字母派的作品指向則是字母與視覺符號轉換之際造成的思考卡頓，⽣成敘事、訊息的斷裂，在其破壞了單⼀透視與空間秩序下，反⽽注意到所有在眼／⽿前的每⼀構成元素。** 
- 葛林柏格的⽅法是將藝術領域設想成「永恆的且處於無休無止的流動狀態，是具有普遍且跨歷史的形式。」然⽽，在字母派的作品思考上，伊蘇並非站在⼀個審視藝術史演變進程的角⾊，若將伊蘇的創作⼿法等同於回應現代主義之遺產，也就忽略了對於**媒材⾃身與其涉及特定的感受性之型態與關係**。也就是說，當前兩節我試圖把捉、歸納聲⾳詩與超圖像中對於詞語、⾳節的還原、作為視覺對象之觀察，側重地不再限制於「關注物理性作為判定的藝術本體論論調」之觀點，⽽是當**媒材強調其⾃身之際與感知經驗的連動關係**——即**創作者如何調動媒材技術來影響、處理與視聽感知之間的關係**。 
- 移轉到電影的介⾯，**話語以字幕與聲⾳的兩種形式顯現也同時對應到眼／⽿的感官差異**。
- 在電影介⾯上以⽂字攻擊圖像，說明了由字幕構成的⽂本電影以及字母派以⼤量話語構成的作品即作為⼀種打開電影閱讀形式的電影未來，也揭⽰了在靜滯電影的範疇下指出⽂本電影或者無圖像電影，電影內部話語的兩種表現形式：**⽂字與聲⾳，也就是從話語本身的表現形式——從無聲⽂字到有聲話語——與影像運動**的探討。
- 討論影像介⾯與書籍介⾯在觀看的經驗差異，以及將⽂字視為影像整體的創作⼿法，似乎可以聯想到字母派在處理在創作介⾯的移轉經驗，透過超圖像將字母作為視覺元素以及將圖像⽂本作為影像整體的思考。也就是說從前⾯兩節歷時性鋪展字母派前後處理⽂字—圖像、以及⽂學遭逢其他藝術領域之間的交互關係
