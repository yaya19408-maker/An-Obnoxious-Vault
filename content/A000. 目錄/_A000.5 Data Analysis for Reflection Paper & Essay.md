# Reflection papers
## For researcher
分析I-identity (only student himself/herself) /Others-identity (with others)
- reliability: Cohen's Kappa
## Modified from:
### I-identity/we-identity
Larina, T. V., Ozyumenko, V. I., & Kurteš, S. (2017). I-identity vs we-identity in language and discourse: Anglo-Slavonic perspectives. _Lodz Papers in Pragmatics_, _13_(1), 109-128.

Zhao, H., Zhang, X., & Lee, Y. (2024). Transform the Interchangeability of ‘We’and ‘I’for an ‘I Write’Identity and Authorial Voice. _American Journal of Educational Research_, _12_(3), 109-117.
## 定義I-identity
criteria: 在單個完整句符合以下條件一次，計一次；符合多個，也僅計一次
1. 提及I, my, me, myself, mine等第一人稱單數
- 任何牽涉my的object，如
	- 2-1. "Here is my..."
	- 2-2. "Based on my..."
	- 2-3. "From my perspective"
	- 2-4. "In my opinion"
	- 2-5. "According to my experience/observation," 等等
	- ###### Exception 
		- "my classmate(s)"
		- "my teacher"
		- "my ChatGPT response"
			- 等不屬於該作者的擁有物，不計
3. 任何針對自己經驗或觀察所下的定義或總結，如"by me"
4. 若以"I think"為起始並對他人觀點進行評論，
	- 4-1. 在提出後評論或給出自己認同他人觀點的理由，計入
	- ###### Exception
		- 僅是提及他人觀點而不給出理由，不計
		- 提及他人觀點，且給出的理由是與ChatGPT的回饋一致，或是與任何第三方一致，不計
5. 若是以we should/could作為呼籲社會行動，則計入
6. 若"we believe that"是建立在社會事實或文化傳統之上，計入

## 定義Others-identity
criteria: 在單個完整句符合以下條件一次，計一次；符合多個，也僅計一次
1. 提及任何第三人稱單複數
- ###### Exception
		- 若是"It was my conception that..."之類以it、here、there等虛主詞為主詞，卻表達自身觀點的陳述，算I-identity
2. 提及we, our, ourselves, ours等第一人稱複數，因涉及他人，並不能代表自身所有觀點，算在此列
	- 若"we believe that..."是在前文就有提及自己與特定對象 (一起討論的一位或多位同學) 並以此句作為銜接或延伸陳述，計入
	- ###### Exception
		- 若"we believe that"是建立在社會事實或文化傳統之上，算I-identity
		- 若是以we should/could/may等modal verbs作為呼籲社會行動，算I-identity
3. 任何權威性陳述，如
	- 3-1. "It is believed/said/claimed/reported that..."
	- 3-2. "According to.../Based on... 但無出現任何第一人稱單數的陳述"
4. 任何從外在聲音反身至自己觀點，需進一步審視後文，例如
	- 4-1. "The feedback from ChatGPT makes me realize that..."
	- 4-2. "The perspective/thought/opinion from... enlightens me that..."
	- 4-3. 若後續陳述高度遵從該句觀點，計入
	- ###### Exception
		- 若是僅提及並表示該觀點很關鍵或值得深思，但並不遵照該觀點繼續寫作，不計

- Formula 1:
	- I-identity % = I-identity/I-identity + Others-identity
- Formula 2:
	- Other-identity % = Others-identity/I-identity + Others-identity
- Formula 3:
	- I-identity % - Other-identity %

**result > 0:** student has engaged in the negotiation with AI and peers and formed a robust writer-identity.
**result = 0:** the change of writer identity through the negotiation with AI and peers is not significant.
**result < 0:** despite engaging the negotiation with AI and peers, student's writer identity, to some extent, has been compromised.

### Guided questions for reflection (examples)
- #### rationale
	- using "you" as the subject to ask $\rightarrow$ students won't have "exterior consideration" about using we or I.
- #### examples (tentative)
	- Please share any ideas from the discussion, especially the feedback from your classmates that is thought-provoking to you.
	- What did you hear from your classmates? Is there any difference between your own thoughts and his/hers/theirs?
	- Do you have any comments for your classmates?
	- Do you expect to learn or discuss more with someone? Why is it important to you? 
	- How much information have you gained from the discussion? Which of them are important to you? Why?
