# 前導
## Extensive Reading (ER)
TO ACQUIRE LANGUAGE
- by understanding messages
	- **comprehension**
- by being exposed to a large quantity of print that is comprehensible and compelling
	- **exposure**
		- children who grow up in a print-rich environment display superior competence in several different aspects of language and literacy, including
			- syntax
			- vocabulary
			- spelling
			- knowledge of history
			- culture
			- literature
			- practical information
- *Smith (1988: 7)*
> Learning about language is not the primary aim, but rather the by-product of some other activities… Language is learned for its uses at the time.
- by joining a literacy club in which all the members are learning on a 'social and collaborative basis', with no risk of being evaluated and excluded.
	- **read collaboratively, freely, and inclusively**
		- second language acquirers also profit from free voluntary reading
			- reading outside of school do better in writing
			- have greater grammatical competence
			- on the TOEFL examination
### PRACTICE
- Regarding ESL/EFL situations, it consistently shows that a curriculum incorporating ER is a teaching practice at least as effective as a curriculum based on skill-building.
- *Krashen (2004)*
> ER is very pleasant to do and is a lifelong approach for language acquisition and intellectual growth
## Sustained Silent Reading (SSR)
investigate the potential of ER as a tool for language acquisition & literacy development
- students participating in sustained silent reading make gains that are equivalent to or better than gains made by comparison students in classes not including SSR, in 
	- reading and vocabulary
	- writing
	- grammar
	- attitudes toward reading
- ER is less labor intensive for the teacher and allows teachers to enjoy good quality reading as a member in the same literacy club.
