- 8-step model
	- Phase I: deciding what to research (before)
		- formulating a research problem [[BIBL012. Formulating a Research Problem]] {Literature review}
			- doable and manageable 
				- financial resources
				- the time available
				- advisor's expertise
				- knowledge in the field of study
			- considerations and steps in formulating a research problem
			- variables and hypotheses: definition and typology (**what**)
			- what
			- about
	- Phase II: planning a research study (during)
		- conceptualizing a research design
			- research design: functions
			- study design
		- constructing an instrument for idea collection (pilot)
		- selecting a sample (premise)
		- writing a research proposal
	- Phase III: conducting a research study (after)
		- collecting data
		- processing data
		- writing a research report

| Qualitative                                                               | Quantitative                                                                      |
| ------------------------------------------------------------------------- | --------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| unstructured/loose                                                        | structured/rigid                                                                  |
| deep and unexpected answers                                               | direct and specific answers                                                       |
| phenomenonal/experiential/naturalistic/ethnoographic/textual/interpretive | numerical/statistical/experimental                                                |
| describe the patterns or processes (idiographic)                          | predict/infer phenomena or behaviors of a population (generalization, nomothetic) |
| fewer cases/participants                                                  | huge amount of samples/subjects                                                   |
| interview (open-ended questions)/case studies/field research              | questionnaire                                                                     |
| why/how self-determination can be increased                               | can increase self-determination through the discussions                           |
| serendipity                                                               | hypotheses                                                                        |
