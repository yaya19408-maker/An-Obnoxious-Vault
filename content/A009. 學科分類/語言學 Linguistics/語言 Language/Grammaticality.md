Grammaticality is the extent to which a ‘string’ of language conforms with a set of given rules. It is assumed that a native speaker’s grammar generates grammatical strings and that the speaker has the ability to judge a certain string to be either acceptable or not in her language.
一串語言遵循了一組既定規則，則該句有一定的文法性。
一位母語者的文法會生成文法字串，且該母語者可以分辨該字串是否在自己的語言內自不自然。

A grammatical string is not necessarily meaningful, as exemplified by Chomsky’s famous sentence ‘Colorless green ideas sleep furiously’. However, it is assumed that even nonsensical grammatical sentences can easily be pronounced with a natural intonation and that speakers are able to recall them more easily than ungrammatical sentences.
文法字串的意義其實沒有那麼必要，就好比如說杭士基的名句'無色的翠綠意想在狂怒中睡去'這句話。它體現出就算這種不合情理卻又符合文法的句子也還是可以自然地唸出來，而且絲毫不阻礙言者去回想這種不符合文法的句子。
