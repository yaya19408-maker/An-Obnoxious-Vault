一句話能從言者 (emic 主位) 和聽者 (etic 客位) 獲得越多在規則和語法上的認可，則該句的可接受性越高。
Acceptability is the extent to which a sentence allowed by the rules to be grammatical is considered permissible by speakers and hearer.

As stressed by Chomsky, acceptability should not be confused with grammaticality: while an acceptable sentence must be grammatical, not just any grammatical sentence is necessarily acceptable.
杭士基一再強調可接受性和文法性並不相同: 一句可以接受的句子一定會遵循文法，但並不意味著所有文法句都是絕對可接受。
