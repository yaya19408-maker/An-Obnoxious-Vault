☀ <span class="category">**多的形容詞：**</span>
<span class="vocabulary">**full**</span> [fʊl] 
<span class="definition">adj. 1 full of sth 擁有或包含很大數量的某事物的：</span>The sky was full of brightly coloured fireworks. 滿天都是色彩絢麗的焰火。/ Life is full of coincidences. 生活中巧合很多。/ Our new brochure is crammed full of inspirational ideas. 我們新的小冊子中啟發性的妙計比比皆是。/ animals pumped full of antibiotics 注入大量抗生素的動物 / She was full of admiration for the care she had received. 她對所受到的關懷照顧讚歎不已。/ He smiled, his eyes full of laughter. 他露出了笑容，雙眼也滿含著笑意。/ The kitchen was absolutely full of flies! 廚房裡滿是蒼蠅！<span class="definition">2 full of sth（人）關於某事物思考（或談論）得很多的：</span>He was full of his new job and everything he’d been doing. 他滔滔不絕地談他的新工作和他所做的一切。

<span class="vocabulary">**rich**</span> [rɪtʃ] 
<span class="definition">adj. 1 非常有趣的，豐富多彩的（生活、歷史、種類等）：</span>the region’s rich history and culture 該地區豐富多彩的歷史和文化 / She leads a rich and varied life. 她過著豐富多彩的生活。/ a culturally rich nation 文化底蘊深厚的民族 <span class="definition">2 rich (in sth) 常構成複合詞，指含有（或提供）大量的…，反義詞為poor：</span>Oranges are rich in vitamin C. 橘子含有豐富的維生素C。/ The area is rich in wildlife. 這個地區的野生動植物很多。/ His novels are a rich source of material for the movie industry. 他的小說為電影業提供了大量素材。
           
<span class="vocabulary">**luxuriant**</span> [lʌgˈʒʊəriənt; 美 -ˈʒʊr-]
<span class="definition">adj.（尤指藝術或氛圍）充滿了美好意蘊的，如豐富的、華麗的、富饒的：</span>the poet's luxuriant imagery 詩人豐富的意象           

<span class="vocabulary">**manifold**</span> [ˈmænɪfəʊld; 美 -foʊld]
<span class="definition">adj. [正式] 很多；多種多樣的；很多種類的：</span>The possibilities were manifold. 有很多的可能性。/ Gaelic can be heard here in manifold forms. 在這裡可以聽見各不相同的蓋爾語。/ The difficulties are manifold. 困難是多種多樣的。
           
<span class="vocabulary">**bountiful**</span> [ˈbaʊntɪfl]
<span class="definition">adj. [正式或文學]（令人愉悅的東西）大量的：</span>a bountiful supply of food 富足的食物供應 / a bountiful period of time 物產充足的一段時期 / The land is bountiful and no one starves. 這片土地物產豐富，沒有人捱餓。/ a bountiful harvest of fruits and vegetables 水果和蔬菜的大豐收 / State aid is less bountiful than it was before. 國家提供的援助比以往減少了。
           
<span class="vocabulary">**copious**</span> [ˈkəʊpiəs; 美 ˈkoʊ-]
<span class="definition">adj. 大量的：</span>copious (= large) amounts of water 大量的水 / I went out for a meal last night and drank copious amounts of red wine. 我昨晚出去吃飯，喝了很多紅酒。/ copious mucus 大量的粘液（或鼻涕）/ He attended his lectures and took copious notes. 他去聽他的課，做了大量的筆記。/ copious references 大量的參考文獻 / She supports her theory with copious evidence. 她以大量的論據來支援自己的理論。
           
<span class="vocabulary">**prolific**</span> [prəˈlɪfɪk]
<span class="definition">adj. 大量存在的，如眾多的、大批的：</span>a pop star with a prolific following of teenage fans 擁有大批少男少女歌迷的流行歌星 / All the big game congregate here, and birdlife is particularly prolific. 所有的大型野獸都聚集在這裡，鳥類尤其豐富。

<span class="vocabulary">**mass**</span> [mæs] 
<span class="definition">adj. 只作前置定語，影響或涉及大批或大量的人或事物的，即大批的；數量極多的；廣泛的：</span>mass unemployment, mass production 大批失業；批次生產 / weapons of mass destruction 大規模殺傷性武器 / the mass market 大眾市場。/ All the lights went off, and mass hysteria broke out. 所有的燈都熄滅了，大家變得歇斯底里起來。
           
<span class="vocabulary">**significant**</span> [sɪgˈnɪfɪkənt]
<span class="definition">adj. 數量相當大的：</span>A small but significant number of 11-year-olds are illiterate. 數量不多但仍有相當一部分11歲孩子是文盲。/ foods that offer a significant amount of protein 富含蛋白質的食物

<span class="vocabulary">**strong**</span> [strɒŋ] 
<span class="definition">adj. 1（表示數目）大量的，眾多的：</span>There was a strong police presence at the demonstration. 示威現場有大批警察在場。<span class="definition">2（用於數字後，表示某個群體的規模）多達…：</span>a 1000-strong crowd 多達1000人的群體 / The crowd was 1000 strong. 聚集的人有1000之眾。

<span class="vocabulary">**healthy**</span> ['helθɪ] 
<span class="definition">adj. 常作前置定語，（常指數量）大而顯得成功的，即可觀的：</span>healthy profits 可觀的利潤 / a healthy bank balance 一大筆銀行結餘

<span class="vocabulary">**fair**</span> [feə] 
<span class="definition">adj.（數量、數額）相當大的，只作前置定語：</span>a fair number of people 相當多的人 / We’ve still got a fair bit (= quite a lot) to do. 我們還有很多事要做。

<span class="vocabulary">**heavy**</span> ['hevɪ] 
<span class="definition">adj. [非正式] heavy on sth 可表示消耗（或使用）大量…的：</span>The tractor is heavy on fuel. 這臺拖拉機耗油量大。/ Don’t go so heavy on the garlic. 別用這麼多蒜。

<span class="vocabulary">**serious**</span> ['sɪərɪəs] 
<span class="definition">adj. [非正式] 用於強調數量之大：</span>to earn serious money 掙一大筆錢 / to do some serious eating 大吃一頓
           
<span class="vocabulary">**substantial**</span> [səbˈstænʃl]
<span class="definition">adj. 大量的：</span>Substantial numbers of people support the reforms. 相當多的人支援這些改革措施。/ The party has just lost office and with it a substantial number of seats. 該黨剛剛競選失利，同時還失去了許多席位。/ He ate a substantial breakfast. 他吃了一頓豐盛的早餐。
           
<span class="vocabulary">**magnificent**</span> [mægˈnɪfɪsnt]
<span class="definition">adj. 數量或數額極大的：</span>magnificent sum 極大的數額

<span class="vocabulary">**mountainous**</span> ['maʊntɪnəs] 
<span class="definition">adj. 數量如山之大的，相當於huge：</span>the company’s mountainous debt 該公司的鉅額債務

<span class="vocabulary">**handsome**</span> ['hænsəm] 
<span class="definition">adj. 數量（或數額）大的：</span>He was elected by a handsome majority (= a lot of people voted for him). 他以高票當選。
           
<span class="vocabulary">**countless**</span> [ˈkaʊntləs]
<span class="definition">adj. [常用於名詞前] 無數的、數不盡的：</span>countless examples 無數的例子 / I've warned her countless times. 我警告過她無數次了。/ The new treatment could save Emma's life and the lives of countless others. 新的療法可拯救埃瑪以及無數其他人的生命。/ She brought joy to countless people through her music. 她用音樂給無數的人帶來歡樂。/ There are countless small ski areas dotted about the province. 該省內遍佈著許多小型滑雪場。/ She received countless letters of support while in jail. 她在獄中收到了無數支援她的信件。
           
<span class="vocabulary">**numerous**</span> [ˈnju:mərəs; 美 ˈnu:-]

☀ <span class="category">**某些、某個的各種詞性：**</span>
<span class="vocabulary">**some**</span> [sʌm, səm, sm] 
<span class="definition">det. 1 用於指一個群體中的某些成員或一個事物的某些型別（而非全部），即某些：</span>Some people find this more difficult than others. 這件事有人覺得難，有人覺得不難。/ I like some modern music. 有些現代音樂我喜歡。<span class="definition">2 與單數名詞連用，表示未知（或未確指）的一個人、地方、事物或時間，即某個：</span>There must be some mistake. 一定是出了什麼差錯。/ He’s in some kind of trouble. 他遇到了什麼麻煩。/ She won a competition in some newspaper or other. 她在某報舉辦的競賽中得了獎。/ I’ll see you again some time, I’m sure. 我敢肯定，什麼時候我們還會再見面的。

<span class="vocabulary">**certain**</span> ['sə:tn] 
<span class="definition">adj. 1 用於提及某個事物、人或群組（而不提及關於其的更多細節），即某事，某事物，某人，某種：</span>For certain personal reasons I shall not be able to attend. 由於某些個人原因，我將不能出席。/ Certain people might disagree with this. 某些人對這事可能不會贊同。<span class="definition">2 [正式] 與一個人的名字連用，以表示說話者不認識該人，即某某，某位，一位叫…的：</span>It was a certain Dr Davis who performed the operation. 是一位叫戴維斯的醫生做的手術。<span class="definition">pron. [正式] certain of ... 用於談及（一組人或事物中）未給出名字的那些成員，即某些：</span>Certain of those present were unwilling to discuss the matter further. 某些在場的人不願意進一步討論這個問題。
