<span class="vocabulary">**ignore**</span> [ɪɡ'nɔ:] 
<span class="definition">vt. 1 不注意或不理會某事物，相當於disregard。一般是主動不理會：</span>He ignored the ‘No Smoking’ sign and lit up a cigarette. 他無視“禁止吸菸”的警示牌，點了根菸。/ to ignore the problem 忽視問題 / to ignore the risk 忽視風險 / to ignore my suggestion 忽視我的建議 / He preferred to ignore these comments. 他寧肯對這些評論置之不理。<span class="definition">2 假裝沒有看到某人（或假裝某人沒在那裡），即假裝未見，相當於take no notice of：</span>She ignored him and carried on with her work. 她沒理他，繼續幹她的活。

<span class="vocabulary">**overlook**</span> [͵əʊvə'lʊk] 
<span class="definition">vt. 1 沒有看到或注意到，即忽略，相當於miss：</span>to overlook a fact 忽略事實 / to overlook this possibility 忽視這一可能性 / to overlook this need 忽視這個需求 / I was prepared to overlook her mistakes this time. 我不準備計較她這次的過失。/ The significance of this should not be overlooked. 不應忽視這件事的意義。<span class="definition">2 看到錯的或壞的…，但決定忽視它，即視而不見，不予理會：</span>We could not afford to overlook such a serious offence. 對這樣嚴重的違法行為，我們決不能視若無睹。<span class="definition">3 overlook sb (for sth) 為崗位或職位選定人員的時候，對某人不予考慮（即使其可能適合該職位），相當於pass over：</span>She’s been overlooked for promotion several times. 幾次提拔時都沒有考慮她。

<span class="vocabulary">**dismiss**</span> [dɪs'mɪs] 
<span class="definition">vt. dismiss sb/sth (as sth) 認為…不重要且不值得考慮（或不值一提），相當於wave aside：</span>to dismiss his objection 對他的異議不予理會 / Children’s fears should never be dismissed lightly. 對於兒童的恐懼千萬別不當回事兒。/ She dismissed their arguments as irrelevant. 她認為他們的論據毫不相關而不予考慮。
           
<span class="vocabulary">**dismissal**</span> [dɪsˈmɪsl]
<span class="definition">n. [U]（對某事之重要性的）不予考慮、不予理會：</span>Her casual dismissal of the threats seemed irresponsible. 她對這些威脅毫不在乎而不予理會，似乎很不負責任。/ their high-handed dismissal of public opinion 他們對公眾意見的蠻橫漠視 / His wholesale dismissal of women composers is indefensible. 他對女作曲家的全盤否定是站不住腳的。/ his callous dismissal of her father's illness 他對她父親的病麻木不仁，不聞不問
           
<span class="vocabulary">**disregard**</span> [ˌdɪsrɪˈgɑ:d; 美 -ˈgɑ:rd]
<span class="definition">vt. 不予考慮；認為…不重要。如不理會、漠視：</span>The board completely disregarded my recommendations. 董事會完全無視我的建議。/ Safety rules were disregarded. 安全規定被忽視了。/ He disregarded the advice of his executives. 他對主管層的建議置若罔聞。/ Critics say he allowed the police and security forces to disregard human rights. 批評人士說他聽任警察和安全部隊漠視人權。<span class="definition">n. [U] ~ (for/of sb/sth) 漠視、忽視、不關心：</span>She shows a total disregard for other people's feelings. 她絲毫不顧及別人的感受。/ He showed complete disregard for the feelings of his family. 他完全不顧及家人的感受。/ their reckless disregard for human life 他們全然不顧人的死活 / With a fine disregard for geography, she decided to drive to the island.（fine為諷刺用法）她完全不考慮地理因素，決定開車去那個島。/ their flagrant disregard of the rules 他們對規定的公然漠視

<span class="vocabulary">**discount**</span> ['dɪskaʊnt] 
<span class="definition">vt. discount sth (as sth) 是一種正式用法，表示認為（或說）…不重要、不真實或不相關，即不理會，相當於dismiss。但不常用：</span>We cannot discount the possibility of further strikes. 我們不能低估再次發生罷工的可能性。/ The news reports were being discounted as propaganda. 人們認為這些新聞報道不過是為了宣傳，不可全信。

<span class="vocabulary">**escape**</span> [ɪ'skeɪp] 
<span class="definition">vt. 被忽視，未被注意，此義項不用被動式：</span>It might have escaped your notice, but I’m very busy at the moment. 也許你沒注意到，可我此刻忙得不可開交。/ They’re so small they can easily escape notice. 他們這麼矮小，很容易避開他人的注意。/ errors that had escaped my notice 我沒注意到的錯誤
           
<span class="vocabulary">**neglect**</span> [nɪˈglekt]
<span class="definition">vt. 沒給與足夠的重視，如忽視、忽略：</span>Dance has been neglected by television. 電視節目一向不重視舞蹈。/ She has neglected her studies. 她忽視了自己的學習。/ He'd given too much to his career, worked long hours, neglected her. 他為事業付出太多，長時間地工作，忽視了她。/ If you are not careful, children tend to neglect their homework. 如果你不注意的話，孩子們往往會不重視他們的家庭作業。/ These are subjects generally neglected by historians. 這些是歷史學家通常會忽略的話題。/ This sector is one of the major growth areas and we cannot afford to neglect it. 這一行業是主要的經濟增長領域之一，我們不能忽視。/ Local communities have been neglected in favour of private sector interests. 為了私營經濟的利益而忽略了當地社群。<span class="definition">vi. [正式] 沒有（或忘記）去做本應該做的事，如疏忽、疏漏：</span>You neglected to mention the name of your previous employer. 你遺漏了你前僱主的名字。/ We often neglect to make proper use of our bodies. 我們經常忘記該合理地使用我們的身體。/ They never neglect their duties. 他們從未翫忽職守。/ They conveniently neglected their responsibilities. 他們有意翫忽職守。<span class="definition">vt. 疏於照管、未予看管：</span>She denies neglecting her baby. 她不承認沒有照看好她的孩子。/ The woman denied that she had neglected her child. 那位女士否認疏於照管自己的孩子。/ Feed plants and they grow, neglect them and they suffer. 人勤地生寶，人懶地長草。/ The buildings had been neglected for years. 這些大樓多年來一直無人看管。<span class="definition">n. [U] ~ (of sth/sb) 疏於照管；未被重視：</span>Medical neglect occurs when medical care is withheld. 拒絕提供醫療護理就是醫療不作為。/ The maximum penalty for child neglect is ten years' imprisonment. 疏於照管孩子的最高刑罰為十年監禁。/ children who are victims of deliberate parental neglect 被父母有意忽視的孩子 / The doctor was guilty of serious neglect of duty. 這個醫生犯嚴重瀆職罪。/ the suffering of children through neglect 由於疏於照管帶給孩子們的痛苦 / The law imposes penalties for the neglect of children. 法律對疏於照管兒童有處罰措施。/ She had concentrated on her music to the neglect of her other studies. 她醉心於音樂，因而忽略了其他方面的學習。/ benign 善意的忽視 / The eighteenth-century interior of the building has survived through benign neglect. 這座建築十八世紀式的內部構造因受到寬待而得以儲存。/ The buildings are crumbling from years of neglect. 由於多年無人維修，這些建築物行將倒塌。/ The buildings suffered neglect for centuries. 這些房屋已經廢棄幾百年了。/ The place smelled of decay and neglect. 這地方有一股汙濁腐朽的氣味。
           
<span class="vocabulary">**neglected**</span> [nɪˈglektɪd]
<span class="definition">adj. 1 未得到足夠關心的，衣食不足的：</span>neglected children 無人照看的孩子 / The group of street children looked ragged and neglected. 這群流浪兒童看上去衣衫襤褸，沒人照顧。<span class="definition">2 未得到足夠關注的：</span>a neglected area of research 被人忽略了的研究領域 / His tools lay neglected on the workbench. 他的工具扔在工作臺上沒人管。/ It is a sadly neglected work. 這項工作被嚴重忽視了。/ The building is sorely neglected. 這棟建築完全被忽略了。/ She is an excellent and unjustly neglected author. 她是一位優秀作家，但卻沒有獲得應有的重視。
                                                       
<span class="vocabulary">**negligent**</span> [ˈneglɪdʒənt]
<span class="definition">adj. [正式或法律] 疏忽的（尤指會造成嚴重結果）：</span>The school had been negligent in not informing the child's parents about the incident. 校方疏忽了，沒有向這孩子的父母通報這件事。/ grossly negligent 嚴重失職 / The jury determined that the airline was negligent in training and supervising the crew. 陪審團裁定航空公司在對全體機組人員的培訓和管理問題上存在疏漏。/ The Council had acted in a negligent manner. 市政會的做法屬於失職。/ The hospital was negligent in the way it cared for this young man. 醫院對這個年輕人疏於照顧。/ The court found him to be negligent over the loss of £18 million by the local authority. 法庭宣判他在當地政府損失1,800萬英鎊一案中犯有翫忽職守罪。

<span class="vocabulary">**negligence**</span> [ˈneglɪdʒəns]
<span class="definition">n. [U] [正式或法律] 未給予某人或某事足夠的關心或關注，如失職；過失；失誤；疏忽。常作為一種罪行：</span>The accident was caused by negligence on the part of the driver. 事故是由於司機的過失造成的。/ The doctor was sued for medical negligence. 這名醫生因為引致醫療事故而被起訴。/ The soldiers were ordered to appear before a disciplinary council on charges of negligence. 士兵們被控翫忽職守，被勒令接受紀律委員會的審查。/ The plaintiff was guilty of contributory negligence for failing to wear a crash helmet.（contributory為英式用法）原告因未戴安全頭盔而負有連帶過失之責。/ The lawyer was accused of professional negligence. 這位律師被控瀆職。/ death arising from negligence 由疏忽而導致的死亡 / negligence case 翫忽職守案 / negligence claim 過失索賠 / His death was brought about by negligence on the doctor's part. 他的死亡是由醫生的過失造成的。/ damage to company property through negligence 由於疏忽而對公司財產造成的損失 / negligence in navigation 導航的失誤 / The court made a finding of criminal negligence. 法庭認定被告犯有過失罪。

<span class="vocabulary">**oversight**</span> [ˈəʊvəsaɪt; 美 ˈoʊvərs-]
<span class="definition">n. [C, U] 遺忘造成的疏忽；沒注意造成的失察：</span>administrative oversight 行政上的疏忽 / I didn't mean to leave her name off the list; it was an oversight. 我不是有意在名單上漏掉她的名字的，這是個疏忽。/ William was angered and embarrassed by his oversight. 威廉因為自己的疏忽而羞憤交加。/ By an unfortunate oversight, full instructions do not come with the product. 由於一個不幸的疏忽，產品未能附上完整的說明。/ By an oversight, we did not send out the agenda for the meeting，我們因疏忽而沒有分發會議議程。/ The mistake was due to an unfortunate oversight on my part.這一錯誤是由於我一時疏忽造成的。/ Through oversight, the bill was still unpaid.由於疏忽，賬單仍未支付。

<span class="vocabulary">**exclusion**</span> [ɪkˈsklu:ʒn]
<span class="definition">n. [U] 將其它事情排除在外，不予考慮：</span>Memories of the past filled her mind to the exclusion of all else. 她滿腦子全是對過去的回憶，其他事情都不想了。/ Diane had dedicated her life to caring for him to the exclusion of all else. 戴安娜一生都在照顧他，對其他一切都無暇顧及。

<span class="vocabulary">**omit**</span> [əˈmɪt]
<span class="definition">vt. [正式] ~ sth/sb (from sth) 未將…包括在內，如遺漏、漏掉、忽略：</span>The acknowledgements were inadvertently omitted from the article. 因疏忽文章中遺漏了致謝詞。/ Some important details were deliberately omitted from the report. 報告中故意漏掉了一些重要的細節。/ Our apologies to David Pannick for omitting his name from last week's article. 我們在上週的文章裡漏掉了戴維·潘尼克的名字，特此向他致歉。

<span class="vocabulary">**omission**</span> [əˈmɪʃn]
<span class="definition">n. 1 [U] ~ (from sth) 遺漏；疏忽。可以是蓄意的，也可以是非蓄意的：</span>the omission of the author's name 作者名字的遺漏 / sins of omission (= not doing things that should be done)（正式）瀆職罪 <span class="definition">2 [C] 被遺漏或疏忽的東西：</span>There were a number of errors and omissions in the article. 這篇文章中有多處錯誤和疏漏。/ There were no glaring omissions in the report. 報告中沒有明顯疏漏。/ I notice one glaring omission from your list. 我發現你列的清單上有一處明顯遺漏。/ I feel compelled to point out two omissions. 我覺得必須指出兩處遺漏。

<span class="vocabulary">**go**</span> [ɡəʊ] 
<span class="definition">vi. go unnoticed, unreported, etc. 未被注意到（或未被報告等）：</span>Police are worried that many crimes go unreported. 令警方感到不安的是許多罪行發生後無人報案。

<span class="vocabulary">**blank**</span> [blæŋk] 
<span class="definition">vt. [英，非正式] 完全忽視某人：</span>I saw her on the bus this morning, but she totally blanked me. 我今天早晨在公交車上見到她，但她連一眼都沒瞧我。

<span class="vocabulary">**miss**</span> [mɪs] 
<span class="definition">vt. 未見到：</span>The hotel is the only white building on the road, you can’t miss it. 酒店是這條路上唯一的白色建築，你不會看不見的。/ His searching eye never missing a detail. 他那銳利的目光從未漏掉一個細節。

<span class="vocabulary">**blind**</span> [blaɪnd] 
<span class="definition">adj. blind (to sth)（對事實、情況等）視而不見的，未覺察到的（常指故意視而不見的，或本應覺察到而實際上沒有覺察到的）：</span>His good looks had always made her blind to his faults. 他英俊的外表總是令她對他的缺點視而不見。/ I must have been blind not to realize the danger we were in. 當時我一定是眼瞎了，竟然沒有意識到我們所處的危險。/ His own problems have made him completely blind to the sufferings of others. 他自己的煩惱使他完全看不見別人的痛苦。

<span class="vocabulary">**deaf**</span> [def] 
<span class="definition">adj. deaf to sth 只作表語，指不願意聽或不願意去注意的，即充耳不聞的：</span>He was deaf to my requests for help. 他對我的求助充耳不聞。/ The committee remained deaf to our suggestions. 委員會對我們的建議仍然充耳不聞。
                     
<span class="vocabulary">**invisible**</span> [ɪnˈvɪzəbl]
<span class="definition">adj. 被人忽視的、不為人注意的：</span>It was strange, how invisible a clerk could feel. 一名辦事員會感到如此受人忽視，令人覺得不可思議。/ The problems of the poor are largely invisible. 窮人的問題大多不受重視。
            
<span class="vocabulary">**unnoticed**</span> [ˌʌnˈnəʊtɪst; 美 -ˈnoʊ-]
<span class="definition">adj. [名詞前不可用] 未被看到的；未被注意到的；被忽視的：</span>I tried to slip up the stairs unnoticed. 我試圖趁沒人注意溜上樓去。/ She slipped away unnoticed from the party. 她偷偷從聚會上溜了出來。/ There's a good chance my absence will go completely unnoticed. 我的缺席很可能完全不會有人注意到。/ Her forty-fourth birthday had just passed, unnoticed by all but herself. 她四十四歲的生日就這樣過去了，除了她自己沒人注意到。/ His kindness did not go unnoticed by his staff. 他的厚道員工瞭然於心。/ The ticket lay unnoticed in my desk drawer for a week. 票放在我書桌的抽屜裡一個星期沒注意到。/ His remark passed unnoticed. 他的話誰也沒在意。/ a hitherto unnoticed detail 一個迄今未有人注意的細節

<span class="vocabulary">**unaware**</span> [ˌʌnəˈweə(r); 美 -ˈwer]
<span class="definition">adj. [名詞前不可用] ~ of sth / ~ that…（對正在發生的事或存在的某事物）沒意識到的、未察覺的：</span>Many people are unaware of just how much food and drink they consume. 許多人並不知道他們究竟消耗掉了多少食品和飲料。/ She was unaware that she was being filmed. 她不知道有人在拍攝她。/ She was unaware that I could see her. 她沒想到我能看見她。/ They remained blissfully unaware of his true intentions. 他們依然很開心，對他的真實意圖毫不知情。

<span class="vocabulary">**unheard**</span> [ʌnˈhɜ:d; 美 ʌnˈhɜ:rd]
<span class="definition">adj. 1 無人理會的：</span>Their protests went unheard. 他們的抗議無人理會。/ His impassioned pleas went unheard. 他充滿激情的請求未被理睬。/ She had not waited for any explanation but had condemned him unheard. 她沒等他解釋，就指責他充耳不聞。<span class="definition">2 未被傾聽（或聽到）的：</span>a previously unheard tape of their conversations 以前未聽過的他們談話的錄音帶 / Martin's weak cries for help went unheard until 6.40pm yesterday. 直到昨天傍晚6點40分，馬丁微弱的呼救聲才被人聽到。

<span class="vocabulary">**contempt**</span> [kənˈtempt]
<span class="definition">n. [U, sing.] ~ for sth（對規則、危險等的）無視、不理會：</span>His remarks betray a staggering contempt for the truth (= are completely false). 他的話表明他完全無視事情的真相。
           
<span class="vocabulary">**hiatus**</span> [haɪˈeɪtəs]
