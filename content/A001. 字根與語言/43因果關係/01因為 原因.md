☀ <span class="category">**為何、何必的副詞和動詞：**</span>
<span class="vocabulary">**why**</span> [waɪ] 
<span class="definition">adv. 1 用於疑問句，以詢問…的原因或目的，即為什麼，為何：</span>Why were you late? 你為什麼遲到？/ Tell me why you did it. 告訴我你為什麼這樣做。<span class="definition">2 用於疑問句，以表示沒必要做某事，即何必：</span>Why get upset just because you got one bad grade? 何必因為一次成績不好就想不開呢？/ Why bother to write? We’ll see him tomorrow. 還費事寫信幹什麼？我們明天就見到他了。<span class="definition">3 用於給出（或說明）一個理由，即為什麼，…的原因：</span>That’s why I left so early. 這就是我早早離去的原因。/ The reason why the injection needs repeating every year is that the virus changes. 每年需要重新注射的原因是這種病毒經常變化。/ I know you did it--I just want to know why. 我知道這是你乾的，我只是想知道為什麼。

<span class="vocabulary">**come**</span> [kʌm] 
<span class="definition">vi. 用於疑問句，表示為什麼：</span>How do you come to be so late? 你怎麼這麼晚？
           
<span class="vocabulary">**attribute**</span> [əˈtrɪbju:t]
<span class="definition">vt. ~ sth to sth 把…歸因於…：</span>We tend to attribute their success to external causes such as luck. 我們傾向於將她們的成功歸因於外部因素，比如運氣。/ They claim that one in twenty deaths can be directly attributed to air pollution. 他們稱每二十例死亡個案中就有一例是空氣汙染直接造成的。/ Climate change is widely attributed to the building-up of greenhouse gases. 人們普遍認為氣候變化是由溫室氣體的增加造成的。
           
<span class="vocabulary">**ascribe**</span> [əˈskraɪb]
<span class="definition">vt. ~ sth to.. [正式] 把…歸因於…：</span>He ascribed his failure to bad luck. 他認為自己的失敗是運氣不好。/ An autopsy eventually ascribed the baby's death to sudden infant death syndrome. 屍檢最終認定孩子死於嬰兒猝死綜合徵。

☀ <span class="category">**因為、由於的連詞：**</span>
<span class="vocabulary">**because**</span> [bɪ'kɒz] 
<span class="definition">conj. 因為。是此義的常規用詞，最為常用：</span>I did it because he told me to. 是他吩咐我才做的。/ Just because I don’t complain, people think I’m satisfied. 就因為我不發牢騷，大家便以為我滿意了。

<span class="vocabulary">**as**</span> [æz] 
<span class="definition">conj. 因為，由於。此時與because在詞義上未見明顯區別，只是遠不如because常用：</span>As you were out, I left a message. 你不在，所以我留了一張字條兒。/ She may need some help as she’s new. 她是新來的，可能需要一些幫助。

<span class="vocabulary">**for**</span> [fɔ:] 
<span class="definition">conj. [舊時或文學] 用於為已做的陳述引入原因或理由，即因為，由於：</span>We listened eagerly, for he brought news of our families. 我們急不可待地聽著，因為他帶來了我們家人的訊息。/ I believed her--for surely she would not lie to me. 我相信她的話——因為她肯定不會對我撒謊。

<span class="vocabulary">**since**</span> [sɪns] 
<span class="definition">conj. 因為，由於。不常用：</span>I’m forever on a diet, since I put on weight easily. 我一直在節食，因為我的體重很容易增加。

☀ <span class="category">**因為、由於的介詞：**</span>
<span class="vocabulary">**for**</span> [fɔ:] 
<span class="definition">prep. 用於表明原因或理由：</span>The town is famous for its cathedral. 這個城鎮以大教堂而聞名。/ She gave me a watch for my birthday. 她送給我一塊手錶作為生日禮物。/ He got an award for bravery. 他因英勇受獎。/ I couldn’t speak for laughing. 我笑得說不出話來。

<span class="vocabulary">**from**</span> [frɒm] 
<span class="definition">prep. 表示…的原因或理由，即因為，由於。是從原因的來源之角度給出的定義：</span>She felt sick from tiredness. 她累得渾身不舒服。/ The problem simply resulted from a difference of opinion. 問題僅因意見不同而引起。/ They really do get pleasure from spending money on other people. 他們確實因把錢花在別人身上而從中得到快樂。
           
<span class="vocabulary">**following**</span> [ˈfɒləʊɪŋ; 美 ˈfɑ:loʊɪŋ]
<span class="definition">prep. 由於：</span>瞭解即可

<span class="vocabulary">**at**</span> [æt] 
<span class="definition">prep. 與形容詞連用，表示某人因某事而…，即因為：</span>They were impatient at the delay. 他們對拖延不耐煩了。/ She was delighted at the result. 對這個結果她感到高興。

<span class="vocabulary">**of**</span> [əv] 
<span class="definition">prep. 用於某些形容詞後，後接與感情相關的人或事物，可理解為因為，由於：</span>to be proud of sth 為某事而自豪 / I have grown very fond of Tom. 我已變得很喜歡湯姆。

<span class="vocabulary">**by**</span> [baɪ] 
<span class="definition">prep. 用於不帶the的名詞前，表示某事的發生是由於…：</span>I met him by chance (or by accident) when I was out for a walk. 我昨天出去散步的時候偶然間遇到了他。/ I opened the poisonous bottle by mistake. 我誤開了那個有毒的瓶子。

<span class="vocabulary">**behind**</span> [bɪ'haɪnd] 
<span class="definition">prep. 是某種情況背後的原因：</span>What’s behind that happy smile? 為什麼會笑得那麼開心？

<span class="vocabulary">**with**</span> [wɪð] 
<span class="definition">prep. 由於，因為：</span>She’s in bed with fever. 她由於發燒而躺在床上。

<span class="vocabulary">**through**</span> [θru:] 
<span class="definition">prep. 因為，由於：</span>This happened through his mistake. 此事源於他的過錯。

<span class="vocabulary">**after**</span> ['ɑːftə] 
<span class="definition">prep. 由於（或鑑於）已發生的事：</span>I’ll never forgive him after what he said. 由於他說了那些話，我永遠也不會原諒他。

☀ <span class="category">**因為、由於的其它詞性：**</span>
<span class="vocabulary">**owe**</span> [əʊ] 
<span class="definition">vt. owe sth to sb/sth / owe sb sth 歸因於：</span>He owes his success to hard work. 他的成功是靠勤奮工作。/ The play owes much to French tragedy. 這部戲頗受法國悲劇的影響。

<span class="vocabulary">**due**</span> [dju:] 
<span class="definition">adj. due to sth/sb 因為，由於。既可用於系動詞後，也可用於句子中間起連線作用：</span>The team’s success was largely due to her efforts. 這個隊的成功在很大程度上是她努力的結果。/ Most of the problems were due to human error. 多數問題都是人為失誤造成的。/ The project had to be abandoned due to a lack of government funding. 這項工程由於缺乏政府的資助而不得不放棄。
           
<span class="vocabulary">**responsible**</span> [rɪˈspɒnsəbl; 美 -ˈspɑ:n-]
<span class="definition">adj. ~ (for sth) 作為…的原因的：</span>Cigarette smoking is responsible for about 90% of deaths from lung cancer. 因患肺癌而死亡者，約90%是吸菸所致。/ No single pathogen seems wholly responsible for the disease. 這個病似乎不完全是由某一個病原體引起的。/ It's been one of the factors chiefly responsible for improved public health. 這是公共衛生得以改善的主要因素之一。

<span class="vocabulary">**out**</span> [aʊt] 
<span class="definition">adv., prep. out of sth 表示某事發生的原因，即因為，出於。與from一樣，二者都是從原因的來源的角度做出的定義：</span>I asked out of curiosity. 我出於好奇問了問。/ He did it out of spite. 他那麼做是出於惡意。/ He took up office out of a sense of duty. 他任職是出於一種責任感。

<span class="vocabulary">**to**</span> [tu:] 
<span class="definition">infinitive marker. 表示某事的原因：</span>I’m sorry to hear that. 我聽到這訊息很難過。

☀ <span class="category">**既然的連詞：**</span>
<span class="vocabulary">**since**</span> [sɪns] 
<span class="definition">conj. 既然：</span>We thought that, since we were in the area, we’d stop by and see them. 我們想，既然到了這個地方，就該順便去看看他們。

<span class="vocabulary">**now**</span> [naʊ] 
<span class="definition">conj. now (that) ... 由於（正發生或已發生的事），既然：</span>Now that the kids have left home we’ve got a lot of extra space. 孩子們都離開家了，我們住著就更寬敞了。/ Now you’re settled, why don’t you take up some serious study? 既然你安頓下來了，為什麼不做些認真的研究呢？

☀ <span class="category">**原因、理由的名詞：**</span>
<span class="vocabulary">**cause**</span> [kɔ:z] 
<span class="definition">n. 1 [C] 是某事發生的原因，起因（可以是人、事物或事件）：</span>cause and effect 因果 / the cause of death 死因 / Unemployment is a major cause of poverty. 失業是貧困的主要原因。<span class="definition">2 [U] cause (for sth)（感情或行為方式的）理由，緣故：</span>There is no cause for concern. 沒有理由擔憂。/ We have good cause to believe that he was involved in the crime. 我們有充分的理由相信他涉嫌此案。

<span class="vocabulary">**reason**</span> ['ri:zn] 
<span class="definition">n. 1 [C] reason (why ...) / reason (that ...) / reason (for sth/for doing sth)（已發生事件或某人已做的事件的）原因或解釋，理由：</span>personal, sentimental reason 個人原因；感情的原因 / economic, legal, political, technical reason 經濟原因；法律原因；政治原因；技術原因 / security, health reason 安全原因；健康原因 / He got angry for no reason. 他平白無故地發怒。<span class="definition">2 [U] reason (to do sth) / reason (why ...) / reason (for sth/for doing sth)（做某事的）正當理由，可稱為道理或情理：</span>They have reason to believe that he is lying. 他們有理由認為他是在撒謊。/ This result gives us all the more reason for optimism. 這個結果使我們更有理由保持樂觀。/ I don’t know why he did that, but I’m sure he had his reasons. 我不知道他為什麼那麼做，但我相信他一定有他的理由。/ Give me one good reason why I should help you. 給我一個必須幫你的正當理由。
                      
<span class="vocabulary">**justification**</span> [ˌdʒʌstɪfɪˈkeɪʃn]
<span class="definition">n. [U, C] ~ (for sth/doing sth) 正當理由；合理的解釋：</span>I can see no possible justification for any further tax increases. 我看不出還能提出什麼理由來進一步加稅了。/ He was getting angry--and with some justification. 他生氣了——而且不是沒有道理的。/ To me the only justification for a zoo is educational. 對我來說，動物園存在的唯一正當理由是其教育作用。/ I knew from the beginning that there was no justification for what I was doing. 我從一開始就知道我的所作所為毫無道理。/ You have every justification for feeling angry. 你完全有理由感到憤怒。/ There was no compelling justification for the invasion. 這次入侵沒有令人信服的理由。/ She had not given him the slightest justification for thinking she was interested in him. 她根本沒有給他任何機會讓他覺得她對他有好感。/ ethical, ideological, moral justification 倫理方面的正當理由；意識形態上的正當理由；道德方面的正當理由 / intellectual, philosophical, rational, scientific, theological, theoretical justification 智力上的正當理由；哲學上的正當理由；理性的正當理由；科學的正當理由；神學的正當理由；理論上的正當理由 / economic, legal, political justification 經濟上的正當理由；法律上的正當理由；政治上的正當理由 / She's unable to provide any justification for her actions. 她無法為自己的行為辯解。/ I could find no real justification for the proposed reorganization. 我沒發現提出重組有什麼正當理由。/ Such a step requires substantial justification to be accepted by the public. 採取這一措施必須有充分的正當理由才能為公眾所接受。/ the argument which he put forward in justification 他用以辯解的理由 / He felt, with some justification, that he had been unfairly treated. 他感覺自己受到了不公正對待也不是沒有理由的。/ She was arrested entirely without justification. 她被毫無理由地逮捕了。/ I can see some justification for her remarks. 我能看出她說那些話有些道理。/ She never presents an ideological justification of her work. 她從來不會從意識形態角度為自己的工作提出辯解。

<span class="vocabulary">**occasion**</span> [əˈkeɪʒn]
<span class="definition">n. [U, sing.] ~ (to do sth) / ~ (of/for sth) [正式] 理由、原因：</span>I've had no occasion to visit him recently. 我最近無緣去拜訪他。/ Her death was the occasion of mass riots. 她的逝世引發了大規模的騷亂。/ I'm willing to go to court over this if the occasion arises (= if it becomes necessary). 如果必要的話，我願意就此出庭。

<span class="vocabulary">**root**</span> [ru:t]

<span class="vocabulary">**source**</span> [sɔ:s; 美 sɔ:rs]

<span class="vocabulary">**basis**</span> ['beɪsɪs]

<span class="vocabulary">**gauge**</span> [geɪdʒ]
