☀ <span class="category">**出席：**</span>
<span class="vocabulary">**attend**</span> [ə'tend] 
<span class="definition">vt.＆vi. 出席（或參加）一場活動。是此義的常規用詞，最為常用：</span>to attend a meeting, to attend a wedding, to attend a lecture 出席會議；參加婚禮；去現場聽講座 / The event was well attended. 許多人參加了這一盛事。
           
<span class="vocabulary">**attendance**</span> [əˈtendəns]
<span class="definition">n. 1 [U, C] 出席、到場、上學出勤：</span>compulsory, mandatory（尤美）強制參加；不得缺席 / church, cinema（英）, college, movie（美）, school attendance 去教堂；看電影；上大學；上學等 / Attendance at these lectures is not compulsory. 這些課不是硬性規定要聽的。/ Teachers must keep a record of students' attendances. 老師必須記錄學生的出勤情況。/ Her attendance at school was sporadic. 她三天兩頭曠課。/ Councillor Paul Tisley will be in attendance at the start of the meeting to welcome us. 議員保羅·提斯里將在會議開始時出席，以歡迎我們。/ Police with riot gear and several fire engines are in attendance. 防暴警察和數輛消防車都到了。/ He lived in considerable style, travelling widely, usually with a cook, valet, butler and chauffeur in attendance. 他活得相當瀟灑，通常帶著廚師、用人、管家和司機到處遊玩。/ Weekly attendance at a religious service was the norm. 每週參加一次宗教儀式是常規。/ Regular attendance at lectures is important. 定期參加講座很重要。/ The ambulances were in attendance within 22 minutes. 救護車 22 分鐘就趕到了現場。/ attendance at the meetings 出席會議 / take attendance（美）（英式英語用take the register）點名 / Mrs Sakamoto had finished taking attendance. 坂本夫人已經點完名了。<span class="definition">2 [C, U] 出席人數：</span>There was an attendance of 42 at the meeting. 有42人參加了會議。/ Average weekly cinema attendance in February was 2.41 million. 2月份，平均每週的觀影人數為241萬。/ This year attendances were 28% lower than forecast. 今年的出席人數比預期少28%。/ It was a record attendance for a midweek game. 對於一場週中期進行的比賽，有這麼多人到場還是首次。/ Despite falling attendances, the zoo will stay open. 儘管參觀人數不斷減少，動物園將繼續開放。/ The average attendance at sports events increased last year. 去年現場觀看體育賽事的平均人數有所增長。/ Average daily school attendance was only 75%. 學生平均每天的出勤率只有75%。/ Building a new stadium has boosted attendances by 40%. 新體育場的修建使觀眾人數提高了 40%。/ Attendances at the pool always fall in winter. 冬天去游泳的人總會減少。/ Movie attendance soared during the war. 戰爭期間去看電影的人數陡增。/ attendance figures, attendance numbers（尤美）, attendance rate, attendance record 出席人數；到場人數；出席率；出席記錄 / Attendance figures were up this year. 今年的出席人數上升了。

<span class="vocabulary">**present**</span> [prɪ'zent, 'preznt] 
<span class="definition">vt. [正式] present yourself at, for, in, etc.（自己）正式出現在某處，如出席等。含義較廣：</span>You will be asked to present yourself for interview. 將請你到場面試。/ She was ordered to present herself in court on 20 May. 她被傳喚於5月20日出庭。/ Get word to him right away that he’s to present himself at City Hall by tomorrow afternoon. 立即通知他，他得於明天下午之前到市政廳來。<span class="definition">adj. present (at sth) 不作前置定語，（人）正在某一地，即在場，出席等：</span>to be present at the meeting 出席會議 / He wasn’t present at the birth of his child. 他的孩子出生時他不在場。
           
<span class="vocabulary">**presence**</span> [ˈprezns]
<span class="definition">n. [U]（人）出席、在場：</span>Your presence is requested at the meeting.（正式）敬請出席會議。/ He hardly seemed to notice my presence. 他似乎沒有注意到我在場。/ Her Majesty later honoured the Headmaster with her presence at lunch. 隨後女王陛下出席了午餐，令校長感到榮幸。/ How nice of you to grace us with your presence! 您的大駕光臨使我們蓬蓽生輝! / The King requested our presence this morning. 國王要求我們今天上午到場。/ Your presence is required two days from now. 要求你兩天後到場。/ Her presence during the crisis had a calming effect. 危難時她的存在穩定了大家的心情。/ Her comforting presence made him feel safe. 她的在場給了他安慰，使他感到很安全。/ the physical presence of actors among the audience 親身出現在觀眾中的演員 / They argued that his presence in the village could only stir up trouble. 他們認為他在村裡出現只會招惹麻煩。/ The talks took place in the presence of a diplomatic observer. 會談是在一位外交觀察員在場的情況下進行的。/ He acknowledged our presence with a nod of his head. 他點了一下頭對我們打招呼。/ He should never have made those remarks in your presence. 他怎麼都不該當著你的面說那些話。/ She certainly made her presence felt in the boardroom. 她確實讓自己在董事會上受到了關注。/ Rather than politely lobbying politicians, Gay Dignity will be making its presence felt through demonstrations. “同性戀尊嚴”遊行活動不會彬彬有禮地向政客們遊說，而會透過示威引起關注。/ The mere presence of children in the room is enough to upset him. 只要有孩子在房間裡，他就會不高興。

<span class="vocabulary">**be**</span> [bi:] 
<span class="definition">vi. 出席一個活動：</span>I’ll be at the party. 我將出席聚會。
           
<span class="vocabulary">**turnout**</span> [ˈtɜ:naʊt; 美 ˈtɜ:rn-]
<span class="definition">n. [C, usually sing., U] 某個事件的出席人數、到場人數：</span>This year's festival attracted a record turnout. 今年的節日吸引的參加者之多創了紀錄。/ On the big night there was a massive turnout. 在這個重要的夜晚，有大批人出席。/ It was a marvellous afternoon with a huge turnout of people. 那是個美妙的下午，出席人數眾多。/ The concert attracted a large turnout. 音樂會吸引了大批的觀眾。/ I think we will have the biggest turnout we've had for quite some time. 我認為我們的參與人數將是很長時間以來最多的。
           
<span class="vocabulary">**registration**</span> [ˌredʒɪˈstreɪʃn]
<span class="definition">n. [U] [英]（課堂上教師看著學生名單對學生進行的）點名（以確定學生是否來上課）：</span>瞭解即可

☀ <span class="category">**缺席：**</span>
<span class="vocabulary">**absence**</span> ['æbsəns] 
<span class="definition">n. [U, C] absence (from ...)（某人對於自己通常所處於的地點的）缺席，不在；也可指缺席（或不在）的場合或一段時間：</span>absence from work 缺勤 / repeated absences from school 一再缺課 / My father did all the cooking in my mother’s absence. 媽媽不在家時都是爸爸做飯。/ He asked for leave of absence from his job. 他向單位請了假。

<span class="vocabulary">**absent**</span> ['æbsənt, æb'sent] 
<span class="definition">adj. absent (from sth) 由於疾病等而不在某地的，即缺席的：</span>He was absent from work for two weeks. 他兩個星期沒有上班了。/ to be absent from the meeting 沒有到會 / to be absent without leave 未經許可而缺席 <span class="definition">vt. [正式] absent yourself (from sth) 缺席或不參加（本應該到場的一個場合）：</span>He had absented himself from the office for the day. 這一天他沒有去辦公室上班。

<span class="vocabulary">**away**</span> [ə'weɪ] 
<span class="definition">adv. away (from sb/sth) 不在，離開：</span>She was away from work for a week. 她有一個星期沒來上班。/ There were ten children away yesterday. 昨天有十個孩子缺席。/ Sorry, he’s away. 對不起，他不在。

周邊詞彙補充：
· miss可指不在某地、沒去某地、錯過；
· excuse可指家長或醫生寫的為何一個學生不能去上課或某人為何不能去工作的紙條，即請假條。
