☀ <span class="category">**原諒、寬恕、饒恕、赦免：**</span>
<span class="vocabulary">**forgive**</span> [fə'ɡɪv] 
<span class="definition">vt.＆vi. 1 forgive sb/yourself (for sth/for doing sth) / forgive sb sth 不再氣憤於別人對你所造成的傷害或煩惱；或不再生自己的氣。即原諒或寬恕。是此義的常規用詞：</span>I’ll never forgive her for what she did. 我絕不會原諒她的所作所為。/ I can’t forgive that type of behaviour. 我不能原諒那種行為。/ Can you forgive me? 你能原諒我嗎？/ He was not the sort of man to forgive and forgot. 他不是那種不記仇的人。<span class="definition">2 forgive me (for doing sth) / forgive my ... 用於禮貌地（對於自己言行的魯莽或愚蠢而）向別人表示道歉，即對不起；請原諒：</span>Forgive my ignorance, but what exactly does the company do? 請原諒我的無知，這家公司到底是幹什麼的？/ Forgive me for interrupting, but I really don’t agree with that. 請原諒我打岔，不過我確實不同意那一點。
           
<span class="vocabulary">**forgiveness**</span> [fəˈgɪvnəs; 美 fərˈg-]
<span class="definition">n. [U] 原諒、寬恕；願意寬恕別人：</span>to pray for God's forgiveness 祈求上帝寬恕 / the forgiveness of sins 對罪惡的寬恕 / He begged forgiveness for what he had done. 他乞求饒恕他的所作所為。/ He begged her forgiveness for his mistake. 他請求她諒解他的錯誤。/ to pray for God's forgiveness 祈求上帝寬恕 / I offered up a short prayer for forgiveness. 我做了個簡短的禱告，祈求寬恕。/ a spirit of forgiveness and national reconciliation 寬容與民族和解的精神 / Do I have your forgiveness? 你能原諒我嗎? / victims of violence who can nevertheless find forgiveness in their hearts 仍存有寬厚之心的暴力受害者

<span class="vocabulary">**excuse**</span> [ɪk'skju:z] 
<span class="definition">vt. 1 excuse sth / excuse sb (for sth/for doing sth) 原諒或寬恕某人所做過的事（如失禮或犯的小錯誤）。程度通常較輕：</span>Please excuse the mess. 這裡凌亂不堪，請見諒。/ I hope you’ll excuse me for being so late. 我來得這麼晚，希望你能原諒。/ Excuse my interrupting you.（正式用法）對不起，打擾您一下。<span class="definition">2 excuse sb/yourself (from sth) 允許某人離開；禮貌地說你想離開：</span>Now if you’ll excuse me, I’m a very busy man. 如果可以，我先行一步了。我很忙。/ She excused herself and left the meeting early. 她說了聲“請原諒”就提前離開了會場。

<span class="vocabulary">**pardon**</span> ['pɑːdn] 
<span class="definition">n. 1 [U] [正式] pardon (for sth)（對某人所做的某事的）原諒、寬恕：</span>He asked her pardon for having deceived her. 他欺騙了她，向她請求原諒。/ I ask your pardon for my actions. 我請求您寬恕我的所作所為。/ She asked for a pardon for her crime. 她請求赦免她的罪行。<span class="definition">2 [C]（英國在法律領域也可用free pardon）對罪犯的赦免或特赦，通常是領袖對政治犯的“特赦”，一般寬容罪犯的罪責但未必完全免除處罰（“大赦天下”中的“大赦”用amnesty，指寬恕破壞社會秩序的某類或各類一般犯罪，通常免除處罰）：</span>posthumous pardon 對死者的赦免 / presidential, royal pardon 總統特赦；王室特赦 / The government offered a free pardon to the rebels. 政府給予叛亂分子特赦。<span class="definition">vt. 1 pardon sb (for sth/for doing sth) 原諒某人所說的話或所做的事（可用於很多表達法中以表示禮貌），相當於excuse，此時二者未見明顯差別：</span>Pardon my ignorance, but what is a ‘duplex’? 請原諒我無知，duplex是什麼呢？/ The place was, if you’ll pardon the expression, a dump. 那個地方，請恕我直言，簡直是個垃圾場。/ Pardon my asking, but is that your husband? 請原諒我多問，那位是您的先生嗎？/ Pardon me for interrupting you. 對不起，打擾您了。<span class="definition">2 赦免或特赦（罪犯）：</span>She was pardoned after serving ten years of a life sentence. 她被判終身監禁服刑十年後被赦免了。
           
<span class="vocabulary">**amnesty**</span> [ˈæmnəsti]
<span class="definition">n. 1 [C, usually sing., U] 對政治犯的赦免或大赦：</span>blanket（尤美）, general amnesty 全部赦免；全面赦免 / The government granted a general amnesty for all political prisoners. 政府大赦了所有的政治犯。/ They announced a general amnesty for crimes committed during the war. 他們宣佈大赦戰時犯罪的人。/ Activists who were involved in crimes of violence will not automatically be granted amnesty. 參與暴力犯罪的激進分子將不會自動得到赦免。/ the President's amnesty program for illegal aliens（美）總統的非法僑民赦免計劃 / political amnesty 政治犯大赦 / tax amnesty（尤美）稅收赦免 / firearms, gun, knife amnesty（均英）槍支赦免；槍炮赦免；刀具赦免 <span class="definition">2 [C, usually sing.] 赦免期（此期間交代罪行或交出武器可獲赦免）：</span>2 000 knives have been handed in during the month-long amnesty. 在一個月的赦免期中交出了2 000把刀。/ The government has announced an immediate amnesty for rebel fighters. 政府已對叛亂士兵宣佈了即刻生效的赦免期限。

<span class="vocabulary">**spare**</span> [spεə] 
<span class="definition">vt. [正式] spare sb/sth (from sth)（尤指當其他人未逃脫時）允許其逃離傷害、損害或死亡，即饒恕；赦免；放過等：</span>They killed the men but spared the children. 他們殺了大人，但留下了孩子。/ We have lost everything, but our lives have been spared. 我們失去了一切，但保住了性命。
           
<span class="vocabulary">**absolve**</span> [əbˈzɒlv; 美 əbˈzɑ:lv]
<span class="definition">vt. ~ sb (from/of sth) 正式宣告某人無罪，或正式宣告某人不需對某事負責：</span>The court absolved him of all responsibility for the accident. 法院宣告他對該事故不負任何責任。/ A police investigation yesterday absolved the police of all blame in the incident. 警方昨天的調查廓清了其在此事件中的任何責任。/ the inquiry which absolved the soldiers 宣告士兵們並無任何過失的調查 <span class="definition">vt. ~ sb (from/of sth) 赦免某人的罪：</span>I absolve you from all your sins. 我赦免你所有的罪過。
           
<span class="vocabulary">**acquit**</span> [əˈkwɪt]
<span class="definition">vt. ~ sb (of sth) 在法庭上正式宣判某人無罪：</span>Mr Ling was acquitted of disorderly behaviour by magistrates. 地方法官宣判林先生擾亂治安罪名不成立。/ The jury acquitted him of murder. 陪審團裁決他謀殺罪不成立。
           
<span class="vocabulary">**acquittal**</span> [əˈkwɪtl]
<span class="definition">n. [C, U] 在法庭上對某人做出的無罪判決：</span>The case resulted in an acquittal. 此案件最終作出無罪判決。/ The trial resulted in an acquittal. 審判的結果是無罪釋放。/ the acquittal of six police officers charged with beating a suspect. 對六名被控毆打嫌犯的警官作出的無罪判決 / The jury voted 8-to-4 in favor of acquittal. 陪審團以8比4的投票結果贊成無罪判決。/ The jury returned an acquittal after only 22 minutes.（return為英式用法）僅22分鐘後，陪審團就宣告了無罪的裁決。/ She claimed she had been intimidated into voting for acquittal. 她宣稱遭到恐蝦而被迫投票贊成無罪判決。/ The jury voted for acquittal.（vote for為美式用法）陪審團表決贊成判定無罪。/ to direct acquittal（英）下令無罪釋放 / The trial judge ordered an acquittal. 承審法官下令無罪開釋。/ to obtain acquittal 獲得無罪宣判 / to secure acquittal（尤英）獲得無罪釋放 / to win acquittal 贏得無罪判決 / The judge upheld their acquittal. 法官維持了他們無罪的裁決。
              
<span class="vocabulary">**exonerate**</span> [ɪgˈzɒnəreɪt; 美 -ˈzɑ:n-]
<span class="definition">vt. [正式] ~ sb (from sth) 正式宣佈某人無責任，即正式免除責任：</span>The police report exonerated Lewis from all charges of corruption. 警方的報告免除了對劉易斯貪汙的所有指控。/ The official report basically exonerated everyone. 官方報道基本上宣佈所有人無罪。/ An investigation exonerated the school from any blame. 一項調查證明該學校沒有任何過失。        

<span class="vocabulary">**exempt**</span> [ɪgˈzempt]
<span class="definition">adj. [名詞前不可用] ~ (from sth) 被免除（責任、款項、規則等）的；被豁免的：</span>The interest on the money is exempt from tax. 這筆錢的利息免稅。/ This income is totally exempt from taxation. 這項收入完全免稅。/ Some students are exempt from certain exams. 有的學生可免除某些考試。/ Men in college were exempt from military service. 大學在校男生免服兵役。/ Children under two years are exempt. 兩歲以下兒童免票。/ Research might be considered exempt from regulation. 研究可能會被認為不受規則約束。<span class="definition">vt. [正式] ~ sb/sth (from sth) 正式免除某人的責任或款項：</span>His bad eyesight exempted him from military service. 他因視力不好而免服兵役。/ In 1983, charities were exempted from paying the tax. 1983年慈善團體均免付稅款。/ South Carolina claimed the power to exempt its citizens from the obligation to obey federal law. 南卡羅來納州聲稱有權力免除該州公民遵守聯邦法規的義務。/ Companies with fifty-five or fewer employees would be exempted from the requirements. 員工在五十五人及以下的公司將免除這些條件。
                      
<span class="vocabulary">**immune**</span> [ɪˈmju:n]
<span class="definition">adj. [名詞前不常用] ~ (from sth) 受保護；免除；豁免：</span>Members of the Bundestag are immune from prosecution for corruption. 德國聯邦議院的成員可免受貪腐罪的起訴。/ He seems to believe that the president is somehow immune from criticism. 他似乎相信總統出於某種原因可以免受批評。

<span class="vocabulary">**release**</span> [rɪˈli:s]
<span class="definition">vt. ~ sb (from sth) 免除某人的責任、職責、義務、任務：</span>The new law released employers from their obligation to recognize unions. 新的法律免除了僱主承認工會的義務。/ Divorce releases both the husband and wife from all marital obligations to each other. 離婚解除了夫妻雙方相互之間所有的婚姻義務。/ This releases the teacher to work with individuals who are having extreme difficulty. 這使得教師可以專門輔導學習特別吃力的學生。

<span class="vocabulary">**exemption**</span> [ɪgˈzempʃn]
<span class="definition">n. [U, C] ~ (from sth) 對待做事項或待支付事項的免除、豁免：</span>She was given exemption from the final examination. 她已獲准期末免試。/ There are parking restrictions with exemptions for disabled drivers. 停車有限制，但殘疾司機無須遵守。/ religious exemptions from statutes and administrative rules（尤美）不受議會立法和行政法規約束的宗教豁免 / medical exemption 醫療豁免 / antitrust exemption（美）反壟斷豁免 / The regulations provide an exemption from this law. 規定中有不受此法約束的一項豁免條文。/ The exemption which applies to home-buyers. 該項豁免適用於購房者。/ exemption clause 豁免條款
           
<span class="vocabulary">**immunity**</span> [ɪˈmju:nəti]
<span class="definition">n. [U, C] ~ (from sth) 受保護；豁免；免除：</span>diplomatic, legal, parliamentary, congressional 外交豁免權；法定豁免權；議會豁免權；國會豁免權 / Several ministers were stripped of parliamentary immunity as a prelude to facing corruption charges. 數名部長被剝奪了議會豁免權，這還只是對其提起腐敗指控的前奏。/ The Supreme Court lifted the company's immunity from criminal prosecution. 最高法院撤銷了該公司的刑事起訴豁免權。/ He has agreed to waive his diplomatic immunity and face prosecution.他已經同意放棄外交豁免權並接受刑事起訴。/ The spies were all granted immunity from prosecution. 這些間諜都獲得免予公訴。/ Unions were granted immunity from prosecution for non-violent acts. 工會被賦予非暴力行動的起訴豁免權。/ Officials of all member states receive certain privileges and immunities. 各成員國的官員均享有某些特權和豁免權。

相關詞義延伸：
· exemption還可指對部分收入實行的免稅；
· forgive還可指（一家銀行、一個國家等）免除對方的債務；
· pardon還可指由於自己未聽到或未理解而請求某人重複某事，即請再說一遍；
· excess可指保險中的免賠額（即需要自己支付的那部分）。

☀ <span class="category">**仁慈、善良的形容詞：**</span>
<span class="vocabulary">**merciful**</span> ['mə:sɪfl] 
<span class="definition">adj. 寬恕別人且展現出善良的，即仁慈的，慈悲的。是此義的常規用詞：</span>merciful God 仁慈的上帝 / The court is merciful. 法庭做出寬大處理。

<span class="vocabulary">**kind**</span> [kaɪnd] 
<span class="definition">adj. 1 kind (to sb/sth) / kind (of sb) (to do sth) 關心別人的；溫柔的，友好的，慷慨的。是此義的常規用詞：</span>kind heart, kind face 仁慈的心；友好的面容 / kind action, kind gesture, kind comment 友好的行為；友好的姿態；友好的評論 / kind invitation 盛情邀請 / kind to animals 愛護動物 / It was really kind of you to help me. 你幫我的忙，我太感激了。/ Soft water is kinder to your hair.（比喻用法）軟性水質不易損傷頭髮。/ The weather was very kind to us.（比喻用法）天氣非常宜人。/ My boss has been extremely kind to me. 我的老闆對我非常好。<span class="definition">2 [正式] 用於做出禮貌的請求或發出命令：</span>Would you be kind enough to close the window? 請把窗子關上好嗎？

<span class="vocabulary">**good**</span> [ɡʊd] 
<span class="definition">adj. good (to sb) / good (of sb) (to do sth) 願意去幫助別人的、對其他人表現出善心的：</span>He was very good to me when I was ill. 我生病時他對我關懷備至。/ It was very good of you to come. 你能來真是太好了。/ I had to take a week off work but my colleagues were very good about it. 我得請一週的假，同事們對此非常諒解。

<span class="vocabulary">**nice**</span> [naɪs] 
<span class="definition">adj. nice (to sb) / nice of sb (to do sth) / nice (about sth) 善良的，好心的；友好的：</span>Our new neighbours are very nice. 我們的新鄰居很和氣。/ He’s a really nice guy. 他真是個好人。/ It was nice of them to invite us. 他們真好，邀請了我們。
           
<span class="vocabulary">**kind-hearted**</span>
<span class="definition">adj. 仁慈的、善良的、好心的：</span>He was a warm, generous and kind-hearted man. 他是個熱情、大方、善良的人。

<span class="vocabulary">**beneficent**</span> [bɪˈnefɪsnt]
<span class="definition">adj. [正式] 有裨益的、行善的、慈善的：</span>optimism about the beneficent effects of new technology 對新技術會帶來有益成效的樂觀態度

<span class="vocabulary">**benevolent**</span> [bəˈnevələnt]
<span class="definition">adj. 1 [正式]（尤指當權者）慈善的、仁慈的：</span>benevolent smile, benevolent attitude 和藹的笑容；和藹的態度 / belief in the existence of a benevolent god 對於仁慈的神之存在的篤信 / They believe that the country needs a benevolent dictator. 他們認為國家需要一位仁愛的獨裁者。/ The company has proved to be a most benevolent employer. 事實證明，這家公司是非常好的僱主。/ The boss smiled at us all like a benevolent uncle. 老闆朝我們笑了笑，像個慈祥的叔叔。<span class="definition">2 用於慈善機構的名稱中：</span>the RAF Benevolent Fund 英國皇家空軍慈善基金（組織）/ the Army Benevolent Fund 陸軍慈善基金
           
<span class="vocabulary">**benign**</span> [bɪˈnaɪn]
<span class="definition">adj.（人）善良的、不傷害任何人的：</span>They are normally a more benign audience. 他們通常觀看時比較溫和。/ Critics of the scheme take a less benign view. 對該方案的批評就沒有這麼溫和了。/ America and Japan have settled back into a policy of benign neglect of their currencies. 美國和日本又回到了不作為貨幣政策的老路。
           
<span class="vocabulary">**clement**</span> [ˈklemənt]
<span class="definition">adj. [正式] 對受懲罰的人仁慈的、寬容的：</span>瞭解即可
           
<span class="vocabulary">**tolerant**</span> [ˈtɒlərənt; 美 ˈtɑ:l-]
<span class="definition">adj. ~ (of/towards sb/sth) 能容忍別人的言行的（即使你不同意對方），如寬容的、包容的：</span>He has a very tolerant attitude towards other religions. 他對其他宗教持非常包容的態度。/ an area known to be religiously tolerant 以宗教寬容度高而聞名的地區 / They are more tolerant towards/toward minorities now. 他們現在對少數族群更加寬容。/ Other changes include more tolerant attitudes to unmarried couples having children. 其他變化包括對非婚夫婦生育更為寬容的態度。/ Mary was surprisingly tolerant of his annoying habits. 瑪麗對他惱人的習慣出奇地寬容。/ He's relatively tolerant of my faults. 他比較能寬容我的過失。/ They learn to be tolerant of other people.他們學著寬容別人。

<span class="vocabulary">**lovely**</span> ['lʌvlɪ] 
<span class="definition">adj. [尤英，非正式]（人）非常善良、慷慨而友好的：</span>Her mother was a lovely woman. 她母親是個心地善良的女人。
           
<span class="vocabulary">**gracious**</span> [ˈgreɪʃəs]
<span class="definition">adj. 1（人或行為）尤指對地位較低者（如下級或晚輩）好心的、和藹的、慈祥的：</span>a gracious lady 好心的女士 / a gracious smile 慈祥的微笑 <span class="definition">2 [英，非正式] [只用於名詞前] 對王室人員及其行為的禮貌用語，如仁慈的、寬厚的：</span>her gracious Majesty the Queen 仁慈的女王陛下 <span class="definition">3 ~ (to sb)（上帝）仁慈的、慈悲的：</span>a gracious act of God 上帝的慈悲

<span class="vocabulary">**charitable**</span> [ˈtʃærətəbl]
<span class="definition">adj. 1 慈善團體的、慈善事業的：</span>charitable institution, charitable foundation, charitable trust 慈善機構；慈善基金會；慈善信託 / charitable donation, charitable gift 慈善捐贈；慈善贈品 / to have charitable status (= to be an official charity)（英）具有慈善者身份（成為正式的慈善機構）<span class="definition">2 幫助窮人（或有需要之人）的，即行善的、佈施的：</span>charitable work for the handicapped 為殘疾人做的慈善工作 <span class="definition">3（尤指判斷別人時）待人寬厚的、仁慈的：</span>Let's be charitable and assume she just made a mistake. 咱們寬容些吧，就當她只是犯了個錯誤。/ They were rather less than charitable towards the referee. 他們對裁判根本不體諒。
           
<span class="vocabulary">**lenient**</span> [ˈli:niənt]
<span class="definition">adj.（懲罰某人或執法時）寬大的、寬容的、仁慈的：</span>lenient sentence, lenient fine 從寬的判刑；從寬的罰款 / The judge was far too lenient with him. 法官對他太寬容了。/ The appeal judge agreed that the original sentence was unduly lenient.（unduly為英式用法）上訴法官承認原判過輕。/ The police are sometimes more lenient with female offenders. 警方有時候對女性違法者比較寬大。/ He believes the government already is lenient with drug traffickers. 他認為政府對毒販已經很寬容了。/ Professor Oswald takes a sightly more lenient view. 奧斯瓦爾德教授的觀點稍微溫和些。

☀ <span class="category">**同情、憐憫的名詞：**</span>
<span class="vocabulary">**pity**</span> ['pɪtɪ] 
<span class="definition">n. [U] pity (for sb/sth) 由對方的苦難或麻煩所引起的一種同情心理，即同情，憐憫。是一個相對簡單的用詞，較為常用：</span>a look of pity 憐憫的表情 / I don’t want your pity. 我用不著你可憐。/ I threw the child some money out of pity. 出於憐憫，我扔給那孩子一些錢。/ I took pity on him and allowed him to stay. 我同情他，允許他留了下來。
           
<span class="vocabulary">**self-pity**</span>
<span class="definition">n. [U] [常貶]（尤指不必要或過度誇張的）自憐：</span>She's not someone who likes to wallow in self-pity. 她不是那種喜歡自憐的人。/ I was unable to shake off my self-pity. 我無法擺脫自憐自哀的情緒。/ Throughout, he showed no trace of self-pity. 他從始至終都沒有表現出一絲的自憐。/ He felt a sudden wave of self-pity. 他突然生出一陣自憐之情。/ to be full of self-pity 充滿自憐 / tears of self-pity 自憐的眼淚

<span class="vocabulary">**sympathy**</span> ['sɪmpəθɪ] 
<span class="definition">n. [U, C, usually pl.] 對某人的同情（表明你理解並關心對方所面臨的問題）。是此義的常規用詞：</span>I have no sympathy for Jane, it’s all her own fault. 我不同情簡，那都是她自己的錯。/ Our heartfelt sympathy goes out to the victims of the war. 我們對戰爭的受害者表示由衷的同情。/ She expressed her deepest sympathy for him. 她向他表達了最深切的同情。/ to waste sympathy 浪費同情心 / I don’t want your sympathy! 我不想要你的同情！ 
           
<span class="vocabulary">**compassion**</span> [kəmˈpæʃn]
<span class="definition">n. [U] ~ (for sb) 同情、憐憫：</span>deep, great compassion 深深的同情；深切的憐憫 / genuine, true compassion 真心的同情 / human compassion 人的惻隱之心 / He had compassion for my weakness. 他憐憫我的軟弱。/ In cities where many people beg, citizens quickly develop compassion fatigue. 在有許多人乞討的城市裡，市民們很快患上了同情心疲勞症。/ He was filled with overwhelming compassion for his wife. 他對妻子充滿了憐愛。/ I felt no compassion towards her. 我對她沒有任何憐憫。/ The old people are treated with great compassion. 老人們得到了深切的同情。/ love and compassion 愛憐

<span class="vocabulary">**empathy**</span> [ˈempəθi]
<span class="definition">n. [U] ~ (with sb/sth) / ~ (for sb/sth) / ~ (between A and B) 理解別人的情感、經歷等的能力，即共情：</span>the writer's imaginative empathy with his subject 作者把想象中的感情投入到筆下的人物 / empathy for other people's situations 對他人所處境況的同情 / Having begun my life in a children's home I have great empathy with the little ones. 由於從小生活在兒童院，我對小傢伙們產生了強烈的共鳴。/ The nurse should try to develop empathy between herself and the patient. 護士應當努力與病人建立心理上的溝通。/ I felt real empathy for my mother and what she had been through. 我對母親和她所經歷的一切都深有同感。/ She had a deep empathy with animals. 她對動物有濃濃的愛心。/ a feeling of empathy 同情心 / a lack of empathy 缺乏同情心

<span class="vocabulary">**commiseration**</span> [kəˌmɪzəˈreɪʃn]

<span class="vocabulary">**feeling**</span> ['fi:lɪŋ] 
<span class="definition">n. [U, pl.] feeling (for sb/sth)（對某人或某事物的）同情：</span>You have no feeling for the sufferings of others. 你對別人的痛苦無動於衷。/ What I love about this book is its genuine feeling for people. 我喜歡這本書是因為它對人懷有真情實感。

<span class="vocabulary">**poor**</span> [pʊə]

<span class="vocabulary">**sympathetic**</span> [ˌsɪmpəˈθetɪk]
<span class="definition">adj. ~ (to/towards sb)（對受傷者或悲傷者）有同情心的、表示同情的：</span>I found the doctors very sympathetic. 我發覺醫生都很有同情心。/ Her aggressive attitude makes me less sympathetic to her plight. 她兇惡的態度使我不再那麼同情她的困境。/ The character's faults actually make him more sympathetic. 這個人物的缺點實則讓他更具同情心。/ a patient and deeply sympathetic man 一位有耐心且很有同情心的人 / He'd written a not entirely sympathetic article in the 'Globe'. 他在《環球》雜誌上發表了一篇不太有同情心的文章。/ Her attitude to my situation was less than sympathetic. 她對我的處境並不同情。/ My boss is being very sympathetic about my problems.我的老闆對我遇到的問題表示非常體諒。/ a sympathetic listener 體恤別人的聽者 / I did not feel at all sympathetic towards Kate. 我對凱特一點也不同情。/ I'm here if you need a sympathetic ear (= sb to talk to about your problems). 要是你想訴訴苦，那就跟我說吧。/ She was very sympathetic to the problems of adult students. 她對成年學生的問題深表同情。/ It may be that he sees you only as a sympathetic friend. 也許他只是把你當作一個可以傾訴的朋友。
           
<span class="vocabulary">**compassionate**</span> [kəmˈpæʃənət]
