☀ <span class="category">**承諾、發誓、許諾的動詞：**</span>
<span class="vocabulary">**promise**</span> ['prɒmɪs] 
<span class="definition">vt.＆vi. promise sth (to sb) / promise sb sth 告訴某人你將明確去做或不去做某事，或告訴其某事一定會發生，即承諾，許諾，保證。是“承諾”的常規用詞，最為常用：</span>‘Promise not to tell anyone!’ ‘I promise.’ “你要保證不告訴別人！”“我保證。” / The government has promised a full investigation into the disaster. 政府已承諾對這次災難進行全面調查。/ I can’t promise anything. 我不能保證什麼。/ I’ve promised myself some fun when the exams are over. 等考試完了，我打算好好玩兒玩兒。/ She promised faithfully that she would come. 她信誓旦旦地答應會來。

<span class="vocabulary">**swear**</span> [sweə] 
<span class="definition">vt.＆vi. 1 發誓，相當於vow。是此義的簡單用詞：</span>I swear (that) I’ll never leave you. 我保證決不離開你。/ I swear to God I had nothing to do with it. 我可以對天發誓，這跟我一點關係也沒有。/ to swear up and down（美）信誓旦旦 / Do you swear on your life? 你以你的性命發誓嗎？/ Lincoln was sworn into office on March 4, 1861. 林肯於1861年3月4日宣誓就職。<span class="definition">2 swear (on sth)（尤指在法庭上）做出一個公開或正式的承諾：</span>Witnesses were required to swear on the Bible. 證人須手按《聖經》宣誓。/ Are you willing to stand up in court and swear that you don’t recognize him? 你願出庭起誓說你不認識他嗎？/ Barons had to swear an oath of allegiance to the king. 貴族須宣誓效忠國王。<span class="definition">vt. swear sb to secrecy/silence 向某人保證（不把某事告訴任何人）：</span>Everyone was sworn to secrecy about what had happened. 關於已發生的事，每個人都依照要求發誓不外傳。
           
<span class="vocabulary">**vow**</span> [vaʊ]
<span class="definition">vt.&vi. 發誓：</span>She vowed never to speak to him again. 她發誓再也不和他說話了。/ He vowed (that) he had not hurt her. 他起誓他沒有傷害過她。/ He silently vowed vengeance on them all. 他暗暗發誓要報復他們所有的人。/ to vow revenge against sb, to vow revenge on sb 發誓向某人報仇 / She vowed to herself that she would not show any emotion. 她暗自發誓一定要不動聲色。/ I vowed never to drink so much again. 我發誓再也不喝這麼多酒了。/ They vowed eternal friendship. 他們立誓要友誼長存。/ While many models vow to go back to college, few do. 很多模特發誓要重回大學校園，但很少有人做到。/ I solemnly vowed that someday I would return to live in Europe. 我鄭重發誓，總有一天我將回到歐洲生活。

<span class="vocabulary">**commit**</span> [kə'mɪt] 
<span class="definition">vt. 1 commit sb/yourself (to sth/to doing sth) 常用被動式，指真誠地許諾一定會去做某事，保證遵從協議或安排等。程度通常較深：</span>He was committed to increasing the investment. 他曾承諾增加投資。/ All sides committed themselves to cease fire in this region. 各方均承諾在該地區停火。<span class="definition">2 commit yourself (to sth) 公開發表意見或做出決定，以致日後難以更改：</span>He had committed himself at the meeting, though he wanted to change it. 他雖然曾想改變自己的意見，但畢竟已經在會議上公開做出了表態。

<span class="vocabulary">**undertake**</span> [͵ʌndə'teɪk] 
<span class="definition">vt.＆vi. [正式] 答應或許諾（你將做某事）。不太常用：</span>He undertook to finish the job by Friday. 他答應星期五或之前完成這一工作。
           
<span class="vocabulary">**pledge**</span> [pledʒ]
<span class="definition">vt.&vi. ~ sth (to sb/sth) 正式承諾給予…或做某事：</span>Japan has pledged $100 million in humanitarian aid. 日本已承諾提供一億元人道主義援助。/ The French President is pledging $150 million in French aid next year. 法國總統許諾為法國明年的援助經費撥款1.5億美元。/ The government has now pledged £170m over the next six years for improving primary care. 為了改善初級衛生保健工作，政府已承諾在未來六年內撥款1.7億英鎊。/ The government pledged their support for the plan. 政府保證支援這項計劃。/ Mr Dudley has pledged to give any award to charity. 達德利先生已承諾將任何所獲的損害賠償額都捐給慈善機構。/ Philip pledges support and offers to help in any way that he can. 菲利普保證給予支援，並主動提出盡其所能提供幫助。/ We all had to pledge allegiance to the flag (= state that we are loyal to our country). 我們都必須對國旗宣誓效忠。/ The group has pledged to continue campaigning. 這個組織發誓繼續投入運動。/ The group has pledged that they will continue campaigning. 這個組織發誓他們將繼續投入運動。

<span class="definition">vt. ~ sb/yourself (to sth) 使保證、使發誓（遵循或支援）：</span>They were all pledged to secrecy. 他們都宣誓保密。/ The government has pledged itself to root out corruption. 政府已承諾剷除腐敗。/ The President pledged himself to increase taxes for the rich but not the middle classes. 總統保證增加對富人而非中產階級的稅收。/ Tony Cottee has pledged himself to Everton Football Club for another three years. 託尼·科蒂已答應同埃弗頓足球俱樂部再續約3年。
