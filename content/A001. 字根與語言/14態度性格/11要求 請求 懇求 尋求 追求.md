☀ <span class="category">**要求的動詞和形容詞：**</span>
<span class="vocabulary">**ask**</span> [ɑːsk] 
<span class="definition">vt. 1 告訴某人你想要其去做某事（或你想要某事發生），即要求，請求。是“要求”的常規用詞：</span>We were asked to complete a questionnaire. 我們被要求填一份調查問卷。/ Eric asked me to marry him. 埃裡克求我嫁給他。<span class="definition">2 ask sth (of sb) 期望或要求（某事物）：</span>You’re asking too much of him. 你對他要求過分了。/ I know it’s asking a lot to expect them to win again. 我知道期望他們再次獲勝未免要求太高了。

<span class="vocabulary">**require**</span> [rɪ'kwaɪə] 
<span class="definition">vt. [正式] require sth (of sb) 使某人做某事或使某人擁有某東西（尤指因為根據某項法律或某些規則而具有必要性）：</span>What exactly is required of a receptionist? 接待員的職責到底是什麼？/ The wearing of seat belts is required by law. 法律規定必須系安全帶。/ required reading 指定讀物 / to reach the required standard 達到規定的標準 / to be required to take a short test 被要求參加一次小測驗

<span class="vocabulary">**request**</span> [rɪ'kwest] 
<span class="definition">vt. [正式] request sth (from sb)（正式或禮貌地）要求某東西（或某人去做某事）：</span>You can request a free copy of the leaflet. 你可以索要一份免費的宣傳單。/ You are requested not to smoke in the restaurant. 請不要在餐館裡吸菸。/ We have requested some information from the company. 我們已經要求公司提供一些資訊。

<span class="vocabulary">**demand**</span> [dɪ'mɑːnd] 
<span class="definition">vt.＆vi. 堅決要求，強烈要求：</span>She demanded an immediate explanation. 她強烈要求立即做出解釋。/ I demand to see the manager. 我堅決要求見經理。/ ‘Where are the keys?’ she demanded angrily.“鑰匙在哪兒？”她生氣地問道。/ The UN has demanded that all troops be withdrawn. 聯合國已要求撤出所有部隊。

<span class="vocabulary">**insist**</span> [ɪn'sɪst] 
<span class="definition">vt.＆vi. insist (on sth) 要求某事發生或要求某人同意某事，即堅持：</span>He insists that she come. 他執意要她來。/ He insists on speaking to you personally. 他堅持和您本人談。/ She loudly insisted that she had a right to stay. 她大聲堅稱自己有權留下。
           
<span class="vocabulary">**insistent**</span> [ɪnˈsɪstənt]
<span class="definition">adj. ~ (on sth/on doing sth) / ~ (that…) 堅決要求的；堅持的：</span>They were insistent on having a contract for the work. 他們堅持要就這項工作立一份合同。/ The men are very insistent that you leave right away. 他們堅決要求你立即離開。/ He is most insistent on this point. 他在這點上很堅持。/ He was insistent on a formal written agreement. 他堅持要求籤訂一份正式的書面協議。/ She was insistent about inviting him. 她堅持要邀請他。/ Why are you so insistent that we leave tonight? 你為什麼一定要我們今晚離開？/ She didn't want to go but her brother was insistent. 她不想去，但她哥哥非得要她去。

<span class="vocabulary">**claim**</span> [kleɪm]
<span class="definition">vt. 要求擁有、索取、認領某事物（因為索要者認為自己在法律上有權擁有此事物）：</span>A lot of lost property is never claimed. 許多失物從未被認領。/ He claimed political asylum. 他要求政治避難。/ Now they are returning to claim what was theirs. 如今他們回來索取本屬於他們的東西。/ An underground organisation has claimed responsibility for the bomb explosion. 一個地下組織已經聲稱對這起炸彈爆炸事件負責。/ He was too modest to claim the credit. 他太謙遜，不願意邀功。<span class="definition">vt.&vi. 向政府或公司索要金錢（因為索要者有權這樣做）：</span>He's not entitled to claim unemployment benefit. 他無權要求領取失業救濟金。/ She claimed damages from the company for the injury she had suffered. 她因受傷向公司要求獲得損害賠償金。/ You could have claimed the cost of the hotel room from your insurance. 你本可以從你的保險中索取旅館住房費。/ You can claim on your insurance for that coat you left on the train. 你可按你的保險索賠你遺忘在火車上的大衣。/ Some 25 per cent of the people who are entitled to claim State benefits do not do so. 有權申領政府補貼的人當中，約有25%沒有提出申請。/ John had taken out redundancy insurance but when he tried to claim, he was refused payment. 約翰辦理過失業保險，可是當他去索賠時卻遭拒付。/ The union claimed a pay rise worth four times the rate of inflation. 工會要求按照通貨膨脹率的4倍提高工資。/ You can claim back some of the cost of your treatment. 你可以追索部分治療費用。/ You might be entitled to claim compensation if you are injured at work. 如果受了工傷，你可能有權要求賠償。/ Can't you claim on your insurance? 你不能要求保險賠償嗎？
             
<span class="vocabulary">**stake**</span> [steɪk]
<span class="definition">vt. 提出權利要求、主張權利：</span>Jane is determined to stake her claim as an actress. 簡決心主張自己作為一名女演員的權利。/ Baguet's success staked his claim for a place in Belgium's world championship team. 巴古埃特的成功使他有資格成為比利時世界錦標賽參賽隊伍中的一員。         

<span class="vocabulary">**stipulate**</span> [ˈstɪpjuleɪt]
<span class="definition">vt. [正式] 明確地規定或要求（某事必須被執行或其必須以何種方式被執行）：</span>The constitution stipulated that a general election must be held within 120 days. 憲法規定必須在120天內舉行大選。/ A delivery date is stipulated in the contract. 合同中規定了交貨日期。/ International rules stipulate the number of foreign entrants. 國際規則規定了外國參賽者的人數。/ The job advertisement stipulates that the applicant must have three years' experience. 招聘廣告明確要求應聘者必須有三年工作經驗。/ She could have stipulated that she would pay when she collected the computer. 她本可以要求她來取電腦時付錢。
