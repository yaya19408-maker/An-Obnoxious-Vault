☀ <span class="category">**相信、信任的動詞：**</span>
<span class="vocabulary">**believe**</span> [bɪ'li:v] 
<span class="definition">vt. 相信（某事屬實或某人說的話屬實）。是此義的常規用詞：</span>I don’t believe you! 我不相信你的話！/ I couldn’t believe what I was hearing. 我無法相信自己所聽到的。/ Believe me, she’s not right for you. 相信我，她不適合你。/ He refused to believe (that) his son was involved in drugs. 他不願相信他的兒子沾染毒品。/ He firmly believed that he was right. 他堅信自己是對的。

<span class="vocabulary">**trust**</span> [trʌst] 
<span class="definition">vt. 1 對某人有信心，相信某人是善良的、真誠的、誠實的等，即信任，信賴。程度較深。與rely近似，但rely為不及物動詞：</span>the only person I truly trusted 我唯一真正信任的人 / I was afraid to trust anyone after that. 從那以後我不敢再信任任何人。/ I’d trust her with my life. 我願把生命託付給她。<span class="definition">2 相信（或認為）某事物是真實的或正確的，或可依賴的：</span>He trusted her judgement. 他相信她的判斷力。/ Don’t trust what the newspapers say! 別相信報紙上的話！

<span class="vocabulary">**accept**</span> [ək'sept] 
<span class="definition">vt. accept sth (as sth) 相信（某事屬實）。是其“接受”之義的一種擴大化用法：</span>I don’t accept his version of events. 我不相信他對事件的說法。/ These figures should not be accepted at face value. 不應該對這些數字信以為真。

<span class="vocabulary">**rely**</span> [rɪ'laɪ] 
<span class="definition">vi. rely on/upon sb/sth 信任或信賴（某人或某事物）：</span>You should rely on your own judgement. 你應該相信你自己的判斷。/ You can rely on me to keep your secret. 你可以相信我一定會為你保守秘密。/ Who can you rely on these days? 如今你可以相信誰呢？/ You can always rely on Holly to say the wrong thing. 霍莉總是說錯話，這一點兒不假。

<span class="vocabulary">**credit**</span> ['kredɪt] 
<span class="definition">vt. 英式用法，主要用於疑問句和否定句，相信某事，尤指相信令人驚奇或意外的事：</span>He’s been promoted--would you credit it? 他升職了，你相信嗎？/ I find what he says rather hard to credit. 我覺得他的話很難令人相信。

<span class="vocabulary">**buy**</span> [baɪ] 
<span class="definition">vt. 相信某事是真的，尤指相信不大可能的事，為非正式用法：</span>You could say you were ill but I don’t think they’d buy it (= accept the explanation). 你可以稱病，但我想他們不會相信的。

<span class="vocabulary">**swallow**</span> ['swɒləʊ] 
<span class="definition">vt. 相信…是真的，即信以為真：</span>I found her excuse very hard to swallow. 我覺得她的理由很難讓人相信。/ He told her a pack of lies, but she swallowed it whole. 他對她講了一堆謊話，可她全都信以為真。

<span class="vocabulary">**depend**</span> [dɪ'pend] 
<span class="definition">vi. depend on/upon sb/sth 確信某事將發生，相當於count on：</span>Depend upon it (= you can be sure) we won’t give up. 請相信，我們決不會放棄。

 <span class="vocabulary">**conviction**</span> [kənˈvɪkʃn]

☀ <span class="category">**可靠的、可信賴的：**</span>
<span class="vocabulary">**reliability**</span> [rɪˌlaɪəˈbɪləti]
<span class="definition">n. 1 [U]（人或事物）可靠：</span>The incident cast doubt on her motives and reliability. 這件事使人懷疑她有何動機，以及她是否可靠。/ Changes in design will ensure the quality and reliability of the printer. 設計上的改變將保證印表機的質量和可靠性。<span class="definition">2 [U]（資訊的正確性或真實性）可信：</span>The reliability of these results has been questioned. 這些結果的可信程度已受到質疑。/ It is important to assess the reliability of the data. 資料的可靠性評定是非常重要的。/ statistical reliability 統計的可靠性 / The reliability of the statistical estimates can be measured. 可以對統計估計的可靠性進行檢測。/ Many experts question the reliability of these figures. 許多專家質疑這些資料的可靠性。

<span class="vocabulary">**reliable**</span> [rɪ'laɪəbl] 
<span class="definition">adj. 1 可信賴（能把某事做好）的；可依靠的。相當於dependable：</span>We are looking for someone who is reliable and hard-working. 我們在物色可靠而又勤奮的人。/ a reliable friend 可信賴的朋友 / My car’s not as reliable as it used to be. 我的車不像過去那樣靠得住了。/ It has a highly reliable control system. 它有一套極可靠的控制系統。<span class="definition">2 很可能是正確或真實的，即真實可信的，可靠的：</span>Our information comes from a reliable source. 我們的訊息來源可靠。/ a reliable witness 可信的目擊證人 / reliable data 可靠的資料 / This statement is not reliable as evidence. 這一陳述作為證據是不可靠的。

<span class="vocabulary">**informed**</span> [ɪnˈfɔ:md; 美 ɪnˈfɔ:rmd]
<span class="definition">adj.（訊息來源）可靠的、有根據的：</span>According to informed sources, those taken into custody include at least one major-general. 據可靠訊息，那些被拘捕者當中至少有一位少將。/ I arm reliably informed that there are plans to close this school. 我得到可靠訊息，說有計劃要關閉這所學校。

<span class="vocabulary">**dependable**</span> [dɪˈpendəbl]
<span class="definition">adj.（做事時）可依賴的、可靠的：</span>He was a good friend, a dependable companion. 他是一個很好的朋友，一個可以信賴的夥伴。/ I find him very dependable. 我認為他很可靠。

<span class="vocabulary">**faithful**</span> [ˈfeɪθfl]
<span class="definition">adj. [只用於名詞前] 可信賴的、值得依賴的：</span>my faithful old car 我那可靠的老爺車
           
<span class="vocabulary">**credible**</span> [ˈkredəbl]
<span class="definition">adj. 可信的。相當於convincing：</span>credible explanation, credible witness 可信的解釋；可信的證人 / It is just not credible that she would cheat. 她會行騙簡直難以置信。/ Baroness Thatcher's claims seem credible to many. 撒切爾女男爵的主張在很多人看來是可信的。/ But in order to maintain a credible threat of intervention, we have to maintain a credible alliance. 然而，為了保持確實有效的干預威脅，我們必須維持可靠的聯盟。/ Im not sure that Ifind her story credible. 我不能確定她的敘述是否可信。/ completely（尤英）, fully（尤美）, quite完全可信；十分可信；相當可信 / You need imagination to make what you write fully credible. 要使你寫的東西完全可信需要具有想象力。/ barely（尤英）, hardly, scarcely（尤英）credible 幾乎不可信 / It seems hardly credible that anyone could have walked so far in a day. 一個人能在一天之內走那麼遠，令人難以相信。
                
<span class="vocabulary">**convincing**</span> [kənˈvɪnsɪŋ]
<span class="definition">adj. 令人信服的：</span>convincing argument, convincing explanation, convincing case 有說服力的論點；有說服力的解釋；有說服力的事例 / She sounded very convincing to me (= I believed what she said). 我覺得她的話很有說服力。/ a convincing victory 令人折服的勝利 / Scientists say there is no convincing evidence that power lines have anything to do with cancer. 科學家們說，並沒有確鑿的證據表明輸電線和癌症之間有什麼聯絡。/ I found his argument pretty convincing. 我認為他的論據相當有說服力。/ Details make your story more convincing. 細節描寫會讓你的故事更可信。/ He was far from convincing as a leader. 作為一個領導，他遠不能令人信服。/ She produced a convincing enough performance as the wronged wife. 她把那個被冤枉的妻子演得非常像。     

<span class="vocabulary">**compelling**</span> [kəmˈpelɪŋ]
<span class="definition">adj. 令人信服的、有說服力的：</span>There is no compelling reason to believe him. 沒有令人信服的理由讓人相信他。/ compelling evidence 有說服力的證據 / Factual and forensic evidence makes a suicide verdict the most compelling answer to the mystery of his death. 事實證據和法庭證據使得自殺成為解開他死因之謎的最令人信服的答案。/ My second and more compelling reason for going to Dearborn was to see the Henry Ford Museum. 我去迪爾伯恩的第二個原因，也是更令人信服的原因,是為了參觀亨利·福特博物館。/ There is no logically compelling argument to support their claims. 沒有任何邏輯上有說服力的證據來支援他們的說法。
           
<span class="vocabulary">**persuasive**</span> [pəˈsweɪsɪv; 美 pərˈs-]
<span class="definition">adj. 有說服力的、令人信服的：</span>persuasive arguments 令人信服的論點 / He can be very persuasive. 他有時很會說服人。/ What do you think were some of the more persuasive arguments on the other side? 你認為對方哪些觀點更有說服力？/ I can be very persuasive when I want to be. 如果我願意的話，我會非常有說服力的。/ Her arguments proved persuasive to the court judges. 對庭審法官而言，她的論點被證明很有說服力。/ What makes his case so persuasive? 是什麼使他的案子如此有說服力?/ His analysis is in many ways highly persuasive. 他的分析在許多方面都很有說服力。/ The evidence was not really persuasive enough. 證據並不是太有說服力。/ His arguments strike me as not entirely persuasive. 他的論點並沒有讓我覺得很有說服力。
           
<span class="vocabulary">**believable**</span> [bɪˈli:vəbl]

相關詞義延伸：
· confess還可指（尤指在羅馬天主教會）向上帝或神父懺悔；
· confession還可指（尤指羅馬天主教會）向神父作的告罪、告解（即把自己做過的壞事告訴神父）。

周邊詞彙補充：
· accept可指願意拿別人所給予的東西，即接受，如接受建議、邀請等。也可指承認你對某事負責，或接受針對某事的指責或責備，即承擔責任；
· acquiesce可指即使不認同，依然接受某事物而不爭辯，即默然接受、預設、默許。
