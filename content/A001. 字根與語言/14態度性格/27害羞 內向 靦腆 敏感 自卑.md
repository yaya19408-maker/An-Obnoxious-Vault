☀ <span class="category">**害羞、矜持、內向、敏感、自卑：**</span>
<span class="vocabulary">**shy**</span> [ʃaɪ] 
<span class="definition">adj. 1（人）見到其他人或與其他人講話時緊張或尷尬的，即靦腆的，羞怯的，相當於timid：</span>Please don’t be shy--I won’t eat you! 別那麼害羞，我又不會吃了你！/ He is a naturally shy, retiring man. 他天生靦腆，不愛交際。<span class="definition">2 表明某人是靦腆的或羞怯的，即顯得靦腆的，看上去羞怯的：</span>a shy smile 靦腆的微笑
                     
<span class="vocabulary">**shyness**</span> ['ʃaɪnəs]
<span class="definition">n. [U] 害羞；羞怯；膽怯：</span>natural shyness 天生的害羞 / He was suddenly overcome with shyness. 他突然害羞起來。/ She had to work very hard to overcome her shyness. 她極其害羞，得下大功夫才能克服。/ She completely forgot her shyness in her fascination with what he was saying. 他的話使她入了迷，完全忘記了羞怯。

<span class="vocabulary">**bashful**</span> [ˈbæʃfl]
<span class="definition">adj. 羞怯的、靦腆的、忸怩的：</span>He seemed bashful and awkward. 他看起來靦腆且侷促不安。/ In our culture we tend to be bashful about our talents and skills. 在我們的文化中，我們往往羞於談論自己的才幹和技能。

<span class="vocabulary">**coy**</span> [kɔɪ]
<span class="definition">adj.（尤指對愛情或性愛）羞答答的、假裝害羞無知的：</span>She gave me a coy smile. 她羞答答地對我笑了笑。/ She is modest without being coy. 她很端莊，絲毫不忸怩。/ I was sickened by the way Carol charmed all the men by turning coy. 卡蘿爾忸怩作態勾引男人的樣子讓我噁心。
           
<span class="vocabulary">**inhibited**</span> [ɪnˈhɪbɪtɪd]
<span class="definition">adj.（人）拘束的、拘謹的、不能放鬆自然地表達自己的感情的：</span>Boys are often more inhibited than girls about discussing their problems. 男孩子往往不如女孩子敢於談論自己的問題。/ We are rather inhibited about touching each other. 我們對身體接觸感覺很不自在。/ He was rather inhibited about discussing politics. 對於討論政治他相當不自在。/ She felt very inhibited by her own lack of experience. 她因為自己缺乏經驗而覺得很拘謹。/ No one should feel inhibited from taking part in the show. 誰都不應該不好意思參加演出。
           
<span class="vocabulary">**inhibition**</span> [ˌɪnhɪˈbɪʃn; ˌɪnɪˈb-]
<span class="definition">n. [C, U] 一種害羞或緊張、不能自然表達自己想法或感情的感覺，如拘束感、拘謹感：</span>The children were shy at first, but soon lost their inhibitions. 孩子們起初很羞澀，但很快就放開了。/ The whole point about dancing is to stop thinking and lose all your inhibitions. 跳舞講求的就是停止思考，完全放開。/ She had no inhibitions about making her opinions known. 她敢於公開地談論自己的想法。/ They had no inhibitions about voicing their feelings. 他們在表達自己感受方面毫無顧忌。/ They behave with a total lack of inhibition. 他們表現得毫無顧忌。/ Young children will participate in a drama class without inhibition. 小孩子參加戲劇班往往毫不拘謹。

<span class="vocabulary">**reserve**</span> [rɪ'zə:v] 
<span class="definition">n. [U] 指一個人不輕易向其他人談論自己的想法、感情等的一種性格，即內向，矜持，與reticence近義：</span>She found it difficult to make friends because of her natural reserve. 她因天性矜持而很難交到朋友。
           
<span class="vocabulary">**introvert**</span> [ˈɪntrəvɜ:t; 美 -vɜ:rt]
<span class="definition">n. [C] 內向的人、不愛交際的人：</span>瞭解即可 <span class="definition">adj. 內向的、不愛交際的，相當於introverted：</span>The music students here are a very introvert lot. 這裡學音樂的學生都非常不愛交際。

<span class="vocabulary">**withdraw**</span> [wɪð'drɔ:] 
<span class="definition">vi. withdraw (from sth) (into sth) 變得更安靜，且花更少的時間與人交往：</span>She withdrew into her own world. 她把自己封閉在個人的世界裡。/ She’s beginning to withdraw into herself. 她開始變得不愛與人交往了。
           
<span class="vocabulary">**withdrawal**</span> [wɪðˈdrɔ:əl; wɪθˈd-] 
<span class="definition">n. [U] [心理學]（行為的）孤僻；自閉：</span>an inability to cope with emotional problems except by retreating into withdrawal 無法應對情感問題，只能消極避世

<span class="vocabulary">**quiet**</span> ['kwaɪət] 
<span class="definition">adj.（人）不願意說太多話的，即文靜的：</span>She was quiet and shy. 她文靜而又靦腆。

<span class="vocabulary">**silent**</span> ['saɪlənt] 
<span class="definition">adj. 只用於名詞前，（尤指男人）很少說話的，相當於quiet：</span>He’s the strong silent type. 他是那種強悍而沉默寡言的人。
                      
<span class="vocabulary">**reserved**</span> [rɪˈzɜ:vd; 美 rɪˈzɜ:rvd]
<span class="definition">adj.（人或其性格）內向的、矜持的、寡言少語的：</span>He was unemotional, quite quiet, and reserved. 他感情淡漠，沉默寡言，性格內斂。/ Even though I'm quite a reserved person, I like meeting people. 我雖然性格極為內斂，但喜歡和人接觸。/ She seems very reserved. 她似乎很矜持。/ his fairly reserved manner 他很拘謹的舉止

<span class="vocabulary">**reticent**</span> [ˈretɪsnt]
<span class="definition">adj. ~ (about sth) 不願與人交談的、寡言少語的：</span>She was shy and reticent. 她羞怯而寡言少語。/ Mrs. Smith, normally a reticent woman, took it upon herself to write to the President. 通常都很沉默的史密斯夫人決定自己給總統寫信。/ He was extremely reticent about his personal life. 他對自己的個人生活諱莫如深。/ She is so reticent about her achievements. 她對自己的成就緘口不言。           

<span class="vocabulary">**demure**</span> [dɪˈmjʊə(r); 美 dɪˈmjʊr]
<span class="definition">adj. 1（通常指年輕女子）嫻靜的、端莊的：</span>a demure young lady 嫻靜的年輕女士 / She's very demure and sweet. 她非常嫻靜可愛。<span class="definition">2（舉止、衣服等）顯得矜持、嫻靜、端莊的：</span>a demure smile 矜持的微笑 / The luscious Miss Wharton gave me a demure but knowing smile. 性感迷人的沃頓小姐對我羞澀地會心一笑。/ a demure navy blouse with a white collar 嚴肅莊重的白領海軍軍服上衣 / a demure high-necked white blouse 端莊的高領白襯衫

<span class="vocabulary">**private**</span> ['praɪvɪt] 
<span class="definition">adj. 常作前置定語，指人不願與別人分享想法或向他人吐露感情的，即內向的：</span>a private person 內向的人
           
<span class="vocabulary">**retiring**</span> [rɪˈtaɪərɪŋ]
<span class="definition">adj. 不愛交際的、孤僻的、離群索居的、害羞的：</span>a quiet, retiring man 話少而又靦腆的男子 / I'm still that shy, retiring little girl who was afraid to ask for sweets in the shop. 我仍然是那個羞怯孤僻，不敢在商店買糖果的小女孩。
           
<span class="vocabulary">**self-conscious**</span>
<span class="definition">adj. ~ (about sth) （因顧慮自己的外表或表現）侷促不安的，害羞的，不自然的：</span>He studied her in a way that made her very self-conscious. 他打量她的那種方式令她很不自在。He started to get self-conscious about his weight. 他開始對自己的體重敏感起來。/ I felt a bit self-conscious in my swimming costume. 我穿著游泳衣覺得有點害羞。/ He's always been self-conscious about being so short. 他老為自己身材矮小而覺得難為情。/ Bess was self-conscious about being shorter than her two friends. 貝絲因比她的兩個朋友個子都矮而感到很不自在。

<span class="vocabulary">**sensitive**</span> ['sensɪtɪv] 
<span class="definition">adj. 1 sensitive (about/to sth) 容易被冒犯或生氣的，即易怒的，神經過敏的：</span>You’re far too sensitive. 你也太敏感了。/ He’s very sensitive about his weight. 他很忌諱別人說他胖。/ She’s very sensitive to criticism. 她一聽批評就急。/ Teenagers are often very sensitive about their appearance. 青少年常常對自己的外表很在意。<span class="definition">2（資訊、話題）不得不謹慎對待的（因為其可能會冒犯別人或使別人生氣），即敏感的：</span>Health care is a sensitive issue. 醫療衛生是一個敏感問題。/ commercially sensitive information 商業上的敏感資訊 / culturally sensitive policies 文化上的敏感政策 / The information was deemed too sensitive to be broadcast. 這條訊息被認為過於敏感而不宜報道。
           
<span class="vocabulary">**hypersensitive**</span> [ˌhaɪpəˈsensətɪv; 美 -pərˈs-]
<span class="definition">adj. ~ (to sth) 很容易生氣的，即脾氣敏感的：</span>He's hypersensitive to any kind of criticism. 他對任何批評都受不了。/ Student teachers were hypersensitive to any criticism of their performance. 實習教師對任何針對他們表現的批評意見都異常敏感。           
           
<span class="vocabulary">**susceptible**</span> [səˈseptəbl]
<span class="definition">adj. 易受感情影響的，即好動感情的、感情豐富的、多愁善感的：</span>She was both charming and susceptible. 她迷人而多情。/ Hers was a susceptible nature. 她很情緒化。

<span class="vocabulary">**sensitivity**</span> [ˌsensəˈtɪvəti]
<span class="definition">n. 1 [U, C, usually pl.] 容易生氣、脾氣敏感：</span>He's a mixture of anger and sensitivity. 他氣量小又太敏感。/ She was blind to the feelings and sensitivities of other people. 她無視他人的情感和敏感之處。<span class="definition">2 [U] 資訊、話題、事件的敏感性：</span>Confidentiality is important because of the sensitivity of the information. 這情報很敏感，務必保密。/ This is a matter of great sensitivity. 這件事極為敏感。/ The producers were aware of the sensitivity of the subject. 製片人很清楚這個話題十分敏感。/ cultural, political sensitivity 文化敏感度；政治敏感度 / Collective living depends on a degree of sensitivity to what is happening around us. 集體生活取決於我們對周圍所發生事情的敏感程度。/ sensitivity training（尤美）敏感性訓練（旨在使人們尊重不同種族的人、殘疾人等）/ Sensitivity training for teacher is always useful. 對教師的敏感性訓練總是很有用。/ sensitivity about racial issues 對種族問題的敏感性 / She broke the news to us with great sensitivity. 她小心翼翼地把訊息告訴了我們。/ She is not known for her sensitivity in dealing with complaints. 她並不擅長處理投訴。/ There is deep sensitivity over the treatment of minority groups. 如何對待少數族群這個問題十分敏感。
           
<span class="vocabulary">**vulnerable**</span> [ˈvʌlnərəbl]
<span class="definition">adj. ~ (to sb/sth) 精神上脆弱的、易受傷害的：</span>emotionally, psychologically vulnerable 情感上脆弱；心理上脆弱 / She is very sensitive and rather vulnerable. 她非常敏感，而且相對脆弱。/ He smiled, making her suddenly feel oddly vulnerable. 他微微一笑，讓她突然感覺自己莫名其妙地脆弱起來。/ You must try not to appear vulnerable. 你千萬不要顯得軟弱好欺負。
           
<span class="vocabulary">**vulnerability**</span> [ˌvʌlnərəˈbɪləti]
<span class="definition">n. [U] ~ (of sb/sth) (to sth) 精神或情感上的脆弱性：</span>emotional vulnerability 情感上的脆弱 / the extreme vulnerability of old people to crime 老年人極易遭受犯罪行為之害

<span class="vocabulary">**brittle**</span> [ˈbrɪtl]
<span class="definition">adj.（情緒、心理、精神等）脆弱的：</span>a brittle temperament 脆弱的性情

<span class="vocabulary">**unsure**</span> [ˌʌnˈʃʊə(r); -ˈʃɔ:(r); 美 -ˈʃʊr]
<span class="definition">adj. [名詞前不可用] ~ (of yourself) 缺乏自信的：</span>Like many women, deep down she was unsure of herself. 和許多女性一樣，她心底裡缺乏自信。/ He made her feel hot, and awkward, and unsure of herself. 他令她內心躁動，手足無措，對自己沒有信心。/ The evening show was terrible, with hesitant unsure performances from all. 晚上的演出很糟，所有人都縮手縮腳，顯得很不自信。
        
<span class="vocabulary">**needy**</span> [ˈni:di]
<span class="definition">adj.（人）缺乏自信的；需要精神支援的：</span>emotionally needy 情感上需要支援的

<span class="vocabulary">**insecure**</span> [ˌɪnsɪˈkjʊə(r); 美 -ˈkjʊr]
<span class="definition">adj. 缺乏自信的；對自己和別人的關係沒有自信的：</span>He's very insecure about his appearance. 他對自己的長相沒有信心。/ Most mothers are insecure about their performance as mothers. 大多數母親對自己為人母的表現都不是很自信。/ She felt nervous and insecure. 她感到侷促不安。/ He felt a little insecure about being left alone. 只剩下他一個人，他覺得有些不安。/ In effect she is punishing her parents for making her feel threatened and insecure. 實質上她是在懲罰父母，因為他們讓她感覺受到威脅，沒有安全感。

<span class="vocabulary">**inadequacy**</span> [ɪnˈædɪkwəsi]
<span class="definition">n. [U]（對某種情況）缺乏信心：</span>his deep-seated sense of inadequacy 他內心那種根深蒂固的不自信

<span class="vocabulary">**inferior**</span> [ɪnˈfɪəriə(r); 美 -ˈfɪr-]
<span class="definition">adj. [正式] [常用於名詞前] 自卑的：</span>Her obvious popularity made me feel inferior. 她那麼受人歡迎，令我自愧不如。

<span class="vocabulary">**inferiority**</span> [ɪnˌfɪəriˈɒrəti; 美 -ˌfɪriˈɔ:r-; -ˈɑ:r-]
<span class="definition">n. [U] 自卑：</span>a sense/feeling of inferiority 自卑感 / inferiority complex 自卑情結 / He had an inferiority complex about his looks. 他對自己的相貌感到自卑。/ She accepted her inferiority to her rivals. 她接受了自己不如對手的現實。
