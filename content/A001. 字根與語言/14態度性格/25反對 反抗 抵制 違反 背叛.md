☀ <span class="category">**抵制、反對、反抗的動詞和形容詞：**</span>
<span class="vocabulary">**boycott**</span> ['bɒɪkɒt] 
<span class="definition">vt. 拒絕購買、使用或參加…以示抗議，即抵制：</span>to boycott foreign products 抵制外國產品

<span class="vocabulary">**black**</span> [blæk] 
<span class="definition">vt. [英] 拒絕處理貨物（或拒絕與某人做生意）以作為一種政治抗議，即抵制，相當於boycott：</span>The unions have blacked all imports from the country. 工會拒絕處理從該國進口的所有貨物。

<span class="vocabulary">**snub**</span> [snʌb]
<span class="definition">vt. 拒絕出席；拒不接受（例如作為一種抗議）。相當於boycott：</span>All the country's leading players snubbed the tournament. 全國的頂尖運動員都抵制那次比賽。/ They snubbed his invitation to a meeting of foreign ministers at the UN. 他們沒有理睬他發出的出席聯合國外交部長會議的邀請。           

<span class="vocabulary">**object**</span> [əb'dʒekt] 
<span class="definition">vi. object (to sb/sth) / object (to doing sth/to sb doing sth) 表達不同意、不贊成或反對…。與oppose含義極其相近，但oppose是及物動詞：</span>Many local people object to the building of the new airport. 許多當地的居民反對興建新機場。/ If nobody objects, we’ll postpone the meeting till next week. 如果沒有人反對，我們就把會議推遲到下週。<span class="definition">vt. 給出…作為反對的理由，相當於protest：</span>I objected on the grounds that it was unkind to the animals. 我反對的理由是這對動物來說太殘忍。/ He objected that the police had arrested him without sufficient evidence. 他抗辯說警方沒有充分的證據就逮捕了他。

<span class="vocabulary">**oppose**</span> [ə'pəʊz] 
<span class="definition">vt. 強烈不贊成某人的計劃、政策等，且嘗試將其改變或阻止其成功，即反對、阻撓、抵制（計劃、政策等）：</span>They would vigorously oppose such a policy. 他們強烈反對這一政策。/ The ban was initially opposed by the US. 這一禁令首先遭到美國的反對。
                      
<span class="vocabulary">**opposed**</span> [əˈpəʊzd; 美 əˈpoʊzd]
<span class="definition">adj. [名詞前不常用] ~ (to sth)（人）強烈反對某事的：</span>She remained bitterly opposed to the idea of moving abroad. 她仍然強烈反對移居國外。/ They are totally opposed to abortion. 他們完全反對墮胎。/ I am utterly opposed to any form of terrorism. 我堅決反對任何形式的恐怖主義。/ We are strongly opposed to the presence of America in this region. 我們強烈反對美國在該地區的存在。

<span class="vocabulary">**disapprove**</span> [ˌdɪsəˈpru:v]
<span class="definition">vi. ~ (of sb/sth) 不贊成、不同意、反對：</span>She wants to be an actress, but her parents disapprove. 她想當演員，可是她父母不同意。/ He strongly disapproved of the changes that had been made. 他強烈反對已進行的變革。/ Most people disapprove of such violent tactics. 多數人反對這種暴力的手段。/ Her mother disapproved of her working in a pub. 她母親不贊成她在酒吧工作。/ He strongly disapproved of the way his daughter was behaving. 他對女兒的行為方式十分不以為然。/ bigots who morally disapprove of homo-sexuality（美）一味在道德上道責同性戀的人 / socially disapproved behavior（美）被社會指責的行為

<span class="vocabulary">**cross**</span> [krɒs] 
<span class="definition">vt. 反對或反駁某人（或其計劃、意願等）。不常用：</span>He crossed me. 他和我作對。
           
<span class="vocabulary">**tackle**</span> [ˈtækl]
<span class="definition">vt. 對施用暴力或威脅手段的人進行制止或反擊，如抓獲、給以顏色：</span>He tackled a masked intruder at his home. 他在家裡抓住了一個私自闖入的蒙面人。/ He claims Pasolini overtook and tackled him, pushing him into the dirt. 他聲稱帕索利尼追上去打他，把他推倒在爛泥裡。

<span class="vocabulary">**resist**</span> [rɪ'zɪst] 
<span class="definition">vt.＆vi. 1 拒絕接受某事且嘗試阻止其發生，如抵制，阻擋，相當於oppose：</span>to resist pressure 頂住壓力 / The bank strongly resisted cutting interest rates. 銀行強烈反對降低利率。/ People naturally resist change. 人生來就抗拒變化。<span class="definition">2（遭到攻擊時）反擊，反抗；用力氣去阻止某事發生（如抵抗）：</span>He tried to pin me down, but I resisted. 他試圖制伏我，但我奮力反抗。/ to resist arrest 拒捕

<span class="vocabulary">**resistant**</span> [rɪˈzɪstənt]
<span class="definition">adj. ~ (to sth) 抵制或阻止某事的：</span>Elderly people are not always resistant to change. 上了年紀的人並不總是抵制變革。/ Some people are very resistant to the idea of exercise. 有些人十分反對鍛鍊。
           
<span class="vocabulary">**rebel**</span> [rɪˈbel]
<span class="definition">vi. ~ (against sb/sth) 反叛政府、體制、父母等：</span>More than forty Conservative MPs rebelled against the government and voted against the bill. 四十多個保守黨議員反對政府的主張，對法案投了反對票。/ MPs planning to rebel over the proposed welfare cuts 打算反對削減社會福利提議的國會議員 / He later rebelled against his strict religious upbringing. 他後來背叛了他所受的嚴格的宗教教育。/ Most teenagers find something to rebel against. 大多數青少年都有反抗意識。/ The child who rebels is unlikely to be overlooked. 叛逆的孩子不太容易被忽視。/ I was very young and rebelling against everything. 我當時非常年輕，對一切都表現出叛逆。
           
<span class="vocabulary">**revolt**</span> [rɪˈvəʊlt; 美 -ˈvoʊlt]
<span class="definition">vi. ~ (against sth) 反抗（上司或上級組織機構）：</span>The prime minister only reacted when three of his senior cabinet colleagues revolted and resigned in protest on Friday night. 直到週五晚上首相的三位高階內閣同僚倒戈並辭職以示抗議時，首相才有所反應。<span class="definition">vi. ~ (against sth)（行為）叛逆，拒絕服從（權威）：</span>Teenagers often revolt against parental discipline. 青少年常常不遵從父母的條條框框。/ Caroline revolted against her ballet training at sixteen. 卡羅琳16歲時拒絕接受芭蕾舞訓練。

<span class="vocabulary">**defy**</span> [dɪˈfaɪ]
<span class="definition">vt. 拒絕服從當權者、法律、規則等，即違抗：</span>Journalists were openly defying the authorities. 新聞記者公然藐視當局。/ He is willing to defy his own party. 他自願違抗自己的黨派。/ The protesters continued to defy a court injunction. 抗議者繼續藐視法庭的一項禁制令。/ to defy the law 藐視法律 / I wouldn't have dared to defy my teachers. 我可不敢不聽老師的話。/ Hundreds of people today defied the ban on political gatherings. 今天有數百人違抗禁止政治集會的規定。/ This was the first (and last) time that I dared to defy my mother. 這是我第一次(也是最後一次)膽敢反抗我的母親。/ Nearly eleven-thousand people have been arrested for defying the ban on street trading. 將近有1.1萬人因為違抗禁止在街上擺攤交易的禁令而被捕。
           
<span class="vocabulary">**insubordinate**</span> [ˌɪnsəˈbɔ:dɪnət; 美 -ˈbɔ:rd-]
<span class="definition">adj. [正式] 拒絕服從命令的、違抗上級的：</span>In industry, a worker who is grossly insubordinate is threatened with discharge. 在企業內部,拒不服從指令的工人會面臨遭解僱的威脅。          
          
<span class="vocabulary">**averse**</span> [əˈvɜ:s; 美 əˈvɜ:rs]
<span class="definition">adj. 1 [名詞前不可用] not ~ to sth /to doing sth 喜歡某事、想要做某事、不反對做某事：</span>I mentioned it to Kate and she wasn't averse to the idea. 我向凱特提起這個想法，她不反對。<span class="definition">2 [名詞前不可用] [正式] ~ to sth /to doing sth 不喜歡某事、不想做某事、反對做某事：</span>He was averse to any change. 他反對任何改變。/ He's not averse to publicity, of the right kind. 他不反對正當的宣傳。
           
<span class="vocabulary">**defiant**</span> [dɪˈfaɪənt]
<span class="definition">adj. 公然違抗的（有事帶有挑釁性）、蔑視的、不服的、挑戰的：</span>a defiant teenager 一個反叛的少年 / Despite the risk of suspension, he remained defiant. 儘管可能會被停職，他仍然桀驁不馴。/ Despite the criticisms, she remained defiant. 儘管受到批評，她仍然目空一切。/ Sylvia tossed back her dark hair in a gesture that was openly defiant. 西爾維婭把黑頭髮往後一甩，公然表示自己的不服。/ The players are in defiant mood as they prepare for tomorrow's game. 球員在備戰明天的比賽時帶著蔑視對手的情緒。/ The terrorists sent a defiant message to the government. 恐怖主義者向政府發出了挑戰書。

<span class="vocabulary">**react**</span> [rɪ'ækt] 
<span class="definition">vi. react against sb/sth 與…作對（尤指故意作對）；反抗。不常用：</span>Most young people react against traditional values. 很多年輕人反對傳統的價值觀。/ to react against his practice 反對他的做法

<span class="vocabulary">**hostile**</span> [ˈhɒstaɪl; 美 ˈhɑ:stl; -taɪl]
<span class="definition">adj. ~ (to sth) 對某事強烈反對的：</span>hostile to the idea of change 強烈反對變革 / Many people were openly hostile to the idea. 很多人公開反對這一想法。/ Many people felt he would be hostile to the idea of foreign intervention. 很多人覺得他會反對外國干預的主意。/ He was deeply hostile to the idea of psychotherapy. 他非常反對心理治療。/ The press became uniformly hostile to the new administration. 新聞界開始一致反對新一屆政府。

<span class="vocabulary">**objection**</span> [əbˈdʒekʃn]
<span class="definition">n. [U, C] ~ (to sth/to doing sth) / ~ (that…) 反對；異議：</span>I have no objection to him coming to stay. 我不反對他來小住。/ I'd like to come too, if you have no objection. 如果你不反對，我也想來。/ The main objection to the plan was that it would cost too much. 反對這個計劃的主要理由是費用過高。/ to raise an objection to sth 對…提出異議 / No objections were raised at the time. 當時沒人提出異議。/ I have no objection to banks making money. 我對銀行營利並不牴觸。/ I no longer have any objection to your going to see her. 我不會再因為你去見她而不高興。/ The proposal will go ahead despite strong objections from the public. 儘管公眾強烈反對，這項提案仍將付諸實施。/ Some managers have recently raised objection to the PFA handling these negotiations. 一些主教練最近就英格蘭職業足球運動員協會負責這些談判提出了異議。/ Despite objections by the White House, the Senate voted today to cut off aid. 參議院不顧白宮的反對於今天投票決定中止援助。
           
<span class="vocabulary">**rebellion**</span> [rɪˈbeljən]
<span class="definition">n. 1 [U, C] ~ (against sb/sth) 對（組織機構、政黨等的）權威的反對、反抗：</span>(a) back-bench rebellion 下院普通議員的反對 / There was a Labour rebellion when some left-wing MPs voted against the Chancellor's tax cuts. 工黨內部發生內訌，一些左翼國會議員對財政大臣提出的減稅政策投了反對票。/ They are in rebellion against the conservative hierarchy of the Church. 他們正在反抗教會保守的等級制度。<span class="definition">2 [U] ~ (against sb/sth) 對權威、行為規則等的叛逆、不順從：</span>adolescent, teenage, youth, youthful 青春期叛逆；少年反叛；青年叛逆 / rebellion against their parents 他們與父母對抗
                      
<span class="vocabulary">**revolt**</span> [rɪˈvəʊlt; 美 -ˈvoʊlt]
<span class="definition">n. [C, U]（對上司或上級組織機構的）反抗、拒絕服從：</span>The prime minister is facing a revolt by party activists over his refusal to hold a referendum. 因為拒絕進行公民投票，首相正面臨著黨內激進分子的反抗。/ Soon the entire armed forces were in open revolt. 很快，整個武裝部隊開始公然抗命。/ the biggest back-bench revolt this government has ever seen 本屆政府所遭遇到的規模最大的一次普通下院議員的反抗 / There was a widespread revolt against the party leadership. 該黨領導層遭到普遍反對。/ Parliament came out in open revolt against the president. 議會公開宣佈反對總統。

<span class="vocabulary">**resistance**</span> [rɪˈzɪstəns]
<span class="definition">n. 1 [U, sing.] ~ (to sb/sth)（對一個計劃、想法等的）反對、抵制、抗拒：</span>As with all new ideas it met with resistance. 和所有的新觀念一樣，它受到了抵制。/ There has been a lot of resistance to this new law. 這項新的法規已經遇到很多的抵制。/ Resistance to change has nearly destroyed the industry. 對變革的抵制幾乎毀了這個行業。/ The US wants big cuts in European agricultural export subsidies, but this is meeting resistance. 美國想大幅度削減歐洲農業出口補貼，但此舉遭到了抵制。/ They mounted stiff resistance to the proposal. 他們對此項提議發起了堅決的抵制。<span class="definition">2 [U, sing.] ~ (to sb/sth) 利用武力進行的反抗或抵抗：</span>armed, military resistance 武裝反抗；軍事抵抗 / non-violent, violent resistance 非暴力抵抗；暴力抵抗 / guerrilla, peasant resistance 游擊隊的抵抗；農民的反抗 / The advancing army met with little resistance. 向前推進的軍隊沒遇到什麼抵抗。/ resistance movement 抵抗運動 / resistance fighter, resistance forces, resistance group, resistance 抵抗運動戰士；抵抗力量；抵抗團體；抵抗領袖 / The attacks did not take place without resistance. 進攻並非沒有遇到抵抗。/ a pocket of resistance（= an area of resistance）零星抵抗 / The defenders put up a strong resistance. 保衛者進行了頑強的抵抗。/ The demonstrators offered little or no resistance to the police. 示威者幾乎沒有對警察進行任何反抗。/ The troops are encountering stiff resistance. 軍隊遭遇了頑強抵抗。/ Police in riot gear cleared the noisy demonstrators, who offered no resistance. 身穿防暴服的警察驅散了喧鬧的遊行者，沒有遇到抵抗。<span class="definition">3 [sing.]（常用the Resistance）（被他國軍隊佔領或被專制政府統治的國家中的）秘密抵抗組織、地下抵抗組織：</span>resistance fighters 秘密抵抗戰士 / They managed to escape after being arrested by the resistance. 他們被抵抗組織抓住後又設法逃脫了。

<span class="vocabulary">**counter**</span> ['kaʊntə] 
<span class="definition">n. [C] 常用單數，counter (to sb/sth) 指對意見、立場等的反對、反駁，為正式用法。不太常用：</span>an effective counter to the blandness of modern culture 對缺乏生氣的現代文化的有效反擊 / The employers’ association was seen as a counter to union power. 僱主協會被看作是工會權力的對頭。

<span class="vocabulary">**quarrel**</span> ['kwɒrəl] 
<span class="definition">n. [U] quarrel (with sb/sth) 尤用於否定句，抱怨（或不贊成）某人（或某事）的理由：</span>We have no quarrel with their plans. 我們沒有理由不贊成他們的計劃。

<span class="vocabulary">**stand**</span> [stænd] 
<span class="definition">n. [C] 常用單數：一個人對於自己（或自己的觀點）的努力捍衛：</span>We must make a stand against further job losses. 我們必須採取措施，防止進一步裁員。/ to take a public stand 採取公開的反對態度 / the rebels’ desperate last stand 反叛者最後的瘋狂抵抗 / moral, principled stand 道德上的抵制；原則性的抵制 

☀ <span class="category">**反對的其它詞性：**</span>
<span class="vocabulary">**against**</span> [ə'ɡenst, ə'ɡeɪnst] 
<span class="definition">prep. 反對；與…相反；不同意：</span>the fight against terrorism 反對恐怖主義的鬥爭 / We’re playing against the league champions next week. 下週我們要和聯賽冠軍隊比賽。/ We were rowing against the current. 我們划船逆流而上。/ That’s against the law. 那是違法的。/ She was forced to marry against her will. 她被迫違心地嫁了人。/ Are you for or against the death penalty? 你贊成還是反對死刑？/ She is against seeing him. 她不想見他。/ I’d advise you against doing that. 我勸你別做那事。

<span class="vocabulary">**with**</span> [wɪð]

<span class="vocabulary">**disapproving**</span> [ˌdɪsəˈpru:vɪŋ]
<span class="definition">adj.（對某人或某事）不贊成的；表示反對的；不以為然的：</span>disapproving glance, disapproving tone, disapproving look 不以為然的一瞥；不以為然的聲調；不以為然的樣子 / Janet gave him a disapproving look. 珍妮特不滿地看了他一眼。

<span class="vocabulary">**so**</span> [səʊ; sə] 
<span class="definition">adv. [非正式] 尤為兒童所使用，以反對對方（即對方說的不對，相反的才對）：</span>‘You’re not telling the truth, are you?’ ‘I am, so!’ “你說的不是實話，對不對？”“就是實話，就是！” 

<span class="vocabulary">**too**</span> [tu:] 
<span class="definition">adv. 用於強調不同意…：</span>瞭解即可

<span class="vocabulary">**come**</span> [kʌm] 
<span class="definition">exclamation. [過時]（表示不太贊同）嗨，得啦：</span>Come, come, Miss Jones, you know perfectly well what I mean. 得啦，得啦，瓊斯小姐，你完全知道我的意思。

相關詞義延伸：
· resist還可指阻止自己去擁有自己非常喜歡的東西（或阻止自己去做自己非常想要去做的事），即忍住，抵擋誘惑；
· reaction還可指對社會（或政治）發展（或變革）的反對、反動、阻礙；
· counter-attack可指（在戰爭、體育運動或爭論中）反攻、反擊。

周邊詞彙補充：
· rebel可指反政府的人、叛亂者。還可指（組織機構、政黨等內部的）反抗權威者、持不同意見者、反對派。亦可指不喜歡遵守規則或不接受正常的行為、著裝等方面的規範的人，即叛逆者、不守規矩者；
· tight可指不允許所提到的某種物質進入的，如不漏…的、不透…的、防…的；
· no可指一場正式辯論（如在議會的辯論）中投反對票的總人數。

☀ <span class="category">**違反、違背、背棄的動詞：**</span>
<span class="vocabulary">**break**</span> [breɪk] 
<span class="definition">vt. 背棄諾言：</span>to break a promise, to break your word 背棄承諾；食言 / to break an appointment 失約

<span class="vocabulary">**betray**</span> [bɪ'treɪ] 
<span class="definition">vt. 1 傷害信任你的人，使其失望，尤指對信任你的人不忠，即辜負，對…不忠：</span>She felt betrayed when she found out the truth about him. 她發現他的真實情況後，感到受了欺騙。/ to betray his trust 辜負他的信任 / He was offered money to betray his colleagues. 有人收買他出賣他的同事。<span class="definition">2 為了達成…或為了給自己獲取優勢、好處等而背叛自己的原則或信仰：</span>to betray his former socialist ideals 背棄他先前的社會主義理想
                      
<span class="vocabulary">**offend**</span> [əˈfend]
<span class="definition">vi. [正式] ~ (against sb/sth) 違背（道德、法律、規則、原則等）：</span>comments that offend against people's religious beliefs 有悖人民宗教信仰的評論 / This bill offends against good sense and against justice. 該法案有悖於理智和公正。/ In showing contempt for the heavyweight championship Douglas offended a stern code. 道格拉斯藐視重量級拳王稱號的行為違背了嚴格的準則。

<span class="vocabulary">**defect**</span> [ˈdi:fekt; dɪˈfekt]
<span class="definition">vi. ~ (from sth) (to sth) 背叛一個政黨、國家等（而投入敵方懷抱）：</span>He tried to defect to the West last year. 去年他試圖叛逃到西方。/ He defected from the party in the late 1970s. 他在20世紀70年代後期叛出了該黨。
           
<span class="vocabulary">**rebel**</span> [rɪˈbel]
<span class="definition">vi. ~ (against sb/sth) 背叛：</span>He later rebelled against his strict religious upbringing. 他後來背叛了他所受的嚴格的宗教教育。
           
<span class="vocabulary">**lapse**</span> [læps]
<span class="definition">vi. ~ (from sth) 背棄，放棄（宗教信仰）：</span>I lapsed in my 20s, returned to it, then lapsed again, while writing the life of historical Jesus. 我20多歲時曾經停止信教，後來一度又信，再後來撰寫歷史上耶穌的生平故事時又再脫教。/ She calls herself a lapsed Catholic. 她稱自己已不再信仰天主教。/ He lapsed from Judaism when he was a student. 他當學生時就放棄了猶太教。

<span class="vocabulary">**rat**</span> [ræt] 
<span class="definition">vi. [英，非正式] rat on sth 違反協議或背棄諾言：</span>to rat on the agreement 違反協議 / to rat on his promise 違揹他的誓言
           
<span class="vocabulary">**deviate**</span> [ˈdi:vieɪt]
<span class="definition">vi. ~ (from sth) 背離；違背：</span>He never deviated from his original plan. 他從未偏離自己最初的計劃。
