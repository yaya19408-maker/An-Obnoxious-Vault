☀ <span class="category">**可能、潛在的形容詞：**</span>
<span class="vocabulary">**possible**</span> ['pɒsəbl] 
<span class="definition">adj. 1 不常作前置定語，可能被做到（或取得）的。是一個常規用詞，含義最廣泛，也最常用：</span>It is possible to get there by bus. 可以乘公共汽車到那裡。/ This wouldn’t have been possible without you. 若沒有你，這事恐怕就辦不成了。/ New technology has made it possible to communicate more easily. 新技術使溝通更方便。<span class="definition">2 可能存在或發生的（但並不確定）：</span>a possible future president 未來可能當選總統的人 / the possible side effects of the drug 這種藥可能產生的副作用 / It’s theoretically possible but highly unlikely ever to happen. 這個從理論上來說是有可能的，但是基本上不會發生。<span class="definition">3 用於形容詞後，以強調某事物是其同類中最好的或最壞的等：</span>the best possible surprise 最大驚喜 / the worst possible time 最糟糕的時候 / the clearest possible way 最明確的方式 <span class="definition">4 儘可能（早、多等）的：</span>as far as possible, as long as possible 儘可能遠；儘可能長 / as quickly as possible, as soon as possible 儘快 / She did as much as possible to help him. 她儘可能幫助他。/ the last possible moment 最後一刻
           
<span class="vocabulary">**probable**</span> [ˈprɒbəbl; 美 ˈprɑ:b-]
<span class="definition">adj. 很可能發生（或存在、真實）的：</span>probable cause, probable explanation, probable outcome 可能的原因；可能的解釋；可能的結果 / The two outcomes are equally probable. 兩種結果都有可能。/ highly, most probable 極可能的；最可能的 / It is probable that the disease has a genetic element. 這種疾病很可能有遺傳因素。/ It is probable that the medication will suppress the symptom without treating the condition. 這種藥物有可能治標不治本。/ She does not want to be taken to an optician with the probable result of having to wear glasses. 她不想被帶去驗光，因為結果可能是她必須配戴眼鏡。/ The Socialists united behind their probable presidential candidate, Michel Rocard. 社會黨人團結在他們很有希望當選的總統候選人米歇爾·羅卡爾周圍。
           
<span class="vocabulary">**prospective**</span> [prəˈspektɪv]
<span class="definition">adj. [常用於名詞前] 被期望（或被預期）去做某事（或變成某事物）的，即有望的、可能的、潛在的：</span>The story should act as a warning to other prospective buyers. 這篇報道應該對其他潛在的購買者起到警示作用。/ When his prospective employers learned that he smoked, they said they wouldn't hire him. 本有可能僱用他的老闆們得知他吸菸後，說他們不會僱他。/ prospective candidate 潛在的候選人 / prospective visitor 可能的到訪者 / prospective investor 潛在的投資者 / prospective customer 潛在的顧客

<span class="vocabulary">**likely**</span> ['laɪklɪ] 
<span class="definition">adj. likely (to do sth) / likely (that ...) 可能的；可被預料（或期望）的。通常程度較深，且常作表語：</span>the most likely outcome 最可能的結果 / Tickets are likely to be expensive. 票可能很貴。/ It is highly likely that the factory will have to close. 這家工廠很可能要關閉。
                      
<span class="vocabulary">**liable**</span> [ˈlaɪəbl]
<span class="definition">adj. 1 [名詞前不常用] ~ to do sth 可能做某事的：</span>We're all liable to make mistakes when we're tired. 人在疲勞時都可能出差錯。/ The bridge is liable to collapse at any moment. 那座橋隨時可能坍塌。/ Only a small minority of the mentally ill are liable to harm themselves or others. 在精神病患者中，只有一小部分有可能會傷害自己或他人。<span class="definition">2 [名詞前不常用] ~ to sth 可能受某事物影響的：</span>You are more liable to injury if you exercise infrequently. 不經常運動就更容易受傷。/ She will grow into a woman particularly liable to depression. 她長大成人後會特別容易患抑鬱症。/ Steroids are used to reduce the inflammation, which makes the muscles of the airways liable to constriction. 類固醇用於消除可能導致氣道肌肉收縮的炎症。<span class="definition">3 [名詞前不常用] ~ to sth 可能受法律懲處的：</span>Offenders are liable to fines of up to $500. 違者可能被處以最多500元的罰款。
           
<span class="vocabulary">**prone**</span> [prəʊn; 美 proʊn]
<span class="definition">adj. 1 ~ to sth/to do sth 很有可能遭受…的；很有可能做壞事的：</span>prone to injury 容易受傷 / People with fair skin who sunburn easily are very prone to develop skin cancer. 皮膚白皙容易曬傷的人極易患皮膚癌。/ She seems very prone to chest infections. 她的胸腔似乎很容易受感染。/ people who are genetically prone to putting on weight 由於遺傳基因而容易發胖的人Working without a break makes you more prone to error. 連續工作不停歇使人更容易出錯。/ For all her experience, she was still prone to nerves. 儘管有經驗，她還是容易緊張。/ Tired drivers were found to be particularly prone to ignore warning signs. 據調查，疲勞駕車時特別容易忽視警示標誌。/ Sun removes the oil and wax. leaving the leather prone to cracking. 陽光曬掉了皮革上的油和蠟，使皮革容易破裂。/ The road is notoriously prone to fog. 眾所周知，這條公路以時常霧氣瀰漫而臭名遠揚。<span class="definition">2 -prone用於構成形容詞，指很有可能遭受…的；很有可能做壞事的：</span>error-prone 容易出錯的 / injury-prone 容易受傷的

<span class="vocabulary">**disposed**</span> [dɪˈspəʊzd; 美 dɪˈspoʊzd]
<span class="definition">adj. [正式] [名詞前不可用] ~ to/towards sb/sth（用於副詞後）（態度、看法）有…傾向的，即對…有好感（或惡感）：</span>She seems favourably disposed to the move. 她似乎對這一行動持贊同態度。/ I saw that the publishers were well disposed towards my book. 我看出出版商對我的書頗有好感。/ Every government is ill-disposed to the press, all or some of the time. 歷屆政府一直以來——或者說某些時候——都對新聞界冷面相待。/ People are naturally disposed towards/toward speculation. 人們天生喜歡猜測。

<span class="vocabulary">**susceptible**</span> [səˈseptəbl]
<span class="definition">adj. [正式] ~ (of sth) 容許某事的；可能…的；可以…的：</span>Is this situation not susceptible of improvement by legislation? 這種狀況有無可能透過立法加以改善？

<span class="vocabulary">**calculated**</span> [ˈkælkjuleɪtɪd]
<span class="definition">adj. ~ to 很可能的：</span>Such a statement was hardly calculated to deter future immigrants. 這樣的宣告不太可能嚇住將來的移民。

<span class="vocabulary">**hot**</span> [hɒt] 
<span class="definition">adj. 只作前置定語，在比賽或競爭中最有可能勝出的：</span>The city is the hot favourite to stage the football match. 這座城市最有可能贏得這場足球比賽的主辦權。/ He is the hot favourite for the position. 他是該職位的熱門人選。

<span class="vocabulary">**potential**</span> [pə'tenʃl] 
<span class="definition">adj. 只用於名詞前，指在未來可能發展成某事物的，或在未來可能得到發展的。即潛在的、可能的，相當於possible：</span>potential customers 潛在的客戶 / a potential prime minister 未來的首相 / potential problems 潛在的問題 / a potential source 潛在的來源
           
<span class="vocabulary">**feasible**</span> [ˈfi:zəbl]
<span class="definition">adj. 可行的、行得通的、做得到的：</span>feasible plan, feasible suggestion, feasible idea 可行的計劃；可行的建議；可行的想法 / It's just not feasible to manage the business on a part-time basis. 兼職管理業務是搞不好的。/ She questioned whether it was feasible to stimulate investment in these regions. 她對在這些地區刺激投資是否可行表示懷疑。/ That may be fine for the US, but it's not feasible for a mass European market. 那也許適合美國，但對於龐大的歐洲市場卻並不可行。/ The treatment has not proved feasible. 尚未證明該治療可行。/ It's perfectly feasible to produce electricity without creating pollution. 發電而不造成汙染完全可行。/ administratively, commercially, economically, financially, politically, technically, technologically（尤美） feasible 管理上可行；商業上可行；經濟上可行；財務上可行；政治上可行；技術上可行；工藝上可行 / A tunnel was not considered economically feasible. 用隧道在經濟上不可行。
           
<span class="vocabulary">**viable**</span> [ˈvaɪəbl]
<span class="definition">adj. 可實施的、切實可行的：</span>viable option, viable proposition 切實可行的選擇；切實可行的提議 / There is no viable alternative. 沒有其他可行的措施。/ None of the projects proved financially viable. 實踐證明，這些專案在財務上都不可行。/ It is only their investment that makes the project economically viable. 正是他們的投資才使這個專案在經濟上具有了可行性。/ a perfectly viable form of political organization 完全可行的政治組織模式 / commercially, politically viable 商業上可行；政治上可行 / Cash alone will not make Eastern Europe's banks viable. 僅靠現金不能使東歐的銀行維持下去。/ The goal has been to establish and sustain a nation of viable family farms. 目標是要建立一個能夠使家庭農場得以存續的國家，並使其維持下去。
           
<span class="vocabulary">**practicable**</span> [ˈpræktɪkəbl]
<span class="definition">adj. [正式]（任務、計劃或想法）切實可行的、行得通的：</span>at the earliest practicable opportunity 在儘可能早的時機 / as soon as (is) practicable 儘快 / The only practicable alternative is to postpone the meeting. 另外唯一可行的辦法就是推遲會期。/ Employers should provide a safe working environment, as far as is reasonably practicable. 只要條件許可，僱主就須提供安全的工作環境。/ It is not reasonably practicable to offer her the original job back. 讓她繼續做原來的工作在情理上行不通。/ Teachers can only be expected to do what is reasonable and practicable. 教師只應該做合情合理而且切實可行的事。/ We will do this as soon as is reasonably practicable. 一旦條件許可，我們就會採取行動。/ to reduce smoking as far as is reasonably practicable 儘可能或少吸菸 / The stadium shall undertake to ensure, so far as reasonably practicable, the safety of persons not in their employment. 該體育場要在合理可行的範圍內儘可能確保非僱傭人員的安全。/ The decision will be made at the earliest practicable date 將在儘可能早的日期作出這項決定。/ We aim to reduce, to the maximum extent practicable, the discharge of pollutants. 我們的目標是在最大可行的程度上減少汙染物的排放。/ The university should provide timely notification where practicable. 大學應該在可行的情況下及時給予通知。

<span class="vocabulary">**strong**</span> [strɒŋ] 
<span class="definition">adj.（發生或成真的可能性或機率）極大的，即很可能成功的，或很可能發生的：</span>There is a strong possibility that ... 極有可能… / a strong candidate for the job 有望獲得這份工作的人選 / We’re in a strong position to negotiate a deal. 我們很有希望透過談判達成協議。

<span class="vocabulary">**set**</span> [set] 
<span class="definition">adj. set for sth / set to do sth 有可能做某事的；某事有可能發生或將要發生的：</span>He was set to become one of the greatest tennis players of the world. 他有可能成為世界上最偉大的網球運動員之一。/ Prices look set to rise again. 看來價格還要上漲。/ He looks set for victory. 看樣子他能贏。

相關詞義延伸：
· likely還可指似乎適合於一個目的的；
· possible還可指（在某種情況下）合理的或可接受的。

周邊詞彙補充：
· practical可指切實可行的；
· fantastic可指不可能去付諸實施（或實踐）的，如無法實現的、不切實際的；
· last可用於強調…是最不可能、最不想要或最不合適的。還可指最不想要的事、最不可能的事；
· hopeful可指有希望的；
· promising可指表現出好（或成功）的跡象的，如有希望的、有前途的；
· latent可指存在但還不是非常明顯、活躍或成熟的，如潛伏的、隱藏的。

☀ <span class="category">**可能、潛在的副詞：**</span>
<span class="vocabulary">**perhaps**</span> [pə'hæps] 
<span class="definition">adv. 1 可能，大概，也許，相當於maybe。是一個常規用詞，含義最為廣泛，也最為常用：</span>‘Are you going to come?’ ‘Perhaps. I’ll see how I feel.’ “你來不來？”“也許來。要看我身體情況了。”/ Perhaps he’s forgotten. 也許是他忘掉了。/ In the end they lose millions, perhaps billions. 最終他們損失了數百萬，也許數億。<span class="definition">2 當說話人想做出一番不太確定的陳述（或發表一個不太確定的見解）時可用此詞，即也許，可能：</span>This is perhaps his best novel to date. 這也許是他迄今為止最好的小說。/ Perhaps the most important lesson to be learned is that you simply cannot please everyone. 或許應吸取的最重要的教訓是你不可能取悅每一個人。<span class="definition">3 用於做出一個粗略的估計，即或許，可能：</span>a change which could affect perhaps 20% of the population 一項可能影響20%的人口的改革 <span class="definition">4 表示不情願地同意（或接受），或不想強烈地表達自己對…的不贊同，即也許，大概：</span>‘You could do it yourself.’ ‘Yeah, perhaps.’ “你可以自己做。”“嗯，也許吧。” <span class="definition">5 用於提出一個禮貌的請求（或建議），或禮貌地主動給予（或提供）…：</span>Perhaps it would be better if you came back tomorrow. 如果你明天回來，也許更好。/ I think perhaps you’ve had enough to drink tonight. 我想今晚你已經喝得夠多了。/ Perhaps I may be permitted a few suggestions. 也許能容許我提幾條建議。

<span class="vocabulary">**maybe**</span> ['meɪbɪ] 
<span class="definition">adv. 1 用於對某事的將要發生、某事的屬實或數字的準確性表示不確定，如大概、也許、可能，相當於perhaps：</span>Maybe he’ll come, maybe he won’t. 他可能來，也可能不來。/ ‘Are you going to sell your house?’ ‘Maybe.’ “你要賣房子嗎？”“也許吧。” <span class="definition">2 用於提出一個建議，如也許、或許，相當於perhaps：</span>I thought maybe we could go together. 我覺得或許我們可以一起去。/ Maybe you should tell her. 也許你應該告訴她。<span class="definition">3 用於對某人表示贊同，並新增（應該得到考慮的）更多資訊，如也許、或許、可能，相當於perhaps：</span>‘You should stop work when you have the baby.’ ‘Maybe, but I can’t afford to.’ “有了孩子你就不應該工作了。”“或許是吧，可我歇不起呀。” <span class="definition">4 當你不確定是同意還是不同意（即不置可否）的時候，用於答覆一個問題或想法，如也許、或許，相當於perhaps：</span>‘I think he should resign.’ ‘Maybe.’ “我覺得他應該辭職。”“也許是吧。”

<span class="vocabulary">**probably**</span> ['prɒbəblɪ] 
<span class="definition">adv. 用於說明某事很可能發生或屬實。與likely在詞義上差別極小，但probably在用法上遵循一般的副詞語法規則，而likely常用於一些固定結構，如as likely as not、most likely或very likely：</span>You’re probably right. 你很可能是對的。/ As you probably know, I’m going to be changing jobs soon. 你或許知道，我不久就要換工作了。/ The two cases are most probably connected. 這兩樁案件極有可能互相關聯。

<span class="vocabulary">**likely**</span> ['laɪklɪ] 
<span class="definition">adv. 1 as likely as not / most likely / very likely 很可能：</span>As likely as not she’s forgotten all about it. 她很可能把這事忘得一乾二淨了。<span class="definition">2 [尤英，非正式] not likely! 用於對一個陳述、說法或建議表示堅決不同意：</span>Me? Join the army? Not likely! 我？參軍入伍？沒門兒！

<span class="vocabulary">**well**</span> [wel] 
<span class="definition">adv. can/could/may/might well 很可能：</span>You may well be right. 你很可能是對的。/ It may well be that it’s going to rain. 很可能要下雨。

<span class="vocabulary">**like**</span> [laɪk] 
<span class="definition">adv. 用於非正式的口語中，例如思考說下句話時用，也可用於解釋或舉例，可翻譯為大概、可能等：</span>It was, like, weird. 這事兒可能是有點怪。/ It was kind of scary, like. 這可能是有幾分可怕。/ It’s really hard. Like I have no time for my own work. 這事真費勁，可能我沒時間做自己的工作了。

<span class="vocabulary">**just**</span> [dӡʌst] 
<span class="definition">adv. could/might/may just 表示某事稍有可能（發生或是真的）。可能的程度一般較輕：</span>He might just be at school. 他也許在學校。

☀ <span class="category">**可能、潛在的名詞：**</span>
<span class="vocabulary">**probability**</span> [ˌprɒbəˈbɪləti; 美 ˌprɑ:b-]
<span class="definition">n. 1 [U, C]（某事發生的）可能性、或然性：</span>The probability is that prices will rise rapidly. 物價有可能會迅速上升。/ There seemed to be a high probability of success. 成功的可能性似乎很高。/ There's an excellent probability that unless action is quickly taken, pipes will freeze. 除非立即採取行動，否則管道很有可能凍住。/ If you've owned property for several years, the probability is that values have increased. 如果一處房產已經擁有多年，有可能它已經增值了。/ There is a high probability that it will snow tonight. 今晚很可能下雪。/ All callers had an equal probability of being chosen. 所有打來電話者都同樣有被選中的可能。/ We can say with a high degree of probability that the poem was written by Shakespeare. 我們認為這首詩很可能是莎士比亞寫的。/ This surgical procedure has a high probability of success. 這臺手術成功的把握很大。/ on a balance of probabilities（英）在分析各種可能性後 / On the balance of probabilities, the pilot had suffered a stroke just before the crash. 綜合分析各種可能性後，飛行員很有可能在墜機前突發中風。<span class="definition">2 [C] 很可能發生的事：</span>A fall in interest rates is a strong probability in the present economic climate. 在目前的經濟形勢下，降低利率大有可能。/ It now seems a probability rather than just a possibility. 這件事似乎十拿九穩，而不是僅有可能。<span class="definition">3 [sing.] in all ~ 很可能：</span>In all probability he failed to understand the consequences of his actions. 他很可能未瞭解到行動的後果。/ The Republicans had better get used to the fact that in all probability, they are going to lose. 共和黨人最好習慣他們很有可能失敗的事實。/ In all probability, the final upturn in their fortunes is some months away yet. 很可能再過數月他們才會轉運。/ In all probability she wouldn't come even if we invited her. 即使我們邀請她，她十有八九也不會來。          

<span class="vocabulary">**likelihood**</span> [ˈlaɪklihʊd]
<span class="definition">n. 1 [U] 可能、可能性：</span>The likelihood of infection is minimal. 感染的可能性極小。/ There didn't seem much likelihood of it happening. 這種事情發生的可能性不大。/ There is very little likelihood of that happening. 幾乎沒有發生那種事情的可能。/ There's every likelihood that she'll be able to help us. 她極有可能會幫助我們。/ Without a guide, there seemed little likelihood of reaching our goal. 如果沒有嚮導，要到達我們的目的地可能性似乎極小。/ Taking regular exercise reduces the likelihood of a heart attack. 經常鍛鍊身體可以降低心臟病發作的機率。/ This shouldn't affect the likelihood of you getting the job. 這不應該影響你獲得這份工作。/ Staff have to calculate continuously the likelihood of danger. 工作人員必須時刻估測危險發生的可能性。/ likelihood for success 成功的可能性 / Is there any likelihood of our getting our money back? 我們有可能要回錢嗎？/ In all likelihood, he'll be fit to play on Saturday. 他極可能恢復健康，參加星期六的比賽。/ In all likelihood (= very probably) the meeting will be cancelled. 這次會議十有八九要被取消。<span class="definition">2 [sing.] 可能的事：</span>The likelihood is that (= it is likely that) unemployment figures will continue to fall. 很有可能失業人數會繼續下降。/ But the likelihood is that people would be willing to pay if they were certain that their money was going to a good cause. 但如果人們確切知道自己的錢會用於做善事，他們就可能更願意掏腰包。/ That, as we all know, is not only a possibility but a likelihood. 眾所周知，那不僅可能發生，而且是很可能發生。
           
<span class="vocabulary">**prospect**</span> [ˈprɒspekt; 美 ˈprɑ:s-]
<span class="definition">n. [正式] ~ (sb) to/towards sth（使）（在思想或行為上）傾向於…：</span>I incline to the view that we should take no action at this stage. 我傾向於認為我們在這個階段不應採取行動。/ I incline to the view that he is right. 我傾向於認為他是正確的。/ the factors which incline us towards particular beliefs 使我們傾向於相信某些特定信仰的因素 / The government is more effective than we incline to think. 政府的效率比我們所慣常以為的要高。/ Lack of money inclines many young people towards crime. 缺錢使很多年輕人產生了犯罪傾向。/ His obvious sincerity inclined me to trust him. 他滿臉的真誠，讓我願意相信他。
                     
<span class="vocabulary">**tilt**</span> [tɪlt]
<span class="definition">vt.&vi.（使）傾向於（某個觀點或形勢）：</span>The conditions may tilt the balance in favour of the Kenyan runners. 這些條件可能對肯亞賽跑運動員有利。/ Popular opinion has tilted in favour of the socialists. 公眾輿論已倒向社會黨人一邊。/ When the political climate tilted towards fundamentalism he was threatened. 當政治氣候傾向於原教旨主義時，他受到了威脅。/ He continued to urge the Conservative Party to tilt rightwards. 他繼續敦促保守黨奉行右傾路線。<span class="definition">n. [sing.]（對某個觀點、形勢等的）傾向、偏向：</span>The chairman also criticised the plan for its tilt towards higher taxes rather than lower spending. 主席也批評該計劃傾向於主張高稅收而不是低消費。

<span class="vocabulary">**tend**</span> [tend] 
<span class="definition">vi. tend (to/towards sth) 朝向某個方向或常具有某種品質或特徵，即趨向：</span>His views tend towards the extreme. 他的觀點趨於偏激。/ Prices have tended downwards over recent years. 近年來物價趨於下降。/ I tend to think that... 我傾向於認為…

<span class="vocabulary">**orientation**</span> [ˌɔ:riənˈteɪʃn]
<span class="definition">n. [U, C] 一個人對於某個問題的基本信念、態度、觀點，可稱為取向、偏好：</span>religious, political orientation 宗教取向；政治取向 / a person's sexual orientation (= whether they are attracted to men, women or both) 某人的性取向

<span class="vocabulary">**trend**</span> [trend] 
<span class="definition">n. [C] trend (towards/in sth) 情況所改變或發展的一個大致方向，即趨勢；動態；動向：</span>economic, social, political trend 經濟趨勢；社會趨勢；政治趨勢 / current trends in language teaching 當前語言教學的趨勢 / fashion trend 時尚潮流 / cultural trend 文化的趨勢 / evolutionary, historical trend 演變的趨勢；歷史的趨勢 / market trend 市場趨勢 / a worrying trend 令人擔憂的趨勢

<span class="vocabulary">**direction**</span> [dɪ'rekʃn, daɪ-] 
<span class="definition">n. [C, U]（人、事物、事件發展的）趨勢；動向；發展方向：</span>a step in the right direction 朝正確方向邁出的一步 / I felt the company was headed in a positive direction. 我認為公司朝著積極的方向發展。/ This points to a new direction for cancer research. 這為癌症研究指明瞭新的方向。/ the future direction of capitalism 資本主義的未來發展方向 / policy, strategic direction 政策方向；戰略方向

<span class="vocabulary">**way**</span> [weɪ] 
<span class="definition">n. [C, usually sing.] which, this, that, etc. way 事件的走勢：</span>There is no indication which way the vote could go. 沒有跡象表明投票的結果會怎麼樣。/ He’s always gone his own way when it comes to design.（比喻用法）在設計方面，他總是走他自己的路。/ When we finished school, we all went our separate ways.（比喻用法）畢業之後，我們就各奔東西了。

<span class="vocabulary">**wind**</span> [wɪnd] 
<span class="definition">n. [C] 通訊記者常用wind指影響事件的某種趨勢或因素：</span>The winds of economic reform are blowing across the country. 經濟改革之風正吹遍全國。
                      
<span class="vocabulary">**bandwagon**</span> [ˈbændwægən]
<span class="definition">n. 1 [usually sing.] 越來越多的人開始參與的活動，如浪潮：</span>The World Cup bandwagon is starting to roll. 世界盃足球賽熱潮即將湧起。/ So what is really happening as the information bandwagon starts to roll? 當資訊大潮滾滾而來的時候究竟發生了些什麼呢？/ the environmental bandwagon 環保熱潮 / The Scottish Nationalist bandwagon is gathering pace.（尤英）蘇格蘭民族主義的勢頭越來越猛。The globalization bandwagon is gathering momentum.（尤英）全球化的勢頭日盛。/ There is now a bandwagon effect with more and more companies following the trend. 現在是大勢所趨，越來越多的公司跟風而動。<span class="definition">2 [usually sing.] [非正式，貶] on the bandwagon（尤指政客順應的）時髦，潮流：</span>In recent months many conservative politicians have jumped on the anti-immigrant bandwagon. 最近幾個月，很多保守政客都搭上了“反移民”這班車。/ Many farms are jumping on the bandwagon and advertising organically grown food. 很多農場正趕潮流宣傳有機食品。/ Competitors have jumped on the bandwagon and started building similar machines. 競爭對手群起仿效，紛紛開始生產類似的機器。
           
<span class="vocabulary">**tide**</span> [taɪd]
<span class="definition">n. [C, usually sing.] 某事（如歷史）的潮流；難以控制的壞事的潮流：</span>They talked of reversing the tide of history. 他們談到扭轉歷史潮流。/ There is anxiety about the rising tide of crime. 犯罪率日益增長令人憂慮。/ The tide of nationalism is still running high in a number of republics. 民族主義浪潮在許多共和國勢頭正盛。/ The tide of war swept back across their country. 戰爭的浪潮回過頭來席捲了全國。/ He didn't have the courage to swim against the political tide. 他沒有勇氣逆政治潮流而動。/ attempts to stem the tide of revolution 遏制革命潮流的企圖 / Seeing the tide was now running in his direction, he renewed his campaign for reform. 看到形勢朝對他有利的方向發展，他重新開始了改革運動。/ In the early 1990s there was a marked turn of the tide. 20世紀90年代初有一次明顯的潮流轉變。

<span class="vocabulary">**disposition**</span> [ˌdɪspəˈzɪʃn]
<span class="definition">n. [C, usually sing.] [正式] ~ to/towards sth / ~ to do sth 行為的傾向：</span>to have a disposition towards violence 有暴力傾向 / This has given him a disposition to consider our traditions critically. 這使他傾向以批判眼光看待我們的傳統。/ They show no disposition to improvise or to take risks. 他們不願意臨時湊合或冒險。
