☀ <span class="category">**教育 教學：**</span>
<span class="vocabulary">**educate**</span> ['edʒukeɪt] 
<span class="definition">vt. 1 常用被動式，指（在學校）教育：</span>He was educated at Stanford. 他是在斯坦福接受的教育。<span class="definition">2 educate sb (in/on sth) 教給某人…，或教其去做某事：</span>to educate the public to respect the environment 教育公眾愛護環境 

<span class="vocabulary">**education**</span> [͵edʒu'keɪʃn] 
<span class="definition">n. 1 [U, sing.] 基本且廣義上的教育。通常是宏觀上的、長時期的、針對個體或群體所進行的品德、技能等各方面素質的培養，包括學校教育和非學校教育，不過尤指學校教育：</span>compulsory education 義務教育 / formal education 正規教育 / basic education 基礎教育 / universal education 全民教育 / preschool education 學前教育 / elementary education 初等教育 / secondary education（尤英）中等教育 / higher education 繼續教育 / vocational education 職業教育 / education policy 教育政策 / education reform 教育改革 <span class="definition">2 [U, sing.] 某種型別的教育或訓練：</span>AIDS education 艾滋病知識教育 / sex education 性教育 / music education 音樂教育 / religious education 宗教教育 / military, teacher education 軍事教育；師範教育 / health education 健康教育 / education about danger on the roads 關於路上危險情況的教育 <span class="definition">3 [U] 教育機構或教育界人士：</span>the Education Department 教育部 / the people of education and culture 教育界和文化界人士 / education committee 教育委員會 / local education authority 當地教育部門 / education expert, education specialist 教育專家
           
<span class="vocabulary">**schooling**</span> [ˈsku:lɪŋ]
<span class="definition">n. [U] 在學校接受的教育：</span>He had very little schooling. 他沒上過幾天學。/ She received the best schooling the town could offer. 她接受了鎮上最好的學校教育。/ Many children are disadvantaged by poor schooling. 許多孩子由於沒受過良好的學校教育而處於劣勢。/ Compulsory schooling ends at sixteen. 義務教育至16歲為止。 / formal schooling 正規教育 / primary, secondary schooling（均尤英）初等教育；中等教育 / home, private 家庭教育;私立教育 / a voucher scheme to help poorer families pay for private schooling 幫助較貧窮家庭支付私立學校學費的代幣券計劃 / mainstream schooling 主流教育 / He has had no formal schooling. 他沒有受過正規的學校教育。/ He had little formal schooling. 他幾乎沒受過正規的學校教育。/ She continued with her schooling after a long period of illness. 長期病休之後，她又開始繼續學業。/ We will stay in this country, as we don't want her schooling to suffer. 因為不願影響她的學業，我們打算留在這個國家。/ children's development in the early years of schooling 孩子們在早期學校教育階段的發展

<span class="vocabulary">**teach**</span> [ti:tʃ] 
<span class="definition">vt.＆vi.（尤指在學校）給學生授課，教書。是educate的一種最常見的形式：</span>I teach them history. 我教他們歷史。/ I am not qualified to teach this subject. 我沒有資格教這門課。/ to teach managerial techniques 教授管理技巧 <span class="definition">vt. 1 教給某人如何去做某事（以使其能夠學會自己去做）：</span>He taught me how to ride a horse. 他教過我騎馬。/ I’m trying to teach my daughter to behave. 我努力教女兒舉止得體。/ Children are taught about the world around them. 孩子們接受認識周圍世界的教育。<span class="definition">2 使某人懂得某個道理，即教導：</span>That experience had taught him humility. 那次經歷教會了他做人要謙虛。<span class="definition">3 [非正式] 使某人嚐到苦頭而不再去做某事，使其引以為戒：</span>to teach you a lesson 給你一個教訓
           
<span class="vocabulary">**teaching**</span> [ˈti:tʃɪŋ]
<span class="definition">n. [U] 教學（工作）：</span>She wants to go into teaching (= make it a career). 她想從事教學工作。/ classroom teaching 課堂教學 / music teaching 音樂教學 / the teaching profession 教學職業 / The Government funds university teaching. 政府為高校教學提供資金。/ the teaching of English in schools 學校的英語教學 / teaching method, teaching strategy, teaching style 教學方法；教學策略；教學風格 / teaching objective 教學目標 / teaching ability, teaching skills 教學能力；教學技能 / teaching experience 教學經驗 / teaching career 教學生涯 / teaching job, teaching post 教學工作；教學崗位 / teaching staff 教職員 / teaching assistant, teaching fellow 助教 / teaching duties, teaching load 教學任務；教學工作量 / teaching hospital 教學醫院

<span class="vocabulary">**tuition**</span> [tjuˈɪʃn; 美 tu-]
<span class="definition">n. [U] [正式] [尤英] ~ (in sth) 教學，尤指對個人或小組的教學、講授：</span>individual, one-to-one, personal tuition 單獨教授；一對一授課 / She received private tuition in French. 她由私人教授法語。/ The courses will give the beginner personal tuition in all types of outdoor photography. 該課程將為初學者一對一地講授各種戶外攝影技巧。/ The foreign students receive free tuition and accommodation. 外國學生免學費和住宿費。/ advanced tuition 高水平的指導 / The students get expert tuition in small groups. 學生分小組接受專家的指導。/ language, music tuition 語言的講授；音樂的講授 / the controversy over university tuition fees關於大學學費的爭論 / She had become expert in Chinese cooking under the tuition of her aunt. 在姑媽的指導下，她已經是烹製中餐的能手。/ tuition for beginners 教授初學者 / extra tuition for the exams 考前補習 / He is receiving tuition from a well-known artist. 他正得到一位著名藝術家的指導。/ tuition in Italian 義大利語的講授

<span class="vocabulary">**private**</span> ['praɪvɪt] 
<span class="definition">adj. 常作前置定語，（功課）由教師等向一個人或一小組的人私人傳授的（教師等由此獲得經濟回報）：</span>to give private French lessons 向私人教授法語
           
<span class="vocabulary">**civilize**</span> [ˈsɪvəlaɪz]
<span class="definition">vt. 使（一個人或社會）更文明，如教化、開化：</span>The girls in a class tend to have a civilizing influence on the boys. 班上的女生往往能讓男生文雅起來。/ a comedy about a man who tries to civilize a woman—but she ends up civilizing him 一出有關一個男人試圖教化一個女人，結果反而被那個女人教化的喜劇 / It exerts a civilizing influence on mankind. 這產生了教化人類的影響。

<span class="vocabulary">**edify**</span> [ˈedɪfaɪ]
<span class="definition">vt.&vi. [正式] 透過交給某人知識來提高其思想或品格，如教化、教誨、啟迪：</span>瞭解即可
            
<span class="vocabulary">**nurture**</span> [ˈnɜ:tʃə(r); 美 ˈnɜ:rtʃ-]
<span class="definition">vt. [正式] 幫助某人或某事物發展或成功：</span>It's important to nurture a good working relationship. 培養良好的工作關係非常重要。<span class="definition">n. [U] [正式] 培養：</span>The human organism learns partly by nature, partly by nurture. 人的學習能力部分是天生的，部分是後天培養的。

<span class="vocabulary">**indoctrinate**</span> [ɪnˈdɒktrɪneɪt; 美 ɪnˈdɑ:k-]
<span class="definition">vt. [貶] 強行灌輸信仰、學說等（而不允許被灌輸者去想別的東西）：</span>They had been indoctrinated from an early age with their parents' beliefs. 他們從小就被滿腦子灌入了他們父母的信仰。/ They have been completely indoctrinated. 他們完全被洗腦了。/ I wouldn't say that she was trying to indoctrinate us. 我不認為她在竭力向我們灌輸一種信仰。

<span class="vocabulary">**plant**</span> [plɑːnt] 
<span class="definition">vt. plant sth (in sth)（尤指悄無聲息地）把思想、信念植根於某人心中：</span>to plant the seeds of freedom in sb’s mind 在某人的思想裡埋下自由的種子
           
<span class="vocabulary">**implant**</span> [ɪmˈplɑ:nt; 美 -ˈplænt]
<span class="definition">vt. ~ (sth) (in/into sth) 將一個思想、觀念、態度等深深地灌輸進一個人的頭腦中：</span>Prejudices can easily become implanted in the mind. 偏見容易在頭腦中紮根。/ The speech implanted a dangerous prejudice in their minds. 那場演講給他們灌輸了一種很危險的偏見。/ His father had implanted in him an ambition to obtain an education. 他的父親早就向他灌輸了要接受教育的志向。

<span class="vocabulary">**sow**</span> [səʊ] 
<span class="definition">vt. sow sth (in sth) 灌輸或散佈感情或思想（尤指灌輸或散佈引起麻煩的感情或思想）：</span>to sow doubts into his mind 在他的頭腦里布下了疑雲          
           
<span class="vocabulary">**breed**</span> [bri:d]
<span class="definition">vt. [常用被動式] ~ sth into sb 以某種方式教育某人；使某人養成…：</span>Fear of failure was bred into him at an early age. 他從小就養成了對失敗的恐懼。

<span class="vocabulary">**brainwash**</span> [ˈbreɪnwɒʃ; 美 -wɑ:ʃ; -wɔ:ʃ]
<span class="definition">vt. ~ sb (into doing sth)（例如透過多次重複或混淆視聽的方式）迫使某人接受一種思想或信仰，即給某人洗腦：</span>The group is accused of brainwashing its young members. 那個團體被指控對年輕成員洗腦。/ They brainwash people into giving up all their money. 他們向人們強行灌輸放棄所有錢財的思想。/ I'd been brainwashed into believing I was worthless. 我被洗了腦，認為自己一文不值。/ Women have been brainwashed into thinking that they must go out to work in order to fulfil themselves. 婦女們被反覆灌輸的思想是：她們必須出去工作，才能實現自己的價值。
                 
<span class="vocabulary">**nourish**</span> [ˈnʌrɪʃ; 美 ˈnɜ:rɪʃ]
<span class="definition">vt. [正式] 培養（才能、感情、信念等）：</span>By investing in education, we nourish the talents of our children. 我們透過教育投資，培養孩子們的才能。                
           
<span class="vocabulary">**upbringing**</span> [ˈʌpbrɪŋɪŋ]
<span class="definition">n. [sing, U]（對孩子的）教養、培養：</span>He was a Catholic by upbringing. 他因受家庭薰陶，從小就是個天主教徒。/ Martin's upbringing shaped his whole life. 馬丁的家庭教養影響了他的一生。/ Sam's mother said her son had a good upbringing and schooling. 薩姆的母親說她兒子家教好，受過良好教育。/ Mine was a conventional family upbringing. 我接受的是傳統家庭教育。/ religious upbringing宗教教育 / cultural upbringing 文化教育 / privileged upbringing 享有特權的教育 / He had a normal middle-class upbringing. 他接受的是正規的中產階級教育。/ They gave their children a strict Catholic upbringing. 他們讓孩子接受嚴格的天主教教育。/ Her upbringing had given her the social skills to cope with such situations. 她所受的教育使她具有處理這種情況的社交能力。/ Part of his upbringing had been not to question his elders. 他曾受過不能質疑長輩的教育。

<span class="vocabulary">**educated**</span> [ˈedʒukeɪtɪd]
<span class="definition">adj. 1 -educated可指受過…教育（或訓練）的；上過…學校（或大學）的：</span>privately educated children 受過私立教育的孩子 / a British-educated lawyer 受英國教育的律師 / He's a Princeton-educated Texan. 他是受過普林斯頓大學教育的得克薩斯人。/ the need for a broadly educated workforce 對普遍受過教育的勞動力的需要 / Less formally educated people can acquire professional competence. 接受的教育不是那麼正規的人可以學習專業技能。<span class="definition">2 受過良好教育（或訓練）的；有教養的：</span>the educated elite 受過良好教育的精英 / an educated and articulate person 受過良好教育且善於表達的人 / He spoke in an educated voice. 他說話很斯文。/ He is an educated, amiable and decent man. 他是個有教養、和藹可親的正人君子。/ She seemed intelligent and well educated. 她看起來聰穎且富有學識。

<span class="vocabulary">**formal**</span> [ˈfɔ:ml; 美 ˈfɔ:rml]
<span class="definition">adj.（教育或培訓）正規的，即在學校接受教育或培訓（而非透過自己的實際經驗來學習）的：</span>He has no formal teaching qualifications. 他沒有正規的教學資歷證明。/ Young children are beginning their formal education sometimes as early as four years old. 幼兒有時早在四歲時就開始接受正規教育。/ Although his formal education stopped after primary school, he was an avid reader. 儘管小學畢業後就再沒有受過正規的教育，他卻十分愛讀書。/ Leroy didn't have any formal dance training. 勒羅伊沒受過任何正規的舞蹈訓練。

相關詞義延伸：
· education還可指教會人某個道理的有趣經歷。
