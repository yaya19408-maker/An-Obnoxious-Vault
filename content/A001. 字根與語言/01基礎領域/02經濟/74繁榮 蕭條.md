☀ <span class="category">**繁榮 蕭條：**</span>
<span class="vocabulary">**strong**</span> [strɒŋ] 
<span class="definition">adj.（企業或某個行業）經營狀況良好的，景氣的：</span>This industry remained strong despite the recession. 儘管出現經濟衰退，但該行業仍然很景氣。/ The business is still going strong. 生意依然不錯。/ Sales were surprisingly strong. 銷量高得驚人。

<span class="vocabulary">**strength**</span> [streŋθ] 
<span class="definition">n. [U] 經濟或行業的增長，即走強：</span>the long-term competitive strength of the economy 該經濟體的有競爭力的長期走強

<span class="vocabulary">**boom**</span> [bu:m] 
<span class="definition">n. [C] boom (in sth) 在商業經濟領域裡，可指貿易和經濟活動的突然增長，即激增；也可指一段繁榮的時期：</span>consumer, credit, economic, investment boom 消費熱潮；信貸潮；經濟繁榮；投資熱潮 / stock-market boom 股市熱 / housing boom 房產熱 / baby boom 生育高峰 / Internet boom 網際網路熱潮 / boom period, boom time 繁榮期；興盛的一年 / boom town 新興城市 <span class="definition">vi. 指商業、經濟迅速發展、激增、繁榮昌盛：</span>The economy is booming. 經濟正欣欣向榮。/ Sales are booming. 銷售量正在激增。
                      
<span class="vocabulary">**prosperity**</span> [prɒˈsperəti; 美 prɑ:ˈs-]
<span class="definition">n. [U]（尤指經濟方面的）繁榮、興旺、昌盛：</span>The country is enjoying a period of peace and prosperity. 國家正值國泰民安、繁榮昌盛的時期。/ a new era of peace and prosperity 和平繁榮的新時代 / Japan's economic prosperity 日本的經濟繁榮 / material prosperity 物質的豐富 / national prosperity國家的繁榮 / personal prosperity 個人的富足 / an area with a level of prosperity higher than the national average 發達程度高於全國平均水平的地區 / The growth of tourism brought prosperity to the island. 旅遊業的發展給這座島帶來了繁榮。/ The island's prosperity depends on its fishing industry. 這個島的繁榮有賴於漁業。/ Our future prosperity depends on economic growth. 我們未來的繁榮昌盛依賴經濟的發展。

<span class="vocabulary">**buoyancy**</span> [ˈbɔɪən sɪ; 美 ˈbu:jən sɪ]
<span class="definition">n. [U] 經濟的繁榮：</span>The likelihood is that the slump will be followed by a period of buoyancy. 蕭條過後經濟有可能會出現一段時期的繁榮。          
           
<span class="vocabulary">**bubble**</span> [ˈbʌbl]
<span class="definition">n. [C] 很可能持續不長的好景或好運，是一種比喻意義上的“泡沫”：</span>At the time the telecoms bubble was at its height. 當時電信業的泡沫正處於極度膨脹時期。/ Everyone is hoping that these hi-tech companies will turn out to be the Microsofts of the future. At the moment they look more like the focus of a speculative bubble. 每個人都希望這些高科技公司能成為未來的微軟。目前它們看上去更像是投機泡沫的中心。/ When the development bubble burst, federal regulators started probing the balance sheets of the biggest banks. 房地產開發泡沫破滅後，聯邦監管機構開始調查審計各家大型銀行的資產負債表。
           
<span class="vocabulary">**inflate**</span> [ɪnˈfleɪt]
<span class="definition">vt.&vi.（使）漲價：</span>The principal effect of the demand for new houses was to inflate prices. 對新住宅需求的主要結果是促使價格上漲。/ Food prices are no longer inflating at the same rate as last year. 食物價格的上漲率已不再像去年那樣高了。/ Clothing prices have not inflated as much as automobiles. 服裝價格的漲幅不及汽車那麼高。/ The promotion of a big release can inflate a film's final cost. 大規模發行所需的廣告宣傳會增加一部電影的最終成本。

<span class="vocabulary">**inflation**</span> [ɪnˈfleɪʃn]
<span class="definition">n. [U] 通貨膨脹；通脹率：</span>the fight against rising inflation 對抗不斷升高的通貨膨脹 / to curb inflation 抑制通貨膨脹 / to reduce inflation 減少通貨膨脹 / a high rate of inflation 高通脹率 / rising unemployment and high inflation 不斷增長的失業人數與高通貨膨脹 / an inflation rate of 3% 通脹率3% Wage increases must be in line with inflation. 工資的增長必須與通貨膨脹率一致。/ Inflation is currently running at 3%. 當前的通貨膨脹率為3%。/ double-digit 兩位數的通貨膨脹率 / consumer-price, price, wage（尤英）inflation 消費物價上漲；物價上漲；工資上漲 / domestic inflation 國內通貨膨脹 / grade inflation（學生考試）分數貶值 / Wages are not keeping pace with inflation. 工資的上漲趕不上通貨膨脹的速度。/ Inflation is down to its lowest level in three years. 通貨膨脹率降到了三年來的最低水平。/ Inflation reached a monthly rate of 5%. 月通貨膨脹率達到了5%。/ Inflation is running at 4%. 目前通貨膨脹率達到4%。/ Inflation has slowed to 7%. 通貨膨脹率已放慢至7%。/ savings eroded by inflation 受通貨膨脹影響而貶值的儲蓄 / inflation rate 通貨膨脹率 / inflation expectations 通貨膨脹預期 / an increase in inflation to 3.5% 通貨膨脹率增至3.5%

<span class="vocabulary">**depression**</span> [dɪˈpreʃn]
<span class="definition">n. [C, U] 經濟的蕭條期、衰退期、不景氣：</span>The country was in the grip of (an) economic depression. 當時國家處於經濟蕭條期。/ the great Depression of the 1930s 20世紀30年代的經濟大蕭條 / The housing market has gone into depression. 房地產市場陷入了蕭條期。/ The depression seems to be deepening. 蕭條狀況開來正在加劇。
     
<span class="vocabulary">**depressed**</span> [dɪˈprest]
<span class="definition">adj.（一個地方或企業）經濟不景氣的、蕭條的：</span>an attempt to bring jobs to depressed areas 給經濟蕭條地區創造就業機會的努力 / legislation to encourage investment in depressed areas 鼓勵向經濟困難地區投資的法律法規 / The construction industry is no longer as depressed as it was. 建築業不再像以往那樣蕭條了。/ The shipping trade was severely depressed. 海運業非常不景氣。
    
<span class="vocabulary">**recession**</span> [rɪˈseʃn]
<span class="definition">n. [C, U] 一國的經濟衰退、經濟萎縮：</span>the impact of the current recession on manufacturing 時下經濟萎縮對製造業的影響 / The economy is in deep recession. 經濟正處於嚴重的衰退之中。/ The recession caused sales to drop off. 經濟不景氣使銷量下降。/ We should concentrate on sharply reducing interest rates to pull the economy out of recession. 我們應該著力大幅下調利率以使經濟走出衰退。/ policies to pull the country out of recession 引導國家走出經濟萎縮的政策 / It was the worst recession since the war. 這是戰爭以來最蕭條的時期。/ economic, industrial recession 經濟蕭條；工業蕭條 / A rise in interest rates plunged Britain deeper into recession. 利率的提高導致英國經濟更加蕭條。/ Germany was suffering a steep recession. 當時德國正陷於急劇的經濟衰退之中。/ As dozens of companies go out of business, others are riding out the recession. 在大量公司紛紛倒閉之際，其他一些公司卻安全挺過蕭條期。/ With a recession looming, consumers are spending less. 經濟日漸蕭條，人們的消費也越來越少了。/ The country has been hit by recession. 該國受到經濟蕭條的衝擊。
                      
<span class="vocabulary">**downturn**</span> [ˈdaʊntɜ:n; 美 -tɜ:rn]
<span class="definition">n. [usually sing.] ~ (in sth)（商業、經濟等的）衰退，下降，衰退期：</span>downturn in sales, downturn in trade, downturn in business 銷量下滑；貿易下滑；營業額的下降 / the economic downturn of the late 1990s 20世紀90年代末的經濟衰退 / They predicted a severe economic downturn. 他們預言會有嚴重的經濟衰退。/ a sharp downturn of the economy 經濟的急劇下滑 / unchanged profits for 1990 due to a sharp downturn in the industry 行業的急劇衰退導致了 1990 年利潤維持不變 / market downturn 市場下滑 / global downturn 全球性衰退 / cyclical downturn 週期性衰退 / The building industry is experiencing a severe downturn in its workload. 建築業正經歷著工程數量的嚴重下滑。/ The 1990s witnessed a sharp downturn in the party's fortunes. 20世紀90年代是該黨遭遇嚴重挫折的時期。

<span class="vocabulary">**trough**</span> [trɒf; 美 trɔ:f]
<span class="definition">n. [C] 低估期，尤指（企業或經濟的）低潮或蕭條階段：</span>There have been peaks and troughs in the long-term trend of unemployment. 長期以來失業率一直時起時伏。/ Looking back afterwards you will see that this was not a terminal trough in your career. 今後回想起來你就會明白，這並非你事業中一蹶不振的低谷。/ American bank shares have risen by 60% since their trough last October. 美國的銀行股自從去年10月跌入低谷以來已經上漲了60%。/ The economy is unlikely to reach its trough until the turn of the year. 經濟在今年年底前不太可能探底. / the peaks and troughs of economic cycles 經濟週期中的高峰和低谷

<span class="vocabulary">**slump**</span> [slʌmp]
<span class="definition">n. [C] 一國經濟或商業的蕭條期、衰退期：</span>the slump of the 1930s 20世紀30年代的大蕭條 / The toy industry is in a slump. 玩具業現在不景氣。/ economic slump 經濟下挫 / The economy is in a prolonged slump. 經濟陷入長期衰退。/ The economy is in a slump. 經濟處於蕭條期。
           
<span class="vocabulary">**slack**</span> [slæk]
<span class="definition">adj.（生意）蕭條冷清的，沒什麼顧客或銷量的、不忙的：</span>a slack period 蕭條期 / The workload can be evened out, instead of the shop having busy times and slack periods. 工作量可以平均開來，不像商店那樣有時很忙，有時很閒。/ The antiques business remained slack. 古董生意仍然冷清。/ This season has been pretty slack for local hotels so far. 本季直至目前對當地旅館來說生意都相當慘淡。

<span class="vocabulary">**weak**</span> [wi:k] 
<span class="definition">adj.（經濟或行業）蕭條的，是此義的一個基礎通用詞：</span>The economy is very weak. 經濟十分蕭條。

<span class="vocabulary">**dead**</span> [ded] 
<span class="definition">adj. [非正式，貶]（商業）蕭條的，停滯的：</span>a dead market 蕭條的市場 / the dead season for the tourist market 旅遊市場的淡季

<span class="vocabulary">**dull**</span> [dʌl] 
<span class="definition">adj. [尤美]（貿易）蕭條的，不景氣的：</span>Don’t sell into a dull market. 不要到蕭條的市場去推銷。

<span class="vocabulary">**flat**</span> [flæt] 
<span class="definition">adj.（商業）不景氣的；（生意）蕭條的：</span>The housing market has been flat for months. 住宅市場已有好幾個月處於低迷狀態。/ Car sales stayed flat. 汽車銷量仍不景氣。
            
<span class="vocabulary">**ailing**</span> [ˈeɪlɪŋ]
<span class="definition">adj. [正式]（公司、政府等）處境艱難且每況愈下的：</span>measures to help the ailing economy 改善經濟不景氣的措施 / The rise in overseas sales is good news for the ailing American economy. 海外銷售額的上升對於不景氣的美國經濟來說是個好訊息。
                      
<span class="vocabulary">**subdued**</span> [səbˈdju:d; 美 -ˈdu:d]
<span class="definition">adj.（商業活動）不活躍的、低迷的、蕭條的：</span>a period of subdued trading 貿易蕭條時期

<span class="vocabulary">**lean**</span> [li:n]
<span class="definition">adj. [常用於名詞前]（一段時間）難以賺錢的、生產不出食物等的，如蕭條的、不景氣的：</span>lean period, lean spell 不景氣時期 / The company recovered well after going through several lean years. 經歷了幾年的蕭條後，這家公司的業務才完全恢復了正常。/ the lean years of the 1930s 蕭條的20世紀30年代 / With fewer tourists in town, the taxi trade is going through its leanest patch for 30 years. 隨著城裡遊客的減少，計程車行業正遭受30年來最不景氣的時期。
