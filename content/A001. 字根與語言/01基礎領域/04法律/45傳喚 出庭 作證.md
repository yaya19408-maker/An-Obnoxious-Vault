☀ <span class="category">**傳喚 出庭 作證：**</span>
<span class="vocabulary">**summon**</span> [ˈsʌmən]
<span class="definition">vt. [正式] 傳喚（某人出庭）：</span>He was summoned to appear before the magistrates. 他被傳喚在地方法院出庭。/ He has been summoned from New York to give evidence at the trial. 他被從紐約召來出庭作證。
          
<span class="vocabulary">**summons**</span> [ˈsʌmənz]
<span class="definition">n. [C] [美也用citation] 法院的傳喚：</span>jury summons（美）陪審團傳喚 / witness summons（英）證人傳喚 <span class="definition">vt. ~ sb (for sth) 傳喚某人出庭：</span>She was summonsed for speeding. 她因超速行車被傳訊。/ He was summonsed to appear in court. 他被傳喚出庭。/ The men were summonsed and last week 30 appeared before Hove magistrates. 這些人被傳喚，上週有30人到霍夫地方法院接受訊問。/ She has been summonsed to appear at St Albans magistrates' court. 她已經收到傳票，要求其到聖奧爾本斯地方法院出庭。
           
<span class="vocabulary">**cite**</span> [saɪt]
<span class="definition">vt. [正式] [法律] 傳喚、傳訊（某人出庭）：</span>She was cited in the divorce proceedings. 她在離婚訴訟中被傳喚。/ The judge ruled a mistrial and cited the prosecutors for outrageous misconduct. 法官裁定為無效審判，並以嚴重瀆職為名傳訊了檢察官。

<span class="vocabulary">**citation**</span> [saɪˈteɪʃn]
<span class="definition">n. [C] [美]（法院的）傳喚：</span>The judge issued a contempt citation against the woman for violating a previous court order. 法官對上一次拒不遵守庭諭的那名婦女發出了藐視法庭的傳訊。

<span class="vocabulary">**appear**</span> [ə'pɪə] 
<span class="definition">vi. 1 出庭（作證或受審）：</span>A man will appear in court today charged with the murder. 一名被控犯謀殺罪的男子今天將出庭受審。/ He has been asked to appear as a witness for the defence. 他已被傳喚出庭為被告作證。/ to appear before a court, to appear before a judge 出庭；出庭受審 <span class="definition">2 appear for/on behalf of sb 在法庭上做某人的律師：</span>He is the lawyer appearing for the defendant. 他作為被告的辯護律師出庭。

<span class="vocabulary">**appearance**</span> [ə'pɪərəns] 
<span class="definition">n. [C] 出庭：</span>court appearance 出庭
           
<span class="vocabulary">**haul**</span> [hɔ:l]
<span class="definition">vt. [常用被動式] ~ sb (up) before sb/sth 使某人出庭受審：</span>He was hauled up before the local magistrates for dangerous driving. 他因危險駕駛而被移交地方法庭審判。/ She was hauled before magistrates for refusing to reveal her age to a policeman. 她因拒絕告訴警察自己的年齡而被地方法院傳訊。           

<span class="vocabulary">**attest**</span> [əˈtest]
<span class="definition">vt. [正式] 作證（例如在法庭上作證）：</span>to attest a will 就遺囑作見證 / The signature was attested by two witnesses. 這個簽名有兩名見證人。
                    
<span class="vocabulary">**testify**</span> [ˈtestɪfaɪ]
<span class="definition">vt.&vi. ~ (against/for sb) / ~ (to/about sth)（對發生的事或某事的真實性）作證，尤指出庭作證：</span>She refused to testify against her husband. 她拒絕出庭作證指控丈夫。/ There are several witnesses who will testify for the defence. 有幾名證人願意為被告作證。/ He was summoned to testify before a grand jury. 他被傳喚出庭在大陪審團前作證。/ He testified (that) he was at the theatre at the time of the murder. 他作證聲稱兇殺案發生時自己正在劇院。/ Several eyewitnesses testified that they saw the officers hit Miller in the face. 幾位目擊證人證明他們看見那幾個警官打了米勒的臉。/ Eva testified to having seen Herndon with his gun on the stairs. 伊娃作證稱看見過赫恩登持槍站在樓梯上。

<span class="vocabulary">**vouch**</span> [vaʊtʃ]
<span class="definition">vi. vouch for sth（因有證據而）為…作證：</span>I was in bed with the flu. My wife can vouch for that. 我患流感臥床休息了。我的妻子可為此作證。
