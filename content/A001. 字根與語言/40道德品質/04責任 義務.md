☀ <span class="category">**責任、義務的名詞：**</span>
<span class="vocabulary">**duty**</span> ['dju:tɪ] 
<span class="definition">n. 1 [C, U] 道德或法律上的責任，義務：</span>a sense of duty 責任感 / contractual, legal duty 合同義務；法律義務 / the general duty of the police to preserve the peace 警察維持治安的基本義務 / I feel it is my civic duty to vote. 我認為投票選舉是我作為市民的義務。/ family, parental, moral duty 家庭責任；父母的責任；道義上的責任 / He had failed in his duty to his daughter. 他對女兒未盡到責任。<span class="definition">2 [U] 工作，上班：</span>to report for duty 報到上班 / What time do you go off duty? 你什麼時候下班？/ You’re not allowed to drink alcohol on duty. 值班時不準喝酒。/ day, night duty 白班；夜班 / active, inactive duty（軍事上的）現役；非現役 / daily, regular, routine duty 日常工作；例行工作 / duty manager（英）值班經理 <span class="definition">3 [pl.] duties，複數形式可指工作中的部分任務、職責（當處於某一職位的人因該職位之規定而負有一定的道德或法律上的責任或義務的時候，也可表示“職責”，此時與responsibility、job或office存在交集，不過duty依然強調道德和法律對行為人的約束）：</span>military, guard, patrol, peacekeeping, sentry duties 軍事任務；警衛任務；巡邏任務；維和任務；放哨 / temporary, official, administrative, professional, teaching duties 臨時任務；公務職責；行政職責；專業職責；教學任務 / He was accused of neglecting his professional duties. 他被指控瀆職。/ household duties, cooking duties, cleaning duties 家務活兒；做飯任務；清掃任務 / his duties as captain 他的隊長職務 / the performance of your duties 職責的履行 / The members of staff each have their own duties. 每個職員都有各自的任務。/ The company prefers to handle translation duties in-house. 這家公司更願意在公司內部處理翻譯任務。

<span class="vocabulary">**responsibility**</span> [rɪ͵spɒnsə'bɪlɪtɪ] 
<span class="definition">n. 1 [U, C] responsibility (for sth/for doing sth) / responsibility (to do sth) 處理或照顧…的責任，如果做錯了則要被責備，即負責：</span>It is on my own responsibility. 這件事由我自己負責。/ Will you take responsibility for arranging the food? 你來負責安排飯食怎麼樣？/ corporate, security, constitutional responsibility 企業責任；安全責任；憲法賦予的責任 / environmental, financial, moral, social responsibility 環境責任；財務責任；道德責任；社會責任 / With great power comes great responsibility. 權大，責任也大。/ a sense of responsibility 責任感 / rights and responsibilities 權利和義務 <span class="definition">2 [C] responsibility (for sth) 對已發生的壞事所受到的責備，或所負的責任。與blame相近：</span>to admit responsibility for the attack 對這起襲擊事件負責 / The bank refuses to accept responsibility for the mistake. 銀行拒絕為這一錯誤承擔責任。/ We retain all responsibility for any shortcomings. 若有任何缺陷，我們承擔一切責任。/ legal, criminal responsibility 法律責任；刑事責任 <span class="definition">3 [U, C] responsibility (to/towards sb) / responsibility (to do sth) 由於身處某個職位而對…所負的幫助或照顧之責任。此時相當於job，但比job正式且常用：</span>The manager thinks he has a responsibility to help his employees. 這位經理認為自己有義務幫助自己的屬下。/ What are the main responsibilities in your job? 你主要的工作職責是什麼？/ departmental, professional, teaching responsibility 部門職責；專業上的職責；教學職責 / childcare, family, parental responsibility 看護小孩的任務；家庭責任；父母的職責 / command, management, supervisory responsibility 指揮職責；管理職責；監管職責 / adult responsibility 成人責任
           
<span class="vocabulary">**obligation**</span> [ˌɒblɪˈgeɪʃn; 美 ˌɑ:b-]
<span class="definition">n. 1 [U]（法律或道德意義上的）義務、職責：</span>I am under no obligation to tell you my name. 我沒有義務告訴你我的名字。/ She did not feel under any obligation to tell him the truth. 她覺得沒有義務告訴他實情。/ You are under no obligation to buy anything. 你不必非買什麼東西不可。/ Ministers are under no obligation to follow the committee's recommendations. 部長不必非得聽從委員會的建議。/ We will send you an estimate for the work without obligation (= you do not have to accept it). 我們將寄上工程估算，僅供參考。/ Our mortgage advice is given free and without obligation. 我們免費提供按揭貸款建議，僅供參考。/ I don't want people coming to see me out of a sense of obligation. 我不想讓別人迫於無奈來看我。<span class="definition">2 [C] 因已承諾（或因法律規定）而必須去做的事情，即義務，責任。相當於commitment：</span>legal, statutory obligations 法律上的義務；法定義務 / obligations arising from your contract of employment 根據僱傭合同你須履行的義務 / The builders failed to meet their contractual obligations. 建築商沒能履行合同中規定的義務。/ ethical, moral, religious, social obligations 道德義務；宗教義務；社會責任 / We have a moral obligation to help. 在道義上我們有義務幫忙。/ debt, financial, pension obligations 債務方面的責任；財務方面的責任；養老金方面的責任 / I can't travel next month because of family obligations. 出於家庭責任，我下個月不能去旅行。/ professional obligations 職業責任 / mutual obligations 共同的責任 / treaty obligations 條約規定的責任 / A lawyer owes an obligation of confidence to the client. 律師有為當事人保密的義務。/ The United States will do that which is necessary to meet its obligations to its own citizens. 美國將為自己的公民盡其應盡的義務。/ When teachers assign homework, students usually feel an obligation to do it. 老師佈置作業時，學生通常認為完成作業是一種義務。
           
<span class="vocabulary">**accountability**</span> [əˌkaʊntəˈbɪləti]
<span class="definition">n. [U] 責任：</span>individual, personal accountability 個人負有的責任 / direct accountability 直接責任 / moral accountability 道德義務 / corporate, financial, managerial, professional accountability 公司的責任；財務責任；管理責任；職責 / democratic, government, parliamentary（尤英）, political accountability 民主的職責；政府的職責；議會的職責；政治責任 / police accountability 警方的職責 / demands for greater public accountability in the police service 要求警方對公眾負更大責任 / This process of centralization further weakens accountability. 這一中央集權化過程進一步削弱了責任意識。/ to ensure accountability for decisions made 確保對所作的決定負責 / the apparent lack of accountability of the security forces 安全部隊的明顯缺乏責任感 / the accountability of a company's directors to the shareholders 公司董事向股東所負之責
           
<span class="vocabulary">**liability**</span> [ˌlaɪəˈbɪləti]
<span class="definition">n. [U] ~ (for sth) / ~ (to do sth)（法律上對某事物的）責任，義務：</span>full liability 完全責任 / legal liability 法律責任 / criminal liability 刑事責任 / They have no legal liability for damage to customers' possessions. 他們對客戶的財物損失不負有任何法律責任 / The company cannot accept liability for any damage caused by natural disasters. 該公司對自然災害造成的任何損失概不承擔責任。/ They have denied liability for the accident. 他們否認對事故負有責任。
           
<span class="vocabulary">**onus**</span> [ˈəʊnəs; 美 ˈoʊnəs]
<span class="definition">n. [sing.] [正式]（通常用the onus）對某事負有的責任、職責、義務：</span>The onus is on employers to follow health and safety laws. 僱主有義務遵行健康安全法。/ The onus is on the shopkeeper to provide goods which live up to the quality of their description. 店主有義務提供質量與其描述相符的商品。/ The onus is on government departments to show cause why information cannot be disclosed. 政府部門有責任給出不披露資訊的原因。/ The onus of proof is reversed in libel actions and placed firmly on the defender. 在誹謗訴訟中，舉證責任反過來由辯方全權承擔。
           
<span class="vocabulary">**oversight**</span> [ˈəʊvəsaɪt; 美 ˈoʊvərs-]
<span class="definition">n. [U] [正式] 負責（一個過程、體系等）：</span>The committee has oversight of finance and general policy. 委員會負責處理財政和綜合政策

<span class="vocabulary">**blame**</span> [bleɪm] 
<span class="definition">n. [U] blame (for sth)（做壞事或錯事的）責任；說某人應該為某件事負責（即責備，指責）：</span>Why do I always get the blame for everything that goes wrong? 為什麼出了事總是讓我背黑鍋？/ The company refused to accept any blame for the damage. 公司拒絕為損失承擔任何責任。/ They placed the blame squarely on the doctor. 他們把責任全然歸咎於醫生。/ The government must share the blame for this confusion. 政府必須為這種混亂局面承擔一部分責任。/ They tried to shift the blame onto someone else. 他們試圖把責任推卸到別人身上。
           
<span class="vocabulary">**guilt**</span> [gɪlt]
<span class="definition">n. [U]（為做錯的事或發生過的壞事所承擔的）罪責、責任、罪過：</span>The investigation will try to find out where the guilt for the disaster really lies. 此調查將盡力查出災難的真正責任所在。/ There is no doubt as to where the guilt lies. 罪責在哪一方面是毫無疑問的。

<span class="vocabulary">**business**</span> ['bɪznɪs] 
<span class="definition">n. [U] 歸某人或機構所管的事，即職責：</span>It is the business of the police to protect the community. 警察的職責是保護社會。/ I shall make it my business to find out who is responsible. 我要親自查出是誰的責任。/ My private life is none of your business. 我的私生活與你無關。/ ‘What are you reading?’ ‘Mind your own business!’ “你在看什麼書？”“不要多管閒事！”

<span class="vocabulary">**commitment**</span> [kə'mɪtmənt] 
<span class="definition">n. [C] 已承諾（或已同意）去做的一件事；不得不去做的一件事。可稱為責任：</span>business, family, social, teaching commitment 商業上的責任；家庭責任；社會義務；教學義務 / She can’t meet her financial commitments. 她無法履行自己的財務責任。/ to honour commitments on reduction of air pollution 履行減少空氣汙染的義務

<span class="vocabulary">**charge**</span> [tʃɑːdӡ] 
<span class="definition">n. [C] 單數名詞，表示任務、職責或責任，為正式用法：</span>His charge was to obtain information. 他的職責是收集資訊。

<span class="vocabulary">**job**</span> [dӡɒb] 
<span class="definition">n. [C] 常用單數，責任或職責。一般表示對具體而瑣碎的事情所負有的職責，是一種生活化用法：</span>It’s not my job to give him help. 我沒有義務幫他。/ It’s not my job to lock up! 上鎖不是我的事兒！

<span class="vocabulary">**work**</span> [wə:k] 
<span class="definition">n. [U] 職責：</span>The accountant described his work to the sales staff. 會計師向銷售部的職員介紹了自己的職責。

<span class="vocabulary">**concern**</span> [kən'sə:n] 
<span class="definition">n. [C, usually sing.] [正式] 某人負責的一件事：</span>This matter is their concern. 這件事由他們負責。
           
<span class="vocabulary">**mission**</span> [ˈmɪʃn]
<span class="definition">n. 1 [C]（個人或團體所負有的）官方使命，尤指出使其它國家時所負有的使命：</span>Salisbury sent him on a diplomatic mission to North America. 索爾茲伯裡派他到北美執行一項外交任務。/ He has been on a mission to help end Lebanon's political crisis. 他已經被派執行一項旨在幫助結束黎巴嫩政治危機的使命。/ a trade mission to China 赴華貿易使命 / a fact-finding mission 核查事實的工作 <span class="definition">2 [C] 某人內心中的使命、天職：</span>a man with a mission 肩負使命的男子 / Her mission in life was to work with the homeless. 她以幫助無家可歸者為己任。/ He viewed his mission in life as protecting the weak from the evil. 他認為自己畢生的使命就是保護弱者不受邪惡勢力的欺凌。/ There is an enormous sense of mission in his speech and gesture. 他的一言一行都帶著強烈的使命感。/ A powerful sense of mission underpins everything he does. 強烈的使命感支撐著他所做的一切。
           
<span class="vocabulary">**calling**</span> [ˈkɔ:lɪŋ]
<span class="definition">n. [C]（強烈想要去做某事的一種）使命感，尤指想幫助他人的一種強烈願望，責任感：</span>He realized that his calling was to preach the gospel. 他體悟到宣講福音是他的使命。
           
<span class="vocabulary">**vocation**</span> [vəʊˈkeɪʃn; 美 voʊ-]
<span class="definition">n. [C, U] ~ (for sth) 認為某種工作或生活方式特別適合自己的一種信念：</span>He has a vocation for teaching. 他是教書的材料。/ She seems to have a vocation for healing. 看來她具備治病救人的才能。/ She is a doctor with a strong sense of vocation. 她是一位具有強烈使命感的醫生。/ This is a job that demands a sense of vocation. 這是一份需要具備使命感的工作。

相關詞義延伸：
· mission還可指各種使命、任務；
· vocation還可指認為自己是被神選中的一個牧師或修女的一種信念，可稱為聖召、神召。

周邊詞彙補充：
· fault可指（已發生或已做出的錯事的）責任、過失、過錯；
· office可指（尤指政府中的）重要職位或權力，也可指此種與職位相關的工作和職責；
· position可指在一些團隊性體育比賽中，某名隊員所處的位置和其所擁有的職責，可以叫職責，位置或角色。

<span class="vocabulary">**responsible**</span> [rɪˈspɒnsəbl; 美 -ˈspɑ:n-]
<span class="definition">adj. 1 ~ (for sb/sth) / ~ (for doing sth) 有責任去做某事（或照看…）的（如果做錯了會受責備）：</span>Mike is responsible for designing the entire project. 邁克負責設計全部工程。/ Even where parents no longer live together, they each continue to be responsible for their children. 即使父母不再共同生活，他們也要分別對子女負責。/ the minister responsible for the environment 負責環境工作的部長 / The man responsible for finding the volunteers is Dr. Charles Weber. 負責尋找志願者的人是查爾斯·韋伯博士。/ I am making you responsible for the cooking. 我讓你負責烹飪。/ They're responsible for cleaning the engine. 他們負責清洗發動機。/ I'm leaving you responsible for Juliet's protection. 我把保護朱麗葉的任務交給你了。/ Thompson is directly responsible for all aspects of the business unit. 湯普森直接負責該業務部門的方方面面。/ if you're solely responsible for your family's welfare 如果你家庭的幸福只是靠你一個人 / Members of the committee are collectively responsible for all decisions taken. 委員們對所作的一切決定集體負責。/ Both parents are equally responsible for raising the children. 父母雙方對於撫養孩子負有同等責任。/ legally responsible 負法律責任 / the person nominally responsible for staff training 名義上負責職員培訓的人 / The board is ultimately responsible for policy decisions. 最終由董事會負責決定採取什麼政策。// We are all equally responsible for its success. 我們大家都同樣為其成功貢獻了力量。<span class="definition">2 ~ (for sth) 應受責備的、需要對壞事負責：</span>Who's responsible for this mess? 是誰弄得這麼亂？/ Everything will be done to bring those responsible to justice. 將竭盡全力把罪魁禍首繩之以法。/ He is mentally ill and cannot be held responsible for his actions. 他有精神病，不能對自己的行為負責。/ He still felt responsible for her death. 他仍然覺得自己對她的死負有責任。/ I want you to do everything you can to find out who's responsible. 我希望你能盡一切可能找出責任人。/ A man arrested in Madrid is believed to be responsible for the bombings. 一個在馬德里被捕的人被認為是爆炸案嫌犯。/ They held him responsible for the failure of the policy. 他們把政策的失敗歸咎於他。/ He was almost single-handedly responsible for the flourishing drug trade in the town. 鎮上猖獗的毒品交易幾乎都是他一手造成的。/ morally responsible 應負道德責任 / criminally responsible 應負刑事責任 / Who was responsible for the mistake? 誰應對這一錯誤負責？<span class="definition">3 ~ to sb/sth 對主管者或主管機構有彙報的責任的：</span>The Council of Ministers is responsible to the Assembly. 內閣須向議會負責。/ The prime minister is directly responsible to Parliament. 首相直接向議會負責。/ I'm responsible to my board of directors. 我對董事會負責。/ The government will be responsible to the President alone. 政府只對總統一人負責。/ a complex web of party bodies ultimately responsible to the Central Committee 最終向中央委員會負責的複雜的政黨機構 <span class="definition">4（人或其行為舉止）可信賴的、可靠的，即有責任心的：</span>Clare has a mature and responsible attitude to work. 克萊爾對待工作成熟而可信賴。/ He's a very responsible sort of person. 他是個非常有責任心的人。/ He feels that the media should be more responsible in what they report. 他覺得媒體應該對報道的內容更負責任些。/ their efforts to make the industry more responsible and responsive to concerns 他們使該行業變得更有責任感且更積極回應人們所關心問題的努力 / The organization needs to become more environmentally responsible. 該組織應該加強環境意識。/ ethically, morally, politically, socially responsible 有道德意識；有政治意識；有社會意識 / Resources must be allocated in a morally responsible way. 資源分配必須要對得起良心。/ They are not very responsible with money. 他們花錢有些大手大腳。/ In California you have to be 21 to be considered responsible enough to drink. 在加利福尼亞州，必須滿21歲才能被認為可以飲酒。<span class="definition">5 [常用於名詞前]（工作或職位）責任重大的；需要可靠的人負責的：</span>I work in a government office. It's a responsible position, I suppose, but not very exciting. 我在政府機關工作。我想那是一個責任重大的職位，但並不是很有意思。/ They have been demoted to less responsible jobs. 他們被降職去做不太重要的工作。

<span class="vocabulary">**accountable**</span> [əˈkaʊntəbl]
<span class="definition">adj. [名詞前不常用] ~ (to sb) (for sth)（對自己的決定或行為）負有責任、負有解釋的義務：</span>Politicians are ultimately accountable to the voters. 從政者最終是向選民負責。/ Someone has to be held ultimately accountable for the accident. 必須有人對這起事故負最終責任。/ Senior managers are directly accountable to the Board of Directors. 高層管理人員直接對董事會負責。/ Someone must be held accountable for the killings. 必須有人要對這些兇殺事件負責。/ The directors are held accountable by the shareholders.股東認為董事應負責任。/ Public officials can finally be held accountable for their actions. 終於要對政府官員實行問責了。/ The major service industries should be accountable to their customers. 主要的服務行業應該對它們的顧客負責。/ Local authorities should be publicly accountable to the communities they serve. 地方當局應對所服務的社群民眾負責。/ democratically accountable leaders 負有民主職責的領導人 / to be financially accountable, to be legally accountable, to be politically accountable 負有財務職責；負有法律責任；負有政治責任 / In the end, we are all accountable for our actions. 我們最終都要對自已的行為負責。/ The real power lies with a bureaucracy that is not accountable to the public. 實權掌據在對公眾不負責仔的官僚手中。/ Politicians are accountable to their constituents. 從政者對自己選區的選民負責。
           
<span class="vocabulary">**culpable**</span> [ˈkʌlpəbl]
<span class="definition">adj. [正式] 應受責備的；難辭其咎的：</span>Their decision to do nothing makes them culpable. 他們不採取任何行動的決定使他們難辭其咎。/ manslaughter resulting from culpable negligence 應該受到處罰的因為疏忽導致的過失殺人罪 / His refusal to admit his mistake makes him even more culpable in my view. 在我看來，他拒不認錯更該受到譴責。/ morally culpable 道德上該受到譴責 / You are equally culpable in this affair.你在這件事上也同樣該受罰。/ She believed him culpable of murder. 她認為他犯有謀殺罪，應受懲罰。
