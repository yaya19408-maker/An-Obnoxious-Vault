☀ <span class="category">**辨別、區分、鑑定的動詞：**</span>
<span class="vocabulary">**identify**</span> [aɪˈdentɪfaɪ]
<span class="definition">vt.（也可用非正式的ID）~ sb/sth (as sb/sth) 辨認出（即因為不同點而找出…）：</span>The bodies were identified as those of two suspected drug dealers. 那兩具屍體被辨認出原是兩名販毒嫌疑犯。/ She was able to identify her attacker. 她認出了襲擊她的人。/ I could identify him easily if I saw him again. 如果再見到他，我很容易就能把他認出來。/ Did you identify all the pictures correctly? 你把所有照片都認對了嗎? / There are a number of distinguishing characteristics by which you can identify a Hollywood epic. 好萊塢的史詩大片有著一些與眾不同的特點。/ I tried to identify her perfume. 我試圖分辨出她用哪種香水。/ The new test will enable us to identify more accurately patients who are most at risk. 新的檢測方法將使我們能夠更加準確地識別哪些病人的情況最危急。/ Participants learn to identify different species by their calls. 參與人員學習透過分析鳴叫聲音來分辨不同的物種。/ Jaime successfully identified all 45 different species. 傑米成功辨別出全部45個物種。/ a computer program to automatically identify any query ending with a question mark 一套自動識別以問號結尾的問句的計算機程式

<span class="vocabulary">**distinguish**</span> [dɪs'tɪŋɡwɪʃ] 
<span class="definition">vt.＆vi. distinguish (between) A and B / distinguish A from B 辨別兩個人（或事物）之間的差異，即區分，辨別，相當於differentiate。是一個常規用詞：</span>to distinguish between right and wrong 明辨是非 / The plant can be readily distinguished. 這種植物很容易識別。/ to distinguish between cause and effect 區分因果 / to distinguish between fact and fiction in this book 分辨書中的真實內容和虛構內容 / She could not distinguish one child from another. 她無法把這兩個孩子區分開。<span class="definition">vt. distinguish A (from B) 成為使兩個人（或動物、事物）相互區分開的特徵，如使有別於…：</span>What was it that distinguished her from her classmates? 是什麼使得她有別於班上其他同學呢？/ distinguishing marks 區別性的標記
                      
<span class="vocabulary">**discern**</span> [dɪˈsɜ:n; 美 -ˈsɜ:rn]
<span class="definition">vt. [正式]（不用於進行時）覺察出；識別出；瞭解某事物（尤指覺察出、識別出或瞭解不明顯的事物）：</span>It is possible to discern a number of different techniques in her work. 從她的作品中可以識別出許多不同的創作手法。/ You need a long series of data to be able to discern such a trend. 你需要一長串的資料才能看出這一趨勢。/ It was hard to discern why this was happening. 很難弄清楚這為什麼會發生。/ He discerned a certain coldness in their welcome. 他覺察到他們的歡迎有點冷淡。/ It is often difficult to discern how widespread public support is. 瞭解公眾支援的廣泛程度常常是困難的。<span class="definition">vt. [正式]（不用於進行時）依稀看到或聽到（但不是很清楚），如分辨出：</span>We could just discern the house in the distance. 我們只能勉強分辨出遠處的房子。/ Below the bridge we could just discern a narrow, weedy ditch. 我們僅能依稀辨出橋下一條雜草叢生的窄溝。/ She could clearly discern a figure walking up to the house.她可以清楚地看見一個人影正走近那幢房子。

<span class="vocabulary">**differentiate**</span> [ˌdɪfəˈrenʃieɪt]
<span class="definition">vt.&vi. ~ (between) A and B / ~ A (from B) 區分、辨別：</span>It's difficult to differentiate between the two varieties. 這兩個品種很難辨別。/ It is not always possible to differentiate between the two diseases. 這兩種疾病並不是總能區分開來。/ I can't differentiate one variety from another. 我無法將這幾個品種區別開來。/ A child may not differentiate between his imagination and the real world. 兒童可能無法將自己的幻想與真實世界區分開來。/ At this age your baby cannot differentiate one person from another. 這個年齡的嬰兒還不能認人。<span class="definition">vt. ~ sth (from sth) 表明…間的差別；構成…間差別的特徵：</span>The male's yellow beak differentiates it from the female. 雄鳥黃色的喙是與雌鳥相區別的主要特徵。/ distinctive policies that differentiate them from the other parties 使他們有別於其他政黨的特殊政策 / features which clearly differentiate this product from other similar ones 將此產品與其他相似產品清楚區分開來的特徵
<span class="definition">vi. ~ between A and B（尤指不公正地）區別對待：</span>瞭解即可
           
<span class="vocabulary">**discriminate**</span> [dɪˈskrɪmɪneɪt]
<span class="definition">vt.&vi. ~ (between A and B) / ~ A from B 區分、區別、辨別：</span>The computer program was unable to discriminate between letters and numbers. 這計算機程式不能辨別字母與數字。/ When do babies learn to discriminate voices? 嬰兒什麼時候學會辨別嗓音呢？/ A number of features discriminate this species from others. 有許多特徵使這一物種與其他物種區別開來。/ He is incapable of discriminating between a good idea and a terrible one. 他分不清主意的好壞。/ The device can discriminate between the cancerous and the normal cells. 這臺儀器可以辨別癌細胞和正常的細胞。

<span class="vocabulary">**recognize**</span> ['rekəɡnaɪz]（英也用recognise）
<span class="definition">vt. recognize sb/sth (by/from sth) 當看到或聽到某人（或某事物）時，知道其是誰（或是什麼），因為以前看到過或聽到過，即認識，認出，辨別出：</span>I recognized him as soon as he came in the room. 他一進屋我就認出了他。/ Do you recognize this tune? 你能聽出這是哪支曲子嗎？/ I immediately recognized the building. 我立刻認出了那座建築。/ I vaguely recognized his voice. 我大致聽出了他的聲音。

<span class="vocabulary">**know**</span> [nəʊ] 
<span class="definition">vt. 1 能夠認出（或辨認出）…：</span>I couldn’t see who was speaking, but I knew the voice. 我看不到誰在講話，但我能辨認出聲音。/ She knows a bargain when she sees one. 她一看就知道有沒有便宜可撿。/ Would she know you if she saw you on the street? 如果在街上見到你，她會認出你嗎？<span class="definition">2 know sb/sth from sb/sth 能把某人或某事物從另一者中區分出來，能分辨：</span>to know right from wrong 分辨是非

<span class="vocabulary">**tell**</span> [tel] 
<span class="definition">vt. tell A from B 將一個事物（或人）與另一者區分開，即區分，辨別，辨認，分辨，識別等。是一個簡單用詞：</span>to tell the difference between the two versions 分辨出這兩個版本的區別 / Can you tell Tom from his twin brother? 你能分得出湯姆和他的孿生弟弟嗎？

<span class="vocabulary">**place**</span> [pleɪs] 
<span class="definition">vt. 通常用於否定句，指能夠認出，辨認出，識別出：</span>I’ve seen her before but I just can’t place her. 我從前見過她，不過現在認不出她來。/ His accent was impossible to place. 他的口音無法辨認。

<span class="vocabulary">**clock**</span> [klɒk] 
<span class="definition">vt. [英，非正式] 認出某人：</span>I clocked her in the driving mirror. 我從汽車後視鏡裡認出了她。
           
<span class="vocabulary">**authenticate**</span> [ɔ:ˈθentɪkeɪt]
<span class="definition">vt. ~ sth (as sth) 官方鑑定某事物的真假：</span>The letter has been authenticated by handwriting experts. 這封信已由筆跡專家證明是真的。/ Experts have authenticated the writing as that of Byron himself. 專家鑑定這字跡是拜倫的親筆。

<span class="vocabulary">**recognition**</span> [ˌrekəgˈnɪʃn]
<span class="definition">n. [U] 認出；識別出：</span>He glanced briefly towards her but there was no sign of recognition. 他瞥了她一眼，但似乎沒認出她來。/ He searched for a sign of recognition on her face, but there was none. 他試圖在她的臉上找出一絲認出他的神情，但是根本沒有。/ George said, 'Ida, how are you?' She frowned for a moment and then recognition dawned. 'George Black. Well, I never.'. 喬治說：“艾達，你好嗎？”她皺了一會兒眉頭，然後才認出他。“喬治·布萊克，噢，我一直都不好。”/ She stared directly at the witness but he did not show a flicker of recognition(= he did not show that he recognized her). 她徑直盯著證人；而證人卻顯得一點兒都不認識她。/ He pulled the hood of his cloak over his head to avoid recognition. 他拉起披風的帽子罩住頭以防別人認出他來。/ Recognition slowly dawned, and I remembered her from my college days. 我漸漸認出了她來，記得那還是大學時代的事了。/ He looked up, glanced at them without recognition, and went on his way. 他抬頭看了他們一眼，沒認出他們，便繼續向前走。/ There was no recognition in his eyes(= he did not look as if he recognized her). 他看上去似乎不認識她。/ character, face, fingerprint, handwriting, speech, text, voice recognition 字元識別；面孔識別；指紋識別；筆跡識別；語音識別；文字識別；聲音識別 / computer recognition 計算機識別 / the automatic recognition of handwriting by computer 計算機自動識別筆跡 / The monitoring system allows recognition of pollution hot spots. 監測系統可以識別出汙染產重的地區。/ recognition software, recognition system, recognition technology 識別軟體；識別系統；識別技術 / Many of those interviewed said their job had changed beyond recognition (= changed completely) over the past five years. 很多接受採訪的人都說近五年來他們的工作發生了翻天覆地的變化。/ The equipment and methods of production have improved out of all recognition(= greatly improved). 生產裝置和生產方式提高之快令人難以置信。/ The bodies were mutilated beyond recognition. 屍體都殘缺不全，無法辨認了。/ The facilities have improved beyond all recognition. 這些裝置經過大幅度的改良，都讓人認不出來了。/ the early recognition of a disease 疾病的早期發現 / brand, name recognition 品牌知名度；知名度 / One of the main goals of marketing is name recognition. 市場營銷的主要目標之一就是建立知名度。/ word recognition 識字/ a test of children's word recognition 兒童識字測試

<span class="vocabulary">**recognizable**</span> [ˈrekəgnaɪzəbl; ˌrekəgˈnaɪzəbl]
<span class="definition">adj. ~ (as sth/sb) 容易認出的；易於識別的：</span>After so many years she was still instantly recognizable. 過了這麼多年，還是一眼就能認出她。/ the mannerisms that make him instantly recognizable 讓人一眼就能認出他的舉止 / recognizable by their distinctive uniforms 透過與眾不同的制服就可以認出他們 / The body was found to be well preserved, his features easily recognizable. 屍體儲存完好，他的容貌特徵清晰可辨。/ The building was easily recognizable as a prison. 很容易看出這座建築是所監獄。/ a building that was immediately recognizable as a prison 一看就知道是監獄的建築 / This tree is always recognizable by its extremely beautiful silvery bark. 這種樹很容易辨認，因為它有著非常漂亮的銀色樹皮。/ John's car was easily recognizable. 約翰的車很容易辨認出來。/ The brand has a universally recognizable logo. 在哪兒都能認出這個品牌的標識。/ a language barely recognizable as English 幾乎辨認不出是英語的語言/ The project's benefits were recognizable to all interest groups. 所有利益集團都能看出這一專案的好處。
           
<span class="vocabulary">**distinguishable**</span> [dɪˈstɪŋgwɪʃəbl]
<span class="definition">adj. 1 ~ (from sb/sth) 可與…區別開的：</span>The male bird is easily distinguishable from the female. 這種鳥容易辨認雌雄。/ The animal is easily distinguishable by the black stripes above its eye. 這類動物很容易透過其眼睛上方的黑色條紋識別出來。/ features that make their products distinguishable from those of their rivals 使他們的產品區別於競爭對手產品的特色 / This is vintage port, and it is distinguishable by its deep red colour. 這是佳釀葡萄酒，它有特別的深紅色。<span class="definition">2（在難以看到或聽到時）能看到或聽到的，如可辨認出的、可辨識出的：</span>He put his ear to the floor and heard angry shouts, but no words were distinguishable. 他把耳朵貼在地板上，聽到了怒吼，但是聽不清所說的內容。/ It was getting light and shapes were more distinguishable. 天亮起來了，萬物變得更清晰了。/ The coast was barely distinguishable in the mist. 在霧中很難看清海岸。

<span class="vocabulary">**discernible**</span> [dɪˈsɜ:n; 美 -ˈsɜ:rn]
<span class="definition">adj. 1 看得清楚的：</span>Far away the outline of the island is just discernible. 遠處，島嶼輪廓僅依稀可辨。/ His face was barely discernible in the gloom. 在黑暗中很難看得清他的臉。<span class="definition">2 辨別得出的：</span>There is often no discernible difference between rival brands. 相互競爭的品牌之間往往看不出明顯的區別。/ The difference between the two is readily discernible. 不難看出兩者之間的差別。/ There has been no discernible overall trend since 1975. 自 1975 年以來一直沒有一個明朗的大趨勢。

相關詞義延伸：
· distinguish還可指能夠聽到某事物；
· identification還可指識別…的存在、重要性或真實性，即確認。

· read可指識字，讀懂。也可指識樂譜；
· separate可表示使某人或某事物在某方面不同於另外的某人或某事物，即區別，區分。不如distinguish常用；
· discernible可指看得清楚的。還可指辨別得出的。
