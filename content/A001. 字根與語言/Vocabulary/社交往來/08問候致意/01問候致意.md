☀ <span class="category">**問候致意：**</span>
<span class="vocabulary">**greet**</span> [ɡri:t] 
<span class="definition">vt. greet sb (with sth) 和某人打招呼、問好；或歡迎某人：</span>He greeted all the guests warmly as they arrived. 客人到達時他都熱情接待。/ The president rose to greet his guests. 總統起身迎接客人。/ She greeted us with a smile. 她微笑著向我們打招呼。/ Stella greeted her mother coolly. 斯特拉冷淡地跟她的母親打了個招呼。/ My parents were waiting to greet us at the door. 我父母正在門口迎候我們。

<span class="vocabulary">**greeting**</span> ['ɡri:tɪŋ] 
<span class="definition">n. 1 [C, U] 為問候或歡迎某人所說的話或所做的事，即問候、迎接、致意：</span>She waved a friendly greeting. 她友好地揮手致意。/ to nod a polite greeting 禮貌地點頭致意 / to exchange greetings 相互致意 / He jumped to his feet and called out a greeting. 他跳起來大聲地打招呼。<span class="definition">2 [pl.] greetings願某人健康、快樂等之類的話語，即問候的話，祝詞，賀詞：</span>birthday, Christmas, New Year greetings 生日祝詞；聖誕祝詞；新年祝詞 / My mother sends her greetings to you all. 我母親向大家問好。/ We extend our greetings to you. 我們向你致以問候 / greeting card（美）, greetings card（英）賀卡

<span class="vocabulary">**acknowledgement**</span> [əkˈnɒlɪdʒmənt; 美 əkˈnɑ:l-]
<span class="definition">n. [U] 打招呼的表示（如微笑等）：</span>She gave me a smile of acknowledgement (= showed that she had seen and recognized me). 她向我微笑打招呼。/ He smiled in acknowledgement and gave a bow. 他微笑示意，又躬身行了個禮。/ Mitzi nodded a perfunctory acknowledgement as her glass was filled. 杯子被倒滿時米芝敷衍性地點頭示意。/ She merely nodded acknowledgement of his statement. 她只是點點頭對他的話表示在聽他講話。/ He raised his hand to her in acknowledgement. 他向她舉手打招呼。
 
<span class="vocabulary">**regards**</span> [rɪ'ɡɑːdz] 
<span class="definition">n. [pl.] regards用於信函結尾，向某人表達良好祝願；或用於讓某人轉達對不在場的某人的良好祝願，即致意，問候。是一種敬語：</span>With kind regards, Yours … 謹此致意，…敬上 / Give your brother my regards when you see him. 看到你哥哥時，代我向他問好。/ The letter ended, ‘Kindest regards, Tom.’ 信以“謹此致意，湯姆敬上”結尾。/ David sends his warmest regards to your parents. 戴維向您的父母致以最熱烈的問候。
                      
<span class="vocabulary">**compliment**</span> [ˈkɒmplɪmənt; 美 ˈkɑ:m-]
<span class="definition">n. [pl.] [正式] compliments可指有禮貌的言辭或良好的祝願，尤用於表達讚美或敬仰。如致意、問候、祝賀：</span>Compliments of the season! (= for Christmas or the New Year)（英）謹致節日的祝賀！（聖誕節或新年時的賀詞）/ Please accept these flowers with the compliments of (= as a gift from) the manager. 請接受經理送的鮮花。/ On my bedside table awaited, with the compliments of the management, an appetizing breakfast. 一份讓人垂涎的早餐擺在我的床頭桌上，那是管理人員的一點小意思。/ Please give this to your boss with my compliments. 請把這個給你的老闆，算是我的一點心意。/ My compliments to the chef!（= to show that you like a particular dish）請向廚師代為致意！/ Give my compliments to your lovely wife when you write home. 寫信回家的時候，代我問候你可愛的妻子。/ compliments slip（英）致意便條 / The only enclosure was a formal compliments slip from the accounts department. 僅隨函附上一張財務部的正式禮帖。/ All guests will receive a bottle of champagne with our compliments. 所有來賓都將得到我們贈送的一瓶香檳。

<span class="vocabulary">**love**</span> [lʌv] 
<span class="definition">n. [U] 由中間人轉達給第三者的問候：</span>Please give her my love. 請代我問候她。

<span class="vocabulary">**acknowledge**</span> [ək'nɒlɪdӡ] 
<span class="definition">vt. 透過微笑、揮手等方式來表明你已注意到了某人或某事物，可稱為致意：</span>I was standing right next to her, but she didn’t even acknowledge me. 我就站在她身邊，可是她理都不理我。

<span class="vocabulary">**salute**</span> [sə'lu:t, -'lju:t] 
<span class="definition">vt. [正式] 向…致意，相當於acknowledge：</span>The players saluted the fans before leaving the field. 球員在退場前向球迷致意。<span class="definition">n. [C, U]（言語或行為上的）致意：</span>He raised his hat as a friendly salute. 他舉帽親切致意。
           
<span class="vocabulary">**tribute**</span> [ˈtrɪbju:t]
<span class="definition">n. [U, C] ~ (to sb) 對逝者表達敬意或仰慕的一個行為、陳述或禮物：</span>floral tributes (= gifts of flowers at a funeral)（英）葬禮獻花 / Floral tributes were piled up outside the church. 教堂外面放滿了敬獻的花圈。/ The prime minister led the tributes to ‘a great statesman and a decent man’. 首相帶領大家向“一位偉大的政治家和正直的人”致敬。
