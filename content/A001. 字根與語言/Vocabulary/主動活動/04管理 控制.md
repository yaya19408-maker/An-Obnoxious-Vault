☀ <span class="category">**管理、控制、支配的動詞：**</span>
<span class="vocabulary">**manage**</span> ['mænɪdӡ] 
<span class="definition">vt. 1 明智地使用金錢、時間、資訊等：</span>Don’t tell me how to manage my affairs. 用不著你來告訴我怎樣管我自己的事。/ to manage your data 管理你的資料 / to manage your time 管理你的時間 / The condition can be easily managed by simple dietary adjustments. 這種病可以透過簡單的飲食調整輕鬆加以管控。<span class="definition">2 使…處於控制之下：</span>to manage an unruly child 管住一個桀驁不馴的孩子 <span class="definition">vt.＆vi. 管理、負責或控制（一家公司、一支團隊、一個組織等）。是此義的一個基礎且常規的用詞，含義最具有概括性，也最為常用：</span>to manage a factory, to manage a hotel, to manage a soccer team 管理工廠；管理酒店；給足球隊當教練 / to manage a department, to manage a project 負責一個部門；負責一個專案 / an exchange rate system that will be tightly managed by the central bank 受中央銀行嚴密監管的匯率體系 / to manage your portfolio 管理你的投資組合
           
<span class="vocabulary">**administer**</span> [ədˈmɪnɪstə(r)]
<span class="definition">vt. [常用被動式] 管理（公司、組織、機構等）；治理（國家等）：</span>to administer a charity, to administer a fund, to administer a school 管理一家慈善機構；管理一項基金；管理一所學校 / The pension funds are administered by commercial banks. 養老基金由商業銀行經管。/ The plan calls for the UN to administer the country until elections can be held. 該方案呼籲聯合國來管理該國，直到能進行選舉為止。/ The legislation was to be centrally administered by the Board of Education. 立法將由教育委員會統一管理。
           
<span class="vocabulary">**regulate**</span> [ˈregjuleɪt]
<span class="definition">vt.&vi.（尤指透過規章制度、規則）控制、約束、管理：</span>The activities of credit companies are regulated by law. 信貸公司的業務受法律的制約。/ It is up to the regulating authority to put the measures into effect. 應該由管理部門落實這些措施。/ The powers of the European Commission to regulate competition are increasing. 歐盟委員會對競爭的監管權越來越大。/ The use of these chemicals is strictly regulated. 這些化學品的使用受到嚴格管制。/ Tobacco is a federally regulated product. 菸草是屬於聯邦監管的產品。/ a code of conduct intended to regulate press reporting on the royal family 為管控媒體報道王室資訊而訂立的一套行為規範

<span class="vocabulary">**control**</span> [kən'trəʊl] 
<span class="definition">vt. 1 對一個人、公司、國家等擁有權力（從而能夠對其行為或事務進行決策），具體可稱為控制、支配、掌管、指揮等：</span>By the age of 21 he controlled the company. 他21歲就掌管了公司。/ The whole territory is now controlled by the army. 現在全境都在軍隊的控制之下。/ Can’t you control your children? 你就不能管管你這些孩子？/ Expenditure within the company is tightly controlled. 該公司內部的開支受到嚴格的控制。<span class="definition">2 限制某事物（使其按照某種方式發生），具體可稱為限制、管控、管理等：</span>government attempts to control immigration 政府試圖限制移民的措施 / Many biological processes are controlled by hormones. 許多生物過程都是由激素控制的。/ Conditions in the greenhouse are carefully controlled. 溫室裡的環境處於精細的控制之下。/ Parents should control what their kids watch on television. 父母應該限定孩子看什麼樣的電視節目。<span class="definition">3 阻止某事物蔓延（或惡化）：</span>Firefighters are still trying to control the blaze. 消防隊員仍在盡力控制火勢的蔓延。/ She was given drugs to control the pain. 給她服用了鎮痛藥。<span class="definition">4 操縱或控制（如一臺機器、一個系統等）：</span>This knob controls the volume. 此旋鈕調節音量。/ The traffic lights are controlled by a central computer. 交通訊號燈由中心計算機控制。/ Once we were in the air, I was allowed to take the controls. 我們一升到空中，我就獲准進行駕駛。/ The shutters can be electronically controlled. 百葉窗是電動的。/ to remotely control 遙控
           
<span class="vocabulary">**govern**</span> ['ɡʌvn] 
<span class="definition">vt. 控制（或影響）某人、某事物、某事的發生或執行等，常用被動式：</span>Prices are governed by market demand. 價格的高低取決於市場的需求。/ All his decisions have been entirely governed by self-interest. 他的所有決定都受利己之心的支配。/ We need changes in the law governing school attendance. 我們需要對影響就學率的法規做些改動。
           
<span class="vocabulary">**dominate**</span> [ˈdɒmɪneɪt; 美 ˈdɑ:m-]
<span class="definition">vt.&vi. 通常以一種令人不快的方式來控制某人或某事物，或對其施加過多影響，可稱為支配、左右等：</span>As a child he was dominated by his father. 他小時候由父親主宰一切。/ He tended to dominate the conversation. 他往往左右著交談的內容。/ She always says a lot in meetings, but she doesn't dominate. 她在會上總是滔滔不絕，但她的話沒什麼影響。/ He denied that his country wants to dominate Europe. 他否認他的國家想要控制歐洲。/ Women are no longer dominated by the men in their relationships. 在兩性關係中女性不再受制於男性。/ He never let his work dominate his life. 他絕不讓工作主宰他的生活。<span class="definition">vt. 在某事物中具有最重要或最明顯的特色：</span>The train crash dominated the news. 火車相撞事故成了最重要的新聞。/ to dominate the headline 成為最重要的新聞標題 / The book is expected to dominate the best-seller lists. 這本書預計會佔據暢銷書排行榜的榜首。/ countries where life is dominated by war 戰事頻繁的國家 / the issues which have dominated Irish politics 主宰愛爾蘭政治的一些問題 / The power station dominates the landscape. 該地區滿眼都是發電站。/ His writings on motorized warfare dominated strategic thinking in the 1930s. 他關於機械化戰爭的文章主宰了20世紀30年代的戰略思維。
           
<span class="vocabulary">**reign**</span> [reɪn]
<span class="definition">vi. ~ (over sb/sth) 在某種情況下（或某個技能領域）成為最佳或最重要者：</span>the reigning champion 冠軍稱號的保持者 / In the field of classical music, he still reigns supreme. 在古典音樂領域，他仍然是最為傑出的。/ Connors reigned as the world No. 1 for 159 consecutive weeks. 康納斯連續159周排名世界第一。/ Coco Chanel reigned over fashion for half a century. 可可·香奈兒在半個世紀裡一直主宰著時尚界。<span class="definition">vi. [文學]（想法、情感或氛圍等）在一地或一時成為最顯著的特徵：</span>At last silence reigned (= there was complete silence). 最後，萬籟俱寂。/ Confusion reigned about how the debate would end. 在這一爭論如何結束的問題上人們陷入了混亂。/ A relative calm reigned over the city. 城市相對平靜了一些。<span class="definition">vi. ~ supreme [文學]（某人或某事物）在一種情況下或一段時間中占主導地位：</span>Senator Sam Nunn reigns supreme over the Senate Armed Services Committee. 薩姆·納恩參議員主導著參議院軍事委員會。/ The bicycle reigned supreme as Britain's most popular mode of transport. 當時腳踏車是英國最流行的交通工具。

<span class="vocabulary">**rule**</span> [ru:l] 
<span class="definition">vt. 支配、操縱某人或事物，或以一種令人不快的方式影響或制約一個人的行動，常含貶義，且常用被動式：</span>The pursuit of money ruled his life. 對金錢的追求支配著他的生活。/ We live in a society where we are ruled by the clock. 我們生活在一個人人都圍著時間轉的社會。

<span class="vocabulary">**handle**</span> ['hændl] 
<span class="definition">vt. 控制或操縱（一臺車輛、一隻動物、一件工具等）：</span>I wasn’t sure if I could handle such a powerful car. 我沒有把握是否能駕駛功率這樣大的車。/ She’s a difficult horse to handle. 那是匹桀驁不馴的母馬。<span class="definition">vi. handle well/badly 駕駛（或控制）起來容易（或困難）：</span>The car handles well in any weather. 這輛車在任何天氣下開起來都很靈便。
 
<span class="vocabulary">**manipulate**</span> [məˈnɪpjuleɪt]
<span class="definition">vt. ~ (sb into sth/into doing sth) 為貶義用法，表示控制某人或某事物、事件、局勢，且常指暗中操控，而受控者還意識不到自己被操控：</span>She uses her charm to manipulate people. 她利用自身的魅力來擺佈別人。/ As a politician, he knows how to manipulate public opinion. 身為一位政客，他知道如何左右公眾輿論。/ to manipulate voters 操縱選民 / She knows how to manipulate the audience. 她知道如何駕馭觀眾。/ They managed to manipulate us into agreeing to help. 他們設法促使我們答應了提供幫助。/ Children try to manipulate you. 孩子們試圖擺佈你。/ genetically manipulated organisms 透過基因來控制的生物體 / to control and manipulate events 操控事件 / to manipulate an situation 操控局勢 / to manipulate the tax system to evade tax 操控稅務系統來逃稅          

<span class="vocabulary">**manoeuvre**</span> [məˈnu:və(r)]
<span class="definition">vt.&vi.（有時透過耍花招來）操控一種形勢：</span>She manoeuvred her way to the top of the company. 她施展手腕使自己進入了公司最高領導層。/ He brilliantly manoeuvred himself back to power. 他依靠手腕巧妙地重掌權力。/ The authorities have to manoeuvre the markets into demanding a cut in interest rates. 當局不得不干預市場，迫其提出削減利率的要求。/ The new laws have left us little room to manoeuvre (= not much opportunity to change or influence a situation). 新法律沒給我們留下多少迴旋的餘地。

<span class="vocabulary">**command**</span> [kə'mɑːnd] 
<span class="definition">vt. 正式用法，指控制或掌握…，擁有…可供使用：</span>The party was no longer able to command a majority in Parliament. 該黨已不再能夠在議會中佔有多數席位。/ the power and finances commanded by the police 警方掌握的權力和資金

<span class="vocabulary">**master**</span> ['mɑːstə] 
<span class="definition">vt. 1 設法控制住情緒，即掌控情緒：</span>to master his temper 控制住他的脾氣 <span class="definition">2 控制住一個動物或人：</span>瞭解即可 <span class="definition">3 掌控一種困難的局勢：</span>to master the situation 掌控局勢

<span class="vocabulary">**take**</span> [teɪk] 
<span class="definition">vt. take sth (from sb) 奪取或佔領一個地方，抓住一個人，控制住某事物：</span>The rebels succeeded in taking the town. 反叛者攻佔了那個城鎮。/ The state has taken control of the company. 政府已經接管了這家公司。/ The rebels took him prisoner. 反叛者把他俘虜了。
           
<span class="vocabulary">**cling**</span> [klɪŋ]
<span class="definition">vi. ~ (to sth) 抓住（職位或財產）不放：</span>He appears determined to cling to power. 看來他決心要抓住權力不放。/ Another minister clung on with a majority of only 18. 另一位部長僅以18票的微弱優勢保住了職位。

<span class="vocabulary">**direct**</span> [dɪ'rekt, daɪ-] 
<span class="definition">vt. 控制或管理某人或某事：</span>to direct the project 管理該工程 / direct day-to-day operations 管理日常工作

<span class="vocabulary">**lead**</span> [li:d] 
<span class="definition">vt.＆vi. 控制；掌管：</span>to lead a discussion 主持討論

<span class="vocabulary">**determine**</span> [dɪ'tə:mɪn] 
<span class="definition">vt. [正式] 使某事以某種方式發生；或使某事成為某種型別。如決定、支配、影響、形成等：</span>determining factors 決定因素 / Upbringing plays an important part in determining a person’s character. 後天培養對於一個人性格的形成起著重要作用。/ a decision which would ultimately determine the fate of the project 會最終決定該專案命運的一項決策 / Reproductive success is directly determined by attractiveness to the female. 繁殖成功與否直接取決於對雌性的吸引力。
           
<span class="vocabulary">**ordain**</span> [ɔ:ˈdeɪn; 美 ɔ:rˈd-]
<span class="definition">vt. [正式]（神、法律或命運）主宰、掌控或決定某事：</span>Fate had ordained that they would never meet again. 他們命裡註定永遠不會再相見。/ Destiny has ordained that they are who they are. 命運已經決定了他們的性格為人。/ His rule was ordained by heaven. 他的統治乃是天命。
           
<span class="vocabulary">**tame**</span> [teɪm]
<span class="definition">vt. 控制、駕馭（危險、難控制或可能造成麻煩的某人或某事物）：</span>Two regiments of cavalry were called out to tame the crowds. 調集了兩個裝甲兵團去威懾、控制人群。/ She made strenuous efforts to tame her anger. 她竭力壓制心頭怒火。
           
<span class="vocabulary">**discipline**</span> [ˈdɪsəplɪn]
<span class="definition">vt. ~ yourself 控制自己的行為、嚴格要求自己：</span>He disciplined himself to exercise at least three times a week. 他規定自己每週至少鍛鍊三次。/ Out on the course you must discipline yourself to let go of detailed theory. 在高爾夫球場上，你必須訓練自己做到不拘泥於細枝末節的理論。/ I'm very good at disciplining myself. 我能做到嚴格自律。/ Dieting is a matter of disciplining yourself. 節食是自我控制的問題。

<span class="vocabulary">**mismanage**</span> [ˌmɪsˈmænɪdʒ]
<span class="definition">vt. 對某事管理不善：</span>75% of voters think the President has mismanaged the economy. 75%的選民認為總統對經濟管理不善。/ The prison service has been badly mismanaged in recent years. 近年來，監獄部門的管理極為混亂。

相關詞義延伸：
· dominate還可指（建築物、山或其它物體）俯視或高聳於（一個地區）。也可指在體育比賽中佔據主導優勢；
· manipulate還可指熟練地控制或使用某事物；
· regulate還可指調節，控制（機器或系統的速度、壓力、溫度等）；
· tame還可指馴化；馴服（野生動物或禽鳥）；
· discipline還可指訓練、管教某人，讓其遵守規則或變得乖巧，尤指管教兒童。

周邊詞彙補充：
· wield可指擁有並運用權力、權威、影響力等，如行使；
· restrain可指控制、抑制…的增長（以防止其變得太大）。還可指約束、控制（自己的感情、情緒、想去做某事的衝動）；
· curb可指控制、限制，尤指控制或限制不好的東西；
· conquer可指徵服。         

☀ <span class="category">**管理、控制、監督的名詞和形容詞：**</span>
<span class="vocabulary">**administration**</span> [əd͵mɪnɪs'treɪʃn] 
<span class="definition">n. 1 [U]（企業、學校或其它機構的）管理，行政。包括計劃、組織和運營活動，強調一種組織性、系統性、協調性和程式性：</span>Administration costs are passed on to the customer. 行政費用轉嫁給了消費者。/ the day-to-day administration of a company 公司的日常管理工作 / the Sales Administration department 銷售管理部門 / poor administration 管理不善 / arts, business, educational, financial administration 藝術管理；企業管理；教育管理；財務管理 <span class="definition">2 [U] 對某事的執行方式進行的管控、監管或協調：</span>the administration of justice 司法
           
<span class="vocabulary">**executive**</span> [ɪgˈzekjətɪv]
<span class="definition">adj. 1 [只用於名詞前] 與公司或組織機構的管理有關的（涉及制定計劃及做出決策），即經營管理的：</span>She has an executive position in a finance company. 她是一家金融公司的領導層成員。/ executive decisions, executive duties, executive jobs 經營管理的決策；經營管理的職責；經營管理的工作 / the executive dining room 管理人員食堂 / A successful job search needs to be as well organised as any other executive task. 成功的求職與執行其他任何任務一樣，都需要良好的條理性。/ I don't envisage I will take an executive role, but rather become a consultant on merchandise and marketing. 我不設想自己將成為管理人員，但很希望成為商品營銷顧問。<span class="definition">2 [只用於名詞前] 有執行權的，即行政的：</span>executive authority 行政當局 / executive board, executive body, executive committee, executive officer 執行董事會；行政機構；執行委員會；行政長官 / the council's executive member for education（英）理事會的教育執行委員 / executive meeting（尤英）執行機構會議 / Executive power is held by the president. 執行權由董事長掌握。/ He sits on the executive committee that manages Lloyds. 他是管理勞埃德保險公司的執行委員會成員。/ Some executive members have called for his resignation. 一些執行委員會成員要求他辭職。

<span class="vocabulary">**charge**</span> [tʃɑːdӡ] 
<span class="definition">n. 1 [U] 主管某一職責。強調對該職位所管轄的事務負責：</span>She has charge of the day-to-day running of the business. 她負責掌管日常業務。/ to take charge of the financial side 主管財務工作 / He took charge of the farm after his father’s death. 他在父親去世後掌管了農場。/ The conductor has overall charge of the train. 列車長對列車負總責。/ She took personal charge of the files. 她親自掌管檔案。/ The child is under my charge until her mother returns. 這孩子在她媽媽回來之前由我照管。/ I need to feel more in charge of my life. 我需要能在更大程度上掌控自己的生活。<span class="definition">2 [C] 被照管的人，為正式和幽默說法：</span>瞭解即可

<span class="vocabulary">**conduct**</span> ['kɒndʌkt] 
<span class="definition">n. [U] [正式] conduct of sth 一種業務（或活動）的組織和管理方式：</span>He was responsible for the proper conduct of community life. 他負責社群生活的正常運作。/ the day-to-day conduct of the business of the company 公司業務的日常管理

<span class="vocabulary">**direction**</span> [dɪ'rekʃn, daɪ-] 
<span class="definition">n. [U] 管理。帶有指導的色彩，即上級對下級在各種事務上進行指導：</span>All such research is under government direction. 此類研究全部由政府管理。
           
<span class="vocabulary">**housekeeping**</span> [ˈhaʊski:pɪŋ]
