☀ <span class="category">**分析：**</span> 
<span class="vocabulary">**analyse**</span> ['ænəlaɪz]（英）（美analyze）
<span class="definition">vt. 為理解或解釋…而探究其性質或結構（尤指透過將其分成若干部分），即分析：</span>to analyse data 分析資料 / The results must be analysed in detail. 必須對結果進行詳盡分析。/ to quantitatively analyse 定量分析

<span class="vocabulary">**analysis**</span> [ə'nælɪsɪs; ə'næləsɪs] 
<span class="definition">n. 1 [U, C]（對事物的）分析；分析得出的結果：</span>The book is an analysis of poverty and its causes. 這本書分析了貧困及其成因。/ comparative, critical, qualitative, quantitative analysis 比較分析；批判分析；定性分析；定量分析 / financial, strategic, structural analysis 財務分析；戰略分析；結構分析 / content, data analysis 內容分析；資料分析 <span class="definition">2 [U, C] 對物質進行的分析（以發現其成分）：</span>chemical, genetic analysis 化學分析；基因分析 / The blood samples are sent to the laboratory for analysis. 血樣要送往實驗室進行分析。
           
<span class="vocabulary">**anatomy**</span> [əˈnætəmi]
<span class="definition">n. [C] [正式] 對事物、事件、主題、思想等的剖析：</span>an anatomy of the current recession 對當前經濟衰退的剖析 / This was a troubling essay on the anatomy of nationhood. 這是一篇剖析民族主義的讓人不安的文章。
           
<span class="vocabulary">**post-mortem**</span> [ˌpəʊst ˈmɔ:təm; 美 ˌpoʊst ˈmɔ:rtəm]
<span class="definition">n. [C] ~ (on sth)（尤指事情失敗後）反思或剖析，即事後分析、事後檢討：</span>to hold a post-mortem on the party's election defeat 對該黨競選失敗進行檢討 / The postmortem on the presidential campaign is under way. 正在對總統競選活動進行反思。
 
相關詞義延伸：
· analysis還可指精神分析（或療法）、心理分析（或療法）。

☀ <span class="category">**判斷、評判、衡量的動詞：**</span>
<span class="vocabulary">**judge**</span> [dӡʌdӡ] 
<span class="definition">vt.＆vi. 1 基於自己已有的資訊來形成對…的觀點，即判斷，認為。是“判斷”的常規用詞：</span>to judge from his words 從他的言語判斷 / The show was judged to have been a great success. 人們認為這場演出大獲成功。/ I am in no position to judge whether what she is doing is right or wrong. 我沒有資格評判她的行為是對還是錯。/ You will be judged by the work you have produced over the year. 將根據你一年來的工作業績對你進行評判。<span class="definition">2 對某人進行評價，尤指給出負面評價：</span>She likes judging others according to their appearance. 她喜歡根據別人的外貌對別人評頭論足。/ He was often judged solely on his looks. 人們常常僅根據長相對他進行評判。/ I think you’re judging her rather harshly. 我覺得你對她的評判過於苛刻。<span class="definition">vt. 估計或猜測…的大小、數量等：</span>to judge the weight of the box 估計這個箱子的重量 / I judged him to be about thirty. 我估計他三十歲左右。/ to judge distances 判斷距離
           
<span class="vocabulary">**gauge**</span> [geɪdʒ]
<span class="definition">vt. 判斷，尤指判斷人的感情或態度：</span>They interviewed employees to gauge their reaction to the changes. 他們與僱員面談以判定他們的應變能力。/ He tried to gauge her mood. 他試圖揣摩她的心情。/ It was difficult to gauge whether she was angry or not. 很難判斷她是否在生氣。/ as he gauged possible enemy moves and his own responses 他在盤算著敵人可能的動向和自己的對策時 / His mood can be gauged by his reaction to the most trivial of incidents. 從他對雞毛蒜皮的小事的反應就可以判斷出他的情緒。

<span class="vocabulary">**tell**</span> [tel] 
<span class="definition">vt.＆vi. 確切地判斷。是一個簡單用詞：</span>As far as I can tell, she’s enjoying the course. 據我判斷，她喜歡這門課程。/ The only way to tell if you like something is by trying it. 要知道是否喜歡一件事情，唯一的辦法是試一試。

<span class="vocabulary">**measure**</span> ['meӡə] 
<span class="definition">vt. 判斷（或估量）…的重要性、價值、影響力等，相當於assess：</span>The policy’s impact cannot be easily measured. 這一政策的影響難以估量。/ The school’s performance is measured against a strict set of criteria. 學校的工作業績是根據一套嚴格的標準來衡量的。/ Success cannot be measured merely in terms of the size of your salary. 成功與否不能僅以工資的多少來衡量。
           
<span class="vocabulary">**diagnose**</span> [ˈdaɪəgnəʊz; -ˈnəʊz; 美 ˌdaɪəgˈnoʊs]
<span class="definition">vt.&vi. ~ sb (as/with) (sth) / ~ sth (as sth) 判斷問題的原因：</span>to diagnose a serious fault in the engine 找出發動機裡一處嚴重故障的原因
           
<span class="vocabulary">**miscalculate**</span> [ˌmɪsˈkælkjuleɪt]
<span class="definition">vt. 誤判（形勢）：</span>She miscalculated the level of opposition to her proposals. 她沒想到她的建議會受到這樣強烈的反對。/ It's clear that he has badly miscalculated the mood of the people. 顯然，他完全沒有想到人們的情緒會是如此。/ The government has seriously miscalculated the economic effect of this policy. 政府嚴重地錯誤估計了這項政策對經濟的影響。
           
<span class="vocabulary">**misjudge**</span> [ˌmɪsˈdʒʌdʒ]
<span class="definition">vt. 對某人或形勢形成錯誤的觀點（尤指導致對此人或此形勢的不公平對待）：</span>She now realizes that she misjudged him. 她現在意識到她錯看了他。/ Perhaps I had misjudged him, and he was not so predictable after all. 也許我看錯了他，畢竟他不是可以讓人一眼看透的人。<span class="definition">vt. 對時間、距離等判斷錯誤：</span>He misjudged the distance and his ball landed in the lake. 他對距離判斷錯誤，球落到了湖裡。/ As I swung down from out of my bunk, I got dizzy and misjudged the distance. 從鋪位一躍而下的時候，我感到一陣眩暈，沒掌握好高度。

☀ <span class="category">**判斷、評判、衡量的名詞和形容詞：**</span>
<span class="vocabulary">**judge**</span> [dӡʌdӡ] 
<span class="definition">n. [usually sing.] 利用自己的知識或技能對…的品質進行評判的人，即鑑定人：</span>He’s a very good judge of character. 他非常善於判別性格。/ He is a good judge of musical talent. 他善於發掘音樂人才。<span class="definition">vt. 估計或猜測…的大小、數量等：</span>to judge the weight of the box 估計這個箱子的重量 / I judged him to be about thirty. 我估計他三十歲左右。/ to judge distances 判斷距離

<span class="vocabulary">**judgement**</span> ['dӡʌdӡmənt]（judgement為英式寫法，常見寫法為judgment）
<span class="definition">n. 1 [U] 判斷力：</span>a lack of judgement 缺乏判斷力 / an error of judgement 判斷失誤 / moral, political, professional judgement 道德上的判斷力；政治上的判斷力；專業上的判斷力 / He trusted his wife’s judgement. 他相信妻子的判斷力。/ He never allows any prejudices to influence his judgement. 他的判斷從不帶任何偏見。<span class="definition">2 [C, U] judgement (of/about/on sth) 對某事進行仔細思考之後所形成的判斷，看法，見解；也可指發表意見的行為：</span>objective judgement 客觀的評判 / personal, subjective judgement 個人看法；主觀判斷 / It’s difficult to form a judgement when you don’t have all the facts. 不瞭解全部事實很難做出評判。/ This latest case confirms my earlier judgement. 最近這件事證實了我先前的判斷。

<span class="vocabulary">**measure**</span> ['meӡə] 
<span class="definition">n. [C] 對…所做的判斷或衡量：</span>an accurate measure of ability 對能力的準確評判 / a good measure of reading comprehension 評判閱讀理解力的好方法
           
<span class="vocabulary">**measurement**</span> [ˈmeʒəmənt; 美 ˈmeʒərm-]
<span class="definition">n. [U, C]（質量、價值或影響的）衡量，評估，估量：</span>the measurement of output in the non-market sector 評估非市場部門的效益 / performance measurement 績效評價
           
<span class="vocabulary">**determination**</span> [dɪˌtɜ:mɪˈneɪʃn; 美 -ˌtɜ:rm-]
<span class="definition">n. [U, C] [術語] 查明；計算；測定：</span>age, sex determination 年齡判斷；性別判定

<span class="vocabulary">**sense**</span> [sens] 
<span class="definition">n. [sing.] 對某事物的理解或領會；對某事物的判斷力：</span>a sense of humour 幽默感 / a sense of purpose 目標感 / a sense of well-being 幸福感 / a sense of rhythm 節奏感 / a sense of timing 時機感 / business, dress, fashion sense 商業頭腦；服裝鑑賞力；時尚品味 / He lacked a clear sense of direction. 他方向感不好。/ She has lost all sense of direction in her life.（比喻用法）她完全喪失了生活的方向。
                      
<span class="vocabulary">**diagnosis**</span> [ˌdaɪəgˈnəʊsɪs; 美 -ˈnoʊ-]（複數形式為diagnoses）
<span class="definition">n. [C, U] ~ (of sth) 問題的確切原因的判斷：</span>瞭解即可
           
<span class="vocabulary">**miscalculation**</span> [ˌmɪskælkjuˈleɪʃn]
<span class="definition">n. [C, U] 對形勢的誤判：</span>grave, gross, huge, serious miscalculation 嚴重的誤判 / slight miscalculation 輕微的誤判 / political miscalculation 政治上的誤判

<span class="vocabulary">**critical**</span> [ˈkrɪtɪkl]
<span class="definition">adj. 有公正（或審慎）的判斷力的：</span>Students are encouraged to develop critical thinking instead of accepting opinions without questioning them. 要鼓勵學生培養判斷力，而非不加質疑地接受各種觀點。/ We need to become critical text-readers. 我們需要培養對文章的評判能力。/ Marx's work was more than a critical study of capitalist production. 馬克思的著作不僅僅是對資本主義生產的批判性研究。

相關詞義延伸：
· tell還可指確切地知道（或看出）；
· sense還可指良好的判斷、（對理智或實際行為的）清晰的認識、（即使不能看到、聽到等，但能）感覺到或意識到…。

☀ <span class="category">**推測、推斷、推理：**</span>
<span class="vocabulary">**conclude**</span> [kən'klu:d] 
<span class="definition">vt. conclude sth (from sth) / conclude (from sth) that ... 斷定…為（所聽到或看到的某事物的）結果，可稱為推斷出，或得出結論。是此義的常規用詞：</span>What do you conclude from that? 你從這件事中得出了什麼結論？/ He concluded from their remarks that they were not in favour of the plan. 他從他們的話語中推斷出他們不贊同此項計劃。/ The report concluded (that) the cheapest option was to close the laboratory. 這份報告認為代價最低廉的選擇是關閉實驗室。
           
<span class="vocabulary">**infer**</span> [ɪnˈfɜ:(r)]
<span class="definition">vt. ~ sth (from sth)（利用已有資訊）推斷、推理：</span>Much of the meaning must be inferred from the context. 大部分含意必須從上下文中推斷。/ It is reasonable to infer that the government knew about these deals. 有理由推想政府知悉這些交易。/ I inferred from what she said that you have not been well. 我從她的話語裡推斷你身體一直不太好。/ By measuring the motion of the galaxies in a cluster, astronomers can infer the cluster's mass. 透過測量星系在一個星系團內的運動,天文學家們可以推定這個星系團的質量。/ It is difficult to infer anything from such evidence. 根據這樣的證據難以作出任何推斷。/ From this study we can reasonably infer that this characteristic is inherited. 根據這項研究，我們可以合理地推斷出這一特性是遺傳的。
           
<span class="vocabulary">**inference**</span> [ˈɪnfərəns]
<span class="definition">n. [U] 推斷、推理：</span>If he is guilty then, by inference, so is his wife (= it is logical to think so, from the same evidence). 如果他有罪，那麼由此可以推斷他的妻子也同樣有罪。/ It had an extremely tiny head and, by inference, a tiny brain. 它的頭極小，由此推斷其腦容量也非常小。
 
<span class="vocabulary">**deduce**</span> [dɪˈdju:s; 美 dɪˈdu:s]
<span class="definition">vt. [正式] ~ (sth) (from sth)（基於已有資訊進行的）推論；推斷；演繹：</span>Can we deduce from your silence that you do not approve? 你保持沉默，我們是否可以據此而推斷出你不贊成？/ We deduced from his absences that he was not happy at college. 從他多次缺課可以推斷出他在大學並不開心。/ We can deduce a lot from what people choose to buy. 從人們選購的東西可以作出多方面的推斷。/ Alison had cleverly deduced that I was the author of the letter. 艾莉森已經聰明地推斷出那封信就是我寫的。/ The date of the document can be deduced from references to the Civil War. 這份檔案的日期可以從其中多次提到內戰之處進行推斷。/ The total amount can be deduced logically from the figures available. 總量可以從現有的數字中按照邏輯推斷出來。/ Using the evidence available, it is possible to deduce a lot about how these people lived. 用現有的證據可能推斷出不少這些人以前的生活方式。

<span class="vocabulary">**calculate**</span> ['kælkjuleɪt] 
<span class="definition">vt. 透過利用現有的資訊來推測、預測或形成觀點，相當於estimate。與conclude的“推斷”相比，calculate的“推測”的結果含有更多的不確定性：</span>They calculate that many species could be lost in this area. 他們預測很多物種可能會從該地區消失。/ to calculate the political consequences 預測政治後果 / He calculated that the others would not dare fight back. 他估計其他人不敢反擊。/ The incident had been coldly calculated to humiliate him. 這起事件是精心算計好羞辱他的。
           
<span class="vocabulary">**calculation**</span> [ˌkælkjuˈleɪʃn]
<span class="definition">n. [C, U]（利用自己的判斷力來）估計、預測、推測（做某事的結果）：</span>For the President, the calculations are equally difficult. If the peacekeeping operation goes wrong he risks appearing weak. 對總統說來，局勢同樣難料。假如維和行動出了岔子，他就會顯得無能。

<span class="vocabulary">**speculate**</span> [ˈspekjuleɪt]
<span class="definition">vt.&vi. ~ (about/on sth) 在不知道全部細節或事實的前提下形成觀點，如猜測、推測：</span>We all speculated about the reasons for her resignation. 我們大家都推測過她辭職的原因。/ It is useless to speculate why he did it. 對他為什麼這麼做妄加猜測毫無用處。/ We can speculate that the stone circles were used in some sort of pagan ceremony. 我們可以推測，這些石頭排成的圓圈是用於某種異教崇拜儀式的。/ Critics of the project speculate about how many hospitals could be built instead. 對該專案持批評態度的人在推測如果改作他用不知可以建多少所醫院。/ It would be unfair to Debby's family to speculate on the reasons for her suicide. 猜測黛比自殺的原因對她的家人來說是不公平的。/ Commentators are openly speculating on whether the accusation is false. 評論家們公開推測這一指控是不是誣告。/ The British press speculated wildly about his disappearance. 對於他的失蹤，英國媒體給出了各種各樣不著邊際的猜測。/ Both sides are free to speculate on the other's motives. 雙方可以隨意推測對方的動機。/ It would seem entirely reasonable to speculate that tribal loyalties influenced the outcome of the election. 對部落的效忠精神影響了選舉結果，這一推測很有道理。/ It would be premature to speculate as to the outcome at this stage. 這個時候就推測結果還為時尚早。/ There was no point in speculating about the possibility of them getting back together. 推測他們是否會重歸於好毫無意義。/ We can only speculate as to this man's identity. 對於這名男子的身份我們只能猜測。/ He refused to speculate on her reasons for leaving. 他不想去猜測她離開的原因。
                                 
<span class="vocabulary">**speculation**</span> [ˌspekjuˈleɪʃn]
<span class="definition">n. [U, C] ~ (that…) / ~ (about/over sth) 在資訊不足的情況下做出判斷，如推測、猜測、推斷：</span>There was widespread speculation that she was going to resign. 人們紛紛推測她將辭職。/ His private life is the subject of much speculation. 他的私生活引起諸多猜測。/ Today's announcement ends months of speculation about the company's future. 今天的宣告使得幾個月來關於公司未來的種種猜測就此煙消雲散。/ She dismissed the newspaper reports as pure speculation. 她說報紙上的報道毫無根據，純屬臆斷。/ Our speculations proved right. 事實證明，我們的推斷是對的。/ Whether or not he will get the job is mere speculation. 他到底能否得到這份工作只是推測而已。/ media, press speculation 媒體的推測；新聞界的推測 / meta-physical，philosophical speculation 形而上學的思辨；哲學的思辨 / Speculation was rife as to who would be chosen as a successor. 至於誰將成為繼任者，各種猜測很多。/ With a lack of credible answers, speculation is running rampant. 因為缺少有說服力的解釋，人們眾說紛紜。/ Speculation is mounting that a new tax will be introduced. 越來越多的人猜測新稅就要開徵了。/ Much speculation surrounds his role in the crisis. 關於他在這場危機中的角色有諸多猜測。/ amateur speculation based on questionable assumptions 基於站不住腳的假設所作的外行的推測 / There has been a lot of speculation linking the defender with a move to Chelsea.（link尤為英式用法）許多人猜測這名後衛將轉會到切爾西。/ He was dropped from the team amid speculation that he was sick. 在一片他患了病的猜測中，他被隊裡除名了。/ This issue has attracted a great deal of speculation among economists. 這個問題引起了經濟學家的許多猜測。/ There was wild speculation among the students as to the reason for postponing the lecture. 關於推遲講座的原因，學生中間有一些胡亂的猜測。/ There has been increasing speculation over the company's future. 對於該企業的未來有越來越多的猜測。/ This is just speculation on my part. 這只是我的推測。

<span class="vocabulary">**conjecture**</span> [kənˈdʒektʃə(r)]
<span class="definition">n. 1 [C] [正式] 猜測或推測出來的觀點：</span>The truth of his conjecture was confirmed by the newspaper report. 新聞報道證明了他的推測果然不假。/ That was a conjecture, not a fact. 那只是猜測，不是事實。/ There are several conjectures. 存在著幾種猜測。/ There are mere conjectures on our part. 這些只不過是我方的猜測。<span class="definition">2 [U] [正式] 猜測、臆測、揣測：</span>What was going through the killer's mind is a matter for conjecture. 兇手作案時心裡是怎樣想的，這個問題只能由人們去推測了。/ Whether she will run for a second term in office is a matter for conjecture. 她是否會競選連任還只是推測。/ Whether the business will survive another ten years is pure conjecture. 這家公司是否能再維持十年，現在只能揣測。/ There was a lot of conjecture as to the extent of her wealth. 對於她有多少財富人們有很多猜測。<span class="definition">vt.&vi. 相關詞義延伸：
· assess還可指核算（或核定、估算）某事物的數量或價值；
· assessment還可指已核算出來的且必定要支付的一個數額，即核定的付款額。

周邊詞彙補充：
· judge可指對某人進行評價，尤指給出負面評價；
· judgement可指對某事進行仔細思考之後所形成的判斷，看法，見解。也可指發表意見的行為；
· make可指估計，是一個簡單用詞；cost可指估算…的成本或價格；
· deliberation可指對某個嚴肅事情的正式商議、審議、評議；
· coroner可指驗屍官。
