☀ <span class="category">**將來 以後：**</span>
<span class="vocabulary">**future**</span> ['fju:tʃə] 
<span class="definition">n. 1 [sing.] the future 將來的時間；將來要發生的事：</span>We need to plan for the future. 我們需要為將來作好打算。<span class="definition">2 [C]（對…來說）將要發生的事：</span>Her future is uncertain. 她前途未卜。<span class="definition">3 [sing., U] 將來成功（或倖存）的可能性，即前途，前程：</span>He has a great future as a designer. 他作為一名設計師，前途無量。/ A great future awaits you. 大好前程在等著你。<span class="definition">adj. 只用於名詞前，（發生或存在於）將來的：</span>future generations 子孫後代 / future developments in computer software 計算機軟體的未來發展 / the future king 未來的國王
           
<span class="vocabulary">**pasture**</span> [ˈpɑ:stʃə(r); 美 ˈpæs-]
<span class="definition">n. [pl.] pastures更好的地方（或工作、條件）；更好的前程。算是一種比喻用法：</span>She decided it was time to move on to pastures new (= a new job, place to live, etc.).（英）她認定換個新工作的時候到了。/ I felt we were off to greener pastures (= a better way of life). 我覺得我們在邁向更好的生活。/ Michael decided he wanted to move on to pastures new for financial reasons. 出於經濟原因，邁克爾決意去尋找更好的前程。/ nurses seeking greener pastures overseas 到國外尋求更好前程的護士們

<span class="vocabulary">**tomorrow**</span> [tə'mɒrəʊ] 
<span class="definition">adv. 1 在明天，在明日：</span>See you tomorrow. 明天見。/ She’s leaving tomorrow. 她明天就走了。<span class="definition">2 在（尤指不久的）將來：</span>What is education going to look like tomorrow? 教育將來會是什麼樣子呢？<span class="definition">n. 1 [U] 明天，明日：</span>Tomorrow is Wednesday. 明天是星期三。/ the day after tomorrow 後天 <span class="definition">2 [U] 未來，將來：</span>tomorrow’s computer industry 未來的計算機行業

<span class="vocabulary">**after**</span> ['ɑːftə] 
<span class="definition">adv. 後來，以後：</span>soon after 不久以後 / ever after 從此以後 / Tomorrow. Or the day after. 明天。或者後天。<span class="definition">adj. [舊時] 只用於名詞前，以後的，後來的：</span>in after years 在以後的歲月中

<span class="vocabulary">**forward**</span> ['fɔ:wəd] 
<span class="definition">adv. 1 朝向未來，即向將來，往後：</span>Looking forward, we hope to expand our operations. 展望未來，我們希望拓展我們的業務。<span class="definition">2 從某時刻起（而屬實）：</span>Velazquez’s work from that time forward was confined largely to portraits of the royal family. 從那以後，委拉斯開茲的作品就很大程度上僅限於王室的肖像畫了。<span class="definition">adj. 與未來有關的：</span>the forward movement of history 歷史的向前發展 / a little forward planning 為未來作的一點打算

<span class="vocabulary">**ahead**</span> [ə'hed] 
<span class="definition">adv. 1（時間）向前面，在前面：</span>We’ve got a lot of hard work ahead. 我們往後還有很多艱苦工作要做。<span class="definition">2 在將來：</span>A much bigger battle is ahead for the president. 總統還將面臨一場更大的戰鬥。

<span class="vocabulary">**will**</span> [wɪl] 
<span class="definition">modal verb. 用於談論或預測將來：</span>How long will you be staying in Paris? 你將在巴黎待多久？/ I’ll call you tonight. 我今晚將會打電話給你。

<span class="vocabulary">**would**</span> [wʊd] 
<span class="definition">modal verb. will的過去式，用於轉述某人的所說所想：</span>He said he would be here at eight o’clock. 他說他將在八點鐘到達這裡。/ She asked if I would help. 她問我是否會幫忙。

<span class="vocabulary">**shall**</span> [ʃæl] 
<span class="definition">modal verb. [正變得過時] 同I和we連用，以談論或預測將來：</span>This time next week I shall be in Scotland. 下週這個時候我就在蘇格蘭了。/ In Chapter 3, I shall describe some of the documentation that I gathered. 在第3章，我將講述我所蒐集到的一些文獻資料。

<span class="vocabulary">**be**</span> [bi:] 
<span class="definition">auxiliary verb. be to do sth 用於說明後來發生的事：</span>He was to regret that decision for the rest of his life. 他終生都將後悔做出了那一決定。

<span class="vocabulary">**about**</span> ['əbaʊt] 
<span class="definition">adj. be about to do sth 可指即將做某事、將要很快做某事：</span>I was just about to ask you the same thing. 我剛才正要問你同一件事情。
           
<span class="vocabulary">**forthcoming**</span> [ˌfɔ:θˈkʌmɪŋ; 美 ˌfɔ:rθ-]
<span class="definition">adj. [只用於名詞前] 即將發生的、即將出版等的：</span>the forthcoming elections 即將舉行的選舉 / the band's forthcoming UK tour 樂隊即將在英國的巡迴演出 / a list of forthcoming books 近期將出版書籍的目錄
           
<span class="vocabulary">**imminent**</span> [ˈɪmɪnənt]
<span class="definition">adj.（尤指不愉快之事）很可能即將發生的：</span>the imminent threat of invasion 迫在眉睫的入侵威脅 / The system is in imminent danger of collapse. 這個體制面臨著崩潰的危險。/ There appeared no imminent danger. 眼前似乎沒有危險。/ They warned that an attack is imminent. 他們警告襲擊即將發生。/ Everyone had the feeling that disaster was imminent. 每個人都感覺到災難即將來臨。/ imminent death 即將到來的死亡 / The guard blew his whistle to warn of the train's imminent departure. 列車長吹哨向大家示警火車即將啟程。/ An announcement about his resignation is imminent. 馬上就要宣佈他的辭職。

<span class="vocabulary">**impending**</span> [ɪmˈpendɪŋ]
<span class="definition">adj. [只用於名詞前]（通常指不愉快的事）很快即將發生的的。詞義與imminent幾無差別，不過imminent還可作表語：</span>warnings of impending disaster 對即將到來的災難的預警 / impending disaster, impending crisis 即將到來的災難；即將到來的危機 / impending death 即將到來的死亡 / impending storm 即將到來的風暴 / impending famine 即將發生的饑荒 / On the morning of the expedition I awoke with a feeling of impending disaster. 出發遠征的那個早上，我醒來就有一種災難即將來臨的感覺。/ As I approached the exam room, I had a feeling of impending doom. 我走近考場時，覺得末日就要來臨。/ Rumours about an impending royal divorce were rife. 皇室即將離婚的傳言盛行。/ He'd spoken to Simon that morning of his impending marriage. 那個早上他已經對西蒙講過他即將結婚的事了。/ his impending retirement 他即將到來的退休 / impending visit to France 即將到訪法國
           
<span class="vocabulary">**prospective**</span> [prəˈspektɪv]
<span class="definition">adj. [常用於名詞前] 被期望（或被預期）很快發生的，即行將發生的、即將來臨的。相當於forthcoming：</span>They are worried about prospective changes in the law. 他們擔心即將修改法律。/ the terms of the prospective deal 即將達成的交易的條款
           
<span class="vocabulary">**subsequent**</span> [ˈsʌbsɪkwənt]
<span class="definition">adj. [正式] 在某事之後發生或到來的，即之後的、後來的、隨後的：</span>subsequent generations 後代 / subsequent version 隨後的版本 / Subsequent events confirmed our doubts. 後來發生的事證實了我們的懷疑。/ Developments on this issue will be dealt with in a subsequent report. 這個問題的發展將在以後的報道中予以說明。/ the increase of population in subsequent years 隨後幾年人口的增長 / Those concerns were overshadowed by subsequent events. 隨後發生的事使之前所關注的那些問題顯得無足輕重。/ They won only one more game subsequent to their Cup semi-final win last year. 繼去年在盃賽半決賽中獲勝後，他們僅又贏過一場比賽。

<span class="vocabulary">**on**</span> [ɒn] 
<span class="definition">adv.（預先安排的事）將發生：</span>The game is still on. 比賽仍將舉行。/ I’m sorry we can’t come--we’ve got a lot on. 很抱歉我們去不了——我們安排得太滿了。

<span class="vocabulary">**upon**</span> [ə'pɒn] 
<span class="definition">prep. upon sb 可表示（對某人來說）某個事件即將發生：</span>The wedding season is upon us. 婚禮旺季即將到來。
           
<span class="vocabulary">**brink**</span> [brɪŋk]
<span class="definition">n. [sing.] the ~ (of sth) 某個處境的邊緣：</span>on the brink of death, on the brink of disaster, on the brink of ruin, on the brink of bankruptcy 瀕於死亡；瀕於災難；瀕於毀滅；瀕於破產 / The country was brought to the brink of chaos. 該國瀕於混亂。/ Failure to communicate had brought the two nations to the brink of war. 溝通不暢使這兩個國家瀕於戰爭的邊緣。/ Their economy is teetering on the brink of collapse. 他們的經濟徘徊在崩潰的邊緣。/ He's pulled the company back from the brink (= he has saved it from disaster). 他使公司起死回生。/ The stress of her job had brought her to the brink of a nervous breakdown. 她的工作壓力讓她處於精神崩潰的邊緣。/ She was babbling, on the brink of hysteria. 她近乎歇斯底里地胡言亂語。/ animals hovering on the very brink of extinction 瀕臨滅絕的動物 / Scientists are on the brink of making a major new discovery. 科學家很快就會有新的重大發現。           

<span class="vocabulary">**verge**</span> [vɜ:dʒ; 美 vɜ:rdʒ]
<span class="definition">n. [C] on/to the ~ of sth/of doing sth 極其接近於某個狀態，如即將、接近於、瀕於：</span>He was on the verge of tears. 他差點兒哭了出來。/ She was on the verge of a nervous breakdown when she finally sought help. 她最終求助時已經瀕臨崩潰。/ The company is teetering on the verge of bankruptcy. 公司瀕於破產。/ This action brought the country to the verge of economic collapse. 此舉使該國經濟瀕於崩潰。/ These animals are now on the verge of extinction. 這些動物正瀕臨滅絕。/ on the verge of hysteria 處於歇斯底里的邊緣 / on the verge of ruin 瀕於毀滅 / He gave a broad hint that he was on the verge of leaving. 他明確表示他即將離開。/ They are on the verge of signing a new contract. 他們即將簽訂一份新的合同。/ The country was on the verge of becoming prosperous and successful. 這個國家即將走向繁榮富強。<span class="definition">vi. ~ on sth 極接近於一種極端狀態或條件：</span>Some of his suggestions verged on the outrageous. 他的一些建議都快到了荒唐的地步。
           
<span class="vocabulary">**loom**</span> [lu:m]
<span class="definition">vi. 重要或危險的事情好像很快就要發生，即迫近：</span>There was a crisis looming. 危機迫在眉睫。/ Another government spending crisis is looming in the United States. 另一場政府開支危機在美國一觸即發。/ We could all see that disaster loomed for the company. 我們都看得出來災難正向該公司逼近。/ With a recession looming, consumers are spending less. 經濟日漸蕭條，人們的消費也越來越少了。/ The threat of renewed civil war looms ahead. 內戰戰火重燃的威脅正迫在眉睫。

相關詞義延伸：
· future還可指期貨、語法上的將來時；
· ahead還可指（計劃、專案、組織等）快速向前發展，也可指提前、提早；
· shall還可指（通常按照規定或規律）某事一定會發生。

周邊詞彙補充：
· then可指將來的某一時間，即那時、屆時；
· set可指某事將要發生的。
