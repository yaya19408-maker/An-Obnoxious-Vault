☀ <span class="category">**厭惡、憎恨的動詞：**</span>
<span class="vocabulary">**dislike**</span> [dɪs'laɪk] 
<span class="definition">vt. [較正式] 不喜歡某人或某事物，即討厭：</span>Why do you dislike him so much? 你為什麼那麼討厭他呢？/ I dislike being away from my family. 我不喜歡同家人分開。/ There are very few foods that I actively dislike. 沒有幾樣食物是我真正反感的。

<span class="vocabulary">**hate**</span> [heɪt] 
<span class="definition">vt.＆vi. 非常不喜歡某事物，即厭惡、憎惡（某事物）。程度較深：</span>I hate butter. 我討厭黃油。/ I hate summer. 我討厭夏天。/ I hate cooking. 我討厭做飯。/ He hated to be away from his family. 他很不願意離開家。/ I’d hate anything to happen to him. 但願他平安無事。<span class="definition">vt. hate sb (for sb) 非常不喜歡某人，即憎恨、憎惡或仇恨（某人）：</span>The two boys hated each other. 那兩個男孩相互仇視。/ I hated myself for feeling jealous. 我恨自己的嫉妒心。/ He was her most hated enemy. 他是她最恨的人。
             
<span class="vocabulary">**feud**</span> [fju:d]
<span class="definition">vi. ~ (with sb) 與某人（或某群人）長期不和、爭吵不休、世代結仇：</span>He feuded with his ex-wife. 他和前妻積怨已久。/ Their families had feuded since their teenage daughters quarrelled two years ago. 自從兩家十幾歲的女兒兩年前吵了一架，兩家人就結下了仇。

<span class="vocabulary">**abhor**</span> [əbˈhɔ:(r)]
<span class="definition">vt. [正式]（不用於進行時）（尤指因道德原因而）憎恨、憎惡某事物（例如憎恨一種行為方式或思考方式）：</span>He was a man who abhorred violence and was deeply committed to reconciliation. 他是一個憎惡採用暴力而堅決主張和解的人。/ If nature abhors a vacuum, journalists abhor a transition, when there is little news to cover. 好比自然界拒絕真空一樣，新聞工作者則厭惡沒有多少新聞可供報道的過渡時期。
           
<span class="vocabulary">**disgust**</span> [dɪsˈgʌst]
<span class="definition">vt.（某件壞事）使人感到震驚且幾乎要嘔吐，如使厭惡、使反感、使作嘔：</span>The level of violence in the film really disgusted me. 影片中的暴力程度實在讓我反感。/ He disgusted many with his boorish behaviour. 他的粗野行為讓很多人都討厭他。
           
<span class="vocabulary">**resent**</span> [rɪˈzent]
<span class="definition">vt.（尤指由於感到不公而）憤憤不平、氣憤、憤恨、憎惡：</span>I deeply resented her criticism. 我對她的批評感到非常氣憤。/ He bitterly resents being treated like a child. 他十分厭惡被別人當孩子對待。/ She resented him making all the decisions. 她討厭什麼事都要聽他的。/ She resented his making all the decisions.（正式）她討厭什麼事都要聽他的。/ She resents her mother for being so tough on her. 她怨恨母親對她太嚴厲。/ I resent being dependent on her. 我很討厭自己對她的依賴。/ She bitterly resented the fact that her husband had been so successful. 她對丈夫如此成功感到憤憤不平。

<span class="vocabulary">**detest**</span> [dɪˈtest]
<span class="definition">vt.（不用於進行時）非常討厭某人或某事物，如厭惡、憎恨：</span>They detested each other on sight. 他們互相看著就不順眼。/ My mother detested him. 我母親對他很是厭惡。/ Jean detested being photographed. 瓊非常討厭拍照。

<span class="vocabulary">**loathe**</span> [ləʊð; 美 loʊð]
<span class="definition">vt.（不用於進行時）非常不喜歡某人或某事物：</span>I loathe modern art. 我很不喜歡現代藝術。/ The two men loathe each other. 兩個男人互相看不順眼。/ She loathed being the child of impoverished labourers. 她憎恨自己是貧困工人的孩子。

<span class="vocabulary">**abominate**</span> [əˈbɒmɪneɪt; 美 əˈbɑ:m-]
<span class="definition">vt. [正式]（不用於進行時）憎恨、厭惡某人或某事物：</span>瞭解即可
           
<span class="vocabulary">**revile**</span> [rɪˈvaɪl]
<span class="definition">vt. [正式] [常用被動式] ~ sb (for sth/for doing sth) 憎恨：</span>He was just as feared and reviled as his tyrannical parents. 他和他專橫殘暴的父母一樣為人懼怕和憎恨。

<span class="vocabulary">**shock**</span> [ʃɒk] 
<span class="definition">vt.＆vi.（髒話，不道德的行為等）使人感到受冒犯、氣憤或厭惡：</span>to shock people by saying outrageous things 透過說些不堪入耳的話而讓人厭惡 / The movie deliberately set out to shock. 這電影存心噁心人。
          
<span class="vocabulary">**revolt**</span> [rɪˈvəʊlt; 美 -ˈvoʊlt]
<span class="definition">vt. 令人厭惡：</span>The way he ate his food revolted me. 他吃飯的樣子讓我感到噁心。

☀ <span class="category">**厭惡、憎恨、敵視的名詞：**</span>
<span class="vocabulary">**dislike**</span> [dɪs'laɪk] 
<span class="definition">n. 1 [U, sing.] dislike (of/for sb/sth) 對某人或某事物的一種厭惡之情：</span>his dislike of his boss 他對自己老闆的反感 / mutual dislike 互相反感 / Consumers show a general dislike for genetically modified food products. 顧客普遍表示不喜歡轉基因食品。/ He developed a strong dislike for the dog. 他對這隻狗產生了極度的厭惡情緒。<span class="definition">2 [C, usually pl.] 你所不喜歡（或討厭）的一個事物：</span>I’ve told you all my likes and dislikes. 我喜歡什麼，不喜歡什麼，都跟你說了。
                      
<span class="vocabulary">**resentment**</span> [rɪˈzentmənt]
<span class="definition">n. [U, sing.] 對不公之事的憤恨、憤慨、怨恨：</span>bitter, considerable, deep, deep-seated, great, lingering, seething, smouldering/smoldering resentment 強烈的怨恨；深深的怨恨；極度憤很；揮之不去的憤恨；翻湧的怨憤 / growing resentment 越來越深的怨恨 / old resentment 宿怨 / She felt all her old resentment flaring up. 她感到她鬱積已久的怨恨爆發了。/ class, racial resentment（均尤美）階級仇恨；種族仇恨 / I felt no resentment towards/toward him. 我並不怨恨他。/ You seem to be harbouring/harboring some resentment against your boss. 你好像對你的老闆心存怨恨。/ She expressed resentment at being interviewed by a social worker. 她表達了對被一位社工採訪的憤恨。/ He struggled to hide his resentment. 他努力隱藏自己的怨氣。/ She could not conceal the deep resentment she felt at the way she had been treated. 受到那樣的待遇，她無法掩藏內心強烈的憤恨。/ Inequality breeds resentment. 不平等導致怨恨。/ Maggie was filled with resentment about her treatment. 瑪吉對自己所受到的對待憤恨不平。/ the growing - resentment against foreigners 對外國人越來越深的仇視 / But the problems of inflation and unemployment still cause a lot of resentment. 但是通貨膨脹和失業問題仍然激起了很多人的怨恨。/ The measures will fuel resentment among students. 這些措施將在學生中激起不滿。/ I felt great resentment at having to work such long hours. 對於不得不工作這麼長時間我深感不滿。/ the bitter resentment between the two brothers 兩兄弟之間的深深怨恨 / their resentment towards/toward each other 他們對彼此的怨恨

<span class="vocabulary">**repugnance**</span> [rɪˈpʌgnəns]
<span class="definition">n. [U] [正式] 對…的強烈厭惡或反感：</span>She was trying to overcome her physical repugnance for him. 她努力剋制對他非常強烈的反感。

<span class="vocabulary">**revulsion**</span> [rɪˈvʌlʃn]
<span class="definition">n. [U, sing.] [正式] ~ (at/against/from sth) 強烈的嫌惡或驚恐：</span>She felt a deep sense of revulsion at the violence. 她對這一暴行深惡痛絕。/ I started to feel a revulsion against their decadent lifestyle. 我對他們那腐朽的生活方式開始感到厭惡。/ Most people viewed the bombings with revulsion. 大多數人對爆炸事件表現出驚恐不安。/ their revulsion at the act of desecration 他們對褻瀆神靈的行為的憎惡 / His voice was filled with horror and revulsion. 他的聲音裡充滿了恐懼和反感。/ The killing caused widespread revulsion. 這一殺戮引起了公憤。/ moral revulsion（尤美）道德上的厭惡 / The children shrank back from him in revulsion. 孩子們厭惡地從他面前後退。/ public revulsion against violence in our society 公眾對我們社會上的暴力行為的憎惡 / He tried to conceal his instinctive revulsion at the idea. 他試圖掩蓋自己對這一想法本能的厭惡。/ He was filled with hatred and revulsion for everything about her. 他對她的一切都感到仇恨和厭惡。/ She seems to feel revulsion towards her own children. 她似乎對自己的孩子感到厭惡。/ a feeling/sense of revulsion 厭惡感

<span class="vocabulary">**hate**</span> [heɪt] 
<span class="definition">n. 1 [U] 對某人的一種非常強烈的厭惡之情，如憎恨、厭惡、仇恨，相當於hatred：</span>a look of hate 憎恨的目光 / hate speech 仇恨言論 / hate mail 詆譭信件 / She became a hate figure for politicians on the left. 她成為左翼政客仇視的物件。<span class="definition">2 [C] [非正式] 你所憎惡的一個人（或事物）：</span>Plastic flowers have always been a particular hate of mine. 我一向特別討厭塑膠花。/ pet hate（英）特別討厭的東西
                      
<span class="vocabulary">**feud**</span> [fju:d]
<span class="definition">n. [C] ~ (between A and B) / ~ (with sb) / ~ (over sb/sth)（兩個人或兩群人之間的）長期不和；爭吵不休；世仇；夙怨：</span>long, long-running, long-standing, old feud 世仇；宿怨；舊怨 / ongoing feud 繼續存在的宿仇 / bitter feud 不共戴天的世仇 / bloody feud 血仇 / petty feud 芥蒂 / blood feud 血親復仇 / Blood feuds added to the local crime rate. 家族血仇增加了當地的犯罪率。/ family, internecine, personal, private feud 家族恩怨；內部世仇；個人恩怨；私人恩怨 / a long-standing family feud 長期的血親復仇 / public feud 公眾的仇恨 / The incident started a family feud. 這個事件引發了家族世仇。/ a time to settle old feuds 解決舊怨的時機 / the feuds between rival companies 相互競爭的公司之間的爭端 / They had a long-running feud over money. 他們因錢的問題長期不和。/ his personal feud with the city authorities 他個人與市政當局的恩怨 / a long-running feud between the two artists 兩個藝術家之間的夙怨 / a feud with the neighbours 與鄰不睦 / a long and bitter feud between the state government and the villagers 州政府和村民間的久積深怨
           
<span class="vocabulary">**loathing**</span> [ˈləʊðɪŋ; 美 ˈloʊð-]
<span class="definition">n. [sing., U] [正式] ~ (for/of sb/sth) 強烈的憎恨、憎惡、仇恨：</span>She looked at her attacker with fear and loathing. 她盯著襲擊她的歹徒，既害怕又憎恨。/ Many soldiers returned with a deep loathing of war. 許多士兵回來時對戰爭都深惡痛絕。/ Deacon made no secret of his loathing of Bayldon. 迪肯毫不掩飾自己對貝爾頓的憎惡。/ She looked at him with loathing. 她厭惡地看著他。/ undisguised loathing 毫不掩飾的憎惡 / mutual loathing 互相的憎惡 / His face was filled with loathing. 他的臉上寫滿了憎惡。/ She felt an intense loathing for her boss.她對老闆極為反感。

<span class="vocabulary">**antipathy**</span> [ænˈtɪpəθi]
<span class="definition">n. [U, C, usually sing.] [正式] ~ (between A and B) / ~ (to/toward(s) sb/sth) 厭惡、反感：</span>They have a mutual antipathy to each other. 他們互相厭惡。/ There was a lot of antipathy between the two men. 兩個男人彼此很反感。/ Despite his personal antipathy to me, he was still able to be polite. 儘管他對我有反感，他仍能禮貌相待。/ a natural antipathy for people in authority 對當權者自然而然的反感 / his antipathy for his boss 他對老闆的反感 / I feel a profound antipathy to using any weapon. 我對使用任何武器都深惡痛絕。/ His antipathy towards/toward swimming dates back to childhood. 他從小就討厭游泳。/ a growing antipathy towards the idea 對這個想法越來越多的反感 / She'd often spoken of her antipathy towards London. 她常提及她對倫敦的反感。

<span class="vocabulary">**aversion**</span> [əˈvɜ:ʃn; 美 əˈvɜ:rʒn]
<span class="definition">n. [C, U] ~ (to sb/sth) 厭惡、憎惡：</span>He had an aversion to getting up early. 他十分討厭早起。/ Many people have a natural and emotional aversion to insects. 很多人生來討厭昆蟲。/ He has a strong aversion to dogs. 他非常討厭狗。/ I underwent aversion therapy for my addiction to smoking. 我為了戒除煙癮而接受了厭惡療法的治療。
           
<span class="vocabulary">**allergy**</span> [ˈælədʒi; 美 ˈælərdʒi]
<span class="definition">n. [C] 厭惡、反感：</span>I developed a temporary allergy to the company of couples. 我一時間很反感和情侶們在一起。
          
<span class="vocabulary">**disgust**</span> [dɪsˈgʌst]
<span class="definition">n. [U] ~ (at/with sth) / ~ (for sb) 厭惡；憎惡；反感：</span>She expressed her disgust at the programme by writing a letter of complaint. 她寫了封投訴信，表示對這個節目的反感。/ The idea fills me with disgust. 這個想法實在令我噁心。/ I can only feel disgust for these criminals. 對這些罪犯我只感到憎惡。/ He walked away in disgust. 他感到厭惡，就走開了。/ Much to my disgust, they refused to help. 他們不肯幫忙，令我極其憤慨。/ She wrinkled her nose in disgust at the smell. 她聞到那氣味，噁心地皺起了鼻子。/ He spoke of his disgust at the incident. 他談到了他對這件事的厭惡。/ A look of disgust came over his face. 他臉上顯出厭惡的表情。/ Mr Haynes shook his head in obvious disgust and walked off. 海因斯先生帶著明顯厭惡的表情搖了搖頭，走開了。/ She tried to hide the disgust that she felt. 她試圖掩飾自己的反感。/ Decent people were filled with disgust for whoever committed the crimes. 正派的人對任何犯下這些罪行的人都感到深惡痛絕。/ He threw her one look, then turned away in disgust. 他督了她一眼，就厭惡地把頭扭向了別處。/ shake your head in disgust 厭惡地搖頭 / Marion threw down the book in disgust. 瑪麗昂氣憤地扔下了書。/ They both looked with disgust at the men. 他倆都厭惡地看著那些人。/ I couldn't find the words to express my disgust at his actions. 我無法用語言來表達我對他所作所為的厭惡。/ expressions of public disgust over the affair 公眾對此事表達的憎惡 / People are showing their disgust with the existing regime. 人們表現出對現政權的厭惡。/ He gave a snort of disgust. 他厭惡地哼了一聲。/ Much to his disgust, he found himself sharing a carriage with a noisy young family. 令他十分厭惡的是，他發現自己與一個嘰嘰喳喳的年輕家庭在同一個車廂。
           
<span class="vocabulary">**distaste**</span> [dɪsˈteɪst]
<span class="definition">n. [U, sing.] ~ (for sb/sth) 不喜歡，如厭惡、反感：</span>He looked around the filthy room in distaste. 他厭惡地環顧著這骯髒的房間。/ Roger looked at her with distaste. 羅傑厭惡地看著她。/ She regarded the child with evident distaste. 她以明顯的厭惡眼光打量那孩子。/ a distaste for politics of any sort 對任何形式的政治的反感 / He professed a distaste for everything related to money. 他聲稱厭惡與金錢有關的一切。/ They are country people with a general distaste for all things urban. 他們是鄉下人，對城市中的一切事物普遍有些厭惡。/ She was trying not to show her distaste. 她儘量不表現出自己的厭惡。/ He couldn't hide the deep distaste that he felt for many of their customs. 他無法掩飾對他們許多習俗的強烈反感。/ She wrinkled her nose in mock distaste. 她皺起鼻子，裝出反感的樣子。/ Jim looked with distaste at the cockroach in his soup. 吉姆厭惡地看著湯裡的蟑螂。/ He couldn't hide his distaste at having to sleep in such a filthy room.要睡在如此汙秒的房間裡，他無法掩飾厭惡之情。/ Joe had a profound distaste for violence. 喬對暴力深惡痛絕。/ an expression of distaste, a look of distaste 厭惡的表情；厭惡的神情

<span class="vocabulary">**hatred**</span> [ˈheɪtrɪd]
<span class="definition">n. [U, C] ~ (for/of sb/sth) / ~ (towards sb) 仇恨、憎恨：</span>He looked at me with intense hatred. 他滿懷敵意地看著我。/ There was fear and hatred in his voice. 他的聲音裡透露著恐懼和仇恨。/ She felt nothing but hatred for her attacker. 她對攻擊她的人只有恨。/ a profound hatred of war 對戰爭的深惡痛絕 / The debate simply revived old hatreds. 這一辯論只不過再次挑起了夙仇。/ Her hatred of them would never lead her to murder. 她雖說仇恨他們，但絕不至於去殺人。/ My hatred for her is so intense it seems to be destroying me. 我對她恨之入骨，都快要無法自拔了。/ She shot him a look of pure hatred. 她滿含怨恨地狠狠瞪了他一眼。/ blind, irrational hatred 盲目的憎恨；不理智的仇恨 / class, religious hatred 階級仇恨；宗教仇恨 / mutual hatred 彼此間的仇恨 / She was full of hatred and bitterness. 她滿腔仇恨和辛酸。/ He has a deep hatred of the police. 他對警察懷有深仇大恨。/ He is accused of stirring up racial hatred. 他被指控煽動種族仇恨。/ Ignorance can breed hatred. 愚昧會滋生仇恨。/ Hatred flared up inside her.（flare尤為美式用法）她心中頓生怨恨。/ She stared at it in hatred. 她怨恨地盯著它。/ They were blamed for inciting hatred against religious minorities. 他們被指責煽動對宗教少數派的仇恨。/ the intense hatred between the two communities 兩個團體之間的強烈仇恨 / I felt no hatred for him. 我對他沒有任何怨恨。/ his hatred of women 他對女人的憎恨 / their hatred towards the oppressors 他們對壓迫者的仇恨 / a feeling of hatred 恨意
           
<span class="vocabulary">**animosity**</span> [ˌænɪˈmɒsəti; 美 -ˈmɑ:s-]
<span class="definition">n. [U, C] ~ (toward(s) sb/sth) / ~ (between A and B) 強烈的仇恨、敵意、憎惡、憤怒：</span>He felt no animosity towards his critics. 他對批評他的人並不心懷怨恨。/ the animosity of some locals towards asylum seekers 一些當地人對尋求避難者的憎惡 / personal animosities between members of the two groups 兩個集團成員之間的私仇 / Sir Geoffrey had no personal animosity towards the Prime Minister. 傑弗裡爵士對首相併無私人恩怨。/ There's a long history of animosity between the two nations. 兩國之間宿怨已久。/ Despite everything, she bore her former boss no animosity. 不管怎樣，她對她以前的老闆並沒有懷恨在心。/ They managed to discuss their past disagreements without animosity. 他們能夠不計前嫌地討論過去的分歧。/ The two rivals for party leadership insist that there is no animosity between them. 兩位競爭政黨領袖一職的對手堅稱他們之間沒有仇恨。

<span class="vocabulary">**malice**</span> [ˈmælɪs]
<span class="definition">n. [U] 對某人的惡意、怨恨：</span>He sent the letter out of malice. 他出於惡意寄出了這封信。/ She is entirely without malice. 她完全沒有惡意。/ There was a strong current of malice in many of his portraits. 他的許多肖像畫中都透著一股強烈的怨恨。/ There was no malice on his part. 他並無惡意。/ He bore me no malice.（bear尤為英式用法）他對我沒有惡意。/‘You're lying,’ he said, without malice. 他並無惡意地說道：“你撒謊。”/ I bear no malice towards anybody. 我對誰都沒有惡意。
