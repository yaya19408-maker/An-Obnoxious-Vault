☀ <span class="category">**指責、譴責、抨擊、批評的動詞：**</span>
<span class="vocabulary">**blame**</span> [bleɪm] 
<span class="definition">vt. blame sb/sth (for sth) / blame sth on sb/sth 認為（或表示說）某人或某事物對某件壞事負責，即責怪，指責，把…歸咎於：</span>She doesn’t blame anyone for her father’s death. 她沒把她父親的死歸罪於任何人。/ A dropped cigarette is being blamed for the fire. 一支亂扔的煙被指為引起這場火災的罪魁禍首。/ Police are blaming the accident on dangerous driving. 警方把事故原因歸咎於危險駕駛。/ Whenever something goes wrong, everyone blames it on me. 一出什麼錯兒，所有人都把責任歸在我身上。/ The government has been widely blamed for the crisis. 政府因這次危機而受到了廣泛指責。

<span class="vocabulary">**charge**</span> [tʃɑːdӡ] 
<span class="definition">vt. charge sb (with sth/with doing sth) 公開指責或譴責某人做了錯事或壞事，為正式用法：</span>Opposition MPs charged the minister with neglecting her duty. 反對黨議員指責女部長翫忽職守。
            
<span class="vocabulary">**chastise**</span> [tʃæˈstaɪz]
<span class="definition">vt. ~ sb (for sth/for doing sth) [正式] 批評、指責、責備某人：</span>He chastised the team for their lack of commitment. 他指責隊伍未竭盡全力。/ Thomas Rane chastised Peters for his cruelty. 托馬斯·拉納嚴厲訓斥彼得斯的殘酷行為。/ The Securities Commission chastised the firm but imposed no fine. 證券委員會嚴厲批評了那家公司，不過沒有處以罰款。          

<span class="vocabulary">**admonish**</span> [ədˈmɒnɪʃ; 美 -ˈmɑ:n-]
<span class="definition">vt. [正式] ~ sb (for sth/for doing sth) 責備：</span>They admonished me for taking risks with my health. 他們責備我不應拿自己的健康冒險。/ She admonished him gently, 'You should rest, not talk so much.' 她溫柔地責備他：“你要多休息，少說話。”
           
<span class="vocabulary">**reprove**</span> [rɪˈpru:v]
<span class="definition">vt. [正式] ~ sb (for sth/for doing sth) 指責、責備、譴責、斥責：</span>He reproved her for rushing away. 他責備她不該匆匆離去。/ 'There's no call for talk like that,' Mrs Evans reproved him. “沒有必要說那種話，”埃文斯夫人斥責他道。/ Women were reproved if they did not wear hats in court. 女性如果在法庭不戴帽子，就會受到譴責。
           
<span class="vocabulary">**condemn**</span> [kən'dem] 
<span class="definition">vt. condemn sb/sth (for/as sth) 針對某人或某事表達強烈的不贊成，常出於道德原因，即譴責，指責：</span>The government issued a statement condemning the killings. 政府發表宣告譴責這些兇殺事件。/ He was condemned as lacking integrity. 他被指責為不夠正直。/ She publicly condemned the deal. 她公開指責這樁交易。
           
<span class="vocabulary">**censure**</span> [ˈsenʃə(r)]
<span class="definition">vt. [正式] ~ sb (for sth)（常指公開地）嚴厲譴責某人：</span>The manager was severely censured for negligence. 經理因翫忽職守而受到嚴厲指責。/ He was censured for leaking information to the press. 他因洩漏訊息給新聞界而受到譴責。/ The ethics committee may take a decision to admonish him or to censure him. 道德委員會也許會決定對他進行訓誡或是嚴厲批評。/ I would not presume to censure Osborne for hating his mother. 我不會擅自批評奧斯本恨他媽媽這件事。

<span class="vocabulary">**denounce**</span> [dɪˈnaʊns]
<span class="definition">vt. ~ sb/sth (as sth) 譴責、斥責：</span>She publicly denounced the government's handling of the crisis. 她公開譴責政府處理這場危機的方式。/ The government was bitterly denounced for the emergency measures. 政府因緊急措施遭到了強烈譴責。/ The project was denounced as a scandalous waste of public money. 這項工程被斥責為揮霍公款，令人憤慨。/ The letter called for trade union freedom and civil rights, but did not openly denounce the regime. 這封信呼籲保障工會自由和公民權利，但沒有公開譴責該政權。/ German leaders denounced the attacks and pleaded for tolerance. 德國領導人譴責襲擊活動，懇請各方剋制。/ He was publicly denounced as a traitor. 他被公開譴責為叛徒。/ These new regulations have been widely denounced. 這些新規遭到廣泛譴責。

<span class="vocabulary">**scold**</span> [skəʊld] 
<span class="definition">vt.＆vi. [正式] scold sb (for sth/for doing sth) 因某人（尤指孩子）做了錯事而對其憤怒地講話，即責罵：</span>He scolded them for arriving late. 他嫌他們遲到，訓了他們一通。/ Rose scolded the child gently for being naughty. 羅絲溫和地批評了那個淘氣的孩子。
            
<span class="vocabulary">**rebuke**</span> [rɪˈbju:k]
<span class="definition">vt. [常用被動式] [正式] ~ sb (for sth/for doing sth) 指責、批評：</span>The company was publicly rebuked for having neglected safety procedures. 公司因忽略了安全規程而受到公開批評。/ The president rebuked the House and Senate for not passing those bills within 100 days. 總統譴責參眾兩院沒有在100天之內透過那些法案。/ She rebuked herself sharply for her stupidity. 她對自己的愚蠢自責不已。          
           
<span class="vocabulary">**reproach**</span> [rɪˈprəʊtʃ; 美 -ˈproʊtʃ]
<span class="definition">vt. [正式] ~ sb (for sth/for doing sth) / ~ sb (with sth/with doing sth) 責備、批評：</span>She was reproached by colleagues for leaking the story to the press. 她因為將這件事透露給新聞媒體而受到同事的指責。/ She is quick to reproach anyone who doesn't live up to her own high standards. 只要有人不符合她自己的高標準，她馬上就會加以指責。/ She had not even reproached him for breaking his promise. 她甚至沒有責怪他違背諾言。<span class="definition">vt. [正式] ~ yourself (for sth/for doing sth) / ~ yourself (with sth) 自責、內疚：</span>He reproached himself for not telling her the truth. 他因為沒有告訴她真相而自責。/ You've no reason to reproach yourself, no reason to feel shame. 你沒有理由自責，也沒有理由感到慚愧。/ We begin to reproach ourselves for not having been more careful. 我們開始自責起來，怪自己不夠小心。

<span class="vocabulary">**reprimand**</span> [ˈreprɪmɑ:nd; 美 -mænd]
<span class="definition">vt. [正式] ~ sb (for sth)（尤指具有權威者）（生氣而嚴肅地）訓斥、斥責、譴責（因為被訓斥者做錯了事）：</span>The officers were severely reprimanded for their unprofessional behaviour. 軍官們因違反職業規則而受到了嚴厲的斥責。/ He was reprimanded by a teacher for talking in the corridor. 他因為在走廊裡講話受到了一位老師的斥責。/ Her attempts to reprimand him were quickly shouted down. 她本想斥責他，但很快就被大聲喝止了。/ reprimanded by the judge 受到法官的訓斥 / She was severely reprimanded for accepting the money. 她因收受了這筆錢而受到嚴厲斥責。

<span class="vocabulary">**criticize**</span> [ˈkrɪtɪsaɪz]
<span class="definition">vt.&vi. ~ sb/sth (for sth) 批評、批判、指責、挑剔：</span>The decision was criticized by environmental groups. 這個決定受到了環保團體的批評。/ The government has been criticized for not taking the problem seriously. 政府因沒有認真對待這個問題而受到指責。/ All you ever do is criticize! 你就知道批評！/ His mother had rarely criticized him or any of her children. 他母親很少指責他或她的任何孩子。/ The minister criticised the police for failing to come up with any leads. 部長批評警方沒能找到任何線索。/ She has openly criticized the government. 她公開批評政府。/ Their record on human rights has been justifiably criticized. 他們的人權狀況受到了公正的批評。/ She criticized the system for being secretive. 她批評該體制不公開。/ The law was widely criticized as racist. 那條法律被認為有種族主義問題，廣受批評。/ The movie was criticized on the grounds that it glorified violence. 那部電影因頌揚暴力而受到批評。

<span class="vocabulary">**attack**</span> [ə'tæk] 
<span class="definition">vt. attack sb/sth (for sth/for doing sth) 抨擊，非難：</span>an article attacking the England football manager 一篇抨擊英格蘭足球隊主教練的文章
                       
<span class="vocabulary">**maul**</span> [mɔ:l]
<span class="definition">vt. 公開猛烈抨擊或批評某人或某事：</span>The cable-TV and health-care industries are both being mauled by government. 有線電視和保健業正在遭受政府的嚴厲批評。

<span class="vocabulary">**blister**</span> [ˈblɪstə(r)]
<span class="definition">vt. [美] 猛烈抨擊或嚴厲批評（某人）：</span>瞭解即可          

<span class="vocabulary">**slam**</span> [slæm]
<span class="definition">vt.（尤用於報章）猛烈抨擊（某人或某事）：</span>The famed film-maker slammed the claims as 'an outrageous lie'. 這位大名鼎鼎的電影製片人聲色俱厲地抨擊了這些說法，稱之為“無恥的謊言”。/ Britain has been slammed by the United Nations for having one of the worst race relations records in the world. 由於在處理種族關係方面的表現位列全世界最糟糕的國家之一，英國遭到了聯合國的嚴厲批評。
                      
<span class="vocabulary">**rap**</span> [ræp]
<span class="definition">vt. ~ sb/sth (for sth/for doing sth)（主要用於報章）（通常指公開地）嚴厲批評某人：</span>Some of the teachers were rapped for poor performance. 一些老師因為表現糟糕而受到嚴厲批評。/ Water industry chiefs were rapped yesterday for failing their customers. 昨天，水利行業的頭頭們因為怠慢客戶而受到批評。/ The minister rapped banks over their treatment of small businesses. 部長批評了銀行對待小企業的態度。<span class="definition">vt.&vi. 訓斥；責備；指責：</span>We rap the manufacturers on their knuckles if the toy is shoddy. 如果玩具質量低劣，我們會痛斥製造商。/ I joined the workers on strike and was rapped over the knuckles. 我參加了工人的罷工，因而受到了訓斥。

<span class="vocabulary">**blast**</span> [blɑ:st; 美 blæst]
<span class="definition">vt. ~ sb/sth (for sth/for doing sth) [非正式] 嚴厲批評或抨擊某人或某事物：</span>The movie was blasted by all the critics. 這部影片受到了所有評論家的嚴厲抨擊。/ Football: Taylor blasts Beck. 足球：泰勒炮轟貝克。/ The Department of Health and a top immunologist have blasted a report in last week's Sunday Times. 衛生部和一位知名免疫學家嚴詞抨擊了上週《星期日泰晤士報》的一篇報道。            

<span class="vocabulary">**lash**</span> [læʃ]
<span class="definition">vt. 非常憤怒地批評某人或某事物，即怒斥：</span>She went quiet for a moment while she summoned up the words to lash him. 她沉默片刻，尋思著抨擊他的話語。/ The report lashes into police commanders for failing to act on intelligence information. 該報告嚴斥警局指揮官沒有根據情報採取行動。            

<span class="vocabulary">**savage**</span> [ˈsævɪdʒ]
<span class="definition">vt. [正式] [常用被動式] 猛烈抨擊或批評：</span>Her latest novel has been savaged by the critics. 她最近的一部小說受到評論家的猛烈批評。/ The show had already been savaged by critics. 這場演出已受到評論家們的激烈抨擊。/ Speakers called for clearer direction and savaged the Chancellor. 發言人紛紛要求進一步明確方向並強烈地抨擊了該財政大臣。           

<span class="vocabulary">**bash**</span> [bæʃ]
<span class="definition">vt. [非正式]（通常指公開地）嚴厲批評、抨擊、痛斥：</span>Bashing politicians is normal practice in the press. 嚴厲批判政治人物乃是新聞界常事。/ The President could continue to bash Democrats as being soft on crime. 總統可能會繼續抨擊民主黨人對待犯罪活動心慈手軟。/ a liberal-bashing administration 打擊自由主義的一屆政府          

<span class="vocabulary">**chide**</span> [tʃaɪd]
<span class="definition">vt. [正式] ~ sb (for sth/for doing sth) 批評、責備某人（因其做了錯事）：</span>She chided herself for being so impatient with the children. 她責怪自己對孩子不夠耐心。/ Cross chided himself for worrying. 克羅斯怨怪自己瞎操心。/ He gently chided the two women. 他溫和地責備了那兩名女子。/ ‘Isn't that a bit selfish? ’ he chided. “那不有點自私嗎？”他責備道。
           
<span class="vocabulary">**castigate**</span> [ˈkæstɪgeɪt]
<span class="definition">vt. [正式] ~ sb/sth (for sth) 嚴厲批評、斥責：</span>He castigated himself for being so stupid. 他責怪自己太笨。/ Marx never lost an opportunity to castigate colonialism. 馬克思抓住每一個機會嚴厲譴責殖民主義。/ She castigated him for having no intellectual interests. 她斥責他沒有智識興趣。

<span class="vocabulary">**decry**</span> [dɪˈkraɪ]
<span class="definition">vt. [正式] ~ sb/sth (as sth) 嚴厲批評、譴責，尤指公開地嚴厲批評、譴責：</span>The measures were decried as useless. 這些措施受到指責，說是不起作用。/ He is impatient with those who decry the scheme. 對於那些反對該方案的人，他表現得很不耐煩。/ People decried the campaign as a waste of money. 人們譴責這一活動是浪費金錢。
           
<span class="vocabulary">**revile**</span> [rɪˈvaɪl]
<span class="definition">vt. [正式] [常用被動式] ~ sb (for sth/for doing sth) 斥責、痛斥：</span>What right had the crowd to revile the England players for something they could not help...? 觀眾有什麼權力因為英格蘭隊員們無能為力的事情而斥責他們？
           
<span class="vocabulary">**berate**</span> [bɪˈreɪt]
<span class="definition">vt. [正式] 痛斥、怒斥、嚴厲批評：</span>Marion berated Joe for the noise he made. 瑪麗昂嚴厲斥責喬吵吵鬧鬧。

<span class="vocabulary">**lecture**</span> ['lektʃə] 
<span class="definition">vt. lecture sb (about/on sth) / lecture sb (about doing sth)（尤指以令被批評者煩惱的方式來對其進行）批評，責備，訓斥，告誡：</span>to lecture me about my work 對我的工作指手畫腳 / The teacher lectured him about his not finishing his homework. 老師批評他未完成作業。

<span class="vocabulary">**knock**</span> [nɒk] 
<span class="definition">vt. [非正式]（尤指不公平地）批評或非難…：</span>The newspapers are always knocking the England team. 報紙總是攻擊英格蘭隊。/ I’m not knocking them: if they want to do it, it’s up to them. 我不是在說他們的壞話：如果他們打算做這件事，由他們決定。

<span class="vocabulary">**roast**</span> [rəʊst] 
<span class="definition">vt. [非正式或幽默] 對某人非常生氣；嚴厲批評某人：</span>瞭解即可

<span class="vocabulary">**needle**</span> ['ni:dl] 
<span class="definition">vt. [非正式] 故意使某人惱怒（尤指透過不斷地批評）：</span>Don’t let her needle you. 別讓她數落你。

<span class="vocabulary">**pan**</span> [pæn] 
<span class="definition">vt. [非正式] 常用被動式，嚴厲批評某事物（如一齣戲劇或一部電影）：</span>瞭解即可

<span class="vocabulary">**carpet**</span> ['kɑːpɪt] 
<span class="definition">vt. [尤英，非正式] 常用被動式，（由於某人做錯了某事而）朝其憤怒地講話，即訓斥，相當於reprimand：</span>瞭解即可

<span class="vocabulary">**rubbish**</span> ['rʌbɪʃ] 
<span class="definition">vt. [英，非正式]（美用trash）嚴厲地批評某人或某事物；或把其看得好像一文不值：</span>Five whole pages of script were devoted to rubbishing her political opponents. 整整五頁講稿都是對她的政敵的詆譭。/ Officials have simply rubbished all positive ideas. 官員們對所有的正面意見一律否決。
           
<span class="vocabulary">**trash**</span> [træʃ]
<span class="definition">vt. [非正式] 強烈批評某事或某人，如抨擊、譴責：</span>瞭解即可
           
<span class="vocabulary">**dump**</span> [dʌmp]
<span class="definition">vi. [非正式，尤美] ~ on sb 嚴厲批評，如非難：</span>瞭解即可
           
<span class="definition">vt.&vi.（在爭論中）反擊：</span>Marion's father counter-attacked by saying that Blaze's claims were cruel and untrue. 瑪麗昂的父親反擊說布拉茲的說法冷酷無情，根本是在撒謊。

相關詞義延伸：
· savage還可指（抨擊）猛烈的。

☀ <span class="category">**指責、譴責、抨擊、批評的名詞和形容詞：**</span>                
<span class="vocabulary">**criticism**</span> [ˈkrɪtɪsɪzəm]
<span class="definition">n. [U, C] ~ (of sb/sth) / ~ (that…) 批評；指責；批評或指責的話語：</span>The plan has attracted criticism from consumer groups. 這項計劃引起了各消費者組織的指責。/ There was widespread criticism of the government's handling of the disaster. 政府對災難的處理方式遭到了普遍的批評。/ People in public life must always be open to criticism (= willing to accept being criticized). 公眾人物必須隨時準備接受批評。/ Ben is very sensitive, he just can't take criticism. 本很敏感，簡直接受不了批評。/ to offer sb constructive criticism (= that is meant to be helpful) 提出建設性的批評意見 / I didn't mean it as a criticism. 我沒有要責備的意思。/ criticisms levelled at (= aimed at) journalists 針對記者的批評 / My only criticism of the house is that it is on a main road. 我對這座房子唯一的批評意見是它處於一條大路上。/ This policy had repeatedly come under strong criticism on Capitol Hill. 該政策在美國國會屢遭強烈批評。/ The criticism that the English do not truly care about their children was often voiced. 常有人指責英國人並不真正關心自己的子女。/ She made a number of valid criticisms. 她提了許多合理的批評意見。/ Two successive defeats have brought fresh criticism. 連續兩次失敗招致新的批評。/ media, press criticism 媒體的批評；新聞界的批評 / public criticism 公眾的批評 / The star faced a barrage of criticism for his actions. 那個明星的行為引來接二連三的批評。/ The report levels criticism at senior managers.報告將批評的矛頭指向高階管理人員。/ The government came under fierce criticism for its policies. 政府因其政策受到猛烈批評。/ The proposal is open to several important criticisms.這項提案樂意接受一些重大的批評意見。/ Scientists have raised strong criticisms of creationist ideas. 科學家們對神創論的觀點提出強烈的批評。/ She sought to deflect criticism by blaming her family. 她責怪家人，企圖轉移對她的批評。/ He finds it hard to take criticism. 他感到接受批評很難。/ The harshest criticism came from right-wingers. 最嚴厲的批評來自右翼分子。/ Criticism centred/centered on the lack of information provided. 批評集中在所提供的資訊不足。/ The main criticism about the information provided is that it arrives too late. 對所提供資訊的主要批評意見是這些資訊來得太晚。/ criticisms against the Church 對基督教會的批評 / They received criticism for continuing to supply the faulty goods. 他們因繼續提供劣質貨品而受到指責。/ We are bound to face criticism from both sides. 我們肯定會面臨來自雙方的批評。/ There has been serious criticism of the teaching methods used in the school. 一直有人嚴厲批評該校使用的教學法。/ We came in for some sharp criticism over this decision. 由於這一決定，我們受到了尖銳的批評。   
                      
<span class="vocabulary">**critique**</span> [krɪˈti:k]

<span class="vocabulary">**accusation**</span> [ˌækjuˈzeɪʃn]
<span class="definition">n. [C, U] ~ (of sth) (against sb) / ~ (that…)（對犯錯的人的）譴責、指責、批評：</span>No one believed her wild accusations against her husband. 無人相信她對她丈夫的無端指責。/ He denied the accusation that he had ignored the problems. 他否認別人對他忽視這些問題的指控。/ There was a hint of accusation in her voice. 她的話音中暗含譴責的意思。/ Kim rejects accusations that Country music is over-sentimental. 對於鄉村音樂過於感傷的指責，金表示反對。/ Another accusation levelled at the Minister is that he does not understand ordinary people. 另一個矛頭則直指部長不懂得體恤百姓。/ They sank into mutual accusation and recrimination. 他們落到了相互指責和歸罪的地步。/ She made a lot of thinly veiled accusations. 她多次提出指責，幾乎不加什麼掩飾。/ They have the nerve to level these accusations against one of our most respected members. 他們競敢將這些指責指向我們最受尊敬的一名成員。/ There seem to be a lot of wild accusations flying around. 胡亂的指責似乎滿天飛。/ You made a public accusation of misconduct against Saunders. 你公開譴責了桑德斯失職。
           
<span class="vocabulary">**condemnation**</span> [ˌkɒndemˈneɪʃn; 美 ˌkɑ:n-]
<span class="definition">n. [U, C] ~ (of sb/sth) 譴責、指責：</span>There was widespread condemnation of the invasion. 那次侵略遭到了人們普遍的譴責。/ There was widespread condemnation of Saturday's killings. 週六的殺人事件受到普遍譴責。/ The raids have drawn a strong condemnation from the United Nations Security Council. 突襲行動受到聯合國安理會的強烈譴責。/ The UN issued a condemnation of the regime. 聯合國發表宣告譴責該政權。/ He issued a blanket condemnation of all terrorism.（blanket尤為英式用法）他對所有的恐怖主義予以全盤譴責。/ public condemnation 民眾的譴責 / There's been no official condemnation of the bombing. 對這次轟炸、官方沒有作出任何譴責。/ I can only express my strong condemnation of this terrible outrage. 對這種惡劣行徑，我只能表示強烈譴責。/ Such treatment deserves condemnation. 這樣的處理應該受到譴責。/ The violence has drawn firm condemnation from all the main political leaders. 這種暴行招致所有主要政界領導人的嚴正譴責。/ Condemnation of this policy has come from all political parties. 所有政黨都譴責這項政策。
           
<span class="vocabulary">**indictment**</span> [ɪnˈdaɪtmənt]
<span class="definition">n. [C]（某事是對另一事的）控訴、譴責：</span>It's a sad indictment of society that policemen are regarded as easy targets by thugs. 警察很容易就成了暴徒下手襲擊的物件，這是一個社會的悲哀。
 
<span class="vocabulary">**censure**</span> [ˈsenʃə(r)]
<span class="definition">n. [U] [正式] 嚴厲的批評、斥責、譴責：</span>public censure 公開的譴責 / moral censure 道德譴責 / It was unavoidable that some artists would face official censure. 有些藝術家會不可避免地面臨官方的譴責。/ He could face censure from his colleagues. 他可能要面對同事的指責。/ Costs will have to be kept down if severe public censure is to be avoided. 要避免公眾嚴厲的指責就得壓縮開支。/ censure motion（英）譴責的動議 / a motion of censure, a vote of censure（均英）表示不信任的動議；表示不信任的投票 / a vote of censure on the government's foreign policy 投票表決譴責政府的外交政策 / Right-wing parties tabled a motion of censure against the government. 右翼黨派提交了不信任政府的動議。

[正式] [常用被動式] 侮辱、冒犯：</span>He hoped they would not feel affronted if they were not invited. 他希望如果他們沒有獲得邀請也不要感到受辱。/ an affronted expression 受到冒犯的表情
           
<span class="vocabulary">**abusive**</span> [əˈbju:sɪv]
<span class="definition">adj.（言語或人）辱罵的、惡語的：</span>verbally abusive 謾罵 / abusive remarks 惡言謾罵 / He was alleged to have used abusive language. 他被指稱使用過侮辱性語言。/ He became abusive when he was drunk. 他喝醉時就滿口髒話罵人了。/ He became openly abusive. 他公開大罵起來。
           
<span class="vocabulary">**foul**</span> [faʊl]
<span class="definition">adj.（語言）含有粗話、髒話和罵人話的：</span>foul language 髒話 / He had a foul mouth. 他愛說髒話。/ I'm sick of her foul mouth (= habit of swearing). 我討厭她一開口就罵人的那張臭嘴。/ He called her the foulest names imaginable. 他用最下流的話罵她。/ He was sent off for using foul language in a match last Sunday. 他在上週日的一場比賽中因為罵人而被罰下場。           

<span class="vocabulary">**oath**</span> [əʊθ; 美 oʊθ]
<span class="definition">n. [C] [舊時]（表示憤怒、驚異等的）咒罵，詛咒的話：</span>She heard the sound of breaking glass, followed by a muttered oath. 她聽到打碎玻璃的響聲，接著是低聲的咒罵。

☀ <span class="category">**誣衊、誹謗、中傷、詆譭的動詞和名詞：**</span>         
<span class="vocabulary">**defame**</span> [dɪˈfeɪm]
<span class="definition">vt. [正式] 誣衊、誹謗、中傷：</span>He complained that the article defamed him. 他申訴說這篇文章對他進行了誹謗。

<span class="vocabulary">**vilify**</span> [ˈvɪlɪfaɪ]
<span class="definition">vt. [正式] ~ sb/sth (as sth) / ~ sb/sth (for sth/for doing sth) 誣衊、誹謗、中傷、詆譭：</span>The agency has been vilified by some doctors for being unnecessarily slow to approve life-saving drugs. 該機構因對救生藥物的審批速度太慢而備受一些醫生的詬病。/ He was vilified, hounded, and forced into exile by the FBI. 他遭到了聯邦調查局的誣衊和追捕，被迫流亡國外。
          
<span class="vocabulary">**smear**</span> [smɪə(r); 美 smɪr]
<span class="definition">vt. 詆譭、誹謗、中傷某人的名譽：</span>The story was an attempt to smear the party leader. 這篇報道企圖玷汙該黨領袖的聲譽。/ an attempt to smear the director-general of the company 汙衊該公司主管的企圖 <span class="definition">n.（尤指政治上的）抹黑、詆譭、誹謗（以損害對方名譽）：</span>political smear 政治詆譭 / Democratic smear 民主黨的詆譭 / smear tactic 造謠中傷的手段 / They have conducted a vicious smear campaign against him. 他們針對他進行了惡毒的誹謗。/ a smear job on President Clinton（美，非正式）對克林頓總統的誹謗 / smear machine（美）進行誹謗的核心機構 / campaign smears against the socialist candidate 對社會黨候選人的造謠中傷 / The article cast a smear on the Minister for Health. 這篇文章對為生部長進行造謠中傷。/ He was a victim of a smear campaign. 他受到對方的毀謗攻擊。/ He puts all the accusations down to a smear campaign by his political opponents. 他把所有的指責都歸結為政治對手們的造謠攻勢。
           
<span class="vocabulary">**slander**</span> [ˈslɑ:ndə(r); 美 ˈslæn-]
<span class="definition">n. [C, U] 口頭誹謗；口頭誹謗罪：</span>malicious slander 惡毒的詆譭 / a vicious slander on the company's good name 對那家公司良好聲譽的惡意誹謗 / He's suing them for slander. 他控告他們口頭誹謗。/ slander suit（尤美）誹謗罪訴訟案 / She could file a slander suit against him for making that statement. 她完全可以因他那句話起訴他誹謗。/ He was found guilty of slander against his employers. 他因誹謗僱主而獲罪。/ Many teachers saw the statement as a vicious slander on their profession. 很多教師把這一言論看作是對他們職業的惡意詆譭。<span class="definition">vt. 口頭誹謗、詆譭、中傷：</span>He angrily accused the investigators of slandering both him and his family. 他氣憤地指責調查者詆譭他和他的家人。
           
<span class="vocabulary">**slur**</span> [slɜ:(r)]
<span class="definition">n. [C] ~ (on sb/sth)（對某人名聲的）誹謗、詆譭：</span>racial, racist, sexual slur 種族歧視言論；性別歧視言論 / anti-gay, anti-Semitic slur 反同性戀的言論；反猶的言論 / The comments cast a slur on her character. 那些評論詆譭了她的人格。/ He had made a series of racial slurs. 他發表過一系列種族歧視的言論。/ The crowd started throwing bottles and shouting racial slurs.（尤美）人群開始扔瓶子，並高聲地進行種族汙辱。/ The joke was seen as a slur against the mentally ill. 那個笑話被看作是對精神病患者的侮辱。/ a slur on his good name 對他良好聲譽的中傷 / This is yet another slur on the integrity of the Metropolitan Police. 這是對倫敦市警察局剛正廉明作風的又一次誹謗。<span class="definition">vt. 誹謗、詆譭（某人的名聲）：</span>瞭解即可
           
<span class="vocabulary">**libel**</span> [ˈlaɪbl]
<span class="definition">n. [U, C]（以文字形式進行的）誹謗、中傷：</span>He sued the newspaper for libel. 他起訴那家報社犯有誹謗罪。/ Warren sued him for libel over the remarks. 沃倫就此言論起訴他誹謗。/ criminal, seditious libel 誹謗罪;（煽動）誹謗政府罪 / libel action, libel case, libel lawsuit, libel proceedings, libel suit 誹謗訴訟；誹謗案件 / libel writ（英）停止誹謗令 / libel law, libel lawyer 誹謗罪法；受理誹謗案件的律師 / libel damages（英）誹謗賠償金 / He has issued a writ for libel against the radio star Michael Clery. 他釋出了不許誹謗廣播明星邁克爾·克利裡的令狀。/ If the jury decided there was a libel, it would have to consider its effect on Miss Smith's position. 如果陪審團裁定誹謗罪成立，那就必須考慮其對史密斯小姐的職位的影響。<span class="definition">vt.（在書報、雜誌等媒體上以文字形式）誹謗（某人）：</span>He claimed he had been libelled in an article the magazine had published. 他聲稱他遭到了那家雜誌發表的一篇文章的誹謗。/ The newspaper which libelled him had already offered compensation. 誹謗他的報紙已經向他提出支付賠償金。
           
<span class="vocabulary">**frame**</span> [freɪm]
<span class="definition">vt. [常用被動式] ~ sb (for sth) 作偽證陷害（清白的人）：</span>He claimed that he had been framed by the police. 他聲稱自己被警察設計陷害了。/ I need to find out who tried to frame me. 我得找出是誰企圖誣陷我。
