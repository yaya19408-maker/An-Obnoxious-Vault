☀ <span class="category">**從不 不常：**</span>
<span class="vocabulary">**never**</span> ['nevə] 
<span class="definition">adv. 從未；從不；未曾；絕不：</span>You never help me. 你從不幫我。/ Never ever tell anyone your password. 不要把你的密碼告訴任何人。

<span class="vocabulary">**seldom**</span> ['seldəm] 
<span class="definition">adv. 不常，（頻率）很少，相當於rarely：</span>He had seldom seen a child with so much talent. 有如此天賦的孩子他以往沒見過幾個。/ They seldom speak. 他們很少說話。
           
<span class="vocabulary">**scarcely**</span> [ˈskeəsli; 美 ˈskers-]
<span class="definition">adv. 1 勉強、剛、幾乎不、簡直不：</span>He could scarcely breathe. 他幾乎喘不過氣來。/ I scarcely knew him. 我幾乎不認識他。/ I can scarcely believe it. 我幾乎不敢相信。/ We scarcely ever meet. 我們難得見一面。/ Scarcely a week goes by without some new scandal in the papers. 報上幾乎週週都會登出新的醜聞。<span class="definition">2 用於表示某事根本不合理或不可能，即實在不應該、根本不可能：</span>It was scarcely an occasion for laughter. 笑得實在不是時候。/ She could scarcely complain, could she? 她壓根兒沒什麼好抱怨的，是不是？/ It can scarcely be coincidence. 它不太可能是巧合。/ Yesterday, however, his views seemed scarcely relevant. 然而，昨天他的觀點似乎毫無關聯。
                      
<span class="vocabulary">**scarce**</span> [skeəs; 美 skers]
<span class="definition">adv. [文學] 勉強、幾乎不；剛：</span>I can scarce remember him. 我幾乎想不起他了。

<span class="vocabulary">**hardly**</span> [ˈhɑ:dli; 美 ˈhɑ:rd-]
<span class="definition">adv. 1 幾乎不；幾乎沒有：</span>We hardly know each other. 我們彼此還不大認識呢。/ Nick, on the sofa, hardly slept. 尼克在沙發上幾乎沒睡。/ There's hardly any tea left. 沒有剩什麼茶了。/ Most of the others were so young they had hardly any experience. 其他人大多都很年輕，幾乎沒什麼經驗。/ Hardly anyone has bothered to reply. 幾乎沒人動嘴回答。/ She hardly ever calls me (= almost never). 她幾乎從未給我來過電話。/ We ate chips every night, but hardly ever had fish. 我們每晚吃炸薯條，但幾乎從不吃魚。/ Hardly a day goes by without my thinking of her (= I think of her almost every day). 我幾乎天天想著她。/ Hardly a day goes by without a visit from someone. 幾乎每天都有人來。<span class="definition">2 尤用於can或could之後，主要動詞之前，強調做某事很難：</span>I can hardly keep my eyes open (= I'm almost falling asleep). 我困得都快睜不開眼了。/ I could hardly believe it when I read the letter. 讀到那封信時，我簡直不敢相信。/ I can hardly believe it's been over eight years since you used to go camping at Cedar Creek. 我幾乎無法相信現在距離你過去常常去松溪露營的日子已經有8年多了。/ My garden was covered with so many butterflies that I could hardly see the flowers. 我的花園裡飛舞著很多蝴蝶，我幾乎看不見花了。<span class="definition">3 用於表明某事不可能、不合理；或用於表示某人的言行愚蠢：</span>He is hardly likely to admit he was wrong. 他不大可能承認自己錯了。/ We have not seen the letter, so we can hardly comment on it. 我們還沒有看到這封信，所以無從發表意見。/ It's hardly surprising she was fired; she never did any work. 她被解僱了不足為怪，她從來沒幹過任何工作。/ It's hardly surprising his ideas didn't catch on. 他的想法沒有被廣泛接受，這並不足為奇。/ It's hardly the time to discuss it now. 現在還不是討論的時候。/ You can hardly expect her to do it for free. 你不可能指望她無償地做這事。/ ‘Couldn't you have just said no? ’ ‘Well, hardly, (= of course not) she's my wife's sister.’ “你不能就說聲不嗎？”“喔，不可能，她是我小姨子。” / 'They all thought you were marvellous!' —'Well, hardly.' “他們都認為你很棒！”“哦，不可能吧！” / 'We could almost have seen it,' — 'Hardly, darling — in the dark and from a distance of a good hundred feet?' “我們幾乎都看到它了。”“不會吧，親愛的——天那麼黑，離得又至少有100英尺呢！”

<span class="vocabulary">**rare**</span> [reə] 
<span class="definition">adj. rare (for sb/sth to do sth) / rare (to do sth) 不是經常地被執行（或被看到、發生等），如少見的，罕見的：</span>rare disease 罕見的疾病 / It’s extremely rare for it to be this hot in April. 四月份就這樣炎熱是極其罕見的。
           
<span class="vocabulary">**uncommon**</span> [ʌnˈkɒmən; 美 -ˈkɑ:m-]
<span class="definition">adj. 在一個大的群體中（或在很多地方）不常見的、罕見的：</span>an uncommon occurrence 不尋常的事情 / Side effects from the drug are uncommon. 這藥很少有副作用。/ It is not uncommon for college students to live at home. 大學生住在家裡並不少見。/ Red squirrels are uncommon in England. 紅松鼠在英格蘭很少見。/ Cancer of the breast in young women is uncommon. 乳腺癌在年輕女子當中並不常見。/ A 15-year lifespan is not uncommon for a dog. 狗活15年並不少見。/ Such attitudes were not at all uncommon thirty years ago. 這些看法在三十年前很常見。/ Such technologies were still uncommon in the US. 這些技術在美國仍然不常見。
           
<span class="vocabulary">**infrequent**</span> [ɪnˈfri:kwənt]
<span class="definition">adj. 不常發生的：</span>her infrequent visits home 她少有的探望家人 / Muggings are relatively infrequent in this area. 在這個地區行兇搶劫事件相對少見。/ John's infrequent visits to London 約翰僅有的幾次倫敦之行 / Disturbances are relatively infrequent in our prisons. 相對來說，我們這裡的監獄不常發生騷亂。
