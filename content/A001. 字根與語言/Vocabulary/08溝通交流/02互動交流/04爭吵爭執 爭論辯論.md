☀ <span class="category">**爭吵爭執 爭論辯論：**</span>
<span class="vocabulary">**argue**</span> ['ɑːɡju:] 
<span class="definition">vi. argue (with sb) (about/over sth) 因不同意對方而生氣地朝其講話，即爭論，爭辯，爭吵：</span>They argue endlessly about money. 他們為錢的事無休止地爭吵。/ She’s always arguing with her mother. 她老是和媽媽鬥嘴。<span class="definition">vt.＆vi. argue (for/against sth) / argue (for/against doing sth) 給出（你認為的）對錯、真假等的理由（尤指為說服別人相信你），即論證，說理：</span>a well-argued article 鑿鑿有據的文章 / She argued the case for bringing back the death penalty. 她為恢復死刑的主張提供論據。/ He argued that they needed more time to finish the project. 他提出理由說他們需要更多的時間來完成該專案。/ The report argues convincingly that economic help should be given to these countries. 該報告提出有力論據，指出應該給予這些國家經濟援助。/ He was too tired to argue the point. 他太累了，不想討論這個問題。

<span class="vocabulary">**quarrel**</span> ['kwɒrəl] 
<span class="definition">n. [C] quarrel (with sb/between A and B) / quarrel (about/over sth) 兩人之間憤怒的爭論或爭辯、口角、拌嘴（常關於私事）。其含義已為argument所涵蓋：</span>a quarrel between family members 家庭成員之間的爭執 / lovers’ quarrel 戀人之間的拌嘴 / private quarrel 私人的爭吵 / I don’t want to pick a quarrel with her. 我不想找事兒和她爭吵。/ a quarrel about money 為錢吵架 / quarrel over 就…而吵架 <span class="definition">vi. quarrel (with sb) (about/over sth) 爭吵，吵架，吵嘴：</span>She quarrelled with her brother over their father’s will. 她和哥哥因為父親的遺囑起了爭執。/ Companies always quarrel about the exact terms of the agreement. 公司總是為協議的具體條款爭來吵去。/ What did you two quarrel about? 你們倆為什麼吵架？
           
<span class="vocabulary">**wrangle**</span> [ˈræŋgl]
<span class="definition">n. [C] ~ (with sb) (over sth) / ~ (between A and B) 就某個問題進行的長時間的、複雜而激烈的爭論或爭吵：</span>a legal wrangle between the company and their suppliers 這家公司與各供貨商之間長期的法律糾紛 / The two countries fell out in a bitter wrangle over imports. 這兩個國家在有關進口問題的激烈爭論中鬧翻了。/ The wrangle between the school and the local authority has gone on for two years. 學校和地方當局之間的紛爭已經持續兩年了。/ I don't want to get into a wrangle with the committee. 我不想同委員會發生爭執。<span class="definition">vi. ~ (with sb) (over/about sth) 就某個問題進行（尤指長時間地）激烈爭論或爭吵、爭辯：</span>They're still wrangling over the financial details. 他們仍在為財務細節爭吵。/ The two sides have spent most of their time wrangling over procedural problems. 雙方大部分時間都在圍繞程式問題爭論不休。/ A group of MPs is still wrangling with the government over the timing of elections. 一群下院議員仍在與政府就選舉的時間安排爭吵個不停。

<span class="vocabulary">**argument**</span> ['ɑːɡjumənt] 
<span class="definition">n. 1 [C, U] argument (with sb) (about/over sth)（兩人或多人之間的）爭論或爭吵，爭辯。一般涉及到講理：</span>We had an argument with the waiter about the bill. 我們和服務員就賬單問題發生了爭吵。/ an argument between her parents 她父母之間的爭吵 / The argument over decentralization will probably continue for ever. 關於權力下放的爭論很可能會一直持續下去。<span class="definition">2 [U] argument (about sth) 用論證的方式（在會話或討論中）所進行的辯論：</span>Let’s assume for the sake of argument that we can’t start till March. 為方便討論，先假定我們要到三月份才能開始。

<span class="vocabulary">**exchange**</span> [ɪks'tʃeɪndӡ] 
<span class="definition">n. [C] 爭論。不如argument常用：</span>They’ve had many spirited exchanges. 他們常爭得面紅耳赤。/ verbal exchange 口角 / angry exchanges about the problem of unemployment 有關失業問題的激烈爭論 / an exchange over the question of Tom’s guilt 一場有關湯姆的犯罪問題的爭論 / Members got into heated exchanges with the chairperson. 委員會成員和主席展開了一場激烈的爭論。
           
<span class="vocabulary">**debate**</span> [dɪ'beɪt] 
<span class="definition">n. 1 [C, U]（在公共集會或議會上就某問題進行的）正式討論，辯論（在辯論中，兩人或多人就某問題發表相反的觀點，然後常以對該問題進行表決的形式結束）：</span>a debate on abortion 關於墮胎的辯論 / televised debate 電視直播的辯論 / congressional, legislative, presidential debate 國會辯論；立法辯論；總統辯論 / policy debate 政策辯論 / The union holds debates for students. 協會舉辦了學生辯論會。<span class="definition">2 [C, U]（各自發表不同意見的）爭論；討論；辯論：</span>There has been heated debate about whether the movie should be allowed. 關於是否准許放映這部影片一直激辯不休。/ The nature of this book is to provoke reasoned debate. 本書旨在引發理性的討論。/ philosophical debate 哲學辯論 / academic, scholarly debate 學術辯論；學問上的辯論 <span class="definition">vt.＆vi.（在做出決定或找到解決方案前）對某事進行討論（尤指正式討論），有時可譯為辯論，類似於discuss：</span>a presidential candidate debating with his opponent 與對手辯論的總統候選人 / Politicians will be debating the bill later this week. 政界將在本週晚些時候討論這個議案。/ The question of the origin of the universe is still hotly debated by scientists. 關於宇宙起源問題，科學家們仍進行著激烈辯論。/ The report has been widely debated in the industry. 這份報告在行業內得到廣泛討論。

<span class="vocabulary">**struggle**</span> ['strʌɡl] 
<span class="definition">vi. struggle (with sb) (for sth)（尤指為得到某東西而）與某人爭辯：</span>瞭解即可

<span class="vocabulary">**battle**</span> ['bætl] 
<span class="definition">n. [C] 爭論：</span>a battle of ideas, a battle of words 思想交鋒；舌戰 

<span class="vocabulary">**fight**</span> [faɪt] 
<span class="definition">vi. fight (with sb) (about/over sth) 就某事與某人爭辯：</span>Children will fight even over small things. 小孩子甚至會為一些雞毛蒜皮的事爭吵。<span class="definition">n. [C] fight (with sb) / fight (over/about sth) 尤用於美式英語，指就某事所進行的爭論，爭吵。與battle類似，通常較為激烈：</span>We had a fight over money. 我們為錢吵了一架。
            
<span class="vocabulary">**contend**</span> [kənˈtend]
<span class="definition">vt. [正式]（尤指在爭論中）聲稱某事是真的：</span>'You were just looking,' contends Samantha. 'I was the one doing all the work.' “你不過是看看而已。”薩曼莎爭辯道，“什麼活兒都是我來幹。”          

<span class="vocabulary">**contention**</span> [kənˈtenʃn]
<span class="definition">n. [U] [正式] 爭吵、爭論、爭執：</span>an area of contention 爭論的範圍 / a bone of contention 爭論的問題 / a point of contention 爭論的要點 / a source of contention 爭論的根源 / Where to go on holiday is always a bone of contention in our family. 到哪兒去度假總是我們家爭論的焦點。/ One area of contention is the availability of nursery care. 爭論的一個方面是提供幼兒保育的可能性。
           
<span class="vocabulary">**contest**</span> [kənˈtest]
<span class="definition">vt. 正式反對一個決定或陳述，如爭辯、提出異議：</span>Gender discrimination is a hotly contested issue. 性別歧視是一個爭論激烈的話題。/ His views on evolution are strongly contested by other scientists. 他的進化論觀點遭到其他科學家的強烈質疑。/ Defence lawyers successfully contested the case. 被告律師對此案進行了成功的辯護。/ to contest a will 對遺囑提出質疑 / The divorce was not contested. 這樁離婚案沒有人提出異議。/ Your former employer has to reply within 14 days in order to contest the case. 你的前任僱主若對本案存有異議，必須在14天內予以答覆。

<span class="vocabulary">**clash**</span> [klæʃ]
<span class="definition">n. [C] ~ (with sb) (over sth) / ~ (between A and B) (over sth) 信仰或觀點不同的兩個人或兩群人之間的爭論、爭執：</span>a head-on clash between the two leaders over education policy 兩位領導人就教育政策進行的針鋒相對的爭論 / The leaders are preparing for a head-on clash at the summit. 首腦們準備在峰會上正面交鋒。<span class="definition">vi. ~ (with sb) (over/on sth) 公開地爭論、辯論：</span>The leaders clashed with party members on the issue. 政黨領袖與該黨黨員在這個問題上產生了分歧。/ Behind the scenes, Parsons clashed with almost everyone on the show. 在幕後，帕森斯幾乎和劇組的所有人都發生過沖突。
           
<span class="vocabulary">**controversy**</span> [ˈkɒntrəvɜ:si; 美 ˈkɑ:ntrəvɜ:rsi; 英也用kənˈtrɒvəsi]
<span class="definition">n. [U, C] ~ (over/about/surrounding sb/sth) 很多人就某件有很大爭議的事進行的公開討論、爭論、辯論或論戰：</span>to arouse/cause controversy 引起爭論 / a bitter controversy over/about the site of the new airport 有關新機場選址的激烈爭論 / the controversy surrounding his latest movie 圍繞他最近一部電影的爭論 / The President resigned amid considerable controversy. 總統在一片爭論聲中辭職。/ The proposed cuts have caused considerable controversy. 削減開支的提議引起了諸多爭議。/ a fierce political controversy over human rights abuses 關於侵犯人權問題的激烈的政治辯論 / public controversy 公開的爭論 / political, legal, ethical, scientific controversy 政治上的爭論；法律上的爭論；道德上的爭論；科學上的爭論 / religious, theological controversy 宗教上的爭論；神學上的爭論 / academic controversy 學術爭論 / What they are doing is bound to stir up controversy. 他們正在做的事情勢必激起爭論。/ This year's championships have been marked by controversy. 今年的各個錦標賽都存在爭議。/ to resolve the controversy 解決爭議 / The network ran into controversy over claims of faked documentary footage. 由於有人指責紀錄片不實，電視網陷入了一場爭論。/ A fierce controversy has broken out over the issue. 這件事已引發了一場激烈的爭論。/ Controversy is raging over the route of the new road. 關於新公路路線的爭論十分激烈。/ Controversy exists as to how safe these drugs are. 這些藥物的安全程度存在爭議。/ The controversy centred on the issue of compensation for the victims. 爭論集中在對受害人的賠償問題上。/ Much controversy surrounds the new exam. 圍繞著新的考試有很多爭議。/ controversy among historians 歷史學家之間的爭論 / the bitter controversy surrounding the introduction of the new regulations 圍繞著新規則實施的激烈爭論 / her long-running controversy with fellow academics 她與學術同行的長期爭論 / a subject of controversy 引起爭論的話題 / The book raised a storm of controversy. 這本書引起了一場爭議的風波。

<span class="vocabulary">**fray**</span> [freɪ]
<span class="definition">n. [sing.] the fray 尤指激烈的或被視為對某人的能力進行考驗的一場爭辯：</span>瞭解即可

<span class="vocabulary">**hassle**</span> [ˈhæsl]
<span class="definition">n. [C, U] [非正式] 分歧；爭論：</span>Do as you're told and don't give me any hassle! 告訴你怎麼做就怎麼做，別跟我頂嘴！           
                   
<span class="vocabulary">**tussle**</span> [ˈtʌsl]
<span class="definition">n. [C] ~ (for/over sth) 短暫的爭論（尤指為了爭得某事物）：</span>a tussle over the closure of the local hospital 關於是否關閉當地醫院的爭論 <span class="definition">vi.（尤指為了爭得某事物而）與…爭論：</span>Officials tussled over who had responsibility for the newly fashionable unemployment agenda. 眾官員為誰負責最近備受關注的失業議題一事爭得不可開交。           

<span class="vocabulary">**dispute**</span> [dɪˈspju:t]
<span class="definition">vt.＆vi.（尤指標對某事物的所有權問題）與某人發生激烈爭論、辯論、爭執：</span>disputed territory 有爭議的領土 / The issue remains hotly disputed. 這個問題至今仍在激烈地辯論中。/ The effectiveness of this treatment is still hotly disputed. 人們仍在激烈地爭論這種療法的有效性。

<span class="vocabulary">**protest**</span> [ˈprəʊtest; 美 ˈproʊ-]
<span class="definition">vt.（尤指被指控時，或人們不相信你時）堅決地表示某事是真實的，也可稱為申辯：</span>‘That's not what you said earlier!’ Jane protested. “你當初不是這麼說的！”簡爭辯說。/ When we tried to protest that Mo was beaten up they didn't believe us. 我們試圖申明莫遭到了毒打，他們卻不相信我們。/ 'I never said any of that to her,' he protested. “我從來沒有對她那麼說過，”他申辯道。
           
<span class="vocabulary">**agitate**</span> [ˈædʒɪteɪt]
<span class="definition">vi. ~ (for/against sth)（尤指為法律、社會狀況等的改變而）激烈爭論，如鼓動、煽動：</span>political groups agitating for social change 鼓吹社會變革的政治團體 / Her family are agitating to have her transferred to a prison in the UK. 她的家人正多方遊說要把她轉到英國監獄。/ The women who worked in these mills had begun to agitate for better conditions. 在這些工廠裡做工的婦女們開始抗議要求改善工作條件。

<span class="vocabulary">**scene**</span> [si:n] 
<span class="definition">n. [C, usually sing.] 大聲的、憤怒的爭論，（尤指當眾的、有失體面的）爭吵：</span>big, little scene 當眾大吵；當眾小鬧一場 / Quiet! Don’t make a scene! 安靜！不要當眾大吵大鬧！/ There have been a couple of ugly scenes between him and the manager. 他和經理之間有過幾次極不愉快的爭吵。
           
<span class="vocabulary">**scrap**</span> [skræp]
<span class="definition">n. [C] [非正式] 短時間的爭吵：</span>He had a bit of a scrap with the boy next door. 他和隔壁的男孩有點兒爭執。           

<span class="vocabulary">**stink**</span> [stɪŋk]
<span class="definition">n. [sing.] [非正式]（意在引人注意的）大吵大鬧：</span>The family's making a hell of a stink. 這家人正在大吵大鬧。
           
<span class="vocabulary">**bicker**</span> [ˈbɪkə(r)]
<span class="definition">vi. ~ (about/over sth) 為小事而爭吵：</span>The children are always bickering about something or other. 孩子們有事沒事總是在爭吵。/ The two women bickered constantly. 這兩個女人經常發生口角。/ I went into medicine to care for patients, not to waste time bickering over budgets. 我行醫是為了治病救人，而不是浪費時間為預算問題爭論不休。

<span class="vocabulary">**squabble**</span> [ˈskwɒbl; 美 ˈskwɑ:bl]
<span class="definition">vi. ~ (with sb) (about/over sth) 為瑣事而爭吵、發生口角：</span>Mother is devoted to Dad although they squabble all the time. 母親很愛父親，儘管他們總是為瑣事拌嘴。/ The children were squabbling over the remote-control gadget for the television. 孩子們正在為搶奪電視機的遙控器而爭吵。/ My sisters were squabbling over what to watch on TV. 我的姐妹正為看哪個電視節目爭吵。<span class="definition">n. [C] 為小事而發生的爭吵：</span>minor, petty squabble 小口角 / bitter squabble 激烈的爭吵 / domestic, family, marital squabble 家庭口角；夫妻間的爭吵 / political squabble 政治爭端 / internal, public squabble 內部爭執；公開爭吵 / squabbles about money 為錢發生的爭吵 / I always have to settle squabbles between the children. 我總是得平息孩子們間的爭吵。/ a squabble with her publisher over royalties 就版稅問題與她的出版商發生的爭吵 / There were endless squabbles over who should sit where. 為誰該坐哪兒吵個沒完沒了。
           
<span class="vocabulary">**uproar**</span> [ˈʌprɔ:(r)]
<span class="definition">n. [U, sing.]（某件令人憤怒的事所引起的）很多公眾對此事的批評和爭論：</span>The town is in uproar over the dispute. 整個城市因為這次爭端而陷入了騷動。/ The surprise announcement could cause an uproar in the United States. 這一突然釋出的公告可能會在美國引起騷亂。/ The article caused (an) uproar. 這篇文章引起了軒然大波。/ a huge public uproar over taxation plans 對徵稅計劃輿論一片譁然 / international uproar 國際上的軒然大波 / Can you imagine the uproar if alcohol was banned? 如果禁酒，你能想象會有怎樣的怨憤嗎? / Financial markets were in uproar after the crash of the rouble. 盧布暴跌後，金融市場極不穩定。/ There was a great uproar over plans to pull down the old library. 拆除舊圖書館的計劃引起了軒然大波。

<span class="vocabulary">**showdown**</span> [ˈʃəʊdaʊn; 美 ˈʃoʊ-]
<span class="definition">n. [usually sing.]（對長久存在的分歧）決出勝負的爭論：</span>Management are facing a showdown with union members today. 今天資方準備和工會成員攤牌。/ The Prime Minister is preparing for a showdown with Ministers. 首相正準備和部長們攤牌。/ military, nuclear 軍事決戰；核攤牌 / Commons, political, Senate 下院較量；政治較量；參議院較量 / courtroom, High Court, Supreme Court 法庭較量；高等法院較量；最高法院較量 / The government is heading for a showdown over the new proposals. 政府將面臨對新提案的最後抉擇。/ Of course I’ll support you if it comes to a showdown. 到最後攤牌的時候我當然會支援你。/ He was now strong enough to force a showdown with them. 現在他的實力足以與他們決一雌雄了。
       
<span class="vocabulary">**side**</span> [saɪd] 
<span class="definition">n. [C]（爭論中的）一方或一派（可以是一個人，也可以是一群人）：</span>We have finally reached an agreement acceptable to all sides. 我們最終達成了一份各方都能接受的協議。/ Let both sides argue their case. 讓雙方各自陳述自己的理由。

<span class="vocabulary">**party**</span> ['pɑːtɪ] 
<span class="definition">n. 爭論中的一方，是一種正式用法：</span>瞭解即可

相關詞義延伸：
· side還可指（在一場爭論中）由某一方所持有的意見、態度或立場。

周邊詞彙補充：
· floor可指一棟建築物（尤指議會）中供人們進行正式討論或辯論的區域（可叫議員席或議政廳）；也可指出席一場討論或辯論的人（為聆聽者，而並非主要發言人）；
· house可指（在一場正式辯論中）參與辯論的一群人；
· champion可指（人、事業、原則、信仰等的）擁護者，捍衛者；
· have可指在辯論中勝過某人；
· confront可指與某人當面對質（或對證）。

· 表示“分歧”的詞群見[[01相異 相同]]
