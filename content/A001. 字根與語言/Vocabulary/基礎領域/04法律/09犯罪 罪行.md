☀ <span class="category">**犯罪 罪行：**</span>
<span class="vocabulary">**commit**</span> [kə'mɪt] 
<span class="definition">vt. commit a crime, etc. 做出錯事或非法的事，即犯錯，犯罪。是此義的基本用詞：</span>to commit a crime 犯罪 / to commit murder 犯謀殺罪
           
<span class="vocabulary">**commission**</span> [kəˈmɪʃn]
<span class="definition">n. [U] [正式] 做出（錯事）、犯下（罪行）：</span>the commission of a crime 犯罪 / If a person uses a gun in the commission of a crime, then he should be given an additional penalty. 如果使用槍支進行犯罪，就應該受到額外的懲罰。

<span class="vocabulary">**pull**</span> [pʊl] 
<span class="definition">vt. [非正式]（對某人耍手腕）得逞；已犯下（罪行）等：</span>He’s pulling some sort of trick on you. 他在耍你呢。

<span class="vocabulary">**crime**</span> [kraɪm] 
<span class="definition">n. 1 [U] 違反法律的活動，即犯罪活動：</span>violent crime 暴力犯罪 / organized crime 有組織的犯罪 / juvenile, youth crime 青少年犯罪 / urban crime 城市犯罪 / street crime 街頭犯罪 / drug, gun, knife crime 毒品犯罪；涉槍犯罪；持刀犯罪 / sex crime 性犯罪 / war crime 戰爭罪行 / terrorist crime 恐怖主義罪行 / crime rate 犯罪率 / crime prevention 罪案預防 / crime problem 犯罪問題 / crime group 犯罪團伙 / crime fiction 犯罪小說 / crime and punishment 罪與罰 <span class="definition">2 [C] crime (against sb) 罪，罪行：</span>to commit a great crime 犯下滔天大罪 / a violent crime 一起暴力犯罪 / a crime of passion 一起激情犯罪 / to check the scene of the crime 勘查犯罪現場 / The crime occurred in broad daylight. 這起罪行發生在光天化日之下。/ Many crimes are never reported to the police. 許多犯罪案件從未向警方報案。
           
<span class="vocabulary">**offend**</span> [əˈfend]
<span class="definition">vi. [正式] 犯罪、犯法：</span>He started offending at the age of 16. 他16歲就開始犯法。/ In Western countries girls are far less likely to offend than boys. 在西方國家，女孩的犯罪傾向遠低於男孩。

<span class="vocabulary">**offence**</span> [ə'fens]（美offense）
<span class="definition">n. [C] offence (against sb/sth) 違法行為，即罪行：</span>to commit an offence 犯罪 / capital offence 死罪 / grave, major, serious offence 嚴重的罪行；滔天大罪 / lesser, minor, petty, trivial offence 輕微的罪行；輕罪 / alleged offence 涉嫌違法 / first offence 初犯 / statutory offence（英）制定法上的罪行 / arrestable（英）, firing（美）, imprisonable（英）, indictable（英）, punishable offence 夠拘捕條件的罪行；可導致解僱的行為；夠拘押條件的罪行；可起訴的罪行；應予懲罰的罪行 / criminal, disciplinary（英）offence 刑事犯罪；違紀行為 / driving（英）, political, sex, sexual, terrorist offence 違章駕駛；政治罪；性犯罪；恐怖主義罪 / non-violent（尤美）, violent offence 非暴力犯罪；暴力犯罪 / drug-related offence 涉毒犯罪 / offences against public decency 有傷風化的犯罪行為

<span class="vocabulary">**count**</span> [kaʊnt] 
<span class="definition">n. [C] 某人被指控所犯下的罪行：</span>She appeared in court on three counts of fraud. 她因三項詐騙罪而出庭受審。/ felony count（美）重罪指控 / to be charged with two counts of murder 被控兩項謀殺罪狀
           
<span class="vocabulary">**felony**</span> [ˈfeləni]
<span class="definition">n. [C, U] [舊時，法律] 嚴重的罪行（如謀殺、強姦、持械搶劫等）：</span>a charge of felony 對犯重罪的指控 / He pleaded guilty to six felonies. 他承認犯有六項重罪。/ federal felony 違反聯邦法律的重罪 / serious felony 嚴重的重罪 / first-degree felony 一級重罪 / aggravated felony 嚴重的重罪 / non-violent, violent felony 非暴力犯罪；暴力犯罪 / drug, drug-related felony 毒品犯罪；涉毒犯罪 / a felony punishable by up to six years in prison 可處以長達六年監禁的重罪 / felony charge 重罪指控 / felony conviction 重罪宣判 / felony offense 重罪的罪行 / felony murder 犯有謀殺重罪 / He was charged with felony assault. 他被指控犯有攻擊重罪。          
           
<span class="vocabulary">**enormity**</span> [ɪˈnɔ:məti; 美 ɪˈnɔ:rm-]
<span class="definition">n. [C, usually pl.] [正式] 非常嚴重的罪行，如滔天罪行；罪大惡極：</span>the enormities of the Hitler regime 希特勒政權的滔天罪行 / the enormities they committed 他們犯下的滔天大罪 / the enormity of what slavery meant 奴隸制代表的罪惡
 
<span class="vocabulary">**delinquency**</span> [dɪˈlɪŋkwənsi]
<span class="definition">n. [U, C]（通常指青少年的）惡劣行為或違法犯罪行為：</span>an increase in juvenile delinquency 青少年犯罪的增加 / He had no history of delinquency. 他沒有違法記錄。/ a whole range of crimes and delinquencies 各種違法犯罪行為
           
<span class="vocabulary">**foul play**</span>
<span class="definition">n. [U] 造成某人死亡的罪行或暴力活動：</span>Police immediately began an investigation, but did not suspect foul play (= did not suspect that the person had been murdered). 警方立即開始調查，但沒有懷疑謀殺。/ The report says it suspects foul play was involved in the deaths of two journalists. 該報道稱懷疑兩名記者死於謀殺。/ The police have ruled out foul play in their investigation of his death.（rule out尤為英式用法）警方在對他的死亡調查中已經排除了謀殺的可能。/ The police found no evidence of foul play. 警方沒有發現犯罪證據。
           
<span class="vocabulary">**guilt**</span> [gɪlt]
<span class="definition">n. [U] 犯罪、有罪、罪行：</span>His guilt was proved beyond all doubt by the prosecution. 他的罪行已被原告方證實確鑿無疑。/ I took his silence as an admission of guilt. 我認為他沉默就是承認自己有錯。/ The trial is concerned only with the determination of guilt according to criminal law. 依照刑法規定，審判僅涉及定罪。/ You weren't convinced of Mr Matthews' guilt. 你不相信馬修斯先生有罪。/ Many of the accused would deny their guilt to the magistrates. 許多被告會在地方法官面前否認自己有罪。/ It might be difficult to prove his guilt. 要證明他的罪行可能很難。/ proof of guilt 罪證
              
<span class="vocabulary">**rap**</span> [ræp]
<span class="definition">n. [C] [美，非正式] 罪名；刑事責任；判刑：</span>a police rap sheet (= a record of the crimes sb has committed) 判刑記錄 / You'll be facing a Federal rap for aiding and abetting an escaped convict. 因涉嫌協助和教唆越獄囚犯，你將面臨聯邦指控。/ When the client was murdered, his wife took the rap, but did she really do it? 當那名客戶被謀殺後，他妻子背上了黑鍋，然而真是她殺的嗎？/ their decision to help Minnie beat the murder rap 他們要幫米妮逃過謀殺罪懲罰的決定

<span class="vocabulary">**spree**</span> [spri:]
<span class="definition">n. [C]（尤用於報章）一陣犯罪活動：</span>crime, killing, looting（尤美）, murder, shooting, wrecking（英）spree 瘋狂犯罪；瘋狂殺人；瘋狂搶劫；瘋狂謀殺；瘋狂射殺；瘋狂破環 / Hungry mobs went on a looting spree in the city. 飢餓的暴民在這座城市裡大肆搶劫。/ Vandals went on a £10 000 wrecking spree. 肇事者肆意破壞，損失達1萬英鎊。

<span class="vocabulary">**battery**</span> ['bætərɪ] 
<span class="definition">n. [U] [美] 對人身進行毆打的罪行，即毆打罪：</span>He punched a man in a club and was charged with battery. 他在一傢俱樂部裡打了人，結果被指控犯了毆打罪。
           
<span class="vocabulary">**kidnap**</span> [ˈkɪdnæp]
<span class="definition">n. [U, C] 綁架罪：</span>Stewart denies attempted murder and kidnap. 斯圖爾特否認對其謀殺未遂和綁架的指控。/ He was charged with the kidnap of a 25 year-old woman. 他被指控綁架一名25歲的女子。/ kidnap victim 綁架案受害者

<span class="vocabulary">**job**</span> [dӡɒb] 
<span class="definition">n. [C] 罪行，尤指偷竊罪，為非正式用法：</span>bank job 銀行搶劫案 / inside job 內部人員作的案 / The gang bungled the job and got caught. 該團伙因罪行敗露而被捕。

<span class="vocabulary">**vice**</span> [vaɪs] 
<span class="definition">n. [U] 涉性或涉毒的犯罪活動：</span>offences connected with vice and drugs 與賣淫和販毒有關的罪行

<span class="vocabulary">**wrong**</span> [rɒŋ] 
<span class="definition">n. [C] [正式] 違法行為，不公平、不誠實或不道德的行為，反義詞為right：</span>to forgive past wrongs 寬恕過往的罪過 / civil, criminal wrong 民事不法行為；刑事犯罪
                             
<span class="vocabulary">**perjury**</span> [ˈpɜ:dʒəri; 美 ˈpɜ:rdʒ-]
<span class="definition">n. [U] [法律] 偽證罪：</span>This witness has committed perjury and no reliance can be placed on her evidence. 這名證人作了偽證，她的證詞不可信。/ charges of perjury 偽證罪指控

<span class="vocabulary">**trespass**</span> [ˈtrespəs]
<span class="definition">vi. 舊時用法，指做錯事：</span>瞭解即可 <span class="definition">n. [C] 舊時用法，指自己做過的道德上的錯事，即罪過：</span>瞭解即可

<span class="vocabulary">**misconduct**</span> [ˌmɪsˈkɒndʌkt; 美 -ˈkɑ:n-]
<span class="definition">n. [U] [正式] 不可接受的行為，尤指專業人士的失職、行為不端、處理不當：</span>He was dismissed from his job for gross misconduct after handing over confidential documents to the press. 在把機密檔案交給新聞界之後，他就因嚴重瀆職而被解職了。/ a doctor accused of gross misconduct (= very serious misconduct) 被控嚴重失職的醫生 / professional misconduct 翫忽職守 / Dr Lee was cleared of serious professional misconduct. 李醫生嚴重瀆職的罪名被撤銷了。/ scientific, research misconduct 學術不端；科研不端 / sexual misconduct 不正當的性關係 / police, prosecutorial（美） misconduct 警察的瀆職行為；檢察官的瀆職行為 / allegations of misconduct by the security forces 對安全部隊不端行為的種種指控 / There was no misconduct on the part of the police. 警方沒有濫用職權。/ Staff can lose their jobs only on grounds of professional misconduct. 只要有瀆職行為，員工就可能丟掉工作。
                       
<span class="vocabulary">**dereliction**</span> [ˌderəˈlɪkʃn]
<span class="definition">n. [U, sing.] [正式或法律] ~ of duty 在自己的職責範圍內沒有盡職，即翫忽職守、瀆職：</span>The police officers were found guilty of serious dereliction of duty. 這些警察被判犯有嚴重瀆職罪。          

周邊詞彙補充：
· negligence可指未給予某人或某事足夠的關心或關注，如失職；過失；失誤；疏忽。常作為一種罪行，如過失罪，翫忽職守罪。

<span class="vocabulary">**misdeed**</span> [ˌmɪsˈdi:d]
<span class="definition">n. [usually pl.] [正式] 惡行、不端行為：</span>the alleged financial misdeeds of his government 人們所指控的他的政府在財政方面的失誤政策

<span class="vocabulary">**violate**</span> ['vaɪəleɪt] 
<span class="definition">vt. 1 [正式] 違反或拒絕遵守法律、協議等：</span>to violate the law 犯法 / to violate the treaty 違反條約 / to violated the rules 違反規則 <span class="definition">2 [正式] 侵犯別人的隱私等：</span>to violate his family’s privacy 侵犯他的家庭隱私
           
<span class="vocabulary">**violation**</span> [ˌvaɪəˈleɪʃn]
<span class="definition">n. [U, C]（對法律、協議或承諾的）違反：</span>They were in open violation of the treaty. 他們公然違反條約。/ gross violations of human rights 對人權的粗暴踐踏 / The attack on civilians is a flagrant iolation of the peace agreement. 襲擊平民的舉動是對和平協定的公然違背。/ practices that are in direct violation of the Geneva Conventions 直接違背《日內瓦公約》的行徑 / civil liberties（尤美）, civil rights（尤美）, human rights violation 對公民自由的踐踏；對民權的踐踏；對人權的踐踏 / privacy violation 對隱私的侵犯 / ethical violation（尤美）對道德的踐踏 / code, law, rule violation（均尤美）對守則的違犯；對法律的違犯；對規則的違犯 / He had been fined for building code violations. 他曾因違反建築規範被罰款。/ arrests for drug law violations 因違反禁毒法而進行的拘捕 / criminal violation（尤美）刑事犯罪 / drug violation（尤美）違反禁毒法 / traffic violation 交通違法行為 / copyright, trademark violation 對版權的侵犯；對商標的侵犯 / parole, probation violation（均尤美）假釋違規；緩刑違規 / immigration, visa violation（均尤美）移民違規；簽證違規 / safety, security violation（均尤美）違反安全規程 / fines for safety violations 對違反安全規程的罰款 / anti-trust violation（美）違反反壟斷法 / The army was accused of committing violations against the accord. 軍隊被控多次違反協定。/ This action constitutes a violation of international law. 這一行動違反了國際法。/ There is plenty of evidence that her actions were in violation of an earlier contract. 有大量證據表明她的行為違反了先前的合同。/ violations against minimum wage agreements 對最低工資協定的違反

<span class="vocabulary">**invade**</span> [ɪnˈveɪd]
<span class="definition">vt. 以一種煩人的方式侵犯別人的私生活：</span>Do the press have the right to invade her privacy in this way? 新聞界有權以這種方式干擾她的私生活嗎？

<span class="vocabulary">**intrude**</span> [ɪnˈtru:d]
<span class="definition">vi. ~ (on/into/upon sth) 侵犯別人的私生活：</span>legislation to stop newspapers from intruding on people's private lives 禁止報章侵犯他人私生活的立法
           
<span class="vocabulary">**intrusion**</span> [ɪnˈtru:ʒn]
<span class="definition">n. [U, C] ~ (into/on/upon sth) 對一種情況（或人們的生活）造成侵擾的事物：</span>This is a governmental intrusion on the freedom of the press. 這是政府對新聞自由的侵犯。/ media intrusion into the lives of celebrities 媒體對名人生活的侵擾 / an unwarranted intrusion upon the singer's privacy 對歌手隱私的無端侵犯 / This was another example of press intrusion into the affairs of the royals. 這是新聞界侵擾王室成員私事的又一例項。
           
<span class="vocabulary">**nuisance**</span> [ˈnju:sns; 美 ˈnu:-]
<span class="definition">n. [C, U] [法律] 某人對別人造成妨害的行為：</span>He was charged with causing a public nuisance. 他被控妨害公共利益罪。

<span class="vocabulary">**break**</span> [breɪk] 
<span class="definition">vt. 做違法的事：</span>to break the law, to break the rules, to break an agreement, to break a contract, to break the speed limit 違反法律；違反規則；違反協議；違反合同；違章超速
           
<span class="vocabulary">**breach**</span> [bri:tʃ]
<span class="definition">n. 1 [C, U] ~ of sth 對法律、規則、紀律等的違背、違犯：</span>a breach of contract, a breach of copyright, a breach of warranty 違犯合同；侵犯版權；違反保證 / a $1 billion breach of contract suit 一起違約金達10億美元的合同違約訴訟案 / They are in breach of Article 119. 他們違犯了第119條。/ The court's decision is in breach of the European Convention on human rights. 法庭的判決違反了歐盟人權公約。/ The congressman was accused of a breach of secrecy rules. 這位議員被控違反了保密規定。/ a minor breach of discipline 輕微的違紀行為 / a breach of security 破壞安全的行為 / a breach of the peace(= the crime of behaving in a noisy or violent way in public) 擾亂治安 <span class="definition">2 [C, U] ~ of sth 破壞各方已約定好的行為方式：</span>a breach of trust 背信 / He refused to bow to the Queen, in deliberate breach of etiquette. 他故意違反禮儀，拒絕向女王鞠躬。<span class="definition">vt. 違反協議、約定；違反法律；違背承諾：</span>The government is accused of breaching the terms of the treaty. 政府被控違反條約中的規定。/ The newspaper breached the code of conduct on privacy. 該報違反了保護隱私的行為準則。/ The film breached the criminal libel laws. 這部電影觸犯了刑事誹謗法。
        
<span class="vocabulary">**impinge**</span> [ɪmˈpɪndʒ]
<span class="definition">vi. [正式] ~ (on/upon sth/sb) 對…有明顯作用（或影響），尤指有壞的作用（或影響），如侵犯：</span>actions which seriously impinge on other people's personal freedoms 嚴重侵犯他人人身自由的行為

<span class="vocabulary">**contravene**</span> [ˌkɒntrəˈvi:n; 美 ˌkɑ:n-]
<span class="definition">vt. [正式] 違犯法律；違反規則：</span>The company was found guilty of contravening safety regulations. 那家公司被判違反了安全條例。/ The Board has banned the film on the grounds that it contravenes criminal libel laws. 委員會決定禁播這部電影，理由是它觸犯了刑事誹謗法。/ He said the article did not contravene the industry's code of conduct. 他說這一條款並未違反行業的行為準則。/ actions that blatantly contravene the rules of civilized warfare 公然違反文明戰爭規則的行為

<span class="vocabulary">**contravention**</span> [ˌkɒntrəˈvenʃn; 美 ˌkɑ:n-]
<span class="definition">n. [U, C] 對法律或規則的違犯或違反：</span>These actions are in contravention of European law. 這些行動違反了歐洲的法律。/ He was in direct contravention of the law. 他直接觸犯了法律。/ This was a clear contravention of the rules. 這明顯違反了規則。

<span class="vocabulary">**infraction**</span> [ɪnˈfrækʃn]
<span class="definition">n. [C, U] [正式] 對規則的違反、對法律的違犯：</span>minor infractions of EU regulations 對歐盟規定的輕微觸犯
           
<span class="vocabulary">**infringement**</span> [ɪn'frɪndʒmənt]
<span class="definition">n. 1 [U, C] 對他人權利的侵犯：</span>infringement of privacy 對隱私的侵犯 / copyright, patent, trademark infringement 侵犯著作權；侵犯專利權；侵犯商標權 / unconstitutional infringement （尤美）違憲侵權 / an infringement of liberty 對自由的侵犯 / They see it as an infringement on their own freedom of action. 他們把它視為對他們行動自由的侵犯。/ The committee ruled that the US ban constituted an infringement of free trade. 委員會裁定美國的禁令對自由貿易構成了侵犯。<span class="definition">2 [U, C] 對法律、規則等的違反、違背：</span>There might have been an infringement of the rules. 可能存在違反規章制度的現象。/ Infringement of the regulation is punishable by a fine. 違反規則應處以罰款。/ sympathy for people who commit minor infringements 對輕微觸犯法規者的同情 / an infringement of the law 犯法

說明：crime和offence（offense）表“罪行”時，①二者作可數名詞時，均表示具體的犯罪事實，在很多情況下可互換使用；②但二者之間依然存在一些區別。第一，crime還可以作不可數名詞，作為一種抽象概念，此時常可作前置定語，offence一般無此用法；第二，在犯罪的法律淵源和依據上，英式英語通常認為crime尤指觸犯普通法的行為，而offence尤指觸犯制定法的行為，但美式英語在此方面的區分度弱些；第三，crime在法律上僅表示嚴格意義上的犯罪行為，需要受到法律的懲罰。但offence可指所有危害社會的行為，包括犯罪行為和構不成犯罪但確實對社會產生危害的行為，如在公共場所亂停車，不能構成一項crime，但卻是一個offence。在這個意義上，offence的含義範圍比crime要廣泛；第四，當二者都表示法律意義上的“罪行”時，在罪行的輕重程度上二者未見有明顯區別，crime可表示重罪、輕罪和微罪，offence也可如此。也許是因為offence也常常用於表示構不成犯罪但確實危害了社會的輕度危害行為，所以很多人（包括一些權威法律詞典）認為crime程度重些，而offence則程度輕些。而實際上這種觀點做出這種結論時，不知不覺中使用了一個錯誤前提，即把offence的非罪但危害社會的那些行為也算進去了，這樣看來就offence就顯得比crime程度輕了。但是，如果僅僅對比二者的“罪行”（而非其他危害社會但不構成犯罪的行為）之義，就會發現這種罪行程度上的輕重之差在它們之間幾乎是不存在的；第五，如果非要說二者之間有什麼差別，那麼crime側重指那些違揹人類自然理性和道德的惡劣行為，如果一個行為尚未被書面上的法律條文所禁止，但從人的理性和道德角度來判斷確屬惡劣，那麼這種行為就是一個crime。因此crime算是人們心中的一部法律，即使有些尚未以書面條文的形式呈現出來，但它依然存在，成為了人類文明秩序的基石。（因此crime還有一個義項表示人們心中認為的“罪過”或“大錯”，如浪費糧食、發動一場戰爭，都是一種crime。）而以此為基礎所發展起來的法律體系，便是“普通法”。與crime相比，offence則暗示出對人們事先制定好的法律條文的觸犯。

關於count，《元照英美法詞典》給出的解釋是：“①罪項，即刑事起訴書中分別指控被告人犯有一項獨立的罪行的部分，在此意義上，count與charge可作為同義語使用。②訴因或訴訟理由，即原告起訴狀中對一項獨立的訴訟請求的陳述。在古時的法律文書中，count常與起訴狀declaration作為同義語使用，但如果一個訴訟包含兩個或兩個以上訴因，或者原告對一個訴因作不同的陳述時，其中的每一個訴因或訴因陳述稱為count，它們合起來構成一個declaration。③古時候可指不動產訴訟中原告的起訴狀。④專利法中可指‘在先發明陳述’，即專利申請書中，對多個專利申請之間或專利申請與現有專利之間的優先性爭議進行闡釋的部分。在數人就同一發明申請專利時，專利局可以依此決定如何授予專利權。”在第一個義項中，有些詞典直接翻譯為“罪行”，因此極容易讓人誤解為是一種犯罪行為本身，實際上只是對一項犯罪行為在刑事起訴書中做出的闡述。
