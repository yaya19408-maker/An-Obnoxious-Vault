☀ <span class="category">**支付 付款：**</span>
<span class="vocabulary">**afford**</span> [ə'fɔ:d] 
<span class="definition">vt.＆vi. 常與can，could或be able to連用，尤用於否定句或疑問句，指買得起，其主語是人。無被動式：</span>I couldn’t afford £50 for a ticket. 我拿不出50英鎊買一張票。/ I couldn’t possibly afford to eat in that restaurant. 那家飯館我根本吃不起。/ She can well afford to pay for herself. 她自己完全能夠付得起錢。

<span class="vocabulary">**buy**</span> [baɪ] 
<span class="definition">vt.（金錢）足夠支付…，其主語常是金錢（如果主語是人，一般用afford to buy的形式）：</span>He gave his children the best education that money can buy. 他讓孩子們接受花錢能買到的最好的教育。/ Five pounds doesn’t buy much nowadays. 如今五英鎊買不到多少東西了。/ I can’t afford to buy a new car. 我買不起新汽車。/ There are some things money can’t buy. 有些東西不是錢能買到的。

<span class="vocabulary">**cover**</span> ['kʌvə] 
<span class="definition">vt.（錢幣）足以支付，夠付。其主語既可以是人，也可以是錢，還可以是賺錢的活動。不過cover比afford更強調金錢的充足程度，以及由於金錢的充足而帶來的安全感和解決問題的能力：</span>$100 should cover your expenses. 100美元該足夠支付你的費用了。/ Your parents will have to cover your tuition fees. 你的父母得支付你的學費。/ The show barely covered its costs. 這場演出勉強夠本。/ The payments he gets barely cover his expenses. 他賺的錢幾乎不夠他花的。
       
<span class="vocabulary">**stretch**</span> [stretʃ]
<span class="definition">vi. ~ (to sth)（用於否定句和疑問句）（一筆錢）足夠買（或支付）：</span>I need a new car, but my savings won't stretch to it. 我需要一輛新車，但我的積蓄不夠。

<span class="vocabulary">**go**</span> [ɡəʊ] 
<span class="definition">vi. 願意付出某個價錢來購買某物：</span>He’s offered £3 000 for the car and I don’t think he’ll go any higher. 他出價3000英鎊買這輛汽車，我看他不會願意再多付了。/ I’ll go to $800 but that’s my limit. 我願意出800美元，這可是最大限度了。

<span class="vocabulary">**pay**</span> [peɪ] 
<span class="definition">vt.＆vi. pay (sb) (for sth) 為購買的貨物或服務而付錢。是此義的基礎用詞：</span>Are you paying in cash or by credit card? 您付現金還是用信用卡？/ Her parents paid for her to go to Canada. 她父母出錢送她去加拿大。/ to pay the taxi driver 付計程車費 / to pay him the rent 向他付租金 / She pays £200 a week for this apartment. 這套房子她每週要付租金200英鎊。<span class="definition">vt. pay sth (to sb) / pay (sb) sth 支付欠款：</span>to pay bill, to pay debt, to pay fine, to pay ransom 交賬單；償債；支付罰金；支付贖金 / to pay damages 賠償損失 / to pay the worker £900 in compensation 向工人支付900英鎊的賠償金 / He still hasn’t paid me the money he owes me. 他還沒歸還欠我的錢呢。

<span class="vocabulary">**give**</span> [ɡɪv] 
<span class="definition">vt. give (sb) sth for sth（為購買…或做某事而）支付；付款；付出，是“付錢”的生活化用詞，語體風格比較隨意：</span>How much will you give me for the car? 你肯出多少錢買我這輛汽車？/ I gave £50 for the lot. 我出50英鎊一下全買了。/ I’d give anything to see him again. 只要能再見他一面我願付出任何代價。
           
<span class="vocabulary">**tender**</span> [ˈtendə(r)]
<span class="definition">vt. [正式] ~ sth (to sb) 將金錢正式提供給某人，如繳納：</span>瞭解即可
           
<span class="vocabulary">**ransom**</span> [ˈrænsəm]
<span class="definition">vt.（為救出某人）向綁匪交贖金：</span>The kidnapped children were all ransomed and returned home unharmed. 被綁架的兒童在交付了贖金後均安然回到家中。/ The same system was used for ransoming or exchanging captives. 相同的制度還應用於贖回或交換俘虜。

<span class="vocabulary">**treat**</span> [tri:t] 
<span class="definition">vt. treat sb/yourself (to sth) 為別人或自己的享受而付費（尤指為不常享受的東西而付費）：</span>I’ll treat you to chocolate. 我請你吃巧克力。/ I’m going to treat myself to a new T-shirt. 我打算給自己買件新T恤衫。/ Tomorrow I’ll treat myself to a day’s reading. 明天我要好好享受一天的閱讀。

<span class="vocabulary">**on**</span> [ɒn] 
<span class="definition">prep. 以…來支付：</span>The old man lives on his pension. 這位老人靠養老金生活。/ I can live on $200 a month. 我靠200美元就能生活一個月。
           
<span class="vocabulary">**checkout**</span> [ˈtʃekaʊt]
<span class="definition">n. [U] 在旅館結賬離開的時間：</span>At checkout, your bill will be printed for you. 結賬時，旅館會把你的賬單列印給你。
                     
<span class="vocabulary">**payment**</span> [ˈpeɪmənt]
<span class="definition">n. 1 [U] ~ (for sth) 付款、支付；收款：</span>payment in instalments, payment in advance, payment by cheque 分期付款；預先付款；支票付款 / There will be a penalty for late payment of bills. 賬單拖延付款要收滯納金。/ immediate（英）, prompt 即時付款；立即支付 / early payment（英）提前付款 / full, part（尤英）, partial（尤美） payment 全部付清；部分付款 / I enclose $65.50, in full payment of the bill. 我附上65.50美元，把賬單全部付清。/ How do you want to make payment?（尤英）你想怎麼付款? / I made the payment in cash. 我用現金付了款。/ Do you accept payment by credit card? 你們接受信用卡付款嗎?/ The department makes payments to farmers for providing improvement to water quality. 該部門為改善水質而付款給農民。/ The buyer is required to enter a code to authorize payment. 買方需要輸入密碼來授權支付。/ I have authorized the bank to stop payment of the cheque/check. 我已經授權銀行停止兌付那張支票。/ We may have to defer payment for a week. 我們也許得延遲一週付款。/ The only way to guarantee payment is to sign a contract. 確保支付的唯一方式就是簽訂合同。/ Some mortgage plans offer a bimonthly payment schedule. 有些按揭方案提供雙月付款方案。/ He requested $8 000 in payment. 他要求付款8，000美元。/ Credit cards are accepted in payment for virtually anything. 幾乎所有東西都接受用信用卡付款。/ She wrote out a cheque in payment of the fees.（in payment of為英式用法）她開了一張支票支付費用。/ He was released on payment of the ransom. 他交了贖金後獲釋。/ payment for work done 為完成的工作支付的款項 / payment to the company from its customers 顧客給公司的付款 / a method of payment 支付方式 / The hostel requires full payment in advance. 這家青年旅舍要求全額預付。/ He demanded payment in full of the $300 000 owed to him. 他要求全部付清欠他的30萬美元。/ He had sought to obtain payment of a sum which he had claimed was owed to him. 他聲稱別人欠他一筆錢，他在想方設法討要回來。<span class="definition">2 [C] ~ (for sth)（將付或應付的）款額，款項：</span>annual payment 每年支付的款項 / regular payment 定期支付的款項 / one-off, one-time（美）, single payment 一次性支付的款項 / All families of the victims will receive a one-off payment of $100 000. 所有遇難者的家屬都會收到一次性支付的10萬美元。/ cash, lump-sum payment 現金付款;一次付款總額 / token payment 象徵性的付款 / She was finding it difficult to make even the minimum payment on her credit card. 她發現她連信用卡最低還款額都還不上。/ advance, upfront payment 預付款；首付金 / down, initial 首期付款額 / additional payment 額外支付的款項 / further, subsequent payment（均尤英）後續支付的款項 / interim payment（尤英）臨時支付的款項 / final payment 最終付款額 / credit-card payment 信用卡支付的款項 / debt, loan payment 債務償付額；貸款償付額 / insurance, interest, lease（尤美）, mortgage, rent, tax, tuition（美）payment 保險繳費；利息款；租賃費；按揭付款；租金；納稅額；學費 / alimony（尤美）, benefit, child-support, compensation, disability（尤美）, maintenance（英）, royalty, severance, welfare payment 贍養費；保險金；兒童撫養費；補償金；傷殘費；生活費；版權費；遣散費；福利金 / My client was unable to meet her rent payments. 我的客戶沒有能力支付房租。/ You could lower your monthly mortgage payments by $160. 你每月的房貸還款可以減少160美元。/ It was my job to collect payment for the trip. 我負責為這次旅行收款。/ Companies deduct interest payments from their taxable income. 公司從他們的應納稅所得額中扣除利息。/ The company guaranteed royalty payments of at least $590 million. 公司保證支付最低 5.9億美元的專利使用費。/ They are finding it difficult to meet the payments on their car. 他們感到很難償付汽車款。/ He agreed to make ten monthly payments of £50. 他同意每月付款50英鎊，十次付清。/ Thousands of its customers are in arrears with loans and mortgage payments. 有數千客戶拖欠其貸款和抵押貸款還款。/ The fund will make payments of just over £1 billion next year. 該基金明年的支付額剛好過10億英鎊。/ an extra s9 million in interest payments 額外的900萬美元利息款 / a generous payment for his services 為他的服務而支付的一大筆錢 / payments to the landlord from his tenants 房客支付給房東的款項 / the balance of payments（經濟學術語）國際收支差額 / measures designed to reduce the balance of payments deficit 旨在降低國際收支差額的措施
           
<span class="vocabulary">**instalment**</span> [ɪnˈstɔ:lmənt]
<span class="definition">n. [C]（分期付款的）一期付款：</span>The amounts are repayable by instalment.（by尤為英式用法）這些款項可以分期償還。/ Repayment is in ten instalments. 還款分十期支付。/ You can pay it in instalments. 你可以分期付款。/ We paid for the car by/in instalments. 我們以分期付款買了這輛車。/ The final instalment on the loan is due next week. 貸款的最後一期付款下個星期到期。/ They were unable to keep up (= continue to pay regularly) the instalments. 他們未能繼續按時交付這筆分期付款。/ The first instalment of £1 per share is payable on application. 每股1英鎊的首期付款應在申請時交付。/ the first month's interest instalment on the loan 貸款第一個月的利息還款 / repayment is by fixed monthly instalments 透過每月定額分期付款方式進行的償付1equal 等額分期付款 / a loan repaid in equal annual instalments 每年等額分期償還的貸款 / She sold the car before she had paid the instalments. 她未交完分期付款就把車賣掉了。/ The next instalment is not due until July. 下一期付款要到七月才交。/ instalment payment 分期支付的款項 / instalment loan（美）分期支付的貸款 / We offer an installment plan. 我們提供一套分期付款方案。

<span class="vocabulary">**payable**</span> [ˈpeɪəbl]
<span class="definition">adj. 1 [名詞前不可用] 應付的、可支付的：</span>A 10% deposit is payable in advance. 須預付10%的押金。/ The price is payable in monthly instalments. 本價格可按月分期付款。/ the date on which the rent becomes payable 應該支付租金的日期 / the costs payable by a client 應該由客戶支付的費用 / No tax is payable on these earnings. 這些收入不需交稅。/ An initial fee is payable to the franchiser. 應付給授特許權公司一筆先期費用。/ payable in instalments/installments 可分期付款 <span class="definition">2 [名詞前不可用]（支票或郵政匯票）應支付給抬頭人、收款人等的：</span>Write, enclosing a cheque made payable to Cobuild Limited. 寫封信，附上一張支付給Cobuild有限公司的支票。/ Cheques should be made payable to Brighton Borough Council.（英）支票應開給布萊頓市政會。/ This amount is payable immediately. 這筆錢應即時支付。

周邊詞彙補充：
· collect可指（電話）由受話人付費的；
· transfer可指轉賬。
