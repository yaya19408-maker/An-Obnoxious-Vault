☀ <span class="category">**射擊 開火：**</span>
<span class="vocabulary">**shoot**</span> [ʃu:t] 
<span class="definition">vt.＆vi. 1 shoot (sth) (at sb/sth) / shoot sth (from sth) 用槍或其他武器射擊，發射：</span>to shoot into the air 向空中鳴槍 / a serious shooting incident 嚴重的槍擊事件 / Don’t shoot. 別開槍。/ to shoot straight 準確命中 / to shot back at an invading plane 向一架入侵的飛機還擊 / The airliner was shot down near Ukraine. 該航班在烏克蘭附近被擊落。/ to shoot an arrow 射箭 <span class="definition">2（槍或其他武器）射出子彈等：</span>to shoot real bullets 發射真子彈 / to bend a bow to shoot arrows 彎弓射箭 <span class="definition">vt. 射殺或射傷人或動物：</span>He was summarily shot as spies. 他被當作間諜立即處決。/ He was shot in the back. 他後背捱了一槍。/ to be shot on sight 被當場擊斃 / to shoot him dead 擊斃他 / The wolf has been shot dead. 那隻狼被槍殺。/ to shoot to kill 開槍射殺 / to be shot to death for a crime 因罪行被槍決 / He shot himself during a fit of depression. 他一時心灰意冷，開槍自殺了。

<span class="vocabulary">**shot**</span> [ʃɒt] 
<span class="definition">n. 1 [C] shot (at sb/sth) 射擊；射擊聲：</span>lucky shot 僥倖射中 / random shot 胡亂開槍 / warning shot 鳴槍示警 / a clear shot 乾脆利落的一槍 / cannon, gun, rifle shot 大炮開炮；槍炮射擊；步槍射擊 / I heard a pistol shot. 我聽到一聲槍響。/ A volley of shots rang out. 一排子彈砰砰射了出來。/ to take/fire a shot at the target 朝靶子開一槍 / The shot hit him in the chest. 子彈射中了他的胸膛。/ a single shot to the head 朝頭部開的一槍 / the opening shot in the election campaign（比喻用法）競選活動的頭一炮 / cheap, parting（均為比喻用法）shot 惡意中傷；離別時撂下的狠話 <span class="definition">2 [C] a good, bad, etc. shot 表示一個好的或糟糕的射手：</span>He was a good shot. 他曾是一名優秀的射手。/ a crack shot with a rifle 步槍神射手 / bad, poor shot 差勁的射手 <span class="definition">3 [U] 子彈中的鉛彈，為一同射出的很多小金屬彈：</span>瞭解即可 <span class="definition">4 [C]（複數依然為shot）舊時用大炮發射的石頭或金屬彈丸：</span>瞭解即可 <span class="definition">vi. 將子彈上膛：</span>瞭解即可

<span class="vocabulary">**plug**</span> [plʌɡ] 
<span class="definition">vt. [美，舊時，非正式] 朝某人射擊：</span>瞭解即可

<span class="vocabulary">**loose**</span> [lu:s] 
<span class="definition">vt. [正式] loose sth (off) (at sb/sth) 射出（子彈、箭等）：</span>瞭解即可

<span class="vocabulary">**fire**</span> ['faɪə] 
<span class="definition">n. [U] 射擊或火力：</span>heavy fire 密集的火力 / anti-aircraft fire 防空火力 / to pushed forward under the covering fire of artillery 在炮兵的火力掩護下向前推進 / enemy fire 敵方火力 / rapid fire 速射 / machine-gun fire 機槍火力 / to open fire on the enemy 向敵人開火 / to return fire 開槍還擊 / ‘Cease fire!’ He yelled.“停止射擊！”他大叫道。/ to hold the fire until the enemy came closer 等敵軍逼近點兒再開槍 / to draw the enemy’s fire 吸引敵方火力 / He was in the line of fire. 他處於射程之內。/ The EU came under fire from the US over its foreign policy.（比喻用法）歐盟在外交政策上受到美國的抨擊。<span class="definition">vt.＆vi. 1 fire (sth) (at sb/sth) / fire (sth) (into sth) / fire (on sb/sth) 從槍炮中發射子彈等，即射擊，開火：</span>He was ready to fire if anyone entered. 他準備好有人進來就開槍。/ He fired the gun into the air. 他朝天鳴槍。/ to fire blindly on protesters 朝抗議者胡亂開槍 / The first shot was fired. 打響了第一槍。/ Missiles were fired at the enemy. 向敵人發射了導彈。/ He purposely fired wide. 他故意射偏了。<span class="definition">2 槍炮射出彈藥：</span>We heard the sound of guns firing. 我們聽見槍炮射擊聲。/ A starter’s pistol fires only blanks. 初學者的手槍發射的只是空彈。<span class="definition">vt. 射箭：</span>She fired an arrow at the target. 她瞄準靶子射箭。
           
<span class="vocabulary">**gunfire**</span> [ˈgʌnfaɪə(r)]
<span class="definition">n. [U] 接連不斷的炮火；接連不斷的炮火聲：</span>an exchange of gunfire with the police 與警方的交火 / They were killed in an exchange of gunfire between riot police and demonstrators. 他們在防暴警察和示威者的交火中身亡。/ I could hear gunfire. 我可以聽到炮聲。/ The sound of gunfire and explosions grew closer. 槍炮聲和爆炸聲越來越近。/ heavy, intense gunfire 猛烈的炮火；密集的炮火 / scattered, sporadic gunfire 疏疏落落的炮火；零星的炮火 / enemy gunfire 敵方炮火 / celebratory gunfire 慶祝的炮火 / Bursts of celebratory gunfire could be heard on the streets. 能聽到大街上一陣陣慶祝的炮火聲。/ distant gunfire 遠處的炮火 / We heard the rapid crackle of automatic gunfire. 我們聽到自動槍炮發出快速的射擊聲。/ A burst of gunfire echoed across the square. 一陣槍炮聲在廣場上回響。/ The terrorists boarded the bus and sprayed the passengers with gunfire. 恐飾分子登上公共汽車，用槍掃射乘客。/ They sprayed gunfire into the crowd. 他們用槍向人群掃射。/ A convoy was hit by gunfire. 一支護航艦隊被炮火擊中。/ Heavy gunfire broke out in the capital last night. 昨晚首都響起了密集的槍炮聲。
           
<span class="vocabulary">**gunshot**</span> [ˈgʌnʃɒt; 美 -ʃɑ:t]
<span class="definition">n. [C] 槍炮的射擊；槍炮聲：</span>I heard the sound/noise of gunshots out in the street. 我聽著外面街上有槍炮聲。/ They heard thousands of gunshots. 他們聽到密集的槍炮聲。/ A balloon popped, sounding like a gunshot. 一隻氣球爆裂了，聽著像是槍響。/ We heard gunshots just before midnight. 快半夜時我們聽到了槍聲。
            
<span class="vocabulary">**barrage**</span> [ˈbærɑ:ʒ; 美 bəˈrɑ:ʒ]
<span class="definition">n. [C, usually sing.] 火力網，（尤指掩護士兵前進或掩護士兵發動攻擊的）火力掩護：</span>heavy, intense, massive barrage 猛烈的火力網；密集的火力網；強大的火力網 / artillery，missile, mortar, rocket barrage 炮兵火力網；導彈火力網；迫擊炮火力網；火箭火力網 / The artillery barrage on the city centre was the heaviest since the ceasefire. 針對市中心的炮火是停戰以來最猛烈的。/ The two fighters were driven off by a barrage of anti-aircraft fire. 兩架戰鬥機被防空火力網逼退。/ barrage balloon （防空的）阻塞氣球 / Troops unleashed a barrage of grenades. 士兵們扔出了密集的手榴彈。

<span class="vocabulary">**discharge**</span> [dɪsˈtʃɑ:dʒ; 美 -ˈtʃɑ:rdʒ]
<span class="definition">vt. [正式]（某人）開火、擊發（槍、炮）：</span>Lewis was tried for unlawfully and dangerously discharging a weapon. 劉易斯因非法和不安全的開槍而受到了審判。/ The police officer accidentally discharged a firearm while unloading it. 警官在給槍退彈時不小心走了火。<span class="definition">n. [C]（槍、炮的）擊發：</span>an accidental discharge from a dropped gun 槍掉在地上走火
 
<span class="vocabulary">**blast**</span> [blɑ:st; 美 blæst]
<span class="definition">vt.（朝某人）開槍射擊：</span>a son who blasted his father to death after a lifetime of bullying. 因從小到大一直被父親虐待而開槍弒父的男子 / Alan Barnett, 28, was blasted with a sawn-off shotgun in Oldham on Thursday. 28歲的艾倫·巴尼特於星期四在奧德姆被人用短管獵槍射殺。
           
<span class="vocabulary">**blaze**</span> [bleɪz]
<span class="definition">vt.&vi. ~ (away)（槍炮）連續射擊：</span>In the distance machine guns were blazing. 機關槍在遠處不停地射擊。/ Guns were blazing, flares going up and the sky was lit up all around. 炮火不斷，訊號彈此起彼伏，四周的天空都被照亮了。/ She took the gun and blazed away with calm and deadly accuracy. 她拿起槍，冷靜地連續射擊，彈無虛發。
               
<span class="vocabulary">**volley**</span> [ˈvɒli; 美 ˈvɑ:li]
<span class="definition">n. [C] 子彈（或石頭等）的同時齊發或群射：</span>A volley of shots rang out. 一排子彈呼嘯而出。/ The police fired a volley of bullets over the heads of the crowd. 警察朝人群的上方射出一排子彈。/ It's still not known how many died in the volleys of gunfire. 現在還不知道有多少人在槍林彈雨中身亡。/ Three mounted officers rode into the field after the volley. 槍炮一起開火後，三名軍官騎著馬衝進戰場。

<span class="vocabulary">**round**</span> [raʊnd] 
<span class="definition">n. [C] 一次射擊：</span>They fired several rounds at the crowd. 他們朝人群開了幾槍。

<span class="vocabulary">**pot**</span> [pɒt] 
<span class="definition">n. [C] 某人未經仔細瞄準所做的射擊，即盲目射擊，相當於potshot：</span>He took a pot at the neighbour’s cat with his air rifle. 他用氣槍向鄰居的貓打了一槍。

<span class="vocabulary">**spray**</span> [spreɪ] 
<span class="definition">n. [U, C] 槍械的掃射：</span>a spray of machine-gun bullets 一陣雨點般的機槍掃射 <span class="definition">vt.＆vi. 掃射：</span>The gunman sprayed the building with bullets. 持槍歹徒向那座房子掃射。/ to spray bullets into shoppers 朝購物者掃射

<span class="vocabulary">**burst**</span> [bə:st] 
<span class="definition">n. [C] 槍炮的一陣短促的射擊：</span>frequent bursts of machine-gun fire 機槍的頻頻掃射 / a rapid burst of gunfire 一陣急促的開火
           
<span class="vocabulary">**recoil**</span> [rɪˈkɔɪl]
<span class="definition">vi. 槍炮射擊時快速向後移動，即反衝，產生後坐力：</span>瞭解即可 <span class="definition">n. [U, sing.]（尤指槍炮射擊時的）突然向後移動：</span>The policeman fires again, tensed against the recoil, unleashing round after round at his assailant. 警察再次開火，緊抵著反衝機構，不斷向襲擊他的人射擊。/ I assembled the weapon, checked the firing and recoil mechanism and loaded it. 我把傢伙組裝起來，檢查了一下擊發和反衝機構，裝上子彈。
           
<span class="vocabulary">**cock**</span> [kɒk; 美 kɑ:k]
<span class="definition">vt. ~ a gun/pistol/rifle 扣（或扳）上扳機準備射擊：</span>His hands were too weak to cock his revolver. 他的手沒勁兒，扳不動左輪手槍的扳機。
    
說明：表“射擊”時，①shoot是此義的基礎通用詞；②shot是shoot的名詞形式（shooting是shoot的動名詞形式）；③fire是一個形象化用法，有時還可翻譯為“火力”。雖然其使用範圍和頻率與shoot難分高下，但如果在戰場上口頭下達射擊命令，常用fire，也許因為其發音適合大喊，因而較有氣勢和攻擊性，類似於漢語的“打”。

周邊詞彙補充：
· report可指槍炮的射擊聲；
· launch可指發射航天器、武器等。
