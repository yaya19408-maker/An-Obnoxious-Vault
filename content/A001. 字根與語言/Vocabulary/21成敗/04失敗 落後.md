☀ <span class="category">**失敗、挫折的名詞：**</span>
<span class="vocabulary">**failure**</span> ['feɪljə] 
<span class="definition">n. 1 [U]（在做某事或達成某事方面）失敗：</span>The success or failure of the plan depends on you. 這項計劃的成敗取決於你。/ All my efforts ended in failure. 我的一切努力最後都無濟於事。/ moral failure 道德敗壞 / academic, financial, military failure 學業失敗；財務失敗；軍事上的失敗 / to admit failure 認輸 / a sense of failure 挫敗感 <span class="definition">2 [C] 失敗的人或事物：</span>The whole thing was a complete failure. 整個事情徹底失敗了。/ He was a failure as a manager. 他當管理者並不成功。/ The movie was one of the rare failures in his career. 這部電影是他職業生涯中少有的敗筆之一。
           
<span class="vocabulary">**setback**</span> [ˈsetbæk]
<span class="definition">n. [C] 挫折；阻礙：</span>The team suffered a major setback when their best player was injured. 最優秀的隊員受了傷，使得這支隊伍的實力大打折扣。/ The breakdown in talks represents a temporary setback in the peace process. 談判破裂意味著和平程序暫時受阻。/ The move represents a setback for the Middle East peace process. 這個行動意味著中東和平程序受挫。/ He has suffered a serious setback in his political career. 他的政治生涯遭遇了一次嚴重挫折。/ financial, military, political setback 金融挫折；軍事失利；政治挫折 / His research has suffered a temporary setback. 他的研究遇到了暫時的挫折。/ She and her teammates experienced some setbacks along the way. 她和她的團隊一路上經歷了一些挫折。/ The company was dealt a setback, when a federal judge ruled against them.（deal尤為美式用法）因聯邦法官作出不利判決，該公司遭遇了挫折。/ He is struggling to overcome the latest setbacks. 他正努力戰勝新近遇到的挫折。/ That would represent a huge setback in the fight to change our criminal justice system. 那將會是在刑事司法體制改革中遇到的重大挫折。/ a further setback for the coal industry 煤炭行業的又一挫折 / a serious setback to his chances of re-election 他連任成功的一大障礙
           
<span class="vocabulary">**frustration**</span> [frʌˈstreɪʃn]
<span class="definition">n. [U] [正式] ~ of sth 受挫、受阻、挫敗：</span>the frustration of all his ambitions 對他所有抱負的打擊 / He was still seething with angry frustration. 他心裡仍因受到挫敗而滿含怒氣。/ He took his pent-up frustration out on his family. 他把因挫敗而鬱積的怒氣發洩在家人身上。/ I agree that we are accomplishing nothing at the moment, and I share your frustration. 我同意我們目前什麼也沒做成，我與你一樣有挫敗感。
               
<span class="vocabulary">**reverse**</span> [rɪˈvɜ:s; 美 rɪˈvɜ:rs]
<span class="definition">n. [C] [正式] 損失；失敗；（從成功到失敗的）倒退：</span>Property values have suffered another reverse. 房地產價值再次遭受損失。/ a damaging political reverse 具有破壞性的政治倒退 / It's clear that the party of the former Prime Minister has suffered a major reverse. 顯然，前首相所在黨派遭受了重大的挫折。/ Their forces have suffered serious reverses in recent months. 他們的部隊在最近幾個月裡嚴重受挫。
       
<span class="vocabulary">**reversal**</span> [rɪˈvɜ:sl; 美 rɪˈvɜ:rsl]
<span class="definition">n. [C] 從成功狀態向失敗狀態的轉變，如倒退。（通常涉及金錢損失的）失敗、挫折：</span>the team's recent reversal 球隊最近的失敗 / The company's financial problems were only a temporary reversal. 公司的金融問題只是暫時的挫折。/ They teach managers to accept reversals as a fact of business life. 他們教給管理人員要將挫折視為生意上的平常事。/ a reversal of fortunes 遭逢厄運 / Cheaper imports played a part in the company's reversal of fortunes. 更為廉價的進口商品使公司形勢惡化。
 
<span class="vocabulary">**defeat**</span> [dɪ'fi:t] 
<span class="definition">n. [U, C] 在戰鬥、遊戲、競賽等活動中失敗，挫敗，也可指未達成自己想實現的目標：</span>election defeat 競選失敗 / to reverse a defeat 扭轉敗局 / a military defeat 軍事上的失敗 / The team went down to their fifth consecutive defeat. 該隊連續第五次失利。/ a narrow defeat 惜敗 / to admit defeat 認輸
                      
<span class="vocabulary">**eclipse**</span> [ɪˈklɪps]
<span class="definition">n. [sing, U]（重要性、權勢等的）喪失，尤指由於另一者變得更重要、更有權勢等：</span>The election result marked the eclipse of the right wing. 選舉結果標誌著右翼的失勢。/ Her work was in eclipse for most of the 20th century. 她的作品在20世紀大部分時間裡都湮沒無聞。
           
<span class="vocabulary">**thrashing**</span> [ˈθræʃɪŋ]
<span class="definition">n. [C] [非正式]（在比賽或競爭中）大敗：</span>Can the New Zealand bowlers fight back after their thrashing at Christchurch? 在克賴斯特徹奇遭遇慘敗後，紐西蘭的投球手們還有還手之力嗎？/ a school renowned for handing out 10-goal thrashings in association football 以在足球賽中多次踢進 10 球大獲全勝而聞名的學校 / The team suffered a thrashing on Sunday. 該隊星期天遭遇了一場慘敗。

<span class="vocabulary">**beating**</span> [ˈbi:tɪŋ]
<span class="definition">n. [C] [非正式]（競爭或選舉中的）慘敗、嚴重受挫：</span>The team has taken a few beatings this season. 本賽季該隊已經幾次嚴重受挫。/ Our firm has taken a terrible beating in recent years. 我們公司近幾年敗得一塌糊塗。
           
<span class="vocabulary">**hammering**</span> [ˈhæmərɪŋ]
<span class="definition">n. [C, usually sing.] [英，非正式] 嚴重的挫敗：</span>Our team took a real hammering in the first half. 上半場我隊吃了個大敗仗。/ They took a real hammering at the hands of their opponents. 他們被對手打得一敗塗地。
           
<span class="vocabulary">**rout**</span> [raʊt]
<span class="definition">n. [sing.] 潰敗、徹底失敗：</span>The game ended in a total rout. 這場比賽以一敗塗地告終。/ They put the rebel army to rout. 他們徹底擊潰了叛軍。

<span class="vocabulary">**loss**</span> [lɒs] 
<span class="definition">n. [C] 比賽的失利，輸：</span>Brazil’s 2-1 loss to Argentina 巴西對阿根廷1:2的落敗
             
<span class="vocabulary">**massacre**</span> [ˈmæsəkə(r)]
<span class="definition">n. [C, U] [非正式] 運動或比賽、競爭中的慘敗：</span>The game was a 10–0 massacre for our team. 這場比賽我們隊以0：10慘敗。         
           
<span class="vocabulary">**collapse**</span> [kəˈlæps]
<span class="definition">n. [C, usually sing. U]（體系、機構、生意、行動等的）突然失敗：</span>economic, financial collapse 經濟崩潰；財務崩潰 / The war has led to the collapse of agriculture in the area. 戰爭導致那一地區農業崩潰。/ a collapse into anarchy 崩潰陷入無政府狀態 / the collapse of law and order in the area 該地區治安的癱瘓 / The peace talks were on the verge of collapse. 和平談判瀕於破裂。
           
<span class="vocabulary">**paralysis**</span> [pəˈræləsɪs]
<span class="definition">n. [U]（活動、工作等）能力的完全喪失，癱瘓：</span>The strike caused total paralysis in the city. 罷工使這座城市完全癱瘓。/ The country was on the verge of complete paralysis due to the strike. 由於罷工，該國瀕臨全面癱瘓。/ The crisis over the constitution may lead to political paralysis. 憲法危機可能會導致政治上的癱瘓。/ The paralysis of the leadership leaves the army without its supreme command. 領導層癱瘓導致軍隊沒有最高指揮。/ a kind of mental paralysis 一種精神癱瘓狀態 / emotional paralysis 情感上的癱瘓

<span class="vocabulary">**flop**</span> [flɒp; 美 flɑ:p]
<span class="definition">n. [C]（電影、戲劇、聚會等）失敗：</span>It is the public who decide whether a film is a hit or a flop. 決定一部電影會走紅還是慘敗的是大眾。/ The show was the biggest flop in TV history. 該節目是電視史上最大的失敗。/ The concert may prove an expensive flop unless more people decide to go. 除非有更多的觀眾決定去聽這場音樂會，否則將會虧大本。/ box-office flop 票房上的失敗 / commercial flop 商業上的失敗
           
<span class="vocabulary">**fiasco**</span> [fiˈæskəʊ; 美 fiˈæskoʊ]
<span class="definition">n. [C]（事件或企圖）慘敗，常指可恥的失敗或尷尬的失敗：</span>It was a bit of a fiasco. 這敗得有點慘。/ What a fiasco! 真是使人下不了臺！/ The blame for the Charleston fiasco did not lie with him. 查爾斯頓慘敗錯不在他。/ Full responsibility for the fiasco lies with the PR department. 這次慘敗，公關部門要負完全責任。

<span class="vocabulary">**blow**</span> [bləʊ] 
<span class="definition">n. [C] blow (to sb/sth) 使人悲傷或失望的突發性事件，即挫折，打擊：</span>Losing his job came as a terrible blow to him. 失業給他造成了沉重的打擊。/ It was a shattering blow to her pride. 那事徹底摧毀了她的自尊心。/ a mortal blow to local industry 對地方工業的致命打擊 / His defeat dealt a crushing blow to the party. 他的失敗對該黨造成了毀滅性的打擊。

<span class="vocabulary">**bomb**</span> [bɒm] 
<span class="definition">n. [sing.] a bomb 美國非正式用法可指一次徹底的失敗：</span>The musical was a complete bomb on Broadway. 那出音樂劇在百老匯的演出完全失敗。

<span class="vocabulary">**dog**</span> [dɒɡ] 
<span class="definition">n. [C] [尤美，非正式] 低質量的一個東西；一次失敗：</span>Her last movie was an absolute dog. 她的最後一部影片徹底砸鍋了。

<span class="vocabulary">**turkey**</span> ['tə:kɪ] 
<span class="definition">n. [C] [非正式] 一次失敗：</span>a real turkey 一大敗筆
     
<span class="vocabulary">**demise**</span> [dɪˈmaɪz]
<span class="definition">n. [sing.]（大型機構、主義、公司等的）失敗：</span>the demise of the reform movement 改革運動的消亡 / The war brought about the industry's sudden demise. 戰爭導致這個行業突然垮了。/ He praised the union's aims but predicted its early demise. 他對聯盟的目標表示讚賞，但預言聯盟很快會失敗。/ the events which contributed to his political demise 導致他政治失敗的事件 / the demise of the USSR 蘇聯的解體

<span class="vocabulary">**loser**</span> [ˈlu:zə(r)]
<span class="definition">n. 1 [C]（比賽、競爭等的）失敗者、輸家：</span>the Dallas Cowboys and Buffalo Bills, the winners and losers of this year's Super Bowl. 達拉斯牛仔隊和布法羅比爾隊，今年超級碗的贏家和輸家 / He's a bad loser (= he accepts defeat well/badly). 他是個輸不起的人。/ He's extremely competitive and a bad loser. 他極好強，卻又輸不起。/ perennial loser（尤美）屢戰屢敗者 / The team has been transformed from perennial losers into real contenders. 這個隊已經從常敗隊轉變成了有力的競爭者。/ sure loser（美）必然的輸家 / The main loser was the Unity Party, which lost eight seats. 大輸家是統一黨，失去了八個席位。/ The movie company thought they'd backed a loser until the film won an Oscar. 在該影片獲得奧斯卡獎之前，電影公司以為他們支援錯了物件。<span class="definition">2 [C] 屢屢失敗的人（尤指評價較低者）：</span>She's one of life's losers. 她是個生活的失敗者。/ He's a born loser. 他生來就是個失敗者。/ They've only been trained to compete with other men, so a successful woman can make them feel like a real loser. 他們只被教過要和其他男人一較高下，所以一個成功的女人會令他們覺得特別失敗。/ She made me feel like a complete loser. 她讓我覺得自己像個徹底的失敗者。/ He's just a pathetic loser（= a stupid unsuccessful person）. 他不過是個可憐的失敗者。

<span class="vocabulary">**disaster**</span> [dɪ'zɑːstə] 
<span class="definition">n. [C, U] [非正式] 一個徹底失敗的人或事：</span>As a teacher, he’s a disaster. 他當老師壓根兒就不稱職。/ The play’s first night was a total disaster. 這出戏頭一晚就徹底演砸了。/ business, electoral, political disaster 生意上的失敗；選舉上的失敗；政治上的失敗 / The shirt was a definite fashion disaster. 這件襯衫毫無時尚可言。/ Buying that house turned out to be a financial disaster. 買那座房子結果證明是財務上的失敗。/ High-rise buildings proved a social disaster. 高層建築證明是個社會災難。
                      
<span class="vocabulary">**havoc**</span> [ˈhævək]
<span class="definition">n. [U] 比喻意義上的災害、浩劫：</span>Continuing strikes are beginning to play havoc with the national economy. 持續的罷工開始嚴重破壞國家經濟。/ The new tax could wreak havoc among smaller companies. 新稅種可能會給小公司帶來一場大災難。/ High winds have been creating havoc for farmers. 大風給農民帶來了嚴重的災害。/ The fog played havoc with flight schedules. 大霧擾亂了航班時刻表。

<span class="vocabulary">**tatters**</span> [ˈtætəz; 美 -tərz]
<span class="definition">n. [pl.] in ~（計劃、心情等）千瘡百孔，歷經挫折，不堪一擊：</span>The government's education policy lies in tatters. 政府的教育政策徹底破產了。/ The economy is in tatters. 經濟已千瘡百孔。/ Her reputation was in tatters. 她已名譽掃地。/ Her marriage now lay in tatters. 她的婚姻現已破裂。/ an injury that left his dreams in tatters 令他夢想破滅的一次受傷 / With their plans now in tatters, they gave up. 如今計劃失敗，他們放棄了。

<span class="vocabulary">**bottom**</span> ['bɒtəm] 
<span class="definition">n. [sing.] bottom (of sth)（在一個班級或清單等中）最低（或最末）的位置；在此位置上（即排名最後）的一個人或一個團隊等：</span>a team at the bottom of the league 聯盟排名最後的一個隊 / I was always bottom of the class in math. 我的數學成績總是班上最後一名。/ He started at the bottom and worked his way up through the company. 他從公司最底層幹起，一步步升至高層。 

<span class="vocabulary">**pack**</span> [pæk] 
<span class="definition">n. [C] 比賽或競爭中落後者的統稱：</span>measures aimed at keeping the company ahead of the pack 旨在使公司領先所有競爭對手的措施

周邊詞彙補充：
· mishap可指小事故；（不會產生嚴重結果的）小黴運、晦氣。

☀ <span class="category">**衰落、衰退、垮臺的名詞：**</span>
<span class="vocabulary">**decline**</span> [dɪ'klaɪn] 
<span class="definition">n. 衰落：</span>a steady decline in manufacturing 製造業的持續衰退 / economic, urban decline 經濟衰退；城市衰落 / the moral decline of the nation 這個民族的道德淪喪 / national decline 民族衰落 / the decline of business 生意下滑 / the decline and fall of a great civilization 一個偉大文明的衰落

<span class="vocabulary">**dip**</span> [dɪp] 
<span class="definition">n. [C]（通常指短暫的）成功的衰退：</span>The nation is experiencing an economic dip. 該國經濟正在衰退。

<span class="vocabulary">**dive**</span> [daɪv] 
<span class="definition">n. [C] 地位的突然暴跌，常見於通訊用語：</span>The economy is on a headlong dive to disaster. 經濟急劇下滑，就要釀成災難。/ His acting career took a nose dive. 他的演藝生涯突然一落千丈。

<span class="vocabulary">**fall**</span> [fɔ:l] 
<span class="definition">n. [sing.] fall (of sth)（政權或經濟等的）垮臺或崩潰；成功的喪失（即崩潰）；戰爭中（城市、國家等）的失守、淪陷或滅亡：</span>the fall of the Roman Empire 羅馬帝國的滅亡 / the rise and fall of British industry 英國工業的興衰 / the fall of Berlin 柏林的淪陷 / the fall of the dictator 獨裁者的垮臺
                       
<span class="vocabulary">**downfall**</span> [ˈdaʊnfɔ:l]
<span class="definition">n. [sing.] 一個人的金錢、權力、社會地位等的喪失；也可指造成上述喪失的原因。如衰落、衰敗、垮臺：</span>The sex scandal finally led to his downfall. 這樁緋聞最終使他身敗名裂。/ a scandal that brought about his downfall 導致他垮臺的醜聞 / The failure of this plan contributed to her eventual downfall. 這個計劃的失敗促使她最終垮臺。/ His lack of experience had led to his downfall. 經驗不足導致了他的下臺。/ His greed was eventually his downfall. 貪婪最終導致了他的垮臺。/ His honesty had been his downfall. 他敗在誠實。/ Alan's downfall was women. 艾倫栽在了“女人”這兩個字上。/ people wishing to see the downfall of the government 希望看到政府倒臺的人們 / They were found guilty of plotting the downfall of the government. 他們被判犯有密謀推翻政府罪。/ The movement's downfall came with the failed coup d'etat. 政變流產了，這場運動也隨之失敗。

<span class="vocabulary">**descent**</span> [dɪˈsent]
<span class="definition">n. [sing.] 形勢的惡化：</span>the country's swift descent into anarchy 國家迅速陷入無政府狀態 / The only lasting solution lies in a political settlement. Without it, their descent into chaos will be guaranteed. 唯一的長久之計是透過政治手段解決。否則，他們勢必會陷入一片混亂。           

<span class="vocabulary">**decay**</span> [dɪˈkeɪ]
<span class="definition">n. [U]（社會、機構、制度等的）衰敗，衰退，衰落：</span>industrial decay（尤英）工業衰敗 / urban decay 城市衰落 / economic decay 經濟衰退 / moral, social decay 道德敗壞；社會衰落          
           
<span class="vocabulary">**disintegration**</span> [dɪsˌɪntɪˈgreɪʃn]
<span class="definition">n. [U]（國家、組織機構、制度、價值觀等的）瓦解：</span>the gradual disintegration of traditional values 傳統價值觀的逐漸瓦解 / political disintegration 政治解體 / indicators of social disintegration such as divorce, suicide and petty theft 社會解體的標誌，例如離婚、自殺和小偷小摸 / This defeat led to the disintegration of the empire. 這次戰敗導致了帝國的瓦解。
            

<span class="vocabulary">**break-up**</span>
<span class="definition">n. 1 [C]（婚姻、家庭、關係或聯絡的）破裂，終結：</span>family, marital, marriage, relationship break-up 家庭解體；婚姻破裂；關係破裂 / bad, messy, painful break-up 糟糕的分手；麻煩的分手；痛苦的分手 / Since the break-up of his marriage he had not formed any new relationships. 他婚姻破裂後沒有談新朋友。/ the acrimonious break-up of the meeting's first session 在爭吵中結束的第一次會議 <span class="definition">2 [C]（組織機構或國家的）分裂、解體：</span>the break-up of British Rail for privatization 在私有化過程中英國鐵路公司的解體 / At no time did a majority of Czechoslavakia's citizens support the country's break-up. 大多數捷克斯洛伐克公民一直反對國家分裂。
           
<span class="vocabulary">**breakdown**</span> [ˈbreɪkdaʊn]
<span class="definition">n. <span class="vocabulary">**bankruptcy**</span> [ˈbæŋkrʌptsi]

<span class="vocabulary">**erosion**</span> [ɪˈrəʊʒn; 美 ɪˈroʊʒn]
<span class="definition">n. [U]（道德的）淪喪：</span>the erosion of moral standards 道德淪喪

<span class="vocabulary">**twilight**</span> [ˈtwaɪlaɪt]
<span class="definition">n. [U] the ~ (of sth) 沒落時期、衰退期：</span>Now both men are in the twilight of their careers. 現在兩人的職業生涯都到了沒落期。
           
<span class="vocabulary">**laggard**</span> [ˈlægəd; 美 -gərd]
<span class="definition">n. [C] [舊時] 一個遲鈍懶惰的人、機構、公司、產品、國家等，如落後者：</span>The company has developed a reputation as a technological laggard in the personal-computer arena. 該公司在個人電腦領域因技術落後遭人詬病。

☀ <span class="category">**失敗、輸掉、落後、垮臺、衰退的動詞：**</span>
<span class="vocabulary">**fail**</span> [feɪl] 
<span class="definition">vi. fail (in sth) 不成功達成某事，未能做某事。如失敗：</span>I failed in my attempt to persuade her. 我未能說服她。/ She failed to get into art college. 她未能進入藝術學院。/ That joke never fails. 那個笑話屢試不爽。/ The song can’t fail to be a hit. 這首歌定會一炮而紅。/ an enterprise that was doomed to fail from the start 從一開始就註定會失敗的事業

<span class="vocabulary">**lose**</span> [lu:z] 
<span class="definition">vt.＆vi. lose (sth) (to sb) / lose (sth) (by sth) 被打敗；輸掉（比賽、訴訟案件、爭論等）：</span>to lose a game, to lose an election, to lose a battle, to lose a war 輸掉比賽；輸掉選舉；輸掉戰鬥；輸掉戰爭 / We lost to a stronger team. 我們輸給了一支實力更強的隊。/ He lost by less than 100 votes. 他以相差不到100張選票敗北。/ Win or lose, the important thing is to remain calm. 不管輸贏，關鍵是要保持冷靜。
            
<span class="vocabulary">**doom**</span> [du:m]
<span class="definition">vt. [常用被動式] ~ sb/sth (to sth) 使…註定失敗（或遭殃、死亡等）：</span>The plan was doomed to failure. 這個計劃註定要失敗。/ The marriage was doomed from the start. 這樁婚姻從一開始就註定要破裂。/ That argument doomed their marriage to failure. 那次爭吵使他們的婚姻註定要失敗。          
           
<span class="vocabulary">**collapse**</span> [kəˈlæps]
<span class="definition">vi.（體系、機構、行動等）突然或徹底地失敗：</span>This system has collapsed in most countries where it ruled. 這一體制在其佔統治地位的大多數國家都失敗了。/ His business empire collapsed under a massive burden of debt. 他的商業帝國無力承擔沉重的債務負擔而垮掉了。/ All opposition to the plan has collapsed. 所有反對此計劃的力量均已消除。/ Talks between management and unions have collapsed. 資方與工會的談判已告破裂。/ In November the strike finally collapsed. 罷工到十一月份終告失敗。/ The scheme collapsed in the face of determined opposition. 該計劃因遭到強硬的反對而失敗。/ The theory collapsed in the face of the evidence. 在證據面前，這一推測不成立。
                      
<span class="vocabulary">**rout**</span> [raʊt]
<span class="definition">vt. 潰敗、徹底失敗：</span>the Battle of Hastings at which the Norman army routed the English opposition 諾曼軍隊徹底擊潰英格蘭軍隊的黑斯廷斯戰役 / The Buffalo Bills routed the Atlanta Falcons 41–14. 布法羅比爾隊以41：14大勝亞特蘭大獵鷹隊。

<span class="vocabulary">**founder**</span> [ˈfaʊndə(r)]
<span class="definition">vi. [正式] ~ (on sth)（計劃等）由於問題或困難而失敗：</span>The peace talks foundered on a basic lack of trust. 由於缺乏基本信任，和平談判擱淺。/ The talks have foundered, largely because of the reluctance of some members of the government to do a deal with criminals. 對話已經破裂了，原因主要是因為一些政府成員不願意和犯罪分子達成協議。

<span class="vocabulary">**flop**</span> [flɒp; 美 flɑ:p]
<span class="definition">vi. [非正式] 完全失敗：</span>The film flopped badly at the box office. 這部電影票房慘淡。/ The play flopped on Broadway. 這出戏在百老匯砸了鍋。

<span class="vocabulary">**sink**</span> [sɪŋk] 
<span class="definition">vt. [非正式] 阻止某人或某人的計劃，使其失敗：</span>I’ve just sunk my chances of getting the job. 我葬送了得到那份工作的機會。/ If the car breaks down, we’ll be sunk. 要是車壞了，咱們可就慘了。
           
<span class="vocabulary">**misfire**</span> [ˌmɪsˈfaɪə(r)]
<span class="definition">vi.（計劃或笑話）不起作用：</span>Some of their policies had misfired. 他們的一些政策已經失敗了。/ Unfortunately their plan misfired badly. 可惜他們的計劃完全失敗了。

<span class="vocabulary">**crash**</span> [kræʃ] 
<span class="definition">vi. [尤英] 體育運動中可指在一場比賽中敗得一塌糊塗，即慘敗、潰敗：</span>The team crashed to their worst defeat this season. 那支隊遭遇了本賽季最嚴重的一次慘敗。

<span class="vocabulary">**tank**</span> [tæŋk] 
<span class="definition">vt.＆vi. 在美式英語中可指輸掉一場體育比賽，尤指故意輸掉：</span>She was accused of tanking the match. 有人指責她故意輸掉這場比賽。

<span class="vocabulary">**throw**</span> [θrəʊ] 
<span class="definition">vt. [非正式] 故意輸掉（本應獲勝的一場體育比賽或競賽）：</span>He was accused of having thrown the game. 他被指責故意放水，輸掉了這場比賽。
            
<span class="vocabulary">**lag**</span> [læg]
<span class="definition">vi. ~ (behind sb/sth) / ~ (behind) 發展緩慢；或比其他人、組織機構等緩慢。即落後：</span>She did well in her first year at school but then started to lag behind. 她第一年乾得很好，但後來就開始落後了。/ The party is still lagging way behind in the opinion polls. 該黨在民意測驗中仍然遠遠落在後面。/ Britain still lags behind most of Europe in its provisions for women who want time off to have babies. 英國在婦女生育福利方面依然落後於歐洲大多數國家。/ The restructuring of the pattern of consumption in Britain also lagged behind. 在消費結構重組方面，英國也落後於人。/ We still lag far behind many of our competitors in using modern technology. 我們在運用現代技術方面仍然遠遠落後於我們的許多競爭對手。/ Catering salaries lag far behind those of other sectors. 餐飲業的工資遠遠落後於其他行業。          

<span class="vocabulary">**trail**</span> [treɪl]
<span class="definition">vt.&vi. ~ (by/in sth)（尤用於進行時）（在比賽或競爭中）落後，失敗：</span>United were trailing 2–0 at half-time. 聯隊在上半場結束時以0:2落後。/ He scored again, leaving Dartford trailing 3-0 at the break. 他又進球了，使得中場休息時達特福德隊以 3: 0 落後。/ We were trailing by five points. 我們落後五分。/ The team are now trailing badly in the league. 現在這個隊在聯賽中遠遠落後於其他隊。/ They were trailing by 12 points until the last few minutes of the game. 直到比賽的最後幾分鐘，他們還落後12分。/ The Conservatives are trailing Labour in the opinion polls. 在民意測驗中保守黨的支援率落後於工黨。/ The polls showed the Tories trailing behind the Government by 17 per cent. 民調顯示，保守黨落後於政府 17 個百分點。/ This country is still trailing badly in scientific research. 這個國家在科研方面仍然大大滯後。

<span class="vocabulary">**fall**</span> [fɔ:l] 
<span class="definition">vi. 被打敗；淪陷；失守；（政權等）垮臺：</span>The coup failed but the government fell shortly afterwards. 政變雖然流產，但是不久以後政府便垮臺了。/ Troy finally fell to the Greeks. 特洛伊城最終落入希臘人之手。
            
<span class="vocabulary">**decay**</span> [dɪˈkeɪ]
<span class="definition">vi.（社會、制度、機構等）衰敗，衰退，衰落（即人、社會等的影響力減弱）：</span>Popular cinema seems to have decayed. 大眾電影似乎已經衰落了。/ Congress has tried dozens of approaches to revitalize decaying urban and rural areas. 國會已試行過數十種方法來重振正在走向衰敗的城鄉地區。/ decaying standards of morality 日趨低下的道德標準          
                    
<span class="vocabulary">**disintegrate**</span> [dɪsˈɪntɪgreɪt]
<span class="definition">vi.（國家、組織機構、制度、價值觀等）瓦解。算是一種形象化用法：</span>The authority of the central government was rapidly disintegrating. 中央政府的權威在迅速瓦解。/ During October 1918 the Austro-Hungarian Empire began to disintegrate. 在1918年10月間，奧匈帝國開始瓦解。/ The social fabric of this country is slowly disintegrating. 這個國家的社會結構正在慢慢瓦解。The country has disintegrated into separate states. 這個國家已經分裂成若干獨立的國家。

<span class="vocabulary">**crack**</span> [kræk]
<span class="definition">vi. 制度等崩潰：</span>The old institutions are cracking. 舊的制度正在瓦解。

<span class="vocabulary">**unravel**</span> [ʌnˈrævl]
<span class="definition">vi.（一個系統、計劃、關係等）解體、崩潰、瓦解：</span>His government began to unravel because of a banking scandal. 他的政府由於一起金融醜聞而開始瓦解。/ When she returned to America, the marriage unravelled. 她回到美國的時候，婚姻破裂了。
           
<span class="vocabulary">**fracture**</span> [ˈfræktʃə(r)]
<span class="definition">vt.&vi. [正式]（使）（團體、組織機構等）分裂：</span>Many people predicted that the party would fracture and split. 很多人預言該黨將分崩離析。/ The company was fractured into several smaller groups. 這家公司被肢解成幾家小公司。/ His policy risks fracturing the coalition. 他的政策有分裂聯盟的風險。/ It might be a society that could fracture along class lines. 它可能成為一個階級分化的社會。

<span class="vocabulary">**decline**</span> [dɪ'klaɪn] 
<span class="definition">vi. 衰退：</span>Support for the party continues to decline. 對該黨的支援繼續下降。/ Her health was declining rapidly. 她的健康狀況迅速惡化。/ The economy has declined sharply in recent years. 近幾年經濟急劇下滑。/ The market for these products is declining fast. 這些產品的市場正在迅速萎縮。

<span class="vocabulary">**fold**</span> [fəʊld] 
<span class="definition">vi.（一家公司、一齣戲劇等）由於失敗而倒閉或停演：</span>瞭解即可

<span class="vocabulary">**meet**</span> [mi:t] 
<span class="definition">vi. 遭遇失敗：</span>His attempt has met with embarrassing failure. 他的嘗試遭遇了尷尬的失敗。

<span class="vocabulary">**fare**</span> [feə] 
<span class="definition">vi. fare badly, etc. 在某種情況中不成功，相當於get on：</span>The party fared very badly in the last election. 該黨上次競選情況很糟。/ This movie fared poorly at the British box office. 這部影片在英國的票房銷售很差。

相關詞義延伸：
· misfire還可指（發動機）打不著火。也可指（槍炮等）射不出彈藥。

☀ <span class="category">**失敗、倒退、落後、墊底、出局的形容詞：**</span>
<span class="vocabulary">**backward**</span> ['bækwəd] 
<span class="definition">adj. 1 與取得進步的方向相反的，即倒退的，退步的：</span>She felt that going back to live in her home town would be a backward step. 她覺得回到家鄉生活就是沒出息。/ The current U.S. farm bill, however, is a big step backward. 然而，目前的美國農業法案卻倒退了一大步。<span class="definition">2 取得的進步比正常小的（即落後的）；發展緩慢的：</span>a backward part of the country 該國的一個落後地區 / backward children 後進生 / to be technologically backward 在技術上落後 
           
<span class="vocabulary">**adrift**</span> [əˈdrɪft]
<span class="definition">adj. 1 [名詞前不可用] [英] ~ (of sb/sth)（在體育運動中）分數落後的、排名在後的：</span>The team are now just six points adrift of the leaders. 現在該隊得分比領先的隊只落後六分。/ Aberdeen are nine points adrift of Rangers at the top of the Scottish League. 在蘇格蘭足球聯賽中，亞伯丁隊比排在首位的流浪者隊落後九分。<span class="definition">2 [名詞前不可用]（某事的發展）出了問題因而不能按原計劃發展的：</span>Our plans had gone badly adrift. 我們的計劃已嚴重受挫。/ We have seen this as an attempt to place the blame for a policy that has gone adrift. 我們已經認識到，這是把責任歸咎於一項已偏離方向的政策的一種企圖。

<span class="vocabulary">**bottom**</span> ['bɒtəm] 
<span class="definition">adj. 名次在最低的位置的：</span>We came bottom with 12 points. 我們以12分墊底兒。

<span class="vocabulary">**out**</span> [aʊt] 
<span class="definition">adj.（在比賽等活動中）出局的：</span>瞭解即可

<span class="vocabulary">**dead**</span> [ded] 
<span class="definition">adj. [非正式，貶]（一個地方）安靜的，死氣沉沉的，無活力的，無趣的：</span>The village was dead. 那個村莊死氣沉沉。
           
<span class="vocabulary">**bankrupt**</span> [ˈbæŋkrʌpt]
<span class="definition">adj. [正式，貶] ~ (of sth) 完全缺乏（有價值的東西）：</span>a government bankrupt of new ideas 完全缺乏新觀念的政府 / a society that is morally bankrupt 道德淪喪的社會 / ideologically, intellectually bankrupt 思想意識破滅；智力枯竭
      
<span class="vocabulary">**sunset**</span> [ˈsʌnset]
<span class="definition">adj. [只用於名詞前]（某事）衰落的：</span>瞭解即可
        
<span class="vocabulary">**decayed**</span> [dɪ'keɪd]
<span class="definition">adj.（社會、機構、制度等的）衰敗的：</span>瞭解即可

<span class="vocabulary">**dismal**</span> [ˈdɪzməl]
<span class="definition">adj. [非正式] 不成功的：</span>Their recent attempt to increase sales has been a dismal failure. 他們最近努力想提高銷售量，全是白費勁。

☀ <span class="category">**落後、倒退、出局的副詞和介詞和名詞：**</span>
<span class="vocabulary">**behind**</span> [bɪ'haɪnd] 
<span class="definition">adv.（成就、業績、進展等）在…之後：</span>The rapid development of technology means that she is now far behind, and will need retraining. 科技的快速發展意味著她現在已經遠遠落後了，將需要再培訓。<span class="definition">prep.（成就、業績、進展等）在…之後，即落後：</span>He’s behind the rest of the class in reading. 他的閱讀能力不及班上其他人。/ She finished second behind the American, Ann Cody, in the 800 metres. 她在800米賽跑中在美國的安•科迪之後跑到終點，位居第二。/ The work is 22 weeks behind schedule. 這項工作的進度比預計時間落後了22周。

<span class="vocabulary">**backward**</span> ['bækwəd] 
<span class="definition">adv. 與取得進步的方向相反，即倒退地：</span>This country is going backward. 這個國家正在倒退。

<span class="vocabulary">**out**</span> [aʊt] 
<span class="definition">adv., prep. 1 板球、棒球等運動中可指出局：</span>The West Indies were all out for 364. 西印度群島隊以364分全隊出局。<span class="definition">2 網球等運動中可指出界：</span>The umpire said the ball was out. 裁判員判球出界。
           
<span class="vocabulary">**dismissal**</span> [dɪsˈmɪsl]
<span class="definition">n. [U, C]（在板球運動中球員或球隊的）出局：</span>瞭解即可

· fall還可指（在戰鬥中）陣亡、被擊斃。也可指（人、組織等由於做錯了某事而導致的）威信的喪失、不再受別人尊敬。

周邊詞彙補充：
· fail可指不做某事，未做某事。也可指（健康或視力）變弱。
