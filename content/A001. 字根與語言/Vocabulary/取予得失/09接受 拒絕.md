☀ <span class="category">**接受的動詞：**</span>
<span class="vocabulary">**accept**</span> [ək'sept] 
<span class="definition">vt.＆vi. 願意拿別人所給予的東西，即接受，如接受建議、邀請等。是一個常規用詞，含義最廣，也最常用：</span>He asked me to marry him and I accepted. 他向我求婚，我答應了。/ to accept apology, to accept advice, to accept invitation 接受道歉；接受忠告；接受邀請 / to accept bribe, to accept his offer, to accept the job 收受賄賂；接受他的幫助；接受這份工作 / She said she’d accept $15 for it. 她說她要15元才賣。/ They accepted the court’s decision. 他們接受法院的判決。<span class="definition">vt. 1 認為合適（或足夠好）而接受某事物：</span>My article has been accepted for publication. 我的文章已被採用準備發表。/ This machine only accepts coins. 這臺機器只接受硬幣。/ Will you accept a cheque? 你收支票嗎？<span class="definition">2 accept sth (as sth) 繼續處於一種困境中而不抱怨（因為你意識到自己無法改變它），即忍受，容忍：</span>to accept the fact, to accept risk 接受事實；接受風險 / to accept poor working conditions 接受惡劣的工作條件 / He just refused to accept that his father was no longer there. 他就是不肯接受他父親已不在的現實。/ I had no choice but to accept the committee’s decision. 我別無選擇，只能接受委員會的決定。<span class="definition">3 accept sb (into sth) / accept sb (as sth) 使某人感到受歡迎並被視為一個群體的一分子，即歡迎，接納，反義詞為reject：</span>to be accepted by the local community 被當地社群接納 / She had never been accepted into what was essentially a man’s world. 她從未被這個本質上屬於男人的世界所接納。/ He never really accepted her as his own child. 他一直沒有真正接納她為自己的女兒。<span class="definition">4 accept sb (into sth) / accept sb (as sth) 允許某人使用某種服務等：</span>The landlord was willing to accept us as tenants. 房東願意把房子租給我們住。/ She was accepted to study music. 她獲准學習音樂。

<span class="vocabulary">**take**</span> [teɪk] 
<span class="definition">vt. 接受或收到。是一個簡單用詞，日常生活中較常用：</span>to take the job, to take bribes, to take a call, to take the blame for sb, to take advice 接受這份工作；收受賄賂；接電話；代人受過；接受勸告 / Does the hotel take credit cards? 這家旅館接受信用卡付款嗎？/ Will you take $10 for the book? 這本書10美元你賣嗎？/ The store took $100 000 last week. 這家商店上星期的營業額為10萬美元。/ She took the news of her father’s death very badly. 她難以接受父親去世的噩耗。/ Stop crying and take it like a man! 別哭了，像個爺們兒！
                      
<span class="vocabulary">**assimilate**</span> [əˈsɪməleɪt]
<span class="definition">vt. [常用被動式] ~ sth into/to sth 使吸收，使接受（想法、態度等）：</span>These changes were gradually assimilated into everyday life. 這些改變逐漸滲進了日常生活。/ I was speechless, still trying to assimilate the enormity of what he'd told me. 我無話可說，一時還無法接受他對我所說之事的嚴重性。

<span class="vocabulary">**embrace**</span> [ɪmˈbreɪs]
<span class="definition">vt. [正式] 接受一個想法、主意、建議、觀點、政治體制、信仰等，尤指熱情地接受：</span>to embrace democracy, to embrace feminism, to embrace Islam 信奉民主；信奉男女平等主義；信奉伊斯蘭教 / the only party which fully embraces the concept of a united Europe 真心接受“一個統一的歐洲”這個概念的唯一政黨 / widely embrace（尤美）廣泛接受 / Soccer has become more widely embraced by Americans. 足球已更加普遍地為美國人所接受。/ She embraced the feminist cause with enthusiasm. 她熱烈地響應女權運動。/ He embraces the new information age. 他迎接新的資訊時代的到來。/ The new rules have been embraced by government watchdog organizations. 政府監察機構已經欣然接受了這些新規則。

<span class="vocabulary">**welcome**</span> ['welkəm] 
<span class="definition">vt. 樂於接納或欣然接受：</span>I’d welcome any suggestions. 任何建議我都會愉快地接受。/ The proposals have been widely welcomed. 這些建議受到普遍的歡迎。/ I warmly welcome this decision. 我熱烈歡迎這一決定。/ We positively welcome applications from all sections of the community. 我們誠摯歡迎來自社會各界的申請。/ Economists cautiously welcomed the president’s initiative. 經濟學家們對總統的倡議反應謹慎。
           
<span class="vocabulary">**commend**</span> [kəˈmend]
<span class="definition">vt. [正式] 受到贊同、得到認可：</span>His outspoken behaviour did not commend itself to his colleagues. 他直言不諱的行為不受他同事的歡迎。/ The Rousseau model commended itself to a lot of early socialists. 盧梭提出的模式被很多早期的社會主義者接受。

<span class="vocabulary">**count**</span> [kaʊnt] 
<span class="definition">vt.＆vi. 被正式接受、接納或認可；正式接受、接納或認可：</span>Don’t go over that line or your throw won’t count. 別越過那條線，否則你的投擲就會被判無效。/ Applications received after 1 July will not be counted. 7月1日以後收到的申請書將不予受理。

<span class="vocabulary">**stick**</span> [stɪk] 
<span class="definition">vi. 被接受：</span>His friends called him Bart and the name has stuck (= has become the name that everyone calls him). 朋友們稱他巴特，這名字就叫開了。

周邊詞彙補充：
· acquiesce可指即使不認同，依然接受某事物而不爭辯，即默然接受、預設、默許；
· receive可指購買（或接受）贓物（即你知道是被盜的物品）。

☀ <span class="category">**接受的其它詞性：**</span>
<span class="vocabulary">**acceptable**</span> [əkˈseptəbl]
<span class="definition">adj. 1 為社會上的多數人所認同的、認可的：</span>Children must learn socially acceptable behaviour. 兒童必須學會社會上認可的行為舉止。/ It is becoming more acceptable for women to drink alcohol. 女性喝酒漸漸被人們接受。/ These actions cannot be considered remotely acceptable in a civilized society. 這些行為在文明社會中是完全無法接受的。/ Are all political groups equally acceptable? 所有的政治團體都同樣受歡迎嗎? / This crosses the boundaries of acceptable conduct. 這一行為超出了可容忍的範圍。<span class="definition">2 ~ (to sb) 好得可接受的、好得達到一定標準的：</span>We want a political solution that is acceptable to all parties. 我們需要一個各方都可接受的政治解決方案。/ We must come up with a solution that our customers will find acceptable. 我們必須想出一個顧客可以接受的解決辦法。/ For this course a pass in English at grade B is acceptable. 英語成績B級就可以學這門課程。/ They have thrashed out a compromise formula acceptable to Moscow. 他們經過討論達成了一項莫斯科可以接受的折中方案。/ a compromise that is acceptable to both sides 雙方都可以接受的折中方案 / an attempt to make the reforms acceptable to both sides 讓雙方都可以接受這些改革的嘗試 / They recently failed to negotiate a mutually acceptable new contract. 他們最近未能談成一個雙方都滿意的新合同。/ The air pollution exceeds most acceptable levels by 10 times or more. 大氣汙染程度超出了普遍認可的標準10倍或更多。/ Air pollution in the city had reached four times the acceptable levels. 這座城市的空氣汙染程度曾高達可接受標準的四倍。/ a grammatically acceptable sentence 合乎語法的句子 / socially acceptable terminology 合乎社會規範的術語
           
<span class="vocabulary">**agreeable**</span> [əˈgri:əbl]
<span class="definition">adj. [正式] ~ (to sb) 可被某人接受的：</span>The deal must be agreeable to both sides. 交易必須是雙方都可以接受的。/ We tried to negotiate a mutually agreeable solution. 我們試圖協商出一個令雙方都滿意的解決方法。
           
<span class="vocabulary">**credible**</span> [ˈkredəbl]
<span class="definition">adj.（因候選人、政策、體系等看似可能成功而）可接受的：</span>Community service is seen as the only credible alternative to imprisonment. 除監禁外，社群勞動被看作是唯一可接受的選擇。/ Mr Robertson would be a credible candidate. 羅伯遜先生將是可能當選的候選人之一。/ The challenge before the opposition is to offer credible alternative policies for the future. 擺在反對黨面前的挑戰是如何為未來發展提出其他可行性政策。

<span class="vocabulary">**palatable**</span> [ˈpælətəbl]
<span class="definition">adj. ~ (to sb)（想法、方法等）可接受的、合意的：</span>Some of the dialogue has been changed to make it more palatable to an American audience. 有些對白有所修改以適應美國觀眾的口味。/ a palatable way of sacking staff 可以接受的裁員方式 / That option is not very palatable. 那個選擇不太可以接受。

<span class="vocabulary">**acceptance**</span> [əkˈseptəns]
<span class="definition">n. 1 [U, C]（對禮物、邀請、建議等的）接受：</span>Please confirm your acceptance of this offer in writing. 請書面確認你接受這項提議。/ He made a short acceptance speech/speech of acceptance. 他作了簡短的獲獎演說。/ Car manufacturers are already producing small numbers of electric vehicles to test customer acceptance. 汽車製造商們已經開始生產少量的電動車輛以測試顧客的接受程度。/ The Party is being degraded by its acceptance of secret donations. 該黨因私下接受捐贈而蒙羞。/ I sent them more than 6,000 cartoons before I had my one and only acceptance by them. 我寄給他們6,000多幅漫畫，最後才有1幅得以採用。/ The idea was slow to gain general acceptance. 這一想法慢慢才被普遍接受。/ Alternative medicines are now winning greater acceptance among doctors. 替代療法現在越來越為醫生所接受。/ These concepts have gained broad acceptance among scientists. 這些觀念已得到科學家的普遍認可。<span class="definition">2 [U]（群體對成員的）接納、接受：</span>an effort to ensure that the disabled achieve real acceptance 為確保殘疾人真正得到接納而作出的努力 / society's acceptance of homosexuality 社會對同性戀的認可 / Your acceptance into the insurance plan is guaranteed. 你參加保險計劃一事已有保證。/ Social acceptance is important for most young people. 對大多數青年來說，為社會所接納很重要。/ acceptance address, acceptance speech（總統候選人接受政黨提名時所作的）接受提名演講 / acceptance letter 錄取通知書 <span class="definition">3 [U]（對逆境、困境等的）接受：</span>acceptance of death, acceptance of suffering 接受死亡；接受苦難 / his calm acceptance of whatever comes his way 他遇事泰然處之的態度 / Popular acceptance of authority had been an early aim of the Nationalists. 國家主義者的一個早期目標是推動民眾對權威的接受。/ Their attitude was condemned as blind acceptance of authority. 他們的態度被指責為盲目接受權威。

<span class="vocabulary">**please**</span> [pli:z] 
<span class="definition">exclamation. 用於客氣地接受某事或某事物：</span>‘Would you like some help?’ ‘Yes, please.’ “您需要幫忙嗎？”“是的，太感謝了。”/ ‘Coffee?’ ‘Please.’ “要咖啡嗎？”“那太好了。”

<span class="vocabulary">**well**</span> [wel] 
<span class="definition">exclamation. 用於表示接受不能改變的事實：</span>‘I can’t help you.’ ‘Oh, well.’ “我幫不了你。”“哦，好吧。”

☀ <span class="category">**拒絕的動詞：**</span>
<span class="vocabulary">**refuse**</span> [rɪ'fju:z] 
<span class="definition">vi. 拒絕（某人讓你做的事）：</span>Go on, ask her; she can hardly refuse. 去吧，去求她，她不大會拒絕。/ He flatly refused to discuss the matter. 他斷然拒絕商討這件事。/ She refused to accept that there was a problem. 她拒不承認有問題存在。/ He refused to cooperate. 他拒絕合作。/ The demand for an apology was pointedly refused. 讓對方道歉的要求遭到了斷然拒絕。/ The Court has repeatedly refused to bend on this point. 法院再三拒絕在這點上予以讓步。/ Workers should be entitled to refuse to work under these conditions. 工人應該有權拒絕在這種環境下工作。/ She offered them tea and it seemed churlish to refuse. 她給他們倒茶，拒絕的話好像沒禮貌。<span class="definition">vt. 1 拒絕（接受別人所給予你的東西），相當於turn down：</span>to refused an invitation, to refuse a job offer, to refuse an offer 拒絕邀請；拒絕工作機會；拒絕報價 <span class="definition">2 拒絕（給予某人所需之物），相當於deny：</span>They refused him a visa. 他們拒絕給他簽證。/ She would never refuse her kids anything. 她對孩子百依百順。

<span class="vocabulary">**reject**</span> [rɪ'dӡekt] 
<span class="definition">vt. 1 拒絕接受或拒絕考慮某事物（如一個論點、想法、計劃等）：</span>to reject an argument, to reject a claim, to reject a decision, to reject an offer, to reject a suggestion, to reject a proposal, to reject a plan, to reject an idea, to reject a charge 拒絕接受一個論點；拒絕接受一項要求；拒絕接受一個決定；拒絕接受一項提議（或報價）；拒絕接受一個建議；否決一項提議；否決一項計劃；拒絕一個想法；拒絕一項指控 / Their design was rejected in favour/favor of one by a rival company. 他們的設計遭到否決，而競爭公司的方案被選中了。<span class="definition">2（因某東西質量不夠好而）決定不去使用、售賣、出版等：</span>Imperfect articles are rejected by our quality control. 我們嚴把質量關，有問題的文章都被退回。/ Consumers have the right to reject faulty goods and demand a refund. 顧客有權拒收次品，並要求退款。<span class="definition">3 沒能給予一個人（或一隻動物）足夠的關心或關愛：</span>The lioness rejected the smallest cub, which died. 母獅排斥最小的幼獅，把它餓死了。

<span class="vocabulary">**decline**</span> [dɪ'klaɪn] 
<span class="definition">vt.＆vi. [正式] 禮貌地拒絕接受；禮貌地拒絕去做某事。常翻譯為“謝絕”：</span>to decline an offer, to decline an invitation 謝絕對方的主動幫助；謝絕對方的邀請 / I offered to give them a lift but they declined. 我主動邀請他們搭車，但他們婉言謝絕了。/ Their spokesman declined to comment on the allegations. 他們的發言人拒絕對這些指控加以評論。
             
<span class="vocabulary">**deny**</span> [dɪˈnaɪ]
<span class="definition">vt. 拒絕承認或拒絕接受某事：</span>She denied all knowledge of the incident. 她矢口否認知曉此事的任何情況。/ The department denies responsibility for what occurred. 這個部門拒絕為所發生的事承擔責任。<span class="definition">vt. [正式] ~ sth (to sb) / ~ (sb) (sth) 拒絕給予某人（其想要的東西）：</span>They were denied access to the information. 他們試圖取得這個情報被拒。/ Access to the information was denied to them. 他們無法得到這個情報。/ If he is unlucky, he may find that his ex-partner denies him access to his children. 如果不走運的話，他可能會發現前妻拒絕讓他接近自己的孩子。/ Don't deny yourself pleasure. 不要有樂不享。/ They were cruelly denied victory by a header from Reece. 里斯的頭球殘酷地葬送了他們的勝利。/ Children could be compelled to work on the farm, effectively denying them schooling. 孩子們可能被迫在農場勞作，這事實上剝奪了他們受教育的權利。/ The country's government systematically denies its citizens basic rights to free expression. 該國政府完全剝奪了其公民言論自由的基本權利。/ You cannot deny this opportunity to me. 你不能拒絕給我這個機會。

<span class="vocabulary">**rebuff**</span> [rɪˈbʌf]
<span class="definition">n. [C] [正式]（對一個友好要求、請求或建議的）不友好的拒絕：</span>Her offer of help was met with a sharp rebuff. 她主動幫忙，卻遭到斷然拒絕。/ Her efforts were met with a sharp rebuff. 他的努力遭到了斷然拒絕。/ He wanted sex with Julie but she rebuffed him. 他想和朱莉上床但被她拒絕了。/ He was too proud to risk a rebuff. 他太傲嬌，害怕被拒絕。/ humiliating rebuff 羞辱性的回絕 / The party suffered a humiliating electoral rebuff in 1954. 該黨在1954年的選舉中遭到選民的冷遇，很是丟臉。/ In a rebuff to the president, Congress voted against the bill. 作為對總統的回絕。國會投票反對了該法案。/ His proposals have already been rebuffed by the Prime Minister. 他的提議已經被首相否決了。/ She suffered a rebuff from her manager when she raised the matter. 她向經理提出這個問題的時候碰了一鼻子灰。
  
<span class="vocabulary">**repudiate**</span> [rɪˈpju:dieɪt]
<span class="definition">vt. [正式] 拒絕接受某事：</span>to repudiate a suggestion 拒絕一項建議 / Leaders urged people to turn out in large numbers to repudiate the violence. 領導們力勸人們集體出面反對暴力。
           
<span class="vocabulary">**veto**</span> [ˈvi:təʊ; 美 -toʊ]
<span class="definition">vt. 拒絕接受、拒絕做（別人建議的事）：</span>I wanted to go camping but the others quickly vetoed that idea. 我想去野營，但這個想法很快遭到了其他人的反對。
           
<span class="vocabulary">**negative**</span> [ˈnegətɪv]
<span class="definition">vt. [正式] 拒絕同意一項提議或一個請求：</span>瞭解即可

<span class="vocabulary">**pass**</span> [pɑːs] 
<span class="definition">vi. pass (on sth) 說你不想要別人給予你的東西，即免掉：</span>I’m going to pass on the dinner, if you don’t mind. 您若不介意，吃飯就免了吧。

<span class="vocabulary">**should**</span> [ʃʊd] 
<span class="definition">modal verb. 用於拒絕某事：</span>Why should I help him? He’s never done anything for me. 我幹嗎要幫他呢？他從來沒為我做過什麼。

相關詞義延伸：
· reject還可指拒絕接納一個求職者；
· repudiate 還可指正式或公開地說某事是錯的，如駁斥、批判。也可作為舊時用法， 指拒絕與某人再來往。

周邊詞彙補充：
· withhold可指拒絕把…給某人；
· dismiss可指駁回案件。

☀ <span class="category">**拒絕的其它詞性：**</span>
<span class="vocabulary">**refusal**</span> [rɪˈfju:zl]
<span class="definition">n. [U, C] ~ (of sth) / ~ (to do sth) 拒絕（做、給予或接受）：</span>the refusal of a request, the refusal of an invitation, the refusal of an offer 拒絕請求；拒絕邀請；拒絕建議 / His refusal to discuss the matter is very annoying. 他拒絕商量這件事，令人很惱火。/ her refusal to accept change 她對變革的拒絕 / His letter in response to her request had contained a firm refusal. 對於她的請求他在信中給予了堅決的回絕。/ the panel's point-blank refusal to release the information 委員會斷然拒絕披露資訊 / her utter refusal to do anything to help 她全然拒絕幫任何忙 / the enemy's steadfast refusal to accept defeat 敵人頑固地拒不認輸 / first refusal（英）優先購買權 / If you decide to sell your car, I hope you'll give me first refusal(= the opportunity to buy it before it is offered to others). 你要是決定把車賣掉，我希望你能給我優先購買權。/ Her appeals for funds to support the cause were met with outright refusals. 她為支援這一事業而籌集資金的呼籲遭到了斷然拒絕。/ He accepted my refusal with a shrug. 他聳聳肩，對我的拒絕表示了接受。/ a refusal by a patient to accept the recommended treatment 病人對推薦的治療方案的拒絕 / a refusal of consent（英，法律）拒絕同意 / a refusal of consent to blood transfusions 不同意輸血 / their refusal on religious grounds to perform military service（refusal on...grounds尤為英式用法）出於宗教原因，他們拒絕服兵役

<span class="vocabulary">**rejection**</span> [rɪˈdʒekʃn]
<span class="definition">n. [U, C]（對論點；想法；計劃等的）拒絕、回絕、不予考慮：</span>Her proposal met with unanimous rejection. 她的建議遭到一致否決。/ painful feelings of rejection 遭到拒絕的痛苦感受 / There is often a knee-jerk-of new ideas. 對新觀點人們往往不假思索地加以拒絕。/ Children who have had bad experiences fear rejection. 有過不幸遭遇的孩子害怕遭人嫌棄。/ It takes a strong personality to cope with rejection. 接受遭拒的結果需要很強的個性。/ rejection letter, rejection slip拒絕信；退稿通知 / a publisher's rejection slip 出版社的退稿通知 / the rejection of the child by its mother 母親對孩子的厭棄 / the rejection of an idea, the rejection of a proposal, the rejection of a theory 對想法的摒棄；對建議的摒棄；對理論的摒棄

<span class="vocabulary">**reject**</span> [rɪ'dӡekt] 
<span class="definition">n. [C] 因有毛病或故障而不能使用或售賣的一個東西，即廢品，次品：</span>瞭解即可
           
<span class="vocabulary">**veto**</span> [ˈvi:təʊ; 美 -toʊ]
<span class="definition">n. [C] ~ (on sth) 某人對某事的拒絕：</span>瞭解即可
           
<span class="vocabulary">**negative**</span> [ˈnegətɪv]
<span class="definition">n. [C] 拒絕或否定：</span>Seventy-nine voted in the affirmative, and none in the negative. 79人投贊成票，沒有人投反對票。
           
<span class="vocabulary">**intolerance**</span> [ɪnˈtɒlərəns; 美 -ˈtɑ:l-]
<span class="definition">n. [U, C]（對別人的行為或觀點）不容忍、不容異己、偏狹：</span>his intolerance of any opinion other than his own 他不能容忍自己觀點以外的任何觀點 / the professor's intolerance to any views other than his own 教授對異己觀點的排斥 / the intolerance of local residents towards/toward refugees 當地居民對難民的排斥 / religious intolerance 宗教信仰上的不容異己 / racial intolerance 種族偏狹 / We need to root out those who preach intolerance and hate. 我們需要清除那些鼓吹偏狹與仇恨的傢伙。/ The town began to show increasing intolerance of immigrants. 小鎮對外來移民開始表現出越來越嚴重的排斥。

<span class="vocabulary">**intolerant**</span> [ɪnˈtɒlərənt; 美 -ˈtɑ:l-]
<span class="definition">adj. [貶] ~ (of sb/sth)（對別的觀點或行為方式）不容忍的、偏狹的：</span>He was intolerant of both suggestions and criticisms. 他對建議與批評都不能容忍。/ intolerant attitudes toward non-Catholics 對非天主教徒的不寬容態度 / They are deeply intolerant of all opposition. 他們對一切反對意見都極為排斥。
           
<span class="vocabulary">**unthinkable**</span> [ʌnˈθɪŋkəbl]
<span class="definition">adj.（某事）在思想上難以接受的：</span>Her strong Catholic beliefs made abortion unthinkable. 篤信天主教讓她完全無法接受墮胎。

<span class="vocabulary">**off**</span> [ɔ:f, ɒf] 
<span class="definition">adj. [尤英，非正式] 只作表語，指不能接受的：</span>It’s a bit off expecting us to work on Sunday. 讓我們星期天上班工作，那可不太行吧。

<span class="vocabulary">**out**</span> [aʊt] 
<span class="definition">adj.（提議等）不能被接受的：</span>That idea is out, I’m afraid. 那個觀點恐怕是不能接受的。

<span class="vocabulary">**not**</span> [nɒt] 
<span class="definition">adv. 用於表示拒絕或不允許：</span>‘Some more?’ ‘Not for me, thanks.’ “再來點兒嗎？”“我不要了，謝謝。”/ ‘Can I throw this out?’ ‘Certainly not.’ “我把這個扔了，行嗎？”“當然不行。”

<span class="vocabulary">**near**</span> [nɪə] 
<span class="definition">prep. 拒絕與某人或某物接觸：</span>He will absolutely not go near a hospital. 他絕不去醫院。
