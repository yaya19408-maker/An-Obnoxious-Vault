☀ <span class="category">**阻止、阻擋、妨礙、干涉的動詞：**</span>
<span class="vocabulary">**stop**</span> [stɒp] 
<span class="definition">vt. 1 使…不再運動。即使停止：</span>A dog stopped her. 一條狗把她攔住了。<span class="definition">2 使程序中斷：</span>The question stopped Alice dead in her tracks. 這個問題一下子把艾麗斯問住了。<span class="definition">3 使結束、使終止：</span>瞭解即可 <span class="definition">4 stop sb/sth (from doing sth) [英亦可stop sb/sth (doing sth)] 阻止或防止某人做某事；阻止或防止某事發生：</span>to stop him from destroying the garden 阻止他破壞花園 / to stop air pollution 防止空氣汙染 / They tried to stop me from leaving. 他們嘗試阻止我離開。<span class="definition">vt.＆vi. 1 使…不再工作（或起作用）：</span>瞭解即可 <span class="definition">2 stop sth (from sth) 阻止金錢的支付，即阻止支付：</span>to stop a cheque 通知銀行停止支付支票 
           
<span class="vocabulary">**obstruct**</span> [əbˈstrʌkt]
<span class="definition">vt. 阻擋道路、入口、通道等（使…不能透過）：</span>Tractors and container lorries have completely obstructed the road. 拖拉機和集裝箱貨車已經將這條路完全堵死了。/ You can't park here, you're obstructing my driveway. 你不能在這裡停車，你擋住了我家的車道。/ A number of local people have been arrested for trying to obstruct lorries loaded with logs. 一些當地人因試圖阻止滿載原木的卡車通行而被逮捕。/ Drivers who park their cars illegally, particularly obstructing traffic flow, deserve to be punished. 非法停車的司機，尤其是那些阻斷交通的，應當予以懲處。/ First check that the accident victim doesn't have an obstructed airway. 首先要確保事故受傷者的氣道通暢。<span class="definition">vt. 遮擋視線：</span>The pillar obstructed our view of the stage. 柱子擋著，我們看不見舞臺。/ Claire positioned herself so as not to obstruct David's line of sight. 克萊爾為了不擋住戴維的視線挪了一下位置。<span class="definition">vt. 妨礙某人（或某事物）做某事，或妨礙某事的進展，尤指故意妨礙：</span>to wilfully obstruct a police officer in the execution of his duty 蓄意妨礙警察執行公務 / They were charged with obstructing the police in the course of their duty. 他們被指控妨礙警察執行公務。/ The authorities are obstructing a United Nations investigation. 當局正在阻撓聯合國的調查。/ terrorists attempting to obstruct the peace process 企圖阻礙和平程序的恐怖分子
           
<span class="vocabulary">**obscure**</span> [əbˈskjʊə(r); 美 əbˈskjʊr]
<span class="definition">vt. 使…難以被看到或聽到：</span>Trees obscured his vision; he couldn't see much of the Square's southern half. 樹木遮擋了他的視線，南半廣場的一大部分他都無法看清。/ The house was obscured from view by a wall. 房子被一堵牆擋住，看不見。

<span class="vocabulary">**block**</span> [blɒk] 
<span class="definition">vt. 1 一個物體擋住另一物體：</span>Black clouds had completely blocked out the sun. 烏雲完全遮住了太陽。<span class="definition">2 阻止某事發生、發展或進步，即阻礙：</span>to block imports of various cheap foreign products 阻止多種外國廉價產品的進口 / The programme has been blocked by the government. 該專案已被政府阻止。/ to block protesters’ attempts to assert their rights 阻止抗議者維權的企圖 / The group has moved to block the government’s proposals. 該團體已採取行動阻撓政府的提案。
           
<span class="vocabulary">**foil**</span> [fɔɪl]
<span class="definition">vt. [常用被動式] 阻止某事（尤指犯法的事）發生；阻止某人做某事：</span>to foil a plan, to foil a crime, to foil a plot 挫敗計劃；挫敗犯罪；挫敗陰謀 / Customs officials foiled an attempt to smuggle the paintings out of the country. 海關關員阻截了一次企圖走私畫作出境的陰謀。/ They were foiled in their attempt to smuggle the paintings. 他們走私繪畫作品的企圖未能得逞。/ A brave police chief foiled an armed robbery on a jewellers' by grabbing the raiders' shotgun. 一位勇敢的警長奪過了意圖襲擊珠寶店的劫匪手中的獵槍，挫敗了這起持械搶劫案。/ The idea of building a roof terrace was also foiled by the planning authorities. 建造一個帶棚陽臺的想法也被規劃局否決了。           

<span class="vocabulary">**hamper**</span> [ˈhæmpə(r)]
<span class="definition">vt. [常用被動式] 妨礙、阻止某人容易地做或達成某事（即使此人做事的過程變得困難）：</span>Rescue efforts were severely hampered by the bad weather. 惡劣天氣嚴重阻礙了救援工作。/ I was hampered by a lack of information. 我受到了資訊不足的牽累。
           
<span class="vocabulary">**hinder**</span> [ˈhɪndə(r)]
<span class="definition">vt. ~ sb/sth (from sth/from doing sth) 使某人做某事（或某事發生）的過程變得困難，即阻礙、妨礙：</span>a political situation that hinders economic growth 妨礙經濟發展的政治局面 / These killings have seriously hindered progress towards peace. 這幾起殺人事件嚴重阻礙了和平程序。/ These laws will hinder rather than help progress. 這些法律將只會妨礙而不是有助於進步。/ Some teachers felt hindered by a lack of resources. 有些教師因資源不足而感到困難重重。/ factors which might help or hinder a child's progress at school 有可能促進或妨礙孩子在學校發展的一些因素 / Does the fact that your players are part-timers help or hinder you? 隊員們都是兼職的，這對你有利還是不利？/ Further investigation was hindered by the loss of all documentation on the case. 由於案件卷宗全部丟失致使進一步的調查受阻。/ Landslides and bad weather are continuing to hinder the arrival of relief supplies to the area. 滑坡和惡劣天氣仍在阻礙救援物資運送到該地區。/ An injury was hindering him from playing his best. 受傷後他無法發揮出最高水平。/ A thigh injury increasingly hindered her mobility. 大腿受傷使她行動愈發不便。/ Bulky clothes tend to hinder movement. 肥大的衣服常常礙手礙腳。

<span class="vocabulary">**impede**</span> [ɪmˈpi:d]
<span class="definition">vt. [常用被動式] [正式] 拖延或阻止某事物的程序，使某人或某事物的移動、發展或程序變得困難：</span>Work on the building was impeded by severe weather. 樓房的施工因天氣惡劣而停了下來。/ The bad weather seriously impeded our progress. 惡劣的天氣嚴重地阻礙了我們的進度。/ Fallen rock is impeding the progress of rescue workers. 墜落的石頭阻滯了救援人員的救援程序。
                      
<span class="vocabulary">**impinge**</span> [ɪmˈpɪndʒ]
<span class="definition">vi. [正式] ~ (on/upon sth/sb) 對…有明顯作用（或影響），尤指有壞的作用（或影響），如妨礙：</span>He never allowed his work to impinge on his private life. 他從不讓他的工作妨礙私生活。

<span class="vocabulary">**inhibit**</span> [ɪnˈhɪbɪt]
<span class="definition">vt. 阻止某事發生，或使其發生得比正常情況更慢或頻率更低，即阻止、阻礙、抑制：</span>A lack of oxygen may inhibit brain development in the unborn child. 缺氧可能阻礙胎兒的大腦發育。/ to inhibit the development of creative thinking 抑制創造性思維的發展 / Wine or sugary drinks inhibit digestion. 葡萄酒或含糖飲料抑制消化。/ Alcohol significantly inhibits the action of the drug. 酒精會顯著地抑制這種藥物發揮作用。/ The high cost of borrowing is inhibiting investment by industry in new equipment. 借款的高成本抑制了企業對新裝置的投資。/ A small manufacturing sector would tend to inhibit growth in the economy. 製造業規模較小往往會抑制經濟的發展。/ It could inhibit the poor from getting the medical care they need. 那會使窮人得不到他們所需要的醫療保健。/ Officers will be inhibited from doing their duty. 官員們將無法履行職責。/ The fear of dismissal inhibited employees from raising problems. 員工們懼怕遭解僱，所以不敢生事。
           
<span class="vocabulary">**handicap**</span> [ˈhændikæp]
<span class="definition">vt. [常用被動式] 使某人的做事過程更艱難，即妨礙、阻礙：</span>British exports have been handicapped by the strong pound. 英鎊堅挺影響了英國的出口。/ Greater levels of stress may seriously handicap some students. 壓力的加重可能會給一些學生帶來嚴重的負面影響。/ The industrial injuries scheme takes into account the actual disability and whether it handicaps a person in working. 工傷賠償方案要考慮實際殘疾情況以及殘疾是否會影響其工作能力。
                      
<span class="vocabulary">**cramp**</span> [kræmp]
<span class="definition">vt. 阻止、阻礙（某人或某事物的發展或進步）：</span>Tighter trade restrictions might cramp economic growth. 較嚴格的貿易限制會妨礙經濟的增長。

<span class="vocabulary">**bar**</span> [bɑː] 
<span class="definition">vt. 1 用鐵條或木條封住，如把門插上門閂：</span>All the doors and windows were barred. 所有的門窗都加上了鐵條。<span class="definition">2 阻擋道路：</span>Two police officers were barring her exit. 兩名警察擋著她的出路。/ We found our way barred by rocks. 我們發現大石塊擋住了我們的路。<span class="definition">3 bar sb (from sth/from doing sth) 禁止、阻止或防止某人做某事：</span>The players are barred from drinking alcohol the night before a match. 運動員在參賽前夜不得喝酒。/ The curfew has effectively barred migrant workers from their jobs. 宵禁實際上使得流動工人無法上班。

<span class="vocabulary">**barricade**</span> [ˌbærɪˈkeɪd]
<span class="definition">vt. 透過設定路障來防禦或阻擋…：</span>The rioters barricaded streets with piles of blazing tyres. 暴徒用一堆堆燃燒的輪胎在大街上築起了路障。/ The doors had been barricaded. 門都被堵住了。    

<span class="vocabulary">**prevent**</span> [prɪ'vent] 
<span class="definition">vt. prevent sb/sth (from doing sth) 阻止某人做某事；阻止某事發生。具體可稱為阻止、阻礙、阻撓等：</span>These barriers prevent new companies from entering the market. 這些障礙使新公司無法進入市場。/ No one can prevent you from attending this meeting. 沒有人能阻止你參加這次會議。
<span class="vocabulary">**contain**</span> [kən'teɪn] 
<span class="definition">vt. 防止有害的事物蔓延或惡化：</span>to contain an epidemic 防止流行病的蔓延 / to contain a rebellion 遏止叛亂
           
<span class="vocabulary">**stunt**</span> [stʌnt]
<span class="definition">vt. 阻礙某人或某事物的正常生長或發展，如遏制：</span>The constant winds had stunted the growth of plants and bushes. 老是颳風，花草、灌木長不大。/ The heart condition had stunted his growth a bit. 心臟病已經多少影響了他的成長。/ His illness had not stunted his creativity. 疾病沒有扼殺他的創造力。/ High interest rates have stunted economic growth. 高利率已經阻礙了經濟的增長。
           
<span class="vocabulary">**suppress**</span> [səˈpres]
<span class="definition">vt. 壓制、阻止、抑制（某事生長、發展或繼續），尤指制止，阻止，抑制（身體的功能或反應）：</span>drugs that suppress the appetite 抑制食慾的藥 / The reproduction and growth of the cancerous cells can be suppressed by bombarding them with radiation. 可以透過放療殺死癌細胞來抑制其再生和擴散。/ the strongest evidence so far that ultraviolet light can suppress human immune responses 迄今為止可證明紫外線能抑制人體免疫反應的最有力證據 / The medication effectively suppressed the pain.藥物有效地抑制了疼痛。<span class="definition">vt. 壓制，抑制，阻止（程序或活動）：</span>'The Government is suppressing inflation by devastating the economy,' he said. 他說，“政府正在透過破壞經濟來抑制通貨膨脹。” / Lawyers said today's ruling would lead to higher prices and would suppress innovation of new products. 律師們稱今天的裁決將會抬升價格，並抑制新產品的創新。
           
<span class="vocabulary">**strangle**</span> [ˈstræŋgl]
<span class="definition">vt. 阻止某事的發展，如抑制、壓制、扼殺：</span>The current monetary policy is strangling the economy. 現行貨幣政策抑制了經濟的發展。/ The country's economic plight is strangling its scientific institutions. 該國的經濟困境阻礙了其科研機構的工作進展。/ His creative drive has been strangled by his sense of political guilt. 他的創造慾望已被他的政治負罪感扼殺了。/ This project should have been strangled at birth.（to strangle sb/sth at birth尤為英式用法）這個專案早該被扼殺在搖籃裡。
            
<span class="vocabulary">**deter**</span> [dɪˈtɜ:(r)]
<span class="definition">vt.&vi. ~ sb (from sth/from doing sth) 透過使某人感到做某事過程中的困難或此事的糟糕結果，而使其不去做某事（或使其不再繼續做此事），如阻嚇、阻止：</span>Supporters of the death penalty argue that it would deter criminals from carrying guns. 死刑的支持者認為它能阻嚇罪犯攜帶槍支。/ The alarm is usually sufficient to deter a would-be thief. 警報通常足以阻嚇一個想偷東西的人。/ Her words of warning would hardly deter him. 她的警告很難嚇住他。/ Arrests and jail sentences have done nothing to deter the protesters. 逮捕和監禁都沒能嚇倒抗議者。/ The skunk releases a pungent smell to deter the enemy. 臭鼬釋放出臭氣讓敵人望而卻步。/ Dogs can deter unwelcome intruders. 狗能阻嚇惡意闖入者。/ The windows are covered with grilles to deter vandals. 窗戶上裝了鐵柵以阻止故意破壞公物者。/ The lack of facilities in the town may deter the casual visitor. 鎮子缺乏便利設施，這嚇退了碰巧來參觀的人。/ Fear of failure should not deter you from trying. 對失敗的恐懼不應該阻止你去嘗試。/ I told him I wasn't interested, but he wasn't deterred. 我已告訴他我不感興趣，可他卻不罷休。/ The high price of the service could deter people from seeking advice. 這麼高的服務費可能使諮詢者望而卻步。/ These new rules are likely to deter people from coming forward for help. 新規則很可能阻礙人們尋求幫助。          
     
<span class="vocabulary">**hamstring**</span> [ˈhæmstrɪŋ]
<span class="definition">vt. [常用被動式] 阻止或妨礙某人或某事物，使其不能正常工作（或行動）：</span>If he becomes the major opposition leader, he could hamstring a conservative-led coalition. 如果他成為主要反對黨的領袖，可能會導致保守黨領導的聯合政府陷於癱瘓。
           
<span class="vocabulary">**preclude**</span> [prɪˈklu:d]
<span class="definition">vt. ~ sth / ~ sb from doing sth [正式] 阻止某事發生；阻止某人做某事；使某事成為不可能：</span>to preclude the possibility 排除可能性 / Lack of time precludes any further discussion. 由於時間不足，不可能進行深入的討論。/ He would rebuff enquiries in such a way as to preclude any further discussion. 他會斷然拒絕詢問，使得無法進行進一步的討論。/ His religious beliefs precluded him/his serving in the army. 他的宗教信仰不允許他服兵役。/ At 84, John feels his age precludes too much travel. 84歲的約翰感到自己年事已高，不能進行太多的旅行。/ A constitutional amendment precludes any president from serving more than two terms. 一項憲法修正案使得任何總統的任職不得超過兩屆。/ In some cases poor English precluded them from ever finding a job. 英語不好有時會讓他們找不到工作。
           
<span class="vocabulary">**stem**</span> [stem]
<span class="definition">vt. 阻止（流動的東西擴散或增長）：</span>The cut was bandaged to stem the bleeding. 傷口進行了包紮止血。/ They discussed ways of stemming the flow of smuggled drugs. 他們討論了遏制走私毒品流通的辦法。/ The government had failed to stem the tide of factory closures. 政府沒有控制住工廠紛紛倒閉的勢頭。/ Austria has sent three army battalions to its border with Hungary to stem the flow of illegal immigrants. 奧地利已派出三個營的兵力去本國和匈牙利交界的邊境地區阻止非法移民的流入。/ The authorities seem powerless to stem the rising tide of violence. 當局似乎無力阻止愈演愈烈的暴力活動。

<span class="vocabulary">**thwart**</span> [θwɔ:t; 美 θwɔ:rt]
<span class="definition">vt. [常用被動式] ~ sth / ~ sb (in sth) 阻止某人去做其想做的事：</span>to thwart sb's plans 阻撓某人的計劃 / Plans to expand the company have been constantly thwarted. 公司的擴張計劃不斷受挫。/ They were thwarted in their attempt to gain overall control of the company. 他們想全面控制公司的企圖遭到挫敗。/ She was thwarted in her attempt to take control of the party. 她控制這個政黨的企圖受阻了。/ The accounting firm deliberately destroyed documents to thwart government investigators. 會計事務所故意毀壞檔案，阻撓政府調查工作。/ Her ambition to become an artist was thwarted by failing eyesight. 她想成為藝術家的夢想由於視力衰減而破滅了。
            
<span class="vocabulary">**restrain**</span> [rɪˈstreɪn]
<span class="definition">vt. ~ sb/sth (from sth/from doing sth)（尤指透過武力）制止、阻止：</span>The prisoner had to be restrained by the police. 警察只好強行制住囚犯。/ They have obtained an injunction restraining the company from selling the product. 他們已經得到阻止這家公司出售這一產品的禁銷令。/ He placed a restraining hand on her arm. 他拉住她的胳膊制止她。/ Wally gripped my arm, partly to restrain me and partly to reassure me. 沃利抓住我的手臂，一是想制止我，二是想穩住我。/ The horse must be properly restrained in a location where it would not hurt itself. 必須把馬妥善關好，讓它不能傷著自己。/ She had to be physically restrained. 她不得不被綁了起來。          

<span class="vocabulary">**encumber**</span> [ɪnˈkʌmbə(r)]
<span class="definition">vt. [常用被動式] ~ sb/sth (with sth) [正式] 使某人做某事（或某事發生）的過程變得困難，即妨礙、阻礙、拖累：</span>The police operation was encumbered by crowds of reporters. 警方的行動被成群的記者所妨礙。/ The narrow quay was encumbered by hundreds of carts. 狹窄的碼頭被數百輛手推車堵得水洩不通。<span class="definition">vt. [常用被動式] ~ sb/sth (with sth) [正式] 大（或重）得難以移動；使負擔沉重：</span>The frogmen were encumbered by their diving equipment. 沉重的潛水裝備使蛙人行動困難。/ Lead weights and air cylinders encumbered the divers as they walked to the shore. 潛水員向海岸走去時，鉛墜和氧氣罐使他們步履維艱。/ It is still labouring under the debt burden that it was encumbered with in the 1980s. 它現在仍舊被20世紀80年代就深受其累的債務壓得喘不過氣。           
           
<span class="vocabulary">**retard**</span> [rɪˈtɑ:d; 美 rɪˈtɑ:rd]
<span class="definition">vt. [正式] 使某事的發展或程序變慢，即阻礙；減緩：</span>The progression of the disease can be retarded by early surgery. 早期手術可以抑制病情的發展。/ Continuing violence will retard negotiations over the country's future. 持續不斷的暴力活動會阻礙關係到國家未來的談判的進行。

<span class="vocabulary">**filibuster**</span> [ˈfɪlɪbʌstə(r)]
<span class="definition">vt. 以冗長演說（或長篇大論）進行阻撓（使表決不能進行，或法案不能透過）：</span>They simply threatened to filibuster until the Senate adjourns. 他們只是威脅說要以冗長演說進行阻撓，直到參議院休會。/ A group of senators plans to filibuster a measure that would permit drilling in Alaska. 一群參議員計劃透過發表長篇大論來阻撓一項允許在阿拉斯加州鑽探石油的議案獲得透過。

<span class="vocabulary">**prohibit**</span> [prəʊ'hɪbɪt]

相關詞義延伸：
· handicap還可指長期的生理或智力缺陷（如弱智、殘疾）（患病者失去了運用某個身體部位或智力的能力）。也可指讓步賽（使參賽者中的優勢方需要跑更遠的距離、增加負重等）；（讓步賽中給優勢方施加的）障礙，不利條件。亦可指（高爾夫球運動中）給弱者增加的杆數（按擊球次數計數，並隨參賽者的進步而減少）；
· hurdle還可指（供人或馬在賽跑中跨越的）一個跨欄。其複數形式hurdles也可指可指（人或馬的）跨欄賽；
· impediment還可指言語障礙（如口吃、結巴）；
· obstacle還可指障礙賽馬運動中需要馬跳過的柵欄、障礙。

周邊詞彙補充：
· fence可表示柵欄；
· wire可指鐵絲網；
· drag可指作用於飛機或其他交通工具的空氣阻力；
· stop可指管風琴的一排音管、管風琴的音栓；
· ditch可指田地或路邊的溝渠；
· trench可指在地上挖掘的一條長長的深溝（例如水渠）。也可指戰壕。還可指海底的長長的深海溝、大洋溝；
· moat可指（城堡等周圍的）護城河（起對敵防禦的作用）。
