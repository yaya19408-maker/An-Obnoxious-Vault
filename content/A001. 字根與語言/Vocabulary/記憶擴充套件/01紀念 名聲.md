☀ <span class="category">**紀念的動詞：**</span>
<span class="vocabulary">**commemorate**</span> [kəˈmeməreɪt]
<span class="definition">vt. 用一個特殊的活動或物品對昔日的某個重要人物或事件進行紀念；作為…的紀念物而存在：</span>A series of movies will be shown to commemorate the 30th anniversary of his death. 為紀念他逝世30週年，有一系列的電影要上映。/ The 200th anniversary of Mozart's death is being commemorated around the world with concerts featuring his work. 世界各地將舉辦以莫扎特的作品為主題的音樂會，以紀念他逝世200週年。/ A plaque commemorates the battle. 有一塊飾板用來紀念那次戰役。/ One room contained a gallery of paintings commemorating great moments in baseball history. 有一個房間裡陳列著很多紀念棒球史上偉大時刻的畫作。

<span class="vocabulary">**remember**</span> [rɪ'membə] 
<span class="definition">vt.（尤指祈禱時）紀念或緬懷某人，相當於commemorate：</span>a church service to remember the war dead 紀念戰爭死難者的教堂禮拜儀式

<span class="vocabulary">**mark**</span> [mɑːk] 
<span class="definition">vt. 慶賀或正式紀念你認為重要的一次事件：</span>a ceremony to mark the 50th anniversary of the end of the war 紀念戰爭結束50週年的慶典

周邊詞彙補充：
· souvenir可指為使自己想起（或保持記憶）一個地方、一個場合或假期而買下來的一個東西，即紀念品。也可指度假時買回來送人的東西。

☀ <span class="category">**名譽、名聲的名詞：**</span>
<span class="vocabulary">**reputation**</span> [͵repju'teɪʃn] 
<span class="definition">n. [C, U] reputation (as sth) / reputation (for sth/for doing sth) 人或事物基於過去所發生的事所擁有的名聲，名譽。是一個常規用詞，極為常用：</span>good, bad reputation 好名聲 / She has built up an enviable reputation as a writer. 她已經成為一名頗有名望的作家。/ international, historical reputation 國際聲譽；歷史上的聲譽 / corporate, professional reputation 企業聲望；專業聲譽 / academic, literary, scientific reputation 學術聲譽；文學聲譽；科學聲譽 / Her posthumous reputation has begun to grow. 她死後名氣開始漸漲。/ He has the reputation of being a hard worker. 他頗有工作勤奮的名聲。

<span class="vocabulary">**fame**</span> [feɪm]
<span class="definition">n. [U] 被很多人知曉並談論的狀態，即名聲、名譽、名氣：</span>to achieve/win instant fame 立即贏得名聲 / to rise/shoot to fame overnight 一夜之間成名 / The film earned him international fame. 這部電影為他贏得了國際聲望。/ She gained international fame as a dancer. 她是國際知名的舞蹈演員。/ brief fame 短暫的名氣 / posthumous fame 死後的聲譽 / He was enjoying his new-found fame. 他新近贏得了聲譽。/ Her fame rests on a single book. 她的成名源於單單一本書。/ a man to whom fame came very late 一個成名很晚的人 / His adventure brought him both fame and notoriety. 他的冒險行動讓他譭譽參半。/ in search of fame and fortune 追逐名利 / At the height of his fame, his every word was valued. 在他聲名鼎盛之時，他的每句話都受到重視。/ The town's only claim to fame is that there was once a riot there. 這個鎮唯一出名之處就是那裡有過一次暴亂。/ The restaurant's fame spread quickly. 這家餐館的名聲很快就傳播開來。

<span class="vocabulary">**renown**</span> [rɪˈnaʊn]
<span class="definition">n. [U] [正式] 人們因為你的事蹟而給予你的名譽和尊敬，即名譽、聲望：</span>He won renown as a fair judge. 他贏得了公平裁判的榮譽。/ a pianist of international renown 一位具有國際知名度的鋼琴家 / great renown 相當的知名度 / She used to be a singer of some renown. 她曾是位小有名氣的歌手。/ His work achieved little renown. 他的工作沒有獲得什麼聲譽。
           
<span class="vocabulary">**prominence**</span> [ˈprɒmɪnəns; 美 ˈprɑ:m-]
<span class="definition">n. [U, sing.] 著名：</span>international, national prominence 國際聲望；國內聲望 / political, public prominence 政治聲望；公共聲望 / a young actor who has recently risen to prominence 最近嶄露頭角的一名年輕演員 / He came to prominence during the World Cup in Italy. 他在義大利的世界盃賽中聲名鵲起。/ She has achieved a prominence she hardly deserves. 她實在不配享有這麼大的名聲。/ She came to national prominence as an artist in the 1960s. 她於20世紀60年代在國內贏得了藝術家聲望。/ The city's rise to prominence as a port began in the early 19th century. 這個城市在19世紀初期開始成為著名的港口。

<span class="vocabulary">**character**</span> ['kærɪktə] 
<span class="definition">n. [C, U] [正式] 人們對某人所持有的觀點，尤其是他們認為此人是否可信賴或可依靠，即人品；聲望；名譽；名氣。是一個基礎用詞：</span>personal attacks on my character 對我的人品進行的人身攻擊 / She was a victim of character assassination. 她是誹謗行為的受害者。/ My teacher agreed to be a character witness for me in court. 我的老師同意出庭做我的品德見證人。

<span class="vocabulary">**name**</span> [neɪm] 
<span class="definition">n. 1 [usually sing.]（某人或某事物所具有的）名譽、名聲；人們對某人或某事物所持有的觀點，可稱為名氣：</span>The college has a good name for languages. 這所大學的語言教學頗有名氣。/ He made his name writing travel books. 他寫遊記出了名。/ The area got a bad name after a series of nasty murders. 這一地區因一系列惡性謀殺案而留下惡名。/ Throughout his years in prison, he fought to clear his name. 在整個服刑期間，他奮力為自己洗脫罪名。/ The company has a name for reliability. 該公司以可靠著稱。<span class="definition">2 [C] 構成複合形容詞，指有…名稱的；有…名聲的或以…著名的（尤指為很多人所知曉的）：</span>a big-name company 著名公司 / brand-name goods 名牌產品 <span class="definition">3 [C] 一個著名的人，即名人：</span>a big name 大人物

<span class="vocabulary">**stature**</span> [ˈstætʃə(r)]
<span class="definition">n. [U] [正式]（一個人因其能力或成就所獲得的）聲望、名望：</span>heroic, public, world stature 英雄形象；知名度；國際聲望 / a writer of international stature 具有國際聲望的作家 / social stature 社會聲望 / His political stature increased during the crisis. 他在危機期間提升了政治聲望。/ a woman of great moral stature 有極好道德形象的女子 / She has grown in stature since winning the award. 她獲獎之後名聲大振。/ an actress of considerable stature 頗有名望的女演員 / Who can deny his stature as the world's greatest cellist? 誰能否認他是世界上最偉大的大提琴演奏家呢？/ The election result enhanced the party's stature. 選舉結果提高了該黨的聲望。/ This club has grown in stature over the last 20 years. 這傢俱樂部在過去 20 年間知名度大增。/ The orchestra has grown in stature. 這支管絃樂隊的聲望有所提高。/ Geomorphology has now achieved full stature as a branch of geology. 地形學現在已經獲得了地質學分支科學的正式地位。
           
<span class="vocabulary">**standing**</span> [ˈstændɪŋ]
<span class="definition">n. [U] 某人或某事物在一個群體或組織內的名聲：</span>public standing 在公眾中的聲望 / She was appointed for her high standing with the general public. 她因在公眾中享有很高的聲望而獲任命。/ the low standing of politicians with the public 在公眾中聲望低的政治人物 / national standing 國內聲譽 / an artist of international standing 具有國際名望的藝術家 / academic, moral, professional standing學術聲望；道德聲望；職業聲望 / Professor Greenan has a high standing in the academic world. 格林南教授在學術界名望頗高。/ her low standing among her fellow scientists 她在科學家同行中較低的聲望 / His standing as a movie director has risen in recent years. 近年來他作為電影導演聲譽日隆。/ his international standing in cancer research 他在癌症研究領域的國際聲望 / He has improved his country's standing abroad. 他提高了自己國家的國際聲望。
           
<span class="vocabulary">**prestige**</span> [preˈsti:ʒ]
<span class="definition">n. [U] 某人或某事物因其社會地位（或其所做的事情）而得到的尊重或敬仰，如威信、威望、聲望：</span>personal prestige 個人聲望 / military, political, social prestige 軍事威望；政治聲望；社會聲望 / academic, intellectual prestige 學術聲望 / occupational prestige（美）職業聲望 / Winning the prize carries immense prestige. 獲獎帶來很高的聲望。/ efforts to build up the prestige of the United Nations 為樹立聯合國的威望所作的努力 / It was his responsibility for foreign affairs that gained him international prestige. 他在負責處理外交事務的過程中贏得了國際聲望。/ an international company that enjoys immense prestige 聲名顯赫的跨國公司 / Lavish hospitality allows the host to gain prestige. 盛情的款待使主人獲得聲望。/ Different jobs are accorded different levels of prestige. 不同的工作被賦予不同的社會聲望。/ Owning landed property confers prestige. 擁有地產給人以聲望。/ The royal couple's prestige was damaged by the allegations. 這對王室夫婦的威信因這些指控而受損。/ the party's prestige among the public 該政黨在公眾中的威望 / The post carried great prestige within the police force. 這個職位在警察系統內很受尊重。/ Doctors have suffered a loss of prestige following a spate of scandals. 接二連三的醜聞使醫生名譽掃地。/ Wearing designer clothes is a matter of personal prestige for many teenagers. 對很多青少年來說，穿名牌服裝是一件關於個人聲望的事。/ There is a lot of prestige attached to owning a car like this. 擁有這樣一部汽車會顯得很氣派。/ jobs with low prestige 地位低微的工作
                      
<span class="vocabulary">**celebrity**</span> [səˈlebrəti]
<span class="definition">n. 1 [C]（也可用非正式的celeb）（尤指娛樂界的）名人、名流、明星：</span>A host of A-list celebrities turned out for the wedding. 眾多大腕明星出現在婚禮上。/ He became a minor celebrity among Manhattan's cultural elite. 他成了曼哈頓文化精英中小有名氣的人。/ instant, overnight celebrity 迅速成名的人；一夜之間成名的人 / guest, visiting celebrity 特邀名人；作客名人 / media, sporting（英）, sports（美）, television celebrity 媒體名人；體育名人；電視名人 / Tonight's show features a host of celebrities. 今晚的演出有許多名人出場。/ The show's success made her an overnight celebrity. 這一節目的成功讓她一夜成名。/ In 1944, at the age of 30, Hersey suddenly became a celebrity. 1944年，30歲的赫西一夜成名。/ He became something of a celebrity in his home town. 他成為家鄉一位小有名氣的人。/ celebrity profile, celebrity status 名人簡介；名人地位 / celebrity chef, celebrity hairstylist, celebrity stylist 名廚；著名髮型師；時尚達人 / celebrity magazine, celebrity website 名人雜誌；名人網站 / celebrity gossip名人八卦 / celebrity client, celebrity guest名人客戶；名人嘉賓 / celebrity couple 名人夫妻 / celebrity culture 名人文化 / celebrity endorsement 名人代言 <span class="definition">2 [U]（尤指娛樂界的）成名的狀態，著名：</span>Does he find his new celebrity intruding on his private life? 他是否感覺到他最近的成名侵擾了他的私生活？/ Joanna has finally made it to the first rank of celebrity after 25 years as an actress. 喬安娜當了25年演員後，終於躋身一線女明星之列。
           
<span class="vocabulary">**personality**</span> [ˌpɜ:səˈnæləti; 美 ˌpɜ:rs-]
<span class="definition">n. [C]（尤指娛樂界或體育界的）名人：</span>personalities from the world of music 音樂界名流 / political, sports personality 政界名人；體育界名人 / media, radio, television, TV personality 媒體名人；電臺名人；電視名人 / on-air personality（美）無線廣播人物 / a broadcast network with well-known on-air personalities 攜有知名播音員的廣播網 / the personality cult surrounding Stalin 對斯大林的個人崇拜
           
<span class="vocabulary">**notable**</span> [ˈnəʊtəbl; 美 ˈnoʊ-]
<span class="definition">n. [usually pl.] [正式] 名人；重要人物：</span>Elected by local notables for nine years Senators lack the democratic legitimacy of members of the National Assembly. 由當地名流選出、任職九年的參議員缺少國民議會議員的民主合法性。/ The notables include five Senators, two Supreme Court judges and three State Governors. 顯要人物包括五名參議員、兩名最高法院法官和三名州長。

<span class="vocabulary">**stock**</span> [stɒk; 美 stɑ:k]
<span class="definition">n. [U] [正式] 某人為其他人所尊敬或喜愛的程度，可稱為名聲、聲望：</span>Their stock is high/low. 他們的聲望高/低。
           
<span class="vocabulary">**goodwill**</span> [ˌgʊdˈwɪl]
<span class="definition">n. [U] 公司與其客戶之間的良好關係，即（可作為一種無形資產的）商譽、商業信譽：</span>We do not want to lose the goodwill built up over 175 years. 我們不想讓歷經175年樹立起來的良好信譽毀於一旦。

<span class="vocabulary">**institution**</span> [͵ɪnstɪ'tju:ʃn] 
<span class="definition">n. [C] [非正式，幽默] 因長時間在某地（或從事某項工作）而廣為人知的一個人：</span>an institution around the town 鎮子裡的知名人士

<span class="vocabulary">**worthy**</span> ['wə:ðɪ] 
<span class="definition">n. [C] 一個重要人物，大人物，常是一種幽默說法：</span>a meeting attended by local worthies 當地知名人士參加的會議
           
<span class="vocabulary">**immortal**</span> [ɪˈmɔ:tl; 美 ɪˈmɔ:rtl]
<span class="definition">n. [C] 名垂千古的人物：</span>She is one of the Hollywood immortals. 她是一位千古流芳的好萊塢名人。
           
<span class="vocabulary">**immortality**</span> [ˌɪmɔ:ˈtæləti; 美 ˌɪmɔ:rˈt-]
<span class="definition">n. [U] 不朽：</span>baseball, cinematic, literary, sporting（英）immortality 棒球史上的不朽輝煌；電影界的不朽輝煌；文學界的不朽輝煌；體育史上的不朽輝煌 / He is well on his way to show business immortality. 他很快就會成為娛樂界永世流芳的人物。

周邊詞彙補充：
· great可指一個非常著名和成功的人或事物。

☀ <span class="category">**壞名聲：**</span>
 <span class="vocabulary">**notoriety**</span> [ˌnəʊtəˈraɪəti; 美 ˌnoʊ-]
<span class="definition">n. [U, sing.] ~ (for/as sth) 壞名聲、惡名：</span>He gained a certain notoriety as a gambler. 他落了個賭徒的惡名。/ public notoriety 惡名昭彰 / He achieved sudden notoriety when the details of his private life were revealed. 私生活的細節曝光後，他一下子變得臭名昭著。/ This make of car has a certain notoriety for rust problems. 這種車型因生鏽問題而聲名不佳。
                      
<span class="vocabulary">**rap**</span> [ræp]
<span class="definition">n. [sing.] [美，非正式] 名氣;（常指並不符合實情的）壞名聲：</span>The rap against Conn was that he was far too reckless. 科恩被人們認為做事太不計後果。/ The rap on this guy is that he doesn't really care. 這傢伙據說並不怎麼在乎。/ Wolves get a bad rap, says a woman who owns three. “狼的名聲不好，這是不公正的。”一位養著三隻狼的女人說。
 
<span class="vocabulary">**disrepute**</span> [ˌdɪsrɪˈpju:t]
<span class="definition">n. [U] 喪失名譽；落得壞名聲：</span>Such people bring our profession into disrepute. 這種人令我們這一行業蒙羞。/ The old system had fallen into disrepute. 舊體制已經聲名掃地。/ Such wild claims bring science into disrepute. 這類荒唐離譜的斷言敗環了科學的名聲。/ The players' conduct is likely to bring the game into disrepute.（英）這些球員的行為很可能使這場比賽臭名遠揚。/ This theory is now in disrepute. 這個理論現在已經聲名狼藉。

<span class="vocabulary">**fall**</span> [fɔ:l] 
<span class="definition">n. [sing.]（人、組織等由於做錯了某事而導致的）威信的喪失；不再受別人尊敬：</span>the TV preacher’s spectacular fall from grace 電視佈道者威信的遽降

<span class="vocabulary">**stain**</span> [steɪn] 
<span class="definition">vt. [正式] 玷汙（或敗壞）名聲等：</span>The events had stained the city’s reputation unfairly. 這些事件使該市背上了不應有的惡名。<span class="definition">n. [sing.] [正式] 人的名譽上的汙點：</span>The scandal left a dark stain on his reputation. 這樁醜聞給他的名聲留下了汙點。

<span class="vocabulary">**notorious**</span> [nəʊˈtɔ:riəs; 美 noʊ-]
<span class="definition">adj. ~ (for sth/for doing sth) / ~ (as sth) 因壞事而出名的，如臭名昭著的：</span>a notorious criminal 惡名昭彰的罪犯 / a notorious murder 一起臭名昭著的謀殺案 / The country is notorious for its appalling prison conditions. 該國因監獄狀況惡劣而臭名遠揚。/ The bar has become notorious as a meeting-place for drug dealers. 這家酒吧作為毒品販子接頭的場所已變得聲名狼藉。

<span class="vocabulary">**dubious**</span> [ˈdju:biəs; 美 ˈdu:-]
<span class="definition">adj.（榮譽、名聲等）不好的、不光彩的：</span>Nagy has the dubious honour of being the first athlete to be banned in this way. 納吉作為第一個以這種方式被禁賽的運動員而“出名”。/ El Salvador has earned the dubious distinction of having the worst soil erosion in continental America. 薩爾瓦多因是美洲大陸土壤侵蝕最嚴重的地方而有了這份不光彩的名聲。

<span class="vocabulary">**disreputable**</span> [dɪsˈrepjətəbl]
<span class="definition">adj. 名聲不好的：</span>She spent the evening with her disreputable brother Stefan. 她同聲名狼藉的弟弟斯蒂芬一起度過了這個傍晚。/ He was found to have been enjoying the company of disreputable women. 他被人發現喜歡與那些不三不四的女人混在一起。/ the noisiest and most disreputable bars 最為喧譁、名聲最差的酒吧 / a disreputable area of the city 城裡破舊骯髒的地方 / He had a vaguely disreputable appearance. 他看上去有些不那麼體面。/ This approach remains intellectually disreputable even now. 即使現在這種做法理智一點兒說也是不光彩的。

周邊詞彙補充：
· blasphemous可指對神明或宗教不敬的，即褻瀆神明（或宗教）的；
· blasphemy可指對上帝或神明的褻瀆；褻瀆神明或上帝的言行。

☀ <span class="category">**著名的形容詞：**</span>
<span class="vocabulary">**famous**</span> ['feɪməs] 
<span class="definition">adj. famous (for sth) / famous (as sth) 為很多人所知曉的，即著名的、出名的：</span>famous artist, famous hotel 著名的藝術家；著名的旅館 / He became internationally famous for his novels. 他以寫小說享譽國際。/ He was famous as both a teacher and a scientist. 他作為老師和科學家都很出名。

<span class="vocabulary">**renowned**</span> [rɪˈnaʊnd]
<span class="definition">adj. ~ (as/for sth)（某人或某地常因好事而）著名且受尊敬的：</span>a renowned author 著名的作家 / James was renowned as a forward thinker. 詹姆斯以其前瞻性的思想而負盛名。/ She is renowned for her patience. 她的耐心是出了名的。/ It is renowned as one of the region's best restaurants. 這是本地區最好的飯店之一。/ The area is renowned for its Romanesque churches. 該地區以其羅馬式的教堂聞名。
           
<span class="vocabulary">**prestigious**</span> [preˈstɪdʒəs]
<span class="definition">adj. [常用於名詞前] 聲望高的、有威望的、受尊敬的：</span>She won a highly prestigious award. 她贏得了極負盛名的大獎。/ a prestigious university 名牌大學 / It's one of the best equipped and most prestigious schools in the country. 這是國內裝置最先進、最有威望的學校之一。
           
<span class="vocabulary">**reputable**</span> [ˈrepjətəbl]
<span class="definition">adj. 聲譽好的、值得信賴的：</span>a highly reputable company 信譽極好的公司 / Customers who are tired of waiting turn to less reputable suppliers. 等不及的顧客轉而選擇不怎麼知名的供應商。/ You are well advised to buy your car through a reputable dealer. 強烈建議您透過值得信賴的經銷商購車。/ He spent a lot of time hanging around the less reputable bars in Chicago. 他長時間泡在芝加哥的一些不知名的酒吧裡。/ Some of the men looked less than reputable. 這些人中有的看上去沒什麼名望。

<span class="vocabulary">**distinguished**</span> [dɪˈstɪŋgwɪʃt]
<span class="definition">adj.（人或其事蹟、作品）非常成功、受尊重且名聲好的，如傑出的、卓越的：</span>a distinguished career in medicine 在醫學領域的輝煌生涯 / a distinguished academic family 傑出的學者之家

<span class="vocabulary">**eminent**</span> [ˈemɪnənt]
<span class="definition">adj.（尤指某一專業領域的人）著名且受尊重的：</span>an eminent architect 著名的建築師 / an eminent scientist 著名的科學家

<span class="vocabulary">**prominent**</span> [ˈprɒmɪnənt; 美 ˈprɑ:m-]
<span class="definition">adj.（某人）重要的；著名的；傑出的：</span>a prominent politician 傑出的政治家 / He played a prominent part in the campaign. 他在這次運動中發揮了重要作用。/ She was prominent in the fashion industry. 她曾在時裝界名噪一時。/ a prominent member of the Law Society 法律協會的重要成員 / the children of very prominent or successful parents 傑出或成功人士的孩子 / prominent as a player and coach作為運動員和教練知名的 / prominent in the trade unions 在各工會之間聲名顯赫
            
<span class="vocabulary">**celebrated**</span> [ˈselɪbreɪtɪd]
<span class="definition">adj.（因質量或品質好而）著名的、馳名的：</span>a celebrated painter 著名的畫家 / He was soon one of the most celebrated young painters in England. 不久他就成了英格蘭最負盛名的年輕畫家之一。/ Dean died three days before the opening of Rebel Without Cause, his most celebrated film. 迪恩在他最知名的電影《無因的反叛》首映的三天之前去世。/ his justly celebrated portrait of Benjamin Franklin 他所畫的不負盛名的本傑明·富蘭克林肖像 / He has become celebrated as an artist. 他已成為大名鼎鼎的藝術家了。/ The area is celebrated for its food and wine. 該地區以食物和酒著名。

<span class="vocabulary">**reputable**</span> [ˈrepjətəb]
<span class="definition">adj.（公司或人）聲譽好的且值得信賴的：</span>reputable dealer, reputable company, reputable supplier 可信賴的交易商；可信賴的公司；值得信賴的供應商

<span class="vocabulary">**high**</span> [haɪ] 
<span class="definition">adj. 通常用於名詞前，指對某人十分贊同或尊敬的，即（聲望或評價）高的：</span>She is held in very high regard by her colleagues. 她很受同事們的敬重。/ You seem to have a high opinion of yourself! 你似乎自我評價很高嘛！/ People have such high expectations of you. 人們對你抱有如此之高的期望。
           
<span class="vocabulary">**household**</span> [ˈhaʊshəʊld; 美 -hoʊld]
<span class="definition">adj. 家喻戶曉的；眾人皆知的：</span>Today, fashion designers are household names. 今天，時裝設計師是家喻戶曉的人物。/ My agent told me, 'This'll make your name a household word.' 我的經紀人告訴我：“這將使你一舉成名。”
           
<span class="vocabulary">**noted**</span> [ˈnəʊtɪd; 美 ˈnoʊt-]
<span class="definition">adj. ~ (for/as sth) 以（特殊的技能或特徵）而聞名的：</span>a noted dancer 著名的舞蹈演員 / He is not noted for his sense of humour. 他沒什麼幽默感。/ The lake is noted as a home to many birds. 這個湖作為許多鳥類的棲息地而聞名。/ a television programme noted for its attacks on organised crime 以抨擊有組織犯罪而知名的電視節目 / Lawyers are not noted for rushing into change. 律師並非以倉促求變著稱。
           
<span class="vocabulary">**immortal**</span> [ɪˈmɔ:tl; 美 ɪˈmɔ:rtl]
<span class="definition">adj. 1（好名聲）流芳百世的；名垂千古的：</span>the immortal Goethe 名垂千古的歌德 / the immortal Reverend Dr Spooner 不朽的斯普納牧師 / Wuthering Heights, Emily Bronte's immortal love story《呼嘯山莊》，艾米莉·勃朗特不朽的愛情小說 <span class="definition">2（語句）著名的、不朽的：</span>In the immortal words of Henry Ford, ‘If it ain't broke, don't fix it.’ 按照亨利·福特的不朽名言：“如果東西沒壞掉，就不要去修理它。”/ Roosevelt's immortal words, 'Speak softly and carry a big stick.' 羅斯福的不朽名言——“說話溫和,手持大棒”/ I keep remembering Hannah Pakula's immortal line: 'Hollywood is no place for a woman over forty with a library card.' 我一直謹記漢娜·帕庫拉的名言：“好萊塢不是年過四十手拿圖書證的女人呆的地方。”

· acclaimed可指著名的；

· designer可指（服裝、品牌等）由著名設計師設計的；昂貴且標有設計師姓名的，如名牌的。
