☀ <span class="category">**顯然、明顯的形容詞：**</span>
<span class="vocabulary">**obvious**</span> ['ɒbvɪəs] 
<span class="definition">adj. 1 obvious (to sb) (that ...) 容易看到的或易於理解的，相當於clear：</span>The answer is perfectly obvious! 答案十分明顯！/ For obvious reasons, I’d prefer not to give my name. 因為顯而易見的原因，我不願透露自己的姓名。/ His manner made it obvious he didn’t like her. 他的態度清楚地表明他不喜歡她。/ It soon became obvious that the machine did not work. 機器故障很快就顯現出來了。<span class="definition">2 大多數人所認為或同意的，如公認的、當然的：</span>She was the obvious choice for the job. 她是這一工作的當然人選。/ There’s no obvious solution to the problem. 這個問題尚無公認的解決辦法。/ This seemed the most obvious thing to do. 這似乎是最順理成章的做法。

<span class="vocabulary">**apparent**</span> [ə'pærənt] 
<span class="definition">adj. apparent (from sth) (that ...) / apparent (to sb) (that ...) 通常不用於名詞前，容易看見的，或易於理解的。如顯而易見、顯然，相當於obvious（與obvious和evident未見明顯區別）：</span>Their devotion was apparent. 他們的忠誠顯而易見。/ The extent of their injuries was not immediately apparent. 他們的傷勢還不能馬上看出來。/ His lack of experience was quite apparent to everyone. 他缺乏經驗，每個人都看得出來。

<span class="vocabulary">**evident**</span> ['evɪdənt] 
<span class="definition">adj. evident (to sb) (that ...) / evident (in/from sth) 清楚的，容易被看到的，即顯而易見的、顯然的，相當於obvious：</span>It was fairly evident from her tone of voice that she disapproved. 從她說話的語調看，她顯然不同意。/ It was evident to me that the mission would fail. 在我看來，這項任務明顯會失敗。/ Their symptoms may be less evident to their caregivers. 在護工看來，他們的症狀也許不那麼明顯。
           
<span class="vocabulary">**self-evident**</span>
<span class="definition">adj. 顯而易見的、不需要任何進一步的證據或解釋的：</span>The dangers of such action are self-evident. 這樣的行動，其危險是明擺著的。/ a self-evident truth 不證自明的真理 / It is self-evident that we will never have enough resources to meet the demand. 不言而喻，我們永遠都不會有足夠的資源來滿足需求。/ The implications for this country are self-evident. 對該國的影響顯而易見。/ These ideas were by no means self-evident when they were first suggested. 這些想法首次提出時絕非顯而易見。/ It was self-evident to her that anything so wonderful could not have evolved accidentally. 在她看來很明顯的是，任何美妙的事物都不可能是偶然產生的。

<span class="vocabulary">**clear**</span> [klɪə] 
<span class="definition">adj. clear (to sb) (that) / clear what, how, whether, etc. 明顯的，且不使人產生任何疑惑的。是一個基礎用詞：</span>This is a clear case of fraud. 這無疑是一樁詐騙案。/ She won the election by a clear majority. 她以明顯的多數贏得選舉。/ a clear advantage 明顯的優勢 / It was quite clear to me that she was lying. 我十分清楚她在撒謊。
                     
<span class="vocabulary">**distinct**</span> [dɪˈstɪŋkt]
<span class="definition">adj. 容易被看到、聽到或感覺到的，如清楚的、清晰的、明顯的：</span>There was a distinct smell of gas. 有一股明顯的煤氣味。/ Now that Tony was no longer present, there was a distinct change in her attitude. 由於託尼不在場了，她的態度有了明顯的轉變。/ Being 6ft 3in tall has some distinct disadvantages! 身高6英尺3英寸有些明顯的不便之處！/ Another Cup marathon between the two sides is now a distinct possibility. 雙方很可能再進行一場馬拉松式的優勝杯比賽。/ His voice was quiet but every word was distinct. 他說話聲音不大，但字字清晰。/ I have distinct memories of him in his last years. 我清楚地記得他最後幾年的情況。/ to impart a distinct flavor with a minimum of cooking fat 用最少量的食用油來調出一種獨特的口味

<span class="vocabulary">**manifest**</span> [ˈmænɪfest]
<span class="definition">adj. [正式] ~ (to sb) (in sth) / ~ (in sth) [正式] 容易看到或理解的，即明顯的，顯而易見的：</span>His nervousness was manifest to all those present. 所有在場的人都看出了他很緊張。/ The anger he felt is manifest in his paintings. 他的憤怒明顯地表現在他的繪畫之中。/ the manifest failure of the policies 政策明顯的失敗之處 / manifest injustice 明顯的不公正          
           
<span class="vocabulary">**marked**</span> [mɑ:kt; 美 mɑ:rkt]
<span class="definition">adj.（改變或區別）顯而易見的、明顯的：</span>marked improvement 明顯的進步 / a marked increase in profits 利潤的顯著提高 / There has been a marked increase in crimes against property. 侵財案件明顯增加了。/ She is quiet and studious, in marked contrast to her sister. 她沉靜勤奮，與她妹妹形成了鮮明的對照。/ He was a man of austere habits, in marked contrast to his more flamboyant wife. 他處事嚴謹，與他愛張揚的妻子形成了鮮明的對比。/ There is a strongly marked difference between the two creatures. 這兩種動物之間有一項非常顯著的區別。
           
<span class="vocabulary">**bold**</span> [bəʊld; 美 boʊld]
<span class="definition">adj.（形狀、線條等）明顯的、輪廓突出的：</span>the bold outline of a mountain against the sky 天空映襯下的山的清晰輪廓 / Each picture is shown in colour on one page and as a bold outline on the opposite page. 每幅圖片的彩樣都單獨印在一頁上，而在該頁的背面會映出清晰的輪廓。
           
<span class="vocabulary">**bald**</span> [bɔ:ld]
<span class="definition">adj.（言語）不需要任何額外資訊或細節來解釋便能理解的，如明擺著的：</span>The bald fact is that we don't need you any longer. 事實很簡單，我們不再需要你了。/ The bald truth is he's just not happy. 事實明擺著，他就是不開心。

<span class="vocabulary">**visible**</span> [ˈvɪzəbl]
<span class="definition">adj. 容易被看見的：</span>The house is clearly visible from the beach. 從海灘可以清楚地看到那所房子。/ We cut the trees down to make the lake visible from the house. 我們把樹砍倒，這樣在屋裡就能看見湖了。/ The mountains were just visible, dusky and black. 黑黝黝的群山朦朧可辨。/ The flat boats were barely visible. 扁平的小船幾乎看不見。/ The sea was now out, leaving the wreck fully visible. 現在潮退了，沉船完全露了出來。/ The warning lights were clearly visible. 警示訊號燈清晰可見。/ The election poster was clearly visible from the street. 選舉海報在街頭頗為醒目。/ She stood, half visible in the dim light. 她站在那裡，在昏暗的光線下朦朧可辨。/ A figure was dimly visible in the evening gloom. 沉沉的暮色裡一個身影隱約可見。/ He was immediately visible to me. 我一眼就能看見他。/ Its contents were visible to all of them. 裡面的東西他們全都看得見。/ a bacterium visible to the human eye 一種肉眼可見的細菌 / Most stars are not visible to the naked eye. 大多數星星肉眼看不見。/ The scars remained visible all her life. 這些傷疤伴隨了她一生。            
          
<span class="vocabulary">**significant**</span> [sɪgˈnɪfɪkənt]
<span class="definition">adj. 顯著的：</span>The results of the experiment are not statistically significant. 從統計學的觀點看，實驗結果意義不明顯。/ There are no significant differences between the two groups of students. 這兩組學生沒有明顯差別。/ Your work has shown a significant improvement. 你的工作有了顯著改進。/ It is significant that girls generally do better in examinations than boys. 很明顯，女生的考試成績一般比男生的好。
           
<span class="vocabulary">**striking**</span> [ˈstraɪkɪŋ]
<span class="definition">adj. 足夠有趣且不尋常，因而能夠吸引注意力的，如顯著的：</span>a striking feature 顯著的特徵 / That hat looks very striking. 那帽子很引人注目。/ The picture was striking in its simplicity. 這幅畫的動人之處在於它的簡潔。/ The most striking feature of those statistics is the high proportion of suicides. 那些統計資料最引人注目之處是其極高的自殺率。/ In striking contrast to their brothers, the girls were both intelligent and charming. 姑娘們既聰明伶俐，又嫵媚動人，跟她們的兄弟形成鮮明的對照。/ What is immediately striking is how resourceful the children are. 即刻引起人們注目的是孩子們的機智聰明。/ She bears a striking resemblance to her older sister. 她酷似她姐姐。/ He bears a striking resemblance to Lenin. 他酷似列寧。

<span class="vocabulary">**definite**</span> [ˈdefɪnət]
<span class="definition">adj. 1 易於看到或易於理解的，即明顯的：</span>The look on her face was a definite sign that something was wrong. 一看她的神色就知道出事了。/ There was a definite feeling that things were getting worse. 人們明顯感到事情越來越糟。/ There has already been a definite improvement. 已經有了顯著的改進。/ That's a very definite possibility. 那是很有可能的。<span class="definition">2（形狀或顏色）清晰的、分明的：</span>Studying his face in the bathroom mirror he wished he had more definite features. 看著浴室鏡子中的臉，他真希望自己的五官能夠更分明一些。
           
<span class="vocabulary">**conspicuous**</span> [kənˈspɪkjuəs]
<span class="definition">adj. 容易看到或注意到的；惹人注意的：</span>Mary's red hair always made her conspicuous at school. 瑪麗的紅頭髮在學校裡總是很惹眼。/ I felt very conspicuous in my new car. 坐在我的新車裡，我感到十分惹人注目。/ You may feel tearful in situations where you feel conspicuous. 覺得自己身處眾目睽睽之下時，你可能會有想哭的感覺。/ The new building was rather conspicuous. 這座新樓相當顯眼。/ The advertisements were all posted in a conspicuous place. 廣告都貼在了顯眼的地方。/ The stain on her dress was horribly conspicuous. 她裙子上的汙漬太顯眼了。/ He played no part in the game and was conspicuous by his absence in the post-match celebrations. 比賽時他沒有上場，並且由於沒有參加賽後的慶祝活動而引起了大家的關注。/ The most conspicuous way in which the old politics is changing is in the growing use of referendums. 舊式政治最明顯的變化在於其越來越多地進行全民公決。/ The event was a conspicuous success (= a very great one). 這項活動相當成功。
           
<span class="vocabulary">**prominent**</span> [ˈprɒmɪnənt; 美 ˈprɑ:m-]
<span class="definition">adj.（某事物）容易被看到的，如顯眼的、顯著的：</span>The church tower was a prominent feature in the landscape. 教堂的尖塔曾經是此地景觀的重要特色。/ Here the window plays a prominent part in the design. 這裡窗戶在設計中起著非常重要的作用。/ The story was given a prominent position on the front page. 這則報道刊登在頭版的顯著位置。/ Romania's most prominent independent newspaper 羅馬尼亞最著名的獨立報紙
                      
<span class="vocabulary">**legible**</span> [ˈledʒəbl]
<span class="definition">adj.（手寫的或印刷的文字）清晰得能夠看懂的：</span>legible handwriting 清楚易讀的筆跡 / My handwriting isn't very legible. 我寫的字不那麼好認。/ The signature was still legible. 簽名仍清晰可辨。/ a barely legible sign 字跡難辨的招牌 / The inscription is still legible. 銘文仍然可以辨認。/ The price must be legible to a purchaser. 價格務必寫清楚，讓買家一眼就能辨認出來。

<span class="vocabulary">**pronounced**</span> [prəˈnaʊnst]
<span class="definition">adj. 非常明顯的、極其顯著的、表達明確的：</span>He walked with a pronounced limp. 他走路明顯跛足。/ She has very pronounced views on art. 她有非常明確的藝術觀。/ Most of the art exhibitions have a pronounced Scottish theme. 大多數的藝術展品帶有明顯的蘇格蘭風格。/ a pronounced Australian accent 明顯的澳大利亞口音 / He walks with a fairly pronounced limp. 他走路時跛得相當明顯。/ These blooms have a very pronounced tendency to hang their heads. 這些花朵非常易於下垂。

<span class="vocabulary">**noticeable**</span> [ˈnəʊtɪsəbl; 美 ˈnoʊ-]
<span class="definition">adj. ~ (in sb/sth) / ~ (that…) 容易看到或注意到的；顯而易見的：</span>a noticeable improvement 顯而易見的改進 / This effect is particularly noticeable in younger patients. 這種效果在年輕一些的病人身上尤為明顯。/ It is noticeable that women do not have the rivalry that men have. 顯而易見，女性不像男人們那樣好勇鬥狠。/ the most noticeable effect of these changes 這些變化帶來的最明顯效果 / These changes are more noticeable in women than in men. 這些變化在女人身上比在男人身上更明顯。/ This may not be noticeable to people from outside New York. 對來自紐約以外的地方的人來說，這也許並不明顯。/ The smell was immediately noticeable when you walked in the front door. 一走進前門就能明顯地聞到這種氣味。
                      
<span class="vocabulary">**notable**</span> [ˈnəʊtəbl; 美 ˈnoʊ-]
<span class="definition">adj. ~ (for sth) 值得注意的；顯著的；重要的：</span>notable success, notable achievement, notable example 顯著的成功；明顯的成就；明顯的事例 / His eyes are his most notable feature. 他的雙眼是他最明顯的特徵。/ The town is notable for its ancient harbour. 這座小鎮因其古老的港口而出名。/ With a few notable exceptions, everyone gave something. 人人都給了些東西，只有幾個人例外，很是顯眼。/ The proposed new structure is notable not only for its height, but for its shape. 擬建的新建築令人矚目，不僅是因其高度，也因其外形。/ Mo did not want to be ruled by anyone and it is notable that she never allowed the men in her life to eclipse her. 莫不想受任何人支配，而且很明顯她從不容許自己生活裡的男性光芒蓋過自己。/ The birds are notable for their rarity. 這些鳥以稀有著稱。

<span class="vocabulary">**noteworthy**</span> [ˈnəʊtwɜ:ði; 美 ˈnoʊtwɜ:rði]
<span class="definition">adj. 值得注意的；顯著的：</span>It is noteworthy that the programme has been shifted from its original August slot to July. 值得注意的是該節目已從原來八月份的檔期換到了七月份。/ His reign has never been considered particularly noteworthy. 從沒有人認為他統治的時期特別值得關注。/ The bridge is noteworthy for its sheer size. 這座橋以宏偉聞名。

<span class="vocabulary">**palpable**</span> [ˈpælpəbl]
<span class="definition">adj. 很容易被頭腦或意識察覺到的：</span>a palpable sense of relief 如釋重負 / The tension in the room was almost palpable. 屋子裡的緊張氣氛幾乎能感覺到。/ The tension between Amy and Jim is palpable. 埃米和吉姆之間明顯關係緊張。/ There is an almost palpable feeling of hopelessness. 幾乎明顯有種無望的感覺。           
           
<span class="vocabulary">**perceptible**</span> [pəˈseptəbl; 美 pərˈs-]
<span class="definition">adj. 1 [正式] 明顯得可覺察得到的、看得出的：</span>perceptible change, perceptible increase, perceptible decline, perceptible impact 可以察覺的變化；可以察覺的增長；可以察覺的下降；可以察覺的影響 / The price increase has had no perceptible effect on sales. 這次提價沒有對銷售產生明顯的影響。/ Her foreign accent was barely perceptible. 她的外國口音幾乎聽不出來。/ Pasternak gave him a barely perceptible smile. 帕斯特納克給了他一個幾乎無法察覺的微笑。/ There was a slight but perceptible air of neglect. 有一種微弱但可察覺的冷落意味。/ The difference is barely perceptible to the average reader. 這種差別一般讀者幾乎是察覺不到的。<span class="definition">2 [正式] [術語] 可用感官來感知的：</span>the perceptible world 可感知的世界

<span class="vocabulary">**visible**</span> [ˈvɪzəbl]
<span class="definition">adj. 明顯的，能被注意到的：</span>He showed no visible sign of emotion. 他絲毫不露聲色。/ She made a visible effort to control her anger. 看得出她竭力控制自己不發火。/ His relief was all too visible. 他顯然如釋重負。/ her quite visible embarrassment 她的十分明顯的尷尬 / a visible police presence 明顯有警察在場 / visible benefits 顯而易見的實惠 / The most visible sign of the intensity of the crisis is unemployment. 預示危機嚴重程度的一個最顯著的跡象就是失業情況。/ Italy has a highly visible environmental movement. 義大利展開了聲勢浩大的環保運動。

<span class="vocabulary">**express**</span> [ɪk'spres] 
<span class="definition">adj. 1 [正式] 只作前置定語，（願望或目的）闡述得清晰明白的、特定的，相當於definite：</span>express wish, express purpose 明確的願望（即明確表示）；特定目的（即特意）<span class="definition">2 [正式] 只作前置定語，（命令或指令）闡述得明確的：</span>the express orders of the president 總統的明確指令

<span class="vocabulary">**transparent**</span> [træns'pærənt] 
<span class="definition">adj.（藉口、謊言等）易於識破的、易看穿的，相當於obvious：</span>a man of transparent honesty 顯然很誠實的人 / a transparent attempt to buy votes 收買選票的明顯企圖 / Am I that transparent (= are my intentions that obvious)? 我就那麼沒有城府？
           
<span class="vocabulary">**emphatic**</span> [ɪmˈfætɪk]
<span class="definition">adj.（勝利、失敗等）明顯的：</span>Yesterday's emphatic victory was their fifth in succession. 昨天的大勝是他們連續第五場勝利。

<span class="vocabulary">**sharp**</span> [ʃɑːp] 
<span class="definition">adj. 常作前置定語，指差異、影象或聲音等清晰可辨的、清楚明確的、鮮明的：</span>to draw a sharp distinction between A and B 將甲和乙截然分開 / a sharp and clear mark 清楚明晰的標記 / sharp photograph, sharp outline 清晰的圖片；清晰的輪廓 / to bring sth into sharper focus 使…成為清晰的焦點 / in sharp contrast to ... 與…形成鮮明對比
           
<span class="vocabulary">**crisp**</span> [krɪsp]
<span class="definition">adj.（影象等）清晰分明的：</span>瞭解即可

<span class="vocabulary">**straight**</span> [streɪt] 
<span class="definition">adj. 只作前置定語，（選擇）簡單明瞭的；非此即彼的：</span>The election was a straight fight between the two main parties.（英式用法）那次選舉是兩大黨間的直接交鋒。/ It was a straight choice between taking the job and staying out of work. 要麼接受這份工作，要麼繼續失業，此外別無其他選擇。

<span class="vocabulary">**plain**</span> [pleɪn] 
<span class="definition">adj. 易於看到的、易於理解的，如清楚的、明顯的、淺顯的，相當於clear：</span>He made it plain that we should leave. 他明確表示要我們離開。/ She made her annoyance plain. 她滿臉不耐煩。/ The facts were plain to see. 事實顯而易見。

<span class="vocabulary">**outstanding**</span> [͵aʊt'stændɪŋ] 
<span class="definition">adj. 常用於名詞前，非常明顯的；或非常重要的。如突出的，相當於prominent：</span>the outstanding features of the landscape 這一風景的突出特徵

<span class="vocabulary">**patent**</span> ['peɪtnt, 'pætnt] 
<span class="definition">adj. 正式用法，強調某件壞事是很清楚明顯的，赤裸裸的：</span>patent nonsense, patent lie 一派胡言；赤裸裸的謊言

<span class="vocabulary">**broad**</span> [brɔ:d] 
<span class="definition">adj.（所給予的暗示）明顯的：</span>a broad hint 明確的暗示
           
<span class="vocabulary">**naked**</span> [ˈneɪkɪd]
<span class="definition">adj. [只用於名詞前]（情感、態度等）表露得直白的、露骨的：</span>The naked hatred in the woman's face shocked me. 那位女士一臉毫不掩飾的憎恨讓我震驚。/ There had been naked misery in her voice when she'd spoken about the letter. 她說起那封信時，聲音裡流露出明顯的痛苦。/ Naked aggression and an attempt to change frontiers by force could not go unchallenged. 公然的侵略和企圖用武力改變邊界的行徑是不能置之不理的。/ violence and the naked pursuit of power 暴力以及對權力赤裸裸的追求 / the naked truth 明擺著的事實
                      
<span class="vocabulary">**unmistakable**</span> [ˌʌnmɪˈsteɪkəbl]
<span class="definition">adj. 清楚明白的：</span>Her accent was unmistakable. 她的口音是很明顯的。/ the unmistakable sound of gunfire 清清楚楚的槍炮聲
