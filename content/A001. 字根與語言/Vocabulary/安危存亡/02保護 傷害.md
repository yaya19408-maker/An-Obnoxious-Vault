☀ <span class="category">**保護的動詞和形容詞：**</span>
<span class="vocabulary">**protect**</span> [prə'tekt] 
<span class="definition">vt.＆vi. protect (sb/sth) (against/from sth) 保護，即確保…不受傷害或損害等。是一個基礎通用詞：</span>to protect you against attack 保護你免遭襲擊 / A cardboard box would protect the product better. 紙板箱子可以更好地保護產品。/ Each company is fighting to protect its own commercial interests. 每家公司都努力維護自身的商業利益。/ a cream that helps to protect your skin against the sun 防止皮膚曬傷的乳霜 / Protect the exposed areas of wood with varnish. 塗上清漆保護木材暴露在外的部分。/ Some pieces of music are protected by copyright. 一些音樂作品受版權保護。<span class="definition">vt. 常用被動式，指制定法律以保護某種動物，某片地區或某個建築物等：</span>to protect the animal 保護這種動物 / a federally protected game preserve 受聯邦法律保護的狩獵地區 / to protect the region as a manufacturing base 保護該地區作為製造基地
                      
<span class="vocabulary">**protective**</span> [prəˈtektɪv]
<span class="definition">adj. 1 [只用於名詞前] 能提供保護的、防護的：</span>Workers should wear full protective clothing. 工人應該穿著全套防護服。/ Protective gloves reduce the absorption of chemicals through the skin. 防護手套可以減少皮膚對化學物質的吸收。/ a protective layer of varnish 清漆防護層 / a protective barrier against the sun's rays 防曬屏障 / Protective measures are necessary if the city's monuments are to be preserved. 要儲存城市古蹟必須採取一些維護性措施。<span class="definition">2 ~ (of/towards sb/sth) 對某人或某事物有保護欲的：</span>She had been fiercely protective towards him as a teenager. 她過去曾極力呵護他這個十幾歲的孩子。/ He is very protective towards his mother. 他對他的母親呵護備至。/ She felt suddenly very protective towards/toward her mother. 她對母親突然萌發出強烈的保護欲。/ Glynis was beside her, putting a protective arm around her shoulders. 格莉妮絲在她身邊，呵護地用胳膊摟住她的肩膀。/ He was extremely protective of his role as advisor. 他極力保護自己的顧問角色。/ He put a protective arm around her shoulders. 他呵護地用胳膊摟住她的肩膀。/ Parents can easily become over-protective of their children (= want to protect them too much). 父母容易過度保護孩子。/ an overly protective father 過度保護孩子的父親 / He's too jealous and protective of her. 他太珍惜愛護她了。/ Lionesses are fiercely protective of their young. 雌獅細心呵護自己的幼崽。

<span class="vocabulary">**safeguard**</span> [ˈseɪfgɑ:d; 美 -gɑ:rd]
<span class="definition">vt.&vi. ~ sth/sb (against/from sth) / ~ against sth [正式] 保護某人或某事物（使其免遭損失或損壞），保衛…的安全：</span>to safeguard a person's interests 維護某人的利益 / The interests of minorities will have to be safeguarded under a new constitution. 新憲法必須能保護少數群體的利益。/ This legislation does not adequately safeguard the rights of consumers. 該立法不足以保障消費者權益。/ We must take steps to safeguard our environment against these threats. 我們必須採取措施保護環境，使其免受此類威脅。/ They will press for international action to safeguard the ozone layer. 他們將督促國際社會採取行動保護臭氧層。/ to safeguard the young plants from frost 保護幼苗免遭霜凍 / The leaflet explains how to safeguard against dangers in the home. 小冊子告訴人們在家裡如何防備各種危險。/ The new card will safeguard the company against fraud. 新卡將保護公司免遭詐騙。/ to safeguard jobs 保住工作崗位
           
<span class="vocabulary">**shield**</span> [ʃi:ld]
<span class="definition">vt. ~ sb/sth (from sb/sth) 保護…，使其免遭危險、損害或不悅：</span>She raised her hand to shield her eyes against the sun. 她抬起手遮住陽光保護眼睛。/ He shielded his head from the sun with an old sack. 他用一隻舊口袋擋住頭頂的陽光。/ He shielded her with his body. 他用自己的身體護住她。/ He carefully shielded the flame with his cupped hand. 他一手捧成杯狀，小心地護住火苗。/ The ozone layer shields the earth from the sun's ultraviolet rays. 臭氧層保護地球不受太陽紫外線的輻射。/ The company does not bet its own money on equities, and so is shielded from market risk. 該公司沒有把自己的錢押在普通股上，這樣就規避了市場風險。/ new laws to shield companies from foreign competition 保護企業免遭外國企業競爭影響的新法律 / to shield the children from the full horrors of the war 不讓孩子們感受到戰爭的恐怖 / Police believe that somebody is shielding the killer. 警方認為有人把殺人兇手窩藏了起來。/ You can't shield her from the truth forever. 你不可能永遠瞞著她，不讓她知道事實真相。

<span class="vocabulary">**preserve**</span> [prɪ'zə:v] 
<span class="definition">vt. preserve sb/sth (from sth) 保持某人或某事物以使其繼續存活（或使其免遭傷害或危險而處於安全狀態中），可稱為保護，相當於save：</span>to preserve endangered species 保護瀕危物種 / to preserve fish stocks 保護漁業資源

<span class="vocabulary">**cover**</span> ['kʌvə] 
<span class="definition">vt. 1 cover sth (with sth) 把…放在…上面或前面以對其進行保護，即掩蓋，遮蓋：</span>He covered the body with a cloth. 他用一塊布蓋住那具屍體。<span class="definition">2（某事物）蓋住另一事物以對其進行保護，即掩蓋，遮蓋：</span>a black patch which covered his left eye 蓋在他左眼上的黑眼罩 <span class="definition">3 用槍掩護某人：</span>Cover me while I move forward. 掩護我前進。

<span class="vocabulary">**shelter**</span> ['ʃeltə] 
<span class="definition">vt. shelter sb/sth (from sb/sth) 給…提供一個地方，使其免遭惡劣天氣或危險；保護：</span>Trees shelter the house from the wind. 樹給房子擋住了風。/ to shelter the homeless 庇護無家可歸者 / Perhaps I sheltered my daughter too much. 也許我對女兒保護過度了。
           
<span class="vocabulary">**sheltered**</span> [ˈʃeltəd; 美 -tərd]
<span class="definition">adj. [有時貶] 受庇護的（因而不被生活中的困難侵擾的）：</span>She had a very sheltered childhood. 她有過一個備受呵護的童年。/ They both lead very sheltered lives. 他們兩人都過著呵護備至的生活。/ Perhaps I've just led a really sheltered life. 也許我一直過的就是一種呵護備至的生活。/ My mother, who had a sheltered upbringing, has fantastic memories of childhood. 我媽媽在呵護下長大，對童年有著非常美好的回憶。
           
<span class="vocabulary">**defend**</span> [dɪ'fend] 
<span class="definition">vt.＆vi. defend (sb/yourself/sth) (from/against sb/sth) 防禦或保衛…，以使其免遭攻擊：</span>to defend against knife attacks 抵禦持刀襲擊 / Troops have been sent to defend the borders. 已派出部隊去守衛邊疆。/ They attacked the left flank, which has only lightly defended. 他們攻擊了防禦薄弱的左翼。/ Computer users need to ensure their systems are properly defended. 電腦使用者需要確保他們的系統做了適當的防護。<span class="definition">vt. 參加比賽或競爭等以保住頭銜、席位等：</span>The champion successfully defended his title against the challenger. 這位冠軍打敗了挑戰者，成功衛冕。/ He intends to defend his seat in the next election. 他想在下屆選舉中尋求連任。

<span class="vocabulary">**defensive**</span> [dɪˈfensɪv]
<span class="definition">adj. 防衛的、防禦的：</span>These are purely defensive measures. 這些純粹是防禦措施。/ Troops took up a defensive position around the town. 部隊在全城採取了守勢。/ The Government hastily organized defensive measures against the raids. 政府急忙佈置了防衛措施抵禦空襲。/ a largely defensive campaign 以防禦為主的戰役 / The union leaders were pushed into a more defensive position by the return of a Republican Congress in November. 由於11月份共和黨重新控制了國會，工會領導人被推到更加被動防守的位置。

<span class="vocabulary">**guard**</span> [ɡɑːd] 
<span class="definition">vt. 1 站在某地、某人或某物附近以看護並保護之，即守衛，護衛：</span>The dog was guarding its owner’s luggage. 狗守護著主人的行李。/ He was under instructions to guard the key with his life. 他奉命要拼死守住鑰匙。/ a bird carefully guarding its eggs 小心護蛋的鳥 / political leaders guarded by the police 由警方保衛的政界領袖們 / pop stars who need to be guarded from their fans 需要保護以免受歌迷騷擾的流行歌星 / They guard the city against attack. 他們保護城市免受襲擊。/ The military base is closely guarded. 這個軍事基地守衛森嚴。<span class="definition">2 看守某人以防止其逃跑，如看守囚犯：</span>The prisoners were guarded by soldiers. 犯人由士兵看守。<span class="definition">3 保護自己擁有的優勢：</span>She jealously guarded her position of power. 她警覺地守護著自己的權位。

<span class="vocabulary">**champion**</span> ['tʃæmpɪən] 
<span class="definition">vt. 為…而鬥爭；捍衛；聲援：</span>to champion the poor 為窮人發聲 / to champion the human rights 為人權而戰

<span class="vocabulary">**cushion**</span> ['kʊʃn] 
<span class="definition">vt. cushion sb/sth (against/from sth) 當某人或某事物遭受到的傷害、損害或不悅事件的時候，對其起到保護或緩衝作用：</span>The south of the country has been cushioned from the worst effects of the recession. 國家南部保護得好，沒有受到經濟衰退造成的最惡劣影響。/ He broke the news of my brother’s death to me, making no effort to cushion the blow. 他把我兄弟死亡的訊息直接告訴了我，沒有試圖減輕此事對我的打擊。
           
<span class="vocabulary">**buffer**</span> [ˈbʌfə(r)]
<span class="definition">vt. ~ sb (against sth) 保護；保障：</span>They tried to buffer themselves against problems and uncertainties. 他們盡力保護自己免受困難和不確定因素的影響。/ The company is buffered by long-term contracts with growers. 公司透過與種植戶簽訂長期合同得到保障。

<span class="vocabulary">**keep**</span> [ki:p] 
<span class="definition">vt. [正式] keep sb (from sth) 保護某人使其免受或免遭…：</span>May the Lord bless you and keep you. 願上帝祝福你、保佑你（用於基督教的祈禱）。/ to keep the boy from harm 不讓這個男孩受到傷害

<span class="vocabulary">**resist**</span> [rɪ'zɪst] 
<span class="definition">vt. 使不受某事物的傷害：</span>A healthy diet should help your body resist infection. 健康飲食有助於身體抵抗感染。/ This new paint is designed to resist heat. 這種新油漆具有耐熱性。
                      
<span class="vocabulary">**withstand**</span> [wɪðˈstænd; wɪθˈs-]
<span class="definition">vt. [正式] 承受住（極端條件等）：</span>The materials used have to be able to withstand high temperatures. 所使用的材料必須能夠耐高溫。/ The material is strong enough to withstand hurricane-force winds. 這種材料很堅固，足以抵抗颶風。/ armoured vehicles designed to withstand chemical attack 具有抵禦化學武器襲擊的設計功能的裝甲車 / The wooden boat was built to withstand just about every weather condition at sea. 這條木船建成後能經受住海上各種各樣的天氣條件。/ The building is strong enough to withstand an earthquake. 這個建築物很堅固，足以抗震。/ Exercise really can help you withstand stresses and strains more easily. 鍛鍊真的能幫助你更輕鬆地應對壓力和緊張情緒。/ They had withstood siege, hunger and deprivation. 他們經受了圍困、飢餓和貧窮。

<span class="vocabulary">**screen**</span> [skri:n] 
<span class="definition">vt. screen sth/sb (from sth/sb)（把…放在某人或事物的前面或周圍來）隱藏或保護（該人或事物），相當於shield：</span>Dark glasses screened his eyes from the sun. 他戴了一副墨鏡，保護眼睛不受陽光照射。/ Most of the road behind the hotel was screened by an apartment block. 旅館後面那條路的大部分都被一排公寓樓擋住了。

<span class="vocabulary">**ward**</span> [wɔ:d] 
<span class="definition">vt.＆vi. ward sb/sth off 指保護自己，使自己免遭危險、疾病或攻擊等。一般表示當對方發起攻擊（如肢體上的暴力攻擊、疾病來襲或言語抨擊等）時進行“即時性防衛”，而非預先做好防禦措施來抵禦後來的攻擊：</span>to ward off criticism 為自己受到的批評開脫 / He put up his left hand to ward off the stick. 他舉起左手，把棍子擋開。
           
<span class="vocabulary">**fend**</span> [fend]
<span class="definition">vt.&vi. fend off sb/sth 抵擋，擋開，避開（攻擊）：</span>He raised his hand to fend off the blow. 他抬起手來抵擋那一拳。/ The police officer fended off the blows with his riot shield. 警察用防暴盾牌抵擋攻擊。

<span class="vocabulary">**secure**</span> [sɪ'kjʊə] 
<span class="definition">vt. secure sth (against sth) 保護某事物，（常指某地）使其安全或很難被攻擊或破壞。強調預先做好防禦措施以使其難以被攻擊或破壞，從而給人帶來一種安全感：</span>to secure a property against intruders 保護房產以免外人闖入 / The windows were secured with locks and bars. 窗戶已經插上栓，上了鎖，都關好了。/ a savings plan that will secure your child’s future（比喻用法）確保你孩子的前途的儲蓄計劃

相關詞義延伸：
· protect還可指貿易保護；
· shield還可表示給機器等加防護罩；
· defend還可指辯解或辯白、在法律上為…辯護、在體育運動中防守。

周邊詞彙補充：
· hold可指守衛…（使其免受攻擊）；
· defence還可指防禦…使其免受攻擊、防禦…使其免受批評；
· keep還可指（在足球、曲棍球、板球等體育運動中）把守球門，即守門；
· insulate可指使…免除不愉快的經歷或免受不良影響。也可指用某種材料來隔熱、隔音等。還可指用某種材料來隔絕電流，即絕緣；
· insulated可指有隔熱（或隔音、絕緣）保護的。

<span class="vocabulary">**protection**</span> [prə'tekʃn] 
<span class="definition">n. 1 [U] protection (for/of sb/sth) (against/from sth) 保護…，或被保護的狀態：</span>The poor are under the protection of the government. 政府為窮人提供保護。/ legal protection 法律保護 / animal, data, environmental, witness protection 對動物的保護；對資料的保護；對環境的保護；對證人的保護 / She carries a gun in her bag for personal protection. 她包裡帶著一把槍以自衛。/ fire, flood protection 防火；防洪 / sun-protection products 防曬產品 / bankruptcy protection 破產保護 / copyright, patent protection 版權保護；專利保護 / The site is under the protection of UNESCO. 該遺址受聯合國教科文組織的保護。/ a means of protection 保護措施 <span class="definition">2 [C] protection (against sth) 起保護作用的事物，護身符：</span>to wear a protection against evil spirits 戴護身符以辟邪 <span class="definition">3 [U] 向犯罪分子交的保護費，使其不損害你的生意或資產：</span>to pay protection money 交保護費
           
<span class="vocabulary">**safeguard**</span> [ˈseɪfgɑ:d; 美 -gɑ:rd]

<span class="vocabulary">**preservation**</span> [ˌprezəˈveɪʃn; 美 -zərˈv-]

[U] 將某事物保持原狀或保持在好的狀態，如保護：</span>building preservation 建築保護 / railroad（美）, railway（英）鐵路保護 / environmental preservation 環境保護 / wildlife 野生動物保護 / farmland, wetland, wilderness preservation（均尤美）農田保護；溼地保護；荒野保護 / historic preservation 歷史性的保護 / preservation order（英）, preservation ordinance（美）保護令；保護條例 / The local authority has placed a preservation order/ordinance on the building. 地方當局已下了保護這幢建築的命令。/ preservation group, preservation society 環保團體；環保組織

<span class="vocabulary">**cover**</span> ['kʌvə] 
<span class="definition">n. 1 [U] 躲避處（可抵禦惡劣天氣或攻擊）：</span>Everyone ran for cover when it started to rain. 雨下起來時，大家都跑著找地方避雨。/ They sought cover from the wind. 他們尋找避風的地方。/ After the explosion the street was full of people running for cover. 爆炸發生以後，滿街的人都奔跑躲避。/ The deer broke cover as the hunters approached. 獵人接近時，鹿從躲藏的地方跑了出來。<span class="definition">2 [U] 在某人的進攻過程中、處於危險之中、或被攻擊的過程中所提供的掩護、防護（常指用武器進行的掩護）：</span>air cover for ground operations 為地面行動提供空中掩護 / They move under cover of darkness. 他們在黑夜的掩護下行進。
