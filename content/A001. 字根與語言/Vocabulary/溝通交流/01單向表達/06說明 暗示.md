☀ <span class="category">**說明 暗示的動詞和形容詞：**</span>
<span class="vocabulary">**indicate**</span> ['ɪndɪkeɪt] 
<span class="definition">vt. indicate sth (to sb) 提及某事（尤指間接提及），即暗示，示意：</span>He indicated his willingness to cooperate. 他透露出了合作的意願。

<span class="vocabulary">**suggest**</span> [sə'dӡest] 
<span class="definition">vt. 間接陳述某事，即暗示，言下之意是說，相當於imply：</span>Are you suggesting (that) I’m lazy? 你言下之意是說我懶？/ I would never suggest such a thing. 我根本沒有這樣的意思。
           
<span class="vocabulary">**hint**</span> [hɪnt]
<span class="definition">vt.&vi. ~ (at sth) 暗示：</span>What are you hinting at? 你在暗示什麼？/ They hinted (that) there might be more job losses. 他們暗示說可能會有更多人失業。/ She suggested a trip to the shops and hinted at the possibility of a treat of some sort. 她建議去逛逛商店，並暗示可能要請客。/ Criticism is hinted at, but never made explicit. 批評總是含沙射影，但從未明說。/ He hinted strongly that he would be resigning soon. 他強烈地示意不久將辭職。/ She hinted darkly that all was not well. 她悲觀地暗示並非一切都順利。/ The problems are only hinted at in the report. 報告只是暗示了這些問題。
           
<span class="vocabulary">**imply**</span> [ɪmˈplaɪ]
<span class="definition">vt. 以不直接說出來的方式來表明自己的所思所想所感或某事是真實的，即暗示：</span>Are you implying (that) I am wrong? 你的意思是不是說我錯了？/ I disliked the implied criticism in his voice. 我討厭他暗中批評的口吻。/ I never meant to imply any criticism. 我從來沒有想要批評的意思。/ The letter seems to imply that the minister knew about the business deals. 這封信似乎暗示總裁知道這幾筆商業交易。/ The article falsely implied that he was responsible for the accident. 文章錯誤地暗示他應該對事故負責。/ The statement logically implies a certain conclusion. 這一陳述在邏輯上隱含著一個特定的結論。/ This statement should not be taken to imply that the government is exonerated of all blame. 不應認為這一陳述暗示政府可以免受指責。/ the express or implied terms of the contract 合同裡明示或暗含的條件
            
<span class="vocabulary">**infer**</span> [ɪnˈfɜ:(r)]
<span class="definition">vt. [非標準英語，許多人認為此用法不正確] 間接表明（某事是真的），如暗示、暗指：</span>Are you inferring (that) I'm not capable of doing the job? 你的言外之意是不是我不能勝任這份工作？/ The police inferred that they found her behaviour rather suspicious. 警方暗示他們發現她行為非常可疑。

<span class="vocabulary">**insinuate**</span> [ɪnˈsɪnjueɪt]
<span class="definition">vt. 間接表明（即暗示）不快之事屬實：</span>The article insinuated that he was having an affair with his friend's wife. 文章含沙射影地點出他和朋友的妻子有染。/ The libel claim followed an article which insinuated that the President was lying. 一篇文章因含沙射影聲稱總統撒謊而遭到誹謗索賠。/ Are you insinuating that I smell? 你是在旁敲側擊地說我身上有臭味嗎？/ What are you trying to insinuate? 你拐彎抹角想說什麼？/ an insinuating smile 暗示的微笑          

<span class="vocabulary">**intimate**</span> [ˈɪntɪmət]
<span class="definition">vt. ~ sth (to sb) [正式] 向某人間接透露你的所想所思或意圖：</span>He has already intimated to us his intention to retire. 他已經向我們透露了他要退休的打算。/ He went on to intimate that he was indeed contemplating a shake-up of the company. 他繼續暗示他確實在考慮對公司進行一次大改組。/ He had intimated to the French and Russians his readiness to come to a settlement. 他已經向法國人和俄國人暗示他願意達成協議。

<span class="vocabulary">**drop**</span> [drɒp] 
<span class="definition">vt. drop a hint（透過說…或做某事來向別人）暗示自己的想法。不常用：</span>Jerry dropped hints that he and Julie were talking about getting married. 傑裡露出口風說他同朱莉已談婚論嫁了。
           
<span class="vocabulary">**connote**</span> [kəˈnəʊt; 美 kəˈnoʊt]
<span class="definition">vt. [正式]（詞語或名字）除了主要意思外還隱含著別的意味，即隱含，使人聯想到…：</span>The term 'organization' often connotes a sense of neatness. “organization”這個詞常常讓人想到整潔。
           
<span class="vocabulary">**smack**</span> [smæk]
<span class="definition">vi. ~ of 令人想起壞的事物（或令人想起類似的壞的事物）：</span>The engineers' union was unhappy with the motion, saying it smacked of racism. 工程師聯盟對這項提議感到很不滿，稱該提議有點種族主義的味道。/ This move smacks a little of desperation. 這一行動有點兒絕望的意味。/ It just smacks of paranoia. 那只是有些偏執。/ Today's announcement smacks strongly of a government cover-up. 今天的公告凸顯出政府掩人耳目的意圖。
          
<span class="vocabulary">**indicative**</span> [ɪnˈdɪkətɪv]
<span class="definition">adj. [正式] [名詞前不常用] ~ (of sth) (formal) 說明；暗示：</span>Recurrent dreams are not necessarily indicative of psychological problems. 夢境反覆出現未必說明心理有問題。

☀ <span class="category">**說明 暗示的名詞：**</span>
<span class="vocabulary">**insinuation**</span> [ɪnˌsɪnjuˈeɪʃn]
<span class="definition">n. 1 [C] 旁敲側擊的話，含沙射影的話，暗示的話：</span>She resented the insinuation that she was too old for the job. 她憎惡暗示她太老不適合這項工作的話。/ I deeply resent the insinuation that I'm only interested in the money. 我對有人旁敲側擊地說我只對錢感興趣十分反感。/ Why did you make those veiled insinuations about me? 你為什麼對我說那些含沙射影的話?/ insinuations against the unsuccessful candidate 對落選者的旁敲側擊 <span class="definition">2 [U] 含沙射影，旁敲側擊：</span>瞭解即可

<span class="vocabulary">**suggestion**</span> [sə'dӡestʃən] 
<span class="definition">n. [U] 透過讓人產生聯想而把某個觀念嵌入到人的頭腦中，如暗示：</span>Most advertisements work through suggestion. 多數廣告都是透過暗示而發揮作用。/ hypnotic suggestion 催眠暗示 / to remove the pain by the power of suggestion 用暗示的力量消除病痛
          
<span class="vocabulary">**hint**</span> [hɪnt]
<span class="definition">n. [C] 用言語或行為來把自己的想法間接告訴某人，即暗示：</span>Should I drop a hint (= give a hint ) to him? 我應該給他一點暗示嗎？/ I dropped a few subtle hints about the payment being due. 我幾次含蓄地暗示該到期付款了。/ OK, I get the hint! 好的，我明白你的意思了！/ Can't you take a hint and leave me alone? 你難道就聽不懂我的暗示？讓我一個人清靜會兒吧。/ Is that a hint to me to leave? 那是在暗示要我離開嗎？/ a hint from my boss about my absences from the office 老闆暗示我數次曠工 / The Minister gave a strong hint that the government were thinking of introducing tax concessions for mothers. 部長明顯地暗示政府正在考慮對母親們實行稅收減免。/ He gave a broad hint (= one that was obvious) that he was thinking of retiring. 他幾乎明示他正在考慮退休。
           
<span class="vocabulary">**innuendo**</span> [ˌɪnjuˈendəʊ; 美 -doʊ]
<span class="definition">n. [C, U] [貶]（通常指對壞事或粗魯之事的）暗指、含沙射影：</span>innuendoes about her private life 對她私生活含沙射影的指責 / The song is full of sexual innuendo. 那首歌充滿了性的暗示。/ The report was based on rumours, speculation, and innuendo. 這份報告建立在謠言、臆斷和含沙射影的基礎之上。
                      
<span class="vocabulary">**allusion**</span> [əˈlu:ʒn]
<span class="definition">n. [C, U] ~ (to sb/sth) 間接提到、暗指、影射、典故：</span>His statement was seen as an allusion to the recent drug-related killings. 他的宣告被視為暗指最近與毒品有關的多起兇殺案。/ The title is perhaps an allusion to AIDS. 該題目可能暗指艾滋病。/ She was made uncomfortable by his veiled allusion to the previous night. 他對頭天晚上的事閃爍其詞令她感到不快。/ He makes several allusions to these events in his latest book. 他在最新出版的書中幾次提及這些事件。/ biblical, classical, cultural, historical, literary, Shakespearean allusion《聖經》典故；典故；文化典故；歷史典故；文學典故；莎士比亞文學作品中的典故 / Her poetry is full of obscure literary allusion. 她的詩隨處可見晦澀的文學典故。
           
<span class="vocabulary">**implication**</span> [ˌɪmplɪˈkeɪʃn]
<span class="definition">n. [C, U] 含意；暗指：</span>The implication in his article is that being a housewife is greatly inferior to every other occupation. 他那篇文章的含意是，當家庭主婦遠遠不如所有其他職業。/ The implication was obvious: vote for us or it will be very embarrassing for you. 弦外之音很明顯：投票給我們，否則你會非常難堪。/ He criticized the Director and, by implication, the whole of the organization. 他抨擊主管，其實是間接批評了整個機構。/ The implication that marital infidelity enhances a leader's credibility is preposterous. 婚姻不忠會提升領導人可信度的暗示荒謬之極。/ The implication is clear: young females do better if they mate with a new male. 含意很清楚：年輕雌性如果與陌生雄性交配表現會更好。/ His remark seemed to have various possible implications. 他的話似乎暗含了好幾層意思。/ unspoken implication 沒有明說的意思 / Brian paused for a moment while he digested the implications of this statement. 布賴恩停了片刻，琢磨這句話的言外之意。/ I resent the implication that I don't care about my father. 我討厭別人暗示我不關心父親。/ In refusing to believe our story, he is saying by implication that we are lying. 他拒不相信我們所講的事情，言外之意就是我們在撒謊。

周邊詞彙補充：
· key可指（地圖或平面圖中）對符號的說明；
· suggestive可指使人想起…的；引起聯想的。還可指讓人們想到性的，即性暗示的、性挑逗的。
