☀ <span class="category">**想象、設想、聯想的動詞：**</span>
<span class="vocabulary">**imagine**</span> [ɪ'mædӡɪn] 
<span class="definition">vt.＆vi. 在頭腦中對…的形象等所進行的設想，即想象，設想。是“想象”的常規用詞：</span>I can’t imagine life without the children now. 我現在無法想象沒有了孩子們的生活。/ She could just imagine her mother’s look of horror. 她完全可以想象出母親臉上恐懼的表情。/ The artist is free to imagine anything she pleases. 藝術家可以任憑想象自由馳騁。/ Close your eyes and imagine (that) you are in a forest. 閉上眼睛，設想自己在森林裡。

<span class="vocabulary">**envisage**</span> [ɪnˈvɪzɪdʒ]
<span class="definition">vt. [尤英] 想象未來將發生某事：</span>What level of profit do you envisage? 你預計會有什麼樣的利潤水平？/ I don't envisage working with him again. 我想象不出再與他一起工作的可能。/ I can't envisage her coping with this job. 我無法設想她如何應付這個工作。/ It is envisaged that the talks will take place in the spring. 談判預期在春季舉行。/ He envisages the possibility of establishing direct diplomatic relations in the future. 他設想在未來可以建立直接的外交關係。/ He had never envisaged spending the whole of his working life in that particular job. 他從沒有想象過自己整個職業生涯都要從事那個工作。/ It was originally envisaged that the talks would take place in the spring. 最初的設想是在春季舉行會談。/ It was never envisaged that this would be a long-term solution. 從未想過這將會是一個長遠的解決方案。/ I can envisage difficulties if we continue with this policy. 如果我們繼續實行這項政策，我可以想象到可能遇到的困難。/ I cannot envisage myself playing again next season. 我無法想象自己下一個賽季還要比賽。

<span class="vocabulary">**envision**</span> [ɪnˈvɪʒn]
<span class="definition">vt. 設想未來，尤指設想自己嚮往的未來：</span>They envision an equal society, free of poverty and disease. 他們嚮往一個沒有貧窮和疾病的平等社會。/ In the future we envision a federation of companies. 我們設想將來會成立一個公司聯盟。

<span class="definition">vt. [尤美] 想象未來發生的事。相當於英式的envisage：</span>They didn't envision any problems with the new building. 他們沒想到這棟新樓會有什麼問題。/ Most people do stop at this point, not envisioning that there is anything beyond. 大多數人確實都在這裡停下，想象不到在這之外還會有些什麼。/ The work took longer than initially envisioned. 這項工作需要的時間比最初設想的要長。/ I can easily envision them working together.我能夠很容易地想象出他們一起工作的樣子。/ Can you envision such a scenario? 你能設想這樣一個場景嗎?/ He envisioned himself dying in a pool of his own blood. 他想象自己奄奄一息地躺在血泊裡。

<span class="vocabulary">**think**</span> [θɪŋk] 
<span class="definition">vt.＆vi. 無被動式，想象…：</span>Just think--we’ll be lying on the beach this time tomorrow. 想想看，明天這個時候我們就躺在海灘上了。/ Try to think yourself into the role. 儘量發揮想象力，使自己進入角色。
                      
<span class="vocabulary">**visualize**</span> [ˈvɪʒuəlaɪz]
<span class="definition">vt. ~ sth (as sth) 在頭腦中形成影象，如想象、設想、構想：</span>Susan visualized her wedding day and saw herself walking down the aisle on her father's arm. 蘇珊想象著婚禮日當天的情景——自己挽著父親的手臂走在教堂的過道上。/ I visualize d him as a typical businessman. 我把他想象成一個典型的商人。/ Try to visualize him as an old man. 儘量設想他是一位老人。/ He could not visualize her as old. 他想象不出她年老時的樣子。/ I can't visualize what this room looked like before it was decorated. 我想象不出這個房間在裝修之前是什麼樣子。/ It can help to visualize yourself making your speech clearly and confidently. 設想自己口齒清晰和充滿信心地演講是很有助益的。/ She couldn't visualize climbing the mountain. 她想象不出如何攀登這座大山。/ I could visualize the scene in the office. 我能想象得出辦公室裡的那一幕。/ It is difficult to visualize how the town must have looked years ago. 很難想象這座小鎮在多年以前是什麼樣子。

<span class="vocabulary">**picture**</span> ['pɪktʃə] 
<span class="definition">vt. picture sb (as sth) 在頭腦中建立一個影象，即想象；設想等。是一個形象化用法：</span>He pictured his wife sitting in the chair, smiling to him. 他想象妻子正坐在椅子上，朝他微笑。/ It’s hard to picture her as the mother of two children. 很難想象她已經是兩個孩子的母親了。/ I tried to pictured what my life would be like ten years later. 我曾努力設想，十年後我的生活將會是什麼樣子的。

<span class="vocabulary">**see**</span> [si:] 
<span class="definition">vt. see sb/sth (as sth) 把…想象或設想為…。是一個簡單用詞，不太常用：</span>I can’t see her changing her mind. 我無法想象她會改變主意。

<span class="vocabulary">**associate**</span> [ə'səʊʃɪeɪt] 
<span class="definition">vt. associate sb/sth (with sb/sth) 在頭腦中將不同的人或事物建立聯絡，即聯想，聯絡：</span>I always associate the smell of baking with my childhood. 一聞到烘烤食物的味道我就想起了童年。/ He is closely associated in the public mind with horror movies. 在公眾的心目中，他總是和恐怖電影緊密聯絡在一起。

☀ <span class="category">**想象、設想、聯想的名詞和形容詞：**</span>
<span class="vocabulary">**imagination**</span> [ɪˌmædʒɪˈneɪʃn]
<span class="definition">n. 1 [U, C] 想象力；想象：</span>vivid, fertile imagination 生動的想象力；豐富的想象 / He's got no imagination. 他缺乏想象力。/ Alistair had a logical mind, and little imagination. 阿利斯泰爾邏輯思維能力很強，但缺乏想象力。/ Antonia is a woman with a vivid imagination. 安東尼婭是個想象力豐富的女人。/ It doesn't take much imagination to guess what she meant. 不難猜出她的意思。/ I won't tell you his reaction ─I'll leave that to your imagination. 我不告訴你他的反應，你自己去想好了。/ Don't let your imagination run away with you (= don't use too much imagination). 不要一味憑空想象。/ The new policies appear to have caught the imagination of the public (= they find them interesting and exciting). 新出臺的政策似乎恰恰投合了公眾的喜好。/ Nobody hates you--it's all in your imagination. 沒人討厭你——都是你在胡思亂想。/ Long before I ever went there, Africa was alive in my imagination. 早在我真正踏足之前很久,非洲就已在我的腦海中活靈活現了。/ Use your imagination! (= used to tell sb that they will have to guess the answer to the question they have asked you, usually because it is obvious or embarrassing)（比喻用法）你自己動動腦筋嘛！<span class="definition">2 [U] 想象（而非真實存在）的事物：</span>She was no longer able to distinguish between imagination and reality. 她再也不能分清幻想和現實了。/ Is it my imagination or have you lost a lot of weight? 是我的錯覺，還是你確實瘦了許多？<span class="definition">3 [U] 頭腦的創造力：</span>His writing lacks imagination. 他的作品缺乏想象。/ With a little imagination, you could turn this place into a palace. 稍微動點腦筋，就能把這個地方變得富麗堂皇。
           
<span class="vocabulary">**vision**</span> [ˈvɪʒn]
<span class="definition">n. 1 [C] 頭腦中對未來情形或社會的想象或展望：</span>a vision for the future 對未來的展望 / He had a vision of a world in which there would be no wars. 他幻想有一個沒有戰爭的世界。/ I have a vision of a society that is free of exploitation and injustice. 我希望建立一個沒有剝削和不公的社會。/ That's my vision of how the world could be. 那是我對未來世界的想象。/ romantic, Utopian vision 浪漫的想法；烏托邦式的想象 / long-term vision 長期願景 / He outlined his vision for the new economic order. 他概述了自己對新經濟秩序的展望。/ The new leader set about imposing his vision on the party. 新任領導人開始在黨內推行自己的設想。<span class="definition">2 [C] 對某種情景的想象：</span>He had a vision of Cheryl, slumped on a plastic chair in the waiting-room. 他想象謝里爾癱坐在候診室的一把塑膠椅子上。/ Maybe you had visions of being surrounded by happy, smiling children. 也許你曾幻想過被快樂嬉笑的孩子所包圍的情景。/ I had visions of us getting hopelessly lost. 我想象我們完全迷失了方向。/ The name Las Vegas' conjures up a vision of casinos. "拉斯維加斯"這個名字使人聯想到賭場。/ poetic vision 詩歌意境 <span class="definition">3 [U] 想象力；（對未來做計劃的）遠見卓識：</span>a leader of vision 有遠見的領袖 / a statesman of great vision 高瞻遠矚的政治家 / The company needs to develop a global vision. 這家公司需要培養全球性的視野。/ The engineers had a clear vision of what they wanted to achieve. 工程師清楚地知道他們想達到的目標。/ They share a common vision for the development of health services. 他們在如何發展衛生保健服務上看法一致。/ strategic vision 戰略眼光 / political vision 政治上的遠見 / revolutionary vision 革命的遠見 / moral vision 道德上的遠見卓識 / He was determined not to let emotions cloud his vision. 他決心不讓感情矇蔽自己的雙眼。/ an alternative vision of society 對社會的另類看法 / His plans for the country's future show a remarkable breadth of vision. 他對國家未來的構想顯示了他的遠見卓識。
           
<span class="vocabulary">**foresight**</span> [ˈfɔ:saɪt; 美 ˈfɔ:rs-]
<span class="definition">n. [U] [褒] 先見之明、預見（以便為將來做準備，或採取合適的行動）：</span>She had had the foresight to prepare herself financially in case of an accident. 她有先見之明，經濟上作了準備以防萬一發生事故。/ He was later criticized for his lack of foresight. 他後來被指責缺少先見之明。/ They had the foresight to invest in new technology. 他們有投資新技術的遠見。/ He had the foresight to bring in the chairs before the rain started. 他真有先見之明，下雨前就把椅子收了進來。/ The plans showed great foresight. 這些計劃極有先見之明。/ a lack of foresight 缺乏先見

<span class="vocabulary">**association**</span> [ə͵səʊsɪ'eɪʃn, ə͵səʊʃɪ'eɪ-] 
<span class="definition">n. [C, usually pl.] 頭腦中所激發出的聯絡性想法，即聯想。是此義的常規用詞：</span>The seaside had all sorts of pleasant associations with childhood holidays for me. 海濱使我聯想起童年假期的各種愉快情景。/ The cat soon made the association between human beings and food. 這隻貓很快就把人類與食物聯絡起來。/ Tourists visit the city for its historical associations. 遊人來該城市觀光是因為其豐富的歷史內涵。/ to trigger associations 引發聯想
                      
<span class="vocabulary">**projection**</span> [prəˈdʒekʃn]
<span class="definition">n. [U] 心理學中可指投射（不自覺地把自己的慾念等加諸他人）：</span>瞭解即可

<span class="vocabulary">**redolent**</span> [ˈredələnt]
<span class="definition">adj. [名詞前不可用] [文學] ~ of/with sth 使人想起…的、使人聯想到…的：</span>an atmosphere redolent of the sea and ships 讓人聯想起大海和船隻的氛圍 / percussion instruments, redolent of Far Eastern cultures. 使人聯想起遠東文化的打擊樂器 / a sad tale, redolent with regret 使人覺得遺憾的悲情故事
                      
<span class="vocabulary">**conceivable**</span> [kənˈsi:vəbl]
<span class="definition">adj. 可想象的；可信的：</span>It is conceivable that I'll see her tomorrow. 我可能明天會見到她。/ a beautiful city with buildings of every conceivable age and style 擁有各個時代和各種風格建築物的美麗城市 / Without their support the project would not have been conceivable. 如果沒有他們的支援，這個專案根本就不可想象。/ It is just conceivable that a single survivor might be found. 或許能找到一個倖存者，這不是沒有可能。/ It's quite conceivable that she hasn't heard the news yet. 完全可以想象得到，她還未聽到這個訊息。/ I suppose it's just conceivable that we've made a mistake.（just尤為英式用法）我想我們犯了一個錯誤也是完全可以理解的。/ We had to draw up plans for every conceivable emergency. 我們得制訂計劃應付所有能想象到的突發事件。

<span class="vocabulary">**imaginable**</span> [ɪˈmædʒɪnəbl]
<span class="definition">adj. 1 與形容詞最高階或與all、every連用，表示強調或概括，如想象得到的：</span>The house has the most spectacular views imaginable. 從這所房子可以看到所能想象的最壯麗的景色。/ They stock every imaginable type of pasta. 他們備有各種能想到的義大利麵食。/ They had every imaginable shade of green. 什麼樣的綠色他們都有。/ They had every shade of green imaginable. 什麼樣的綠色他們都有。/ all of the other imaginable ramifications of this technology 這項技術可能產生的其他一切後果 / Parents encourage every activity imaginable. 父母鼓勵孩子參與一切可能的活動。<span class="definition">2 常與 best，worst 等形容詞最高階連用，表示可想象的、想象得到的：</span>These technological developments were hardly imaginable 30 years ago. 這些科技新產品在30年前幾乎是不可想象的。/ their imprisonment under some of the most horrible circumstances imaginable 他們被關押在可以想象出的最恐怖的環境中 / He could not disguise that he had had the worst imaginable day for any minister. 他剛剛經歷了對於任何部長而言都是最糟糕的一天，這對他來說無法掩飾。/ To such poor people, the idea of having a choice of food is barely imaginable. 對如此貧苦的人來說，挑選食物是想也不敢想的事。/ It was the most boring movie imaginable! 這部電影要多無聊有多無聊！/ They live in the worst conditions imaginable. 他們的居住條件之差你怎麼形容都不為過。

<span class="vocabulary">**imaginative**</span> [ɪˈmædʒɪnətɪv]
<span class="definition">adj.（某人或其想法）富於想象力的、有獨創性的：</span>imaginative writer 想象力豐富的作家 / imaginative approach, imaginative idea, imaginative child 有創意的方法；有創意的思想；富有想象力的孩子 / a wonderfully imaginative story 極富想象力的故事 / recipes that make imaginative use of seasonal vegetables 妙用時令蔬菜的菜譜 / hundreds of cooking ideas and imaginative recipes 成百上千個烹飪點子和富有創意的食譜
           
<span class="vocabulary">**suggestive**</span> [səˈdʒestɪv; North American English also səgˈdʒ-]
<span class="definition">adj. ~ (of sth) 使人想起…的；引起聯想的：</span>music that is suggestive of warm summer days 使人想起溫暖夏日的音樂 / The fingers were gnarled, lumpy, with long, curving nails suggestive of animal claws. 手指粗糙，關節突出，指甲又長又彎，讓人想起動物的爪子。/ These headaches were most suggestive of raised blood pressure. 這類頭痛多是血壓升高的徵兆。/ The results were highly suggestive of malignancy. 結果表明很可能是惡性腫瘤。

周邊詞彙補充：
· scenario可指（對將來情況的）設想、預測；
· blueprint可指行動方案、計劃、藍圖、設想、構想。

☀ <span class="category">**想象生動的、活躍的：**</span>
<span class="vocabulary">**vivid**</span> ['vɪvɪd] 
<span class="definition">adj. 1（記憶、描述等）在頭腦裡產生非常清晰的圖片的，即生動的，逼真的，相當於graphic：</span>vivid memories 清晰的記憶 / a vivid account of his life 對他生活的生動描述 / a vivid imagination 生動逼真的想象 / to be vivid in sb’s mind 在某人腦海中栩栩如生 <span class="definition">2（人的想象）能夠在頭腦中對觀念、情況等輕易地形成圖片的：</span>瞭解即可

<span class="vocabulary">**active**</span> ['æktɪv] 
<span class="definition">adj. 活躍的且充滿想法的：</span>an active imagination 豐富的想象力 / to remain mentally active 保持敏捷的思維
           
<span class="vocabulary">**graphic**</span> [ˈgræfɪk]
<span class="definition">adj.（尤指對令人不快之事的描述）形象的、生動的、逼真的：</span>a graphic account/description of a battle 對戰鬥的生動敘述/描述 / He kept telling us about his operation, in the most graphic detail. 他不停地向我們繪聲繪色地講述他動手術的詳細情況。/ The descriptions of sexual abuse are graphic. 這些性虐待的描寫繪聲繪色。/ graphic scenes of drug taking 赤裸裸的吸毒場景

☀ <span class="category">**腦中的場景：**</span>
<span class="vocabulary">**picture**</span> ['pɪktʃə] 
<span class="definition">n. [C, usually sing.] 頭腦中的景象：</span>Because she’s not involved, she can see the bigger picture. 她因為是個局外人，所以視角更寬廣。/ to build up a broad picture of the culture 對這種文化有大致的瞭解 / mental picture 腦海中的景象 / The police are trying to build up a picture of what happened. 警方正在試圖掌握事情發生的經過。/ a complex picture of family rivalry 家庭成員間勾心鬥角的複雜場景 
           
<span class="vocabulary">**prospect**</span> [ˈprɒspekt; 美 ˈprɑ:s-]
<span class="definition">n. 1 [sing.] ~ (of sth/of doing sth) 前景、展望、設想：</span>an exciting prospect 令人興奮的前景 / I don't relish the prospect of having to share an office. 一想到將來要和別人共用一間辦公室我就感到不快。/ Travelling alone around the world is a daunting prospect. 想象著將要隻身走遍世界頗令人心悸。/ The prospect of becoming a father filled him with alarm. 一想到將為人父他就滿懷憂思。/ They now face the prospect of having to wear a cycling helmet by law. 他們現在面臨的情形是：不久將需要依法佩戴摩托車頭盔。/ After supper he'd put his feet up and read. It was a pleasant prospect. 晚飯後，他會架起腳來看書。想起來就讓人愉快。<span class="definition">2 [pl.] prospects (for/of sth) 成功的前景：</span>future, long-term prospects前程；遠景 / development, economic, growth prospects發展前景；經濟前景；增長前景 / career, employment, job prospects職業前景；就業前景；工作前途 / survival prospects生存前景 / Their prospects of employment look better than last year. 他們的就業前景看起來好於去年。/ At 25 he was an unemployed musician with no prospects. 25歲的他是個沒有工作、前途渺茫的樂師。/ Long-term prospects for the economy have improved. 長期的經濟前景已有所改善。/ I chose to work abroad to improve my career prospects. 我選擇出國工作以求在事業上有更好的發展。/ a detailed review of the company's prospects 對該公司前景的詳細考查
           
<span class="vocabulary">**outlook**</span> [ˈaʊtlʊk]
<span class="definition">n. [sing.] ~ (for sth) 前景：</span>uncertain outlook 不確定的前景 / business, political outlook 商業前景；政治前景 / The outlook for jobs is bleak. 就業市場前景暗淡。/ the country's economic outlook 國家的經濟前景 / The economic outlook is one of rising unemployment. 展望未來的經濟，失業率將會上升。/ Has motherhood changed your career outlook? 當了母親是否改變了你的職業前途？/ The drug improves the long-term outlook of migraine sufferers. 堅持服用這種藥使偏頭痛患者的長期預後有所改善。/ short-term, long-term outlook 短期前景；長期前景

☀ <span class="category">**幻想、夢想：**</span>
<span class="vocabulary">**fantasy**</span> ['fæntəsɪ] 
<span class="definition">n. 1 [C] 所想象出來的但不太可能發生的一種美好情景，即幻想。是“幻想”的常規用詞：</span>his childhood fantasies about becoming a famous football player 他兒時想成為著名足球運動員的幻想 / She dismissed the idea as a wild fantasy. 她覺得那個想法太過不切實際而不予考慮。/ romantic fantasy 浪漫幻想 <span class="definition">2 [C] 想象出來的產物，幻想出來的作品：</span>Her books are usually escapist fantasies. 她的書通常是逃避現實的幻想作品。<span class="definition">3 [U] 幻想（或想象）的行為；一個人的幻想（或想象）：</span>a work of fantasy 幻想的作品 / fantasy life 幻想的生活 / fantasy figure 幻想中的人物 / fantasy film, fantasy game, fantasy novel 奇幻電影；夢幻遊戲；奇幻小說 / fantasy team 夢之隊 / Stop living in a fantasy world. 別再生活在幻想世界中了。

<span class="vocabulary">**fancy**</span> ['fænsɪ] 
<span class="definition">n. [C, U] 想象的事物；想象或想象力。其含義接近於“幻想”，相當於fantasy，不過不如fantasy常用：</span>night-time fancies that disappear in the morning 在早上逝去的夜間幻想 / a child’s wild flights of fancy 孩子的異想天開
                           
<span class="vocabulary">**mirage**</span> [ˈmɪrɑ:ʒ; mɪˈrɑ:ʒ; 美 məˈrɑ:ʒ]
<span class="definition">n. 1 [C] 海市蜃樓、幻境：</span>Through my half-closed eyelids I began to see mirages. 透過我半睜半閉的雙眼，我看到了海市蜃樓。/ It hovered before his eyes like the mirage of an oasis. 它在他眼前晃動，好像海市蜃樓裡的綠洲。<span class="definition">2 [C] 一種不會實現的希望或願望，即幻想、妄想：</span>The girl was a mirage, cast up by his troubled mind. 那個女孩是他的幻覺，是他那憂鬱不安的頭腦妄想出來的。/ The objectivity of science is a mirage. 所謂科學的客觀性是一種幻想。/ His idea of love was a mirage. 他的愛情觀不現實。/ Perhaps we are all just chasing a mirage. 也許我們都只是在追逐一個幻想。
 
<span class="vocabulary">**illusion**</span> [ɪˈlu:ʒn]
<span class="definition">n. 1 [C, U]（尤指對某人或對某個情況的）錯誤的觀念，如幻想：</span>I have no illusions about her feelings for me (= I know the truth is that she does not love me). 我對她跟我的感情不抱什麼幻想。/ I never had any grand illusions of winning. 我從未奢望過能贏。/ She's under the illusion that (= believes wrongly that) she'll get the job. 她存有幻想，認為她會得到那份工作。/ He could no longer distinguish between illusion and reality. 他再也分不清幻想與現實之間的區別了。/ Do not have any illusions that an industrial tribunal will right all employment wrongs. 不要幻想一個勞資仲裁法庭能糾正所有的僱傭不公現象。/ No one really has any illusions about winning the war. 沒有人幻想能贏得這場戰爭。/ I abandoned my romantic illusions a long time ago. 我很久以前就拋棄那些浪漫的幻想了。/ They are under no illusions about the difficulties ahead of them. 他們對將要面對的種種困難沒有抱任何幻想。/ Within the first week at university all my illusions were shattered. 大學的第一個星期還沒結束，我所有的幻想就已全部破滅了。/ I think I have lost all the illusions I had left. 我覺得我原先抱有的所有幻想都已經破滅了。/ She had no illusions about her attractiveness. 她對自己的吸引力不抱任何幻想。<span class="definition">2 [C] 幻想出來的（實際不存在的）事物，如錯覺、幻象、假象：</span>Mirrors in a room often give an illusion of space. 房間裡的鏡子常給人一種空間增大的錯覺。/ The idea of absolute personal freedom is an illusion. 絕對個人自由的觀念是一種幻想。/ Floor-to-ceiling windows can give the illusion of extra height. 從地板直抵天花板的窗子會給人以高出實際距離的幻覺。/ This eerie calm is an illusion. 這種怪誕的平靜是一種假象。/ To believe you have nothing more to learn is a dangerous illusion. 以為自己再沒什麼科學的想法是一種危險的錯覺。/ The road ahead looks wet, but in fact this is an optical illusion. 前方的路面看上去溼漉漉的，但實際上那只是視覺錯覺。/ I don't want to give him any false illusions. 我不想給他任何錯覺。/ The slogan provides the comforting illusion that something is being done. 這標語給人錯覺，讓人以為正在採取某種措施而感到欣慰。/ The figure was only a trick of light, a mere illusion. 那個影像只不過是燈光造成的幻影，純粹是錯覺。/ The huge size of the vehicle gives the illusion of safety. 龐大的車身給人以安全的錯覺。/ They are trying to maintain the illusion that the company is in good shape. 他們在努力維持公司運轉良好的假象。/ It turned out that their happy marriage was all an illusion. 結果發現他們的幸福婚姻完全是個錯覺。
    
<span class="vocabulary">**hallucination**</span> [həˌlu:sɪˈneɪʃn]
<span class="definition">n. [C] 幻覺產生的幻象（即幻覺中看到或聽到的東西）：</span>Was the figure real or just a hallucination? 那個人影是真的呢，還是幻象？/ Perhaps the footprint was a hallucination. 腳印可能只是幻象。

<span class="vocabulary">**delusion**</span> [dɪˈlu:ʒn]
<span class="definition">n. 1 [C]（對於自己或自己的處境的）錯覺、錯誤的觀念：</span>I was under the delusion that he intended to marry me. 我誤認為他要娶我。/ the delusions of the mentally ill 精神病患者的錯覺 / Don't go getting delusions of grandeur (= a belief that you are more important than you actually are). 不要變得妄自尊大。/ He suggested that we suffer delusions of grandeur as a football nation. 他暗示我們妄自尊大地以為自己是個足球民族。/ mansions built by men with delusions of grandeur 自以為是的人建造的豪宅 / dangerous delusion 危險的錯覺 / He dismissed the so-called miracle as a collective delusion. 他認為這個所謂的奇蹟只是一種集體錯覺，不值一提。/ The psychiatrist said she was suffering from paranoid delusions. 心理醫生說她患了偏執妄想症。/ He seemed to be under the delusion that he would make his fortune within a few years. 他似乎幻想著幾年內會發財。/ He had no delusions about his feelings for Kate.他對自己對凱特的情感不抱幻想。/ She had delusions of persecution. 她有受迫害的錯覺。<span class="definition">2 [U] 使自己相信錯誤的東西，如（對自己的）欺騙、妄想、妄念：</span>This was not optimism, it was delusion. 這不是樂觀，這是妄想。
           
<span class="vocabulary">**vision**</span> [ˈvɪʒn]
<span class="definition">n. [C]（尤指宗教的）夢幻、幻象、神示、異象：</span>The idea came to her in a vision. 她在神示中想到了這個主意。/ It was on 24th June 1981 that young villagers first reported seeing the Virgin Mary in a vision. 1981年6月24日那天，年輕的村民們首次報告說看到了聖母馬利亞的幻象。/ an apocalyptic vision of the end of civilization 人類文明終結的末世景象 / mystic, mystical, prophetic, religious vision 神秘的幻象；有預見性的幻象；宗教幻象 / A young girl in the town experienced a prophetic vision. 鎮上的一個小女孩曾預見到尚未發生的事。/ inner, mental, spiritual vision 心中的幻象；腦海裡的幻象；精神上的幻象 / As he approached, the vision faded and there was no one there. 他走上前去，幻象消失了，眼前空無一人。/ The idea came to her in a vision. 她在幻想中想出了這個主意。

<span class="vocabulary">**daydream**</span> [ˈdeɪdri:m]
<span class="definition">n. [C] 讓你忘記當前的美好幻想，即白日夢，是一種比喻用法：</span>She stared out of the window, lost in a daydream. 她凝視窗外，沉浸在幻想之中。/ He escaped into daydreams of beautiful women. 他躲進白日夢中幻想美女。/ The idea for the story came to him in a daydream. 這個故事的念頭是他做白日夢的時候突然想到的。<span class="definition">vi. ~ (about sb/sth) 幻想美好的事情：</span>I would spend hours daydreaming about a house of my own. 我常常一連幾個小時幻想自己有一所房子。/ Do you work hard for success rather than daydream about it? 你是否付出艱苦努力以取得成功，而不是隻在那裡做白日夢？/ He daydreams of being a famous journalist. 他幻想著自己成為著名記者。   

<span class="vocabulary">**dream**</span> [dri:m]

<span class="vocabulary">**guess**</span> [ɡes] 
<span class="definition">vt.＆vi. guess (at sth) 在一些事實尚不明確的前提下試圖給出一個答案或做出一個判斷，即猜測，估計。是“猜測”的常規用詞：</span>I’d guess that she’s about 30. 我猜她大約30歲。/ What star sign are you? <span class="vocabulary">**guesswork**</span> [ˈgeswɜ:k; 美 -wɜ:rk]
<span class="definition">n. <span class="vocabulary">**surmise**</span> [səˈmaɪz; 美 sərˈm-]
