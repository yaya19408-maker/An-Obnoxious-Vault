☀ <span class="category">**證據 證人：**</span>
<span class="vocabulary">**evidence**</span> ['evɪdəns] 
<span class="definition">n. [U] 法庭上的證據。是此義的常規用詞，極為常用：</span>prima facie evidence（法律用語）初步證據 / corroborative evidence 佐證 / to withhold crucial evidence 隱瞞關鍵證據 / circumstantial evidence 間接證據 / to give false evidence 作偽證 / to destroy evidence 銷燬證據 / The court was shown photographic evidence. 有人向法庭出示了影像證據。
           
<span class="vocabulary">**proof**</span> [pru:f]
<span class="definition">n. [U, C] ~ (of sth/that…) 法律上的證據：</span>There is no proof that the knife belonged to her. 沒有證據證明那把刀子是屬於她的。/ Do you have positive proof that she took the money? 你有確鑿證據證明她拿了那些錢嗎?/ I have no real proof that he was in the country at the time. 我沒有可靠的證據證明當時他在該國。/ The photo was final proof of her husband's infidelity. 這張照片最終證明了她丈夫的不忠。/ legal proof 法律證據 / He is unlikely to make wild accusations without proof. 沒有證據他不可能胡亂指控。/ Her account gives us no concrete proof of his guilt. 她的敘述並不能成為證明他有罪的確鑿證據。/ the burden of proof, the onus of proof 舉證責任 / The burden of proof lies on us to prove negligence. 證明對方犯有疏忽罪的舉證責任落在我們身上。/ The men were acquitted for lack of proof. 因為證據不足，這些人被宣告無罪。/ Civil proceedings require a lower standard of ~than criminal cases.民事訴訟比刑事案件的舉證標準低。
           
<span class="vocabulary">**testimony**</span> [ˈtestɪməni; 美 -moʊni]
<span class="definition">n. [C, U]（尤指在法庭上的）正式的書面或口頭證詞：</span>credible, reliable testimony 可信的證詞；可靠的證詞 / uncorroborated testimony 未經證實的證詞 / false, perjured testimony 偽證 / sworn testimony 宣誓證詞 / verbal, written testimony 口頭證詞；書面證詞 / video, videotaped testimony 影片證詞；錄影證詞 / eyewitness, witness, personal testimony 目擊者證詞；證人證詞；個人證詞 / expert testimony 專家證言 / court testimony 法庭證言 / a transcript of the trial testimony 庭審證言的文字記錄 / public testimony（美）公開做證 / congressional, grand jury testimony（均美） 國會做證；大陪審團做證 / Her testimony was corroborated by the other witnesses. 她的證言得到了其他證人的證實。/ The government was able to compel his testimony. 政府能夠迫使他做證。/ He had by his own testimony taken part in the burglary. 根據他本人的證言，他參與了這起入室盜竊案。/ In testimony before the Crown Court, she described her movements on the day of the murder. 在向刑事法庭提供的證言中，她講述了兇殺案案發當日她的行蹤。/ The witness was called to give oral testimony about the incident outside the theatre. 證人被傳喚就劇院外面發生的事件做證。/ The court heard her testimony against the accused. 法庭聽取了她指證被告的證言。/ his testimony before a Senate committee 他在一個參議員委員會所做的證言。/ Can I refuse to give testimony? 我能拒絕作證嗎？/ His testimony was an important element of the Prosecution case. 他的證詞是這宗公訴案件中的一個重要因素。/ Prosecutors may try to determine if Robb gave false testimony when he appeared before the grand jury. 檢方可能要努力查證羅布在大陪審團面前是否作了偽證。
                       
<span class="vocabulary">**exhibit**</span> [ɪgˈzɪbɪt]
<span class="definition">n. [C]（在法庭上出示的）物證，證據：</span>The first exhibit was a knife which the prosecution claimed was the murder weapon. 當庭出示的第一件物證就是控方稱為殺人兇器的一把刀。

<span class="vocabulary">**deposition**</span> [ˌdepəˈzɪʃn]
<span class="definition">n. [C] 法律上指未出庭的人（如罪案的目擊證人）所提供的一份正式的書面陳述，可用於法庭作為證人陳述，可稱為書面證詞：</span>The jury heard 200 pages of depositions. 陪審團聽取了長達 200 頁的書面證詞。

<span class="vocabulary">**case**</span> [keɪs] 
<span class="definition">n. [C, usually sing.] case (for/against sth) 在審判活動中用以支援一方的一系列事實或論據。case此時的含義比evidence要廣，evidence僅表示“證據”，但case包括了為說服法官和陪審團所提出的所有理由、事實、證據等：</span>prima facie case（法律用語）初步證據 / defence/defense, prosecution case 被告方理由；控方理由 / to prepare case 準備論據 / Our lawyer didn’t think we had a case (= a good enough case). 律師認為我們沒有充足的論據可以勝訴。/ I thought she argued her case very well. 我覺得她為自己辯護得很好。
           
<span class="vocabulary">**alibi**</span> [ˈæləbaɪ]
<span class="definition">n. [C] 不在犯罪現場的證明：</span>The suspects all had alibis for the day of the robbery. 嫌疑人均有證據證明搶劫當天不在犯罪現場。/ The accused was not able to provide an alibi for the evening. 被告不能提供當晚不在犯罪現場的證據。/ She was in the office all of Wednesday and so has a solid alibi. 她星期三—整天都在辦公室，所以有不在犯罪現場的確鑿證據。/ false alibi 不在犯罪現場的偽證 / They relied on witnesses to establish alibis for the accused. 他們依靠目擊者來證明被告不在犯罪現場。/ It was alleged that the accused had created an alibi to save himself. 據稱被告編造了不在犯罪現場的證據為自己開脫。

<span class="vocabulary">**safe**</span> [seɪf] 
<span class="definition">adj. 在法律上可指基於確鑿證據的：</span>a safe verdict 基於確鑿證據的判決

<span class="vocabulary">**witness**</span> ['wɪtnɪs] 
<span class="definition">n. 1 [C] 法庭上的證人：</span>reluctant, unwilling witness 不情願作證的證人 / hostile witness 敵意證人 / defence/defense, prosecution witness 辯方證人；控方證人 / expert witness 專家證人 / character witness 品格證人 / civilian, police（英）witness 平民證人；警方證人 / The defence called their first witness. 被告傳喚他們的第一個證人。/ witness box（英）, witness stand（美）證人席 / witness testimony（尤美）證人證詞 <span class="definition">2 [C]（也叫eyewitness）罪案的目擊者，見證人：</span>witnesses to the attack 此次襲擊的目擊者 / to appeal for witness（尤英）呼籲目擊者作證 / witness account, witness statement（尤英）目擊者描述 / According to witnesses, the thief escaped through the bedroom window. 根據目擊者的描述，小偷是從臥室的窗戶逃走的。/ a witness to murder 謀殺案目擊者 / silent witness（比喻用法）沉默的見證人 <span class="definition">3 [C] 你簽署了一份正式檔案後，在該檔案上進行簽字的另一個人，（該人的簽字旨在證明其見證了你的簽署）即連署人：</span>The document was signed and dated by you in the presence of two witnesses. 該檔案由你當著兩個連署人的面簽名並寫上日期。/ a witness at a wedding 婚禮的證婚人 / The marriage contract is signed in front of witnesses. 婚約是當著幾個見證人的面簽訂的。/ Would you be willing to act as a witness to my signature when I sign my will? 你願意做我簽署遺囑的見證人嗎？

<span class="vocabulary">**stand**</span> [stænd] 
<span class="definition">n. [C] 法庭上的證人席，相當於witness box：</span>He took the stand as the first witness. 他第一個出庭作證。
