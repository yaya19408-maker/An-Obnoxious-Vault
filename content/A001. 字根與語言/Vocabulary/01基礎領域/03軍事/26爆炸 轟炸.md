☀ <span class="category">**爆炸 轟炸：**</span>
<span class="vocabulary">**blast**</span> [blɑ:st; 美 blæst]
<span class="definition">n. [C] 爆炸，或爆炸引起的強大的空氣運動（即氣浪、衝擊波）：</span>grenade, nuclear, shotgun blast 手榴彈爆炸；核爆炸；獵槍開火 / A huge bomb blast rocked central London last night. 昨晚一次劇烈的炸彈爆炸震動了倫敦市中心。/ Officials say they do not know what caused the blast. 官員們稱他們不知道爆炸是什麼引起的。/ The blast occurred at about 9 a.m. 爆炸發生在上午9點左右。/ The blast blew a hole in the front of the building. 爆炸在建築物的正面炸出一個洞。/ Twenty people were killed in the blast. 二十人在爆炸中喪生。27 schoolchildren were injured in the blast. 27名學齡兒童在爆炸中受了傷。<span class="definition">vt. &vi. 用炸藥把…炸成碎片、炸燬、爆破：</span>All the windows were blasted inwards with the force of the explosion. 爆炸的震動力把所有窗戶都震碎到屋裡。/ The jumbo jet was blasted out of the sky. 那架巨型噴氣式飛機在空中被炸成碎片。/ There is a risk that toxic chemicals might be blasted into the atmosphere. 爆炸後有毒化學物質可能會進入大氣層。/ a terrible accident in which his left arm was blasted off by some kind of a bomb. 他的左臂被某種炸彈炸飛的可怕事故 / They blasted a huge crater in the runway. 他們在飛機跑道上炸了一個大坑。/ Their work was taken up with boring and blasting rock with gelignite. 他們的工作是用葛裡炸藥炸石頭，非常枯燥。/ They're using dynamite to blast away rocks to put a road in. 他們用炸藥炸石築路。/ Danger! Blasting in Progress! 危險！爆破進行中！

<span class="vocabulary">**burst**</span> [bə:st] 
<span class="definition">vt.＆vi. 爆炸。本義是指物理上爆裂或脹開、炸碎（此現象常由於內部的壓力而形成）。偶爾用於表示軍事上的爆炸：</span>Shells were bursting all around us. 炮彈在我們四周爆炸。

<span class="vocabulary">**arm**</span> [ɑːm] 
<span class="definition">vt. 使爆炸裝置（如炸彈、導彈等）處於準備爆炸的狀態，不太常用：</span>瞭解即可

<span class="vocabulary">**bomb**</span> [bɒm] 
<span class="definition">vt. 透過投擲（或放置）炸彈來發動襲擊，如果從飛機上投擲則是轟炸。bomb一般是人所發出的動作：</span>Terrorists bombed several army barracks. 恐怖分子炸了幾處兵營。/ The city was heavily bombed in the war. 這座城市在戰爭中遭到了猛烈轟炸。
            
<span class="vocabulary">**bombing**</span> [ˈbɒmɪŋ; 美 ˈbɑ:m-]
<span class="definition">n. [C, U] 炸彈的投擲或安放：</span>recent bombings in major cities 近期發生在大城市的投放炸彈事件 / enemy bombing 敵機轟炸 / aerial, roadside bombing 空中轟炸；路邊炸彈襲擊 / the aerial bombing of ports 對港口進行的空襲 / pinpoint, precision bombing 精確轟炸 / accidental bombing 意外轟炸 / heavy, massive bombing 猛烈轟炸；大規模轟炸 / carpet, indiscriminate, saturation bombing 地毯式轟炸；無差別轟炸；飽和轟炸 / strategic bombing 戰略轟炸 / terror, terrorist bombing 恐怖爆炸襲擊 / suicide bombing 自殺性爆炸袋擊 / bombing attack爆炸襲擊 / bombing campaign 轟炸戰 / bombing mission, bombing raid, bombing run, bombing sortie 轟炸任務；轟炸襲擊；轟炸飛行；轟炸出擊 / He was shot down during a bombing raid over the south. 他在一次對南部的轟炸襲擊中被擊落。/ bombing target 轟炸目標 / bombing victim 轟炸受害者

<span class="vocabulary">**bombard**</span> [bɒmˈbɑ:d; 美 bɑ:mˈbɑ:rd]
<span class="definition">vt. ~ sb/sth (with sth) 用大炮或炸彈來轟擊、轟炸一個地方：</span>Rebel artillery units have regularly bombarded the airport. 叛軍的炮兵部隊經常轟炸機場。/ The town has been heavily bombarded by the army backed by the airforce. 該鎮遭到了有空軍掩護的陸軍部隊的猛烈攻擊。
           
<span class="vocabulary">**bombardment**</span> [bɒm'bɑ:dmənt; 美bɒm'bɑdmənt]
<span class="definition">n. [U, C]（猛烈而持久的）炮擊或轟炸：</span>The city has been flattened by heavy artillery bombardments. 城市已被猛烈的炮火夷為平地。/ The capital is still under constant bombardment by the rebel forces. 首都仍在遭受叛軍的持續轟炸。/ The city came under heavy bombardment. 那座城市受到猛烈轟炸。/ intense, massive bombardment 密集的轟炸；大規模的轟炸 / aerial, air, artillery, naval bombardment 空中轟炸；炮擊；艦炮轟炸 / The city has been under constant bombardment for three days. 這座城市已連續三天受到持續的轟炸。/ They have withstood heavy bombardment for many months. 他們抵住了數月的猛烈轟炸。/ Many people were killed in the bombardment. 許多人死於那次轟炸。/ The army launched artillery bombardments against enemy positions. 部隊對敵軍陣地發動了炮擊。/ bombardment from the air 空中轟炸 / a three-hour bombardment with rockets and mortars 用火箭彈和迫擊炮進行的三個小時的轟炸

<span class="vocabulary">**explode**</span> [ɪk'spləʊd] 
<span class="definition">vt.＆vi. 爆炸，或使爆炸（即爆破），相當於blow up。explode一般是爆炸物本身所發出的動作（如其動作由人發出，則為“引爆”的意思）：</span>Bombs were exploding all around the city. 城裡到處都響起炸彈的爆炸聲。/ Bomb disposal experts exploded the device. 拆彈專家引爆了這個裝置。/ to explode prematurely 提前爆炸 / The bomb failed to explode. 炸彈沒有爆炸。
           
<span class="vocabulary">**explosion**</span> [ɪkˈspləʊʒn; 美 -ˈsploʊ-]
<span class="definition">n. 1 [C]（炸彈等的）爆炸：</span>There were two loud explosions and then the building burst into flames. 兩聲巨響之後建築物便燃燒起來。/ 300 people were injured in the explosion. 有300人在爆炸中受傷。/ minor, small explosion 輕微爆炸；小爆炸 / muffled explosion 沉悶的爆炸聲 / The floor shook with a distant explosion. 地板隨著遠處的爆炸而震顫。/ controlled explosion 受控的爆炸 / a nuclear test explosion 核試爆 / accidental explosion 意外的爆炸 / bomb, chemical, gas, mine， volcanic 炸彈爆炸；化學品爆炸；煤氣爆炸；礦井爆炸；火山爆發 / atomic, nuclear explosion 原子彈爆炸；核爆炸 / terrorist explosion 恐怖分子製造的爆炸 / The build-up of gas caused a small explosion. 煤氣的積聚導致了一次小爆炸。/ The explosion came 20 minutes after a coded warning to the police. 在警察接到密碼警告20分鐘後，爆炸發生了。/ The explosion occurred just after noon. 爆炸就發生在午後。/ A massive explosion erupted behind him. 他身後突然發生了大爆炸。/ A huge explosion rocked the entire building. 爆炸使整棟房子搖晃起來。 / The explosion shook nearby homes. 爆炸震顫了附近的房子。/ An explosion blew out the front windows. 爆炸炸燬了前窗。/ A massive explosion ripped through the chemical works. 巨大的爆炸摧毀了化工廠。/ The explosion caused major structural damage. 爆炸使主體結構遭到破壞。/ A loud explosion echoed around the valley. 巨大的爆炸聲在山谷中迴盪著。<span class="definition">2 [C, U] 爆破、引爆：</span>Bomb disposal experts carried out a controlled explosion on the suspect package.（carry out尤為英式用法）炸彈處理專家對可疑包裹實施了受控引爆。/ France has carried out an underground nuclear explosion on Mururoa Atoll in the South Pacific. 法國已經在南太平洋的穆魯羅瓦環礁進行了地下核爆。/ Bomb Squad officers carried out a controlled explosion of the device. 炸彈處理小組官員在嚴密控制下引爆了這一裝置。
           
<span class="vocabulary">**explosive**</span> [ɪkˈspləʊsɪv; -zɪv; 美 -ˈsploʊ-]
<span class="definition">adj. 易爆炸的；可能爆炸的：</span>The explosive device was timed to go off at the rush hour. 爆炸裝置被設定在高峰時間引爆。

<span class="vocabulary">**dynamite**</span> [ˈdaɪnəmaɪt]
<span class="definition">vt. 炸燬、爆破：</span>The rebels dynamited power lines. 叛亂分子炸燬了輸電線。/ The slum is to be dynamited. 貧民窟要被炸掉了。

<span class="vocabulary">**pound**</span> [paʊnd] 
<span class="definition">vt. 在一段時間內對一個地區狂轟濫炸，在詞義範圍上，pound隸屬於bomb，但pound更強調轟炸的猛烈性：</span>The area is still being pounded by rebel guns. 該地區仍然遭受著叛軍炮火的轟擊。

<span class="vocabulary">**blow**</span> [bləʊ] 
<span class="definition">vt. 用炸藥炸開：</span>The safe had been blown by the thieves. 保險櫃被竊賊炸開了。
           
<span class="vocabulary">**shell**</span> [ʃel]
<span class="definition">vt.&vi. 朝著…發起炮擊、炮轟：</span>They shelled the city all night. 他們整夜炮轟那座城市。/ The rebels shelled the densely-populated suburbs near the port. 叛亂者炮轟了港口附近人口密集的郊區。/ Just as they were leaving the rebels started shelling. 他們正撤離時，叛軍開始炮擊。

相關詞義延伸：
· blast還可指透過槍擊而衝出一條路，炸開一條道路、隧道等。

周邊詞彙補充：
· report可指爆炸聲。
