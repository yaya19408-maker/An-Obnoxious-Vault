☀ <span class="category">**相遇見面 會見覲見：**</span>
<span class="vocabulary">**meet**</span> [mi:t] 
<span class="definition">vt.＆vi. 1（偶然地）與某人相遇（並同其交談）：</span>Maybe we’ll meet again some time. 說不定我們什麼時候還會再見面的。/ He’s the kindest and sincerest person I’ve ever met. 他是我見過的最和善、最真誠的人。<span class="definition">2（經安排後與某人進行的）會面或集合（是一種社交活動）：</span>Let’s meet for a drink after work. 下班後我們一起去喝一杯吧。/ We’re meeting them outside the theatre at 7. 我們7點鐘在劇院外面和他們會合。<span class="definition">3 與某人初次相識，即結識；也可指被引見（或介紹）給某人：</span>nice to meet you（尤美）, pleased to meet you（尤英）很高興認識你 / There’s someone I want you to meet. 我想介紹你認識一個人。/ Hey, Terry, come and meet my Dad. 嗨，特里，過來見見我爸爸。/ the place where they had first met 他們初次見面的地方
            
<span class="vocabulary">**encounter**</span> [ɪnˈkaʊntə(r)]
<span class="definition">vt. [正式] 遇到某人或某事物，尤指偶然或意外地碰到新人或新事物：</span>She was the most remarkable woman he had ever encountered. 她是他所見到過的最出色的女性。/ Did you encounter anyone in the building? 你在大樓裡遇到誰了麼？/ Renata wrote him that she had encountered her long-estranged father. 雷娜塔寫信告訴他說她碰到了分離多年的父親。/ Walruses were commonly encountered in the Shetland Islands until quite recently. 以往在設得蘭群島遇到海象是很平常的事，最近情況有所不同了。<span class="definition">n. [C] ~ (with sb/sth) / ~ (between A and B)（意外、突然或暴力的）相遇、邂逅、遭遇：</span>The story describes the extraordinary encounter between a man and a dolphin. 這個故事描述了一個男人與一隻海豚之間的奇遇。/ I decided not to risk a second close encounter with the snakes. 我決定不再冒第二次遇到蛇的風險了。/ The author tells of a remarkable encounter with a group of South Vietnamese soldiers. 作者講述了和一群南越士兵的奇遇。/ examination of UFO sightings and alien encounters 對目睹不明飛行物和遭遇外星人事件的調查 / I've had a number of close encounters (= situations that could have been dangerous) with bad drivers. 我好幾次都險些與技術不佳的司機相撞。/ her early encounters with contemporary art 她早年和當代藝術的接觸 / I did not see him again, except for a brief encounter on a train. 除了有一次在火車上短暫相遇，我再也沒看見過他。/ It was a chance encounter that led to the setting up of the new political party. 是一次偶然的碰面導致了這個新政黨的建立。/ The press conference was her first direct encounter with the media. 這次新聞釋出會是她第一次直接面對媒體。/ the language we use in everyday social encounters 我們在日常社交中使用的語言。/ romantic encounter 浪漫的邂逅 / Few men survive an encounter with her! 很少有男人遇到她而不被迷倒！/ my first encounter with my new boss 我和新老闆的第一次見面         
 
<span class="vocabulary">**contact**</span> [ˈkɒntækt; 美 ˈkɑ:n-]
<span class="definition">n. [U] 遇見某人：</span>In her job she often comes into contact with (= meets) lawyers. 她在工作中常與律師接觸。/ In his job, he comes into contact with many different people. 他在工作中接觸到各種各樣的人。/ Doctors I came into contact with voiced their concern. 我接觸到的醫生都表示了他們的擔心。

<span class="vocabulary">**meeting**</span> ['mi:tɪŋ] 
<span class="definition">n. [C] 兩人或多人的（偶然或經過約定的）會面、見面，相當於encounter：</span>At our first meeting I was nervous. 我們第一次見面時我很緊張。/ I had a chance meeting with an old friend last week. 上週我邂逅了一位老朋友。
           
<span class="vocabulary">**rendezvous**</span> [ˈrɒndɪvu:; -deɪ-; 美 ˈrɑ:n-]（來自法語）
<span class="definition">n. 1 [C] ~ (with sb)（常指秘密的）約會，會面，會合：</span>secret rendezvous 秘密會面 / romantic rendezvous 戀人約會 / midnight rendezvous 午夜會面/ little rendezvous 短暫的會面 / Although it was late, there was still enough time to keep the rendezvous. 雖然很晚了，但還有足夠的時間去赴這個約會。/ I had almost decided to keep my rendezvous with Tony. 我差不多已經決定如約和託尼見面了。/ She made the rendezvous with only minutes to spare. 她還差幾分鐘時才來赴約。/ The platoon made its way to the prearranged rendezvous point in the desert. 這個排向預先部署好的沙漠中的會合點進發。/ I have a rendezvous with Peter at a restaurant. 我約了彼得在一家餐館見面。<span class="definition">2 [C] 約定的見面地點：</span>Their rendezvous would be the Penta Hotel at Heathrow Airport. 他們將在希思羅機場的五角飯店碰面。<span class="definition">3 [C]（酒吧等）熱門約見場所：</span>a lively Paris rendezvous 巴黎一個很熱鬧的公共場所 <span class="definition">vi. ~ (with sb)（在約定的時間和地點）會面，相會：</span>The plan was to rendezvous with him on Sunday afternoon. 計劃在週日下午和他會面。/ She wondered where they were going to rendezvous afterwards. 她想知道之後他們要去哪裡約會。

<span class="vocabulary">**interview**</span> ['ɪntəvju:] 
<span class="definition">n. [C] interview (with sb) 私下的問答式的會談，面談：</span>an interview with the careers adviser 和職業顧問的面談 / an interview with the British leader 與英國領導人的會談 / a police interview with suspected terrorists 警方與恐怖分子嫌疑人的面談 <span class="definition">vt. interview sb (about sth) 私下裡向某人提問或與其面談：</span>The police are waiting to interview the injured man. 警察正等著向受傷的男子問話。

<span class="vocabulary">**audience**</span> ['ɔ:dɪəns] 
<span class="definition">n. [C] 與一位重要人士的一次正式會見，覲見：</span>an audience with the Pope 覲見教皇 / He sought a private audience with the emperor. 他尋求單獨覲見皇帝。

<span class="vocabulary">**visit**</span> ['vɪzɪt] 
<span class="definition">n. [C] [美，非正式] visit (with sb)（兩人或多人非正式的）碰面並交談：</span>瞭解即可

<span class="vocabulary">**see**</span> [si:] 
<span class="definition">vt. see sb (about sth) 與某人會見，會晤。是一個簡單用詞，日常口語中較常用：</span>You ought to see a doctor about that cough. 你得找個大夫看看你的咳嗽。/ What is it you want to see me about? 你找我有什麼事？/ Mick wants to see you in his office right away. 米克要你馬上去他的辦公室見他。/ I’m here to see Tom. 我是來見湯姆的。

<span class="vocabulary">**see**</span> [si:] 
<span class="definition">vt. 近距離認出某人，或偶然遇到某人：</span>Guess who I saw at the party last night! 你猜猜，昨天我在晚會上碰見誰了！/ We used to see quite a lot of his wife, Carolyn. 我們過去經常見到他的妻子卡羅琳。
