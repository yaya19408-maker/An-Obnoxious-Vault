☀ <span class="category">**功能、作用的名詞：**</span>
<span class="vocabulary">**function**</span> ['fʌŋkʃn] 
<span class="definition">n. [C, U] 作用，功能，機能，職能。是“功能”的常規用詞：</span>form and function 形式和功能 / The committee has a dual function, both advisory and regulatory. 這個委員會具有諮詢和監管雙重職能。/ All members carry out their own particular functions. 所有成員都有各自的職責要履行。/ The club serves a useful function as a meeting place. 這個俱樂部是一個會面的好地方。

<span class="vocabulary">**role**</span> [rəʊl] 
<span class="definition">n. [C] 在一種情況或一項活動中，某人或某事物所捲入的程度（即在多大程度上捲入了該情況或活動）；也可指該人或事物對該情況或活動所造成的影響：</span>the role of diet in preventing disease 飲食在預防疾病中的作用 / The media play a major role in influencing people’s opinions. 媒體在影響民意方面發揮著重要作用。

<span class="vocabulary">**play**</span> [pleɪ] 
<span class="definition">n. [U] 活動；作用；影響。是一個簡單用詞：</span>the free play of market forces 市場力量的自由調節作用 / The real existence of a military option will come into play. 現有的軍事手段將開始起作用。/ Personal feelings should not come into play when you are making business decisions. 為公事做決策時不應讓個人情感介入。/ This event will bring new factors into play. 本次事件將引發新的變數。

<span class="vocabulary">**action**</span> ['ækʃn] 
<span class="definition">n. [sing.] 某個組織機構的功能、作用：</span>A fire has put the factory out of action. 一場火災使工廠陷入癱瘓。

<span class="vocabulary">**job**</span> [dӡɒb] 
<span class="definition">n. [C] 起到的作用：</span>Try wedging it open--that should do the job. 找個楔子把它撐開——應該管用。
           
<span class="vocabulary">**interaction**</span> [ˌɪntərˈækʃn]
<span class="definition">n. [U, C]（各物質間的）相互作用：</span>the interaction of bacteria with the body's natural chemistry 細菌和身體的天然化學變化的相互作用 / the complex interaction between animals and their environment 動物與生存環境之間複雜的相互影響

相關詞義延伸：
· action還可指（鋼琴、槍炮、鐘錶等）各機械部件的活動方式。也可指一種物質（或化學品）對另一種物質（或化學品）所起的作用。

☀ <span class="category">**功能、作用的動詞：**</span>
<span class="vocabulary">**act**</span> [ækt] 
<span class="definition">vi. act as/like sth 充當某一角色，起作用：</span>Can you act as interpreter? 你能擔任口譯員嗎？/ hormones in the brain that act like natural painkillers 大腦中起著天然止痛藥作用的激素 

<span class="vocabulary">**play**</span> [pleɪ] 
<span class="definition">vt. play a part/role (in sth) 起作用，發揮作用：</span>to play a major role in monitoring market 在監督市場方面起主要作用

<span class="vocabulary">**function**</span> ['fʌŋkʃn] 
<span class="definition">vi.（作為某物而）起作用：</span>This room will function as a office. 這間屋子將作為一間辦公室。 
           
<span class="vocabulary">**fulfil**</span> [fʊlˈfɪl]
<span class="definition">vt. 起…作用：</span>Nursery schools should fulfil the function of preparing children for school. 幼兒園應該起到為兒童進小學作準備的作用。/ The building is still fulfilling its original purpose admirably. 這幢建築物仍在發揮其最初的功用，真了不起。

<span class="vocabulary">**operate**</span> ['ɒpəreɪt] 
<span class="definition">vi.（某事物）起作用：</span>Wall lights can operate independently. 牆上的燈能夠獨立操作。

<span class="vocabulary">**perform**</span> [pə'fɔ:m] 
<span class="definition">vt. 事物行使某種功能：</span>Each part performs a different function. 每一部分各自發揮不同的功能。
           
<span class="vocabulary">**assert**</span> [əˈsɜ:t; 美 əˈsɜ:rt]
<span class="definition">vt. ~ itself 生效、開始起作用：</span>Good sense asserted itself. 明智服人。
           
<span class="vocabulary">**interact**</span> [ˌɪntərˈækt]
<span class="definition">vi. ~ (with sb)（兩個事物）相互影響、相互作用：</span>Perfume interacts with the skin's natural chemicals. 香水和皮膚的天然化學物質相互作用。/ You have to understand how cells interact. 你必須明白細胞是怎樣相互作用的。/ This hormone interacts closely with other hormones in the body. 這種荷爾蒙和體內的其他荷爾蒙有著密切的相互作用。/ Atoms within the fluid interact with the minerals that form the grains. 液體中的原子與形成顆粒的礦物質相互作用。

相關詞義延伸：
· play還可指在運動隊中擔當或充任某一角色。

周邊詞彙補充：
· take可指起作用，行得通，成功。

☀ <span class="category">**功能、作用的其它詞性：**</span>
<span class="vocabulary">**active**</span> ['æktɪv] 
<span class="definition">adj. 定期進行某項活動的；起作用的：</span>sexually active dogs 性慾旺盛的狗 / animals that are active only at night 僅在夜間活動的動物 / The virus is still active in the blood. 這種病毒仍然在血液中起作用。/ an active volcano 活火山

<span class="vocabulary">**here**</span> [hɪə] 
<span class="definition">adv. here to do sth 用於表示某人在某種情況中的角色或作用：</span>I’m here to help you. 我是來幫你的。

<span class="vocabulary">**there**</span> [ðeə] 
<span class="definition">adv. there to do sth 表示一個人或事物在某一情況中的作用或角色：</span>The fact is, they’re there to make money. 真實的情況是，他們去那兒是為了賺錢。

<span class="vocabulary">**for**</span> [fɔ:] 
<span class="definition">prep. 用於表示功能：</span>a machine for slicing bread 切麵包片機

相關詞義延伸：
· active還可指有化學作用的、產生化學作用的、（化學上）有效的。

☀ <span class="category">**影響的動詞：**</span>
<span class="vocabulary">**influence**</span> ['ɪnflʊəns] 
<span class="definition">vt. 1（尤指透過提供一個可供追隨或模仿的範例而）對某人的行為方式或思維方式產生影響：</span>His writings have influenced the lives of millions. 他的作品影響了千百萬人。/ The wording of questions can influence how people answer. 問題的措辭會影響人們的回答。/ He was naive and easily influenced by his friends. 他很幼稚，容易受朋友影響。<span class="definition">2 對某種形勢及該形勢的發展方式產生影響：</span>A number of social factors influence life expectancy. 諸多社會因素左右著人的預期壽命。/ Pressure from industry bosses has directly influenced government policy. 來自實業界老闆們的壓力直接影響了政府政策。
          
<span class="vocabulary">**impact**</span> [ˈɪmpækt]
<span class="definition">vt.&vi. ~ (on/upon) sth 對某事物有影響、有作用：</span>The company's performance was impacted by the high value of the pound. 公司的業績受到了英鎊高值的衝擊。/ to impact on inflation and competition 對通貨膨脹和競爭產生影響 / Such schemes mean little unless they impact on people. 這些計劃沒什麼意義，除非它們對人民產生影響。/ Her father's death impacted greatly on her childhood years. 父親去世對她的童年造成巨大影響。
                      
<span class="vocabulary">**impinge**</span> [ɪmˈpɪndʒ]
<span class="definition">vi. [正式] ~ (on/upon sth/sb) 對…有明顯作用（或影響），尤指有壞的作用（或影響）：</span>measures which directly or indirectly impinge upon women' lives 直接或間接影響婦女生活的措施 / the cuts in defence spending that have impinged on two of the region's largest employers 已經衝擊到該地區兩個最大就業製造者的防禦開支削減 / The music industry rarely impinges on my life. 音樂產業極少影響到我的生活。

<span class="vocabulary">**infect**</span> [ɪnˈfekt]
<span class="definition">vt. 影響：</span>He thought they might infect others with their bourgeois ideas. 他認為他們可以用他們的資產階級思想去影響別人。

<span class="vocabulary">**affect**</span> [ə'fekt] 
<span class="definition">vt. 常用被動式，在某人或某事物中產生一個變化，即影響：</span>How will these changes affect us? 這些變化對我們會有什麼影響？/ Your opinion will not affect my decision. 你的意見不會影響我的決定。/ Sales did not seem unduly affected. 銷售似乎並未受到過分的影響。/ developments that are likely to affect the environment 一些很有可能影響環境的開發 / The south of the country was worst affected by the drought. 該國南方旱情最嚴重。

<span class="vocabulary">**shape**</span> [ʃeɪp] 
<span class="definition">vt. 對…的發展（或產生）歷程產生重要影響：</span>His ideas had been shaped by his experiences during the war. 他的思想深受戰時經歷的影響。/ Memory can be profoundly shaped by subsequent experience. 記憶會受到後來經歷的深刻影響。/ The media had great influence in shaping public opinion. 媒體對公眾意見的影響力很大。/ Money played a major role in shaping his decision. 他做這個決定，錢起了重要作用。/ the role that faith has played in shaping American society 信仰在塑造美國社會中所起的作用
           
<span class="vocabulary">**mould**</span> [məʊld; 美 moʊld]
<span class="definition">vt. ~ sb/sth (into sb/sth) 對某人性格、觀點等的形成產生強烈影響：</span>The experience had moulded and coloured her whole life. 這次經歷影響了她的一生。/ She was only 17 at the time and the experience moulded her personality. 那時她只有17歲，那次經歷影響了她的性格。/ He moulded them into a superb team. 他將他們塑造成一支非凡的隊伍。/ Here we outline some of the sometimes conflicting forces moulding the debate. 在此我們概述一些有時影響爭論的相互對立的力量。

<span class="vocabulary">**form**</span> [fɔ:m] 
<span class="definition">vt. 對…的發展有影響：</span>Positive and negative experiences form a child’s character. 正反兩方面的經歷都影響兒童性格的形成。/ Anger at injustice formed his character. 對不公正現象的憤怒塑造了他的性格。

<span class="vocabulary">**inform**</span> [ɪn'fɔ:m] 
<span class="definition">vt. [正式] 對某事有影響：</span>Religion informs every aspect of their lives. 宗教影響著他們生活的各個方面。

<span class="vocabulary">**overcome**</span> [͵əʊvə'kʌm] 
<span class="definition">vt. 常用被動式，受到某事物的極大（或強烈）影響，相當於overwhelm：</span>Her parents were overcome with grief at the funeral. 在葬禮上她的父母悲痛欲絕。/ She was quite overcome by their kindness. 她因他們的熱情而感到手足無措。/ She was almost overcome by a tide of relief. 強烈的解脫感使她幾乎不能自持。/ Several firefighters had been overcome by smoke and fumes. 有幾名消防員被煙氣燻倒了。/ He was suddenly overcome with remorse for the harm he had done. 他猛然對由自己所造成的傷害感到懊悔不已。
           
<span class="vocabulary">**mediate**</span> [ˈmi:dieɪt] 
<span class="definition">vt. [正式，或術語] [常用被動式] 影響…的發生；使…可能發生：</span>Educational success is mediated by economic factors. 經濟因素影響著教育的成功。/ People's responses to us have been mediated by their past experience of life. 人們對我們作出的反應受到了他們過去生活經歷的影響。

<span class="vocabulary">**act**</span> [ækt] 
<span class="definition">vi. act (on sth) 對…產生作用或影響：</span>Alcohol acts quickly on the brain. 酒精對大腦迅速產生影響。

<span class="vocabulary">**weigh**</span> [weɪ] 
<span class="definition">vi. weigh (with sb) (against sb/sth) 對某人的看法或某事的結果有影響：</span>His past record weighs heavily against him. 他過去的記錄對他很不利。/ This fact weighed heavily in her favour/favor. 這個事實對她頗為有利。/ His evidence weighed strongly with the judge. 法官認為他的證據很有分量。

<span class="vocabulary">**decide**</span> [dɪ'saɪd] 
<span class="definition">vt.＆vi. 影響（或決定）某事的結果：</span>A mixture of skill and good luck decided the outcome of the game. 技術和運氣結合在一起決定了比賽的結果。/ A number of factors decide whether a movie will be successful or not. 一部電影成功與否是由許多因素決定的。

<span class="vocabulary">**colour**</span> ['kʌlə]（美color）
<span class="definition">vt. 影響某事物（尤指負面地影響）：</span>This incident coloured her whole life. 這事件影響了她的一生。/ Don’t let your judgement be coloured by personal feelings. 不要讓你的判斷受到個人感情的影響。/ His opinions are heavily coloured by his own experiences. 他的觀點深受個人經歷的影響。

<span class="vocabulary">**condition**</span> [kən'dɪʃn] 
<span class="definition">vt. 對某人或某事物有一個重要影響；影響（某事物的發生方式）：</span>Gender roles are often conditioned by cultural factors. 文化因素常常對性別的角色有著重要的影響。

<span class="vocabulary">**tell**</span> [tel] 
<span class="definition">vi. tell (on sb) 對…產生影響或效果（尤指負面影響或效果）。不太常用：</span>The strain was beginning to tell on the rescue team. 過度的疲勞開始讓救援隊吃不消了。
           
<span class="vocabulary">**grip**</span> [grɪp] 
<span class="definition">vt. 一種情緒或形勢對某人或某事物產生強烈影響：</span>I was gripped by a feeling of panic. 我驚恐萬狀。/ Terrorism has gripped the country for the past two years. 兩年來恐怖主義一直籠罩著這個國家。
           
<span class="vocabulary">**infuse**</span> [ɪnˈfju:z]
<span class="definition">vt. [正式] 對某事物的各部分產生影響，即全面影響：</span>Politics infuses all aspects of our lives. 政治影響著我們生活的各個方面。
           
<span class="vocabulary">**skew**</span> [skju:]
<span class="definition">vt. 改變或影響某事，使其結果不準確、不公正、不正常等：</span>to skew the statistics 影響統計數字的準確性 / The arithmetic of nuclear running costs has been skewed by the fall in the cost of other fuels. 核運營成本的計算由於其他燃料的降價而出現了偏差。/ Today's election will skew the results in favor of the northern end of the county. 今天的選舉將使結果有利於這個郡的北部地區。

相關詞義延伸：
· affect還可指（一種疾病）侵襲某人或身體的某個部位，使某人生病。

周邊詞彙補充：
· bite可指（行為、行動或政策等）產生嚴重或不良影響；
· touch可指（短暫地）影響到…。

☀ <span class="category">**影響的名詞：**</span>
<span class="vocabulary">**influence**</span> ['ɪnflʊəns] 
<span class="definition">n. 1 [U, C] influence (on/upon sb/sth) 某人或某事物對一個人的思維方式、行為方式（或對某事物的執行方式、發展方式）所產生的影響或作用。是一個常規用詞，極為常用：</span>He had a profound influence on modern poets. 他對現代詩人產生了深遠的影響。/ the influence of the climate on agricultural production 氣候對農業生產的影響 / the effects of peer influence on crime 同輩影響在犯罪中的作用 <span class="definition">2 [U] influence (over sb/sth) 某人或某事物對其它人或事物的行為方式所具有的影響力、支配力：</span>Her parents no longer have any real influence over her. 她的父母對她不再有任何真正的影響力了。/ He committed the crime under the influence of drugs. 他是在吸毒後犯罪的。/ The unions have been able to extend their influence over all industries. 工會已將其影響力擴大到所有行業。/ The Internet’s influence is growing every day. 網際網路的影響力日益增強。<span class="definition">3 [C] influence (on sb/sth) 對一個人的行為方式或思維方式具有影響力的一個人或事物：</span>His first music teacher was a major influence in his life. 他的第一位音樂老師是他一生中對他影響非常大的人。/ He is a writer who draws upon diverse cultural influences. 他是一位在寫作中汲取多種文化養料的作家。

<span class="vocabulary">**impact**</span> [ˈɪmpækt]
<span class="definition">n. [C, usually sing, U] ~ (of sth) (on sb/sth) 對某人或某事物產生的巨大影響：</span>cultural, ecological, economic, emotional, financial, health, political, psychological, social impact 文化影響；生態影響；經濟影響；情感影響；財務影響；健康影響；政治影響；心理影響；社會影響 / Businesses are beginning to feel the full impact of the recession. 工商企業開始感受到了經濟衰退的全面衝擊。/ the impact of the debt crisis on developing countries 本次債務危機對發展中國家的影響 / Variations in the interest rate will have an impact on the whole housing market. 利率的變動將對整個房地產市場產生影響。/ the initial impact of the reforms 改革的最初影響 / the likely impact of the changes on employment patterns 這些變化對僱傭模式可能產生的影響 / to minimize the impact of price rises on our customers 努力使漲價對我們的顧客產生的影響降至最低 / to resist the impact of poverty on the health of their children 避免貧困對孩子的健康產生影響 / They say they expect the meeting to have a marked impact on the future of the country. 他們稱他們預期會議對國家的未來會產生顯著影響。/ the wider impact of this proposal 這份提議的更廣泛的影響 / the personal impact of party leaders 政黨領袖的個人影響力 / the environmental impact of tourism 旅遊事業對環境的巨大影響 / the impact of AIDS on the gay community 艾滋病對同性戀群體的影響 / the major impact of this epidemic 這種傳染病的重大影響 / Architecturally, these churches reflected the impact of the Renaissance. 從建築風格來看，這些教堂受到了文藝復興的影響。/ The railways made a direct physical impact on the landscape. 鐵路對地貌產生直接影響。/ We'll show you how to dress for maximum impact at the all-important audition. 這次試演關係重大，我們會教你如何著裝才能取得最佳效果。/ the cumulative impact of a series of damaging events 一系列危害事件所產生的累積影響 / Her speech made a profound impact on everyone. 她的講話對每個人都有深遠的影響。/ You certainly made a big impact on Carter. 你確實對卡特產生了很大影響。/ the human impact on the dolphins 人類對海豚的影響 / physical, visual impact 生理影響；視覺影響 / potential impact 潛在的影響 / decisive impact 決定性的影響 / positive, negative impact 積極的影響；消極的影響
           
<span class="vocabulary">**repercussion**</span> [ˌri:pəˈkʌʃn; 美 -pərˈk-]
<span class="definition">n. [usually pl.] 事件的間接結果或影響，且通常為壞的結果或影響：</span>economic, political, social repercussion 經濟影響；政治後果；社會影響 / legal, financial repercussion 法律後果；財務後果 / A fall in oil production would have severe repercussions for the global economy. 石油產量下降會對全球經濟產生嚴重影響。/ The collapse of the company will have repercussions for the whole industry. 這家公司的垮臺將會給整個行業造成間接的負面影響。/ Changes in the industry had major repercussions for the local community. 該行業的變革對當地社群產生了重大影響。/ The pay cuts are likely to have serious repercussions on productivity. 削減工資可能會嚴重影響生產力。/ Members of congress were warned of possible repercussions if their vote went through. 國會議員被警告如果他們的投票透過可能會帶來不良影響。/ It was an effort which was to have painful repercussions. 這麼做會帶來極壞的負面影響。/ He felt the repercussions of the earlier incident. 他感受到了早先事件的影響。/ a resident who did not want to be named for fear of repercussions 一個因擔心出現不良後果而不願透露自己名字的居民
           
<span class="vocabulary">**implication**</span> [ˌɪmplɪˈkeɪʃn]
<span class="definition">n. [C, usually pl.] ~ (for/of sth)（一個行動或決策的）可能的影響（或作用）：</span>The development of the site will have implications for the surrounding countryside. 這個地點的開發將會影響周圍的鄉村。/ They failed to consider the wider implications of their actions. 他們沒有考慮到他們的行動會產生更廣泛的影響。/ The low level of current investment has serious implications for future economic growth. 當前低迷的投資水平會對未來的經濟增長產生嚴重影響。/ The broader implications of the plan were discussed. 對計劃更為廣泛的影響進行了討論。/ Now they realized the full implications of the new system. 他們現在認識到了新體制帶來的全面影響。/ negative implication 負面影響 / These results have important practical implications. 這些結果具有重大的實際意義。/ commercial, economic, financial implication 商業影響；經濟影響；金融影響 / environmental implication 環境影響 / educational, psychological, social, societal implication 教育影響；心理影響；社會影響 / political implication 政治影響 / the constitutional implications of a royal divorce 王室離婚事件對憲法的影響 / ethical, ideological, metaphysical, moral, philosophical, theological implication 倫理影響；意識形態影響；形而上學意義；道德影響；哲學影響；神學意義 / cost, health, policy, resource（尤英）, safety, security, tax implication 成本影響；健康影響；政策影響；資源影響；安全影響；治安影響；稅收影響 / Her article attempts to draw out the implications of this argument. 她的文章試圖揭示這一論據的意義。/ Their work addresses the implications of new technology. 他們的工作是探討新技術的意義。/ Given the resource implications involved, the plan will have to be scaled down. 考慮到對資源的影響，計劃規模不得不縮小。/ The research has far-reaching implications for medicine as a whole. 這項研究對整個醫學界都有深遠影響。

<span class="vocabulary">**hand**</span> [hænd] 
<span class="definition">n. <span class="vocabulary">**clout**</span> [klaʊt]

<span class="vocabulary">**leverage**</span> [ˈli:vərɪdʒ; 美 ˈlev-]
<span class="definition">n. ☀ <span class="category">**影響的其它詞性：**</span>
<span class="vocabulary">**influential**</span> [ˌɪnfluˈenʃl]
<span class="definition">adj. ~ (in sth/in doing sth) 很有影響力的：</span>a highly influential book 十分有影響力的書 / She is one of the most influential figures in local politics. 她是本地政壇舉足輕重的人物。/ It helps to have influential friends. 有幾個位高權重的朋友很有好處。/ the influential position of president of the chamber 很有影響力的議院議長之位 / The committee was influential in formulating government policy on employment. 委員會左右著政府就業政策的制定。/ As a writer she was hugely influential. 作為—名作家，她有巨大的影響力。/ The group was influential in setting up the new schools. 該團體對這些新學校的設立頗有影響。

<span class="vocabulary">**subject**</span> ['sʌbdӡɪkt] 
<span class="definition">adj. subject to sth 很可能受…影響的（尤指很可能受壞事影響的）：</span>Prices of food may be subject to the drought. 食物價格很可能受乾旱氣候的影響。/ His illness left him subject to asthma attacks. 他的病使他容易哮喘發作。/ At this stage these proposals are still subject to change. 在這個階段，這些提議仍然可以更改。
                      
<span class="vocabulary">**susceptible**</span> [səˈseptəbl]
<span class="definition">adj. [名詞前不常用] ~ (to sb/sth) 極易受影響的：</span>He's highly susceptible to flattery. 他愛聽恭維話。/ Young people are the most susceptible to advertisements. 年輕人最容易受廣告的影響。/ James was extremely susceptible to flattery. 詹姆斯聽幾句奉承話就忘乎所以。

<span class="vocabulary">**pliable**</span> [ˈplaɪəbl]
<span class="definition">adj.（人）易受影響的或容易受控制的：</span>If we go on expecting our daughters to be decorative and pliable and empty-headed, they'll be inadequately prepared for the future. 如果我們繼續期望自己的女兒只重外表、毫無主見、頭腦空空的話，她們就會對未來準備不足。

<span class="vocabulary">**malleable**</span> [ˈmæliəbl]
<span class="definition">adj.（人、思想等）易受影響或易被改變的，即有可塑性的：</span>The malleable mayor of New York was under his control. 這位聽話的紐約市市長聽命於他。/ She was young enough to be malleable. 她還年輕，還很乖。
           
<span class="vocabulary">**impressionable**</span> [ɪmˈpreʃənəbl]
<span class="definition">adj. 人（通常指年輕人）易受影響的、無主見的、無判斷力的：</span>children at an impressionable age 處於易受外界影響的年齡的兒童 / seven years old, which is apparently the age at which you are most impressionable 七歲，明顯是一個最易受人影響的年齡 / The law is intended to protect young and impressionable viewers. 該法律意圖保護那些年輕且無判斷力的電視觀眾。/ He is in a highly impressionable state. 他正處於極易受影響的狀態。
          
<span class="vocabulary">**inspired**</span> [ɪnˈspaɪəd; 美 ɪnˈspaɪərd]
<span class="definition">adj. -inspired 與名詞、形容詞以及副詞構成形容詞，指受…影響的：</span>politically-inspired killings 政治性的謀殺事件

<span class="vocabulary">**big**</span> [bɪɡ] 
<span class="definition">adj. [非正式]（一個人在某個組織內、活動中或地方）有影響力的；或有權威的：</span>Their father was very big in the army. 他們的父親在軍界很有威望。

<span class="vocabulary">**under**</span> ['ʌndə] 
<span class="definition">prep. 受…影響：</span>The wall collapsed under the strain. 牆壁因承受不了重壓而坍塌了。/ I’ve been feeling under stress lately. 我最近感到壓力很大。/ I’m under no illusions about his promise. 對於他的承諾，我從不存在任何幻想。/ You’ll be under anaesthetic, so you won’t feel a thing. 你將被麻醉，所以什麼也感覺不到。
