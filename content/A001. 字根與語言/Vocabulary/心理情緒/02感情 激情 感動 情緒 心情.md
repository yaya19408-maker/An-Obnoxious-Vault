☀ <span class="category">**感情：**</span>
<span class="vocabulary">**feeling**</span> ['fi:lɪŋ] 
<span class="definition">n. [pl.] feelings可指一個人的情感或感情（而非其思想或想法）。是此義的常規用詞：</span>personal feelings 個人情感 / human feelings 人類的情感 / I didn’t mean to hurt your feelings. 我不是故意傷害你的感情。/ They begin to develop feelings for one another. 他們彼此之間開始有了好感。/ I kept my feelings to myself. 我把自己的情感埋在心底。/ I fought back my feelings of jealousy. 我剋制住了我的嫉妒。

<span class="vocabulary">**expression**</span> [ɪk'spreʃn] 
<span class="definition">n. [U]（演奏音樂、講話、進行表演等活動時所強烈展現出的一種）感情：</span>Try to put a little more expression into it! 儘量注入更多的感情！ 

<span class="vocabulary">**soul**</span> [səʊl] 
<span class="definition">n. [U, C] 人類強烈且美好的感情（尤指灌注於藝術作品而使其具有靈氣的真摯情感或高尚情操、氣魄等）：</span>It was a very polished performance, but it lacked soul. 這場演出技藝很精湛，但缺少真摯的情感。/ She sang the song with passion and soul. 她充滿激情地唱這首歌。

<span class="vocabulary">**being**</span> ['bi:ɪŋ] 
<span class="definition">n. [U] [正式] 一個人的思想和全部感情，即身心：</span>I love you with my whole being. 我從心底裡愛著你。

☀ <span class="category">**強烈的感情、激情：**</span>
<span class="vocabulary">**passion**</span> [ˈpæʃn]
<span class="definition">n.  [C, U] 非常強烈的愛、恨、憤怒、熱情等感情，如激情：</span>He's a man of violent passions. 他是個性情暴烈的人。/ a crime of passion 因情慾妒忌而造成的犯罪 / She killed her husband's lover in a crime of passion. 她殺死了丈夫的情人，構成激情犯罪。/ He argued his case with great passion. 他很激動地據理力爭。/ Passions were running high (= people were angry and emotional) at the meeting. 會上群情沸騰。/ He spoke with great passion. 他發表了熱情洋溢的講話。/ a speech full of passion 熱情洋溢的演講 / the passion and commitment of the Republican candidate 共和黨候選人的激情和奉獻精神 / This issue always rouses passion. 這個話題總能喚起大家的熱情。/ That passion drove me to get to the top. 那種激情推動著我到達頂峰。/ There were moments of real passion in the game. 這場比賽有幾次令觀眾情緒激昂。/ The team has been playing with renewed passion this season. 這個賽季該隊在比賽中重新燃起了鬥志。
           
<span class="vocabulary">**emotion**</span> [ɪˈməʊʃn; 美 ɪˈmoʊʃn]
<span class="definition">n. [C, U] 強烈的感情（例如愛、恐懼或憤怒）：</span>Emotions are running high (= people are feeling very excited, angry, etc.). 群情激奮。/ Mary was overcome with emotion. 瑪麗激動得不能自持。/ Her voice trembled with emotion. 她的聲音因情緒激動而顫抖。/ Her voice was choked with emotion. 她因感情激動而聲音哽咽。/ She realized she was shaking all over with emotion. 她意識到自己激動得渾身顫抖起來。/ an incident that has aroused strong emotions locally 激起當地人強烈感情的一個事件 / a speech that was charged with emotion 充滿激情的演講 

<span class="vocabulary">**feeling**</span> ['fi:lɪŋ] 
<span class="definition">n. [U, C] 強烈的情緒，如激動或憤怒（也可用feelings，尤其在美式英語中更是如此）：</span>She spoke with feeling about her sufferings. 她激動地講述了她的遭遇。/ Feelings are running high. 群情激奮。/ to stir up feeling against the war 激起反戰情緒 / bad feeling 敵意

<span class="vocabulary">**heat**</span> [hi:t] 
<span class="definition">n. [U] 強烈的感情，尤指憤怒或激動：</span>‘No, I won’t,’ he said with heat in his voice.“不，我絕不。”他怒氣衝衝地說。/ He tried to take the heat out of the situation. 他盡力平息人們的激奮情緒。/ In the heat of the moment she forgot what she wanted to say. 她因為過於激動而忘記了要說的話。/ in the heat of the argument 在激烈爭論的時候 / Heat flooded his cheeks. 他激動得兩頰通紅。

<span class="vocabulary">**flame**</span> [fleɪm] 
<span class="definition">n. [C] [文學] 強烈的感情；激情：</span>His childhood interest in the game had ignited a flame of passion for football. 他童年時對足球的興趣點燃了他對這項運動的激情。/ to rekindle the flames of romance 重燃愛火 / a flame of anger 一團怒火

<span class="vocabulary">**fire**</span> ['faɪə] 
<span class="definition">n. [U] 非常強烈的感情，尤指憤怒和熱情（enthusiasm）：</span>Her eyes were full of fire. 她的雙眼充滿憤怒的火花。

<span class="vocabulary">**storm**</span> [stɔ:m] 
<span class="definition">n. [C] storm (of sth) 很多人突然就某事表達其強烈感情，即群情迸發：</span>to cause a storm 引起強烈反響 / a gathering storm of discontent 正在積聚的不滿情緒 / media storm 媒體風暴 / The political storm had blown over at last. 這場政治風暴終於平息了。/ amid a storm of controversy 在強烈的爭議浪潮中 / a storm of protest 一陣抗議浪潮

<span class="vocabulary">**transport**</span> [træns'pɔ:t] 
<span class="definition">n. [pl.] [文學] transports of sth 強烈的感情或情感：</span>to be in transport of delight 興高采烈
           
<span class="vocabulary">**spark**</span> [spɑ:k; 美 spɑ:rk]
<span class="definition">n. 1 [U, sing.]（使某人非常聰明、幽默的）激情：</span>As a writer he seemed to lack creative spark. 作為作家，他似乎缺少創作激情。<span class="definition">2 [C, usually pl.] 人們之間的激動的情感：</span>Sparks flew at the meeting (= there was a lot of argument). 會上爭論激烈，火星四濺。/ Her eyes shot sparks of contempt. 她的眼裡滿是蔑視。

相關詞義延伸：
· passion還可指強烈的愛情。也可指對某事物的酷愛；熱衷的愛好（或活動等）。

☀ <span class="category">**情緒 心情：**</span>
<span class="vocabulary">**emotion**</span> [ɪˈməʊʃn; 美 ɪˈmoʊʃn]
<span class="definition">n. 1 [C, U] 人的情緒、情感：</span>He lost control of his emotions. 他情緒失去了控制。/ They expressed mixed emotions at the news. 他們對這個訊息表現出複雜的感情。/ She showed no emotion at the verdict. 她對這一裁定無動於衷。/ Happiness was an emotion that Reynolds was having to relearn. 雷諾茲已經不知道什麼是快樂。/ She felt torn by conflicting emotions. 矛盾的情感折磨著她。/ We can teach people to handle negative emotions such as fear and anger. 我們教人們怎樣應對恐懼和憤怒等消極情感。/ a moving performance full of raw emotion 充滿真情實感的動人表演 / the heightened emotions which resulted from the terrorist attack 恐怖襲擊導致的高漲情緒 / Years of pent-up emotion came out as he sobbed. 他啜泣著，多年壓抑的情感得到釋放。/ Fear is a normal human emotion. 恐懼是正常的人類情感。/ There wasn't a hint of emotion in his eyes. 他的眼中並沒流露出任何感情。/ She felt a sudden rush of emotion at the thought of seeing him again. 一想到要再見到他，她就心潮起伏。/ She could not cope with such public displays of emotion. 她應付不來這種公開的情感表達。/ the emotions that we experience as children 孩提時經歷的情感 / He felt no emotion as she left. 她離開時他感覺心情很淡然。/ Drama can help children to express their emotions. 戲劇能幫助孩子們表達他們的情感。/ The woman's face showed no emotion. 那個女人面無表情。/ The film captures the real emotion of this terrible event. 電影抓住了這一恐怖事件造成的真實情感。/ Releasing these emotions is part of the healing process. 釋放這些情感是康復過程的一部分。/ Boys often find it difficult to share their emotions. 男孩們時常發現很難與別人分享他們的感情。/ Victims of crime are encouraged to confront their emotions. 罪案受害人受到鼓勵去直面自己的情感。/ You could read his emotions by looking into his eyes. 仔細觀察他的眼睛，就能明白他的情感。/ She spoke with deep emotion. 她講話時充滿感情。/ The movie has a surprising depth of emotion for a comedy. 作為喜劇，這部電影的感情深度出乎意料。/ Her performance in the play covered the whole gamut of emotions. 她在劇中的表演情感跨度很大。<span class="definition">2 [U]（與理想相對的）感性：</span>the split between reason and emotion 理性和感性的劃分 / The decision was based on emotion rather than rational thought. 這個決定不是基於理性的思考而是基於感性做出的。           
                      
<span class="vocabulary">**emotional**</span> [ɪˈməʊʃənl; 美 ɪˈmoʊ-]
<span class="definition">adj. [常用於名詞前] 感情的；情感的；情緒的：</span>emotional problems, emotional needs 情感問題；情感需求 / emotional stress 情緒緊張 / a child's emotional and intellectual development 兒童的情感和智力發展 / Mothers are often the ones who provide emotional support for the family. 母親通常是家庭的情感支柱。/ I needed this man's love, and the emotional support he was giving me. 我需要這個男人的愛，以及他在情感上給我的支援。/ Victims are left with emotional problems that can last for life. 受害者心中留下了可能會持續一生的情感創傷。

<span class="vocabulary">**mood**</span> [mu:d]
<span class="definition">n. [C] 心情、情緒：</span>She's in a good mood today (= happy and friendly). 她今天心情很好。/ He's always in a bad mood (= unhappy, or angry and impatient). 他總是情緒不好。/ to be in a foul/filthy mood 情緒很差 / Some addicts suffer violent mood swings (= changes of mood) if deprived of the drug. 一些吸毒成癮的人一旦沒有毒品就會出現情緒的激烈波動。/ She was in an upbeat mood about the future of the company. 她對公司的未來充滿信心。/ I can't keep up with his constantly changing moods. 他喜怒無常，讓我無所適從。/ The workers were in defiant mood as they entered the tribunal. 員工們走進仲裁法庭時看上去蔑視一切。/ The music helped to put them in a more relaxed mood. 音樂使他們心情更加放鬆。/ Choose clothes to match your mood. 要選擇適合你心情的衣服。/ The weather seemed to reflect his dark mood. 天氣陰沉沉的，正如他陰鬱的心情。/ He seemed able to read her mood. 他似乎能讀懂她的心情。/ He could sense her gloomy mood. 他能感覺到她憂鬱的心境。/ | capture a film that has captured the mood of the moment | gauge / Not wanting to dampen her good mood, I quickly changed the subject. 因為不想影響她的好心情，我很快改變了話題。/ It immediately brightened her mood and brought a smile to her face. 這立刻讓她心情開朗，滿面笑容。/ Serotonin is a brain chemical which regulates mood. 血清素是一種調整情緒的大腦化合物。/ His mood lifted as he concentrated on his driving. 他集中精力開車時，心情又好了起來。/ After the accident he suffered violent mood swings. 出事之後，他的情緒波動很大。/ mood state 心境 / the challenge of coping with negative mood states 應對負面情緒狀態的挑戰 / Mood disorders can disrupt relationships. 情緒障礙可能擾亂人際關係。/ I tried to make him laugh, but he was in no mood for jokes. 我盡力想逗他笑，可他根本就沒心思聽笑話。/ He was in no mood for being polite to visitors. 他當時沒心思以禮待客。/ I'm just not in the mood for a party tonight. 我今晚就是沒心情參加聚會。/ Instantly he felt her change of mood. 他立刻感到了她情緒上的變化。/ She could be a very funny girl when the mood struck her. 她心情好的時候會變得很風趣。

<span class="vocabulary">**sentiment**</span> [ˈsentɪmənt]
<span class="definition">n. [C, U] [正式]（尤指基於思想或情感的）情緒或態度：</span>consumer, investor sentiment（均尤美）消費者情緒；投資者情緒 / bearish, bullish sentiment（均尤美）（股市等的）看跌情緒；看漲情緒 / There was a steep rise in bullish sentiment as foreign investors rushed in. 當外國投資者紛紛進入股市，看漲情緒陡然上升。/ nationalist, patriotic sentiment 民族主義情緒；愛國主義情緒 / anti-Japanese sentiment 反日情緒 / anti-war sentiment among the civilian population 平民的反戰情緒 / anti-government sentiment 反政府情緒 / Public sentiment rapidly turned anti-American. 公眾情緒迅速轉變，開始反對美國。/ These actions are likely to inflame anti-Western sentiment. 這些行為很可能使人們反西方情緒高漲。/ pro-American sentiment 親美情緒 / racist sentiment 種族主義情緒 / political sentiment 政治情緒 / moral, religious sentiment 道德情感；宗教情感 / The people are renowned for their deep religious sentiment. 這些人以篤信宗教著稱。/ In the 19th century, anti-Catholic sentiment ran high. 19世紀反天主教情緒高漲。/ It would be a mistake to ignore their strong sentiments on the issue. 無視他們在這個問題上的強烈情緒將是個錯誤。/ The killings helped arouse popular sentiment against the organization. 殺戮激起了大眾對該組織的敵對情緒。/ critical sentiment towards/toward government policy 對政府政策的不滿情緒 / Even though I disagree with you, I appreciate the sentiments that prompt you to speak out. 雖然我的看法和你不同，但我很理解你的想法。/ He's found growing sentiment for military action. 他發現支援採取軍事行動的情緒日益高漲。/ This is a sentiment I wholeheartedly agree with. 這種態度我完全贊同。
           
<span class="vocabulary">**temper**</span> [ˈtempə(r)]
<span class="definition">n. [C] 心情、心境、情緒：</span>Come back when you're in a better temper. 心情好些時再回來。/ to be in a foul temper 心情很壞 / I was in a bad temper last night. 昨晚我心情不好。/ He was in a very good temper. 他當時心情非常好。/ Peter's comments were responsible for her ill temper. 彼得的話讓她心情不好。/ Frayed tempers at the end led to three players being sent off. 終場前的急躁情緒導致三名隊員被罰下場。<span class="definition">adj. -tempered 用於構成形容詞，指心情…的：</span>瞭解即可

<span class="vocabulary">**humour**</span> ['hju:mə]（美humor）
<span class="definition">n. [C, U] [正式] 感覺或精神在某一時間的狀態，即心情，精神狀態：</span>The meeting dissolved in ill humour. 會議不歡而散。/ to be out of humour 心情不好 / He was obviously in a good humour this evening. 他今晚顯然心情不錯。

相關詞義延伸：
· sentiment還可指（尤指對過去的事過度或不當的）傷感、柔情、哀傷等。

<span class="vocabulary">**poison**</span> ['pɒɪzn] 
<span class="definition">n. [C] 極其有害的感情：</span>瞭解即可

<span class="vocabulary">**baggage**</span> ['bæɡɪdӡ] 
<span class="definition">n. [U] 過去的經歷對情感、思想等所形成的阻礙作用，即包袱，也可以視為一種比喻用法：</span>cultural, emotional baggage 文化包袱；感情包袱 / The party has shed its ideological baggage. 該黨拋棄了其意識形態包袱。

相關詞義延伸：
· feeling還可指（對某人或某事物的）同情或愛；
· mood還可指憤怒或不耐煩的情緒。

周邊詞彙補充：
· voice可指內心中（或已表達出來）的意見、態度或感情、心聲等；
· electricity可指強烈的感情、興奮、激動等；
· pulse可指（一個社會群體在某一時間的）感情；
· flame可指火藥味的電子郵件（或網際網路資訊）。

☀ <span class="category">**感情強烈的名詞：**</span>
<span class="vocabulary">**depth**</span> [depθ] 
<span class="definition">n. 1 [U] 感覺或感情的強度和力量，即深厚；誠摯；強烈等：</span>the depth of his feelings for her 他對她的深情 / music of great emotional depth 飽含深情的音樂 / The rejection plunged her into the dark depths of despair. 遭到拒絕使她陷入了絕望的深淵。/ the depth of feeling against the war 強烈的反戰情緒 <span class="definition">2 [C, usually pl.]（具有文學性）眼神的最深處：</span>He gazed into the depths of her eyes. 他深深凝望著她的眼睛。

<span class="vocabulary">**strength**</span> [streŋθ] 
<span class="definition">n. [U]（感情的）強烈程度或深度：</span>strength of feeling 情感的強烈程度

<span class="vocabulary">**violence**</span> ['vaɪələns] 
<span class="definition">n. [U] 情感的激烈，具有文學色彩：</span>the violence in his tone 他語氣激烈 / the violence of her feelings 她的感情之強烈

☀ <span class="category">**感情強烈的形容詞和副詞：**</span>
<span class="vocabulary">**fierce**</span> [fɪəs] 
<span class="definition">adj.（情感）強烈的、猛烈的：</span>fierce loyalty 極度的忠誠

<span class="vocabulary">**wild**</span> [waɪld] 
<span class="definition">adj. 充滿強烈感情的：</span>wild laughter 開懷大笑 / The children are wild with joy. 孩子們欣喜若狂。/ As George himself came on stage they went wild. 當喬治本人登上舞臺的時候，他們都發狂了。/ It makes me wild to see such waste. 看到這種浪費現象讓我非常生氣。

<span class="vocabulary">**violent**</span> ['vaɪələnt] 
<span class="definition">adj. 表現出強烈感情的，或由其引起的：</span>There was a violent reaction from the public. 公眾反應強烈。
           
<span class="vocabulary">**ferocious**</span> [fəˈrəʊʃəs; 美 -ˈroʊ-]
<span class="definition">adj.（感情）強烈的、決然的：</span>Lindbergh was startled at the ferocious depth of anti-British feeling. 反英情緒之深之烈令林德伯格大為震驚。/ a man driven by ferocious determination 為強烈的決心所驅使的人

<span class="vocabulary">**deep**</span> [di:p] 
<span class="definition">adj.（情感）強烈的；深切的；衷心的：</span>deep respect 深深的敬意 / a deep sense of loss 強烈的失落感 / deep sympathy 深切的同情 / This suspicion runs very deep among them. 這種猜疑在他們之中很強烈。<span class="definition">adv. deep (below, into, under, etc.) 深深地；在深處；至深處：</span>He gazed deep into her eyes. 他深深凝望著她的眼睛。/ Deep down, she supported her husband’s. 她從內心深處支援丈夫。
           
<span class="vocabulary">**lyrical**</span> [ˈlɪrɪkl]
<span class="definition">adj. 優美地（且帶有想象力地）表現強烈感情的，如抒情的、浪漫的、熱情奔放的：</span>a lyrical melody 抒情的旋律 / He began to wax lyrical (= talk in an enthusiastic way) about his new car. 他開始興高采烈地談論他的新車。/ His paintings became more lyrical. 他的畫作變得更加熱情奔放。/ its remarkable free-flowing and often lyrical style 其令人驚歎的流暢文筆和往往富有詩意的寫作風格 / He waxed lyrical about the variety of fish in the river. 他興高采烈地談論了河裡的魚品種多樣。/ There are some intensely lyrical passages in his first symphony. 他的第一首交響曲裡有些特別抒情的片段。/ She wrote an almost lyrical account of her childhood. 她敘述了自己的童年，寫得幾乎和抒情詩一樣優美。

<span class="definition">vi.（一種感情）出現：</span>Emotions quickly bubble to the surface. 感情很快表露出來。

<span class="vocabulary">**betray**</span> [bɪ'treɪ] 
<span class="definition">vt. 向別人流露情感、洩露資訊等，常是無意識地流露，相當於give away：</span>His voice betrayed the worry he was trying to hide. 他的聲音掩飾不了內心的擔憂。/ She was terrified of saying something that would make her betray herself (= show her feelings or who she was). 她害怕說話時洩了自己的底。

<span class="vocabulary">**express**</span> [ɪk'spres] 
<span class="definition">vt. express itself [正式] 指想法或感情呈現出來，即流露、表現：</span>His anxiety often expresses itself as anger toward others. 他的焦慮常常表現為對其他人的憤怒。

<span class="vocabulary">**stamp**</span> [stæmp] 
<span class="definition">vt. 常用被動式，讓感情清晰地顯露在某人的臉上或行動中等：</span>His face was stamped with unhappiness. 他神情不悅。
           
<span class="vocabulary">**demonstration**</span> [ˌdemənˈstreɪʃn]
<span class="definition">n. [C] 情感的表達、表露：</span>a public demonstration of affection 公開表露愛慕之情 / physical demonstrations of affection 身體上的親暱 / a demonstration of support for the reforms 表示對改革的支援 / There's been no public demonstration of opposition to the President. 沒有人對總統公開表示反對。

相關詞義延伸：
· register還可指（正式地或公開地、清楚地）表達感情。

周邊詞彙補充：
· signal可指做某事以表達自己的感情；
· flame可指人的臉因強烈情緒而變紅，或使…變紅。也可指透過網際網路（如電子郵件）向某人傳送憤怒或侮辱性的訊息；
· flicker可指（情緒、思想等）短時間閃現、一閃而過。

☀ <span class="category">**宣洩感情：**</span>           
<span class="vocabulary">**release**</span> [rɪˈli:s]
<span class="definition">vt. 宣洩情感（如憤怒、擔憂等）：</span>She burst into tears, releasing all her pent-up emotions. 她放聲大哭，發洩出全部鬱積起來的情感。/ I personally don't want to release my anger on anyone else. 我個人並不想把自己的憤怒發洩在別人身上。<span class="definition">n. [U, sing.] 情感或壓力的宣洩：</span>Crying gave some emotional release. 哭泣是一種情緒宣洩。/ sexual release 性宣洩 / Sometimes we just need some release from the pressure. 有時我們只是需要緩解一下壓力。/ All societies have social mechanisms for the release of tension. 每一個社會都有釋放壓力的機制。
           
<span class="vocabulary">**vent**</span> [vent]
<span class="definition">vt. [正式] ~ sth (on sb) 強烈表達情感（尤指憤怒），即發洩：</span>He vented his anger on the referee. 他把氣撒在裁判身上。/ She telephoned her best friend to vent her frustration. 她給最要好的朋友打電話傾訴自己的沮喪。/ The rioters were prevented from venting their anger on the police. 鬧事者向警察發洩怒氣的企圖沒有得逞。

☀ <span class="category">**感動、打動：**</span>
<span class="vocabulary">**move**</span> [mu:v] 
<span class="definition">vt. move sb (to sth) 使某人擁有強烈的感情（尤指同情或悲傷），即打動，使感動：</span>The story moved me. 這個故事打動了我。
           
<span class="vocabulary">**moving**</span> [ˈmu:vɪŋ]
<span class="definition">adj. 令人感動的，令人感傷（或同情）的：</span>a deeply moving experience 非常動人的經歷 / It is very moving to see how much strangers can care for each other. 看到陌生人之間這般彼此關照，實在令人感動。/ It was a moving moment for Marianne. 對瑪麗安娜來說，那是動人的一刻。/ I found the story intensely moving. 我覺得這個故事非常感人。/ a deeply moving account of life on the streets 對街頭流浪生活極為感人的描述

<span class="vocabulary">**touch**</span> [tʌtʃ] 
<span class="definition">vt. 使某人感動，觸動；或使其產生同情心。不過touch表此義時一般無被動式。且主語是讓人感動的事物或事件等，而一般不是被感動者：</span>The story touched me very deeply. 這個故事深深打動了我。
           
<span class="vocabulary">**touched**</span> [tʌtʃt]
<span class="definition">adj. [名詞前不可用] ~ (by sth) / ~ (that…)（對別人給予的幫助）感激的、受感動的；受到某事觸動的：</span>She was touched by their warm welcome. 她對他們的熱烈歡迎十分感動。/ I was touched that he still remembered me. 他仍然記得我，使我十分感動。/ She was touched by the plight of the refugees. 難民的困境使她受到觸動。/ She was especially touched by the chairman's appeal. 主席的呼籲使她尤為感動。/ We were deeply touched by your concern. 你的關心讓我們深受感動。
           
<span class="vocabulary">**stir**</span> [stɜ:(r)]
<span class="definition">vt. ~ sb (to sth) 打動：</span>She was stirred by his sad story. 他那悲慘的故事打動了她。/ The voice, less coarse now, stirred her as it had then. 現在那聲音已不那麼刺耳，它又如當年一樣讓她怦然心動。/ I was intrigued by him, stirred by his intellect. 我被他的聰明才智所打動，對他產生了好奇心。
