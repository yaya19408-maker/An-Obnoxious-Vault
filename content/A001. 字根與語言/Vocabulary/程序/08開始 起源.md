☀ <span class="category">**開始、起源、發起、啟用的動詞：**</span>
<span class="vocabulary">**begin**</span> [bɪ'ɡɪn] 
<span class="definition">vt.＆vi. 開始做某事；執行某事的第一部分。側重於強調“開始”這一動作或階段：</span>We began work on the project in May. 我們於五月份啟動了這項工程。/ He began his political career as a student. 他從當學生時起就開始了他的政治生涯。/ Shall I begin? 我可以開始了嗎？/ Let’s begin at page 9. 咱們從第9頁開始。/ It was beginning to snow. 開始下雪了。<span class="definition">vi. 1（尤指從一個特定的時間）開始發生或存在：</span>When does the concert begin? 音樂會什麼時間開始？/ Work on the new bridge is due to begin in September. 新橋定於九月份動工。/ Their troubles are only beginning. 他們的麻煩才剛剛開始。<span class="definition">2 begin as sth 起初是…，本來是…（後來變成了其他東西）：</span>He began as an actor, before starting to direct films. 他先是當演員，後來開始執導影片。/ What began as a minor scuffle turned into a full-scale riot. 開始的小衝突演變成了大規模的暴亂。<span class="definition">3 使某事物作為第一部分，或使其作為其他事物的起始點。如從…開始，以…為起點：</span>words beginning with a vowel 以母音開頭的詞 / Each chapter begins with a quotation. 每一章的開頭都有一條引語。/ Where does Europe end and Asia begin? 歐洲和亞洲的交界處在哪裡？/ The path begins at Livingston village. 這條小路始於利文斯頓村。

<span class="vocabulary">**start**</span> [stɑːt] 
<span class="definition">vt.＆vi. 1 開始做某事，或開始使用某東西。側重於強調“開始”這一事實：</span>I start work at nine. 我一般九點開始工作。<span class="definition">2 開始發生；使某事開始發生：</span>When does the class start? 什麼時刻開始上課？/ Have you any idea where the rumour started? 你知不知道謠言是從哪兒傳出來的？/ Her heart suddenly started to race. 她的心臟突然開始狂跳起來。<span class="definition">3 start (sth) (up) 開始存在；使某事物開始存在：</span>There are a lot of small businesses starting up in that area. 小型企業在那一地區大量湧現。/ They decided to start a catering business. 他們決定開辦一家酒席承辦公司。<span class="definition">4 start (out/off) (sth) (as sth) 以某種方式來開始，但該方式後來又改變，即起初是…，以…方式起步或起家（如職業生涯上的起步）：</span>She started as a secretary but ended up running the department. 她起初只是一個秘書，但最後掌管起了整個部門。/ The company started out with 30 employees. 該公司初創時期只有30名員工。<span class="definition">vi. 從某個地方（或數額、數量、情況）開始：</span>The trail starts just outside the town. 足跡從剛出城的地方開始。/ Hotel prices start at €50 a night for a double room. 旅館的雙人間一晚50歐元起價。/ The evening started badly when the speaker failed to turn up. 那天晚上的活動一開始挺糟糕，因為演講者沒有來。
           
<span class="vocabulary">**commence**</span> [kəˈmens]
<span class="definition">vt.&vi. ~ (with sth) [正式] 開始發生；開始某事、著手：</span>The academic year commences at the beginning of October. 學年於10月初開始。/ The day commenced with a welcome from the principal. 那天由校長致歡迎詞開始。/ The meeting is scheduled to commence at noon. 會議定於午間召開。/ I will be on leave during the week commencing 15 February. 我將從2月15日開始一週的休假。/ She commenced her medical career in 1956. 她於1956年開始行醫。/ We commence building next week. 我們下週破土動工。/ They commenced a systematic search. 他們開始進行徹底搜查。
           
<span class="vocabulary">**embark**</span> [ɪmˈbɑ:k; 美 ɪmˈbɑ:rk]
<span class="definition">vi. embark on/upon sth 從事，著手，開始（新的或艱難的事情）：</span>She is about to embark on a diplomatic career. 她即將開始外交生涯。/ He's embarking on a new career as a writer. 他即將開始新的職業生涯——當一名作家。/ The government embarked on a programme of radical economic reform. 政府開始實施一項徹底的經濟改革計劃。/ The company has recently embarked on a new venture. 公司近期創辦了一家新企業。/ She has already embarked on her studies. 她已經著手進行研究。/ She was now ready to embark on her adventure. 她已為探險做好準備。

<span class="vocabulary">**open**</span> ['əʊpən] 
<span class="definition">vi. 為人們前來而做準備，即準備接待：</span>The new hospital opens in July 1st. 這家新醫院七月一日開診。/ The museum is due to open next year. 博物館預計明年開放。<span class="definition">vt. open sth (with sth) 開始一項活動或事件：</span>to open a bank account 開立銀行賬戶 / to open an investigation into the death 開始對這樁命案進行調查 / They will open the new season with a performance of ‘Carmen’. 他們將以上演《卡門》來展開新的戲劇季。/ to open fire on the crowds 向人群開火 <span class="definition">vt.＆vi.（活動）以…開始：</span>to open by saying, “...” 以這句話作為開場白：“…” / The exhibition opened with a model show. 展覽以一場模特表演開始。
        
<span class="vocabulary">**preface**</span> [ˈprefəs]
<span class="definition">vt. [正式] ~ sth by/with sth / ~ sth by doing sth（在講話、回答問題等之前）說開場白：</span>I must preface my remarks with an apology. 講話前，我必須先表示歉意。/ I will preface what I am going to say with a few lines from Shakespeare. 進入正題之前，我先引用莎士比亞的幾句詩作為開場白。/ The president prefaced his remarks by saying he has supported unemployment benefits all along. 總統在發表講話前說他一直以來都支援發放失業津貼。

<span class="vocabulary">**enter**</span> ['entə] 
<span class="definition">vt. 1 無被動式，開始在某一專業組織中工作（即開始從事）：</span>to enter politics 開始從政 / to enter Parliament 成為英國議會議員 / to enter the Church (= become a priest) 成為神職人員 <span class="definition">2 開始（或捲入）一項活動、一種情況等：</span>to enter relationship, to enter conflict, to enter war 建立關係；發生衝突；參戰 / Several new firms have now entered the market. 有幾家新公司已進入市場。/ The investigation has entered a new phase. 調查已進入新階段。/ The strike is entering its fourth week. 罷工正進入第四周。

<span class="vocabulary">**spring**</span> [sprɪŋ] 
<span class="definition">vi. 是…的結果，即起源於…：</span>Ethiopia’s art springs from its early Christian as well as its Muslim heritage. 衣索比亞的藝術起源於其早期的基督教和穆斯林教遺產。<span class="definition">vt. spring sth (on sb) 讓某人意想不到地去做某事：</span>She sprang a surprise by winning the tournament. 這次比賽她獲得冠軍，爆了個大冷門。

<span class="vocabulary">**come**</span> [kʌm] 
<span class="definition">vi. 1 來自（某個地方或事物）：</span>Nearly half the students come from overseas. 幾乎一半學生來自海外。/ Chocolate comes from the cacao tree. 巧克力來自可可樹。<span class="definition">2 源自：</span>There is a feeling of power that comes from driving fast. 開快車可以產生力量感。/ Some good might come of all this gloomy business. 某種好的結果也許會從這種慘淡的經營中產生。

<span class="vocabulary">**born**</span> [bɔ:n] 
<span class="definition">vt. 僅用於被動式，且不用by，指（思想、組織機構、感情等）產生，誕生：</span>The idea was born in his study. 這個想法是在他的書房裡產生的。/ The CIA was born at that time. 中央情報局那時便誕生了。/ He acted with a courage born (out) of desperation. 絕望驅使他鼓起勇氣，做出行動。/ The movement was born in the university. 該運動誕生於這所大學。
           
<span class="vocabulary">**originate**</span> [əˈrɪdʒɪneɪt]
<span class="definition">vi. [正式] 在某地或某種情況中頭一次發生或出現，即起源、發源於：</span>The disease is thought to have originated in the tropics. 這種疾病據說起源於熱帶地區。/ The disease originated in Africa. 這種病源於非洲。/ All carbohydrates originate from plants. 所有的碳水化合物均來自植物。/ He believes that all religions originated from a single source. 他認為所有的宗教都有同一個本源。
                      
<span class="vocabulary">**derive**</span> [dɪˈraɪv]
<span class="definition">vt.&vi.（使）（文字、感情等）起源於或來自於：</span>Anna's strength is derived from her parents and her sisters. 安娜的堅強源自於她的父母和姐姐們。/ The word ‘politics’ is derived from a Greek word meaning ‘city’. politics一詞源自希臘語，意思是city。/ The word Easter derives from Eostre, the pagan goddess of spring. Easter (復活節)一詞由Eostre(多神教的春天女神)衍生而來。

<span class="vocabulary">**stem**</span> [stem]
<span class="definition">vi. ~ from（不用於進行時）起源於、來自：</span>All my problems stem from drink. 我所有的問題都源於酗酒。
           
<span class="vocabulary">**attribute**</span> [əˈtrɪbju:t]
<span class="definition">vt. ~ sth (to sb) 認為(文章、藝術品或評論）出自…：</span>This play is usually attributed to Shakespeare. 人們通常認為這出戏劇是莎士比亞所寫。/ This, and the remaining frescoes, are not attributed to Giotto. 這個，還有那些殘存的溼壁畫，都不是喬託的作品。/ a Madonna and Child attributed to Pietro Lorenzetti 被認為出自彼得羅·洛倫澤蒂之手的作品《聖母子》/ a quote that has often been falsely attributed to George Patton 一段經常被誤認為是喬治·巴頓的語錄
           
<span class="vocabulary">**ascribe**</span> [əˈskraɪb]
<span class="definition">vt. ~ sth to sb 認為某事物（如語錄、藝術作品）是由某人創造的：</span>He mistakenly ascribes the expression 'survival of the fittest' to Charles Darwin. 他錯誤地認為“適者生存”是查爾斯·達爾文說的。/ This play is usually ascribed to Shakespeare. 通常認為這部劇是莎士比亞所寫。

<span class="vocabulary">**rise**</span> [raɪz] 
<span class="definition">vi. 變得更成功、更重要、更強大等，如崛起：</span>a rising young politician 嶄露頭角的年輕政治家 / She rose to power in the 70s. 她於20世紀70年代掌握了大權。/ He rose to the rank of general. 他升至將級軍官。/ She rose through the ranks to become managing director. 她從普通員工逐步晉升為總經理。

<span class="vocabulary">**go**</span> [ɡəʊ] 
<span class="definition">vi. 開始（一項活動）：</span>I’ll say ‘One, two, three, go!’ as a signal for you to start. 我喊“一、二、三，開始！”作為你開始的訊號。/ As soon as he gets here we’re ready to go. 他一到我們就可以開始。

<span class="vocabulary">**turn**</span> [tə:n] 
<span class="definition">vi. 開始做（某一活動或某份工作）；開始用（某種做事方式）：</span>These communities are now turning to recycling as a cheaper alternative to landfills. 這些社群現正開始用回收這種較為廉價的方法替代廢物填埋法。

<span class="vocabulary">**get**</span> [ɡet] 
<span class="definition">vt. 1 開始有（一個想法、印象或感受）：</span>I get the feeling that you’re an honest man. 我開始覺得你是個誠實的男人了。<span class="definition">2 用於get moving、get going或get working等結構中，表示開始做某事（尤指迅速開始去做）：</span>I got talking to her. 我開始與她談起來。/ We need to get going soon. 我們需要馬上出發。

<span class="vocabulary">**break**</span> [breɪk] 
<span class="definition">vi.（白天、黎明或風暴）開始：</span>They continued the search as dawn broke. 他們在天亮之後繼續搜尋。

<span class="vocabulary">**dawn**</span> [dɔ:n] 
<span class="definition">vi.（一段時期）開始：</span>A new technological age had dawned. 新技術時代已經開始。/ A new century was dawning. 一個新的世紀開始了。

<span class="vocabulary">**be**</span> [bi:] 
<span class="definition">vi. be from ... 用於說明某人出生於某地或某人來自哪裡：</span>She’s from Italy. 她是義大利人。

<span class="vocabulary">**hail**</span> [heɪl]
<span class="definition">vi. [正式] ~ from（人或事物）來自某地、出生於某地：</span>His father hailed from Italy. 他父親出生於義大利。/ He hails from an affluent background. 他出身於一個富有的家庭。/ The band hail from Glasgow. 樂團來自格拉斯哥。/ This is a film which seems to hail from the hippie era. 這看起來是一部嬉皮士時代的影片。           

<span class="vocabulary">**descend**</span> [dɪˈsend]
<span class="definition">vi.（人、動物等）是…的後裔：</span>He claims to be descended from a Spanish prince. 他聲稱自己是一位西班牙王子的後人。/ The breed is almost directly descended from the Eurasian wild boar. 這一品種幾乎是歐洲野豬的直系後代。
                     
<span class="vocabulary">**mount**</span> [maʊnt]
<span class="definition">vt. 組織或發起一場活動：</span>to mount a protest 發起抗議 / The ANC announced it was mounting a major campaign of mass political protests. 非洲人國民大會宣佈他們正在開展一次大規模政治抗議活動。/ a security operation mounted by the army 軍隊開展的維護安全軍事行動 / The gallery has mounted an exhibition of art by Irish women painters. 畫廊已舉辦了一場愛爾蘭女畫家的作品展。/ The company successfully mounted a takeover bid in 1996. 該公司1996年成功發起了一次競價收購。

<span class="vocabulary">**launch**</span> [lɔ:ntʃ]
<span class="definition">vt. 開始從事一項活動，尤指發起有組織的活動：</span>to launch an appeal, to launch an inquiry, to launch a campaign 發起上訴；發起質詢；發起一場運動 / to launch an attack, to launch invasion 發起攻擊；發動侵略 / Heavy fighting has been going on after the guerrillas had launched their offensive. 游擊隊發起攻擊後，激烈的戰鬥一直持續不斷。/ The police have launched an investigation into the incident. 警方已對該事件展開調查。<span class="definition">vt. 產品首次上市或發行：</span>The new model will be launched in July. 新型號產品將在七月推出。/ to launch a new jam 推出一款新果醬 / to launch a new range 推出新系列產品 / a party to launch his latest novel 他最新小說的首發式 <span class="definition">vt. 使船（尤指新船）下水：</span>The Navy is to launch a new warship today. 海軍今天有一艘新軍艦要下水。/ The lifeboat was launched immediately. 那艘救生艇被立刻放下了水。/ There was no time to launch the lifeboats because the ferry capsized with such alarming speed. 渡輪瞬間傾覆，根本沒時間放救生艇下水。<span class="definition">vt. 發射航天器、武器等：</span>to launch a communications satellite 發射通訊衛星 / to launch a rocket 發射火箭 / to launch a missile, to launch a torpedo 發射導彈；發射魚雷
           
<span class="vocabulary">**initiate**</span> [ɪˈnɪʃieɪt]
<span class="definition">vt. [正式] 使某事發生，即發起、創始：</span>to initiate legal proceedings against sb 對某人提起訴訟 / The enquiry was formally initiated last month. 調查於上個月正式啟動。/ The government has initiated a programme of economic reform. 政府已開始實施經濟改革方案。/ They wanted to initiate a discussion on economics. 他們想啟動一次經濟學討論。/ The trip was initiated by the manager of the community centre. 這次旅行由社群活動中心的經理發起。           

<span class="vocabulary">**wage**</span> [weɪdӡ] 
<span class="definition">vt. wage sth (against/on sb/sth) 發動、進行一場戰爭、戰鬥等：</span>to wage a war 發動戰爭 / He alleged that a press campaign was being waged against him. 他聲稱有人正在對他發起新聞攻勢。

<span class="vocabulary">**activate**</span> [ˈæktɪveɪt]
<span class="definition">vt. 啟用一個裝置或化學過程：</span>The burglar alarm is activated by movement. 這防盜警報器一動就會響。/ Video cameras with night vision can be activated by movement. 帶夜視鏡的攝像機一有動靜就會啟動。/ a voice-activated computer 聲控電腦 / The gene is activated by a specific protein. 這種基因由一種特異性蛋白啟用。

相關詞義延伸：
· originate還可指創立、建立、發明新事物；
· initiate還可表示向某人解釋某事並使其第一次經歷此事，如傳授基礎知識。也可表示（尤指在秘密儀式上）使某人加入一個群體。

周邊詞彙補充：
· renew可指（中斷後）重新開始。還可指重新建立關係。

☀ <span class="category">**開始、起源、發起的名詞：**</span>
<span class="vocabulary">**beginning**</span> [bɪˈgɪnɪŋ]
<span class="definition">n. 1 [C, usually sing.] ~ (of sth)（事件或活動的）開始部分、開端、起點：</span>Let's start again from the beginning. 讓我們再從頭開始。/ The birth of their first child marked the beginning of a new era in their married life. 第一個孩子的出世使他們的婚姻生活開始了一個新階段。/ This was also the beginning of her recording career. 這也是她唱片生涯的開始。/ Think of this as a new beginning. 把這當作一個新起點。<span class="definition">2 [pl.] beginnings 開始或早期的思想、標誌或階段，即原始思想；前兆；初級階段：</span>Did democracy have its beginnings in ancient Greece? 民主制度創始於古希臘嗎？/ He built up his multimillion-pound music business from small beginnings. 他從小本生意起步，逐步建立起了數百萬英鎊的音樂企業。/ I had the beginnings of a headache. 我有了頭痛的先兆。/ The discussions were the beginnings of a dialogue with Moscow. 討論拉開了同莫斯科對話的序幕。<span class="definition">3 [sing.] 初期，早期：</span>We're going to Japan at the beginning of July. 我們七月初要去日本。/ She's been working there since the beginning of last summer. 她自從去年夏初起就一直在那裡工作。/ The wedding will be at the beginning of March. 婚禮定於三月初舉行。<span class="definition">4 [C]（作品的）開頭、開篇：</span>We missed the beginning of the movie. 我們錯過了電影的開頭部分。/ the question which was raised at the beginning of this chapter 在本章開頭所提出的問題 / I've read the whole book from beginning to end and still can't understand it. 我把整本書從頭到尾看了一遍，但還是沒看懂。<span class="definition">5 [pl.]（一個人、公司或群體的）出身；背景；起源：</span>His views come from his own humble beginnings. 他的觀點源於他卑微的出身。           
                      
<span class="vocabulary">**onset**</span> [ˈɒnset; 美 ˈɑ:n-; ˈɔ:n-]
<span class="definition">n. [sing.]（尤指壞事的）開始、開端：</span>the sudden onset of the disease 疾病的突然發作 / Symptoms include the acute onset of pain. 症狀包括疼痛的急性發作。/ the onset of old age 老年的開始 / The development of pubic hair signals the - of puberty. 長出陰毛標誌著青春期的開始。/ His first hit record marked the onset of an astonishing career. 他的第一張風行一時的唱片標誌著輝煌事業的開始。/ after the onset of sleep 進入睡眠後 / Most of the passes have been closed with the onset of winter. 冬天來臨，大部分關口均已關閉。/ With the onset of war, oil prices climbed past $30 a barrel. 戰爭一爆發，油價就攀過了每桶30美元。

<span class="vocabulary">**initiation**</span> [ɪˌnɪʃiˈeɪʃn]
<span class="definition">n. [U] [正式] 開始、發起：</span>the initiation of criminal proceedings 提起刑事訴訟 / They announced the initiation of a rural development programme. 他們宣佈啟動一項農村發展計劃。/ There was a year between initiation and completion. 從開始到完成經過了一年的時間。

<span class="vocabulary">**germ**</span> [dʒɜ:m; 美 dʒɜ:rm]

<span class="definition">prep. 1 [非標準英語或美國英語，非正式] off of 來源於；從：</span>I got it off of my brother. 這是我從我弟弟那裡弄到的。<span class="definition">2 從某人那裡得到：</span>I don’t really get a lot of information, and if I do I get it off Mark. 我真沒得到很多資訊，即便有也是從馬克那裡得到的。

<span class="vocabulary">**by**</span> [baɪ] 
<span class="definition">prep. 用於補充資訊，所補充的資訊可以是關於某人來自何地：</span>by birth 在血統上（或祖籍等）

相關詞義延伸：
· start還可指啟動車輛，（車輛）啟動。也可指開始一個旅程、離開，即出發，動身，啟程。亦可指開始朝某個方向移動，如起身走向…，奔向…而去。或可指速度比賽的起點之地。另外可指在速度比賽的開始階段，某人相對於其他參賽者所具有的一個時間或距離上的提前量；
· open還可指開門營業、開業、股市開盤、（電影、戲劇或其他公共活動）開始或開演、（故事、電影等）以…開篇（或開始）、為建築物舉行揭幕儀式（以此宣佈啟用該建築物）；
· opening還可指開幕式。也可指開始的、開篇的、第一；rise還可指（河流）在某地發源，起源；head可指河流的源頭；
· raw還可指（食物）生的，未烹製的。也可指（人）對於一份工作或一項活動來說生疏並因此而沒有經驗或技能的；
· root還可指植物的根莖，根。也可指頭髮、牙齒、指甲、舌頭的根部。亦可指單詞的詞根。或可指數學中的方根，根；
· spring還可指讓某人意想不到地去問某事或說某事；
· from還可指…由…原料製成。也可表示兩地的距離。亦可用於區別二者，即與…不同。

周邊詞彙補充：
· begin可指開始講話。not begin則可指沒有去做某事的企圖，或不選擇去做某事；
· beginner可指初學者；
· working可指用於作為工作、討論等活動的基礎，將來很可能會改變或改進，即暫定的、初步的；
· sit可指（議會、委員會、法庭等）開會、開庭（以處理正式或官方事務）；
· off可指起跑；
· ancestor可指動物的原種（即某個現代動物的祖先）、機器的原型；
· parent可指（動植物的）父本或母本，親本，親代。還可指創始性的一個組織機構（其擁有或控制一些同型別的小機構）；
· father可指基督徒對上帝的稱呼，如天父。也可指創始人，鼻祖，先驅，奠基者等；
· date可指找出或表明某事物存在（或被製作）的年代；
· light可指“點火”；
· reference可指書籍或論文等材料中的引文（該引文出自於一本書、一首詩、一部戲劇等）。也可指（註明引文出處的）註釋。
