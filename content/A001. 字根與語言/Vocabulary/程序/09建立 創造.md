☀ <span class="category">**建造的動詞：**</span>
<span class="vocabulary">**build**</span> [bɪld] 
<span class="definition">vt. 1（人）逐漸建立（機構、社團、關係等）：</span>to build a company 建立一家公司 / to build a fair society and a strong economy 建立一個公平的社會和一個強大的經濟體 <span class="definition">2 以…為基礎來建立組織、體系、產品等：</span>We will then have a firmer foundation of fact on which to build theories. 那時我們將有更堅實的事實根據來作為理論的基礎。<span class="definition">3 建立；開發：</span>to build a new career 開闢一段新的職業生涯 / to build a better life 創造更好的生活 / to build a picture of the attacker 描畫出襲擊者的相貌

<span class="vocabulary">**construct**</span> [kən'strʌkt] 
<span class="definition">vt. 透過把不同的部分放在一起從而形成一個事物（如想法、文章、體系等），即組建；搭建；構建：</span>to construct a logical argument 確立合乎邏輯的論點 / to construct a theory 創立一種理論 / a well-constructed novel 構思巧妙的小說 / to construct a huge business empire 構建一個龐大的商業王國
           
<span class="vocabulary">**erect**</span> [ɪˈrekt]
<span class="definition">vt. [正式] 建造建築物；搭建帳篷：</span>The church was erected in 1582. 此教堂建於1582年。/ the newly erected station buildings 新建的車站建築 / An electric fence was erected around the campus. 校園四周安裝了帶電的圍欄。/ A large monument was erected on the battlefield. 戰場上矗立起一座大型紀念碑。/ to erect a tent 搭帳篷 / The event will take place in a specially erected marquee. 這個活動將在一個專門搭建的搭帳篷裡舉行。
           
<span class="vocabulary">**pitch**</span> [pɪtʃ]
<span class="definition">vt. 短時間搭建帳篷或紮營：</span>We could pitch our tent in that field. 我們可以臨時把帳篷搭在那塊地上。/ They pitched camp for the night near the river. 他們靠河邊紮營過夜。/ He had pitched his tent in the yard. 他在院子裡搭起了帳篷。/ At dusk we pitched camp in the middle of nowhere. 黃昏時分，我們在茫茫荒野中紮了營。

<span class="vocabulary">**rebuild**</span> [ri:'bɪld] 
<span class="definition">vt. 重建：</span>After the earthquake, the people set about rebuilding their homes. 地震過後，人們開始重建家園。/ The hall had to be rebuilt after the fire. 火災後這座禮堂不得不重建。/ to rebuild the country 重建該國

☀ <span class="category">**建造的名詞：**</span>
<span class="vocabulary">**construction**</span> [kən'strʌkʃn] 
<span class="definition">n. [U, C] 某事物（如某個體系、理論、理念、觀點、知識等的）創立：</span>the construction of a just system of criminal justice 公正的刑事司法體制的建立 / the construction of a new theory 新理論的創立

<span class="vocabulary">**architect**</span> ['ɑːkɪtekt] 
<span class="definition">n. [C]（思想、事件、形勢的）設計者，締造者，創造者：</span>He was one of the principal architects of the revolution. 他是那次革命的主要發動者之一。/ Jones was the architect of the team’s first goal. 瓊斯是球隊入第一球的進攻發起者。/ the principal architect of the constitution 憲法的主設計師

相關詞義延伸：
· construct還可指修建公路；
· construction還可指用字詞來構造句子、短語等的方式，即語法結構；
· architect還可指建築師；
· erect還可指設定障礙。

☀ <span class="category">**創造的動詞和形容詞：**</span>
<span class="vocabulary">**create**</span> [krɪ'eɪt] 
<span class="definition">vt. 使某事發生或存在，如創造：</span>Scientists disagree about how the universe was created. 科學家對宇宙是怎樣形成的有分歧。/ to create wealth 創造財富 / to create jobs 創造就業機會 / Create a new directory and put all your files into it. 建立一個新的目錄，然後把你所有的檔案都放進去。/ Try this new dish, created by our head chef. 品嚐一下這道新菜吧，是我們廚師長首創的。

<span class="vocabulary">**make**</span> [meɪk] 
<span class="definition">vt. 寫；出產；制定（規則、法律等）：</span>These regulations were made to protect children. 這些規章制度是為了保護兒童而制訂的。/ to make the laws 制定法律 / to make a will 立遺囑 / to make a movie 製作電影
                      
<span class="vocabulary">**elaborate**</span> [ɪˈlæbərət]
<span class="definition">vt. 詳細制定（計劃、想法、理論等）：</span>His task was to elaborate policies which would make a market economy compatible with a clean environment. 他的任務是制定詳細政策，使得市場經濟和清潔環境能夠共存。/ the plan elaborated by the five permanent members of the UN Security Council 由聯合國安全理事會五個常任理事國詳細制定的方案

<span class="vocabulary">**formulate**</span> [ˈfɔ:mjuleɪt; 美 ˈfɔ:rm-]
<span class="definition">vt. 制訂：</span>to formulate a policy, to formulate a theory 制訂政策；創立理論

<span class="vocabulary">**father**</span> ['fɑːðə] 
<span class="definition">vt. 創造新的思想（或新的做事方式）：</span>瞭解即可
                      
<span class="vocabulary">**creative**</span> [kriˈeɪtɪv]
<span class="definition">adj. 1 [只用於名詞前] 創造性的；創作的：</span>creative writing 創造性寫作 / a course on creative writing (= writing stories, plays and poems) 創作課程 / creative arts 創作型藝術 / the creative and performing arts 創作與表演藝術 / creative thinking (= thinking about problems in a new way or thinking of new ideas) 創造性思維 / the company's creative team 公司的創新組 / the creative process 創作的過程 <span class="definition">2 有創造力的；（尤指藝術作品）表現出創造力的：</span>She's very creative--she writes poetry and paints. 她極富創造力，既賦詩又作畫。/ Do you have any ideas? You're the creative one. 你有何高見？你是有創見的人。/ Like so many creative people he was never satisfied. 像許多富於創造力的人一樣，他從不滿足。/ her obvious creative talents 她顯而易見的創作才能 / his creative use of words 他對詞語的創造性運用 / I don' always feel creative. 我並不總是感覺自己有創造力。/ a highly creative artist 非常有創意的藝術家
           
<span class="vocabulary">**original**</span> [əˈrɪdʒənl]
<span class="definition">adj. 獨創的、有獨創性的：</span>an original idea 獨到的見解 / That's not a very original suggestion. 那個建議沒什麼新意。/ an original thinker 有創意的人 / an original writer 獨樹一幟的作家 / She has a highly original mind. 她是個非常有創新精神的人。/ It is one of the most original works of imagination in the language. 這是用該語言寫成的最具獨創性和想象力的作品之一。/ They are tackling the problem in a completely original way. 他們正用全新的方法解決這個問題。
 
<span class="vocabulary">**inventive**</span> [ɪnˈventɪv]
<span class="definition">adj. 1（尤指人）能想到新奇有趣的主意的：</span>She has a highly inventive mind. 她的頭腦非常善於創新。/ It inspired me to be more inventive with my own cooking. 它激發我在自己烹飪時更富創造性。<span class="definition">2（思想）新奇有趣的：</span>Stroman's ceaselessly inventive choreography 斯特羅曼永遠求新的舞蹈設計

<span class="vocabulary">**productive**</span> [prəˈdʌktɪv]
<span class="definition">adj. [正式] ~ of sth 創造…的；生產…的；產生…的：</span>Land, labor and capital are all productive of wealth. 土地、勞動和資本都能創造財富。

<span class="vocabulary">**fertile**</span> [ˈfɜ:taɪl; 美 ˈfɜ:rtl]
<span class="definition">adj. [常用於名詞前]（人的思想或想象力）能產生很多新想法的：</span>the product of a fertile imagination 想象力豐富的產物 / A chess player must have a fertile imagination and rich sense of fantasy. 一個棋手必須有豐富的想象力，並善於奇思妙想。

<span class="vocabulary">**innovation**</span> [ˌɪnəˈveɪʃn]
<span class="definition">n. 1 [U] ~ (in sth)（新事物、思想或方法的）創造；創新：</span>We must promote originality and encourage innovation. 我們必須提倡創意，鼓勵革新。/ architectural, artistic, cultural, educational, industrial, musical, organizational, scientific, stylistic, technical, technological innovation 建築創新；藝術創新；文化創新；教育創新；工業創新；音樂創新；組織創新；科學創新；風格創新；技術創新 / design, policy, product innovation 設計創新；政策革新；產品創新 / Too strict a regulatory system will stifle innovation. 過度嚴格的管理體制會扼殺創新。/ innovation process創新過程 / innovation in engineering 工程方面的創新 <span class="definition">2 [C] ~ (in sth)（創造的或新發現的）新思想、新方法：</span>The vegetarian burger was an innovation which was rapidly exported to Britain. 素食漢堡是一種迅速傳到了英國的新開發食品。/ recent innovations in steel-making technology / innovations in machinery and instruments 機械和工具方面的創新 / 新近的鍊鋼技術革新 / scientific, technical, technological innovation 科學創新；工藝革新；技術創新等 / methodological innovation 方法上的創新 / His last novel pays tribute to Joyce's stylistic innovations. 他的上一部小說是向喬伊斯的風格創新的致敬之作。/ She believed she had come up with one of the greatest innovations of modern times. 她相信自己提出的革新思想是當代最偉大的思想之一。/ Many innovations were introduced by the 1919 Act. 很多創新都是由1919年的法案引入的。/ technological innovations designed to save energy 旨在節約能源的技術革新

☀ <span class="category">**建立、開創的動詞：**</span>        
<span class="vocabulary">**establish**</span> [ɪˈstæblɪʃ]
<span class="definition">vt. 建立、建立（一個組織機構、一種制度等）：</span>The committee was established in 1912. 這個委員會創立於1912年。/ The League was formally established in 1920. 該聯盟正式成立於1920年。/ He was appointed to the newly established Department of the Environment. 他被派到新成立的環境部門工作。/ The School was established in 1989 by an Italian professor. 這所學院由一名義大利教授於1989年建立。/ The new treaty establishes a free trade zone. 新條約設立了自由貿易區。/ The Internet was originally established by scientists to share information. 最初科學家創立網際網路是為了共享資訊。/ The UN has established detailed criteria for who should be allowed to vote. 聯合國已經制定了有關投票資格的標準細則。<span class="definition">vt. 與其他人、群體或國家建立關係（尤指正式關係）：</span>The school has established a successful relationship with the local community. 這所學校與當地社群建立了良好的關係。/ We had already established contact with the museum. 我們已經和那家博物館建立了聯絡。/ The two countries agreed to establish full diplomatic relations. 兩國同意確立全面的外加關係。<span class="definition">vt. ~ sb/sth/yourself (in sth) (as sth) 確立（地位、聲譽）、站穩腳跟（使別人接受你並尊敬你）：</span>His position in the organization is now firmly established. 他在組織中的地位現已牢固確立了。/ He has established himself as a pivotal figure in US politics. 他已經確立了自己在美國政界的核心地位。/ The exhibition helped her establish herself as an artist. 這次展覽有助於確立她作為畫家的地位。/ He has now established his reputation as a popular musician. 他現在已經成為廣受歡迎的音樂家。/ By then she was established as a star. 那時她作為明星的地位已經確立。/ This is going to be the show where up-and-coming comedians will establish themselves. 這將是大有前途的喜劇演員們讓自己獲得認可的節目。/ He has just set up his own business but it will take him a while to get established. 他剛建立起自己的公司，但要站穩腳跟還得花上一段時間。/ We have now clearly established ourselves as the leader in the market. 我們現已明顯確立了自己在市場中的領導地位。/ It was this campaign that established the paper's reputation. 正是這場運動確立了這家報紙的聲譽。/ By the 7th century Buddhism was fully established in Japan. 到7世紀，佛教在日本的地位已經確立起來了。/ The festival has become established as one of the town's annual events. 這個節日已被確立為該鎮的年度盛事之一。/ Traditions get established over time. 傳統是隨著時間的推移而得到認可的。/ We have successfully established clear rules that most students accept. 我們成功制定了為大多數學生所接受的明確規定。/ a previously established formula 先前確立的方案
           
<span class="vocabulary">**cultivate**</span> [ˈkʌltɪveɪt]
<span class="definition">vt. [有時貶] 建立友誼、獲得支援：</span>He purposely tried to cultivate good relations with the press. 他特意設法與新聞界搞好關係。/ It helps if you go out of your way to cultivate the local people. 主動結交當地人大有好處。/ Howe carefully cultivated Daniel C. Roper, the Assistant Postmaster General. 豪謹慎地和郵政副部長丹尼爾·C.羅珀拉近關係。/ Estonia has done much to cultivate the friendship of western European countries. 愛沙尼亞努力發展與西歐國家的友誼。<span class="definition">vt. 逐漸形成某種態度、言行方式、形象、技能等：</span>She cultivated an air of sophistication. 她養成了一派精明練達的氣度。/ He has written eight books and has cultivated the image of an elder statesman. 他已經寫了八本書，樹立起了其政界元老的形象。/ This modern image is actively cultivated by the company. 公司積極塑造其現代形象。/ Cultivating a positive mental attitude towards yourself can reap tremendous benefits. 培養一種自信的積極心態會讓人受益匪淺。
           
<span class="vocabulary">**engage**</span> [ɪnˈgeɪdʒ]
<span class="definition">vi. ~ (with sth/sb) to become involved with and try to understand sth/sb 與某事物或某人建立密切關係並盡力理解：</span>She had vowed to go out of her way to engage with the Irish people at local community level. 她已經發誓要努力和當地的愛爾蘭人建立關係。

<span class="vocabulary">**found**</span> [faʊnd] 
<span class="definition">vt. 1（尤指透過提供資金來）建立或創辦（例如建立一個組織或機構），相當於establish：</span>to found a club, to found a company, to found a college 創辦俱樂部；創辦公司；創辦學院 <span class="definition">2 建立或興建（一個城鎮或國家）：</span>to found a town 建立城鎮 <span class="definition">3 found sth (on sth) 常用被動式，把…基於…，把…建立在…的基礎上：</span>Their marriage was founded on love. 他們的婚姻建立在愛情的基礎上。
           
<span class="vocabulary">**erect**</span> [ɪˈrekt]
<span class="definition">vt. [正式] 建立機構；建立體制；創立理論：</span>Japanese proprietors are erecting a complex infrastructure of political influence throughout America. 日本商人正在美國各地建立複雜的、具有政治影響力的基礎結構。/ He erected a new doctrine of precedent. 他創立了一套新的判例原則。
           
<span class="vocabulary">**forge**</span> [fɔ:dʒ; 美 fɔ:rdʒ]
<span class="definition">vt. 艱苦幹成某事、努力加強某事（使此事持續長久）。如建立、開創：</span>a move to forge new links between management and workers 努力建立勞資新關係的措施 / Strategic alliances are being forged with major European companies. 正與歐洲主要公司設法結成戰略同盟。/ The Prime Minister is determined to forge a good relationship with America's new leader. 首相決意要和美國的新任領導人建立良好的關係。/ They agreed to forge closer economic ties. 他們同意建立更加緊密的經濟聯絡。/ She forged a new career in the music business. 她在樂壇上另創一番新事業。/ The project will help inmates forge new careers. 這項計劃會幫助犯人開始新的職業。/ Tito forged a unique model of communism after breaking with Stalin in 1948. 1948 年鐵托在和斯大林決裂後建立了一種獨特的共產主義模式。

<span class="vocabulary">**set**</span> [set] 
<span class="definition">vt. 1 樹立榜樣或標準（以使其他人仿效該榜樣或達到該標準）：</span>to set a high standard 設定一個高標準 / to set a good example 樹立一個好榜樣 / to set new record 創造新的紀錄 / to set a precedent 開先例 / to set a new fashion 開創一種新時尚 <span class="definition">2 設定目標：</span>I’ve set myself clear objectives. 我已經給自己設定了明確的目標。<span class="definition">3 設定某個價值：</span>She sets a high value on autonomy. 她高度重視自主權。

<span class="vocabulary">**begin**</span> [bɪ'ɡɪn] 
<span class="definition">vt.＆vi. 第一次啟動，或使某事物第一次啟動，如建立、創辦、創始。是一個基礎用詞：</span>The school began in 1920. 這所學校建立於1920年。/ to begin a new magazine 創辦一份新雜誌

<span class="vocabulary">**start**</span> [stɑːt] 
<span class="definition">vt.＆vi. start (sth) (up) 創辦（機構等）。也是一個基礎用詞，與begin一般可互換：</span>There are a lot of small businesses starting up in that area. 小型企業在那一地區大量湧現。/ to start a business 開辦一家公司

<span class="vocabulary">**form**</span> [fɔ:m] 
<span class="definition">vt.＆vi. 建立一個組織（如委員會、公司等），或在此組織內集合（或團結在一起）。與found有時可互換：</span>to form a band, to form a study group, to form a party, to form a government 組建樂隊；組建研究小組；組建政黨；組建政府

<span class="vocabulary">**raise**</span> [reɪz] 
<span class="definition">vt. 透過籌措資金或召集人員的方式來組建：</span>He set about raising an army. 他著手組建一支部隊。
           
<span class="vocabulary">**originate**</span> [əˈrɪdʒɪneɪt]
<span class="definition">vt. [正式] 創立、建立、發明新事物：</span>to originate an idea 創立一種思想 / Locke originated this theory in the 17th century. 洛克於17世紀創立了這個理論。

<span class="vocabulary">**pioneer**</span> [͵paɪə'nɪə] 
<span class="definition">vt. 當開拓者或先鋒（去做某事、發現或使用新東西等）：</span>a new technique pioneered by surgeons in a London hospital 由倫敦一家醫院的外科醫生開發使用的新技術
           
<span class="vocabulary">**inaugurate**</span> [ɪˈnɔ:gjəreɪt]
<span class="definition">vt. [正式] 引入一個新的發展階段或重要變革，如開創、開始：</span>The moon landing inaugurated a new era in space exploration. 登陸月球開創了太空探索的新紀元。/ Pan Am inaugurated the first scheduled international flight. 泛美航空公司開通了第一條國際定期航班線路。
           
<span class="vocabulary">**charter**</span> [ˈtʃɑ:tə(r); 美 ˈtʃɑ:rt-]
<span class="definition">vt. 正式宣告設立一個新機構、城鎮或大學，並授予其特定權利，即特許設立：</span>瞭解即可

相關詞義延伸：
· inaugurate還可指為某人舉行就職典禮。也可指為建築物舉行落成儀式，或為機構舉行建立儀式。亦可指召開會議。
           
周邊詞彙補充：           
· recruit可指透過招募新人來組建一支軍隊、團隊等；
· renew可指重新建立關係。
