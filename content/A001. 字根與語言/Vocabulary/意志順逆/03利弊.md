☀ <span class="category">**利益、好處的名詞：**</span>
<span class="vocabulary">**good**</span> [ɡʊd] 
<span class="definition">n. [U] 好處、益處、用處。是一個簡單用詞：</span>Cuts have been made for the good of the company. 實行裁減是為了公司的利益。/ I’m only telling you this for your own good. 我把這事告訴你只是為你好。/ It will do you good to get out of the house more often. 常到戶外走走對你有好處。/ What’s the good of earning all that money if you don’t have time to enjoy it? 要是沒時間去享受，賺這麼多錢有什麼用？ 

<span class="vocabulary">**benefit**</span> ['benɪfɪt] 
<span class="definition">n. [U, C]（某事物所給予你的）優勢；（某事物所具有的）用處或益處。是此義的常規用詞，最為常用：</span>I’ve had the benefit of a good education. 我得益於受過良好教育。/ He couldn’t see the benefit of arguing any longer. 他看不出再爭論下去有什麼好處。/ mutual benefit 互惠互利 / practical benefit 實惠 / public benefit 公共利益 / personal benefit 個人利益 / clinical, economic, material, nutritional, social benefit 臨床效用；經濟效益；物質利益；營養價值；社會效益 / fitness, health benefit 對身體的益處
           
<span class="vocabulary">**boon**</span> [bu:n]
<span class="definition">n. [C] ~ (to/for sb) 非常有用的東西、給生活帶來便利的事物、很大的益處：</span>This battery booster is a boon for photographers. 這種電池充電器是攝影工作者的好幫手。/ These machines have proved a real boon to disabled people. 事實證明這些機器能為殘疾人提供很大便利。/ The new software will prove a boon to Internet users. 這種新軟體將會對網際網路使用者大有益處。/ It is for this reason that television proves such a boon to so many people. 就是因為這個原因，電視才會給這麼多人帶來如此大的便利。/ The building scheme comes as an enormous boon for the building industry. 該建築方案對建築行業帶來很大益處。/ High productivity has been a boon for corporate profits. 生產效率高使公司獲益頗豐。

<span class="vocabulary">**interest**</span> ['ɪntrɪst] 
<span class="definition">n. 1 [C, U] 作可數名詞時常用複數，指好處，利益：</span>It’s not in your best interests to let your boss know you’re looking for a new job. 讓你的老闆知道你想跳槽對你不利。/ national, public interest 國家利益；公眾利益 / class, sectional interest 階級利益；群體利益 / corporate, economic, political, strategic interest 公司利益；經濟利益；政治利益；戰略利益 / interest group 利益集團 / Although he was sometimes too strict with his children, he had their best interests at heart. 儘管他有時對孩子們過於嚴厲，但他是為他們著想。<span class="definition">2 [C, U] interest (in sth) 指一個人、國家或組織在一次事件或一種情況中所擁有的利益關係，利害關係：</span>I should, at this point, declare my interest. 到了這個時候，我應該申明我的利益關係。
           
<span class="vocabulary">**stake**</span> [steɪk]
<span class="definition">n. [sing.] ~ in sth（在公司、計劃等中的）重大利益，重大利害關係：</span>She has a personal stake in the success of the play. 這出戏成功與否對她個人有重大利害關係。/ He has a personal stake in the outcome of the war. 戰爭的結果與他有切身的利害關係。/ Many young people no longer feel they have a stake in society. 很多年輕人不再覺得他們與社會休慼相關。/ He was eager to return to a more entrepreneurial role in which he had a big financial stake in his own efforts. 他渴望回到一個更多參與創業的角色，那樣他所付出的努力會與自己的經濟利益息息相關。/ Detectives now believe the Mafia also had a stake in the plot and killed him when it went wrong. 偵探們現在相信黑手黨在這個陰謀中也有利害關係，並在事敗後殺了他。

<span class="vocabulary">**gain**</span> [ɡeɪn] 
<span class="definition">n. [C] 好處，利益，改進之處，與lose相反：</span>efficiency gains 效率提高 / These policies have resulted in great gains in public health. 這些政策使公共衛生得到極大改進。/ Our loss is their gain. 我們之所失即他們之所得。/ commercial, economic, material gain 商業利益；經濟收益；物質利益 / political gain 政治收益 / military, territorial gain 軍事收益；領土的擴大 / This change in the tax system will mean big gains for some companies. 稅制上的這一變化將會給一些公司帶來很大收益。
           
<span class="vocabulary">**mileage**</span> [ˈmaɪlɪdʒ]
<span class="definition">n. [U] [非正式]（從某個事件或情況中得到的）好處（或利益）的數量：</span>I don't think the press can get any more mileage out of that story. 我不認為報界可以再從那件事中撈到什麼好處。/ It's obviously important to get as much mileage out of the convention as possible. 很明顯，從這項協議中獲取儘可能多的利益十分重要。/ The administration clearly decided there was no mileage in provoking a huge row with Congress. 顯然，政府認定和國會大起衝突是沒有好處的。

<span class="vocabulary">**bonus**</span> ['bəʊnəs] 
<span class="definition">n. [C] 超出期待（即比期待的更多或更好）的任何令人愉悅的事物，即意外收穫：</span>Being able to walk to work is an added bonus of the new job. 能夠步行去上班是這份新工作額外的好處。/ The house is comfortable, and as an added bonus, it’s near my work. 這所房子很舒適，而且還有額外的好處——離我工作的地方很近。/ The sunshine on the final day was a welcome bonus for the spectators. 決賽當天陽光明媚，觀眾心情格外好。

<span class="vocabulary">**profit**</span> ['prɒfɪt] 
<span class="definition">n. [U] 正式用法，指從做某事中所獲得的好處，裨益。不太常用：</span>Future lawyers could study this text with profit. 未來的律師研讀這一文字也許會有裨益。
           
<span class="vocabulary">**dividends**</span> [ˈdɪvɪdend]
<span class="definition">n. [pl.] pay ~s 某事日後帶來好處或優勢，即有回報：</span>Steps taken now to maximize your health will pay dividends later on. 現在採取措施增強體質會使你日後受益。/ Her hard work paid dividends when she won the school dancing competition. 她在學校舞蹈比賽中勝出，她的努力得到了回報。/ economic, political dividends 經濟收益；政治收益 / peace dividends 和平紅利 / The company reaped rich dividends with its new strategy. 公司推出新戰略後獲得了豐厚的回報。/ The chain's investment in new stores is bringing dividends in new customers. 連鎖企業投資開設新店吸引了新顧客，帶來了收益。

<span class="vocabulary">**carrot**</span> ['kærət] 
<span class="definition">n. [C]（為說服某人，從而讓其去做某事）所許諾的好處或報酬，相當於incentive：</span>They are holding out a carrot of $120 million in economic aid. 他們許諾給予1.2億美元的經濟援助。/ They dangled the carrot of a large salary in front of me. 他們以高薪引誘我。/ to adopt the carrot-and-stick approach 採取威逼利誘的手段
           
<span class="vocabulary">**spoil**</span> [spɔɪl]
<span class="definition">n. [pl.] spoils 可指成功所帶來的好處：</span>the spoils of high office 身居高位的連帶利益 / Competing warlords and foreign powers scrambled for political spoils. 相互爭鬥的軍閥及外國列強們正在爭奪政治上的收益。
           
<span class="vocabulary">**blessing**</span> [ˈblesɪŋ]
<span class="definition">n. [C] 好事、有益之事、福氣、福祉：</span>Lack of traffic is one of the blessings of country life. 往來車輛少是鄉村生活的一大好處。/ It's a blessing that nobody was in the house at the time. 幸好當時屋子裡沒人。/ Rivers are a blessing for an agricultural country. 河流對於一個農業國家來說是一種恩賜。/ the blessings of prosperity 繁榮昌盛的福祉 / Some would argue this was no burden in fact, and that she should count her blessings. 有些人可能會說這事實上算不上什麼負擔，她應該想想自己有多幸運。/ a blessing in disguise(= a thing that seems unfortunate, but is later seen to be fortunate) 看似禍實是福 / The failure to conclude the trade talks last December could prove a blessing in disguise. 去年十二月份貿易談判中止可能因禍得福。/ great, real blessing 很大的福氣 / A TV can be a real blessing for old people. 對於老年人來說，有一臺電視機可算是一大好事了。/ mixed blessing（= a thing that has advantages and disadvantages）利弊兼有的事 / For ordinary Italians, Sunday's news probably amounts to a mixed blessing. 對普通的義大利人民來說，星期天的新聞可以說是悲喜交加。/ count your blessings(= to be grateful for the good things you have) 知足感恩
 
<span class="vocabulary">**self**</span> [self] 
<span class="definition">n. [U] [正式] 自己（而非他人）的利益或享樂，也可叫做私心：</span>She didn’t do it for any reason of self. 她那樣做決不是出於私心。
          
<span class="vocabulary">**trappings**</span> [ˈtræpɪŋz]
<span class="definition">n. [pl.] [正式，尤貶] ~ (of sth) （權力、財富、地位、工作等帶來的）額外實惠：</span>The family were in government for several generations and evidently loved the trappings of power. 那個家族好幾代人都曾在政府任職，顯然對隨權力而來的各種額外實惠情有獨鍾。/ They enjoyed all the trappings of wealth. 他們享有所有象徵財富的東西。/ She scorns the visible trappings of success, preferring to live unnoticed. 她鄙視將成功炫耀於人，更願意默默無聞地生活。/ the trappings of fame, the trappings of success名聲的標誌；成功的標誌

☀ <span class="category">**有益、有好處的形容詞：**</span>
<span class="vocabulary">**good**</span> [ɡʊd] 
<span class="definition">adj. 1 good (for sb/sth) 對某人或某事物有用或有益的：</span>Too much sun isn’t good for you. 曬太陽太多對你並沒有好處。/ It’s probably good for you to get some criticism now and then. 偶爾受點批評或許對你還有好處。<span class="definition">2 no good doing sth / no good to sb 沒有用處或沒有益處的：</span>It’s no good complaining--they never listen. 抱怨毫無用處——他們根本不聽。/ This book is no good to me: I need the new edition. 這本書對我沒用，我需要新版本的。 

<span class="vocabulary">**beneficial**</span> [͵benɪ'fɪʃl] 
<span class="definition">adj. beneficial (to sth/sb) 對於改善一種情況有利的，有益的，有幫助的，有用的，相當於advantageous或favourable：</span>A good diet is beneficial to health. 良好的飲食有益於健康。/ The arrangement was mutually beneficial. 這種安排對彼此都有利。/ economically, environmentally, socially beneficial 對經濟有益；對環境有益；對社會有益 / I think it would be beneficial for each committee member to have a copy. 我認為委員會成員人手一份比較好。
                                 
<span class="vocabulary">**favourable**</span> [ˈfeɪvərəbl]
<span class="definition">adj. ~ (to/for sb/sth)（條件）有利的、有助於…的：</span>The terms of the agreement are favourable to both sides. 協議條款對雙方都有利。/ favourable economic conditions 有利的經濟環境 / The court's verdict was favourable to their client. 法庭的裁決對他們的當事人有利。/ It's believed the conditions in which the elections are being held are too favourable to the government. 人們認為舉行選舉的條件對政府過於有利。/ favourable weather conditions 有利的天氣狀況
           
<span class="vocabulary">**fertile**</span> [ˈfɜ:taɪl; 美 ˈfɜ:rtl]
<span class="definition">adj. [常用於名詞前]（環境或條件）能產生好結果的，有助於…的：</span>a fertile partnership 有成效的合夥關係 / The region at the time was fertile ground for revolutionary movements (= there were the necessary conditions for them to develop easily). 當時該地區是革命運動的沃土。/ a fertile breeding ground for this kind of violent racism 易於滋生這種極端種族主義的溫床

<span class="vocabulary">**profitable**</span> [ˈprɒfɪtəbl; 美 ˈprɑ:f-]
<span class="definition">adj. 對某人有益的、有好處的：</span>She spent a profitable afternoon in the library. 她在圖書館待了一個下午，頗有收穫。/ Some churches have found it profitable to hold services during the week. 有些教堂發現在非週末的日子舉行禮拜大有益處。/ Cooperation could be mutually profitable. 合作可能對雙方都有益。/ collaboration which leads to a profitable exchange of personnel and ideas 有利於人員和觀念交流的合作
 
<span class="vocabulary">**rewarding**</span> [rɪˈwɔ:dɪŋ; 美 -ˈwɔ:rd-]
<span class="definition">adj.（活動、經歷等）值得做的；有益的；有意義的：</span>rewarding experience, rewarding job 有益的經歷；有益的工作 / a career which she found stimulating and rewarding 她覺得刺激而有意義的職業 / I found it immensely rewarding working with the less able children. 為能力差的孩子工作我覺得是非常值得的。/ I did not expect the job to be intrinsically rewarding 我並沒有指望這份工作本身會有什麼好處。a mutually rewarding partnership 互惠的夥伴關係

<span class="vocabulary">**advantageous**</span> [ˌædvənˈteɪdʒəs]
<span class="definition">adj. ~ (to sb)（在某種情況中）有利的、有好處的：</span>Free exchange of goods was advantageous to all. 商品自由交換對各方都有利。/ an opportunity to purchase a set of tools at very advantageous prices 以非常優惠的價格購買一套工具的機會 / It may be advantageous to buy in bulk. 批次購買也許會有利。/ Membership of the union could prove advantageous. 協會的會員資格可能會有幫助。/ Lower fares make it advantageous to travel in winter. 較低的交通費使冬季旅行更有利。/ You may find it advantageous to wait a few weeks before replying. 你會發現等幾周再作答覆是很有好處的。/ This trade arrangement could be mutually advantageous. 這項貿易協定可對雙方都有利。/ This plan could be advantageous for people on low incomes. 這—計劃可能會對低收入者有利。/ The new tax system is advantageous to small businesses.新稅制對小企業有利。

<span class="vocabulary">**useful**</span> ['ju:sfl] 
<span class="definition">adj. useful (to do sth) / useful (to sb) / useful (for sth/for doing sth) 能夠幫助你去做（或實現）自己想做的事的，即有用的，有幫助的，有益的：</span>useful tool 有用的工具 / It can be useful to write a short summary of your argument first. 先把你的論點綱要寫下來可能會有幫助。/ It was useful as a short-term measure. 作為短期措施它是有用的。/ He might be useful to us. 我們也許用得上他。/ This information could prove useful. 這條資訊往後也許有用。
           
<span class="vocabulary">**conducive**</span> [kənˈdju:sɪv; 美 -ˈdu:s-]
<span class="definition">adj. ~ to sth 使某事容易（或有可能）發生的。如有助（於…）的；有益（於…）的：</span>Daylight is highly conducive to good plant growth. 日照非常有助於植物的良好生長。/ The noise was hardly conducive to a good night's sleep. 這些噪聲讓人晚上難以睡好覺。/ Make your bedroom as conducive to sleep as possible. 儘量把臥室佈置得適宜睡眠。/ an environment conducive to learning 有助於學習的環境 / Sometimes the home environment just isn't conducive to reading. 有時候，家裡的環境確實不適合看書。/ Chairs in rows are not as conducive to discussion as chairs arranged in a circle. 椅子成排擺放不如成圈擺放便於討論。
                  
<span class="vocabulary">**informative**</span> [ɪnˈfɔ:mətɪv; 美 -ˈfɔ:rm-]
<span class="definition">adj. 提供有用資訊的：</span>The adverts are not very informative. 這些廣告並沒有包含太多有用資訊。/ 'Holidays That Don't Cost the Earth' is a lively, informative read.《綠色環保的假期》是本生動有趣、增長見聞的讀物。/ The talk was both informative and entertaining. 這次談話既長見識又饒有趣味。/ The survey proved highly informative. 事實證明該項調查提供了極其豐富的資訊。/ The book is extremely informative about life in Roman times. 該書包含大量關於羅馬時代生活的資訊。

<span class="vocabulary">**instructive**</span> [ɪnˈstrʌktɪv]
<span class="definition">adj. 能給人提供很多有用資訊的：</span>a most instructive experience 獲益良多的經歷 / It was a most instructive day. 那一天獲益良多。

<span class="vocabulary">**positive**</span> ['pɒzɪtɪv] 
<span class="definition">adj. 1 以解決問題為目的的，或產生成功的結果的，即積極的，建設性的：</span>to take positive action 採取積極行動 / to take positive steps to deal with the problem 採取積極步驟來解決這個問題 / to make a positive decision 做出積極的決定 / positive changes 積極的改變 <span class="definition">2 好的，有用的：</span>to make a positive contribution to the discussion 在討論中獻計獻策 / to have a positive influence on him 對他有積極的影響 / This policy has had a positive effect on employment. 這項政策對就業有積極影響。

<span class="vocabulary">**friendly**</span> ['frendlɪ] 
<span class="definition">adj. 常構成複合形容詞，有幫助的且易於使用的；對某人或某事物有幫助或無害的：</span>This software is much friendlier than the previous version. 這個軟體比之前的版本好用得多。/ ozone-friendly cleaning materials 對臭氧無害的清潔材料 / environmentally friendly cleaning products 環保型清潔用品
        
<span class="vocabulary">**weighted**</span> [ˈweɪtɪd]
<span class="definition">adj. [名詞前不可用] ~ towards sb/sth / ~ in favour of sb/sth 有利於：</span>The proposal is weighted towards smaller businesses. 這項提議對小型企業有利。/ measures that are weighted towards/toward small investors 有利於小投資者的措施 / The current electoral law is still heavily weighted in favour of the ruling party. 當前的選舉法仍然對執政黨非常有利。/ The course is heavily weighted in favour of engineering. 這個課程非常側重於工程學。
           
<span class="vocabulary">**loaded**</span> [ˈləʊdɪd; 美 ˈloʊd-]
<span class="definition">adj. ~ in favour of sb/sth（不公平地）對…有利：</span>a system that is loaded in favour of the young (= gives them an advantage) 對年輕人有利的體制

☀ <span class="category">**有益處、有利的動詞：**</span>
<span class="vocabulary">**benefit**</span> ['benɪfɪt] 
<span class="definition">vt.（在某個方面）對於某人（或對於改善其生活）有用，有好處：</span>The new law clearly benefits those earning the most money. 新法律顯然使那些收入最高的人受益。<span class="definition">vi. benefit (from/by sth) 由於某事物而處於一種更好的境況中，即得益於：</span>We benefited directly from the reorganization. 我們從重組中直接受益。

<span class="vocabulary">**pay**</span> [peɪ] 
<span class="definition">vt.＆vi.（對某人來說）受益：</span>Crime doesn’t pay. 犯罪是划不來的。/ It would probably pay you to hire an accountant. 聘一名會計師或許對你有好處。

<span class="vocabulary">**favour**</span> ['feɪvə]（美favor）
<span class="definition">vt. 為某個人、某個群體等提供合適的條件，即有助於，有利於。不太常用：</span>The warm climate favours many types of tropical plants. 溫暖的氣候對多種熱帶植物生長有利。

<span class="vocabulary">**laugh**</span> [lɑːf] 
<span class="definition">vi. [非正式] be laughing 用於表示（尤指因為已成功做了某事而）處於一個非常有利的地位：</span>If we win the next game we’ll be laughing. 要是贏了下一場比賽，我們就佔優勢了。

<span class="vocabulary">**serve**</span> [sə:v] 
<span class="definition">vt. 對某人實現或達成…有用；能滿足某人…的需要：</span>These experiments serve no useful purpose. 這些實驗沒有任何實際意義。/ to best serve the needs of customers 最能滿足顧客的需要 / His linguistic ability served him well in his chosen profession. 他的語言能力對他所選擇的職業有很大幫助。

<span class="vocabulary">**profit**</span> ['prɒfɪt]

<span class="definition">adj. 造成一些問題的；造成某事物的損失的：</span>costly failure 造成重大損失的失敗 / Mining can be costly in terms of lives (= too many people can die). 採礦有時會造成重大的生命損失。/ These teaching methods are too costly in terms of staff resources. 這些教學方法需要耗費太多的師資。/ Psychometric tests can save organizations from grim and costly mistakes. 心理測試能夠使組織機構避免犯下損失慘重的大錯。/ This sort of scandal in international banking has been politically costly. 國際銀行業的這種醜聞一直以來都會造成極其惡劣的政治影響。/ The candidate's mistake proved embarrassing and politically costly. 事實證明候選人的這個錯誤不僅使他難堪，而且政治代價高昂。/ The six-month delay will be costly for the company.拖延半年對公司來說會造成嚴重損失。/ This process is costly in computer time. 這個過程要耗費大量的計算機時間。/ These measures could be costly to employers.這些措施對僱主來說可能會代價很高。

<span class="vocabulary">**ill**</span> [ɪl] 
<span class="definition">adj. 常作前置定語，指壞的，有害的：</span>ill health 健康狀況不佳 / ill effects 不良影響 / a man of ill repute 名聲不好的人
           
<span class="vocabulary">**undesirable**</span> [ˌʌndɪˈzaɪərəbl]
<span class="definition">adj.（某事）有害的；不得人心的：</span>undesirable consequences, undesirable effects 不良後果；不良影響 / Inflation is considered to be undesirable because of its adverse effects on income distribution. 通貨膨脹被認為是有害的，因為它會對收入分配產生不利影響。/ It would be highly undesirable to increase class sizes further. 再增加班級人數，是大家都極不願意的。/ prostitution and other undesirable practices 賣淫和其他不良勾當 / a situation which is highly undesirable 非常麻煩的情況 / It is clearly undesirable for the issue to be ignored. 這個問題顯然不容忽視。/ Why is he mixing with socially undesirable types? 他為什麼與一些不三不四的人混在一起?/ It was felt that big cars were socially and economically undesirable. 人們發現從社會和經濟兩方面考慮大轎車不值得購買。

<span class="vocabulary">**weighted**</span> [ˈweɪtɪd]
<span class="definition">adj. [名詞前不可用] ~ against sb/sth 不利於：</span>They say the peace process is so heavily weighted against them that it will never achieve results. 他們說和平程序對他們十分不利，根本不會取得任何成果。/ This new legislation is weighted against the small farmer. 新的立法對小農戶不利。/ Everything seemed weighted against them. 一切似乎都與他們過不去。
           
<span class="vocabulary">**loaded**</span> [ˈləʊdɪd; 美 ˈloʊd-]
<span class="definition">adj. ~ against sb/sth（不公平地）對…不利：</span>The article was heavily loaded against Morrissey. 文章對莫里西非常不利。/ The odds were slightly loaded against us. 情況對我們稍有不利。
           
<span class="vocabulary">**hostile**</span> [ˈhɒstaɪl; 美 ˈhɑ:stl; -taɪl]
<span class="definition">adj.（情況、形勢等）（對某事的發生或達成）有阻礙的、不利的：</span>hostile conditions for plants to grow in 不利於植物生長的環境 / some of the most hostile climatic conditions in the world 世界上最惡劣的幾種氣候條件 / If this round of talks fails, the world's trading environment is likely to become increasingly hostile. 如果這一輪談判破裂了，世界貿易環境可能會變得越來越糟糕。

<span class="vocabulary">**hurt**</span> [hə:t] 
<span class="definition">vt. 對…有不良影響：</span>Many people on low incomes will be hurt by the government’s plans. 很多低收入的人將受到政府這些方案的打擊。
           
<span class="vocabulary">**backfire**</span> [ˌbækˈfaɪə(r)]
<span class="definition">vi. ~ (on sb) 產生事與願違的不良（或危險）後果：</span>Unfortunately the plan backfired. 不幸的是，計劃產生了適得其反的結果。/ The President's tactics could backfire. 總統的策略可能會適得其反。/ It all backfired on me! 一切都與我的願望背道而馳！/ The surprise I had planned backfired on me. 我設計好給人一個驚喜，結果卻事與願違。/ He pretended to be sick, a ploy which ultimately backfired. 他假裝生病，到頭來卻害了自己。

<span class="vocabulary">**against**</span> [ə'ɡenst, ə'ɡeɪnst] 
<span class="definition">prep. 對…不利：</span>The evidence is against him. 證據對他不利。/ Her age is against her. 她的年齡對她不利。
