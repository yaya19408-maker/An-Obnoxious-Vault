☀ <span class="category">**緊張、恐慌、焦慮、煩躁的形容詞：**</span>
<span class="vocabulary">**nervous**</span> ['nə:vəs] 
<span class="definition">adj. 1 nervous (about/of sth)（對某事）焦慮的或惶恐的、擔憂的。是一個常規用詞：</span>Sit down--you’re making me nervous! 坐下吧——你站著讓我很緊張！/ Consumers are very nervous about the future. 消費者對未來非常憂慮。/ The horse may be nervous of cars. 這匹馬可能害怕汽車。/ I felt really nervous before the interview. 面試前我感到惶恐不安。/ a nervous glance 膽怯的一瞥 <span class="definition">2 易於擔心或害怕的，即神經質的，膽怯的：</span>She was a nervous girl. 她是個神經兮兮的女孩兒。

<span class="vocabulary">**tense**</span> [tens] 
<span class="definition">adj.（人）神經緊張的，擔心的，不能放鬆的。可以是某種場景中臨時性的緊張，也可以是長期的性格上的神經兮兮。此時相當於nervous，不過不如nervous常用：</span>He’s a very tense person. 他是個神經非常緊張的人。/ I was feeling a little tense and restless. 我感到有點兒緊張不安。/ to be tense about the situation 對形勢感到緊張 
      
<span class="vocabulary">**taut**</span> [tɔ:t]
<span class="definition">adj. 顯得緊張或焦慮的：</span>Her face was taut and pale. 她神色緊張，臉色蒼白。/ Ben sat up quickly, his face taut and terrified. 本一骨碌坐了起來，表情緊張而驚恐。/ Little by little she lost the taut, strained air of perpetual anxiety. 長期焦慮帶來的緊張而疲憊的神色從她臉上慢慢消失了。/ a voice taut with anger氣得發緊的聲音

<span class="vocabulary">**strained**</span> [streɪnd]
<span class="definition">adj. 神色緊張憂慮的、表現出壓力的。相當於tense：</span>She looked a little pale and strained. 她看起來面色有點蒼白，神情憂慮。/ She looked rather strained and miserable. 她看起來很是緊張和可憐。/ Her face looked strained and weary. 她的臉色顯得憔悴疲憊。/ He spoke in a low, strained voice. 他焦慮地低聲說話。/ Gil sensed something wrong from her father's strained voice. 吉爾從父親緊張的聲音中感覺到出了什麼事了。
          
<span class="vocabulary">**stressed**</span> [strest]
<span class="definition">adj. [名詞前不可用]（也可用非正式的stressed out）太焦慮勞累而不能放鬆的：</span>Work out what situations or people make you feel stressed and avoid them. 弄清是什麼狀況或什麼人讓你焦慮不安，然後避開他們。/ She's just making herself more stressed. 她只是讓自已更加緊張罷了。/ I was just totally stressed out and afraid I couldn't continue. 那時我整個人心力交瘁，擔心自己堅持不下去了。/ She felt overly stressed and needed to calm down.（overly尤為美式用法）她感到自己過度緊張，需要鎮定下來。/ He's stressed out about money. 他因為錢的問題而焦慮不安。

<span class="vocabulary">**anxious**</span> ['æŋkʃəs] 
<span class="definition">adj. 1 anxious (about sth) / anxious (for sb) 感到焦慮的，憂慮的，擔心的：</span>Parents are naturally anxious for their children. 父母自然為兒女擔心。/ We are extremely anxious for her safety. 我們極為擔心她的安全。/ She was watching the clock and becoming increasingly anxious. 她盯著鍾，越來越焦慮不安。/ Students are anxious about getting work after graduation. 學生對畢業後找工作感到擔憂。<span class="definition">2 表現出憂慮的：</span>anxious expression 憂慮的表情
  
<span class="vocabulary">**preoccupied**</span> [priˈɒkjupaɪd; 美 -ˈɑ:k-]
<span class="definition">adj. ~ (with sth) 心事重重的：</span>She looked very preoccupied. 她看起來心事重重。/ He was too preoccupied with his own thoughts to notice anything wrong. 他只顧想著心事，沒注意到有什麼不對。

<span class="vocabulary">**uneasy**</span> [ʌnˈi:zi]
<span class="definition">adj. ~ (about sth/about doing sth)（某人）（對局勢）擔憂的、焦慮的；心神不定的、不自在的：</span>an uneasy laugh 不自然的大笑 He was beginning to feel distinctly uneasy about their visit. 他對他們的造訪明顯地感到不安起來。/ I felt distinctly uneasy about lending her so much money. 借給她這麼多錢，我明顯感到心神不寧。/ She felt uneasy about leaving the children with them. 把孩子託付給他們，她心裡七上八下的。/ We were starting to grow slightly uneasy. 我們開始有些不安起來。/ She always felt uneasy with her body shape. 她總是對自己的體形感到不安。

<span class="vocabulary">**disturbed**</span> [dɪˈstɜ:bd; 美 -ˈstɜ:rbd]
<span class="definition">adj.（對某事）心煩意亂的、心神不安的：</span>Doctors were disturbed that less than 30 percent of the patients were women. 醫生們焦慮不安，因為患者中有不到30%為女性。/ I was disturbed to hear that the selection committee originally decided not to send a British team to this year's Championships. 當我聽說選拔委員會原本決定不派英國隊去參加今年的錦標賽時，我覺得很不安。/ I was deeply disturbed and depressed by the news. 這訊息使我深感不安和沮喪。/ I felt vaguely disturbed by the incident. 這件事讓我感到隱約有些不安。
  
<span class="vocabulary">**troubled**</span> [ˈtrʌbld]
<span class="definition">adj.（人）憂慮的、焦慮的：</span>She looked into his troubled face. 她仔細打量著他那張佈滿愁容的臉。/ She was conscious of a troubled expression on Ann's face. 她注意到了安臉上露出了苦惱的表情。/ Rose sounded deeply troubled. 羅斯聽起來頗為煩惱。/ She still felt vaguely troubled by it all. 她仍然對這一切隱約有些憂心。/ He was deeply troubled by the news. 這個訊息使他非常不安。

<span class="vocabulary">**excited**</span> [ɪkˈsaɪtɪd]
<span class="definition">adj. 1 緊張不安且不能放鬆的，如受刺激的：</span>Some horses become excited when they're in traffic. 有些馬在車流中會受驚。<span class="definition">2 心煩的、憂慮的：</span>I don't think there's any reason to get excited about inflation. 我認為大可不必為通貨膨脹而心煩。

<span class="vocabulary">**uncomfortable**</span> [ʌn'kʌmfətəbl] 
<span class="definition">adj. 焦慮的、尷尬的、害怕的、不輕鬆自在的：</span>The way he looked at her made her distinctly uncomfortable. 他那樣看著她，使她明顯感覺不自在。/ He looked uncomfortable at the news. 他看上去對這一訊息感到不安。/ I had the uncomfortable feeling that it was my fault. 我內心惴惴不安，覺得那是我的錯。
                      
<span class="vocabulary">**self-conscious**</span>
<span class="definition">adj. ~ (about sth) （因顧慮自己的外表或表現）侷促不安的，不自然的：</span>He studied her in a way that made her very self-conscious. 他打量她的那種方式令她很不自在。/ He's always been self-conscious about being so short. 他老為自己身材矮小而覺得難為情。/ Bess was self-conscious about being shorter than her two friends. 貝絲因比她的兩個朋友個子都矮而感到很不自在。

<span class="vocabulary">**fussy**</span> [ˈfʌsi]
<span class="definition">adj.（做事時）緊張得發抖的，如侷促不安的：</span>a fussy manner 侷促不安的舉止 / the quick, fussy movements of her small hands 她的一雙小手快速而緊張不安的動作

<span class="vocabulary">**agitated**</span> [ˈædʒɪteɪtɪd]
<span class="definition">adj. 行為、動作或聲音中表現出焦慮不安的、煩惱的、激動的：</span>Calm down! Don't get so agitated. 冷靜下來！別那麼激動。/ Susan seemed agitated about something. 蘇珊似乎因為什麼事而煩躁不安。/ The man in the house was in a very excited and agitated state. 房子裡的那名男子情緒非常激動且焦灼不安。/ He sounded very agitated on the phone. 他在電話中聽起來十分焦慮。/ She's agitated about getting there on time. 她十分擔心能否按時到那裡。/ She started to grow agitated at the sight of the spider. 看到蜘蛛她就激動起來。/ By now he was in a very agitated state. 此前他一直焦慮不安。
           
<span class="vocabulary">**hysterical**</span> [hɪˈsterɪkl]
<span class="definition">adj. 一種不可控的極端激動、喊叫、大笑等的狀態，即歇斯底里的：</span>hysterical screams 歇斯底里的尖叫 / a hysterical giggle 無法控制的傻笑 / The audience was almost hysterical with laughter. 觀眾狂笑不已。/ He became almost hysterical when I told him. 我告訴他時，他幾乎要發瘋了。/ Let's not get hysterical. 咱們別太激動。/ She realized that she sounded hysterical. 她意識到她說話時有些歇斯底里。/ Calm down, you're getting hysterical. 冷靜點兒，你有點兒歇斯底里了。/ Police and bodyguards had to protect him as the almost hysterical crowds struggled to approach him. 當情緒幾近失控的人群拼命想接近他時，警察和保鏢不得不保護他。/ He made headlines and received hysterical hate mail. 他成了新聞人物，收到了瘋狂的攻擊性郵件。/ Her parents were hysterical with panic.（比喻用法）她的父母害怕得發狂了。/ He thought I was being a hysterical female.（貶）他覺得我當時像個歇斯底里的女人。

<span class="vocabulary">**tight**</span> [taɪt] 
<span class="definition">adj. 表情看起來（或嗓音聽起來）顯得緊張、焦慮、煩躁、忐忑、生氣等的：</span>‘I’m sorry,’ she said, with a tight smile.“對不起。”她勉強一笑說。

<span class="vocabulary">**precious**</span> ['preʃəs] 
<span class="definition">adj. [貶]（尤指人或其行為）非常正式的、誇張的且不自然的，如矯揉造作的，相當於affected：</span>Actors were too precious and neurotic. 演員們太做作，並且都神經兮兮的。 
           
<span class="vocabulary">**restless**</span> [ˈrestləs]
<span class="definition">adj. 1 因無聊或需要改變而坐立不安的，如煩躁的、不耐煩的：</span>restless mood 坐立不安的心情 / I was feeling a bit tense and restless. 我當時有點緊張，坐立不安。/ My father seemed very restless and excited. 我父親看起來坐立不安，激動不已。/ The audience was becoming restless. 觀眾開始不耐煩了。/ After five years in the job, he was beginning to feel restless. 這份工作幹了五年以後，他開始厭煩了。/ By 1982, she was restless and needed a new impetus for her talent. 到了1982年，她感到厭倦了，需要新的動力來激發她的才能。/ a major new initiative to placate the country's restless intellectuals 安撫國家不安分的知識分子的重大新舉措 / restless horse 煩躁的馬匹 / Owls were believed to be restless spirits who had returned to earth. 人們以前認為貓頭鷹是不安分的鬼魂重返世間。<span class="definition">2 沒有真正休息或睡眠的。相當於disturbed：</span>a restless night 不眠之夜 / a restless sleep 輾轉反側 / The shocking revelations of the 700-page report had caused him several restless nights. 這篇長達700頁的報告中披露的驚人資訊使他幾夜未眠。/ Hurt had spent a restless few hour on the plane from Paris. 赫特在從巴黎起飛的飛機上輾轉反側了幾個小時。
           
<span class="vocabulary">**edgy**</span> [ˈedʒi]
<span class="definition">adj. [非正式]（尤指對於將要發生的事）緊張的、煩躁不安的：</span>She's been very edgy lately. 她近來一直煩躁不安。/ She was all edgy that evening. 那天晚上她坐立不安。/ She had been a little edgy all day. 她一整天都有點兒焦躁。/ She was nervous and edgy, still chain-smoking. 她緊張不安，還在一根接一根地抽著煙。/ After the recent unrest there is an edgy calm in the capital. 最近的騷亂之後，首都平靜得令人不安。/ He began to get very edgy. 他開始變得非常煩躁。/ She made Jeff edgy with her constant demands. 她接連不斷的要求使傑夫很煩躁。/ Hester seemed edgy about something. 赫斯特似乎為了什麼事焦慮不安。/ He was rather edgy with her. 他對她很不耐煩。
          
相關詞義延伸：
· hysterical還可指（通常指因驚嚇而）情緒異常激動的，癔病的。

周邊詞彙補充：
· fidget可指因為緊張、厭煩、激動等而手腳動來動去，如坐立不安。

☀ <span class="category">**令人緊張、焦慮、煩躁的形容詞：**</span>
<span class="vocabulary">**tense**</span> [tens] 
<span class="definition">adj.（形勢、事件、一段時間、氣氛等）令人緊張、擔憂的，焦慮的，且通常不能公開表達出來的，即憤懣的，鬱憤的：</span>I spent a tense few weeks waiting for the results of the tests. 等候測試結果的這幾個星期裡我寢食不安。/ The atmosphere in the meeting was getting more and more tense. 會議的氣氛越來越緊張。/ The situation grew increasingly tense. 形勢越來越緊張。/ Relations between the two leaders are visibly tense. 兩位領導人的關係明顯緊張。
                      
<span class="vocabulary">**uneasy**</span> [ʌnˈi:zi]
<span class="definition">adj.（某事）令人不安的、令人不舒服的：</span>She woke from an uneasy sleep to find the house empty. 她睡得不安穩，醒來時發現屋子裡空無一人。

<span class="vocabulary">**strained**</span> [streɪnd]
<span class="definition">adj.（局勢、關係等）緊張的、不友好的。相當於tense：</span>There was a strained atmosphere throughout the meeting. 會議自始至終氣氛緊張。/ The atmosphere remained somewhat strained all evening. 整個晚上氣氛一直有點兒緊張。/ Relations between the two families are strained. 兩家關係緊張。/ Relations between the two countries had become severely strained. 兩國關係已經變得非常緊張。a period of strained relations between the prime minister and his deputy 總理和副總理之間關係緊張的時期 / Relations between us were rather strained. 我們之間的關係非常緊張。/ Their already strained relationship was made worse by this incident. 這次事件使他們本來已經非常緊張的關係更加惡化。
           
<span class="vocabulary">**stressful**</span> [ˈstresfl]
<span class="definition">adj.（狀況、經歷）造成很大壓力的、讓人非常擔憂的：</span>a stressful job 造成沉重壓力的工作 / My job's getting more and more stressful. 我的工作壓力越來越大。/ I find these meetings rather stressful. 我覺得這些會議令人感到壓力頗大。/ It was a stressful time for all of us. 對我們所有人來說，那是一個艱難的時期。/ It was an emotionally stressful time for him. 這對他來說是一個感情壓力很大的時期。
           
<span class="vocabulary">**charged**</span> [tʃɑ:dʒd; 美tʃɑrdʒd]
<span class="definition">adj. 形勢緊張的：</span>There was a highly charged atmosphere. 氣氛高度緊張。/ politically, racially（尤美）charged 政治上可能引起激烈反應的；充滿種族情緒的

<span class="vocabulary">**anxious**</span> ['æŋkʃəs] 
<span class="definition">adj. 引起焦慮的，令人焦慮的；表現出憂慮的：</span>anxious moments 令人焦慮不安的時刻 / anxious expression 憂慮的表情

<span class="vocabulary">**disturbing**</span> [dɪ'stə:bɪŋ] 
<span class="definition">adj. 使人感覺到焦慮、不安或震驚的：</span>a disturbing piece of news 一則令人不安的訊息 / a disturbing experience 一段令人不安的經歷
          
<span class="vocabulary">**perturbed**</span> [-ˈtɜ:bd; 美 -ˈtɜ:rbd]
<span class="definition">adj. ~ (at/about sth) 某事令人不安的：</span>He apparently was not perturbed by the prospect of a policeman coming to call. 顯然，對警察可能會上門來訪，他並沒有表現得很不安。

<span class="vocabulary">**uncomfortable**</span> [ʌn'kʌmfətəbl] 
<span class="definition">adj. 使人感覺焦慮的、尷尬的、害怕的、不輕鬆自在的：</span>There was an uncomfortable silence. 有一種令人不安的寂靜。
           
<span class="vocabulary">**disconcerting**</span> [ˌdɪskən'sɜ:rtɪŋ]
<span class="definition">adj.（某事物）令人不安的：</span>I found all that noise rather disconcerting. 我覺得如此大的噪聲有些讓人受不了。

<span class="category">**緊張、不安、恐慌、焦慮、煩亂的名詞：**</span>
<span class="vocabulary">**nerve**</span> [nɜ:v; 美 nɜ:rv]
<span class="definition">n. [pl.] nerves可指擔憂或焦慮的情緒，如神經質、神經緊張：</span>exam nerves（尤英）考試引起的緊張 / first-night nerves（英）初次登場的緊張 / jittery nerves（美）緊張不安 / It game me time to calm my jittery nerves before meeting her. 這讓我在見她之前有時間穩定我緊張不安的神經。/ She took a few deep breaths to calm her nerves. 她做了幾次深呼吸來穩定緊張情緒。/ I had an attack of nerves just before I went on stage. 登臺前那一刻我感到非常緊張。/ By the time of the interview, I was a bundle of nerves. 到面試時，我十分緊張。/ Even after years as a singer, he still suffers from nerves before a performance. 儘管已做歌手多年，他在演出前仍然神經緊張。/ I need something to calm/steady my nerves. 我需要點東西來穩定一下我的情緒。/ Everyone's nerves were on edge (= everyone felt tense ). 人人都覺得緊張。/ He lives on his nerves (= is always worried). 他就愛神經緊張。/ I just played badly. It wasn't nerves. 我只是打得很糟，不是緊張造成的。
           
<span class="vocabulary">**nervousness**</span> ['nɜ:vəsnəs;美ˈnəvəsnɪs]
<span class="definition">n. [U] 緊張：</span>There has been great nervousness about the future. 對未來一直有種十分緊張的感覺。/ She managed to speak without betraying her nervousness. 她說話時設法掩蓋了自己的緊張。/ She laughed to hide her nervousness. 她大聲笑著來掩飾自己的緊張。/ I could sense his nervousness. 我能覺察到他的緊張。/ I was babbling from nervousness. 我緊張得胡言亂語起來。/ He found himself chattering away out of nervousness. 他發現自己由於緊張而喋蝶不休起來。/ her nervousness about working with children 她對跟小孩子打交道的緊張感 / He felt some nervousness at the prospect of meeting her. 想到要與她見面，他感到有些緊張。/ Adjusting your tie is often a sign of nervousness. 調整自己的領帶通常是心情緊張的表現。
           
<span class="vocabulary">**neurosis**</span> [njʊəˈrəʊsɪs; 美 nʊˈroʊ-]
<span class="definition">n. [C, U] 任何強烈的恐懼或憂慮：</span>anxiety neurosis 神經緊張 / the director's neurosis about actors arriving late for filming 導演對演員拍片遲到的神經緊張

<span class="vocabulary">**tension**</span> ['tenʃn] 
<span class="definition">n. [U] 心理情緒上的緊張、擔憂：</span>inner tension 內心的緊張 / emotional, nervous tension 情緒緊張；神經緊張 / tension headache 緊張性頭痛 / Laughter can be a great release of tension. 大笑能有效地釋放緊張情緒。
           
<span class="vocabulary">**dither**</span> [ˈdɪðə(r)]
<span class="definition">n. [sing.] [非正式] 緊張、焦慮、慌亂：</span>Don't get yourself in a dither over everything. 不要什麼事都緊張兮兮的。

<span class="vocabulary">**fever**</span> ['fi:və] 
<span class="definition">n. [sing.] fever (of sth) 緊張激動的狀態。不過一般要與其他類似的詞（如anxiety、impatience等）構成fever of sth結構，才可表示此義：</span>She was in a fever of anxiety about him. 她為他感到焦慮。/ to wait in a fever of impatience 焦急不安地等待

<span class="vocabulary">**panic**</span> ['pænɪk] 
<span class="definition">n. 1 [U, C, usually sing.] 突然產生的一陣巨大且難以抑制的恐懼（使人難以清晰地思考），可稱為驚恐或恐慌。是此義的常規用詞，最為常用：</span>a panic decision 慌亂中做出的決定 / panic reaction, panic symptoms 恐慌反應；恐慌症狀 / a look of panic 驚恐的神色 / She went into a blind panic when she couldn’t find the exit. 她找不到出口，陷入了莫名的恐慌之中。<span class="definition">2 [C] 人心惶恐不安的一種局面（在該局面中人們擔憂焦慮、不經仔細思考就匆匆行動）：</span>News of the losses caused (a) panic among investors. 虧損的訊息令投資者人心惶惶。/ a last-minute panic 事到臨頭手忙腳亂 / panic buying, panic selling 恐慌購買；恐慌拋售 / panic about food contamination 對食物汙染的恐慌 / public panic 公眾的恐慌 / financial panic 金融恐慌 / They fled in panic as the fire took hold. 火勢起來時，他們驚慌逃出。/ panic button 緊急按鈕
                      
<span class="vocabulary">**consternation**</span> [ˌkɒnstəˈneɪʃn; 美 ˌkɑ:nstərˈn-]
<span class="definition">n. [U] [正式] 憂慮：</span>His decision caused consternation in the art photography community. 他的決定在藝術攝影圈內引起了恐慌。/ There is some consternation among business leaders. 商界領袖中存在一些恐慌情緒。

<span class="vocabulary">**fluster**</span> [ˈflʌstə(r)]
<span class="definition">n. [sing.] [英] 緊張且混亂的狀態，即慌亂、慌張：</span>瞭解即可

<span class="vocabulary">**anxiety**</span> [æŋɡ'zaɪətɪ] 
<span class="definition">n. 1 [U] anxiety (about/over sth) 感到緊張或擔心壞事將要發生的一種心理狀態，即焦慮，憂慮：</span>the high level of anxiety created by entering a new environment 進入新環境產生的高度焦慮 / A lot of anxiety surrounds the issue of human cloning. 人們對克隆人的問題很擔憂。/ anxiety dream 焦慮夢（由於焦慮而做的夢）/ There are anxieties over the effects of unemployment. 對於失業後果有種種擔憂。<span class="definition">2 [C] 對某事的擔憂、擔心或害怕：</span>If you’re worried about your health, share your anxieties with your doctor. 你要是擔心自己的健康，就把你的憂慮告訴醫生吧。
           
<span class="vocabulary">**agitation**</span> [ˌædʒɪˈteɪʃn]
<span class="definition">n. [U] 焦慮不安、煩亂、煩惱：</span>Dot arrived in a state of great agitation. 多特到達時十分焦慮不安。/ Danny returned to Father's house in a state of intense agitation. 丹尼回到神父的房子時顯得極為焦慮不安。/ Diane lit a cigarette, trying to mask her agitation. 黛安娜點燃一根香菸，試圖掩飾她的焦躁不安。/ She was trying not to show her agitation. 她儘量掩飾她的焦慮。/ He could not hide his agitation. 他無法掩飾自己的焦慮。/ He knocked his glass over in his agitation. 他一激動把杯子打翻了。/ She was wriggling on the seat with agitation. 她不安地在座位上扭來扭去。/ a feeling of agitation 焦慮的感覺
                      
<span class="vocabulary">**unease**</span> [ʌnˈi:z]
<span class="definition">n. [U]（對某事的）焦慮、不安：</span>She felt a vague unease. 她感到莫名的不安。/ There was a growing unease about their involvement in the war. 他們對捲入戰爭感到日益不安。/ his unease with the situation 他對局勢的擔憂 / He was unable to hide his unease at the way the situation was developing. 他無法掩飾對局勢演變的憂慮。/ I smiled to hide my unease. 我微笑了一下來掩飾自己的不安。/ Sensing my unease about the afternoon ahead, he told me,' These men are pretty easy to talk to.' 他感覺到了我對即將到來的下午忐忑不安，便對我說：“這些人說話都比較隨和。” / We left with a deep sense/feeling of unease, because we knew something was being hidden from us. 我們離開時心裡感到很不安，因為我們知道有什麼事瞞著我們。/ They expressed their deep unease about the lack of security arrangements. 他們對缺乏安保措施深表憂慮。/ He sensed a certain unease in her. 他感覺到她有些不自在。/ Many felt unease about the methods used. 很多人對所使用的方法感到憂慮。/ He looked in vain for the source of his unease. 他試圖找出自己焦慮不安的緣由，但徒勞無果。

<span class="vocabulary">**complex**</span> ['kɒmpleks]

<span class="vocabulary">**preoccupation**</span> [priˌɒkjuˈpeɪʃn; 美 -ˌɑ:k-]
<span class="definition">n. [U] 因沉擔憂某事而造成的一種心情，如心事重重；憂心忡忡：</span>She spoke slowly, in a state of preoccupation. 她說話慢吞吞的，顯得心事重重。
           
<span class="vocabulary">**discomfort**</span> [dɪsˈkʌmfət; 美 -fərt]

<span class="vocabulary">**funk**</span> [fʌŋk]
<span class="definition">n. [sing.] [舊時，非正式]（也可用blue funk）（尤因處於自己無法控制的情形中而出現的）一種恐懼或憂慮的狀態：</span>He was in a blue funk! Worse than me! 他神經極為緊張！比我還糟！/ My face went crimson (which it does out of sheer funk). 我的臉變得通紅（我極度恐慌時就會如此）。

<span class="vocabulary">**flap**</span> [flæp]

<span class="vocabulary">**flutter**</span> [ˈflʌtə(r)]

<span class="vocabulary">**tremor**</span> [ˈtremə(r)]

<span class="vocabulary">**turmoil**</span> [ˈtɜ:mɔɪl; 美 ˈtɜ:rm-]
<span class="definition">n. [U, sing.] 精神上的混亂：</span>emotional, inner, internal, mental, personal turmoil 情感混亂；心靈混亂；內心混亂；精神混亂；個人混亂 / Her mind was in (a) turmoil. 她心亂如麻。/ Her mind was in a state of constant turmoil. 她的腦子總是一片混亂。/ He came to a stop, his thoughts in turmoil. 他停下來，腦子裡一片混亂。
           
<span class="vocabulary">**hysteria**</span> [hɪˈstɪəriə; 美 -ˈstɪr-]
<span class="definition">n. [U]（一個人或一群人）歇斯底里，即狂野情緒的爆發且不可控（如激動、憤怒或驚慌，且開始大叫或大笑）：</span>There was mass hysteria when the band came on stage. 樂隊登臺時觀眾一片瘋狂。/ A note of hysteria crept into her voice. 她的聲音聽來有點歇斯底里。/ She sometimes flew into fits of hysteria. 她有時歇斯底里。/ She was babbling, on the verge/point of hysteria. 她近乎歇斯底里地胡言亂語。/ No one could help getting carried away by the hysteria. 所有人都不由自主地受到狂熱情緒的感染。/ religious hysteria 宗教狂熱 / anti-communist, war hysteria 反共狂熱；戰爭狂熱 / Several were hurt in the panic. 'It was mass hysteria,' said Rev John Borders. 數人在恐慌中受了傷。“人們都瘋了，”約翰·博德斯牧師說。/ Sam arrived in a state of excitement bordering on hysteria. 薩姆來的時候處於一種近乎於歇斯底里的興奮狀態。/ the hysteria that swept through the country 席捲全國的狂熱情緒

<span class="category">**緊張、不安、恐慌、焦慮的動詞：**</span>
<span class="vocabulary">**tense**</span> [tens] 
<span class="definition">vt.＆vi. tense (sth) (up)（使）精神緊張，常由於擔憂、焦慮或害怕：</span>She tensed, hearing the strange noise again. 再次聽到那個奇怪的聲音，她緊張起來。
           
<span class="vocabulary">**flap**</span> [flæp]
<span class="definition">vi. [英，非正式] 憂慮或激動地行事：</span>There's no need to flap─I've got everything under control. 不必擔心——一切都已在我控制之中。

<span class="vocabulary">**panic**</span> ['pænɪk] 
<span class="definition">vt.＆vi. 驚慌失措（以致說些蠢話、做些蠢事或危險的事等）；使某人驚慌失措：</span>I panicked when I saw smoke coming out of the engine. 我看見發動機冒煙時，嚇得手足無措。/ The gunfire panicked the horses. 槍聲驚到了馬匹。       

<span class="vocabulary">**fluster**</span> [ˈflʌstə(r)]
<span class="definition">vt. [常用被動式] 尤指透過使某人忙亂而使其緊張或迷惑，如使慌亂：</span>The General refused to be flustered. 將軍沒有亂了方寸。/ She was a very calm person. Nothing could fluster her. 她是個非常沉著的人。沒什麼能讓她驚慌的。
           
<span class="vocabulary">**fuss**</span> [fʌs]
<span class="definition">vi. ~ (about sth) 為不重要的事而煩惱或憂慮：</span>Don't fuss, Mum, everything is all right. 別瞎操心了，媽媽，一切都好。
           
<span class="vocabulary">**discomfort**</span> [dɪsˈkʌmfət; 美 -fərt]
<span class="definition">vt. [正式] [常用被動式] 使某人憂慮：</span>瞭解即可

<span class="vocabulary">**disturb**</span> [dɪ'stə:b] 
<span class="definition">vt. 使某人焦慮（或不安、煩惱）：</span>The letter shocked and disturbed me. 這封信使我感到震驚和不安。/ It disturbed her to realize that she was alone. 她意識到自己孤單一人，心裡感到很不安。
           
<span class="vocabulary">**disconcert**</span> [ˌdɪskənˈsɜ:t; 美 -ˈsɜ:rt]
<span class="definition">vt. 使某人不安、焦慮：</span>The ambassador was clearly disconcerted by the British reaction. 顯然，英國的反應讓這位大使很不安。
   
<span class="vocabulary">**bug**</span> [bʌg]
<span class="definition">vt. [非正式] 使某人煩惱：</span>Stop bugging me! 別煩我了！/ It's something that's been bugging me a lot recently. 那事使我最近一直大傷腦筋。/ I only did it to bug my parents. 我這麼做只是為了讓我父母心煩。

<span class="vocabulary">**sting**</span> [stɪŋ]
<span class="definition">vt. ~ sb (to/into sth) / ~ sb (into doing sth)（言語）使某人不安：</span>He was stung by their criticism. 他們的批評使他心煩意亂。/ They launched a stinging attack on the government. 他們對政府進行了猛烈的抨擊。

<span class="vocabulary">**sweat**</span> [swet] 
<span class="definition">vi. [非正式] 感到擔心，焦慮或不安：</span>The sales manager made me sweat during the talk. 談話過程中，銷售經理使我忐忑不安。

<span class="vocabulary">**exercise**</span> ['eksəsaɪz] 
<span class="definition">vt. [正式] exercise sb/sth (about sth) 使某人非常焦慮，常用被動式：</span>She was exercised about the life of herself in the future. 她對自己未來的生活很焦慮。

<span class="vocabulary">**choke**</span> [tʃəʊk] 
<span class="definition">vi. [美，非正式] 指人（如因緊張而）不能做某事：</span>瞭解即可

相關詞義延伸：
· nervous還可指與神經系統有關的（且常影響人的心理或情緒的）；
· tense還可指肌肉緊張。也可指形勢緊張；
· tension還可指作家或電影導演製造的緊張氣氛。也可指緊張局勢；
· complex還可指不正常的精神狀態、情結。

周邊詞彙補充：
· thrill可指（對身體產生影響的）一陣突然而強烈的感覺；
· inhibit可表示使某人感到緊張或尷尬（從而使其不能去做某事）。
