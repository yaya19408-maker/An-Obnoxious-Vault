☀ <span class="category">**失望、氣餒：**</span>
<span class="vocabulary">**disappoint**</span> [͵dɪsə'pɒɪnt] 
<span class="definition">vt.＆vi. 因某人所希望或期待的事未發生，或不如希望的那樣好而使其感到悲傷，即使…失望。是此義的常規用詞：</span>His latest novel does not disappoint. 他最近發表的這部小說沒有讓人失望。/ I hate to disappoint you, but I’m just not interested. 我不想使你掃興，但我確實不感興趣。/ The movie had disappointed her. 這部電影使她失望。<span class="definition">vt. 使某人所希望的事未變成現實，即使破滅，使落空：</span>The new government had soon disappointed the hopes of many of its supporters. 新政府不久便使許多支持者的希望破滅了。

<span class="vocabulary">**fail**</span> [feɪl] 
<span class="definition">vt. 使某人失望；在某人需要的時候未能起到幫助作用：</span>When he lost his job, he felt he had failed his family. 他失去工作以後，感到辜負了他的家庭。/ She tried to be brave, but her courage failed her. 她想勇敢，但卻鼓不起勇氣。/ Words fail me.（比喻用法）我無法表達自己的感受。

<span class="vocabulary">**discourage**</span> [dɪs'kʌrɪdӡ] 
<span class="definition">vt. discourage sb (from doing sth) 使某人對做某事不再那麼有信心或有熱情，如使灰心，使洩氣，使喪失信心，相當於dishearten：</span>Don’t be discouraged by the first failure--try again! 不要因第一次失敗就灰心喪氣——再試一次吧！/ The weather discouraged people from attending. 這天氣使得人們不願意出席。

<span class="vocabulary">**dash**</span> [dæʃ] 
<span class="definition">vt.（事件或人）使某人的希望或期望破滅，為通訊用語，具有一定的文學性：</span>The civil war has dashed hopes for a peaceful country. 這場內戰擊碎了人們建立一個和平國家的希望。 

<span class="vocabulary">**disillusioned**</span> [ˌdɪsɪˈlu:ʒnd]
<span class="definition">adj. ~ (by/with sb/sth) 因自己原來追求的美好事物（或人）不再美好而變得失望的，如幻想破滅的、不再抱有幻想的：</span>I soon became disillusioned with the job. 我不久便對這個工作不再抱幻想了。/ I've become very disillusioned with politics. 我對政治完全不再抱幻想了。/ He had become disillusioned because he could not find a job. 他因找不到工作而備感失望。/ They felt bitter and disillusioned by the decision. 他們對這一決定感到氣憤與失望。

<span class="vocabulary">**disillusionment**</span> [ˌdɪsɪˈlu:ʒnmənt]
<span class="definition">n. [U, sing.] ~ (with sth) 幻滅；不再抱有幻想；醒悟：</span>his growing disillusionment with his work 他對他的工作越來越不抱幻想 / the pain, disillusionment and despair we experience when someone we idolized has betrayed our trust 我們視作偶像的人辜負了我們的信任時，我們所體會到的痛苦、失望和絕望 / There is widespread disillusionment among young people. 年輕人普遍感到失望。/ political disillusionment 政治幻滅 / There is growing public disillusionment with the present system of government. 公眾對於現行的政府體制越來越不抱幻想。

<span class="vocabulary">**disappointed**</span> [͵dɪsə'pɒɪntɪd] 
<span class="definition">adj. disappointed (at/by sth) / disappointed (in/with sb/sth) / disappointed (to see, hear, etc.) / disappointed that ... / disappointed (not) to be ... 失望的：</span>They were bitterly disappointed at the result of the game. 他們對比賽結果極為失望。/ I’m disappointed in you! 你真讓我失望！/ The decision left them very disappointed. 這項決定讓他們很失望。
           
<span class="vocabulary">**crestfallen**</span> [ˈkrestfɔ:lən]
<span class="definition">adj.（因失敗、未達到預期而）傷心失望的、垂頭喪氣的：</span>瞭解即可
           
<span class="vocabulary">**disappointing**</span> [ˌdɪsəˈpɔɪntɪŋ]
<span class="definition">adj. 令人失望的：</span>disappointing result, disappointing performance 令人失望的結果；令人失望的演出 / The outcome of the court case was disappointing for the family involved. 訴訟案的結果使得涉及本案的這家人非常失望。/ The wine was excellent, but the food was disappointing. 酒棒極了，但是飯菜卻讓人不敢恭維。/ The recession is largely blamed for the disappointing response to the appeal. 對此呼籲的反應令人失望，主要歸咎於經濟衰退。/ The movie was terribly disappointing. 這電影太讓人失望了。/ This was very disappointing for all the fans watching the game. 這令觀看比賽的廣大粉絲大失所望。/ That was very disappointing to me. 那令我非常失望。
           
<span class="vocabulary">**disappointment**</span> [ˌdɪsəˈpɔɪntmənt]

Every failed attempt brings disappointment and discouragement. 每一次失敗的嘗試都會帶來失望和沮喪。/ Their expectations were doomed to disappointment. 他們的期望是註定會以失望告終的。/ Ethan noticed the disappointment in Lisa's eyes. 伊桑注意到莉莎眼中的失望。/ I know you will share our disappointment at the lack of progress on this issue. 這件事沒有進展，我知道你會與我們同樣失望。/ He let his disappointment show. 他沒有掩飾自己的失望。/ He couldn't help the feelings of disappointment that filled him. 他心中滿是失望之情，難以釋懷。/ The problem must be rectified before disappointment sets in. 這一問題必須矯正，免得讓大家失望。/ Cross expressed disappointment about the damage. 克羅斯表達了對這一損失的失望之情。/ decision. 參與這場運動的人表達了對政府決定的失望之情。/ He expressed to me his disappointment with art in the 20th century. 他向我表達了他對20世紀藝術的失望。/ a feeling of disappointment, a sense of disappointment 失望的心情；沮喪感 / tears of disappointment 失望的淚水 / You can imagine my disappointment at finding all the plants damaged by caterpillars. 你可以想象我發現所有植物被毛毛蟲損害時的失望之情。<span class="definition">2 [C] ~ (to sb) 令人失望的人（或事物）：</span>That new restaurant was a big disappointment. 那家新餐館使人大失所望。/ For many, their long-awaited homecoming was a bitter disappointment. 對許多人來說，他們期盼已久的還鄉之行卻令他們大失所望。/ this ne'er-do-well brother who was such a disappointment to his family 這個令他家人如此失望的沒出息的兄弟 / I'm afraid I was a sad disappointment to my mother. 我恐怕自己讓母親傷心失望了。/ The ending of the book is a real disappointment. 這本書的結尾真令人失望。/ A small disappointment for me was the poor quality of the photographs. 照片的質量較差，令我稍感失望。/ Her past is marred by romantic disappointments. 她的過去因缺乏浪漫而黯然失色。/ The film was a box-office disappointment. 這部電影的票房表現令人失望。/ She's had a lot of disappointments in the past. 她以往經歷了許多不如意。/ Over the years they suffered one disappointment after another. 這些年來他們經歷了接二連三的不如意。/ This news has come as a disappointment ☀ <span class="category">**麻木、茫然：**</span>
<span class="vocabulary">**numb**</span> [nʌm] 
<span class="definition">adj.（感覺、思考、反應、感情等）麻木的，木然的。是此義的常規用詞：</span>numb with grief 由於悲傷而木然 / numb with fear 嚇呆 / He felt numb with shock. 他驚呆了。/ The news left us numb. 這條訊息讓我們目瞪口呆。<span class="definition">vt. 使某人在情感或精神上變得麻木：</span>I was numbed by the shock of his death. 他的去世讓我驚呆了。

<span class="vocabulary">**dead**</span> [ded] 
<span class="definition">adj. dead to sth（感情上）無動於衷的，麻木的，無感覺的。是一種形象化用法：</span>She was dead to all feelings of pity. 她毫無同情心。
                 
<span class="vocabulary">**paralysis**</span> [pəˈræləsɪs]
<span class="definition">n. [U] 精神上的癱瘓：</span>a kind of mental paralysis 一種精神癱瘓狀態 / emotional paralysis 情感上的癱瘓

<span class="vocabulary">**glassy**</span> [ˈglɑ:si; 美 ˈglæsi]
<span class="definition">adj.（眼神或表情）沒表現出感情或情緒的，如無表情的、木然的、呆滯的：</span>glassy eyes 目光呆滯的眼睛 / glassy look, glassy stare 呆滯的神色；呆滯的盯視 / He was very still, his eyes glassy. 他紋絲不動，目光呆滯。/ He looked flushed and glassy-eyed. 他顯得滿臉通紅，目光呆滯。/ Henry gave Paul a glassy-eyed stare. 亨利木然地盯著保羅。

<span class="vocabulary">**blank**</span> [blæŋk] 
<span class="definition">adj. 沒有表現出感覺的（如沒表情的）或沒表現出興趣的：</span>She stared at me with a blank expression on her face. 她木然地盯著我。

<span class="vocabulary">**vacant**</span> ['veɪkənt] 
<span class="definition">adj.（一個人的目光、表情等）表明某人沒有思考任何事的，如無神的、茫然的、呆滯的：</span>a vacant look 呆滯的目光
                      
<span class="vocabulary">**vacancy**</span> [ˈveɪkənsi]
<span class="definition">n. [U] 沒興趣；沒主意：</span>the vacancy of her expression 她那茫然若失的表情

<span class="vocabulary">**vacuous**</span> [ˈvækjuəs]
<span class="definition">adj. [正式]（人或其言論）缺乏思想或感情的，如茫然的：</span>a vacuous expression 茫然的表情               

<span class="vocabulary">**deadpan**</span> [ˈdedpæn]
<span class="definition">adj. 面無表情的，通常指開玩笑或作弄人時假裝嚴肅的、故意板著臉的：</span>deadpan humour 冷麵幽默 / He cracks jokes with a deadpan expression on his face. 他說笑話時緊繃著臉。/ She put the letter on the desk in front of me, her face deadpan, not a flicker of a smile. 她把那封信放在我面前的桌子上，故意一臉嚴肅，沒有一絲的笑容。/ She delivered her monologue in a deadpan voice. 她故作嚴肅地說著獨白。       

<span class="vocabulary">**daze**</span> [deɪz]
<span class="definition">n. [sing.] in a ~ 處於迷惑、茫然、恍惚的狀態：</span>I've been in a complete daze since hearing the news. 自從聽到那訊息，我一直茫然不知所措。/ For 35 minutes I was walking around in a daze. 我茫然地走了35 分鐘。           

<span class="vocabulary">**glaze**</span> [gleɪz]
<span class="definition">vi. ~ (over)（一個人的眼睛）變得看起來無聊或疲憊，如變呆滯：</span>A lot of people's eyes glaze over if you say you are a feminist. 如果你說你是女權主義者，好多人都會愣住。/ ‘I'm feeling rather tired, ’ he said, his eyes glazing. “我有點累了。”他目光呆滯地說。

☀ <span class="category">**孤獨、寂寞、失落、空虛：**</span>
<span class="vocabulary">**alone**</span> [ə'ləʊn] 
<span class="definition">adj.（不用於名詞前）＆adv. 孤獨且不開心，或沒有任何朋友。常是一種客觀狀態：</span>Carol felt all alone in the world. 卡羅爾感到自己在世界上無依無靠。/ I’ve been so alone since you went away. 你走了以後我一直很寂寞。

<span class="vocabulary">**lonely**</span> ['ləʊnlɪ] 
<span class="definition">adj. 1（由於沒有朋友或與之談話的人而）孤獨的，寂寞的。常是一種心理感受：</span>She lives alone and often feels lonely. 她孑然一身，常感到寂寞。/ It gets pretty lonely here in winter. 這裡的冬天很孤寂。/ She was desperately lonely at school. 她在學校裡極其孤獨。<span class="definition">2（一種情況或一段時間）在孤單中悲傷度過的：</span>all those lonely nights at home watching TV 所有那些在家看電視的孤寂夜晚
                      
<span class="vocabulary">**loneliness**</span> [ˈləʊnlinəs]
<span class="definition">n. [U] 孤獨、寂寞：</span>I have so many friends, but deep down, underneath, I have a fear of loneliness. 我雖然有很多朋友，但心底裡還是懼怕寂寞。/ a period of loneliness in his life 他一生中孤苦伶仃的一段時間 / He experienced terrible loneliness after the loss of his wife. 妻子去世後，他飽嘗寂寞之苦。/ To ease her loneliness, she spent a lot of time with her dogs. 為了排遣孤寂，她很多時間與自己的狗相伴。/ a feeling/sense of loneliness 孤獨感

<span class="vocabulary">**forlorn**</span> [fəˈlɔ:n; 美 fərˈlɔ:rn]
<span class="definition">adj.（人）孤苦伶仃的：</span>She looked so forlorn, standing there in the rain. 她站在雨中，顯得孤苦伶仃。/ One of the demonstrators, a young woman, sat forlorn on the pavement. 一名年輕的女示威者獨自一人坐在人行路上。/ He looked a forlorn figure as he limped off after 26 minutes. 26 分鐘之後他一瘸一拐地走出來，顯得孤單淒涼。/ She didn't belong, and felt utterly forlorn. 她與周圍的人格格不入，感到完全孤立無助。
           
<span class="vocabulary">**desolate**</span> [ˈdesələt]
<span class="definition">adj. 非常孤獨且難過的：</span>He was desolate without her. 沒有她，他感到孤獨而淒涼。/ She was utterly desolate after losing her baby. 失去寶寶後，她萬分孤寂。/ Her voice sounded a little desolate. 她的嗓音聽起來有點淒涼。
           
<span class="vocabulary">**isolated**</span> [ˈaɪsəleɪtɪd]
<span class="definition">adj. 不與其他人或國家聯絡的，如孤獨的、孤立的：</span>a child who is isolated from other children 一個不與其他孩子交往的孩子 / Elderly people easily become socially isolated. 上了年紀的人很容易變得與社會隔絕。/ They found themselves socially isolated and ostracized. 他們發現自己被孤立和排斥於社會之外。/ She kept herself almost isolated from her colleagues. 她幾乎不與同事來往。/ I felt very isolated in my new job. 我在新的工作崗位上覺得很孤獨。/ Men can feel isolated at work. 人們工作時可能感到孤立無助。/ Some patients may become very isolated and depressed. 一些病人可能變得非常孤獨沮喪。/ economically, geographically, physically isolated 經濟上孤立；地理上隔絕；物理上隔離 / a culturally isolated community 一個文化上孤立的社群 / Unless a compromise could be reached the country would be diplomatically isolated on this issue. 除非就此事達成妥協，否則該國將在外交上陷入孤立。/ The decision left the country isolated from its allies. 這個決定使這個國家在盟國中受到孤立。/ The President is looking increasingly isolated from the whole agenda. 總統似乎越來越遊離於整個議程之外了。
                      
<span class="vocabulary">**isolation**</span> [ˌaɪsəˈleɪʃn]
<span class="definition">n. [U] ~ (from sb/sth) 孤獨：</span>Many unemployed people experience feelings of isolation and depression. 很多失業者有孤獨沮喪的感覺。/ Many deaf people have feelings of isolation and loneliness. 很多失聰者都有孤獨無助的感覺。/ He also talked briefly about the isolation he endured while in captivity. 他還簡單談及了他被俘期間忍受的孤獨。/ emotional isolation 情感上的孤獨
 
<span class="vocabulary">**solitary**</span> [ˈsɒlətri; 美 ˈsɑ:ləteri]
<span class="definition">adj.（人或動物）獨處的、獨居的：</span>He was a solitary child. 他是一個孤僻的孩子。/ Paul was a shy, pleasant, solitary man. 保羅是一個羞怯、友善、慣於獨處的男人。/ He led a solitary life. 他過著獨居生活。/ Tigers are solitary animals. 虎是獨居動物。<span class="definition">n. [C] [正式] 獨居者：</span>瞭解即可
           
<span class="vocabulary">**friendless**</span> [ˈfrendləs]
<span class="definition">adj. 沒有朋友的：</span>The boy was unhappy because he thought he was friendless. 這個男孩自認為沒有朋友，所以悶悶不樂。/ With the departure of his remaining colleague he was left friendless. 僅存的一位同事的離去使得他沒了朋友。/ alone and friendless 孤苦伶仃

<span class="vocabulary">**adrift**</span> [əˈdrɪft]
<span class="definition">adj. [名詞前不可用] [英]（人）孤獨且沒有人生目標的，如隨波逐流的、漂泊無依的、無所適從的、茫然的：</span>young people adrift in the big city 在大城市漂泊的年輕人 / Amy had the growing sense that she was adrift and isolated. 埃米愈發感到自己孤立無援，孑然一身。/ She felt cast adrift in a vulgar, materialistic society. 她感到在庸俗的、物質至上的社會中隨波逐流。

<span class="vocabulary">**loss**</span> [lɒs] 
<span class="definition">n. [C] 某人所喜歡的人或物離開時，這個人所擁有的失落感：</span>feelings of loss and grief 失落和悲傷 / When she died I was filled with a sense of loss. 她去世後我心裡空落落的。

<span class="vocabulary">**empty**</span> ['emptɪ] 
<span class="definition">adj.（一個人或其生活）因生活無目標而不開心（常在某件壞事發生之後），可稱為空虛的：</span>My life seems empty without you. 沒有你，我的生活似乎就沒有了意義。/ We all feel very empty now she’s gone. 她走了，我們都覺得心裡空蕩蕩的。
                                 
<span class="vocabulary">**void**</span> [vɔɪd]
<span class="definition">n. [C] [文學]（感情的）空白：</span>an aching void of loneliness 痛苦的孤寂感 / The void left by his mother's death was never filled. 他母親死後留下的空虛感永遠沒能填補上。/ It seemed that nothing could fill the aching black void left by Rachel's death. 雷切爾去世造成的痛苦空虛感似乎用什麼都無法填補。

相關詞義延伸：
· isolated還可指單獨的、只出現一次的，即（事件）孤立的、個別的。

周邊詞彙補充：
· seclusion可指隱居、與世隔絕、清靜（不與外人聯絡）；
· autistic可指自閉症的、孤獨症的；
· retiring可指不愛交際的、孤僻的、離群索居的、害羞的；
· privacy可指獨處。
