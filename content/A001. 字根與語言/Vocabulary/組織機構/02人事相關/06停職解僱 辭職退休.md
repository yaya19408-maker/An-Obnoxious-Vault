☀ <span class="category">**停職解僱 辭職退休：**</span>
<span class="vocabulary">**suspension**</span> [səs'penʃn] 
<span class="definition">n. [U, C] 正式讓某人從其職位、學校、團隊等暫停工作一段時間（通常作為一種懲罰），即停職、停學、停賽等：</span>suspension from school 暫被停學 / a five-game suspension for violating the league’s drug policy 因違反聯賽藥品規定而停賽五場 / He is serving a one-match suspension. 他正在接受停賽一場的處罰。/ a detective under suspension from his job 被停職的警探 / his suspension from the club 他被俱樂部暫時除名

<span class="vocabulary">**resign**</span> [rɪ'zaɪn] 
<span class="definition">vt.＆vi. resign (from sth) / resign (as sth)（從公司、組織機構等）辭去職務，辭職：</span>He resigned as chairman. 他辭去了主席一職。
           
<span class="vocabulary">**resignation**</span> [ˌrezɪgˈneɪʃn]
<span class="definition">n. 1 [U, C] 辭職：</span>his letter of resignation 他的辭職信 / There were calls for her resignation from the board of directors. 有人要求她辭去董事會中的職務。/ The protesters called for his immediate resignation. 抗議者要求他馬上辭職。/ Further resignations are expected. 預計還會有人辭職。/ His resignation announcement was widely expected. 大家都預見到他會宣佈辭職。/ resignation speech, resignation statement 辭職演說；辭職宣告 / forced resignation 被迫辭職 / mass resignation 集體辭職 / The accusation prompted the resignation of the party leader. 這一指控導致該黨領袖的辭職。/ her resignation as party leader 她辭去黨的領導職務 / Illness forced his resignation from the team. 他因病被迫辭去團隊中的職務。/ My resignation is effective from May 1. 我的辭職於5月1日開始生效。/ A scandal led to his resignation from office.一樁醜聞導致了他的辭職。<span class="definition">2 [C] 辭職信、辭呈：</span>to tender your resignation 提交辭職信 / She handed in her resignation following the dispute over company policy. 就公司政策問題發生爭端後，她遞交了辭呈。/ Mr Morgan has offered his resignation and it has been accepted. 摩根先生己經遞交了辭呈，並已獲准。/ We haven't received his resignation yet. 我們還沒有收到他的辭呈。/ She has refused to accept the resignation of her deputy. 她拒絕接受副手的辭呈。

<span class="vocabulary">**dismiss**</span> [dɪs'mɪs] 
<span class="definition">vt. dismiss sb (from sth) 正式解除某人的職位，即解僱，免職。當一個人被dismiss的時候，通常有合理的理由，且需要走正常的人事流程，詞義色彩上也比較溫和而中立，比較常用。常用被動式：</span>She claims she was unfairly dismissed from her post. 她聲稱自己被無理解僱。
           
<span class="vocabulary">**dismissal**</span> [dɪsˈmɪsl]
<span class="definition">n. [U, C] 解僱；撤職：</span>The dismissals followed the resignation of the chairman. 董事長辭職後緊接著就是解僱人員。/ Mr Low's dismissal from his post at the head of the commission 免去洛先生的委員會主任一職 / automatic, immediate, instant（尤英）, summary dismissal 自動免職；即刻解僱；馬上開除 / His attack on the manager led to his instant dismissal. 他攻擊經理使他當即去了工作。/ an employee claim for unfair dismissal 僱員對無理解僱提出索賠 / Crash victims are calling for the dismissal of the bus driver. 撞車事故受害者要求解僱公交車司機。/ They were warned that they risked dismissal if the strike continued. 他們被警告說如果繼續罷工就有遭解僱的危險。/ These mistakes were not nearly serious enough to warrant his dismissal. 這些錯誤遠沒有嚴重到將他解僱的程度。/ Cooke, who was with the firm 30 years, claims unfair dismissal. 庫克在這家公司工作已30年，他訴稱自己是被無理解僱的。/ his dismissal for poor performance 他由於表現不好而遭解僱 / She is now faced with dismissal on the grounds of misconduct. 由於瀆職她現在面臨解僱。
           
<span class="vocabulary">**redundancy**</span> [rɪˈdʌndənsi]
<span class="definition">n. [U, C, usually pl.] [英]（因勞動力過剩而造成的）裁員、解僱：</span>Thousands of factory workers are facing redundancy. 數千名工廠工人面臨裁員。/ The closure of the mine led to large-scale redundancies. 礦山的關閉造成大批工人失業。/ compulsory, voluntary redundancy 強制性裁員；自願被裁減 / The ministry has said it hopes to avoid compulsory redundancies. 該部稱希望避免強制性裁員。/ staff redundancy 人員過剩 / a fresh wave of redundancies 新一輪的裁員 / The bank will be making 3 500 redundancies over the next five years. 在未來五年內該銀行將裁員3，500人。/ 34 members of staff have taken voluntary redundancy. 34名員工已經接受自願離職。/ redundancy programme 裁員計劃 / Redundancy notices have been sent to 200 workers. 200名工人接到了裁員通知。/ redundancy package, redundancy terms 遣散條件 / redundancy money, redundancy pay, redundancy payment 遣散費；裁員費 / The company has had to make redundancy payments of £472 million. 該公司不得不為裁員支付4.72億英鎊的補償款。/ Most of the companies' losses stemmed from redundancy costs. 該公司虧本主要是由於支付冗員費用造成的。
           
<span class="vocabulary">**redundant**</span> [rɪˈdʌndənt]
<span class="definition">adj. [英]（人）因公司的勞動力過剩而被裁剪的：</span>to be made redundant from your job 成為冗員而被裁減 / My husband was made redundant late last year. 我丈夫去年年底被裁了。/ a redundant miner 失業的礦工 / redundant employees 受裁汰的員工 / the decision to make 800 employees compulsorily redundant 強行裁減800名員工的決定

<span class="vocabulary">**fire**</span> ['faɪə] 
<span class="definition">vt. 上司突然且專橫地將某人“炒魷魚”，使此人被迫離開其工作崗位，強調該上司當時的一種憤怒感。感情色彩比較強烈。是一種非正式用法，常用被動式。日常生活中比較常用：</span>We had to fire him for dishonesty. 他不誠實，我們不得不開除他。/ He was responsible for hiring and firing staff. 他負責招聘和解僱職員。 

<span class="vocabulary">**drop**</span> [drɒp] 
<span class="definition">vt. 某人被一支運動隊或一個組織除名或解職。暗示此人對該組織已不再重要：</span>He has been dropped from his job as sales director. 他已經被拿掉了銷售總監的職務。/ He has been quietly dropped from the England team. 他被英格蘭隊悄然除名。
                      
<span class="vocabulary">**relieve**</span> [rɪˈli:v]
<span class="definition">vt. 解除（或免除）…的職務：</span>The officer involved was relieved of his duties because he had violated strict guidelines. 那名相關的官員被免除了職務，因為他違反了要求嚴格的規章制度。/ The party leader has been relieved of his post. 這個黨的領導人被免除了職務。

<span class="vocabulary">**discharge**</span> [dɪsˈtʃɑ:dʒ; 美 -ˈtʃɑ:rdʒ]
<span class="definition">vt. [常用被動式] ~ sb (from sth) 給予某人正式許可，讓其離開一份工作；使某人丟掉一份工作（如解僱）：</span>He was discharged from the army following his injury. 他受傷後就退伍了。/ He was found guilty and dishonourably discharged from the army. 他被判有罪，被開除軍籍。/ She was discharged from the police force for bad conduct. 她因行為不軌被清除出警察隊伍。<span class="definition">n. [U, C] ~ (from sth) 免職：</span>瞭解即可
             
<span class="vocabulary">**expel**</span> [ɪkˈspel]
<span class="definition">vt. ~ sb/sth (from sth) 正式讓某人離開一所學校或一個組織機構，如開除、除名：</span>He was/got expelled from school for taking drugs at 15. 他15歲時因吸食毒品而被學校開除。/ More than five-thousand secondary school students have been expelled for cheating. 5,000多名中學生因為作弊被開除。/ a boy expelled from school for making death threats to his teacher 因為威脅要殺死老師而被學校開除的男孩 / Olympic athletes expelled for drug-taking 因服禁藥被取消比賽資格的奧運會運動員         
           
<span class="vocabulary">**exile**</span> [ˈeksaɪl; ˈegzaɪl]
<span class="definition">vt. [常用被動式] ~ sb (from…) 開除：</span>He has been exiled from the first team and forced to play in third team matches. 他被開除出甲級球隊，被迫參加丙級球隊的比賽。

<span class="vocabulary">**sack**</span> [sæk]
<span class="definition">vt. [非正式，尤英] 解僱：</span>She was sacked for refusing to work on Sundays. 她因拒絕在星期天上班被解僱了。/ Earlier today the Prime Minister sacked 18 government officials for corruption. 今天早些時候，首相解除了 18 名涉嫌貪汙腐敗的政府官員的職務。/ Science teacher James Wood was sacked for slapping a schoolboy. 理科教師詹姆斯·伍德因為掌摑一名男生而被開除。<span class="definition">n. [sing.] [英，非正式] the sack可指（通常由於做錯事而被）開除、解僱：</span>to get the sack（英，美） 被解僱 / She got the sack after 20 years of service. 她工作20年後被解僱了。/ He got the sack for swearing. 他因說髒話而被開除。/ Her work was so poor that she was given the sack. 她工作乾得很差，被炒了魷魚。/ Four hundred workers face the sack. 四百名工人面臨解僱的危險。
                      
<span class="vocabulary">**axe**</span> [æks]
<span class="definition">n. [sing.] [尤英] [美式英語用ax] [非正式] the axe可指（某人）被解僱：</span>Up to 300 workers are facing the axe at a struggling Merseyside firm.（face尤為英式用法）艱苦掙扎的默西賽德公司有多達300名工人面臨被解僱。<span class="definition">vt. [英] [美式英語用ax] [常用被動式] 將某人解僱、開除：</span>Jones has been axed from the team. 瓊斯已被開除出隊。

<span class="vocabulary">**displace**</span> [dɪsˈpleɪs]
<span class="definition">vt. [常用被動式] [尤美] 將某人從一個工作崗位或職位上移除，如撤職、免職、辭退：</span>displaced workers 被辭退的工人          

<span class="vocabulary">**release**</span> [rɪˈli:s]
<span class="definition">vt. ~ sb (from sth) 與某人解除合同等：</span>The club is releasing some of its older players. 俱樂部正在解聘一些老隊員。

<span class="vocabulary">**can**</span> [kæn] 
<span class="definition">vt. [美，非正式] 解僱某人，即炒…的魷魚，或捲鋪蓋走人。與bounce未見明顯差別。不過二者作為非正式用法，都不如fire常用：</span>The extremists prevailed, and the security chief was canned. 極端分子佔了上風，那名安全主管被解僱。

<span class="vocabulary">**remove**</span> [rɪ'mu:v] 
<span class="definition">vt. 解除或免除（某人的職務等）。暗示有其他人來接班：</span>The elections removed the government from power. 這次選舉使得政府倒臺。
           
<span class="vocabulary">**removal**</span> [rɪˈmu:vl]
<span class="definition">n. [U] ~ (of sb) 解僱、免職：</span>events leading to the removal of the president from office 導致總統下臺的一些事件 / They demanded her removal from office. 他們要求解除她的職務。/ his immediate removal from power 他被立即免職 / Parliament had decided that his removal from power was illegal. 國會決議認為他的免職是非法的。
                      
<span class="vocabulary">**shed**</span> [ʃed]
<span class="definition">vt.（常用於報章）裁員：</span>The factory is shedding a large number of jobs. 這家工廠正大批裁員。/ The firm is to shed 700 jobs. 公司將裁員 700 人。

<span class="vocabulary">**expulsion**</span> [ɪkˈspʌlʃn]
<span class="definition">n. [U, C] ~ (from…) 將某人從學校或組織機構開除、除名：</span>The headteacher threatened the three girls with expulsion. 校長以開除來威脅這三名女學生。/ Her hatred of authority led to her expulsion from high school. 她對權威的仇恨導致她被中學開除。/ Several students now face expulsion. 有幾個學生現在面臨被開除學籍的處分。/ Copying from another candidate results in automatic expulsion. 抄襲其他應試者將被自動取消資格。/ The club faces expulsion from the football league. 這傢俱樂部面臨被足協開除的處境。/ an ex-party member who intends to appeal against his expulsion（appeal against為英式用法）打算對被開除黨籍提出上訴的前黨員 / His disruptive conduct was felt to be sufficient grounds for his expulsion. 他的擾亂行為被視為開除他的充分理由。

<span class="vocabulary">**bounce**</span> [baʊns] 
<span class="definition">vt. [尤美，非正式] bounce sb (from sth) 迫使某人離開一個工作崗位、團隊等：</span>He was soon bounced from the post. 他不久被解職。
           
<span class="vocabulary">**layoff**</span> ['leɪɒf;美'leɪˌɔf]
<span class="definition">n. [C]（通常指因工作崗位不足而導致的）解僱、裁員、下崗：</span>It will close more than 200 stores nationwide resulting in the layoffs of an estimated 2,000 employees. 它將關閉全國200多家店鋪，估計會有2,000名員工因此失業。
           
<span class="vocabulary">**bump**</span> [bʌmp]
<span class="definition">vt.（從某群體）調出，開除（某人）：</span>The coach told him he had been bumped from the crew. 教練通知他已被調出賽艇隊。

<span class="vocabulary">**go**</span> [ɡəʊ] 
<span class="definition">vi. 1（常指被迫）離職：</span>He had made a humiliating tactical error and he had to go. 他犯下了一個令他恥辱的戰術錯誤，被迫離職。<span class="definition">2 sb/sth must/has to/can go 用於談論想要清除某人：</span>He’s useless--he’ll have to go. 他毫無用處——得辭掉他。

<span class="vocabulary">**leave**</span> [li:v] 
<span class="definition">vt.＆vi. 離開（居住地點、所屬的群體或工作單位）：</span>My secretary has threatened to leave. 我的秘書以辭職相要挾。

<span class="vocabulary">**quit**</span> [kwɪt] 
<span class="definition">vt.＆vi. [非正式] quit (as sth) 離開（工作崗位、學校等）：</span>to quit over pay 因為薪資問題而辭職 / He has decided to quit as manager of the team. 他已決定辭掉球隊教練的職務。

<span class="vocabulary">**departure**</span> [dɪ'pɑːtʃə] 
<span class="definition">n. [C, U] departure (from ...) 離職：</span>Her disagreement with the MD probably hastened her departure. 她與總經理的意見不和很可能加速了她的離職。

<span class="vocabulary">**retire**</span> [rɪ'taɪə] 
<span class="definition">vt.＆vi. retire (from sth) / retire (as sth)（尤指因達到某一年齡或由於疾病而）退職，辭職；使退職，使辭職：</span>retiring age 退休年齡 / In a few years, I’ll be eligible to retire. 再過幾年我就可以退休了。/ He officially retired from the day-to-day operations of his company. 他正式退出了公司的日常運營。
           
<span class="vocabulary">**retirement**</span> [rɪˈtaɪəmənt; 美 -ˈtaɪərm-]
<span class="definition">n. 1 [U, C] 退休；退休年齡：</span>At 60, he was now approaching retirement. 他60歲了，就要退休了。/ Susan is going to take early retirement (= retire before the usual age). 蘇珊要提前退休。/ the proportion of the population who are over retirement age 超過退休年齡的人口比例 / compulsory（英）, enforced（尤英）, forced（美）, mandatory（尤美）retirement 強制性退休 / compulsory retirement at 60 60歲強制性退休。/ her enforced retirement from the sport 她從體育界被強制退役 / She took voluntary retirement in 2001. 她於2001年自願退休。/ It was their final concert before entering retirement. 這是他們退休前最後一場音樂會。/ They presented him with a watch to mark his retirement. 他們送給他一塊手錶作為退休紀念。/ retirement age 退休年齡 / Her official retirement date is March 12.她正式退休的日期是3月12日。/ retirement benefits, retirement package, retirement pension（尤英）（一整套）退休福利；退休金 / retirement plan, retirement programme/program（尤美）退休計劃 / They have about $14 800 in retirement savings. 他們有大約14，800美元的退體儲蓄金。/ retirement account（美）退休賬戶 / retirement fund 退休基金 / retirement income, retirement money（尤美）, retirement pay（尤美）退休收入；退休金 / He has a good retirement income. 他有不錯的退休收入。/ retirement portfolio（尤美）/ important matters like health care and retirement security 衛生保健和退休保障之類的重要事項 / retirement gift, retirement party 退休禮物；退休聚會 / retirement facility（美）, retirement home養老院 / retirement community（美）, retirement village 老人社群；老人村 / Your pension plan provides a cash lump sum at retirement. 你的養老金計劃讓你在退休時一次性獲得一筆現金。/ His father was now living in retirement in France. 他父親已退休住在法國。o She has found a new hobby in her retirement. 她退休後找到了一個新的消遣。/ He remained in the post until his retirement last year. 直到去年退休，他一直留在那個崗位上。/ her retirement as sales director 她退休時是銷售主管 / The age of retirement for all employees is 60. 所有僱員的退休年齡都是60歲。<span class="definition">2 [U, sing.] 退休生活：</span>to provide for retirement 為退休生活作準備 / We all wish you a long and happy retirement. 我們大家祝願你的退休生活長久而幸福。/ Up to a third of one's life is now being spent in retirement. 現在人們一生中能有三分之一的時間過退休生活。/ He provided for a comfortable retirement by selling the business. 他把企業賣掉，過上了舒適的退休生活。/ The website helps you plan your retirement. 這個網站幫助你規劃退休生活。/ investments to fund their retirement 為養老而進行的投資 / I intend to spend my retirement playing golf. 我打算退休後打高爾夫球。/ She's saving for her retirement. 她在為退休存錢。/ I've been thinking about where I might like to spend my retirement years. 我一直在想退休後去哪兒生活比較好。

<span class="vocabulary">**outgoing**</span> ['aʊtɡəʊɪŋ] 
<span class="definition">adj. 只用於名詞前，即將卸任某一責任職位的（即不再對該職位上的事務之對錯或功過負責的）：</span>the outgoing president, the outgoing government 即將下臺的總統；即將倒臺的政府

周邊詞彙補充：
· depose可指將某人（尤指統治者或政治領導人）從權力的寶座上趕下臺，如廢黜；
· deposition可指清除某人，尤指將統治者從其權力的寶座上廢黜、罷免；
· abdicate可指（國王或女王）退位；
· exclusion可指孩子由於行為惡劣而被學校開除學籍。
