☀ <span class="category">**確定、必定的形容詞：**</span>
<span class="vocabulary">**certain**</span> ['sə:tn] 
<span class="definition">adj. 1 certain (that ...) / certain (to do sth) / certain (of sth/of doing sth) 能夠依賴其發生或屬實的，即確實，確定，肯定：</span>It is certain that they will agree. 他們肯定會同意。/ She looks certain to win an Oscar. 看來奧斯卡金像獎非她莫屬。/ The climbers face certain death if the rescue today is unsuccessful. 救援行動如果今天不能成功，登山隊員必死無疑。<span class="definition">2 certain (that ...) / certain (of/about sth) 堅定地相信某事，沒有懷疑，即確信，確定無疑：</span>She wasn’t certain (that) he had seen her. 她不敢肯定他見過她。/ To my certain knowledge he was somewhere else at the time. 我敢肯定他當時在別處。/ Make certain that you lock the door if you go out. 如果你出門，一定要把門鎖好。

<span class="vocabulary">**sure**</span> [ʃʊə] 
<span class="definition">adj. 1 sure to do sth 確定去做某事，或確定發生，如一定，必定：</span>The exhibition is sure to be popular. 這一展覽肯定受歡迎。/ It’s sure to rain. 一準會下雨。<span class="definition">2 常用於名詞前，可被信任的，或可被依賴的：</span>It’s a sure sign of economic recovery. 這是經濟復甦的確切跡象。/ There’s only one sure way to do it. 做這件事只有一個保險的辦法。/ He is a sure bet for the presidential nominations. 他是總統候選人提名的鐵定人選。
           
<span class="vocabulary">**definite**</span> [ˈdefɪnət]
<span class="definition">adj. 1 ~ (that…)（決定或安排）確定的、肯定的、不可能改變的：</span>Can you give me a definite answer by tomorrow? 你最晚明天能給我一個確定的答覆嗎？/ Is it definite that he's leaving? 他肯定要離開嗎？/ a definite offer of a job 明確給予一份工作 / They have very definite ideas on how to bring up children. 關於如何培養孩子，他們有非常明確的想法。/ Her Royal Highness has definite views about most things. 女王陛下對大多數事情都有明確的觀點。<span class="definition">2（證據或資訊）確鑿的、確切的：</span>We didn't have any definite proof. 我們沒有任何確實的證據。/ If you have any definite news of my husband, please let me know. 如果有我丈夫的確切訊息，請一定告訴我。/ I've heard rumours, but nothing definite. 我聽到一些流言，但都不確定。/ I'm not sure─I can find out for definite if you like. 我沒把握——如果你願意我可以去核實。
           
<span class="vocabulary">**distinct**</span> [dɪˈstɪŋkt]
<span class="definition">adj. [只用於名詞前] 確實的、確定無疑的：</span>Being tall gave Tony a distinct advantage. 託尼個子高是個明顯的優勢。/ I had the distinct impression I was being watched. 我確實感到有人在監視我。/ A strike is now a distinct possibility. 目前罷工確有可能發生。
           
<span class="vocabulary">**unmistakable**</span> [ˌʌnmɪˈsteɪkəbl]
<span class="definition">adj. 不會弄錯的；確定無疑的：</span>He didn't give his name, but the voice was unmistakable. 他沒留下姓名，但是那個聲音是絕不會錯的。/ the unmistakable smell of marijuana drifted down 大麻的氣味飄了下來，肯定不會弄錯的 / The main symptom is a scarlet rash that's quite unmistakable. 主要症狀是猩紅熱疹，這是確定無疑的。

<span class="vocabulary">**bound**</span> [baʊnd] 
<span class="definition">adj. bound to do/be sth 不用於名詞前，一定或很可能（會發生、會去做某事或會是…）。一般只強調一種客觀的必然性（而sure即可強調客觀的必然性，又可強調主觀上的確信）：</span>It’s bound to be sunny again tomorrow. 明天肯定又是陽光燦爛。/ You’ve done so much work--you’re bound to pass the exam. 你下了這麼大工夫，考試絕對沒問題。/ It was bound to happen sooner or later. 這事遲早都是要發生的。/ You’re bound to be nervous the first time. 第一次總是會緊張的。
           
<span class="vocabulary">**doomed**</span> [duːmd]
<span class="definition">adj. 某件壞事註定要發生的，（某人或某事）註定要失敗（或毀滅）的：</span>Their plans seemed doomed to failure. 他們的計劃好像註定要失敗。/ The project was doomed to failure from the start. 這項工程從一開始就註定要失敗。/ He knew that if he lived, he would be doomed to spend the war as a prisoner. 他知道如果他活下來，他將註定以戰俘的身份度過戰爭。/ The species was doomed to extinction. 這一物種註定要滅絕。/ Fireman battled through the smoke in a doomed attempt to rescue the children. 消防隊員奮力穿過煙霧企圖去救孩子們，而這注定是要失敗的。/ I used to pour time and energy into projects that were doomed from the start. 我過去常常將大量時間和精力投入到從開始就註定要失敗的計劃中。

<span class="vocabulary">**necessary**</span> ['nesɪsərɪ] 
<span class="definition">adj. 只用於名詞前，必定存在或發生且不可避免的，即必然的，無法避免的，相當於inevitable：</span>This is a necessary consequence of progress. 這是發展的必然後果。
                      
<span class="vocabulary">**inevitable**</span> [ɪnˈevɪtəbl]
<span class="definition">adj. 不可避免的；無法防止的：</span>It was an inevitable consequence of the decision. 那是這個決定的必然後果。/ It was inevitable that there would be job losses. 裁員已是不可避免的事。/ A rise in the interest rates seems inevitable. 提高利率似乎是不可避免的。/ If the case succeeds, it is inevitable that other trials will follow. 如果該案勝訴,其他審判必然隨之而來。/ The defeat had inevitable consequences for British policy. 戰敗對英國政策不可避免地產生了影響。/ The scandal made her resignation inevitable. 醜聞使得她不得不辭職。/ They came to see defeat as inevitable. 他們逐漸認識到失敗是不可避免的。/ She bowed to the inevitable (= accepted a situation in which she had no choice）and resigned. 她迫於無奈，只得辭職。
           
<span class="vocabulary">**unavoidable**</span> [ˌʌnəˈvɔɪdəbl]
<span class="definition">adj. 無法避免的；無法防止的：</span>unavoidable delays 不可避免的延誤 / The recession has resulted in an unavoidable increase in spending on unemployment benefit. 經濟蕭條導致失業救濟金的支出不可避免地增加。/ Managers said the job losses were unavoidable. 經理們說裁員是不可避免的。/ This latest incident makes his dismissal unavoidable. 最近的這次事件使他無法逃過被免職的命運。
           
<span class="vocabulary">**incidental**</span> [ˌɪnsɪˈdentl]
<span class="definition">adj. [術語] ~ to sth 作為某事的自然結果而發生的，如伴隨而來的、免不了的：</span>These risks are incidental to the work of a firefighter. 這些風險是擔任消防員不可避免的。

<span class="vocabulary">**inexorable**</span> [ɪnˈeksərəbl]
<span class="definition">adj. [正式]（一個過程）不可阻擋的或無法改變的：</span>the seemingly inexorable rise in unemployment 看似不可阻擋的失業增長 / the inexorable rise of crime 阻遏不了的犯罪趨勢 / to prevent the inexorable slide into war 防止滑向戰爭的深淵 / his steady, inexorable decline 他的身體狀況一天不如一天 / Who can halt Woods' inexorable progress towards yet another championship? 伍茲的勢頭不可阻擋，誰能中止他邁向下一個冠軍的腳步？/ the inexorable march of time 時間的腳步無法阻擋 / inexorable logic 必然邏輯

<span class="vocabulary">**clear**</span> [klɪə] 
<span class="definition">adj. clear (about/on sth) /clear what, how, whether, etc. 沒有（或不感覺到）懷疑或困惑的，即無疑的，清楚的，明白的：</span>Are you clear about the arrangements for tomorrow? 你清楚明天的安排嗎？/ I'm still not clear what the job involves. 我仍然不明白這項工作包括哪些內容。/ Are you clear on that point? 在那一點上你確定嗎？/ My memory is not clear on that point. 那一點我記不清了。

<span class="vocabulary">**absolute**</span> ['æbsəlu:t] 
<span class="definition">adj. 1 只用於名詞前，（尤用於英語口語中）強調自己正在說的內容，如確實的、十足的：</span>There’s absolute rubbish on television tonight. 今晚的電視節目簡直糟糕透頂。/ He must earn an absolute fortune. 他準會賺一大筆錢。<span class="definition">2 明確且沒有任何疑問（或混淆）的：</span>There was no absolute proof. 沒有確鑿的證據。/ He taught us that the laws of physics were absolute. 他教導我們說，物理定律是不容置疑的。/ The divorce became absolute last week. 離婚在上週已成定局。

<span class="vocabulary">**set**</span> [set] 
<span class="definition">adj. 常作前置定語，指安排好的，確定的，固定的：</span>a set number of computers 固定數目的電腦 / to follow a set pattern 遵循一種固定的模式 / a set period of training 一段固定的培訓期 / He was given a set job to do. 分配給他的工作是預先確定好的。

<span class="vocabulary">**automatic**</span> [͵ɔ:tə'mætɪk] 
<span class="definition">adj. 由於某種行為或情況而必然發生的，也可叫當然的：</span>A fine for this offence is automatic. 這種違法行為當然要罰款。/ Promotion was almost automatic after two or three years. 兩三年後幾乎是自動獲得晉升。

<span class="vocabulary">**hard**</span> [hɑːd] 
<span class="definition">adj. 只用於名詞前，確實屬實且基於可證實的資訊的，如確鑿的，可證實的，可靠的：</span>hard evidence 確鑿的證據 / hard facts 可靠的事實根據

<span class="vocabulary">**sacred**</span> ['seɪkrɪd] 
<span class="definition">adj.（某事物）太重要以致於不容更改或干涉的：</span>My memories are sacred. 我的記憶不能泯滅。
           
<span class="vocabulary">**undeniable**</span> [ˌʌndɪˈnaɪəbl]
<span class="definition">adj. 不可否認的：</span>He had undeniable charm. 他具有不可否認的魅力。/ It is an undeniable fact that crime is increasing. 犯罪在增加是無可爭辯的事實。/ Her charm is undeniable. 她的魅力毋庸置疑。
           
<span class="vocabulary">**assured**</span> [əˈʃʊəd; əˈʃɔ:d; 美 əˈʃʊrd]
<span class="definition">adj. 1 一定會發生的，即確定的：</span>Success seemed assured. 看來已必勝無疑。/ Our victory is assured; nothing can stop us. 我們必勝，我們銳不可當。/ Yesterday, her future seemed assured. 昨天，她的未來似乎已經在握。<span class="definition">2 ~ of sth（一個人）肯定會得到某事物或達成某事的：</span>You are assured of a warm welcome at this hotel. 你在這家旅館肯定會受到熱情歡迎。/ Laura Davies is assured of a place in Europe's team. 勞拉·戴維斯確信能在歐洲隊中佔據一席之地。/ Bickerstaffe looks assured of being elected general secretary. 比克斯塔夫看上去對於當選總書記志在必得。
           
<span class="vocabulary">**conclusive**</span> [kənˈklu:sɪv]
<span class="definition">adj. 結論性的；不容置疑的；確鑿的：</span>conclusive evidence, conclusive proof, conclusive results 確鑿的證據；不容置疑的結果 / Her attorneys claim there is no conclusive evidence that any murders took place. 她的律師聲稱並無確鑿證據表明發生了謀殺。/ The argument was far from conclusive. 這個論據很難令人信服。/ Research on the matter is far from conclusive. 對這一問題的研究還遠遠沒有定論。

☀ <span class="category">**確定、必定的副詞：**</span>
<span class="vocabulary">**sure**</span> [ʃʊə] 
<span class="definition">adv. [尤美，非正式] 1 用於強調你正在說的事情，如確實，的確：</span>Boy, it sure is hot. 好傢伙，這天兒可真熱。/ ‘Amazing view.’ ‘Sure is.’ “景色真美。”“沒錯。”/ He sure looked unhappy. 他的確顯得不高興。/ <span class="definition">2 用於回答他人剛剛對你表示的感謝，如不客氣，應該的：</span>‘Thanks for the ride.’ ‘Sure--anytime.’ “謝謝你載我過來。”“不用客氣，隨時願意效勞。”

<span class="vocabulary">**indeed**</span> [ɪn'di:d] 
<span class="definition">adv. 1 用於強調一個肯定的陳述或答覆：</span>‘Was he very angry?’ ‘Indeed he was.’ “他很生氣嗎？”“的確很生氣。”/ It is indeed a remarkable achievement. 這的確是個非凡的成就。<span class="definition">2 [尤英] 用於very和形容詞或副詞之後，以強調一個陳述、描述等：</span>Thank you very much indeed! 真的很感謝您！/ I was very sad indeed to hear of your father’s death. 聽到令尊大人去世，我感到非常難過。<span class="definition">3 [尤英，非正式] 用於表明你對某事感到驚訝，或覺得某事物荒謬：</span>A ghost indeed! I’ve never heard anything so silly. 真是見鬼！我可從沒聽說過這樣無聊的事。

<span class="vocabulary">**just**</span> [dӡʌst] 
<span class="definition">adv. [非正式] 確實：</span>It’s just wonderful! 實在是太好了！

☀ <span class="category">**確定、必定的動詞：**</span>
<span class="vocabulary">**fix**</span> [fɪks] 
<span class="definition">vt. 決定把某件事放在某個日期、時間，或確定…的數量：</span>to fix the date 確定日期 / to fix a time 確定時間 / to fix the price 確定價格
           
<span class="vocabulary">**pinpoint**</span> [ˈpɪnpɔɪnt]
<span class="definition">vt. 明確指出，確定（某事發生的準確位置或時間）：</span>He was able to pinpoint on the map the site of the medieval village. 他能在地圖上準確找出那個中世紀村莊的位置。/ I could pinpoint his precise location on a map. 我能在地圖上指明他的準確位置。/ With this you can pinpoint the precise location of the sound. 你用這個就能找出聲音的確切位置。/ Computers pinpointed where the shells were coming from. 計算機確定了炮彈發射的位置。<span class="definition">vt. 準確解釋原因，或準確描述：</span>It was almost impossible to pinpoint the cause of death. 幾乎不可能確認死因。/ Stress at work was pinpointed as the cause of his illness. 工作壓力被確定為他生病的原因。/ if you can pinpoint exactly what the anger is about 如果你能確切地解釋憤怒從何而來 / The report pinpointed the areas most in need of help. 報告精確說明了亟待援助的地區。
           
<span class="vocabulary">**locate**</span> [ləʊˈkeɪt; 美 ˈloʊkeɪt]
<span class="definition">vt. 找出或確定…的準確位置：</span>The mechanic located the fault immediately. 機修工立即找到了出故障的地方。/ Rescue planes are trying to locate the missing sailors. 救援飛機正在努力查明失蹤水手的下落。/ The scientists want to locate the position of the gene on a chromosome. 科學家們希望找出該基因在染色體中的位置。/ We've simply been unable to locate him. 我們就是沒辦法找到他。/ The machine can accurately locate radioactive material. 這臺機器能準確地確定放射性物質的位置。/ We haven't yet been able to locate a suitable site. 我們仍然沒能找到一個合適的場所。/ Some stars are very easy to locate with a telescope. 有些星星用望遠鏡可以很容易地找到。/ She took time to help me locate research materials. 她花時間幫我找研究材料。
           
<span class="vocabulary">**navigate**</span> [ˈnævɪgeɪt]
<span class="definition">vt.&vi.（例如用地圖）確定（自己、船、飛機、汽車等的）位置和方向：</span>to navigate by the stars 根據星辰確定航向 / Captain Cook safely navigated his ship without accident for 100 voyages. 庫克船長駕駛的船安全出航100次無事故。/ The purpose of the visit was to navigate into an ice-filled fiord. 這次出行是為了能駛入冰雪覆蓋的峽灣。/ I'll drive, and you can navigate. 我開車，你引路。/ When travelling on fast roads at night it is impossible to drive and navigate at the same time. 晚上在高速路上行駛時根本做不到邊開車邊找路。/ the relief at successfully navigating across the Golden Gate Bridge to arrive here 駕車成功駛過金門橋抵達這裡後的輕鬆 / She became expert in driving and navigating across the desert. 她成了驅車穿越沙漠的高手。/ strategies to navigate your way through the forest 穿越森林的策略 <span class="definition">vt.（魚、動物或昆蟲）找到正確的移動方向：</span>In tests, the bees navigate back home after being placed in a field a mile away. 在多次試驗中，放置於一英里以外田野中的蜜蜂都能飛回蜂巢。/ These birds navigate by the sun. 這些鳥靠太陽確定方向。

<span class="vocabulary">**set**</span> [set] 
<span class="definition">vt. 安排、確定或決定：</span>to set a deadline of this Friday 設定本週五為最後期限 / to set a date for the wedding 確定婚期 / to set strict limits on smoking in public places 為在公共場合吸菸設定嚴格的限制
            
<span class="vocabulary">**assure**</span> [əˈʃʊə(r); -ʃɔ:(r); 美 əˈʃʊr]
<span class="definition">vt. ~ yourself (of sth) 使自己對某事確定，如弄清、查明：</span>He assured himself of her safety. 他確定她是安全的。/ She assured herself that the letter was still in the drawer. 她查清楚信仍然在抽屜裡。          

<span class="vocabulary">**clinch**</span> [klɪntʃ]
<span class="definition">vt.（將不確定的事情）確定：</span>‘I'll pay your airfare. ’ ‘Okay, that clinches it ─I'll come with you.’ “我會幫你付飛機票。”“好，就這麼說定——我跟你一起去。”
           
<span class="vocabulary">**assign**</span> [əˈsaɪn]
<span class="definition">vt. ~ sth to sth 確定某事物有某個價值、數值或功能，或確定某事發生在某時或某地：</span>Assign a different colour to each different type of information. 給每類資訊分別確定一種顏色。/ The painting cannot be assigned an exact date. 這幅畫的年代確定不了。

<span class="vocabulary">**mean**</span> [mi:n] 
<span class="definition">vt. 註定（要發生）：</span>I was never meant for the army. 我根本就不是塊當兵的料。/ Philip and Kim were meant for each other. 菲利普和金真是天生的一對。

<span class="vocabulary">**determine**</span> [dɪ'tə:mɪn] 
<span class="definition">[正式] 正式決定或安排某事，如確定、安排、裁決等：</span>A date for the meeting has yet to be determined. 會議日期尚待確定。/ The court determined (that) the defendant should pay the legal costs. 法庭裁決由被告支付訴訟費用。
           
<span class="vocabulary">**compel**</span> [kəmˈpel]
<span class="definition">vt. [正式] 用於feel compelled to結構，表示使某事成為必須：</span>I feel compelled to write and tell you how much I enjoyed your book. 我覺得必須寫信告訴你我是多麼欣賞你的書。/ Dickens felt compelled to return to the stage for a final good-bye. 迪金斯覺得必須返回舞臺作最後的道別。/ I felt morally compelled to help. 我覺得從道義上我必須幫忙。

<span class="vocabulary">**must**</span> [mʌst] 
<span class="definition">modal verb. 1 用於表明某事是有必要的（或非常重要的），如必須：</span>All visitors must report to reception. 所有來賓必須到接待處報到。/ Cars must not park in front of the entrance. 車輛不得停在入口處。/ You mustn’t say things like that. 你千萬別說那樣的話。/ I must go to the bank and get some money. 我得上銀行取點兒錢。<span class="definition">2 用於表明某事是很可能的（或符合邏輯的），即一定：</span>You must be hungry after all that walking. 走了這麼遠的路，你一定餓了吧。/ She must have left already. 她準是走了。<span class="definition">3 [尤英] 用於推薦某人去做某事（因為你認為這是一個好主意），即應該、得：</span>You simply must read this book. 這本書你可一定要看一看。/ We must get together soon for lunch. 我們得馬上集合去吃午飯。

<span class="vocabulary">**shall**</span> [ʃæl] 
<span class="definition">modal verb.（通常按照規定或規律）某事一定會發生：</span>The president shall hold office for five years. 總統將任職五年。

<span class="vocabulary">**would**</span> [wʊd] 
<span class="definition">modal verb.（因擁有充分的理由而）認為或猜想某事屬實：</span>You wouldn’t know him. 你一定不認識他。/ His fans would already be familiar with Caroline. 他的崇拜者們一定已熟悉卡羅琳。

<span class="vocabulary">**can**</span> [kæn, kən] 
<span class="definition">modal verb. 1 用於否定句，表示你確定某事不真實：</span>That can’t be Mary--she’s in New York. 那不可能是瑪麗——她在紐約呢。/ He can’t have slept through all that noise. 他不可能在那種鬧哄哄的環境裡睡好覺。<span class="definition">2 [非正式] 用於表示對方必須做某事（尤指在你生氣時）：</span>You can shut up or get out! 你給我閉嘴，要不然就滾出去！

<span class="vocabulary">**be**</span> [bi:] 
<span class="definition">auxiliary verb. be to do sth 用於表明必須做的事：</span>I am to call them once I reach the airport. 我一到機場就得給他們打電話。

<span class="vocabulary">**do**</span> [du:] 
<span class="definition">auxiliary verb. 句中無其他助動詞時，用以加強語氣（即強調正在說的內容）：</span>He does look tired. 他的確顯得很疲倦。/ She did at least write to say thank you. 她至少還寫了通道謝。/ Do shut up!（英）把嘴給我閉上！

<span class="vocabulary">**to**</span> [tu:] 
<span class="definition">infinitive marker. am, is, are, was, were to 表示必須、一定做某事：</span>You are not to talk during the exam. 考試期間不許說話。

周邊詞彙補充：
· doom可表示使…註定失敗（或遭殃、死亡等）。

<span class="vocabulary">**certainty**</span> [ˈsɜ:tnti; 美 ˈsɜ:rtnti]
<span class="definition">n. 1 [C] 確定的事、必然的事：</span>political, moral certainties 政治上確定無疑的事；道德上確定無疑的事 / Her return to the team now seems a certainty. 她的歸隊現在似乎已成定局。/ There are no certainties in modern Europe. 當代歐洲沒有什麼是確定無疑的。/ The collapse of old certainties is reshaping the political parties. 陳規舊矩的崩塌正在重塑各個政黨。/ It's a virtual certainty that essential foodstuffs will go up in price. 幾乎可以肯定基本食品的價格將會上漲。/ The one certainty left in a changing world is death. 在不斷變化的世界中唯一不變的就是死亡。/ A general election became a certainty three weeks ago. 三個星期前舉行普選已確定無疑。<span class="definition">2 [U] 某事一定會發生的一種事實，如必然（性）、確定（性）：</span>the certainty of more violence and bloodshed 發生更多暴力和流血事件的必然性 <span class="definition">3 [U] 確定或有把握的狀態：</span>I can't say with any certainty where I'll be next week. 我根本不知道我下週會在什麼地方。/ It's difficult to predict with any degree of certainty how much it will cost. 要花多少錢很難作出任何確切的預測。/ I couldn't say with absolute certainty that he's here. 我沒有絕對的把握說他現在在這兒。/ I have told them with absolute certainty there'll be no change of policy. 我已經十分肯定地告訴他們，政策不會變。/ There is too little certainty about the present state of the German economy. 德國當前的經濟非常不穩定。/ There is no certainty that the president's removal would end the civil war. 總統的下臺是否會結束內戰現在還很難說。/ There seems to be a lack of certainty over what we should do. 看來我們應該做什麼還無法確定。
                     
<span class="vocabulary">**confidence**</span> [ˈkɒnfɪdəns; 美 ˈkɑ:n-]
<span class="definition">n. [U] 自己對某事感到有把握、肯定：</span>I can say with confidence that such rumors were totally groundless. 我可以肯定地說這樣的謠言完全沒有根據。/ They could not say with confidence that he would be able to walk again after the accident. 他們不能肯定他經過那場事故後還能行走。/ He expressed his confidence that they would win. 他表示了自己的信心：他們必定取勝。
            
<span class="vocabulary">**necessity**</span> [nəˈsesəti]
<span class="definition">n. 1 [U] ~ (for sth) / ~ (of sth/of doing sth) / ~ (to do sth)（某事發生的）必然性：</span>This is, of necessity, a brief and incomplete account. 這必然是一份簡略的、不完全的描述。<span class="definition">2 [C, usually sing.] 不可避免的情況或行動：</span>Living in London, he felt, was an unfortunate necessity. 他覺得在倫敦生活是迫於無奈。/ The President pleaded that strong rule from the centre was a regrettable, but temporary necessity. 總統辯解說，實行高度的中央集權雖不盡如人意，但暫時來說卻是必要的。

<span class="vocabulary">**inevitability**</span> [ɪnˌevɪtəˈbɪləti]
<span class="definition">n. [U, sing.] 不可避免；必然：</span>We are all bound by the inevitability of death. 我們早晚都會有一死。/ She was learning to accept the inevitability of death. 她正在學著接受死亡的必然性。/ Success is an inevitability for us. 勝利對我們來說是必然的。/ There was an inevitability about their defeat. 他們的失敗自有其必然。/ tragic inevitability 悲劇的必然性 / historical inevitability 歷史的必然 / The tragedy had a certain inevitability about it. 這一悲劇的發生有一定的必然性。
           
<span class="vocabulary">**inevitable**</span> [ɪnˈevɪtəbl]
<span class="definition">n. [sing.] the inevitable可指不可避免的事、必然發生的事：</span>You have to accept the inevitable. 你得接受必然發生的事。/ The inevitable happened--I forgot my passport. 逃不掉的事情發生了——我忘了帶護照。

<span class="vocabulary">**fix**</span> [fɪks] 
<span class="definition">n. [sing.] 對船或飛機的位置的確定：</span>to get a fix on the plane’s position 確定飛機的位置
           
<span class="vocabulary">**location**</span> [ləʊˈkeɪʃn; 美 loʊ-]
<span class="definition">n. [U] 定位：</span>瞭解即可
 
<span class="vocabulary">**must**</span> [mʌst] 
<span class="definition">n. [usually pl.] [非正式] 必須做（或看、買等）的事情或事物：</span>His new novel is a must for all lovers of crime fiction. 他的新作是所有犯罪小說愛好者的必讀書。
 
<span class="vocabulary">**definite**</span> [ˈdefɪnət]
<span class="definition">n. [sing.] [非正式] 你確信會發生的事；肯定會做某事的人：</span>‘We're moving our office to Glasgow. ’ ‘That's a definite, is it? ’ “我們的辦事處要搬到格拉斯哥去。”“這事兒定了，是嗎？” / ‘Is Sarah coming to the party? ’ ‘Yes, she's a definite. ’ “薩拉要來參加聚會嗎？”“是的，她肯定來。”
           
<span class="vocabulary">**territory**</span> [ˈterətri; 美 -tɔ:ri]
<span class="definition">n. [sing.] come with the ~ 是難免的事、是必然的事：</span>Doing human rights work is risky business. That comes with the territory. 做人權工作是要冒風險的。這是難免的。/ You can't expect not to have a debate; that's what comes with the territory in a democracy. 不要指望不會發生辯論，那在民主社會已成為必然。
 
<span class="vocabulary">**please**</span> [pli:z] 
<span class="definition">exclamation. 用於加強請求或陳述的語氣：</span>Please don’t leave me here alone. 請千萬別把我一個人留在這兒。/ Please, please don’t forget. 請千萬不要忘記呀。/ Please, I don’t understand what I have to do. 我的確不明白我該做什麼。
