☀ <span class="category">**運氣、運程的名詞：**</span>
<span class="vocabulary">**fortune**</span> ['fɔ:tʃn] 
<span class="definition">n. 1 [U] 機會、運氣，尤指影響人生的機會或運氣。是一個常規用詞：</span>bad, ill fortune 壞運氣；厄運 / to bring good fortune 帶來好運 / Fortune smiled on me that day. 那天我交了好運。<span class="definition">2 [C, U] 作可數名詞時常用複數，一個人、家庭、國家等所經歷的好的和壞的事情，即際遇、命運、局勢、運勢等：</span>the changing fortunes of the film industry 電影業的變遷 / the fortunes of war 戰爭的局勢 / a reversal of fortune(s) 命運的扭轉 / political fortunes 政治命運 / fans who follow the fortunes of their chosen team 與自己選擇的球隊共命運的球迷 <span class="definition">3 [C] 一個人的命運或前途：</span>She can tell your fortune by looking at the lines on your hand. 她可憑看手紋替你算命。/ He went to have their fortunes read. 他去找人算命。/ fortune teller, fortune telling 算命者；算命

<span class="vocabulary">**chance**</span> [tʃɑːns] 
<span class="definition">n. [U] 某事的發生沒有任何（你所看到的或理解的）原因，可稱為運氣、機緣。不如fortune常用：</span>By a happy chance he bumped into an old friend on the plane. 他很幸運，竟然在飛機上碰到了一個老朋友。/ I got most answers right through sheer chance. 我純粹是靠運氣答對了大部分題目。

<span class="vocabulary">**luck**</span> [lʌk] 
<span class="definition">n. [U] 機遇；讓好事或壞事發生在人們身上的力量，可稱為命運、運氣，相當於fortune：</span>to have good luck, to have bad luck 運氣好；運氣壞
           
<span class="vocabulary">**lottery**</span> [ˈlɒtəri; 美 ˈlɑ:t-]
<span class="definition">n. [sing.] [常貶] 聽天由命的事；全憑運氣的事：</span>Some people think that marriage is a lottery. 有些人認為婚姻靠的是運氣。/ The stockmarket is a lottery. 股市買賣全看運氣。/ Which judges are assigned to a case is always a bit of a lottery. 一個案子安排哪些法官來審理，往往有點看運氣。
           
<span class="vocabulary">**destiny**</span> [ˈdestəni]
<span class="definition">n. 1 [C] 命運、天數：</span>the destinies of nations 國家的命運 / We are masters of our own destiny. 我們是自己命運的主宰者。/ He wanted to take control of his own destiny. 他想要掌握自己的命運。/ She set up her own business because she wanted to be in control of her own destiny. 她建立了自己的公司，因為她要掌握自己的命運。/ It is my destiny one day to be king. 我命中註定有一天要成為國王。/ human destiny 人類的宿命 / He was driven on by a strong sense of destiny. 他被一種強烈的宿命感所驅使。/ He came to Paris and found his true destiny as a poet. 他來到巴黎，命中註定成為一個詩人。/ I believe that only God knows what our ultimate destinies are. 我相信只有上帝知道我們的最終宿命。/ The time was right for him to meet his destiny. 他命中註定的時刻就要到了。/ She felt that she had fulfilled her destiny. 她感覺她的宿命應驗了。/ There's not much you can do but accept your destiny. 除了接受命運的安排以外，你沒有太多可做的事情。/ No man can escape his destiny. 沒有人能逃脫自己的宿命。/ Can we control our own destiny? 我們能掌握自己的命運嗎？/ It was a decision which could have changed my destiny. 這是一個本來會改變我命運的決定。/ Something was about to happen that would shape her destiny. 決定她命運的事情即將發生。/ the destiny that awaited him 等待著他的命運 / Her destiny lay in that city. 她的命運繫於那座城市。<span class="definition">2 [U] 天意、主宰事物的力量、命運之神：</span>I believe there's some force guiding us─call it God, destiny or fate. 我總認為有某種力量在指引著我們——叫它上帝也罷，天意也罷，還是命運也罷。/ Is it destiny that brings people together, or is it accident? 人們的相遇是天意還是偶然？

被判刑的人只好接受自己的命運。/ Instead of just bemoaning your fate, why not do something to change it? 與其悲嘆自己的命運，何不行動起來去改變它呢？/ She has taken steps to control her own fate. 她已採取措施以掌控自己的命運。/ He had signed his to their fate. 將軍們不再管那些士兵，讓他們生死由命。/ The convicts awaited their fate in prison. 已決犯在獄中等待著自己的命運。/ the prophet who predicts fate and can see the future 能預測命運、預知將來的預言家 / The fate of the African wild dog hangs in the balance (= is uncertain). 非洲野狗的命運還是個未知數。/ This team's fate depends on how it performs today. 該隊的命運取決於它今天在比賽中的表現。/ The jury held the fate of the accused in their hands. 陪審團掌握著被告的命運。/ Getting married seemed a fate worse than death. 結婚似乎比死還要難受。/ His fate rests in the hands of the judges. 他的命運掌握在法官們的手中。<span class="definition">2 [U] 命運、天數、天意、定數：</span>Fate was kind to me that day. 那天我很幸運。/ By a strange twist of fate, Andy and I were on the same plane. 由於命運的奇特安排，我和安迪乘坐了同一架飛機。/ I see no use quarrelling with fate. 我看不出和命運抗爭有什麼用。/ the fickleness of fate 命運的無常 / He believed that the 僅僅幾周後，她再次遭受命運打擊，不能參加比賽。/ Anne accepted the cruel hand that fate had dealt her. 安妮接受了殘酷的命運。/ Fate took a hand in (= influenced) the outcome of the championship. 冠軍爭奪賽的最終結果系天命所歸。/ Little did she know <span class="vocabulary">**streak**</span> [stri:k]
<span class="definition">n. [C]（尤指體育比賽或賭博中）一系列的好運或黴運：</span>hot（尤美）, lucky streak 好運連連 / unbeaten（尤美）, undefeated（尤美）, winner streak 不敗；連勝 / The Yankees continued their six-game winner streak. 揚基隊取得了他們的六連勝。/ losing, winless streak 接連失利；連敗 / a losing streak that had extended back to June 1 從6月1日開始就頻頻失利 / Selby finally ended their five-game winless streak. 塞爾比總算終結了他們隊的五連敗。/ scoreless streak（美）連續不進球得分的階段 / The team has been on a winning streak since it won against Lazio. 自從打敗拉齊奧隊以後，這支球隊連連取勝。/ a streak of good luck 運氣好的一段時間 / The casinos had better watch out since I'm obviously on a lucky streak! 很明顯我現在手氣正旺，賭場最好當心了！
           
<span class="vocabulary">**horoscope**</span> [ˈhɒrəskəʊp; 美 ˈhɔ:rəskoʊp; ˈhɑ:r-]
<span class="definition">n. [C] 占星預言、星象預言（指某人誕生時各天體的位置，用以占卜命運）：</span>To cast someone's horoscope, you need to know their date and exact time of birth. 要想用星相替某人算命、就得知道他確切的出生日期和時辰。/ to read horoscope 看星相 / What does your horoscope say? 你的星相說了些什麼?

 <span class="vocabulary">**star**</span> [stɑː] 
<span class="definition">n. [pl.] stars可表示星象，即根據某人出生時各天體的位置而對該人將來的命運做出的描述，相當於horoscope：</span>There was nothing in my stars to say I’d have health problems! 我的星相上沒說我會有健康問題！

☀ <span class="category">**幸運的形容詞：**</span>
<span class="vocabulary">**lucky**</span> ['lʌkɪ] 
<span class="definition">adj. 1 lucky (to do sth) / lucky (that ...) 有好運的、幸運的，相當於fortunate：</span>She was lucky enough to be chosen for the team. 她很幸運被選中加入該隊。/ You were lucky (that) you spotted the danger in time. 你幸好及時發現了險情。<span class="definition">2 lucky (for sb) (that ...) 由好運所帶來的：</span>It was lucky for us that we were able to go. 我們能去是我們的運氣好。/ a lucky escape 幸運的逃脫 / a lucky guess 僥倖猜中 <span class="definition">3 帶來好運的：</span>a lucky charm 吉祥符

<span class="vocabulary">**fortunate**</span> ['fɔ:tʃənɪt] 
<span class="definition">adj. fortunate (to do sth) / fortunate (in having ...) / fortunate (for sb) (that ...) 擁有（或帶來）一個優勢、一個機會或一個好運氣等的，即幸運的，吉利的，相當於lucky，二者未見明顯差別：</span>I was fortunate in having a good teacher. 我很幸運，有位好老師。/ It was fortunate for us that the rain stopped. 我們很幸運，雨停了。

<span class="vocabulary">**merciful**</span> ['mə:sɪfl] 
<span class="definition">adj.（事件）還算幸運的（尤其因為該事件能終結某人的問題或痛苦）：</span>to come to a merciful end 有一個還算不錯的結果 / to come as a merciful release 算是一種幸運的解脫

<span class="vocabulary">**happy**</span> ['hæpɪ] 
<span class="definition">adj. 幸運的；成功的。相當於fortunate。是一個簡單用詞：</span>By a happy coincidence, we arrived at exactly the same time. 碰巧運氣好，我們剛好同時到達。/ He is in the happy position of never having to worry about money. 他運氣真好，從來不用為金錢操心。

☀ <span class="category">**幸運的其它詞性：**</span>
<span class="vocabulary">**luck**</span> [lʌk] 
<span class="definition">n. [U] 偶然發生在你身上的好事情，而非由於你自身的努力或能力，即好運、幸運、僥倖：</span>So far I have had no luck with finding a job. 我找工作一直不走運。/ We wish her luck in her new career. 我們祝願她在新的職業中一帆風順。/ You’re in luck--there’s one ticket left. 你運氣不錯——還剩一張票。/ You’re out of luck. She’s not here. 真不巧，她不在。<span class="definition">vi. [美，非正式] luck out 可指走運或交好運：</span>I guess I really lucked out when I met her. 我想，我遇到她真是交了好運。

<span class="vocabulary">**mercy**</span> ['mə:sɪ] 
<span class="definition">n. [C, usually sing.] [非正式] 一種被感激的事件或情況（常由於其阻止了不幸的事發生），可叫做幸運或恩惠：</span>It’s a mercy she wasn’t seriously hurt. 幸運的是她傷勢不重。/ be grateful for small mercies, be thankful for small mercies 感激點滴恩惠；慶幸情況還不算太壞

<span class="vocabulary">**envy**</span> ['envɪ] 
<span class="definition">vt. not envy sb (sth) 慶幸自己不必去做別人不得不做的事：</span>It’s a difficult situation you’re in. I don’t envy you. 你的處境很困難。幸虧我不是你。/ I don’t envy her that job. 我慶幸不必幹她的那份工作。
           
<span class="vocabulary">**charm**</span> [tʃɑ:m; 美 tʃɑ:rm]
<span class="definition">n. [C]（鏈或手鐲上的）吉祥小飾物：</span>a lucky charm 吉祥飾物 / a charm bracelet 帶有吉祥飾物的手鐲

☀ <span class="category">**不幸、不吉利的形容詞和名詞：**</span>
<span class="vocabulary">**unlucky**</span> [ʌnˈlʌki]
<span class="definition">adj. 1 ~ (to do sth)（某人）不幸的；倒黴的：</span>He was very unlucky not to win. 他不幸輸了。/ By some unlucky chance, her name was left off the list. 倒黴的是，名單上沒有她的名字。/ Argentina's unlucky defeat by Ireland. 阿根廷隊惜敗於愛爾蘭隊。/ He was unlucky in love. 他情路很不順。/ She has been very unlucky with injuries this year.（with尤為英式用法）她今年很不走運、曾經多次受傷。/ She was desperately unlucky to fall as badly as she did.（desperately尤為英式用法）她非常不幸，摔得很產重。/ soldiers who were unlucky enough to be captured 不幸被俘計程車兵 <span class="definition">2 ~ (to do sth)（某事物或事件）帶來壞運氣的；不吉利的：</span>Some people think it's unlucky to walk under a ladder. 有些人認為從梯子下面走過不吉利。/ Thirteen is often considered an unlucky number. 十三常被認為是不吉利的數字。/ a date that is unlucky for that family 一個對那個家庭來說不吉利的日於 / Some people think it is unlucky to look at a new moon through glass. 有些人認為透過玻璃看新月是不吉利的。

<span class="vocabulary">**unfortunate**</span> [ʌn'fɔ:tʃnɪt] 
<span class="definition">adj. 擁有壞運氣的；由壞運氣造成的。即不幸的、倒黴的，相當於unlucky：</span>He was unfortunate to lose in the final round. 他不幸在最後一輪輸了。/ an unfortunate accident 不幸的事故 <span class="definition">n. [C] [文學] 一個沒有很多金錢等東西的人，可稱為不幸之人：</span>one of life’s unfortunates 人生中的不幸者之一
                      
<span class="vocabulary">**warning**</span> [ˈwɔ:nɪŋ; 美 ˈwɔ:rn-]
<span class="definition">adj.（預感）不詳的：</span>Warning bells began to ring (= it was a sign that sth was wrong) when her letters were returned unopened. 當她的信原封不動被退回時，不祥之感就來了。
           
<span class="vocabulary">**sinister**</span> [ˈsɪnɪstə(r)]
<span class="definition">adj. 看上去邪惡或危險的，讓人感覺到壞事要發生的，即邪惡的、不詳的、有凶兆的：</span>There was something sinister about him that she found disturbing. 他身上有種邪惡的東西讓她很不安。/ I found his silence faintly sinister. 他默不做聲，令我感到有些不妙。/ a sinister and frightening place 一個邪惡而恐怖的地方 / There is another, more sinister, possibility. 還有另一種更糟糕的可能。

<span class="vocabulary">**disturbed**</span> [dɪˈstɜ:bd; 美 -ˈstɜ:rbd]
<span class="definition">adj.（經歷）不幸的、坎坷的、多災多難的：</span>The killer had a disturbed family background. 殺人兇手出身於一個坎坷不幸的家庭。/ these disturbed times 這些動盪不安的歲月 / women from disturbed backgrounds 有不幸經歷的女性

<span class="vocabulary">**ill**</span> [ɪl] 
<span class="definition">adj. [正式] 帶來（或被認為能夠帶來）壞運氣的，即不吉利的，不祥的：</span>to bring ill luck 帶來厄運 <span class="definition">n. [U] [文學] 厄運：</span>I wish you no ill. 我並不希望你倒黴。

<span class="vocabulary">**tough**</span> [tʌf] 
<span class="definition">adj. [非正式] tough (on sb)（對某人來說）不幸的或倒黴的，且看起來不公平的：</span>‘I can’t get it finished in time.’ ‘Tough!’（諷刺用法）“我無法按時完成。”“活該倒黴！” / It was tough on her being dropped from the team like that. 她就這樣被隊裡刷了下來，真是倒黴。
           
<span class="vocabulary">**doom**</span> [du:m]
