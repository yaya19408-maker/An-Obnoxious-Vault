☀ <span class="category">**需要、需求的名詞：**</span>
<span class="vocabulary">**need**</span> [ni:d] 
<span class="definition">n. 1 [sing., U] need (for sth) / need (for sb/sth) to do sth需要或必須去做某事的一種情況，即需要，必須：</span>There is no need for you to get up early tomorrow. 你明天不必早起。/ He’s in constant need of treatment. 他需要持續的治療。/ The house is in need of a thorough clean. 這房子需要來個大掃除。/ a crying need for skilled workers 急需技術工人/ political, social need 政治需要；社會需要 / There’s no need to worry. 不必擔心。<span class="definition">2 [C, U] 想要某人或某事物，或必須擁有某東西的一種強烈感覺，即強烈需要：</span>She felt the need to talk to someone. 她特別想和人聊聊。/ I’m in need of some fresh air. 我很想呼吸一點新鮮空氣。/ She had no more need of me. 她再也不需要我了。<span class="definition">3 [C, usually pl.] 某人為生活過得舒服（或實現其願望）所需要的事物：</span>a basic human need 人類的一項基本需要 / material needs of food and shelter 對食物與住所的物質需要 / dietary need 飲食的需要 / medical need 醫療的需要 / bodily, emotional, spiritual need 身體上的需要；情感上的需要；精神上的需要 / business, economic need 商業需求；經濟需求 / customer, patient need 顧客的需求；病人的需要

<span class="vocabulary">**requirement**</span> [rɪ'kwaɪəmənt] 
<span class="definition">n. [pl.] [正式] 通常用requirements，你所需要（或想要）的東西：</span>the basic requirements of life 基本生活所需 / a software package to meet your requirements 滿足你需要的一個軟體包 / These goods are surplus to requirements. 這些貨物超過了我們的需要。/ daily requirements of vitamin C 每日所需的維生素C / business requirements 業務需求 / customer, market requirements 顧客需求；市場需求 / course requirements 課程要求 / food, nutritional requirements 食物需求；營養需求 / information requirements 資訊需求 / labour/labor requirements 勞動力需求 / capital, energy, performance requirements 資金需求；能源需求；效能需求 / I have some very simple requirements. 我有一些很簡單的要求。

<span class="vocabulary">**demand**</span> [dɪ'mɑːnd] 
<span class="definition">n. [C] 所需之物：</span>economic, emotional, financial, physical, practical, social, technical demands 經濟需求；感情需求；財務需求；體力需求；實際需求；社會需求；技術需求
           
<span class="vocabulary">**requisite**</span> [ˈrekwɪzɪt]
<span class="definition">n. [C] [正式] ~ (for/of sth)（對某一目的來說）必需的事物：</span>toilet requisites (= soap, toothpaste, etc.) 洗漱用品 / A university degree has become a requisite for entry into most professions. 大學學位已經成為在大多數行業謀職的必要條件。/ An understanding of accounting techniques is a major requisite for the work of the analysts. 懂得會計知識是從事分析員工作的一個必要條件。/ his little bag of hunting requisites 他的一小包狩獵必需品

<span class="vocabulary">**want**</span> [wɒnt] 
<span class="definition">n. [C, usually pl.] [正式] 需要（或想要）的東西：</span>human want 人的需要 / material want 物質需要 / Society can’t satisfy all human wants. 社會不可能滿足人們各方面的需要。
           
<span class="vocabulary">**essential**</span> [ɪˈsenʃl]
<span class="definition">n. [usually pl.]（在某種情況中或做某事）必需的東西、必需品：</span>the bare essentials for existence 生存的必需品 / I only had time to pack the bare essentials (= the most necessary things). 我只來得及裝上最基本的用品。/ The relief agencies are trying to provide food and other basic essentials. 救援機構正努力提供食物和其他必需品。/ The flat contained the basic essentials for bachelor life. 那套公寓配有單身生活的基本必需品。/ The studio had all the essentials like heating and running water. 工作室裡備有基本設施，如暖氣裝置和自來水。
           
<span class="vocabulary">**tyranny**</span> [ˈtɪrəni]
<span class="definition">n. [C] [貶] 支配了人們生活的一件必需品：</span>The telephone is one of the great tyrannies of modern life. 電話是現代生活中讓人最反感的必需品之一。/ These days it seems we must all submit to the tyranny of the motor car. 如今，似乎所有人都離不開汽車了。/ An artist's need for money imposes the tyranny of popular taste. 藝術家需要金錢，就不得不服從大眾的趣味。
           
<span class="vocabulary">**necessity**</span> [nəˈsesəti]
<span class="definition">n. 1 [U] ~ (for sth) / ~ (of sth/of doing sth) / ~ (to do sth)（必須做某事的）必要性；（對某事物的）需要：</span>We recognize the necessity for a written agreement. 我們認為有必要簽訂一份書面協議。/ There is agreement on the necessity of reforms. 對改革的必要性已經達成了共識。/ As soon as the necessity for action is over the troops must be withdrawn. 一旦軍事行動的必要性不復存在，軍隊就必須撤出。/ There had never been any necessity for her to go out to work. 她從來就沒有出去工作的必要。/ We were discussing the necessity of employing more staff. 我們在討論是否需要僱用更多員工。<span class="definition">2 [C] 必需的事物；必需品：</span>Water is a basic necessity of life. 生命離不開水。/ food, fuel and other daily necessities 食物、燃料和其他日常必需品 / Many people cannot even afford basic necessities such as food and clothing. 許多人甚至買不起食物和衣服之類的基本必需品。/ They have nothing but the barest necessities. 除了最基本的必需品之外，他們一無所有。/ Air-conditioning is an absolute necessity in this climate. 這樣的氣候絕對需要有空調。/ Policies which address these issues are an urgent necessity. 迫切需要的是解決這些問題的政策。

<span class="vocabulary">**call**</span> [kɔ:l] 
<span class="definition">n. 1 [U] no call for sth 對某事物沒有需要或需求：</span>There isn’t a lot of call for small specialist shops nowadays. 如今對小型專賣店已沒有多大需求了。/ There’s not much call for this product. 對這款產品的需求不多。<span class="definition">2 [C] call on sb/sth 被置於某人或某事物上的需求、需要或壓力：</span>She is a busy woman with many calls on her time. 她是個大忙人，有很多事等著她去辦。

<span class="vocabulary">**cry**</span> [kraɪ] 
<span class="definition">n. [C] cry (for sth) 對某事物的迫切需求（或請求、要求）：</span>Her suicide attempt was really a desperate cry for help. 她的自殺企圖實際上表明瞭她極度需要幫助。

<span class="vocabulary">**purpose**</span> ['pə:pəs] 
<span class="definition">n. [pl.] purposes可指在某種情況中所需要的東西，即情勢所需：</span>For the purposes of this study, the three groups have been combined. 為了這項研究工作，三個小組業已合併。/ administrative, legal, medical, teaching purposes 管理方面的需要；法律方面的需要；醫療方面的需要；教學方面的需要 / You will need to have the vehicle valued for insurance purposes. 你得找人給車子估價以便購買保險。

周邊詞彙補充：
· keep可指一個人的生活必需品（如食物、衣服等）；
· basics可指必需品。

☀ <span class="category">**需要、必要的動詞：**</span>
<span class="vocabulary">**need**</span> [ni:d] 
<span class="definition">vt.＆vi. 要求某事物或某人，因為其是必要的或非常重要的，而不僅因為你想要，即需要。是此義的常規用詞：</span>Do you need any help? 你需要幫忙嗎？/ She needed some money badly. 她急需一些錢。/ I need to get some sleep. 我需要睡會兒覺。/ This shirt needs washing. 這件襯衣該洗了。/ Research is urgently needed into the causes of this illness. 急需對這種病的起因進行研究。<span class="definition">vi. 用於表示你應該（或不得不）去做，即有必要：</span>All you need to do is complete this form. 你要做的就是填好這個表格。/ I didn’t need to go to the bank after all--Mary lent me the money. 我最終還是不必去銀行了——瑪麗借我錢了。/ He needs to win this game to stay in the match. 他得贏下這場比賽以免被淘汰出局。<span class="definition">modal verb. [英] need (not) do sth / need (not) have done sth用於表示某事沒必要、或必要性很小、或詢問是否有必要，即需要：</span>You needn’t finish that work today. 你不必今天做完這項工作。/ I need hardly tell you that the work is dangerous. 這工作很危險，這就不用我說了。/ If she wants anything, she need only ask. 她想要什麼東西，只要開一下口就行了。/ All you need bring are sheets. 你需要帶的就是床單。/ Need you have paid so much? 你用得著花那麼多錢嗎？

<span class="vocabulary">**require**</span> [rɪ'kwaɪə] 
<span class="definition">vt. [正式] 需要某東西；倚靠某人（或某東西）：</span>These pets require a lot of care and attention. 這些寵物需要悉心照顧。/ to require medical treatment 需要治療 / This condition requires urgent treatment. 這種情況得緊急處理。/ Do you require anything else? (= in a shop/store, for example) 您還需要什麼嗎？/ True marriage requires us to show trust and loyalty. 真正的婚姻有賴於我們表現出信任與忠誠。/ to require soaking before cooking 在烹飪前需要浸泡 / These plants absolutely require shade. 這些植物必須養在陰涼處。

<span class="vocabulary">**demand**</span> [dɪ'mɑːnd] 
<span class="definition">vt.（為成功做成某事而）需要…：</span>This sport demands both speed and strength. 這項運動既需要速度也需要體力。
           
<span class="vocabulary">**entail**</span> [ɪnˈteɪl]
<span class="definition">vt. 不可避免地牽涉到某事物，即使…成為必需：</span>The job entails a lot of hard work. 這工作需要十分艱苦的努力。/ Restructuring will inevitably entail compromises. 重組免不了要有一些妥協。/ Such sweeping changes must logically entail a redefinition of the concept. 從邏輯上說，這樣徹底的改變必然要對這一概念重新定義。/ The procedure will simply entail the interviewee answering yes or no. 這一步驟只是要求受訪者回答是或否。/ The girls learn exactly what is entailed in caring for a newborn baby. 姑娘們學的正是怎樣照看新生兒。

<span class="vocabulary">**imply**</span> [ɪmˈplaɪ]
<span class="definition">vt.（一個事件或一種情況）意味著…，需要：</span>The project implies an enormous investment in training. 這個專案需要在培訓方面做巨大的投資。
           
<span class="vocabulary">**involve**</span> [ɪnˈvɒlv; 美 ɪnˈvɑ:lv]
<span class="definition">vt.（一種情況、一個事件或活動）牽涉或影響某事物。有時可翻譯為“需要”，相當於entail：</span>Many of the crimes involved drugs. 許多罪案都與毒品有關。/ The job involves me travelling all over the country. 這份工作需要我在全國各地跑。/ The job involves my travelling all over the country.（正式）這份工作需要我在全國各地跑。/ This involved me in a round trip of over 400 miles. 為此我需要往返旅行 400 多英里。/ The reforms will inevitably involve a lot of new paperwork for teachers. 改革難免需要教師做大量新的書面工作。/ Running a kitchen involves a great deal of discipline and speed. 料理好廚房需要把一切都安排得規規矩矩，且要講求速度。/ Nicky's job as a public relations director involves spending quite a lot of time with other people. 尼基作為公共關係主管，需要花很多時間與別人打交道。

<span class="vocabulary">**take**</span> [teɪk] 
<span class="definition">vt. 因…而需要或要求：</span>It only takes one careless driver to cause an accident. 只要有一個粗心大意的司機便會發生車禍。/ It doesn’t take much to make her angry. 她動輒就發脾氣。

<span class="vocabulary">**want**</span> [wɒnt] 
<span class="definition">vt.＆vi. [非正式] 需要（某事物）。其動作發出者是事物：</span>We’ll want more furniture for the new office. 我們的新辦公室需要添些傢俱。/ What this house wants is a good clean. 這房子需要好好打掃一下。/ The plants want watering daily. 這些花草需要天天澆水。<span class="definition">vt. 常用被動式，需要某人在場：</span>She’s wanted immediately in the director’s office. 她得馬上到主任辦公室去。/ Excuse me, you’re wanted on the phone. 對不起，有你的電話。

<span class="vocabulary">**exact**</span> [ɪɡ'zækt] 
<span class="definition">vt. [正式] exact sth (from sb) 向某人要求或索取某東西：</span>She was determined to exact a promise from him. 她決意要他做出承諾。

<span class="vocabulary">**have**</span> [hæv] 
<span class="definition">vt.（也可用have got）需要或有必要（去做某事）：</span>I’ve got a lot of homework tonight. 我今晚有很多家庭作業要做。/ I must go--I have a bus to catch. 我必須得走了——我得去趕公交車。
           
<span class="vocabulary">**warrant**</span> [ˈwɒrənt; 美 ˈwɔ:r-; ˈwɑ:r-]
<span class="definition">vt. [正式] 使…在某種情況中有必要，或使正當、使恰當：</span>Further investigation is clearly warranted. 進一步調查顯然是必要的。/ The allegations are serious enough to warrant an investigation. 這些指控很嚴重，有必要進行一番調查。/ The TV appearance was so brief that it hardly warranted comment. 在電視上露面的時間太短了，幾乎不值得作任何評論。/ The situation scarcely warrants their/them being dismissed. 這種情況很難證明解僱他們是正當的。/ No matter was too small to warrant his attention. 再小的事也會引起他的注意。

<span class="vocabulary">**gasp**</span> [gɑ:sp; 美 gæsp]
<span class="definition">vi. [英，非正式] be gasping (for sth) 非常想要或需要某事物（尤指飲料或香菸）：</span>瞭解即可

☀ <span class="category">**需要、必要的形容詞和名詞：**</span>
<span class="vocabulary">**ready**</span> ['redɪ] 
<span class="definition">adj. ready for sth 急需某東西的，需要儘快得到某東西的：</span>Right, I’m ready for bed. 對，我現在就想睡覺。/ After the long walk, we were all ready for a drink. 長途步行之後，我們大家急需喝水。

<span class="vocabulary">**necessary**</span> ['nesɪsərɪ] 
<span class="definition">adj. necessary (for sb/sth) (to do sth) 對於某一目的（或理由）來說是被需要的，即必要的，必需的，相當於essential：</span>It may be necessary to buy a new one. 也許有必要買個新的了。/ It doesn’t seem necessary for us to meet. 我們似乎沒必要見面。/ I’ll make the necessary arrangements. 我會做一些必要的安排。/ The cold weather has made it necessary to protect the crops. 天氣寒冷，莊稼必須加以保護。/ We are here to give help and support when necessary. 如有需要，我們隨時提供幫助和支援。 
           
<span class="vocabulary">**requisite**</span> [ˈrekwɪzɪt]
<span class="definition">adj. [只用於名詞前] [正式]（對某一目的來說）必需的、必備的、必不可少的：</span>She lacks the requisite experience for the job. 她缺少做這份工作所必需的經驗。/ She filled in the requisite paperwork. 她完成了必要的書面工作。
           
<span class="vocabulary">**occasion**</span> [əˈkeɪʒn]
<span class="definition">n. [sing.] have ~ to 有必要去做某事：</span>We have had occasion to deal with members of the group on a variety of charges. 我們有必要以多項罪名起訴該組織的成員。

☀ <span class="category">**希望、期望、渴望、盼望的動詞：**</span>
<span class="vocabulary">**hope**</span> [həʊp] 
<span class="definition">vt.＆vi. hope (for sth) 想要某事發生並認為這是可能的，即希望、期望：</span>We are hoping for good weather on Sunday. 我們盼望著星期天天氣好。/ I sincerely hope that you will be successful. 我衷心祝願你成功。/ They hoped desperately that their missing son would come home. 他們焦急地盼望失蹤的兒子能夠回來。
           
<span class="vocabulary">**brighten**</span> [ˈbraɪtn]
<span class="definition">vt.&vi. ~ (sth) (up)（使）有希望、變得樂觀：</span>That does not do much to brighten the prospects of kids in the city. 那並不能使城裡孩子們的前途更光明些。

<span class="vocabulary">**want**</span> [wɒnt] 
<span class="definition">vt.＆vi. 對某事懷有一種渴望或願望，即要，想要，希望。其動作發出者是人或組織：</span>Do you want some more tea? 你再要點兒茶嗎？/ She’s always wanted a large family. 她一直希望生一大群孩子。/ All I want is the truth. 我只想知道實情。/ The party wants her as leader. 這個政黨希望由她做領袖。/ I just wanted to know if everything was all right. 我只是想知道是否一切都好。/ Do you want me to help? 你要我幫忙嗎？/ We didn’t want this to happen. 我們並不希望發生這樣的事情。/ I want it (to be) done as quickly as possible. 我希望這件事儘快完成。

<span class="vocabulary">**like**</span> [laɪk] 
<span class="definition">vt. 想，想要，類似於want：</span>Do what you like--I don’t care. 你想做什麼就做什麼——我不在乎。/ You can dye your hair whatever colour you like. 你的頭髮你想怎麼染就怎麼染。<span class="definition">vt.＆vi. 與would或should連用，表示客氣，即想，想要，希望：</span>Would you like a drink? 你想喝一杯嗎？/ I’d like an explanation. 我想要一個解釋。/ Would you like to come with us? 你想不想和我們一塊兒去？/ I’d like to think it over. 我想考慮一下這個問題。 

<span class="vocabulary">**mean**</span> [mi:n] 
<span class="definition">vt. mean sb for sth / mean sb to be sth 常用被動式，指想要某人成為…；想要某人去做某事：</span>His father meant him to be an engineer. 他父親想讓他當個工程師。

<span class="vocabulary">**fancy**</span> ['fænsɪ] 
<span class="definition">vt. [英，非正式] 想要去做某事，相當於feel like：</span>Fancy a drink? 想喝一杯嗎？/ Do you fancy going out this evening? 今晚你想不想出去？

<span class="vocabulary">**wish**</span> [wɪʃ] 
<span class="definition">vi. 1 希望某事發生或屬實（即使不太可能或不可能）：</span>I wish I were taller. 我要是個子高一些就好了。/ When I see the kids playing football, I almost wish I was their age again. 看到孩子們踢足球時，我真希望能再回到他們那個年齡。<span class="definition">2 wish (for sth) 非常想要某事物（尤指只有透過好運或魔法才能得到的事物或達成的事情）：</span>She shut her eyes and wished for him to get better. 她閉上眼睛祈盼他好起來。/ It’s no use wishing for the impossible. 不可能的事祈願也沒有用。/ He has everything he could possibly wish for. 他可能想要的一切東西他都有了。<span class="definition">vt.＆vi. [尤英，正式] 想要做某事；想要某事發生：</span>You may stay until morning, if you wish. 如果你願意，你可以一直待到早晨。/ I wish to speak to the manager. 我想跟經理談談。/ I don’t wish to be rude, but could you be a little quieter? 我不想無禮，但請您安靜一點兒好嗎？

<span class="vocabulary">**will**</span> [wɪl] 
<span class="definition">vt. 1 [舊時或正式] 僅用於一般現在時，指想要；希望；願意；喜歡：</span>Call it what you will, it’s still a problem. 不管怎麼說，這仍然是個問題。<span class="definition">2 運用意志力去做某事或使某事發生：</span>As a child he had thought he could fly, if he willed it enough. 他小時候曾經以為，只要有足夠的決心，想飛就能飛起來。/ She willed her eyes to stay open. 她使勁睜著眼睛。/ He willed himself not to panic. 他竭力讓自己不要恐慌。<span class="definition">3 運用精神力量（而非體力）去嘗試使某事發生，可稱作希望：</span>I looked at the telephone, willing it to ring. 我看著電話，希望它響起來。<span class="definition">4 [舊時] 想要某事發生：</span>They thought they had been victorious in battle because God had willed it. 他們以為自己打了勝仗是上帝的旨意。

<span class="vocabulary">**would**</span> [wʊd] 
<span class="definition">modal verb. [文學] would that ... 用於表達一種強烈的願望：</span>Would that he had lived to see it. 他要是能活到看見這該多好哇。

<span class="vocabulary">**may**</span> [meɪ] 
<span class="definition">modal verb. [正式] 用於表示願望或希望，即但願：</span>May she rest in peace. 願她安息。/ Business has been thriving in the past year. Long may it continue to do so. 在過去的一年裡生意蒸蒸日上。但願這種情況能持續下去。
