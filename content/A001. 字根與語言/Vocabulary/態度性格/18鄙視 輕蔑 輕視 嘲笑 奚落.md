☀ <span class="category">**鄙視、輕蔑、貶低的動詞和形容詞：**</span>
<span class="vocabulary">**despise**</span> [dɪˈspaɪz]
<span class="definition">vt.（不用於進行時）厭惡且不尊重某人或某事物，如鄙視、蔑視：</span>She thoroughly despised him for his weakness. 她因為他的軟弱而十分鄙視他。/ He despised himself for being so cowardly. 他為自己如此怯懦而自慚形穢。/ I can never, ever forgive him. I despise him. 我永遠不會原諒他。我鄙視他。/ He secretly despised his father. 他暗地裡瞧不起自己的父親。/ She secretly despises his work. 她暗地裡瞧不起他的工作。/ She despised gossip in any form. 她對任何形式的流言飛語都嗤之以鼻。           
           
<span class="vocabulary">**disdain**</span> [dɪsˈdeɪn]
<span class="definition">vt. [正式] 鄙視、蔑視：</span>She disdained his offer of help. 他提出要幫助，遭到她的鄙棄。/ Jackie disdained the servants that her millions could buy. 傑姬鄙視那些她用錢就可以收買的奴僕。<span class="definition">vi. [正式] 不屑於做某事：</span>He disdained to turn to his son for advice. 他不屑於向自己的兒子請教。/ Franklin told Sara that he had himself disdained to take the job. 富蘭克林告訴薩拉他不屑於去做那份工作。

<span class="vocabulary">**scorn**</span> [skɔ:n; 美 skɔ:rn]
<span class="definition">vt. 輕蔑、鄙視：</span>She scorned their views as old-fashioned. 她對他們的觀點嗤之以鼻，認為陳腐過時。/ Several leading officers have quite openly scorned the peace talks. 幾位高官相當公開地對和平談判表示出不屑。/ People scorn me as a single parent. 人們因為我一個人帶著孩子就看不起我。<span class="definition">vt.&vi. [正式] 因太高傲而拒絕擁有或使用…，即不屑於、輕蔑地拒絕：</span>to scorn an invitation 輕蔑地回絕邀請 / She would have scorned to stoop to such tactics. 她就不會下作到使用那樣的伎倆。/ people who scorned traditional methods 擯棄傳統做法的人
           
<span class="vocabulary">**mock**</span> [mɒk; 美 mɑ:k]
<span class="definition">vt. [正式] 對某事物不尊重，如蔑視：</span>The new exam mocked the needs of the majority of children. 新的考試無視大多數孩子的需要。/ I distinctly remember mocking the idea. 我清楚地記得曾經對這個想法嗤之以鼻。
           
<span class="vocabulary">**flout**</span> [flaʊt]
<span class="definition">vt. 公然藐視（法律等）：</span>The protesters have openly flouted the law. 抗議者公然蔑視法律。/ Motorists regularly flout the law. 駕車者經常無視法律。/ illegal campers who persist in flouting the law 一向對法律熟視無睹的非法露營者 / Building regulations have been habitually flouted. 建築法規一向都無人遵守。/ to flout authority, to flout convention 公然藐視權威；公然藐視慣例                      

<span class="vocabulary">**dismissive**</span> [dɪsˈmɪsɪv]
<span class="definition">adj. ~ (of sb/sth) 認為某人或某事物不重要的或沒有價值的，如輕視的、輕蔑的、鄙視的：</span>dismissive gesture, dismissive tone 輕蔑的手勢；輕蔑的語調 / Mr Jones was dismissive of the report, saying it was riddled with inaccuracies. 瓊斯先生對報告不屑一顧，稱其中充斥著謬誤。/ the dismissive attitude scientists often take in regard to questions such as telepathy or homeopathic medicine 涉及心電感應、順勢療法等問題時科學家們常抱有的鄙視態度 / Other critics were equally dismissive. 其他評論家同樣目中無人。/ She was very dismissive of his achievements. 她對他的成就非常不屑。

<span class="vocabulary">**contumacious**</span> [ˌkɒntjuˈmeɪʃəs; 美 ˌkɑ:ntu-]
<span class="definition">adj. [舊時或法律] 藐視權威的：</span>瞭解即可

<span class="vocabulary">**spit**</span> [spɪt] 
<span class="definition">vi. spit (at/on sb/sth) 啐唾沫（常表示憤怒或鄙視）：</span>She spat in his face and went out. 她朝他臉上啐了一口，然後走了出去。

<span class="vocabulary">**slight**</span> [slaɪt] 
<span class="definition">vt. 常用被動式，指粗魯無禮或不尊重地對待某人，相當於insult：</span>She felt slighted because she hadn’t been invited. 她沒有受到邀請，覺得受了冷落。/ They felt slighted by not being adequately consulted. 因為沒有充分地徵求他們的意見，他們感到被怠慢了。
           
<span class="vocabulary">**snub**</span> [snʌb]
<span class="definition">vt. 冷落、怠慢某人：</span>I tried to be friendly, but she snubbed me completely. 我儘量和氣，但她根本不答理我。/ He snubbed her in public and made her feel an idiot. 他當眾冷落她，讓她覺得自己像一個白痴似的。/ He was not invited to the party, and felt snub bed. 他未獲邀請參加聚會，感到受了冷落。
           
<span class="vocabulary">**belittle**</span> [bɪˈlɪtl]
<span class="definition">vt. 貶低、小看：</span>She felt her husband constantly belittled her achievements. 她覺得她的丈夫時常貶低她的成就。/ It makes no sense to belittle the enormity of the disaster. 沒有理由低估這場災難的嚴重性。           

<span class="vocabulary">**diminish**</span> [dɪˈmɪnɪʃ]
<span class="definition">vt. 貶低或輕視某人或某事物：</span>I don't wish to diminish the importance of their contribution. 我並不想貶低他們所作貢獻的重要性。/ He never put her down or diminished her. 他從未奚落或貶低過她。/ He could no longer cope; he relied on me, and felt diminished by it. 他再也無法承受，他依賴我，卻因此覺得是受了輕賤。

<span class="vocabulary">**depreciate**</span> [dɪˈpri:ʃieɪt]
<span class="definition">vt. [正式] 貶低、輕視：</span>I had no intention of depreciating your contribution. 我並不想貶低你的貢獻。
           
<span class="vocabulary">**devalue**</span> [ˌdi:ˈvælju:]
<span class="definition">vt. 降低…的價值；貶低…的價值：</span>Work in the home is often ignored and devalued. 家務勞動常常被忽視和貶低。/ They spread tales about her in an attempt to devalue her work. 他們散佈一些有關她的流言，企圖貶低她的工作。/ Wabel's victory was in no way devalued by the absence of series leader Tom Fisher. 瓦貝爾的勝利絕沒有因系列賽領先者湯姆·費希爾的缺陣而失色。

<span class="vocabulary">**derogate**</span> [ˈderəgeɪt]
<span class="definition">vt. [正式] 貶低…的價值、貶損：</span>瞭解即可        

<span class="category">**鄙視、輕蔑的名詞和形容詞：**</span>
<span class="vocabulary">**contempt**</span> [kənˈtempt]
<span class="definition">n. 1 [U, sing.] ~ (for sb/sth) 認為…沒有價值因而不值得尊重，如蔑視、輕蔑、鄙視：</span>She looked at him with barely disguised contempt. 她看著他，幾乎毫不掩飾自己的輕蔑。/ I shall treat that suggestion with the contempt it deserves. 我對那項建議當然會不屑一顧。/ His treatment of his children is beneath contempt (= so unacceptable that it is not even worth feeling contempt for). 他對待自己子女的那種行徑為人所不齒。/ Politicians seem to be generally held in contempt by ordinary people. 一般百姓似乎普遍看不起從政者。/ They had shown a contempt for the values she thought important. 他們對她所認為重要的價值表示蔑視。/ He has contempt for those beyond his immediate family circle. 他鄙視直系親屬圈子以外的人。/ I hope voters will treat his advice with the contempt it deserves. 我希望選民們給予其建議應有的鄙視。/ Small wonder that many voters hold their politicians in contempt. 無怪乎很多選民看不起他們的政治家。/ She'd developed what she considered a healthy contempt for authority.（healthy尤為英式用法）她養成了蔑視權威的習慣，認為這是正常合理的。/ He felt nothing but contempt for them. 他對他們只有蔑視。/ Politicians seem to be generally held in contempt by the police. 政治人物似乎通常都受到警方的蔑視。/ He has a deep contempt for racists. 他對種族主義者極端蔑視。/ His treatment of his children is beneath contempt (= so bad it is not even worth feeling contempt for). 他對自己的孩子這麼狠，真讓人不齒。<span class="definition">2 [U, sing.] ~ for sth（對規則、危險等的）不擔心或不害怕，如置之度外：</span>The firefighters showed a contempt for their own safety. 那些消防隊員已把他們自己的安全置之度外。/ His remarks betray a staggering contempt for the truth (= are completely false). 他的話表明他完全無視事情的真相。<span class="definition">3 [U, sing.] 藐視法庭（罪），相當於contempt of court：</span>civil, criminal contempt 民事藐視法庭罪；刑事藐視法庭罪 / He could be jailed for two years for contempt. 他由於藐視法庭可能被監禁兩年。/ She was held in contempt for refusing to testify. 她因拒絕作證而被判藐視法庭罪。
                      
<span class="vocabulary">**contemptuous**</span> [contemptuous]
<span class="definition">adj. ~ (of sb/sth) 表示輕蔑的、蔑視的、藐視的、鄙視的。相當於scornful：</span>She gave him a contemptuous look. 她鄙夷地看了他一眼。/ He was contemptuous of everything I did. 他對我所做的一切都不屑一顧。/ He was contemptuous of private farmers. 他看不起私營農場主。/ He's openly contemptuous of all the major political parties. 他公然蔑視各大黨派。/ Her voice sounded almost contemptuous. 她的語氣近乎輕蔑。/ He was utterly contemptuous of her efforts. 他全然蔑視她的努力。

<span class="vocabulary">**disdain**</span> [dɪsˈdeɪn]
<span class="definition">n. [U, sing.] ~ (for sb/sth) 鄙視、蔑視：</span>to treat sb with disdain 鄙視某人 / a disdain for the law 對法律的藐視 / Janet looked at him with disdain. 珍妮特輕蔑地看著他。/ She shared her daughter's disdain for her fellow countrymen. 她和女兒都瞧不起自己的同胞。/ She did not hesitate to express the disdain that she felt. 她毫不猶豫地表達了她的鄙視之情。/ Traditionalists look upon the changes with disdain. 傳統主義者鄙視這些變革。/ She had trouble hiding his distain for the man. 她難以掩飾她對這個男人的鄙視。/ She turned her head away in disdain. 她不屑地把頭賺了過去。/ He has an aristocratic disdain for money. 他對金錢有一種倨傲的不屑態度。/ an expression of disdain, a look of disdain 不屑的表情

<span class="vocabulary">**scorn**</span> [skɔ:n; 美 skɔ:rn]
<span class="definition">n. [U] ~ (for sb/sth) 強烈的輕蔑、鄙視，尤指透過說話方式表現出來的對…的輕蔑：</span>Her fellow teachers greeted her proposal with scorn. 別的老師對她的提議不屑一顧。/ They had nothing but scorn for his political views. 他們對他的政治觀點只有鄙夷。/ Franklin shared the family's scorn for his wife's new friends. 富蘭克林和家裡人一樣，也對他夫人的新朋友們不屑一顧。/ It is fashionable these days to pour scorn on those in public life. 如今人們都看不起那些從事公務工作的人。/ He used to heap scorn on Dr Vazquez's socialist ideas. 他以前總是嘲笑巴斯克斯博士的社會主義觀點。/ She reserved her most withering scorn for journalists. 她最瞧不起的就是記者了。/ He has suffered public scorn and humiliation. 他在大庭廣眾之下遭到了蔑視和羞辱。/ Abuse and scorn were heaped upon the new tax. 新開徵的稅種招致鋪天蓋地的咒罵和鄙視。/ He reserved particular scorn for the director. 他對那位主任尤為不屑。/ What have I done to deserve such scorn? 我做了什麼讓你這麼瞧不起？/ He stared with scorn at his interviewers. 他有些輕蔑地盯著面試官們。/ She expressed her scorn for the rules. 她表達了對規則的蔑視。/ He didn't try to hide his scorn for our way of doing things. 他並不想掩飾對我們做事方法的不屑。/ His poetry was the object of scorn. 他的詩歌成了人們嘲笑的物件。

<span class="vocabulary">**slight**</span> [slaɪt] 
<span class="definition">n. [C] slight (on sb/sth) 批評…或冒犯某人的行為或話語，如藐視，相當於insult：</span>a slight on his abilities as a manager 藐視他當經理的能力 <span class="definition">adj. [正式] 無需重視的，微不足道的：</span>a slight novel 微不足道的小說

<span class="vocabulary">**sniff**</span> [snɪf] 
<span class="definition">n. [C] 以抽鼻子的方式來表達態度；抽鼻子聲：</span>She gave a loud sniff of disapproval. 她不以為然地大哼了一聲。/ ‘I’m sorry,’ he said between sniffs.“抱歉。”他輕蔑地說。/ ‘I’m fine,’ she said with a sniff.“我很好。”她不屑地說。
           
<span class="vocabulary">**snub**</span> [snʌb]
<span class="definition">n. [C] ~ (to sb) 冷略、怠慢的言辭或行為：</span>Her refusal to attend the dinner is being seen as a deliberate snub to the President. 在人們看來，她拒不出席宴會是有意讓總統難堪。/ Ryan took it as a snub. 瑞安覺得這是一種怠慢。/ The German move was widely seen as a deliberate snub to Mr Cook. 德國方面的舉動被普遍視為對庫克先生的有意怠慢。/ He took the opportunity to deliver us another snub. 他藉機再次冷落我們。/ It was never intended as a snub. 這絕非有意怠慢。/ The deal amounts to something of a snub for the rival online music company. 做這筆交易等於是有些冷落了對手的線上音樂公司。/ Some are suggesting that it was a snub by the French. 有人說那表示受到了法國人的冷落。/ an unintentional snub of President Johnson 約翰遜總統無心的怠慢

周邊詞彙補充：
· underestimate可指低估。

☀ <span class="category">**嘲笑、取笑的動詞：**</span>
<span class="vocabulary">**mock**</span> [mɒk; 美 mɑ:k]
<span class="definition">vt.&vi. 嘲笑，尤指透過模仿其言行來嘲弄：</span>He's always mocking my French accent. 他總是嘲笑我的法國口音。/ I thought you were mocking me. 我還以為你是在嘲笑我。/ The other children mocked her, laughing behind their hands. 其他孩子學她的樣子，用手捂著嘴笑。/ You can mock, but at least I'm willing to have a try! 你可以嘲笑我，但我至少願意試一試。/ ‘Too scary for you?’ he mocked gently.“你嚇壞了吧？”他輕聲取笑道。/ He openly mocked his parents. 他公開嘲笑自己的父母。/ The play subtly mocks the conventions of romance. 這部劇含蓄地諷刺了愛情小說的種種慣用套路。/ He mocked at her hopes of stardom. 他對她想當明星的願望嘲笑了一番。/ She mocked him for his failure. 她譏笑他的失敗。/ She mocked him with her smile. 她對他嘲弄地微微一笑。
           
<span class="vocabulary">**sneer**</span> [snɪə(r); 美 snɪr]
<span class="definition">vt.&vi. ~ (at sb/sth) 嘲笑、譏諷：</span>He sneered at people who liked pop music. 他嘲笑喜歡流行音樂的人。/ a sneering comment 譏諷的話 / ‘You? A writer? ’ she sneered. “你？是作家？”她不屑地說。/ There is too great a readiness to sneer at anything the Opposition does. 對反對黨的任何舉動都想加以嘲笑的心理過於急切了。/ If you go to a club and you don't look right, you're sneered at. 如果你去夜總會卻穿得不合時宜，人家會笑你的。
           
<span class="vocabulary">**jeer**</span> [dʒɪə(r); 美 dʒɪr]
<span class="definition">vt.&vi. ~ (at sb) 嘲笑、譏諷、奚落：</span>a jeering crowd 起鬨的一群人 / The police were jeered at by the waiting crowd. 警察受到在等待的人群的嘲弄。/ Marchers jeered at white passers-by, but there was no violence, nor any arrests. 遊行者奚落路過的白人，但沒有發生暴力事件，也沒有人被逮捕。/ The players were jeered by disappointed fans. 球員受到大失所望的球迷奚落。/ His motorcade was jeered by angry residents. 他的車隊遭到憤怒的當地居民的嘲諷。
                      
<span class="vocabulary">**ridicule**</span> [ˈrɪdɪkju:l]
<span class="definition">vt. 譏笑、嘲笑、奚落（某人或某事物）：</span>I admired her all the more for allowing them to ridicule her and never striking back. 她能聽任他們嘲笑卻從不還擊，這讓我更加敬佩她。/ I don't think his faith should be ridiculed. 我認為不應該嘲笑他的信仰。

<span class="vocabulary">**scoff**</span> [skɒf; 美 skɔ:f; skɑ:f]
<span class="definition">vt.&vi. ~ (at sb/sth) 嘲笑、譏諷：</span>He scoffed at our amateurish attempts. 他對我們不在行的嘗試嗤之以鼻。/ Don't scoff─she's absolutely right. 別嘲笑她——她絕對正確。/ At first I scoffed at the notion. 剛開始我對那種想法嗤之以鼻。/ You may scoff but I honestly feel I'm being cruel only to be kind. 你可能不以為然，但我真的認為我狠下心來只是出於一片好意。
           
<span class="vocabulary">**taunt**</span> [tɔ:nt]
<span class="definition">vt. 辱罵；嘲笑、諷刺、奚落：</span>The other kids continually taunted him about his size. 其他孩子不斷地恥笑他的個頭兒。/ A gang taunted a disabled man. 一夥小混混嘲笑一位殘疾人。/ Other youths taunted him about his clothes. 其他年輕人笑話他的衣服。/ He was constantly taunted for being foreign. 他因為是外國人而不斷受到嘲弄。/ to openly taunt 公開奚落 / to repeatedly taunt 一再奚落 / He claimed he had been racially taunted and abused.（racially為英式用法）他聲稱受到了種族嘲弄和欺凌。/ Some of the girls taunted her about her weight. 女孩中有些人譏笑她的體重。/ The other children taunted him with nicknames. 其他孩子叫他各種綽號取笑他。

<span class="vocabulary">**tease**</span> [ti:z] 
<span class="definition">vt.＆vi.（友好地、或令人惱怒或尷尬地）取笑或嘲笑某人，開某人的玩笑：</span>I used to get teased about my name. 過去別人總拿我的名字開玩笑。/ Jane used to tease him about his clothes. 簡過去常常笑話他的衣著。/ Don’t get upset--I was only teasing. 別生氣——我逗你玩兒呢。<span class="definition">vt.（尤指透過撫摸、拽尾巴等方式）惹惱或招惹、逗弄一隻動物：</span>瞭解即可
           
<span class="vocabulary">**torment**</span> [ˈtɔ:ment; 美 ˈtɔ:rm-]
<span class="definition">vt.（為取樂而）殘忍地戲弄或捉弄（一個人或動物）：</span>My older brother and sister used to torment me by singing it to me. 哥哥姐姐以前常唱那首歌來戲弄我。

<span class="vocabulary">**ride**</span> [raɪd] 
<span class="definition">vt. [美] 以一種令人惱怒的方式批評或嘲笑某人，即奚落、嘲弄、取笑：</span>Everyone is riding him today. 大家今天都取笑他。

<span class="vocabulary">**rag**</span> [ræɡ] 
<span class="definition">vt. [舊時，英] rag sb (about sth) 嘲笑或戲弄某人，相當於tease：</span>瞭解即可

周邊詞彙補充：
· bait可指故意以逗弄或侮辱性言語來激怒某人；
· haze可指戲弄（新生等，有時作為加入美國大學生聯誼會的條件），為美式用法。

☀ <span class="category">**嘲笑、取笑的名詞和形容詞：**</span>
<span class="vocabulary">**sarcasm**</span> [ˈsɑ:kæzəm; 美 ˈsɑ:rk-]
<span class="definition">n. [U] 諷刺、嘲諷、挖苦：</span>‘That will be useful, ’ she snapped with heavy sarcasm (= she really thought it would not be useful at all). “還真有用咧。”她狠狠挖苦道。/ His voice was heavy with sarcasm. 他的話語裡明顯有諷刺意味。/ dry sarcasm（尤美）不漏聲色的諷刺 / His voice dripped sarcasm. 他的語氣裡充滿了嘲諷。/ James caught the sarcasm in her voice. 詹姆斯在她的語調裡察覺到了一絲譏諷的味道。/ 'Great!' she said, sarcasm dripping from her voice. “真是太棒了！”她說道，語調裡流露出譏諷。/ a trace of sarcasm in his voice 他話語中流露的嘲諷腔調 / I detected a touch of sarcasm in his remarks. 我察覺到他的話中有一絲譏諷的味道。/ 'What a pity,' Graham said with a hint of sarcasm. “太遺憾了，”格雷厄姆略帶挖苦地說道。

<span class="vocabulary">**sneer**</span> [snɪə(r); 美 snɪr]
<span class="definition">n. [usually sing.] 嘲笑、譏諷：</span>A faint sneer of satisfaction crossed her face. 她的臉上掠過一絲得意的冷笑。/ He gave an arrogant sneer. 他露出傲慢輕蔑的笑容。/ The smile slowly turned into a sneer. 那微笑慢慢露出輕蔑之意。/ Her lips twisted into a sneer. 她一撇嘴，露出輕蔑的笑容。/ a sneer in your voice 聲音中透出的嘲諷 / a sneer on sb's face 某人臉上流露出的嘲諷

<span class="vocabulary">**jeer**</span> [dʒɪə(r); 美 dʒɪr]
<span class="definition">n. [usually pl.] 嘲笑、譏諷、奚落（的言語）：</span>He walked on to the stage to be greeted with jeers and whistles. 他登上舞臺，結果迎來陣陣嘲笑和口哨噓聲。/ He stared sullenly into space, pretending not to hear the jeers. 他面色陰鬱地凝視前方，假裝沒聽見那些冷嘲熱諷。/ the heckling and jeers of his audience 觀眾對他的起鬨和嘲笑
                      
<span class="vocabulary">**ridicule**</span> [ˈrɪdɪkju:l]
<span class="definition">n. [U]（對某人或某事物的）譏笑、嘲笑、奚落：</span>As a heavy child, she became the object of ridicule from classmates. 由於體胖，她成了班上同學嘲笑的物件。/ to hold sb up to ridicule (= make fun of sb publicly) 公然取笑某人 / The process of judicial selection was held up to ridicule. 司法選舉的過程成為了笑柄。/ She is an object of ridicule in the tabloid newspapers. 她是小報譏笑諷刺的物件。/ public ridicule 大眾的嘲笑

<span class="vocabulary">**taunt**</span> [tɔ:nt]
<span class="definition">n. [C] 嘲笑、諷刺、奚落（的言辭）：</span>playground taunt（尤英）操場嘲弄 / schoolyard taunt（美）校園嘲弄 / Black players often had to endure racist taunts. 黑人運動員經常得忍受種族歧視性的奚落。

<span class="vocabulary">**tease**</span> [ti:z] 
<span class="definition">n. 1 [usually sing.] 愛捉弄別人的一個人，愛拿別人開玩笑的一個人（尤指透過對別人說謊或不說出別人想知道的事情）：</span>瞭解即可 <span class="definition">2 [usually sing.] 戲弄，捉弄，取笑：</span>瞭解即可

<span class="vocabulary">**joke**</span> [dӡəʊk] 
<span class="definition">n. [C] 取笑：</span>They often make jokes at each other’s expense. 他們經常相互取笑。
          
<span class="vocabulary">**crack**</span> [kræk]
<span class="definition">n. [C] [非正式用法] 笑話，尤指挖苦人的笑話：</span>He made a very unfair crack about her looks. 他開玩笑地損了一下她的長相，言語很是刻薄。/ When Paul made the crack about the 'famous girl detective', I began to suspect that he had it in for you. 當保羅挖苦你是“著名的女偵探”時，我開始懷疑他是不是故意刁難你。

<span class="vocabulary">**dig**</span> [dɪɡ] 
<span class="definition">n. [C] dig (at sb/sth) 使某人惱怒或不安的一席話，即挖苦：</span>He kept making sly little digs at me. 他總是拐彎抹角地挖苦我。
           
<span class="vocabulary">**satire**</span> [ˈsætaɪə(r)]
<span class="definition">n. 1 [U, C]（對一個人、主意、制度等的）嘲諷、諷刺、譏諷：</span>The novel is a stinging satire on American politics. 這部小說是對美國政治的尖銳諷刺。/ The movie is a brilliant satire on Hollywood. 這部影片是對好萊塢的絕妙諷刺。/ The commercial side of the Christmas season is an easy target for satire. 聖誕季商業化的一面很容易招來譏諷。<span class="definition">2 [C] 諷刺作品：</span>political, social satire 政治諷刺作品；社會諷刺作品 / a work full of biting satire 一部充滿辛辣諷刺的作品
           
<span class="vocabulary">**irony**</span> [ˈaɪrəni]
<span class="definition">n. 1 [U, C]（出乎意料的）奇異可笑之處；有諷刺意味的情況：</span>The irony is that when he finally got the job, he discovered he didn't like it. 諷刺的是，當他最終得到那份工作時，他發現自己並不喜歡它。/ It was one of life's little ironies. 那是生活中的一個小小的嘲弄。/ The irony is that many officials in Washington agree in private that their policy is inconsistent. 具有諷刺意味的是，華盛頓的很多官員都私下裡承認他們的政策前後不一致。/ Opposition parties lost no time in stressing the irony of his return to power after being rejected by voters in November. 他11月份遭選民否決後又重新上臺，反對黨馬上對這一諷刺性現象大做文章。<span class="definition">2 [U]（常指笑話中的）反語；反話：</span>‘England is famous for its food,’ she said with heavy irony. “英格蘭的食物很有名哪。”她極其諷刺地說道。/ There was a note of irony in his voice. 他的聲音裡有一絲挖苦的味道。/ She said it without a hint/trace of irony. 她說此話沒有一點嘲諷之意。/ They find only irony in the narrator's concern. 他們發現敘述者只是一味地嘲諷。/ Sinclair examined the closed, clever face for any hint of irony, but found none. 辛克萊審視著那張不動聲色的精明的臉龐，試圖尋找任何冷嘲的跡象，但是卻什麼都沒發現。

<span class="vocabulary">**sarcastic**</span> [sɑ:ˈkæstɪk; 美 sɑ:rˈk-]
<span class="definition">adj. 諷刺的、嘲諷的、挖苦的：</span>sarcastic comments 冷嘲熱諷的話 / She poked fun at people's shortcomings with sarcastic remarks. 她冷嘲熱諷地拿別人的缺點開玩笑。/ a sarcastic manner 嘲諷的態度 / ‘There's no need to be sarcastic, ’ she said. “不必挖苦人嘛。”她說。/ His tone was lightly sarcastic. 他的語氣略帶嘲諷。/ He was very sarcastic about my attempts at telling jokes. 他對我試圖講笑話大加譏諷。
           
<span class="vocabulary">**ironic**</span> [aɪˈrɒnɪk; 美 -ˈrɑ:n-]
<span class="definition">adj. 1（某人的話語）表示反諷的：</span>an ironic comment 譏諷的話 / At the most solemn moments he will flash a mocking smile or make an ironic remark. 在最莊嚴的時刻他臉上會閃現嘲弄的微笑或說一句諷刺挖苦的話。/ People used to call me Mr Popularity at high school, but they were being ironic. 人們中學時常把我稱作“萬人迷先生”,但他們是在挖苦我。<span class="definition">2（情形）（因出乎意料而）奇怪的，好笑的，具有諷刺意味的，令人啼笑皆非的：</span>It's ironic that she became a teacher--she used to hate school. 令人啼笑皆非的是她成了教師——她過去一向厭惡上學。/ Does he not find it ironic that the sort of people his movie celebrates hardly ever watch this kind of movie? 他電影裡頌揚的那些人幾乎從不看這種電影,難道他不覺得這是種諷刺嗎？
