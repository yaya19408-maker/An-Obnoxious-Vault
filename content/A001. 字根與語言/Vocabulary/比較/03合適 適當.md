☀ <span class="category">**合適、適當、適用、相容的形容詞：**</span>
<span class="vocabulary">**suitable**</span> ['sju:təbl] 
<span class="definition">adj. suitable (for sth/sb) / suitable (to do sth)（對於某一目的、宗旨或場合來說）恰當的、合適的。是一個常規用詞：</span>suitable candidate, suitable place, suitable moment 合適的人選；合適的地方；合適的時機 / The shampoo is suitable for everyday use. 這種洗髮水適合日常使用。/ This programme is not suitable for children. 這個節目兒童不宜。/ conditions suitable to their development 適合他們發展的環境

<span class="vocabulary">**suited**</span> [ˈsu:tɪd; British English also ˈsju:-]
<span class="definition">adj. 1 ~ (to/for sb/sth) [名詞前不可用] 合適的、適當的：</span>She was ideally suited to the job. 她是這項工作的理想人選。/ She was ideally suited to the part of Eva Peron. 她演愛娃·庇隆這個角色再合適不過了。/ He is not really suited for a teaching career. 他不大適合做教師。/ He was not really suited for army life. 他不太適合軍隊生活。/ an approach especially suited to the adult learner 特別適合成年學習者的方法 / / a song ill suited to male voices 不適合男聲演唱的歌曲 / This diet is suited to anyone who wants to lose weight fast. 這一食譜適合每一個希望迅速減肥的人。/ The area is well suited to road cycling as well as off-road riding. 這個地方很適合舉行腳踏車公路賽和越野賽。/ land that is well suited to dairy farming 非常適合奶牛飼養的土地 / Satellites are uniquely suited to provide this information. 只有人造衛星能提供此類資訊。<span class="definition">2 [名詞前不可用]（兩個人，尤指情侶）般配的：</span>Jo and I are very well suited. 我跟喬非常合得來。/ They were well suited to each other. 他們倆是天造地設的一對。/ They were not suited to one another. 他們倆彼此不般配。

<span class="vocabulary">**fit**</span> [fɪt] 
<span class="definition">adj. fit for sb/sth / fit to do sth 合適的；品質恰當的；素質或技能合格的、能勝任的：</span>a fit place for me to live 一個適合我居住的地方 / It was a meal fit for a king. 這飯菜算得上御膳了。/ You must do as you think fit. 你覺得怎麼合適就怎麼做吧。/ She’s not fit to be a mother! 她不適合做母親。/ He’s so angry he’s in no fit state to see anyone. 他正在氣頭上，現在不適合見人。
           
<span class="vocabulary">**fitting**</span> [ˈfɪtɪŋ]
<span class="definition">adj. 1 [正式] 適合（某場合）的：</span>It seemed entirely fitting that she should be wearing black. 看起來她穿黑色很合適。/ I did not think it fitting to ask James about his daughter's death. 我認為向詹姆斯詢問他女兒去世的事不太好。/ The award was a fitting tribute to her years of devoted work. 這個獎項是對她多年全心全意工作的恰如其分的褒獎。/ A fitting end to the meal would be a glass of port. 餐後最好來一杯波爾圖葡萄酒。/ It is fitting that the new centre for European studies should be in a university that teaches every European language. 新的歐洲研究中心應設在教授所有歐洲語言的大學裡才合適。/ A solitary man, it was perhaps fitting that he should have died alone. 他是個離群索居的人，孤獨地死去也許正得其所。/ The President's address was a fitting end to a bitter campaign. 總統的講話為一場激烈的競選運動適時畫上了句號。<span class="definition">2 -fitting用於構成形容詞，指合身的、合適的：</span>a tight-fitting dress 緊身的連衣裙
           
<span class="vocabulary">**favourable**</span> [ˈfeɪvərəbl]
<span class="definition">adj. ~ (to/for sb/sth)（條件）適宜的：</span>Conditions are now favourable for sking. 現在的天氣條件正適合滑雪。
           
<span class="vocabulary">**fertile**</span> [ˈfɜ:taɪl; 美 ˈfɜ:rtl]
<span class="definition">adj. [常用於名詞前]（環境或條件）適於…的：</span>The region at the time was fertile ground for revolutionary movements (= there were the necessary conditions for them to develop easily). 當時該地區是革命運動的沃土。/ a fertile breeding ground for this kind of violent racism 易於滋生這種極端種族主義的溫床

<span class="vocabulary">**appropriate**</span> [ə'prəʊprɪət] 
<span class="definition">adj. appropriate (for/to sth)（對於某種情況或條件來說）合適的（或可接受的、恰當的）。相對正式：</span>appropriate response, appropriate measure, appropriate method, appropriate time 恰如其分的反應；恰當的措施；恰當的方法；合適的時候 / to take appropriate action 採取適當的行動 / Jeans are not appropriate for a formal party. 正式聚會上穿牛仔褲不合適。/ It was thought appropriate to award her the prize. 大家都認為應該把獎頒給她。

<span class="vocabulary">**proper**</span> ['prɒpə] 
<span class="definition">adj. [尤英] 只用於名詞前，正確的、恰當的、符合規則的：</span>We should have had a proper discussion before voting. 我們本應在表決之前好好討論一下才是。/ Please follow the proper procedures for dealing with complaints. 請按正當手續處理投訴。/ Nothing is in its proper place. 東西都放得亂七八糟。/ the prim and proper town of South Springs 一座規規矩矩的南泉小鎮

<span class="vocabulary">**due**</span> [dju:] 
<span class="definition">adj. 正式用法，指在某種條件下合適的，適當的，只作前置定語：</span>He was charged with driving without due care and attention.（英）他被控魯莽駕駛。/ due consideration 適當考慮 / to make due allowance for sth 適當考慮某事

<span class="vocabulary">**fair**</span> [feə] 
<span class="definition">adj. fair (to/on sb)（在某種情況中）合適的，適當的，可接受的，反義詞為unfair：</span>fair deal, fair wage, fair price, fair question 公平交易；合理的工資；公道的價格；恰當的問題 / The punishment was very fair. 這個處罰很公正。/ To be fair, we hadn’t really spent enough time on the job. 老實說，我們在這項工作上花的時間不夠。

<span class="vocabulary">**right**</span> [raɪt] 
<span class="definition">adj. 1 在道德、法律或個人義務上正當的，妥當的，常作表語。與wrong相反：</span>morally right 道德上正確 / You were quite right to criticize him. 你批評他批評得很對。/ I hope we’re doing the right thing. 我希望我們這樣做是妥當的。/ It may be a very easy way to make money, but that doesn’t make it right. 這可能是個很輕鬆的賺錢方法，但並不等於是正當的。<span class="definition">2 對某一情況、事物或人來說是正確的，即恰當的，正好的，適當的。與wrong相反：</span>He’s the right man for the job. 他是這份工作的合適人選。/ I’m glad you split up. She wasn’t right for you. 我很高興你們分手了。她不適合你。/ I was waiting for the right moment to ask him. 我在等待時機問他這件事。

<span class="vocabulary">**good**</span> [ɡʊd] 
<span class="definition">adj. good (for sth/to do sth) / good (for sb) 合適的、適宜的、恰當的。是一個簡單用詞：</span>Now is a good time to buy a house. 現在買房子正是時候。/ She would be good for the job. 她幹這份工作很合適。/ Can we change our meeting--Monday isn’t good for me. 我們把見面時間改改吧——星期一我不方便。

<span class="vocabulary">**perfect**</span> ['pə:fɪkt, pə'fekt] 
<span class="definition">adj. perfect for sb/sth 對於…來說正合適的，理想的，相當於ideal：</span>It was a perfect day for a picnic. 那是野餐最理想的天氣。/ She’s the perfect candidate for the job. 她是這項工作的最佳人選。/ ‘Will 2.30 be OK for you?’ ‘Perfect, thanks.’ “2:30對你合適嗎？”“正合適。謝謝。” / The town’s position in the region makes it perfect for touring. 這個城鎮在此地區的地理位置使它成為理想的觀光地。
           
<span class="vocabulary">**ideal**</span> [aɪˈdi:əl]
<span class="definition">adj. ~ (for sth) 完美的、最合適的，如理想的：</span>She's the ideal candidate for the job. 她是這項工作最合適的人選。/ She decided that I was the ideal person to take over the job. 她認定我是接手這份工作的最佳人選。/ This beach is ideal for children. 這個海灘是孩子的理想去處。/ The hotel's size makes it ideal for large conferences. 這家賓館的規模對舉辦大型會議來說非常理想。/ The houses are absolutely ideal for families with young children. 對有小孩子的家庭來說，這些房子可算是絕對理想了。/ I really love the area and see it as an ideal place to start my managerial career. 我真的很喜歡那個地區，把它視為開創我管理事業的理想之地。/ The trip to Paris will be an ideal opportunity to practise my French. 去巴黎旅行將是我練習法語的絕好機會。/ Language learning often takes place in a less than ideal environment. 語言學習經常在不盡理想的環境中進行。

<span class="vocabulary">**great**</span> [ɡreɪt] 
<span class="definition">adj. [非正式] great for (doing) sth 對某事物來說非常合適或有用的：</span>This gadget’s great for opening jars. 這小玩意兒開廣口瓶挺好。/ Try this cream--it’s great for spots. 試試這種乳霜吧——對皮膚斑點挺有效。

<span class="vocabulary">**excellent**</span> ['eksələnt] 
<span class="definition">adj. 適合於：</span>These potatoes are excellent for baking. 這些土豆適合烘烤。

<span class="vocabulary">**happy**</span> ['hæpɪ] 
<span class="definition">adj. [正式]（言語、思想或行為）對某一情況來說合適的，適當的：</span>That wasn’t the happiest choice of words. 那樣的措辭並不是十分恰當。

<span class="vocabulary">**just**</span> [dӡʌst] 
<span class="definition">adj. 常作前置定語，（在某種情況中）合適的，恰當的：</span>a just reward 應有的報償 / a just punishment 應有的懲罰 / He got his just deserts. 他罪有應得。

<span class="vocabulary">**likely**</span> ['laɪklɪ] 
<span class="definition">adj. 似乎適合於一個目的的，相當於promising：</span>She seems the most likely candidate for the job. 這項工作，她似乎是最適宜的人選了。

<span class="vocabulary">**consistent**</span> [kən'sɪstənt] 
<span class="definition">adj.（論點、論據或一套觀點）相互連貫的：</span>His argument is not even internally consistent. 他的論點甚至本身也自相矛盾。

<span class="vocabulary">**square**</span> [skweə] 
<span class="definition">adj. square with sth（和某事物）相一致的，相吻合的：</span>That isn’t quite square with what you said yesterday. 這跟你昨天所講的不大吻合。

<span class="vocabulary">**worthy**</span> ['wə:ðɪ] 
<span class="definition">adj. -worthy 構成複合詞，指適合於…的：</span>roadworthy 適於在公路上行駛的
           
<span class="vocabulary">**decent**</span> [ˈdi:snt]
<span class="definition">adj. 1 在某種情況中為人們所接受的，如合宜的、適當的：</span>She ought to have waited for a decent interval before getting married again. 她再次嫁人也應該適當等上一段時間。/ But, after a decent interval, trade relations began to return to normal. 但是適當地間斷一段時間後，貿易關係開始恢復正常。/ He should do the decent thing and resign. 他應該識時務地辭職。<span class="definition">2 [非正式] 穿好了衣服因而適宜見人的：</span>I can't go to the door─I'm not decent. 我不能去開門——我還沒穿好衣服。
           
<span class="vocabulary">**applicable**</span> [əˈplɪkəbl; ˈæplɪkəbl]
<span class="definition">adj. [名詞前不常用] ~ (to sb/sth) 適用於…的：</span>Much of the form was not applicable (= did not apply) to me. 表格中很多部分不適用於我。/ What is a reasonable standard for one family is not applicable for another. 對一個家庭合理的標準並不適用於另一個家庭。/ Appraisal has traditionally been seen as most applicable to those in management and supervisory positions. 傳統上認為業績評定最適用於那些身處管理和監督職位之人。/ Give details of children where applicable (= if you have any). 如有子女請提供詳情。/ new developments that could prove directly applicable to the treatment of some cancers 可證明能直接應用於某些癌症治療的新成果 / The rules have now been made equally applicable to all members. 這些規則現在對所有成員都同樣適用。/ The theory does not seem easily applicable in this case. 該理論似乎不能簡單地應用於這一個案。/ His message is very applicable to high-school students.（very尤為美式用法）他的資訊對高中生很適用。/ The offer is only applicable for flights during the week. 這個報價只適用於本週內的航班。/ The law is applicable to everyone. 該法律適用於所有人。
                   
<span class="vocabulary">**compatible**</span> [kəmˈpætəbl]
<span class="definition">adj. 1 ~ (with sb/sth)（機器，尤指計算機）可共用的，即相容的：</span>compatible software 可相容的軟體 / The new system will be compatible with existing equipment. 新的系統將與現有的裝置相互相容。<span class="definition">2~ (with sb/sth)（一些想法、信念、方法或事物）可共存的、可共用的，即相容的：</span>Are measures to protect the environment compatible with economic growth? 保護環境的措施與經濟的增長協調嗎？/ compatible blood groups 相容的血型 / Free enterprise, he argued, was compatible with Russian values and traditions. 他認為自由企業制並不違背俄羅斯的價值觀和傳統。/ Marriage and the life I live just don't seem compatible. 婚姻和我的生活好像就是沒法相容。/ The theory does not seem compatible with his other ideas. 這個理論似乎與他的其他想法不一致。
   
<span class="vocabulary">**kosher**</span> [ˈkəʊʃə(r); 美 ˈkoʊ-]
<span class="definition">adj. 人們通常認為正確的、合適的：</span>Acting was not a kosher trade for an upper-class girl. 演戲對一個出身上流社會的女孩兒來說不是個合適的職業。/ I guessed something wasn't quite kosher. 我覺得有點不對勁兒。/ It all seems perfectly kosher. 一切似乎都完全沒有問題。

相關詞義延伸：
· kosher還可指（食物）合乎猶太教的教規要求的。也可指誠實的；合法的。

☀ <span class="category">**合適、適當的副詞和介詞：**</span>
<span class="vocabulary">**properly**</span> ['prɒpəlɪ] 
<span class="definition">adv. [尤英] 以一種正確或適當的方式，即正確地、適當地：</span>How much money do we need to do the job properly? 我們需要多少錢才能做好這件事？/ The television isn’t working properly. 這臺電視機出問題了。/ Make sure the letter is properly addressed. 一定要正確書寫信上的地址。

<span class="vocabulary">**to**</span> [tu:] 
<span class="definition">prep. 用於表示某人對…的態度或反應：</span>His music isn’t really to my taste. 他的音樂實在不合我的口味。

☀ <span class="category">**合適、適當的名詞：**</span>
<span class="vocabulary">**fit**</span> [fɪt] 
<span class="definition">n. 1 [C, U] 常與形容詞連用，適合，尤指衣服合身：</span>The sweater is a comfortable fit--not too tight and not too loose. 這件毛衣不鬆不緊，非常合身。/ The door was a poor fit and didn’t open properly. 這道門和門框尺寸不合，不好開啟。/ File away any excess metal until a snug fit is achieved. 銼平多餘的金屬直到完全吻合。<span class="definition">2 [C] fit (between A and B) 匹配，相互合適：</span>a good fit between the recruit and the job 新僱員和工作之間很好的匹配 / We need to achieve the best fit between the staff required and the staff available. 我們需要在用人上實現供需兩方面的最佳吻合。/ He argues that there is a lack of fit between our system of values and capitalism. 他認為我們的價值體系和資本主義不相稱。
           
<span class="vocabulary">**fitness**</span> [ˈfɪtnəs]
<span class="definition">n. [U] ~ for sth/to do sth 適合（某事物或做某事）、稱職：</span>He convinced us of his fitness for the task. 他使我們相信他適合做這項工作。/ There were doubts about her fitness to hold office. 她是否稱職還不確定。

<span class="vocabulary">**match**</span> [mætʃ] 
<span class="definition">n. [sing.] 相配的人或事物：</span>Her choice of dress was a good match for her skin tone. 她選擇的服裝與她的膚色搭配得很好。/ The blouse and skirt are a perfect match. 這襯衣和裙子堪稱絕配。/ This fabric makes a good match for the wallpapers. 這種織物跟牆紙很相配。/ The colour and light are a good match. 顏色和光線非常搭配。/ They are a perfect match for each other. 他們是天造地設的一對兒。/ Our job is to find the right match for our clients. 我們的工作是為客戶找到適合自己的東西。
           
<span class="vocabulary">**suitability**</span> [ˌsu:təˈbɪləti; British English also ˌsju:-]
<span class="definition">n. [U] 合適：</span>There is no doubt about her suitability for the job. 毫無疑問，她適合做這個工作。/ the greater suitability of women for the work 婦女更適合幹這項工作 / to assess the suitability of the buildings for disabled people 評估住房是否適合殘疾人居住 / Potential donors can be tested for suitability. 潛在供體可以接受適合性檢驗。
          
<span class="vocabulary">**proportion**</span> [prəˈpɔ:ʃn; 美 -ˈpɔ:rʃn]
<span class="definition">n. [U, C, usually pl.]（在程度、重要性等方面）正確的比例：</span>correct proportion 正確的比例：</span>Always try to keep a sense of proportion (= of the relative importance of different things). 應常記在心，事有輕重緩急之分。/ We must not lose our sense of proportion. 我們不能喪失主次觀念。/ Try to keep your view of the situation in proportion (= not think it is more serious than it is). 看待情勢要客觀。/ The punishment was out of all proportion to the crime. 罰不當罪。/ The costs of the plan are out of proportion to the budget available. 計劃的成本與現有的預算不成比例。/ Everything just got blown out of proportion. 所有一切都變得不可收拾了。/ We've got to keep this in proportion. 我們應當正確對待這一點。/ This issue was about to be blown out of proportion. 這個問題快要被誇大得沒邊沒沿兒了。/ The problem has been exaggerated out of all proportion. 問題被過分誇大了。

<span class="vocabulary">**possible**</span> ['pɒsəbl] 
<span class="definition">n. [C] 適合於某一工作（或目的）因而可以被選擇的人或事物，即合適的人（或事物）：</span>Out of all the people interviewed, there are only five possibles. 在所有面試過的人中，僅有五個合適的人選。

<span class="vocabulary">**catch**</span> [kætʃ] 
<span class="definition">n. [sing.] [舊時]（所看中的適合於結婚或僱傭等的）物件：</span>瞭解即可 

☀ <span class="category">**適合、適用於的動詞：**</span>
<span class="vocabulary">**fit**</span> [fɪt] 
<span class="definition">vt.＆vi. 1 形狀和尺寸適合於某人或某物。是一個常規用詞：</span>The T-shirt fits you well. 這件T恤衫你穿很合身。/ The key doesn’t fit the lock. 這把鑰匙打不開這把鎖。/ If the top of the box fits badly, the contents will spill out. 如果盒蓋蓋不嚴，裡面的東西就會灑出來。<span class="definition">2 與…一致、相符、相稱；或使…與…一致、相符、相稱：</span>to fit career into family life 把事業跟家庭生活協調起來 / to fit the punishment to the crime 根據罪行量刑 / That chair should fit into the room easily. 那把椅子應該容易和房間相配。/ The words fit perfectly with the music. 歌詞與音樂非常相配。<span class="definition">vi. 尺寸、型別、數量等可放在某處，即可容納等：</span>to fold long legs to fit under the table 把腿彎起來伸到桌子下 / The screws fit the holes exactly. 這些螺絲剛好能擰進這些孔。/ Will this box fit into the cupboard? 這個箱子能放進壁櫥裡嗎？/ It’s won’t fit. 擱不下。<span class="definition">vt. fit sb/sth (for sth) [尤英] 使…適合（或勝任）某項工作、任務或角色：</span>His past experience fits him for the job. 他過去的經驗讓他能夠勝任這項工作。
           
<span class="vocabulary">**suit**</span> [sju:t]
