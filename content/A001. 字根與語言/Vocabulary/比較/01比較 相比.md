☀ <span class="category">**比較、比喻的動詞：**</span>
<span class="vocabulary">**compare**</span> [kəm'peə] 
<span class="definition">vt. 1 compare A and B / compare A with/to B 將不同的人或事物進行對比或比較（以看其相似或相異程度）：</span>to compare their situation and ours 把他們的狀況與我們的相比 / We compared the two reports carefully. 我們仔細地比較了這兩份報告。/ Standards in health care have improved enormously compared to 40 years ago. 與40年前相比，衛生保健的水平得到了極大提高。<span class="definition">2 compare A to B 表明某人或某事物與其他人或事物相似，如比喻等：</span>瞭解即可
           
<span class="vocabulary">**contrast**</span> [kənˈtrɑ:st; 美-ˈtræst]
<span class="definition">vt. ~ (A and/with B) 對兩個事物進行對比、對照（以找出差異）：</span>It is interesting to contrast the British legal system with the American one. 把英國的法制與美國的加以對比很有意思。/ The poem contrasts youth and age. 這首詩對比了青春與老年。/ She contrasted the situation then with the present crisis. 她將當時的情況和目前的危機進行對比。/ Contrast that approach with what goes on in most organizations. 將那個方法和大多數機構現行的做法進行對比。/ He contrasted her brashness unfavourably with his mother's gentleness. 他拿她的粗魯與自己母親的溫柔相比，那她就遜色多了。/ Compare and contrast the two main characters in the play. 比較並對照劇中的兩個主要角色。<span class="definition">vi. ~ (with sth)（靠近或作比較時）顯出明顯的差異，形成對比：</span>Her actions contrasted sharply with her promises. 她的行動與她的諾言相差甚遠。/ Her actions and her promises contrasted sharply. 她的行動與她的諾言相差甚遠。/ Johnson's easy charm contrasted sharply with the prickliness of his boss. 約翰遜的親和力和他老闆易怒的性格形成了鮮明的對比。/ The open approach contrasts favourably with the exclusivity of some universities. 這種開放式的做法與一些大學的封閉排外相比，具有明顯優勢。/ This statement contrasts starkly with his previous statements. 這一陳述與他先前的陳述形成了鮮明的對比。/ Paint the wall in a contrasting colour. 把這面牆刷成對比鮮明的顏色。/ His cream shirt contrasted beautifully with his tan skin. 米黃色的襯衫恰到好處地反襯出他曬黑的皮膚。

<span class="vocabulary">**pale**</span> [peɪl] 
<span class="definition">vi. pale in/by comparison結構可表示顯得遜色，相形見絀：</span>When someone you love has a life-threatening illness, everything else pales in comparison. 當你所愛的人得了致命的疾病時，其他的一切就都顯得不重要了。
           
<span class="vocabulary">**eclipse**</span> [ɪˈklɪps]
<span class="definition">vt. 使…的重要性黯然失色或相形見絀：</span>Though a talented player, he was completely eclipsed by his brother. 他雖是一個天才的運動員，但與他的兄弟相比就黯然失色了。/ The gramophone had been eclipsed by new technology such as the compact disc. 鐳射唱片等新技術使留聲機黯然失色。/ Of course, nothing is going to eclipse winning the Olympic title. 當然，沒有什麼能比得過贏得奧運會冠軍。

☀ <span class="category">**比較、對比、比喻的名詞：**</span>
<span class="vocabulary">**comparison**</span> [kəmˈpærɪsn]
<span class="definition">n. 1 [U] ~ (with sb/sth) 對兩個或兩個以上的人或事物進行比較的行為，即比較：</span>Comparison with other oil-producing countries is extremely interesting. 與其他石油生產國作一比較是很有意思的。/ I enclose the two plans for comparison. 茲附上兩份計劃以供比較。/ The education system bears/stands no comparison with that in many Eastern European countries (= it is not as good). 這種教育制度比不上許多東歐國家的教育制度。/ a price-comparison site 價格比較網站 / It's difficult to make a direct comparison--the two things are so different. 這兩樣東西太不一樣了，很難直接比較。/ The similarity between the two invites compartment. 兩者的相似性讓人很容易拿它們作比較。/ Jane is still young, and Fiona seems old by compartment. 簡依然年輕，而菲奧娜相比之下就顯老。/ Let's put them side by side for compartment. 把它們放在一起比較一下吧。/ The glasses are small in compartment with the old ones. 與舊杯子相比這些杯子小一些。/ to provide a basis for compartment 提供比較的基礎 <span class="definition">2 [C] ~ (of A and/to/with B) / ~ (between A and B) 一次比較、相比：</span>a comparison of the rail systems in Britain and France 英國和法國鐵路系統的比較 / a comparison of the British and German economies. 英國經濟與德國經濟的比較 / Its recommendations are based on detailed comparisons between the public and private sectors. 建議是建立在對國營部門與私營部門進行詳細比較的基礎上的。/ comparisons between Britain and the rest of Europe 英國與歐洲其他國家之間的比較 / a comparison of men's salaries with those of women 男女工資的比較 / It is difficult to make a comparison with her previous book--they are completely different. 這很難與她以前的書相比——兩者是截然不同的。/ You can draw comparisons with the situation in Ireland (= say how the two situations are similar). 這種情形可與愛爾蘭的相比。<span class="definition">3 [C] 比喻、比擬：</span>a comparison of the brain to a computer (= showing what is similar) 將大腦比作計算機 / It is demonstrably an unfair comparison. 這樣打比方顯然不公平。/ He finds the comparison of insect wings with a sailing boat useful up to a point. 他發現把昆蟲的翅膀比作一艘帆船在某種程度上是有用的。
           
<span class="vocabulary">**metaphor**</span> [ˈmetəfə(r)]
<span class="definition">n. [C, U] 比喻、隱喻、暗喻：</span>appropriate, apt, good, perfect metaphor 貼切的比喻；恰當的比喻 / one of the central metaphors in the book 該書中的核心隱喻之一 / an extended metaphor for human existence 對人類生存的延喻 / musical, spatial, visual metaphor 音樂隱喻；空間隱喻；視覺隱喻 / mixed metaphor 混雜隱喻 / He uses the metaphor of fire to represent hatred. 他用火來比喻仇恨。/ The ladder metaphor works in several ways. 梯子的隱喻有幾重含義。/ This 'vale of tears' is a metaphor for the human condition. "淚之谷"是塵世的隱喻說法。/ the metaphor of life as a journey 把人生比作旅程的隱喻 / a game of football used as a metaphor for the competitive struggle of life 用來喻指生活中的激烈鬥爭的一場足球比賽 / the writer's striking use of metaphor 這位作家對於隱喻的獨到運用 / the avoidance of 'violent expressions and metaphors' like 'kill two birds with one stone' 避免使用“有暴力意味的表達和隱喻”，例如“一石二鳥” / the writer's use of metaphor 作者對隱喻的運用 / To mix yet more metaphors, you were trying to run before you could walk, and I've clipped your wings. 混用更多的隱喻來說，你還沒學會走就想跑，而我又剪掉了你的翅膀。

<span class="vocabulary">**contrast**</span> [ˈkɒntrɑ:st; 美 ˈkɑ:ntræst]
<span class="definition">n. 1 [C, U] ~ (between A and B) / ~ (to/with sb/sth) 對兩個或多個人或事物進行的對比、對照：</span>to show a sharp/stark/striking contrast with sth 與某事物形成鮮明（或明顯、顯著）的對比 / Careful contrast of the two plans shows some important differences. 把兩個計劃仔細地加以對比就可看出一些重要的差異。/ The private sector, by contrast, has plenty of money to spend. 相比之下，私營部門就有很多錢可供開銷。/ In contrast, the lives of girls in well-to-do families were often very sheltered. 相反，生活在富裕家庭的女孩子通常都備受呵護。/ There is a stark contrast between the lives of the rich and those of the poor. 窮人和富人的生活之間有著鮮明的對比。/ Her hair was black, a stunning contrast to her pale complexion. 她的頭髮烏黑，與蒼白的膚色形成極大的反差。/ The two cities make an interesting contrast. 兩座城市形成了有趣的對照。/ The fresh fruit provides a contrast to the rich chocolate pudding. 新鮮的水果同甜膩的巧克力布丁形成對比。/ He draws a sharp contrast between himself and his opponents. 他與對手之間形成了鮮明的對比。/ When you look at their new system, ours seems very old-fashioned by contrast. 看看他們的新系統，相比之下我們的就顯得太過時了。/ The company lost $13 million this year, in contrast with a profit of $15 million last year. 同去年1,500萬元的盈利相比，公司今年虧損了1,300萬元。/ Their attitudes towards love and marriage stand in stark contrast to those of their parents. 他們對待婚姻的態度同他們的父母形成鮮明的對比。<span class="definition">2 [U]（攝影或繪畫中的）顏色反差，明暗對比（以創造一種特殊的效果）：</span>The artist's use of contrast is masterly. 這位藝術家嫻熟地運用了明暗對比。/ The flowers provide a contrast with the dark background. 這些花朵與深色的背景形成反差。<span class="definition">3 [U]（電視螢幕的）影象明暗對比度，反差：</span>Use this button to adjust the contrast. 用此按鈕調節影象明暗的對比度。/ a television with brighter colours, better contrast, and digital sound 顏色更為鮮豔、影象對比度更高、具備數字音響效果的電視
           
<span class="vocabulary">**analogy**</span> [əˈnælədʒi]
<span class="definition">n. 1 [C] ~ (between A and B) / ~ (with sth)（兩個事物之間的）相似、類似；類比、比喻：</span>She drew an analogy between running the economy and a housewife's weekly budget. 她用家庭主婦一週的預算類比管理經濟。/ She suggested an analogy between the human heart and a pump. 她打了個比喻，把人的心臟比作一個泵。/ It is probably easier to make an analogy between the courses of the planets, and two trains travelling in the same direction. 將行星的執行比作兩列火車朝同一方向行駛可能更容易些。/ There are no analogies with any previous legal cases. 以往的法律案件沒有哪一宗可與此案類比。/ A close analogy with the art of singing can be made. 可以用歌唱藝術作一個很切近的類比。/ historical, musical, sporting（英）, sports（美）analogy 歷史類比；音樂類比；體育類比 / The Wild West analogy does not fit here. 西大荒的類比用在這兒不合適。/ The computer is a useful analogy for the brain. 計算機是人腦很恰當的類比物。/ There is an analogy here with the way an engine works. 這個與發動機的工作原理類似。<span class="definition">2 [U] 類比或類推的過程：</span>learning by analogy 用類推法學習 / We can understand this theory by analogy with human beings. 我們用人來作類比就能理解這個理論了。/ Argument from analogy is not always valid. 類比得出的結論並不總是能站住腳。

☀ <span class="category">**比較的介詞和連詞：**</span>
<span class="vocabulary">**against**</span> [ə'ɡenst, ə'ɡeɪnst] 
<span class="definition">prep. 用於比較兩個事物：</span>You must weigh the benefits against the cost. 你一定要權衡利益與成本。/ Check your receipts against the statement. 核對你的收據與結算單是否相符。/ What’s the rate of exchange against the dollar? 與美元的兌換率是多少？

<span class="vocabulary">**to**</span> [tu:] 
<span class="definition">prep. 用於引出比較或比率的第二部分，即比：</span>I prefer walking to climbing. 我喜歡散步多於喜歡爬山。/ The industry today is nothing to what it once was. 這一行業的現狀與昔日的盛況相比微不足道。/ We won by six goals to three. 我們以六比三獲勝。

<span class="vocabulary">**on**</span> [ɒn] 
<span class="definition">prep. 與…相比：</span>Sales are up on last year. 銷售量比去年增長了。

<span class="vocabulary">**beside**</span> [bɪ'saɪd] 
<span class="definition">prep. 與…相比：</span>My painting looks childish beside yours. 同你的相比，我的畫顯得很幼稚。

<span class="vocabulary">**after**</span> ['ɑːftə] 
<span class="definition">prep. 與…對照或對比：</span>It was pleasantly cool in the house after the sticky heat outside. 與戶外的溼熱相比，屋裡真是涼爽愜意。

<span class="vocabulary">**for**</span> [fɔ:] 
<span class="definition">prep. 用於dollar for dollar或mile for mile等結構中，表示對應（用於比較不同事物間的價值或品質）：</span>the Antarctic, mile for mile one of the planet’s most lifeless areas 每英里都是地球上最無生命的地區之一的南極洲。

<span class="vocabulary">**than**</span> [ðæn; ðən] 
<span class="definition">prep., conj. 1 用於引出比較的第二部分，即比：</span>I’m older than her. 我比她年齡大。/ It was much better than I’d expected. 這比我預料的要好得多。<span class="definition">2 more/less/fewer, etc. than 用於比較數量、距離、價值等（如多於，小於，少於）：</span>They talked on the phone for more than an hour. 他們在電話裡聊了一個多小時。/ It’s less than a mile to the beach. 離海灘不足一英里遠。/ There were fewer than twenty people there. 那裡有不到二十個人。

<span class="vocabulary">**or**</span> [ɔ:, ə] 
<span class="definition">conj. 用於引出一個對比性的概念：</span>He was lying--or was he? 他在說謊，還是沒有說謊？

相關詞義延伸：
· against還可指以…為背景作為對比，即襯托。

☀ <span class="category">**比較的形容詞和副詞：**</span>
<span class="vocabulary">**comparable**</span> [ˈkɒmpərəbl; 美 ˈkɑ:m-]
<span class="definition">adj. 可比較的：</span>The situation in the US is not directly comparable to that in the UK. 美國的情況與英國的不能直接相比。/ The regional variation in Australian accent is not in any way comparable to that found in the UK or US. 澳大利亞口音的地域差異與英國或美國的根本沒法比較。/ In other comparable countries real wages increased much more rapidly. 在其他具有可比性的國家，實際工資上漲更快。/ By contrast, the comparable figure for the Netherlands is 16 per cent. 相反，荷蘭的相對數字為16%。

<span class="vocabulary">**relative**</span> ['relətɪv] 
<span class="definition">adj. 1 [正式] 與其他事物相比較著來被考慮和判斷，相比較而言的：</span>the relative merits of the two plans 相比較之下兩個計劃各自的優點 / It’s all relative. 一切都是相對的。<span class="definition">2 [正式] relative (to sth) 根據與其他事物相比較時所得出的位置或關聯來被考慮的，即相對的，相關聯的：</span>the position of the sun relative to the earth 太陽與地球的相對位置 <span class="definition">3 [正式] 只用於名詞前，只有與其他事物相比較之下才存在或有某種品質的，相當於comparative：</span>They now live in relative comfort. 他們現在過得比較舒適。/ Given the failure of the previous plan, this turned out to be a relative success. 由於前面那個計劃失敗了，這個計劃算是相對成功的。

<span class="vocabulary">**so**</span> [səʊ; sə] 
<span class="definition">adv. not so ... (as ...) 用於比較，與…程度不同，即不如…（這麼…）；不像…（那樣…）：</span>I haven’t enjoyed myself so much for a long time. 我好長時間沒有這麼快活了。/ It wasn’t so good as last time. 這次不如上次好。/ It’s not so easy as you’d think. 不像你想的那麼容易。/ It’s not so much a hobby as a career. 這與其說是消遣，不如說是職業。

· with可用於說明兩個事實之間的關係；
· weigh可表示權衡。
