☀ <span class="category">**重要性、要點、意義：**</span>
<span class="vocabulary">**importance**</span> [ɪm'pɔ:tns] 
<span class="definition">n. [U] 重要性。是一個基礎且常規的用詞，最常用：</span>They attach great importance to the project. 他們高度重視這個專案。/ commercial, strategic importance 商業上的重要意義；戰略上的重要意義 / The overseas markets have now declined in importance. 海外市場的重要性現在已經有所下降。/ Deal with the issues in order of importance. 按重要程度來處理這些問題。

<span class="vocabulary">**meaning**</span> ['mi:nɪŋ] 
<span class="definition">n. 1 [U] 一種感情或經歷的真正重要性、價值：</span>the meaning of love 愛的意義 / the meaning behind an event 事件背後的意義 <span class="definition">2 [U] 使人感到自己的生命有價值的一種目標感或目的感，即人生的意義，價值，目標等：</span>meaning of life 生命的意義 / Having a child gave new meaning to their lives. 有了一個孩子使得他們的生活有了新的意義。/ For him, art held little intrinsic meaning. 對他來說，藝術沒有多少內在價值。

<span class="vocabulary">**significance**</span> [sɪɡ'nɪfɪkəns] 
<span class="definition">n. [U, C] 某事物的重要性，尤指對將來發生的事所具有的重要性，此時可稱為意義：</span>a decision of major political significance 一項具有重大政治意義的決定 / practical, strategic, symbolic, theoretical significance 實際意義；戰略意義；象徵意義；理論意義 / economic, historical, moral, social significance 經濟意義；歷史意義；道德意義；社會意義 / a meal that has particular significance for a Jewish family 對猶太家庭有特殊意義的一頓飯
                      
<span class="vocabulary">**relevance**</span> [ˈreləvəns]
<span class="definition">n. [U]（對一種形勢或一個人的）重要性、意義：</span>There are additional publications of special relevance to new graduates. 還有對於新畢業生來說特別重要的其他出版物。/ I don't see the relevance of your question. 我不懂你這個問題有什麼意義。/ a classic play of contemporary relevance 在當代仍有價值的古典戲劇 / He claims that the laws are antiquated and have no contemporary relevance. 他聲稱這些法律已經過時，沒有現實意義。/ practical relevance 實際意義 / political relevance 政治意義 / The theory bears little relevance to practice. 這一理論基本沒有實用價值。/ Constant revision of the curriculum must be undertaken to ensure its continuing relevance. 必須經常修訂課程設定以確保課程始終具有實用性。

<span class="vocabulary">**substance**</span> [ˈsʌbstəns]
<span class="definition">n. [U] [正式] 重要性：</span>matters of substance 重大問題 / Nothing of any substance was achieved in the meeting. 會議沒有取得任何實質性成果。/ It's questionable whether anything of substance has been achieved. 是否已經取得了任何實質性進展還是個問題。/ Syria will attend only if the negotiations deal with issues of substance. 只有在談判涉及重大問題時敘利亞才會參加。
           
<span class="vocabulary">**magnitude**</span> [ˈmægnɪtju:d; 美 -tu:d]
<span class="definition">n. [U] [正式] ~ (of sth) 重要；重要性：</span>These are issues of great magnitude. 這些問題極為重要。/ a discovery of the first magnitude 一項極重要的發現 / We did not realize the magnitude of the problem. 我們沒有意識到這個問題的重要性。/ America and Russia do not face a problem of the same order of magnitude as Japan. 美俄兩國所面臨問題的重要性與日本的不可同日而語。

<span class="vocabulary">**purpose**</span> ['pə:pəs] 
<span class="definition">n. [C, U]（對你來說）重要的意義，或有價值的意義：</span>Volunteer work gives her life (a sense of) purpose. 做志願工作使她的生活有了意義。
          
<span class="vocabulary">**prominence**</span> [ˈprɒmɪnəns; 美 ˈprɑ:m-]
<span class="definition">n. [U, sing.] 重要；明顯：</span>Crime prevention had to be given more prominence. 防止犯罪方面應當受到更多的重視。/ The newspapers have given undue prominence to the story. 報章對這件事的報道太多了。/ a performance which gives a special prominence to the part of Hamlet's mother 特別突出哈姆雷特的母親這一角色的表演 / His account gives due prominence to the role of the king. 他的敘述恰如其分地突出了國王的作用。/ The former rebels were given positions of prominence in the new government. 以前的反叛者在新一屆政府中被委以重任。

<span class="vocabulary">**point**</span> [pɒɪnt] 
<span class="definition">n. 1 [C]（說話或做事的）要點，重點，核心問題，一般用the point：</span>I’ll come straight to the point. 我就直說吧。/ That’s not the point. 問題不在這兒。/ Hurry up and get to the point! 快進入正題吧！/ More to the point, did they get away? 更重要的是，他們逃走了嗎？/ They argued the point for hours. 這一點他們爭論了好幾個小時了。/ a case in point 恰當的例證 / talking point 話題 / action point（會議上達成的）行動方案 <span class="definition">2 [C] 認為某人說的是重要的，或值得考慮的：</span>She has a point. 她說得有道理。/ I take your point. 我認為你說得對。
           
<span class="vocabulary">**core**</span> [kɔ:(r)]
<span class="definition">n. [C] 某事物（如問題、爭論、關注等）最重要（或核心）的部分：</span>the ability to get straight to the core of a problem 直擊問題要害的能力 / The notion that blacks comprise a problem is at the core of racist reasoning. 黑人總愛惹事這一觀念是種族主義論斷的核心。/ the core of the argument 爭論的核心 / Concern for the environment is at the core of our policies. 對環境的關注是我們政策的核心。/ At the core of our convictions is belief in individual liberty. 我們信仰的核心就是相信個人自由。/ These ideas formed the core of his philosophy. 這些思想構成了他哲學體系的核心。/ This is seen as the central core of the government's policy. 這被認為是政府政策的核心。/ the emotional core of her music 她的音樂的情感核心
                      
<span class="vocabulary">**pillar**</span> [ˈpɪlə(r)]
<span class="definition">n. [C]（組織機構、制度、信仰、協議等的）核心、基礎：</span>the central pillar of this theory 這一理論的核心支柱 / The pillar of her economic policy was keeping tight control over money supply. 她經濟政策的核心是嚴格控制貨幣的供應。

<span class="vocabulary">**plank**</span> [plæŋk]
<span class="definition">n. [C] 組織機構（尤指政黨）的政策要點、政策核心：</span>The central plank of the bill was rural development. 這一法案的核心是農村發展。/ Encouraging people to shop locally is a central plank of his environment policy. 他的環境政策的核心是鼓勵人們購買當地產品。/ The proposed law was a central plank in the manifesto. 擬議中的法律是這項宣言的主旨。/ Health and security will be key planks in the party's election campaign. 健康和安全將是該黨在競選綱領中的關鍵點。/ the main plank of the strategy 該戰略的要點

<span class="vocabulary">**weight**</span> [weɪt] 
<span class="definition">n. 1 [U]（根據重要性或意義所賦予的）權重：</span>They put different weight on the conclusions of different models. 他們對不同模型的結論賦予的權重各不相同。<span class="definition">2 [U] 重要性：</span>Consumers generally place more weight on negative information than on the positive when deciding what to buy. 消費者在決定購買的時候，一般更看重負面資訊而不是正面資訊。/ They attach too much weight to academic achievement. 他們過於強調學術成果。/ Her opinion seemed to carry little weight in the company. 她的意見在公司裡似乎不受重視。

<span class="vocabulary">**agenda**</span> [ə'dӡendə] 
<span class="definition">n. [C] 其比喻用法可指事情的重要程度：</span>In our company, quality is high on the agenda. 我們公司十分重視質量問題。/ For the government, education is now at the top of the agenda. 對政府來說，現在教育是當務之急。

<span class="vocabulary">**stress**</span> [stres] 
<span class="definition">n. [U] stress (on sth) 所賦予的特殊重要性，即強調；重點：</span>She lays great stress on punctuality. 她十分注重守時。/ to place stress on cost 強調成本 / a study of child development, with the stress on acquisition of social skills 一項以社交技能習得為重點的兒童成長研究

<span class="vocabulary">**consequence**</span> ['kɒnsɪkwəns] 
<span class="definition">n. [U] [正式] 重要性：</span>It’s of no consequence. 別擔心，這無關緊要。/ Matters of justice are of great consequence. 司法問題舉足輕重。/ I’d never done anything of consequence in my life. 我一生中從未乾過什麼大事。/ Tom’s first feature film of any consequence 湯姆第一部有分量的故事片 / The majority of these losses are of little consequence. 這些損失大部分並不嚴重。
           
<span class="vocabulary">**grandeur**</span> [ˈgrændʒə(r); -djə(r)]
<span class="definition">n. [U] 某人所擁有的（或此人認為自己所擁有的）重要性，或其社會地位的高貴、顯赫：</span>He has a sense of grandeur about him. 他覺得自己很了不起。/ She is clearly suffering from delusions of grandeur (= thinks she is more important than she really is). 她顯然是犯了妄自尊大的毛病。/ He is wholly concerned with his own grandeur. 他只關心自己的顯赫地位。

<span class="vocabulary">**import**</span> [ɪm'pɔ:t] 
<span class="definition">n. [U] 重要性，為正式用法：</span>matters of great import 非常重要的事情

<span class="vocabulary">**gravity**</span> ['ɡrævɪtɪ] 
<span class="definition">n. [U] [正式]（事件等的）極端的重要性：</span>The threat is not being treated with the gravity it deserves. 恐嚇沒有得到應有的重視。
           
<span class="vocabulary">**priority**</span> [praɪˈɒrəti; 美 -ˈɔ:r-; -ˈɑ:r-]
<span class="definition">n. 1 [C] 優先事項、在重要性上需要優先處理的事項：</span>You need to get your priorities straight.（美）你需要把個人事情的輕重緩急分清楚。/ You need to sort out your priorities before making a decision about the future. 對未來做出決定之前，你有必要分出主次。/ To reduce stress you may have to rethink your priorities in life. 要減輕壓力，你可能得重新考慮生活中的輕重緩急。/ high, low priority 重點事項；非重點事項 / Education is a top priority. 教育是當務之急。/ Our first priority is to improve standards. 我們的頭等大事是提高水平。/ Financial security was high on his list of priorities. 在他的心目中，金融安全是十分重要的一環。/ You need to get your priorities right (= decide what is important to you). 你需要把自己的事情分出輕重緩急。/ Being a parent is her first priority. 做好母親是她的頭等大事。/ The government's priority is to build more power plants. 政府的首要任務是建造更多的發電站。/ Getting food, medicine and blankets to flood victims is the most urgent priority. 為洪災地區提供食物、藥品和毯子是當務之急。/ Material possessions have always been a low priority for Mike. 對邁克來說，物質的追求一直都是次要的。/ budget, budgetary, legislative（尤美）, spending, strategic priority 預算優先考慮的問題；立法優先考慮的問題；消費優先考慮的問題；戰略上優先考慮的問題 / Using the Internet is one of the company's top strategic priorities. 使用網際網路是該公司頂級的優先戰略之一。/ We assign a high priority to research and development. 我們高度重視研究和開發。/ a list of priorities 按事情的輕重緩急列出的清單 / We need to place a higher priority on family and social issues. 我們需要優先考慮家庭和社會問題。<span class="definition">2 [U] ~ (over sth) 在各種事項中（或一群人中）的優先權、優先地位：</span>Club members will be given priority. 俱樂部成員享有優先權。/ The school will give priority to science, maths and modern languages. 學校將重點發展理科、數學和現代語言。/ The bank seems to give priority to new customers. 這家銀行似乎優先考慮新客戶。/ The proposals deserve support as they give priority to the needs of children. 這些提議值得擁護，因為它們優先考慮了兒童的需求。/ The search for a new vaccine will take priority over all other medical research. 研製新的疫苗將排在其他一切醫學研究之前。/ When hospital funds are being allocated children take priority. 分配醫院經費的時候優先考慮兒童。/ Her family takes priority over her work. 對她來說，家庭比工作重要。/ The fight against inflation took priority over measures to combat the deepening recession. 抵制通貨膨脹比抑制日益加劇的經濟衰退更為重要。/ I disagree with the premise that economic development has priority over the environment. 我不贊成經濟發展應優先於環境保護這個前提。/ Priority cases, such as homeless families, get dealt with first. 優先事項，比如無住房家庭的問題，得到優先處理。/ List the tasks in order of priority. 把各項任務按重要程度依次列出來。<span class="definition">3 [U] [英] 車輛在某處的優先通行權：</span>Buses have priority at this junction. 在這個路口，公共汽車有優先通行權。
                      
<span class="vocabulary">**precedence**</span> [ˈpresɪdəns]
<span class="definition">n. [U] ~ (over sb/sth)（某人的）優先、優先權：</span>She had to learn that her wishes did not take precedence over other people's needs. 她必須懂得，自己的願望不能先於別人的需要。/ Have as much fun as possible at college, but don't let it take precedence over work. 在大學要盡情娛樂，但不要把它看得比學習還重要。/ As the King's representative he took precedence over everyone else on the island. 作為國王的代表，他比島上其他任何人的地位都要高。/ The French kings claimed precedence over those of Spain. 法國國王比西班牙國王享有優先權。/ The needs of the patient take precedence over those of the student doctor. 先考慮病人的需要，再考慮實習醫生的需要。/ The speakers came on to the platform in order of precedence (= the most important one first). 講演人按行輩魚貫上臺。/ The guests were seated strictly in order of precedence. 客人嚴格按照地位高低就座。/ You should give your schoolwork precedence. 你應該首先完成作業。

<span class="vocabulary">**substance**</span> [ˈsʌbstəns]
<span class="definition">n. [U] 實質性內容：</span>The real substance of the report was in the third part. 這篇報告的實質性內容在第三部分。/ The image of him that the media have presented has no substance. 媒體所展示的他的形象很空洞。/ There's no difference in substance between the two points of view. 這兩種觀點事實上沒有差別。/ Nothing of substance was achieved at the meeting. 會議沒有取得任何實質性成果。

☀ <span class="category">**重要的：**</span>
<span class="vocabulary">**important**</span> [ɪm'pɔ:tnt] 
<span class="definition">adj. 1 important (to sb) 對人們或事物具有重大影響的、有重大價值的，即重要的。是一個基礎且常規的用詞，最常用：</span>important decision, important factor 重要決定；重要因素 / I have an important announcement to make. 我要宣佈一件重要的事情。/ historically important buildings 具有重大歷史意義的建築 / Spending time with children is important to me. 對我而言，和孩子們共度時光很重要。<span class="definition">2（一個人）擁有很大影響力（或權威）的：</span>an important member of the team 舉足輕重的一位隊員 / He made me feel important by asking me lots of questions about myself. 他問了許多關於我自己的問題，讓我感覺自己很重要。
           
<span class="vocabulary">**significant**</span> [sɪgˈnɪfɪkənt]
<span class="definition">adj. 意義重大的：</span>a highly significant discovery 有重大意義的發現 / Time would appear to be the significant factor in this whole drama. 時間似乎是整部劇中一個舉足輕重的因素。/ very significant piece of legislation 一條非常重要的法規 / The move was regarded as significant in Japan. 這一舉動在日本被認為意義重大。/ What makes this discovery significant is that it goes against our theory. 這一發現的重要意義在於它與我們的理論背道而馳。/ His records are impressive, but equally significant are the methods that he used to achieve them. 他的記錄令人印象深刻，但是同樣值得注意的是他取得這些記錄的方法。/ These differences are not statistically significant. 這些差異在統計學上意義不大。/ biologically, culturally, historically, militarily, politically significant 生物學上意義重大；文化上意義重大；歷史上有重大意義；軍事上意義重大；政治上意義重大 / This development proved highly significant for the whole town. 這一，開發對整個城鎮來說意義重大。/ rituals which are deeply significant to Christians 對基督徒具有深刻意義的儀式

<span class="vocabulary">**vital**</span> ['vaɪtl] 
<span class="definition">adj. vital (for/to sth) 對某事的成功或某事物的存在來說必需的、必不可少的、極其重要的：</span>the vitamins that are vital for health 保持健康必不可少的維生素 / Team spirit is vital for success. 團隊精神對成功至關重要。/ Reading is of vital importance in language learning. 閱讀在語言學習中至關重要。/ the strategically vital industrial zone 戰略上極其重要的工業區
           
<span class="vocabulary">**essential**</span> [ɪˈsenʃl]
<span class="definition">adj. ~ (to/for sth) / ~ (to do sth) | ~ (that…) 完全必要的、必不可少的、（在某種情況中或對某一活動）極其重要的：</span>essential part, essential ingredient, essential component 必不可少的一部分；必不可少的成分；必不可少的組成部分 / Money is not essential to happiness. 金錢對於幸福並非必不可少。/ Experience is essential for this job. 對於這個工作，經驗是非常重要的。/ essential services such as gas, water and electricity 諸如燃氣、水、電等必不可少的設施 / The museum is closed while essential repairs are being carried out. 博物館正在進行大修，在此期間暫停開放。/ It is essential to keep the two groups separate. 將這兩組隔開是完全必要的。/ It is essential that you have some experience. 你必須得有些經驗。/ Even in small companies, computers are an essential tool. 即使在小公司裡，計算機也是必不可少的工具。/ It was absolutely essential to separate crops from the areas that animals used as pasture. 將莊稼和放牧區分開絕對必要。/ As they must also sprint over short distances, speed is essential. 由於他們也必須進行短跑，因此速度至關重要。/ It is becoming almost essential for students to have a second language. 學生掌握第二語言幾乎是必需的了。/ Increased competition makes it essential for the business to innovate. 日益激烈的競爭使企業創新變得極為重要。/ Do you consider these textbooks essential for the course? 你認為這個課程必須採用這些教科書嗎？/ A printer is clearly essential to such a task. 顯然，這一工作必須要有一臺印表機。/ Diet is crucial, exercise is equally essential. 飲食很關鍵，鍛鍊也同樣必不可少。/ Although useful, the accessories are by no means essential. 儘管這些配件很有用，但並非必不可少。/ the skills essential for success 成功的必備技能 / He believed that some form of religion was essential to human life. 他認為宗教信仰對於人生不可或缺。
              
<span class="vocabulary">**substantial**</span> [səbˈstænʃl]
<span class="definition">adj. 相當重要的：</span>a substantial change 重大變化 / That is a very substantial improvement in the present situation. 在當前形勢下這是一個非常巨大的進步。        
   
<span class="vocabulary">**prominent**</span> [ˈprɒmɪnənt; 美 ˈprɑ:m-] 
<span class="definition">adj.（某人）重要的：</span>He played a prominent part in the campaign. 他在這次運動中發揮了重要作用。/ a prominent member of the Law Society 法律協會的重要成員

<span class="vocabulary">**noteworthy**</span> [ˈnəʊtwɜ:ði; 美 ˈnoʊtwɜ:rði]

<span class="definition">prep.（重要性）低於：</span>瞭解即可
