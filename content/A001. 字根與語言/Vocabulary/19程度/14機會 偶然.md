☀ <span class="category">**機會的名詞：**</span>
<span class="vocabulary">**chance**</span> [tʃɑːns] 
<span class="definition">n. [C] chance (of sth) / chance (to do sth) / chance (for sb to do sth) 讓自己有機會去做某事的一個合適時間（或情況），可稱為機會、機遇、時機。是一個基礎和常規用詞，極為常用：</span>a chance of a holiday 度假機會 / Please give me a chance to explain. 請給我一個解釋的機會。/ to wait for a chance 等待機會 / This is the ideal chance for him to show his ability. 這是他展示自己才能的大好機會。/ to waste a golden chance 浪費一次良機

<span class="vocabulary">**opportunity**</span> [ˌɒpəˈtju:nəti; 美 ˌɑ:pərˈtu:-]
<span class="definition">n. [C, U] ~ (to do sth) / ~ (for sth/for doing sth) / ~ (of doing sth)（做某事或達成某事的）時機、機會：</span>You'll have the opportunity to ask any questions at the end. 你們最後將有機會提問任何問題。/ There was no opportunity for further discussion. 沒有機會進行深入討論了。/ At least give him the opportunity of explaining what happened. 至少要給他機會解釋一下發生了什麼事。/ Our company promotes equal opportunities for women (= women are given the same jobs, pay, etc. as men). 本公司提倡男女機會均等。/ I'd like to take this opportunity to thank my colleagues for their support. 我謹藉此機會感謝同事的支援。/ May I take this opportunity to congratulate Ruth on her promotion. 讓我藉此機會對魯斯的晉升表示祝賀。/ He is rude to me at every opportunity (= whenever possible). 他動不動就對我粗魯無禮。/ He lost no opportunity to vent his anger on those around him. 他動不動就拿周圍的人出氣。/ They intend to close the school at the earliest opportunity (= as soon as possible). 他們打算儘早關閉學校。/ a window of opportunity (= a period of time when the circumstances are right for doing sth) 行事的良機 / The ceasefire has created a window of opportunity to rescue the peace process. 停火為挽救和平程序敞開了一扇機會之門。/ I had an opportunity to go to New York and study. 我曾經有一個去紐約學習的機會。/ The best reason for a trip to London is the super opportunity for shopping. 絕佳的購物機會是前往倫敦的最好理由。/ We'll have plenty of opportunity to talk later. 我們以後會有很多機會聊天。/ a golden opportunity to invest in new markets 投資新市場的良機 / We need to give them a reasonable opportunity to look at the display. 我們需要給他們提供一個合適的機會來看展覽。/ Students should take every opportunity to widen their experience. 學生應利用一切機會拓展自己的經歷。/ career, educational, employment, job, training opportunity 職業機會；教育機會；就業機會；工作機會；培訓機會 / business, commercial, economic, investment, market, sales a missed sales opportunity 商機；經濟上的機會；投資機會；市場機會；銷售時機 / photo opportunity(= an opportunity to take photographs of famous or important people)（為名人安排的）接受拍照的時機 / We didn't get much opportunity to swim. 我們沒有太多的機會游泳。/ to provide better educational opportunities 提供更好的受教育機會 / He saw a great opportunity to make some money. 他發現了一個賺錢的良機。/ When the opportunity came, I seized it with both hands. 當機會來臨時，我用雙手抓住了它。/ The job will offer you excellent opportunities for promotion. 這份工作將給你極好的晉升機會。/ the opportunity of a lifetime 千載難逢的機會
           
<span class="vocabulary">**occasion**</span> [əˈkeɪʒn]
<span class="definition">n. [sing.] ~ (for sth/doing sth) 某事發生的適當時機：</span>It should have been an occasion for rejoicing, but she could not feel any real joy. 原本應該是高興的時刻，她卻絲毫未感到快樂。/ I'll speak to him about it if the occasion arises (= if I get a chance). 有機會的話，我要跟他談談這件事。/ He used the occasion to announce further tax cuts. 他利用這個機會宣佈再次減稅。/ It is an occasion for all the family to celebrate. 這是個值得舉家慶祝的時刻。/ It is always an important occasion for setting out government policy. 這歷來都是闡明政府政策的重要時機。/ I bought the camera last year, but never had the occasion to use it. 這臺相機是我去年買的，可是一直沒有機會使用。/ His death became an occasion for widespread discussion of his character. 他的去世成了廣泛討論他的為人的機會。/ Last year we had occasion to visit relatives in Florida. 去年我們得以拜訪佛羅里達州的親戚。/ These workshops provide an occasion for talking about art. 這些研討會提供了一個討論藝術的機會。/ I want to use this occasion to thank you all for your hard work. 我想利用這個機會感謝大家的辛勤工作。

<span class="vocabulary">**prospect**</span> [ˈprɒspekt; 美 ˈprɑ:s-]
<span class="definition">n. [pl.] prospects (for/of sth) 成功的機會：</span>promotion prospects（尤英）（美式通常用prospects for promotion）晉升機會 / election, electoral, re-election prospect 獲選的機會；重新當選的機會 / This position offers excellent promotion prospects. 這一職位有極好的晉升機會。/ Getting the right qualifications will enhance your employment prospects. 取得一些適當的資格證明將大大提高你的就業機會。/ What are the prospects of promotion in this job? 做這份工作有多少晉升的機會？
 
<span class="vocabulary">**break**</span> [breɪk] 
<span class="definition">n. [C] [非正式]（去做某事的）機會，機遇（尤指取得自己想要的…或實現成功的機會）：</span>I got my lucky break when I won the match. 我時來運轉，在比賽中取勝。/ to have a bad break 遭遇厄運

<span class="vocabulary">**possibility**</span> [͵pɒsə'bɪlətɪ] 
<span class="definition">n. [C, usually pl.] 機會，契機，相當於opportunity：</span>The course offers a wide range of possibilities for personal development. 該課程為個人發展提供多種機會。
           
<span class="vocabulary">**go**</span> [ɡəʊ] 
<span class="definition">n. [C] [英] [英，美也用turn]（一個人在如出牌或下棋的遊戲或活動中）輪到的機會：</span>Whose go is it? 輪到誰了？/ It’s your go. 輪到你啦。/ ‘How much is it to play?’ ‘It’s 50p a go.’ “玩這遊戲多少錢？”“一盤50便士。”

<span class="vocabulary">**hearing**</span> ['hɪərɪŋ] 
<span class="definition">n. [sing.]（解釋你的行動、行為、思想或意見的）一個機會：</span>At least give our ideas a fair hearing before you reject them. 至少應該聽一聽我們的想法，然後再拒絕。/ Their views deserve a hearing. 他們的觀點值得一聽。

<span class="vocabulary">**life**</span> [laɪf] 
<span class="definition">n. [C] 在兒童遊戲中，可指一個人出局前的幾次機會之一：</span>You’ve lost three lives, there’s only two left. 你已經失去了三次機會，還剩兩次機會。

<span class="vocabulary">**opening**</span> ['əʊpənɪŋ] 
<span class="definition">n. [C]（對某人來說）一次好機會，一次良機：</span>Winning the competition was the opening she needed for her career. 在這次比賽中獲勝是她未來職業發展的良好開端。/ too miss a good opening in the game 在比賽中錯過一個好機會 / to create an opening 創造機會 / a political opening 政治機遇

<span class="vocabulary">**outlet**</span> [ˈaʊtlet]
<span class="definition">n. [C] ~ (for sth)（感情、思想或精力的）表現機會：</span>She needed to find an outlet for her many talents and interests. 她的多才多藝、興趣廣泛需要找個施展的機會。/ Her father had found an outlet for his ambition in his work. 她父親在工作中找到了施展抱負的機會。

<span class="vocabulary">**platform**</span> ['plætfɔ:m] 
<span class="definition">n. [C]（供某人公開表達其意見或供其在某一領域獲得發展的）機會或地點：</span>Thank you to provide this platform for us to give our opinions. 感謝您提供這樣一個平臺，能夠讓我們發表各自的意見。/ He regarded the magazine as a platform to voice his opinions to the public. 他把這份雜誌看作是一個自己對公眾發表意見的陣地。
           
<span class="vocabulary">**arena**</span> [əˈri:nə]
<span class="definition">n. [C] [正式] 機會：</span>Oil speculation proved a natural arena for his skills. 在石油業中進行投機活動對他來說遊刃有餘。/ The conference should provide an arena for marketing our products. 那個大型會議可以成為我們推銷產品的一個機會。/ Some documents have now emerged into the public arena. 現在公眾已可以看到一些檔案。

<span class="vocabulary">**window**</span> ['wɪndəʊ] 
<span class="definition">n. [C] 一絲機會，常指短暫的時機：</span>to have a small window of opportunity 有一線機會

周邊詞彙補充：
· odds可指某事發生的可能性、機率；
· time可指時機；
· access可指使用某事物的機會或權利。也可指見到（或接觸到）某人或某事物的機會或權利。還可指使用某種資訊科技的機會或權利；
· place可指加入（或參加）…的機會，尤指進入學校（如大學）的求學機會，或參加某門課程的學習的機會。也可指（在一種情況或活動中去做某事的）一個好時機或階段；
· turn可指（在一群人中依次輪到的）機會；
· use可指對某事物（如屬於別人的事物）的使用權或使用機會；
· view可指觀看（或瞻仰）…的一次特別的機會，如一睹；
· open可指（事實或問題）有討論（或解釋）餘地的。

☀ <span class="category">**抓住機會的動詞：**</span>
<span class="vocabulary">**grab**</span> [græb]
<span class="definition">vt.&vi. ~ (at sth) 抓住機會：</span>This was my big chance and I grabbed it with both hands. 這是我的大好機會，我緊緊抓住。/ She grabbed the chance of a job interview. 她抓住了一次求職面試的機會。/ He grabbed at the opportunity to buy his castle. 他抓住機會買下了他的城堡。/ He'll grab at any excuse to avoid doing the dishes. 他會隨便找個藉口來逃避洗碗。

<span class="vocabulary">**grasp**</span> [ɡrɑːsp] 
<span class="definition">vt. grasp a chance/an opportunity 毫不猶豫地抓住機會（並利用之）：</span>to grasp the opportunity 抓住機會

<span class="vocabulary">**seize**</span> [si:z] 
<span class="definition">vt. seize a chance, seize an opportunity 表示抓住機會，相當於grab。是一個基礎和常規用詞：</span>He had failed to seize his chance. 他沒能把握住時機。
           
<span class="vocabulary">**snatch**</span> [snætʃ]
<span class="definition">vt.（因沒有太多時間而）抓緊時間做（如吃東西、休息等），抓住機會：</span>I snatched a glance at the mirror. 我乘機瞥了一眼鏡子。/ You can even snatch a few hours off. 你甚至可以抓緊時間休息幾個小時。/ I managed to snatch an hour's sleep. 我偷空兒睡了一小時的覺。
           
<span class="vocabulary">**leap**</span> [li:p]
<span class="definition">vi. 趕緊抓住（機會）：</span>The post of principal of the theatre school became vacant and he leapt at the chance. 戲劇學校校長一職出缺，他馬上抓住了這個機會。

周邊詞彙補充：
· get可指有機會去做某事，或有（做…的時間或機會）；
· speculate可指商業投機（即透過倒賣貨物、不動產、股票等來獲利，同時面臨著損失的風險）。

· 表示“浪費機會”的詞群見[[03奢侈 浪費]]

☀ <span class="category">**偶然、意外、巧合的名詞：**</span>
<span class="vocabulary">**accident**</span> ['æksɪdənt] 
<span class="definition">n. [C, U] 意外且未經提前計劃而發生的事，即偶然的事，意外：</span>It was just an accident. 那僅僅是個意外。/ an accident of change 變化的偶然性 / an accident of fate 命運的偶然性 / happy accident 意外的驚喜 / historical accident 歷史的偶然 / We met by accident at the airport. 我們在機場偶然相遇。
           
<span class="vocabulary">**revelation**</span> [ˌrevəˈleɪʃn]
<span class="definition">n. [U, C] come as/be a revelation (to sb) 出乎意料：</span>The noise, the buildings, the people, came as a revelation. 這裡的噪音、建築物和人都令人出乎意料。/ The demonstration proved something of a revelation for our teachers. 這次示威從某種程度上說出乎我們老師的意料。

<span class="vocabulary">**chance**</span> [tʃɑːns] 
<span class="definition">n. [U] 某事的發生沒有任何（你所看到的或理解的）原因，可稱為偶然、碰巧、意外：</span>I met her by chance. 我碰巧遇見她。/ It was pure chance that we were both there. 我們倆當時都在場純屬巧合。
           
<span class="vocabulary">**contingency**</span> [kənˈtɪndʒənsi]
<span class="definition">n. [U, C] 未來可能發生的事，如不測事件、偶發事件：</span>We must consider all possible contingencies. 我們必須考慮一切可能發生的事。/ to make contingency plans (= plans for what to do if a particular event happens or does not happen) 擬訂應急方案 / contingency reserve, contingency fund (= to pay for sth that might happen in the future) 意外開支準備金

<span class="vocabulary">**coincidence**</span> [kəʊ'ɪnsɪdəns] 
<span class="definition">n. [C, U]（兩件或多件相似或相關的事情）偶然地同時發生，即巧合：</span>What a coincidence! I wasn’t expecting to see you here. 真巧！我沒料到會在這裡見到你。/ a series of strange coincidences 一連串奇怪的巧合 / By coincidence, we both went to the same school. 碰巧我們倆上的是同一所學校。

☀ <span class="category">**偶然、意外、巧合的其它詞性：**</span>
<span class="vocabulary">**happen**</span> ['hæpən] 
<span class="definition">vt.＆vi. 恰巧或碰巧（做某事或是…）：</span>She happened to be out when we called. 我們打電話時她剛巧不在家。/ We happened to discover we had a friend in common. 我們碰巧發現我們有個共同的朋友。<span class="definition">vi. 用於告訴某人某事，尤指當你不同意他們或對其所說的表示惱怒的時候：</span>That happens to be my mother you’re talking about! 你們談論的是我母親！

<span class="vocabulary">**chance**</span> [tʃɑːns] 
<span class="definition">vi. [正式] 偶然（或碰巧）發生（或去做某事）：</span>They chanced to be staying at the same hotel. 他們碰巧住在同一家旅館。<span class="definition">adj. 只用於名詞前，非被計劃的，即意外的、偶然的、碰巧的，相當於unplanned：</span>chance meeting, chance encounter 邂逅

<span class="vocabulary">**catch**</span> [kætʃ] 
<span class="definition">vt. 突然意外地發生（而將某人置於困境）：</span>I got caught in a thunderstorm. 我遇上了雷雨。/ His arrival caught me by surprise. 他的到來讓我感到意外。

<span class="vocabulary">**accidental**</span> [ˌæksɪˈdentl]
<span class="definition">adj. 意外（或偶然）發生的、非計劃的：</span>The jury returned a verdict of accidental death. 陪審團作出意外死亡的正式裁決。/ His hand brushed against hers; it could have been either accidental or deliberate. 就在有意無意間，他的手輕輕拂過她的手。/ I didn't think our meeting was accidental--he must have known I would be there. 我認為我們相遇不是偶然的——他肯定知道我要去那裡。
           
<span class="vocabulary">**coincidental**</span> [kəʊˌɪnsɪˈdentl; 美 koʊ-]
<span class="definition">adj. [名詞前不常用] 碰巧發生的、非計劃之中的：</span>I suppose your presence here today is not entirely coincidental. 我認為你今天來這裡不完全是碰巧。/ Any resemblance to actual persons, places or events is purely coincidental. 如與真人、真實地點或真實事件有雷同之處，純屬巧合。/ I think that it is not coincidental that we now have arguably the best bookshops in the world. 我認為我們現在擁有了可以說是世界上最好的書店，這並非巧合。/ It's purely coincidental that we both chose to call our daughters Emma. 我們倆都給自己的女兒取名叫埃瑪，這完全是巧合。/ These parallels cannot be merely coincidental. 這些相似之處不可能只是巧合。/ The timing is hardly coincidental. 選擇這個時間很難說是巧合。

<span class="vocabulary">**incidental**</span> [ˌɪnsɪˈdentl]
<span class="definition">adj.（某事）非有意為之的：</span>Any resemblance of a character in this book to a living person is purely incidental. 書中人物若與現實人物雷同，純屬巧合。

<span class="vocabulary">**casual**</span> ['kæӡuəl] 
<span class="definition">adj. 只作前置定語，偶然發生的，碰巧做某事的，或未經計劃的：</span>casual encounter, casual meeting 不期而遇；邂逅 / a casual passer-by 碰巧過路的人 / The disease is not spread by casual contact. 此病不會透過偶然接觸傳染。/ a casual remark 隨意說的話
           
<span class="vocabulary">**inadvertent**</span> [ˌɪnədˈvɜ:tənt; 美 -ˈvɜ:rt-]
<span class="definition">adj. 偶然的、無意的、不經意的：</span>an inadvertent omission 不經意的疏忽 / The government has said it was an inadvertent error. 政府聲稱那是因疏忽造成的過失。           

<span class="vocabulary">**unexpected**</span> [ˌʌnɪkˈspektɪd]
<span class="definition">adj. 出乎意料的、意外的：</span>an unexpected result 意想不到的結果 / an unexpected visitor 不速之客 / The announcement was not entirely unexpected. 這個通告並非完全出乎意料。/ His death was totally unexpected. 他的死亡完全出乎意料。/ He made a brief, unexpected appearance at the office. 他出乎意料地在辦公室露了一下臉。/ It happened in rather unexpected circumstances. 這件事的發生相當意外。
           
<span class="vocabulary">**snap**</span> [snæp]
<span class="definition">exclamation. [英，非正式]（對於兩件相同的事情或事物表示驚訝）真巧：</span>Snap! I've just bought that CD too! 真是巧了！我也剛買了那張光碟！

相關詞義延伸：
· happen還可指偶然發生。

周邊詞彙補充：
· hit可指偶然的發現，或使突然想起（或突然意識到）；
· spectacle可指奇特的現象，意外的情況。

· 表示“偶爾”、“有時”的詞群見[[03偶爾 有時]]
