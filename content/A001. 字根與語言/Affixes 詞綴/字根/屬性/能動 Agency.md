# Agency 能動性
- Snow and Benford (1992, p. 136), for instance, understand agents as being creative, and thus they ‘do not view social movements merely as carriers of extant ideas and meanings that stand in isomorphic relationship to structural arrangements or unanticipated events’, but rather they ‘see movement organizations and actors as actively engaged in the production and maintenance of meaning’
