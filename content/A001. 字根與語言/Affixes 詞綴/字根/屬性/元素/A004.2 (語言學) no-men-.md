## **Linguistics 語言學**
詞彙(lexicon)是構成語言結構不可或缺的要素，詞庫裡詞與詞之間的聯繫並不是鬆散、漫無章法，而是相互連結成一個有結構性、組織性以及系統性的詞彙網絡(Aitchison 1994, 2003; Miller 1996; Fellbaum 1998; Cruse 2000; Schmitt 2000;Givón 2001: 175-201)。在這個網絡中，詞與詞之間透過各種關係相互連結，這些關係包含了同義關係(synonymy)、反義關係(antonymy)、部分與整體關係(meronymy)、上下位關係(hyponymy)、及搭配共現關係(collocation)等等。

#### **subtype-supertype**
Ref: 上位詞與下位詞的篇章功能 Hypernyms and Hyponyms in Discourse
- **hypernym, superordinate 上位詞**
	- 上位詞 (hypernym 或superordinate)與下位詞 (hyponym 或subordinate)在篇章回指的語境中呈現兩種分佈型態，分別是「下位詞…上位詞」(如大冠鷲…這隻雙翼超過四尺長的大鳥)和「上位詞…下位詞」(如植物…塔花、風輪花、林投、榕樹)。
	- Avians (鳥類，hypernym) $\subset$ eagles, parrots, swan, penguin... (hyponym)
	- **umbrella term, blanket term 總稱; 傘式術語**
		- 和 hypernym 相近, 表示該用詞為概括數個術語而集成的用詞
		- 如密碼學（cryptology）為一總稱，同時包括加密技術（cryptography）和密碼分析（cryptanalysis）與其他領域的技術。又如微軟的COM技術，也是一個總稱，包含OLE、OLE自動化、ActiveX、COM+、DCOM等多項技術。
- **hyponym, subordinate 下位詞**
	- 上下位關係是指兩個詞在意義上具有種類包含的關係 (class inclusion)，例如「動物」所涵蓋的種類包含了「鳥類」，因此這兩個詞在意義上便構成了上下位關係。在上下位關係中，我們稱其中意義比較籠統，但可以包含的種類較多、較廣且概括性較強的詞為上位詞 (hypernym 或superordinate)，而另一個概括性較弱，包含的種類較少，但意義相對比較精確的詞為下位詞 (hyponym 或 subordinate)
	- **cohyponym, coordinate terms 共下位詞**
		- If the hypernym Z consists of hyponyms X and Y, then X and Y are identified as co-hyponyms (cohyponyms), also known as coordinate terms. Co-hyponyms are labelled as such when separate hyponyms share the same hypernym but are not hyponyms of one another, unless they happen to be synonymous.
		- Avians (鳥類，hypernym) $\subset$ eagles, parrots, swan, penguin... (hyponym), and hyponym-hyponym $\in$ cohyponym
	- **autohyponym 自下位詞; vertical polysemy 垂直多義性; licensed polysemy 特許多義性**
		- The term "autohyponym" was coined by linguist Laurence R. Horn in a 1984 paper, Ambiguity, negation, and the London School of Parsimony.
		- A word is an autohyponym if it is used for both a hypernym and its hyponym
		- 當一個hypernym其下的一個hyponym同形，則該hyponym為autohyponym
			- 以性別區分dog，分為公狗 (dog) 和母狗 (bitch)，而公狗即無性別區分的狗的autohyponym

#### LINGUISTIC LANDSCAPE
- **logonym 語言景觀主體詞**
	- 在語言景觀的背景下，logonym 的研究範圍相當廣泛：它不僅關注動機、結構、詞形成或語義價值，還涉及到標誌的視覺構成及其多層次的視覺方面，這些方面會影響觀察者。許多 logonyms 及其視覺形式受到刻板印象的顯著影響。刻板印象在現實社會的重建中扮演著角色——它是基於對共性元素的概括而對一組對象進行簡化的表現形式。這種方式創造的符號目的是引發與某個產品、公司或服務相關的聯想，這些聯想旨在激發觀察者的正面反應。
		- Logonym research in the context of the linguistic landscape is rather broad: it not only focuses on the motivation, structure, word formation, or semantic value but also visual composition and multiple planes found in the visual aspects of signs carrying the connotations, which affect the percipient. Many logonyms and their visual forms are significantly influenced by stereotypisation. Stereotypes play a role in the way reality is socially reconstructed  — it is a simplified representation of a group of objects based on generalisation of the dominant (common) elements share by them. The goal of semiotic signs created in this way is to elicit connotations related to a certain product, company, or service, which are supposed to trigger a positive response in the percipient.
	- **linguistic landscape (LL) 語言景觀**
		- 公共路牌、區域、廣告看板上的語言應用
		- 分為:
			- informative function 信息功能 (information providability)
			- symbolic function 象徵功能 (reflectability of the power or position of specific group)
		- 應用: 有指出地名 (toponym) 的地標、路牌、商標、街頭塗鴉 (graffiti)、刺青
		- addtion: multilingualism 多語現象
		- e.g., 一塊寫著"嘉義火雞肉飯 Chiayi Turkey Rice"的招牌
			- LL $\in$ context, the overall isovist $\rightarrow$ symbolic construction
			- 語言標牌 linguistic sign $\in$ 招牌本身
			- logonym $\in$ "嘉義火雞肉飯"
			- toponym $\in$ 嘉義，Chiayi
			- multilingualism $\in$ "嘉義火雞肉飯 Chiayi Turkey Rice" (CH + EN)
			- agency 能動性 $\in$ determine the sign itself to be official or private
			- $\in$ geosemiotics (2003) 地理符號學
				- discourse in place (Scollon, R. & Scoolon, S.)
					- code preference 語碼取向 (text intentionality)(why)
					- inscription 字刻 (text formation)(how/encoding signified)
					- emplacement 置放 (text occasion)(where/when)

#### **rhetoric devices containing no-men**
- **paronomasia, agnomination, pun 雙關**
	- or annomination, whereas it's an obsoletism
	- a form of word play that exploits multiple meanings of a term, or of similar-sounding words, for an intended humorous or rhetorical effect. These ambiguities can arise from the intentional use of:
		- **homophonic 同音雙關**
			- Puns, Blackwell, London, 1984: "To pun is to treat homonyms as synonyms."
			1. in George Carlin's phrase "atheism is a non-prophet institution", the word prophet is put in place of its homophone profit, altering the common phrase "non-profit institution".
			2. "Question: Why do we still have troops in Germany? Answer: To keep the Russians in Czech" relies on the aural ambiguity of the homophones check and Czech.
		- **homographic 同形雙關**
			- A homographic pun exploits words that are spelled the same (homographs) but possess different meanings and sounds. Because of their origin, they rely on sight more than hearing, contrary to homophonic puns. They are also known as heteronymic puns.
			- Homographic and homophonic: Douglas Adams's line "You can tune a guitar, but you can't tuna fish. Unless of course, you play bass." The phrase uses the homophonic qualities of tune a and tuna, as well as the homographic pun on bass, in which ambiguity is reached through the identical spellings of /beɪs/ (a string instrument), and /bæs/ (a kind of fish).
		- **metonymic 轉喻雙關**
			- Metonymic puns exploit the metonymic relationship between words - where a word or phrase is used to represent something it's closely associated with. In such puns, one term is substituted for another term with which it's closely linked by a concept or idea. The humor or wit of the pun often comes from the unexpected yet apt connection made between the two concepts.
			-  "The White House loses its balance." In this case, "The White House" is used metonymically to represent the U.S. government, and "balance" could be interpreted both as physical stability (as if the building itself is tipping over) or fiscal balance (as in the budget), thereby creating a pun.
		- figurative language
			- **sylleptic/heteronymic
				- Syllepsis, or heteronymy, is a form of punning where a single word simultaneously affects the rest of the sentence, while it changes the meaning of the idiom it is used in. This form of punning uses the word in its literal and metaphorical senses at once, creating a surprising and often humorous effect.
				- "She lowered her standards by raising her glass, her courage, her eyes and his hopes." In this case, "raising" applies in different ways to each of the items listed, creating a series of linked puns. This type of punning can often be seen in literature, particularly in works that play extensively with language. (She razed his self-esteem in how she raised the children.)
			- **paronomasia**
				- Paronomasia is the formal term for punning, playing with words to create humorous or rhetorical effect. Paronomastic puns often manipulate well-known idioms, proverbs, or phrases to deliver a punned twist. The classic structure of a joke, with a setup leading to a punchline, is a common format for paronomastic puns, where the punchline alters the expected phrase in a way that plays on multiple meanings of a word.
				- frequently used in advertisements, comedy, and literature to provide a clever and memorable message.
				- "I used to be a baker, but I couldn't make enough dough,"
					- the word "dough" is used paronomastically to refer both
						- to the substance used to make bread and 
						- to slang for money.
			- **antanaclasis**
				- Antanaclasis is a type of pun where a single word or phrase is repeated, but the meaning changes each time. The humor or wit derives from the surprising shift in meaning of a familiar word or phrase. This form of punning often relies on homophones, homonyms, or simply the contextual flexibility of a word or phrase.
				- Benjamin Franklin's statement, "We must, indeed, all hang together or, most assuredly, we shall all hang separately." In this quote, the word "hang" is first used to mean "stay" or "work together," but then, it is repeated with the meaning "be executed."

#### **other rhetoric device**
- **syllogism 直言三段論**
	- major premise 大前提: All men are mortal.
	- minor premise 小前提: Socrates is a man.
	- conclusion 結論: Therefore, Socrates is mortal.
	- Aristotle's Prior Analytics did not, however, incorporate such a comprehensive theory on the modal syllogism—**a syllogism that has at least one modalized premise (情態化前提)**, that is, a premise containing the modal words **necessarily (必然)**, **possibly (或然)**, or **contingently (偶然)**.
	- **hypothetical syllogism 假言三段論**: If A, then B. If B, then C. Therefore, if A, then C.
		- 若我愛他，則我希望長命百歲，與他長廂斯守。若我希望長命百歲，與他長廂斯守，則我應多吃蔬菜。所以，若我愛他，我應多吃蔬菜。
	- **disjunctive syllogism 選言三段論**: A is B, A is also C. If not C, then B.
		- 愛因斯坦是物理學家，又或愛因斯坦是廚師。愛因斯坦不是廚師。所以，愛因斯坦是物理學家。
	- **enthymeme 省略三段論**
		- 三段論法可以藉由消除任何一個前提而縮短，因此出現了稱為「省略式三段論法」（enthymeme）的論證。在典型的省略式三段論法中，其中一個前提是假設其為相關領域背景知識的一部分。
		- 省略大前提 (with an unstated major premise): (凡真理都是不怕批评的，)马克思主义是不怕批评的，因为马克思主义是真理。
		- 省略小前提 (with an unstated minor premise): 所有奇数都是自然数，(3是奇数，)所以3是自然数。
		- 省略結論 (with an unstated conclusion): 逻辑规律是科学规律，而一切科学规律都是不能违反的 (，逻辑规律是不能违反的。)
		- 省略三段论也有前提(被省略的前提)是否真实、推理形式是否正确的问题。比如，在“金星是有卫星的，因为它是行星”这一省略三段论中，省去的大前提“凡行星都是有卫星的”，就是一个虚假的命题。
	- **modus ponens 離斷律**: if P, then Q; now P, so Q
		- 若他愛我，他會送花給我。他愛我。所以，他會送花給我。
	- **modus tollens 否定後件律**: if P, then Q; if not Q, then not P
		- 若我愛他，當我見到他的時候我的心跳會加速。當我見到他的時候我的心跳並沒有加速。所以，我不愛他。
		- 假使從理論P可得出某預測Q，但實驗卻証明Q是錯的，我們便可引用否定後件律，推翻理論P。同樣，著名的歸謬法 (reductio ad absurdum)，亦其實是引用否定後件律。應用歸謬法，我們首先假設某判斷P是正確的。接著，我們証明從P可以得出一個錯的結論Q。因此我們可以推斷P是錯的。否定後件律是一個對確的論證形式。否定前項（denying the antecedent）的形式十分相似，但卻並不對確
	- **polysyllogism 複合三段論**: a complex argument
		- **prosyllogism 前置三段論**: 複合三段論裡除了最後一個三段論以外的所有三段論 (因為最後一個三段論不作為其他三段論的前提)
			- Each constituent syllogism is called a prosyllogism except the last, because the conclusion of the last syllogism is not a premise for another syllogism.
		- It is raining. (premise 1) If we go out while it is raining, we will get wet. (consequence 1, premise 1) If we get wet, we will get cold. (consequence 2, premise 2) Therefore, if we go out we will get cold. (conclusion)
		- **sorites 連鎖三段論**: A=B，B=C，C=D，therefore, D=A
			-  a specific kind of polysyllogism in which the predicate of each proposition is the subject of the next premise.
		- **epicheirema, epicherem (Cicero) 帶證三段論**
			- 每個前提都必須有根據
			- A syllogism in which the proof of the major or minor premise, or both, is introduced with the premises themselves, and the conclusion is derived in the ordinary manner.
		- **dilemma 兩難**
			- 你可以接受現實，亦可不接受。若你接受現實，請你不要再抱怨。若你不接受，請你積極改變現況。所以，請你不要再抱怨，又或積極改變現況。
- **simile 明喻**
	- tenor 喻體
	- vehicle 喻依
	- indicator of resemblance, simile marker, connective 喻詞
- **kenning 日耳曼詩歌中的複合隱喻語**
	- https://vocus.cc/article/6401af33fd89780001f12736
	- 一種以迂言法呈現的比喻，這種比喻常牽涉利用比喻的合成詞取代語意更為固定的單一詞。這種隱喻語常與古北歐詩歌和古英語詩歌相關；而這種隱喻語，及與之高度相關的詩歌代稱詞 (heiti)，在之後的數世紀依舊是包括冰島史詩民謠（Rímur）在內的冰島詩歌的特色之一。
- **difrasismo 中美原住民雌歌中的複合隱喻語**
	- a term derived from Spanish that is used in the study of certain Mesoamerican languages, to describe a particular grammatical construction in which two separate words are paired together to form a single metaphoric unit.
- **anacoluthon 斷句；破格文體；句法融合**
	- 同一句子中結構的前後不一致或不連貫
	- 有時被認為是一種文體上的缺陷（一種意識流），有時被認為是故意的修辭效果
	- 破格文體(anacoluthon) 的常見用法是模仿思想或言語，並將必要的資訊移到句子的開頭。它經常用於文學作品和休閒演講。在隨意對話中，它的使用方式使該句子在語法上不會被認為是正確的。但是，在書面作品中，它被用來模仿不合語法、混亂和非正式的演講，並引起讀者的注意。 
	- Anacoluthon 在詩歌、戲劇和戲劇獨白中被廣泛使用。另外，由於思想並不總是連貫有邏輯，並且在語法上幾乎是正確的，因此該修辭技巧非常適合計劃用來表示彼此接近的思想的意識流寫作風格。
	- 羅伯特·M·福勒（Robert M. Fowler）指出
		- 「口語很容易原諒，甚至更喜歡anacoluthon」（_讓讀者理解_, 1996)。
	- (Arthur Quinn and Lyon Rathbun in the Encyclopedia of Rhetoric and Composition, ed. by Theresa Enos. Routledge, 2013)
		- 「Anacoluthon 是指說話者以暗示某種邏輯開始，卻以不同的方式結束句子，Anacoluthon 在口語中很常見。」
	- 範例
		1. I can't believe Christmas is-I just haven't finished my shopping yet! I agree that children should-Wait! Did you just see that car run the red light?
			- 我簡直不敢相信聖誕節是...我還沒完成購物！我同意孩子應該... 等等！您剛剛看到那輛汽車闖紅燈了嗎？ 
		2. "I will have such revenges on you both/That all the world shall―I will do such things/What they are, yet I know not." (William Shakespeare, King Lear) 
			- 「我將對你們倆進行報復，全世界將會……我會做這樣的事情，他們是什麼，但我不知道。 」
