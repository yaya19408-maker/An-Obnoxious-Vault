 語言學家根據已有的lexeme、morpheme、phoneme等，又創造了許多新術語，比如grapheme（字素）、tagmeme（序位）、kineme（身勢語素）、kinemorpheme（體語詞素）等，我們在翻譯這類術語時，就要譯出其中所含的後綴eme（素、位）。
- ##### Emic ($\in$ ENDO-) 局內人
	- Derived from phonemics (音位學: 語音比較學)
	- Definition $\rightarrow$ 站在研究對象視角看他物
- ##### Etic ($\in$ EXO-) 局外人
	- Derived from phonetics (語音學: 語音分析)
	- Definition $\rightarrow$ 以外在觀點分析

- A lexical quantor, as any language unit, is considered to be a linguistic sign. The sign nature of a lexical quantor is much stipulated by the basic characteristics of a linguistic sign which have been outlined in linguosemiotics. 
- **The functions of language** (Towards More Exhaustive Descriptions of Languages)
	- **α-function**: function of expressing the content and the units of contents themselves
		- phonemes, prosodemes, syllabemes, morphemes, lexemes, syntagmemes, and sememes would be important α-units
		- The α-units belonging to some given individual can be prefixed idio-: idiophoneme, idioprosodeme, idiosyllabeme, idiomorpheme, idiolexeme, idiosyntagmeme, etc. An individual may have the same number of units of some kind as other individuals of a certain sociolect. This would, in general, be true of phonemes but not of lexemes (as some have a wider vocabulary than others) or of syntagmemes.
	- **β-function**: function about how something is said
		- 'variation', 'varieties', or 'variants' are usually employed for different ways of saying something.
		- register: has become familiar in English linguistic usage for reference to specific situations; varieties, or 'functional varieties', has been used in American linguistics in relation to broad domains
			- **β $_{1}$ -function**: attitudes (abrupt, affable, affected, affectionate, aggressive, agreeable, airy, angry, amused)
				- **β-units** at the phonetic level: contouremes
					- The Swedish word fy can be pronounced with different variants (contours) within the contoureme 'disgust' (1966: 9). (The non-discrete contouremes must be carefully distinguished from the discrete prosodemes. Prosodemes distinguish, for instance, the verb import from the noun import.)
				- **β-properties** at morphology, syntax, and semantics levels: stylistic
			- **β $_{2}$ -function**: registers determined by speech in words' widest sense 
				- Formal, reverential, informal, casual, etc., would be adjectives used to distinguish different registers.
				- Each register, or register property, is used under given circumstances and the circumstances are used for purposes of definition: such and such a register, or register feature, is the one which is used under such and such circumstances.
		- At the β-level, the same individual would usually not have all possible attitudes and registers. Nor would each individual be capable of expressing in every detail every possible attitude or have registers including every possible detail.
	- **γ-function**: function of being communicated to the addressee in speech acts
		- **γ $_{1}$ -function** (idiolectal): The language system of a given individual (together with the speech acts produced by him in accordance with his system)4 is this individual's idiolect. The specific properties (whether they are specific variants within phonetics, morphology, syntax or semantics or /β-facts) which distinguish one idiolect from another may be called γ $_{1}$ -properties.
		- **γ $_{2}$ -function** (sociolectal): Language properties which characterize groups of individuals within a society have γ $_{2}$ -function. A group of idiolects characterized by given γ $_{2}$ -properties can be called a sociolect. In a somewhat loose way, 'γ $_{2}$ ' and 'sociolectal' may be given the same meaning. However, a sociolect contains properties which have no γ $_{2}$ -function but are just 'normal' properties of the language.
		- **γ $_{3}$ -function** (dialectal): In a somewhat loose way 'γ $_{3}$ ' and 'dialectal' can be given the same meaning. However, a dialect contains properties which have no γ $_{3}$ -function but are just 'normal' properties of a language. A dialect is a set of idiolects or sociolects. (A sociolect is a set of idiolects.) 
			- As is well known, all speakers in a given dialect area do not speak the dialect one might expect. Some may have moved in from some other area while some may speak a standard variant which is only vaguely conditioned by geographical factors.
			- In the taxonomy presented by Chiu (1973: 53), 'Language' appears at the highest level. Language is then subdivided at the next level into temporal, geographical, social, and individual dialects.
	- **glottolect 口頭方言:** A glottolect has an inventory of lexemes (words) which is larger than the inventory of any simple individual, and individuals differ very much as to the size of their 'vocabulary'. An individual may be characterized by using a wide and varied vocabulary or by the contrary. This seems to be the case: α→γ $_{1}$.
		- In most cases it would be true to say that a glottolect is a set of dialects, that dialects are sets of sociolects and that sociolects are sets of idiolects. (This implies that not only a sociolect but also a glottolect or a dialect is a set of idiolects.)
	- **b-linguistics:** (broad) everything is broadly defined in the language 
		- most trends in linguistics, such as traditional, neogrammarian, structuralist, and generativist linguistics, have been b-α-linguistics.
	- **n-linguistics:** (narrow) to describe as many details as possible
		- A linguistics attempting to account for everything communicated in speech acts6 would have to be an n-α, β, γ-linguistics.
- **theme 主題；主位：** 説話者的出發點和句子的主題，what to say about (new, backgrounded)
	- 布拉格學派提出句子的 Theme-Rheme 分析法，其中Theme 涵蓋着兩個概念：説話者的出發點和句子的主題。Rheme 是 Theme 以外的部分。布拉格學派學者研究的語言（捷克語、俄羅斯語、英語）正好一般都把主題放在説話者的出發點位置（即句首），形成“主題-述題”結構，因此他們就以為説話者的出發點和句子的主題是分不開的。但是並不是每個語言都把主題放在説話者的出發點位置，比如，菲律賓語的主題一般出現在句末，形成“述題-主題”結構。雖然如此，説話者的出發點還是很重要的，因為説話者一開始説話，對方就開始推測説話者的整個意思和方向。這種推測在互動語言學理論裏叫做“projection”（預測）。不同語言用不同的方法去幫助聽話者預測説話者所要傳達的意思，菲律賓語也不例外。為了探討這些用來幫助聽話者預測説話者所要傳達的意思的方法，在分析語言結構的時候，最好把 Theme-Rheme 和 Topic-Comment 視為不同的功能結構。
- **rheme 述題；述位：** theme 以外的部分，what to say (given, foregrounded)

		- Dividing his book into three main sections, Albright examines the ways in which his three chosen poets drew upon science, particularly the new insights of quantum physics, to seek to understand how poetry works, whether as part of the whole culture and discourse from which it derives (the pretext), as the finished or published work (the text) or as the responses to it (the post-text). - **EE046. Gusteme (food studies)**
	- ![[EE000. 主位與客位-20240826162335318.webp]]
