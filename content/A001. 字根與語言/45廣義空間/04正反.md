☀ <span class="category">**反、抵消的動詞、副詞和形容詞：**</span>
<span class="vocabulary">**offset**</span> [ˈɒfset; 美 ˈɔ:f-; ˈɑ:f-]
<span class="definition">vt. ~ sth (against sth) 抵消、彌補、補償。可以是經濟上抵消，也可以是其它方面抵消：</span>Prices have risen in order to offset the increased cost of materials. 為補償原料成本的增加而提高了價格。/ The increase in pay costs was more than offset by higher productivity. 提高的生產率抵消了增加的工資成本後仍有結餘。/ The company's losses in the USA were more than offset by gains everywhere else. 該公司在其他地方的利潤不足以彌補其在美國的損失。/ Your donations to charity can be offset against tax.（英）你對慈善事業的捐贈可獲得稅項減免。/ The money will help offset big medical bills. 這筆錢有助於償付鉅額的醫藥費。/ The carbon emissions are largely offset by planting trees. 植樹可以在很大程度上抵消碳排放。/ The move is designed to help offset the shortfall in world oil supplies caused by the UN embargo. 此舉旨在緩解聯合國禁運所造成的世界石油供應短缺的局面。

<span class="vocabulary">**compensate**</span> ['kɒmpenseɪt] 
<span class="definition">vi. compensate (for sth) 彌補，抵消壞事造成的影響，相當於make up for：</span>Nothing can compensate for the loss of a loved one. 失去心愛的人是無法補償的。/ The advantages of the plan more than compensate for the risks associated with it. 這項計劃的優點大大地彌補了其所帶來的風險。/ His strengths more than compensate for his weaknesses. 他的強項大大彌補了他的弱點。/ His voice doesn’t have much range but he compensates with clever lyrics. 他嗓音的音域不是很寬，但他用巧妙的歌詞做了彌補。

<span class="vocabulary">**balance**</span> ['bæləns] 
<span class="definition">vt.＆vi. balance (sth) (out)（在價值、數量等方面）抵消，相抵，相當於offset：</span>This year’s profits will balance our previous losses. 本年度的利潤將可彌補我們之前的虧損。/ He balanced his budgets by rigid control over public expenditure. 他透過嚴格控制公共開支來平衡預算。/ His lack of experience was balanced by a willingness to learn. 他的好學彌補了他經驗的不足。/ The good and bad effects of any decision will usually balance out. 任何決策的效果往往利弊互見。

<span class="vocabulary">**counter**</span> ['kaʊntə] 
<span class="definition">vt. 抵消壞事的影響，與counteract相同：</span>Businesses would like to see new laws to counter late payments of debts. 商界希望見到對付債務逾期不還的新法律出臺。

<span class="vocabulary">**counteract**</span> [ˌkaʊntərˈækt]
<span class="definition">vt. 抵消或減少某事（常是壞事）的影響：</span>These exercises aim to counteract the effects of stress and tension. 這些訓練旨在抵消壓力與緊張的影響。/ My husband has to take several pills to counteract high blood pressure. 我丈夫不得不吃幾片藥來降壓。/ This event will counteract such trends. 這一事件將會削弱此類趨勢。
           
<span class="vocabulary">**temper**</span> [ˈtempə(r)]
<span class="definition">vt. [正式] ~ sth (with sth)（透過新增帶有相反效果的事物來）緩和…：</span>Justice must be tempered with mercy. 法外尚需施恩。/ For others, especially the young and foolish, the state will temper justice with mercy. 對待其他人時，尤其是年輕人和愚蠢的人，國家會剛柔並濟。/ He had to learn to temper his enthusiasm. 他得學著給自己的熱情降溫。
           
<span class="vocabulary">**resistant**</span> [rɪˈzɪstənt]
<span class="definition">adj. 1 ~ (to sth) 對…有抵抗力的：</span>plants that are resistant to disease 抗病植株 / The body may be less resistant if it is cold. 天冷時，身體的抵抗力會下降。/ a metal that is highly resistant to corrosion 極耐腐蝕的金屬 <span class="definition">2 -resistant 用於構成形容詞，指抗…的、耐…的：</span>disease-resistant plants 抗病植株 / fire-resistant materials 耐火材料

<span class="vocabulary">**back**</span> [bæk] 
<span class="definition">vt. [英] 常用被動式，指位於某物的後面，尤指建築物背朝…或背靠…：</span>The house is backed by woods. 房子的後面是樹林。

<span class="vocabulary">**retrieve**</span> [rɪˈtri:v]
<span class="definition">vt. 扭轉頹勢：</span>You can only retrieve the situation by apologizing. 你只有道歉才能挽回這個局面。
           
<span class="vocabulary">**reverse**</span> [rɪˈvɜ:s; 美 rɪˈvɜ:rs]
<span class="definition">vt.（趨勢、過程等）逆轉、轉變到對立面：</span>to reverse a procedure, to reverse a process, to reverse a trend 徹底改變程式；扭轉過程；扭轉趨勢 / The government has failed to reverse the economic decline. 政府未能扭轉經濟滑坡的趨勢。/ The decline in this industry has now been completely reversed. 這個行業現在已經完全扭轉了昔日的衰退局面。/ It is sometimes possible to arrest or reverse the disease. 有時可以阻止病情發展或使病情徹底好轉。/ The rise, the first in 10 months, reversed the downward trend in Belgium's jobless rate. 10個月來失業率的首次上升逆轉了比利時失業率持續下降的趨勢。<span class="definition">vt. 使物體反轉；使次序顛倒：</span>Writing is reversed in a mirror. 鏡子裡的字是反的。/ You should reverse the order of these pages. 你該把這幾頁的順序顛倒過來。/ In the squares place a penny, nickel, dime and quarter in that order. The object is to reverse the order of these coins. 在這些方塊中按順序放置1分、5分、10分和25分的硬幣各一枚。目標是顛倒這些硬幣的順序。/ The normal word order is reversed in passive sentences. 正常詞序在被動句中是顛倒的。/ To solve the puzzle, simply reverse the order of the numbers. 要解開這個謎，只要顛倒數字的順序即可。

<span class="vocabulary">**counter**</span> ['kaʊntə] 
<span class="definition">adv. counter to sth 指相反地、逆向地、反對地：</span>The government’s plans run counter to agreed European policy on this issue. 政府的計劃違反了有關這個問題已協商好的歐洲政策。
           
<span class="vocabulary">**reverse**</span> [rɪˈvɜ:s; 美 rɪˈvɜ:rs]
<span class="definition">adj. 1 相反的：</span>to travel in the reverse direction 向相反方向行進 / The winners were announced in reverse order (= the person in the lowest place was announced first). 獲勝者是按逆序宣佈的。/ The experiment had the reverse effect to what was intended. 實驗的結果與原來的意圖相反。/ The wrong attitude will have exactly the reverse effect. 錯誤的態度會產生完全相反的效果。<span class="definition">2 與正面相反的，即背面的、反面的：</span>Iron the garment on the reverse side. 這件衣服要從反面熨。

<span class="vocabulary">**reversible**</span> [rɪˈvɜ:səbl; 美 -ˈvɜ:rs-]
<span class="definition">adj. 1（衣服、材料等）可翻轉的；可兩面穿的；正反兩用的：</span>a reversible jacket 可兩面穿的夾克 <span class="definition">2（步驟、過程、行動或疾病）可逆的、可恢復原狀的：</span>Is the trend towards privatization reversible? 私有化趨勢可能逆轉嗎？/ reversible kidney failure 可逆的腎衰竭 / Heart disease is reversible in some cases, according to a study published last summer. 根據去年夏天公佈的一項研究，心臟病在某些情況下是可以治癒的。/ The operation is easily reversible. 這一操作可以輕易地反向進行。

周邊詞彙補充：
· redeem可表示彌補、補救；
· neutralize可表示使…無效。還可在化學中表示中和，使某種物質成為中性。也可表示使一個國家或地區中立。

☀ <span class="category">**反、抵消的名詞：**</span>
<span class="vocabulary">**back**</span> [bæk] 
<span class="definition">n. 1 [usually sing.] back (of sth) 紙張、檔案等的背面：</span>the back of the paper 紙的背面 <span class="definition">2 [U]（房屋等建築物的）後面，（車、建築物等的）後部：</span>a room at the back of house 房子後面的一間屋子 / the trees in back of the building 建築物後面的樹 / to be out back of the house 在房子的後面（並非房屋內部的後面）/ Come around back and I’ll show you the pool. 繞到後面，我帶你看看泳池。
           
<span class="vocabulary">**reverse**</span> [rɪˈvɜ:s; 美 rɪˈvɜ:rs]
<span class="definition">n. 1 [sing.] the reverse可指相反的情況（或事物）：</span>This problem is the reverse of the previous one. 這個問題和上一個問題相反。/ Although I expected to enjoy living in the country, in fact the reverse is true. 儘管我原以為會喜歡鄉村生活，但實際情況正好相反。/ In the south, the reverse applies. 在南方，情況相反。/ It wasn't easy to persuade her to come--quite the reverse. 說服她過來不容易——實在太難了。/ There is absolutely no evidence at all that spectators want longer cricket matches. Quite the reverse. 完全沒有證據表明觀眾想看時間更長的板球比賽。情況恰恰相反。/ This would lead one to expect a fat, dense and detailed autobiography. The reverse is true. The book is short and spare. 這會讓人以為是一本內容晦澀而詳盡的大部頭自傳。但正好相反，這本書簡短明瞭。/ If you tell children to do something, they will often do the exact reverse. 告訴孩子要做什麼事，他們往往會反著幹。<span class="definition">2 [sing.] the reverse可指（硬幣、材料、紙張等的）背面、後面、反面：</span>The coin has a date on one side and the emperor's head on the reverse. 硬幣的一面是日期，另一面是君主的頭像。/ Cheques should be made payable to Country Living and your address written on the reverse. 支票抬頭必須寫“《鄉村生活》雜誌”，你的地址應寫在背面。<span class="definition">3 [sing.] in reverse 反向；逆序：</span>The secret number is my phone number in reverse. 這個密碼是我的電話號碼的逆序排列。/ We did a similar trip to you, but in reverse. 我們走了和你相似的旅程，但方向相反。<span class="definition">4 [sing.] go/put sth into reverse（使）出現逆轉，轉化為對立面：</span>In 2002 economic growth went into reverse. 2002年，經濟增長髮生了逆轉。/ The downward trend went into reverse and the scores started to creep up again. 比分下降的趨勢發生逆轉，分數又開始逐漸上升了。
           
<span class="vocabulary">**reversal**</span> [rɪˈvɜ:sl; 美 rɪˈvɜ:rsl]
<span class="definition">n. [C, U] ~ (of sth)（過程、政策或趨勢的）顛覆性的改變，如徹底轉變、逆轉、反轉：</span>a dramatic reversal of policy 政策的劇烈轉變 / The government suffered a total reversal of fortune(s) last week. 上星期，政府的命運發生逆轉。/ The Financial Times says the move represents a complete reversal of previous US policy.《金融時報》說此舉一反美國以往的政策。/ the dramatic reversal in population decline 人口下降趨勢的突然轉變 / a reversal of current trends 目前趨勢的轉變 / This marked a 7% increase on the previous year and the reversal of a steady five-year downward trend. 這標誌著在前一年基礎上有了7％的增長，扭轉了五年來持續下降的趨勢。

<span class="vocabulary">**pole**</span> [pəʊl] 
<span class="definition">n. [C]（對立或相反的）任何一方（如品質、觀點或信仰截然相反的兩極），即極端：</span>Their opinions were at opposite poles of the debate. 他們的意見在辯論中截然相反。/ The two authors represent the opposite poles of fictional genius. 這兩位作者代表了天才小說家的兩個極端。/ an artistic compromise between the poles of abstraction and representation 在抽象與具象這兩種藝術極端之間做出的折中 / In temperament, she and her sister are poles apart.（比喻用法）他和姐姐的性情截然相反。
           
<span class="vocabulary">**compensation**</span> [ˌkɒmpenˈseɪʃn; 美 ˌkɑ:m-]
<span class="definition">n. [C, usually pl] ~ (for sth) 對不利局面的補償：</span>I wish I were young again, but getting older has its compensations. 我要是能再次年輕就好了，但上了年紀也有上了年紀的好處。/ Helen gained some compensation for her earlier defeat by winning the final open class. 海倫在公開組決賽中取勝，這對她早先的失敗算是一種補償。/ Despite a reduction in earnings there are compensations in moving to the north-east where the quality of life is excellent. 儘管錢會掙得少一點，搬到生活質量高的東北部去還是有很多好處的。
