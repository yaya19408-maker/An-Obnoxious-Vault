☀ <span class="category">**宣佈宣告 宣告闡明：**</span>
<span class="vocabulary">**announce**</span> [ə'naʊns] 
<span class="definition">vt. 1 announce (sth) (to sb) 正式地將某事（尤指一個決定、一項計劃等）告知給某人，即正式宣佈或宣告：</span>The government yesterday announced to the media plans to create a million new jobs. 政府在昨天向媒體宣佈了創造一百萬個新工作的計劃。/ The company proudly announced the launch of its new range of cars. 該公司驕傲地宣佈其新系列轎車的推出。/ Mr and Mrs James are pleased to announce the engagement of their daughter, Louise. 詹姆斯先生和太太高興地宣佈他們的女兒露易絲訂婚。<span class="definition">2 大聲且（或）嚴肅地說出某事，即聲稱，宣稱：</span>She announced that she’d given up smoking. 她宣稱她已戒菸。/ ‘I’m hungry!’ he loudly announced.“我餓了！”他大聲說道。

<span class="vocabulary">**announcement**</span> [ə'naʊnsmənt] 
<span class="definition">n. [U] 將某事公開告知給人們的一種行為，即宣佈，宣告：</span>Announcement of the verdict was accompanied by cheers. 在一片歡呼聲中宣佈了判決。

<span class="vocabulary">**declare**</span> [dɪ'kleə] 
<span class="definition">vt.＆vi. 1 正式或公開地說出某事，即宣佈、宣告。發出訊息的主體常是一個官方組織，當然也可以是作為個體的人等。與announce在詞義上未見明顯差別。但announce僅為及物動詞：</span>The government has declared a state of emergency. 政府已宣佈進入緊急狀態。/ to declare war 宣戰 / I declare this bridge open. 我宣佈大橋正式啟用。/ The area has been declared a national park. 該地區已宣佈為國家公園。/ The court declared that strike action was illegal. 法庭宣判罷工為非法。/ The contract was declared void. 這份合同被宣佈無效。/ The painting was declared to be a forgery. 這幅畫被判定為贗品。<span class="definition">2 堅定而清楚地闡述，即斷言，聲稱，宣告。發出斷言的主體常是作為個體的人：</span>‘I’ll do it!’ Tom declared.“讓我來！”湯姆果斷地說。/ He declared that he was in love with her. 他聲稱他已愛上她。/ to proudly declare 驕傲地宣稱 / to declare confidently 自信地宣稱 / Stravinsky famously declared that music expressed nothing. 斯特拉文斯基有一句名言，說音樂什麼也不表達。
           
<span class="vocabulary">**declaration**</span> [ˌdekləˈreɪʃn]
<span class="definition">n. 1 [C, U]（尤指關於政府或組織機構的計劃的）官方或正式的宣告、公告、宣言：</span>solemn declaration 鄭重宣告 / official declaration 官方宣告 / public declaration 公開宣告 / joint declaration 聯合宣告 / unilateral declaration 單方面宣告 / ringing declaration 擲地有聲的宣告 / The Russian leader received a ringing declaration of support yesterday. 俄羅斯領導人昨天收到了給予強有力支援的宣告。/ The government will issue a formal declaration tomorrow. 政府將於明天釋出正式宣告。/ All four countries have adopted the declaration against hunting these rare animals. 四國都透過了反對捕獵這些珍稀動物的宣言。/ the declaration of war 宣戰 / The opening speeches sounded more like declarations of war than offerings of peace. 開場的幾段講話聽上去更像是戰爭宣言而不是和平倡議。/ the UN declaration on Human Rights《聯合國人權宣言》/ the Declaration of Independence (= of the United States)（美國）獨立宣言 / They will sign the declaration tomorrow. 他們明天將簽署這份公告。<span class="definition">2 [C]（尤指對於人的感受或信念的）宣告、宣佈、宣稱、表白：</span>a declaration of love, a declaration faith, a declaration guilt 表白愛情；表達信念；宣判有罪 / declarations of undying love 至死不渝之愛的告白 / She needed time to adjust to Clive's declaration. 她需要時間來適應克萊夫的表白。<span class="definition">3 [C] 申報單，(可用作法庭證據的）書面宣告：</span>a declaration of income 收益申報表 / customs declarations (= giving details of goods that have been brought into a country) 報關單（帶入境內物品的詳細清單）/ On the customs declaration, the sender labeled the freight as agricultural machinery. 在報關單上，寄貨人將貨物列為農用機械。/ They will ask you to sign a declaration allowing your doctor to disclose your medical details. 他們將要求你簽署一份允許醫生透露你的身體健康狀況的書面宣告。

<span class="vocabulary">**state**</span> [steɪt] 
<span class="definition">vt. 1（尤指以一種細緻而清晰的方式）正式寫出或說出某事，此時在個別語境中可翻譯為“宣告”（但並非專門表示“宣告”，而是“說明”、“陳述”）：</span>He has already stated his intention to run for election. 他已宣告打算參加競選。/ The contract was stated to be invalid. 那份合同宣佈作廢。<span class="definition">2 常用被動式，（尤指以一份書面檔案的形式）規定或公佈某事的細節：</span>You must arrive at the time stated. 你必須在規定時間到達。/ Do not exceed the stated dose (= of medicine). 不要超過規定的劑量。/ Clearly state your address and telephone number. 清楚地寫明你的地址和電話號碼。/ The police report stated that he was arrested for allegedly assaulting his wife. 警方報告稱，他因涉嫌毆打妻子而被捕。

<span class="vocabulary">**statement**</span> ['steɪtmənt] 
<span class="definition">n. [C] statement (on/about sth) 對於事實或觀點的一份正式或官方說明，詞義上相當於declaration，如：</span>The two heads of state issued a joint statement. 兩國元首發表了一份聯合宣告。/ formal, official statement 正式宣告；官方宣告 / They decided to make a political statement by refusing to vote. 他們決定把拒絕投票作為一種政治宣告。/ oral, signed, sworn, written statement 口頭宣告；簽署的宣告；誓詞；書面宣告 / a statement on human rights 有關人權的宣告 / His lawyer made a brief statement to the press. 他的律師向新聞界發表了簡短宣告。/ The police will take a statement from each of you. 警方將給你們每個人錄一份口供。/ An explicit statement of objectives is vital before the project begins. 專案開始之前，確切地說明目標至關重要。/ the company’s mission statement 該公司的使命宣言 / He was making a fashion statement by wearing a pink suit.（比喻用法）他穿了套粉色西裝，以此彰顯其時尚品味。
           
<span class="vocabulary">**bulletin**</span> [ˈbʊlətɪn]
<span class="definition">n. [C]（官方對重要事件的）公告、佈告：</span>a bulletin on the President's health 關於總統健康狀況的公告 / At 3.30 p.m. a bulletin was released announcing that the president was out of immediate danger. 下午3點30分發布了公告，稱總統已經暫時脫離危險。/ all-points bulletin（通常縮寫為APB）（美）（向執法機關頒發描述通緝犯情況等的）詳細通報 / The government will issue an official bulletin later this week. 本週晚些時候政府將釋出一則官方公報。           

<span class="vocabulary">**communique**</span> [kəˈmju:nɪkeɪ; 美 kəˌmju:nəˈkeɪ]
<span class="definition">n. [C]（尤指對報刊釋出的）官方公報：</span>official, unofficial communique 正式公報；非正式公報 / joint communique 聯合公報 / The announcement was made in a communique issued by the Defence Minister. 這項通告發布在國防部長髮表的公報中。

<span class="vocabulary">**pronounce**</span> [prə'naʊns] 
<span class="definition">vt.（正式、官方或公開地）宣佈或發表意見（此時與announce和declare幾無差別），一般不太用於日常生活中的非正式場合（而announce和declare則可表示非正式場合中“正式宣佈”）：</span>He pronounced the country to be in a state of war. 他宣佈全國進入戰爭狀態。/ The committee has pronounced in favour/favor of the merger. 委員會已表示支援合併。/ The judge will pronounce sentence today. 法官將於今天宣判。/ She pronounced him the winner of the competition. 她宣佈他是競賽的優勝者。/ I now pronounce you man and wife. 我現在正式宣佈你們結為夫妻。/ She was pronounced dead on arrival at the hospital. 到達醫院時她被宣告已經死亡。/ to pronounce an opinion 發表意見 / Reality TV was officially pronounced dead by the critics. 評論界正式宣佈真人秀沒有生命力。/ He pronounced himself delighted with the judge’s decision. 他宣佈自己對法官的判決感到高興。
            
<span class="vocabulary">**claim**</span> [kleɪm]
<span class="definition">vt. 宣稱、聲稱、斷言（某事是真的，但尚未經過證明，別人可能不相信）：</span>He claims (that) he was not given a fair hearing. 他聲稱他未得到公正的申述機會。/ Scientists are claiming a major breakthrough in the fight against cancer. 科學家們宣稱攻克癌症已有重大的突破。/ It was claimed that some doctors were working 80 hours a week. 據說有些醫生每週工作80小時。/ He claimed that it was all a conspiracy against him. 他聲稱這一切都是一場針對他的陰謀。/ The company had falsely claimed that its products were biodegradable. 公司謊稱其產品可生物降解。/ He tried to claim that he had acted in self-defence. 他極力宣稱自己是自衛。/ I don't claim to be an expert. 我不敢自稱為專家。/ A man claiming to be a journalist threatened to reveal details about her private life. 一個自稱是記者的人威脅要公開有關她的私生活的細節。<span class="definition">n. [C] ~ (that…) 宣稱、聲稱、斷言（某事是真的，但尚未經過證明，別人可能不相信）：</span>The singer has denied the magazine's claim that she is leaving the band. 這名歌手已否認那家雜誌有關她要離開樂隊的說法。/ He repeated his claim that the people of Trinidad and Tobago backed his action. 他再次聲稱，千里達及托巴哥的人民支援他的行動。/ He rejected claims that he had affairs with six women. 他拒不承認他和六個女人有染的說法。/ There are conflicting claims about the cause of the fire. 關於失火的原因有一些矛盾的說法。/ the central claim of the book 該書的中心論斷 / The company had made false claims about its products. 公司對其產品的表述不實。/ I make no claim to understand modern art. 我沒有說過自己理解現代藝術。/ We have heard claims like this many times before. 我們以前聽到過很多次這樣的說法。/ They accepted her claim that she had been ill-treated. 他們相信了她受過虐待的說法。/ Claims of a cover-up were dismissed. 聲稱有人掩蓋真相的種種說法未被採納。/ They were able to produce witnesses to support their claim. 他們能夠提供證人來支援自己的說法。/ to investigate claims about appalling prison conditions 調查有關監獄條件極端惡劣的說法 / Claims of corruption within the police force were denied. 警察內部腐敗的說法遭到了否認。

<span class="vocabulary">**proclaim**</span> [prəˈkleɪm]
<span class="definition">vt. 公開且正式地向人們宣告重要的事：</span>The president proclaimed a state of emergency. 總統宣佈了緊急狀態。/ The charter proclaimed that all states would have their own government. 憲章規定，各州皆允許建立各自的政府。/ He proclaimed himself emperor. 他自封為皇帝。/ The Boers rebelled against British rule, proclaiming their independence on 30 December 1880. 布林人反抗英國統治，並於1880年12月30日宣佈獨立。/ Britain proudly proclaims that it is a nation of animal lovers. 英國驕傲地聲稱自己是一個熱愛動物的國家。/ to hereby proclaim（常為幽默用法）特此宣佈 / I hereby proclaim June 12 as a computer-free day. 我特此宣佈6月12日為無計算機日。<span class="definition">vt. 明確地說（有一種強調的意味）：</span>'I think we have been heard today,' he proclaimed. “我想今天我們說的大家都聽見了，”他明確地說。/ He confidently proclaims that he is offering the best value in the market. 他信心百倍地聲稱他的產品是市場上最物有所值的。/ They continue to proclaim their innocence. 他們繼續宣稱自己無辜。/ She proclaimed her innocence to the world. 她向全世界表明她的無辜。/ She boldly proclaimed that her goal was to win the championship. 她大膽地宣佈，她的目標是贏得冠軍。/ She repeatedly proclaimed her devotion to the cause. 她反覆強調她對事業的獻身精神。/ Everyone is proclaiming him as the next president. 大家都說他會是下一任總統。
                      
<span class="vocabulary">**proclamation**</span> [ˌprɒkləˈmeɪʃn; 美 ˌprɑ:k-]
<span class="definition">n. [C, U]（官方對重要事情對公眾做的）宣言、公告、宣告；也可指此種行為：</span>a proclamation of independence 獨立宣告 / official, presidential, public, royal proclamation 官方宣告；總統宣告；公開宣告；王室宣告 / bold proclamation（尤美）大膽的宣告 / The President signed a proclamation declaring martial law. 總統簽署了公告，宣佈戒嚴。/ The government restricted the use of water by proclamation. 政府透過公告宣佈限制用水。

<span class="vocabulary">**affirm**</span> [əˈfɜ:m; 美 əˈfɜ:rm]
<span class="definition">vt. [正式] 堅定或公開地申明（某事屬實或對某事表示強烈支援）：</span>Both sides affirmed their commitment to the ceasefire. 雙方均申明答應停火。/ I can affirm that no one will lose their job. 我可以肯定，誰都不會丟掉工作。/ The House of Lords affirmed that the terms of a contract cannot be rewritten retrospectively. 上議院申明合同條款改寫後不具備追溯效力。/ a speech in which he affirmed a commitment to lower taxes 他做出減稅承諾的演講
           
<span class="vocabulary">**protest**</span> [ˈprəʊtest; 美 ˈproʊ-]
<span class="definition">n. [C] 強烈的申明、宣告或斷言：</span>That was how she usually dealt with their protests that she was spoiling her grandchildren. 當他們堅持說她是在溺愛孫兒，她通常會作出那樣的回應。
           
<span class="vocabulary">**assert**</span> [əˈsɜ:t; 美 əˈsɜ:rt]
<span class="definition">vt. 明確斷言（某事屬實）：</span>She continued to assert that she was innocent. 她仍然堅稱自己無辜。/ The defendants, who continue to assert their innocence, are expected to appeal. 各被告仍然堅稱無辜，預計會提起上訴。/ ‘That is wrong, ’ he asserted. “那是錯的。”他斷言道。/ It is commonly asserted that older people prefer to receive care from family members. 人們普遍確認，老年人更願意由家人照顧。/ Mr. Helm plans to assert that the bill violates the First Amendment. 赫爾姆先生打算宣告該法案違反了憲法第一修正案。/ The report asserts confidently that the industry will grow. 報告斷言這個產業會發展起來。/ He had no real evidence--he simply asserted that what he said was true. 他沒有確鑿的證據——只是堅稱他說的是實話。
           
<span class="vocabulary">**assertion**</span> [əˈsɜ:ʃn; 美 əˈsɜ:rʃn]
<span class="definition">n. [C] 明確肯定，斷言某事的真實性：</span>He was correct in his assertion that the minister had been lying. 他認定部長說謊，事實果然如此。/ Do you have any evidence to support your assertions? 你的斷言是否有真憑實據？/ sweeping assertions about the role of women in society 關於女性在社會中的作用的種種籠統說法 / The argument needs to progress beyond the simple assertion that criminals are made not born. 僅僅斷言說罪犯並非本性不良還不夠，還得展開論證。factual 基於事實的說法 / How can you make such an assertion? 你怎麼會作出這樣的斷言? / Your assertion is not supported by the facts. 你的斷言沒有事實依據。/ Researchers have recently challenged these assertions. 研究人員最近對這些斷言提出了異議。/ assertions about human nature 關於人類本性的種種斷言

<span class="vocabulary">**clarify**</span> ['klærɪfaɪ] 
<span class="definition">vt. [正式] 使某事更清晰易懂，或更易於理解，即闡明，澄清：</span>to clarify situation, to clarify problem, to clarify issue 澄清情況；澄清問題 / I hope this clarifies my position. 我希望這能闡明我的立場。/ She asked him to clarify what he meant. 她要他說清楚他是什麼意思。/ Headings and sub-headings further clarify the structure of the article. 標題和小標題使文章的結構更加清楚。/ I am happy to clarify any points that are still unclear. 我樂意對任何仍不清楚的地方進行說明。
           
<span class="vocabulary">**clarification**</span> [ˌklærəfɪˈkeɪʃn]
<span class="definition">n. [U, C] 澄清、闡明：</span>I am seeking clarification of the regulations. 我正在努力弄清楚這些規則。/ Employers are seeking further clarification of the proposals. 僱主們正在尋求進一步澄清這些提案。

<span class="vocabulary">**pretend**</span> [prɪ'tend] 
<span class="definition">vt.＆vi. pretend (to sth) 通常用於否定句和疑問句，宣稱（自己是…、自己做某事或自己擁有某東西），如自詡，自稱，自認為等：</span>I can’t pretend to any great musical talent. 我不能妄稱自己多有音樂天賦。/ I don’t pretend (that) I know much about the subject, but … 我不敢說自己對這個主題有多瞭解，但是… / The book doesn’t pretend to be a great work of literature. 這本書並未自封為文學傑作。

<span class="vocabulary">**develop**</span> [dɪ'veləp]

<span class="vocabulary">**page**</span> [peɪdӡ]

<span class="vocabulary">**promulgate**</span> [ˈprɒmlgeɪt; 美 ˈprɑ:m-]
<span class="definition">vt. [正式] 官方或公開宣佈一個新法律或體制：</span>A new constitution was promulgated last month. 上個月頒佈了一部新憲法。

<span class="vocabulary">**record**</span> [rɪ'kɔ:d] 
<span class="definition">vt. 就某事發表正式的（或法律方面的）宣告：</span>The coroner recorded a verdict of murder. 驗屍官判定這是一起謀殺案。

<span class="vocabulary">**pass**</span> [pɑːs] 
<span class="definition">vt. pass sth (on sb/sth) 說或陳述某事，尤指正式地，即宣佈、宣告：</span>to pass sentence 宣判 / It’s not for me to pass judgement on his behaviour. 我無權對他的行為做出評判。/ to pass a friendly remark 說句親切的話

<span class="vocabulary">**post**</span> [pəʊst] 
<span class="definition">vt. [尤美] 公開或正式地宣佈某事，尤指財經資訊或警告：</span>to post a rainstorm warning for this region 該地區發出暴雨警報 / The aircraft was posted missing. 據報道這架飛機已失蹤。/ The company posted a $100 million loss. 這家公司公佈了1億美元的虧損。

<span class="vocabulary">**deliver**</span> [dɪ'lɪvə] 
<span class="definition">vt. 發表演說，或做出其他正式陳述。在個別語境中也可翻譯為“宣佈”，但不具有代表性，只是偶爾的一種用法：</span>to deliver a lecture 發表演講 / He delivered his lines confidently. 他沉著地說了他的臺詞。/ The jury delivered its verdict. 陪審團宣佈了裁決。

<span class="vocabulary">**out**</span> [aʊt] 
<span class="definition">adv., prep. [非正式] 向他人公開自己的同性戀身份：</span>an out gay man 已公開同性戀身份的男子 / He was out. 他公開了自己的同性戀身份。<span class="definition">vt. 公開說出某人的同性戀身份（尤指在同性戀者本人不願公開的情況下）：</span>He is outed by one of his friends. 他的一個朋友揭露了他的同性戀身份。

<span class="vocabulary">**return**</span> [rɪ'tə:n] 
<span class="definition">n. [C] 向政府或其它機構提供某種資訊的一份正式報告或陳述：</span>census returns 人口普查報告 / election returns 選舉結果報告

<span class="vocabulary">**profession**</span> [prə'feʃn] 
<span class="definition">n. [C] profession of sth 表明或陳述自己的信念、信仰、感覺或對某事的想法，有時是公開地宣告，與declaration近義：</span>a profession of faith 表明信仰

<span class="vocabulary">**condemn**</span> [kən'dem] 
<span class="definition">vt. condemn sth (as sth) 正式說出（即宣告）使用…不夠安全，常用被動式：</span>a condemned building 一座已宣告不能居住的危樓 / The meat was condemned as unfit to eat. 這種肉被宣告不宜食用。
