☀ <span class="category">**秘語 低語：**</span>
<span class="vocabulary">**whisper**</span> ['wɪspə] 
<span class="definition">vt.＆vi. 對某人以極安靜的方式說話，使其他人聽不見你說什麼，如私語，耳語，低語等，相當於murmur：</span>What are you two whispering about? 你們倆在低聲說些什麼？/ He whispered to me that he was afraid. 他低聲對我說他害怕。/ ‘Come on,’ he whispered urgently. “來吧。”他急切地小聲說道。/ I felt that everyone was whispering about me. 我感到每個人都在低聲議論我。<span class="definition">vt. 常用被動式，指以一種私人或秘密的方式來說（或暗示）…：</span>It was whispered that he would soon die and he did. 有人私下說他將不久於人世，他果然死了。<span class="definition">n. [C] 人低聲安靜說的話，即低語（或低語聲），漢語中還可指耳語（或耳語聲）、私語（或私語聲），相當於murmur：</span>Her voice dropped to a whisper. 她壓低聲音小聲說話。/ Their voices were very quiet, hardly above a whisper. 他們的聲音很低，幾乎聽不見。
           
<span class="vocabulary">**murmur**</span> [ˈmɜ:mə(r); 美 ˈmɜ:rm-]
<span class="definition">vt.&vi.（難以聽到或理解的）低聲細語、喃喃細語：</span>She murmured her agreement. 她低聲表示同意。/ He murmured something in his sleep. 他在睡夢裡嘟囔了些什麼。/ She was murmuring in his ear. 她在他耳邊悄聲說話。/ He turned and murmured something to the professor. 他轉身對教授小聲說了些什麼。/ She murmured a few words of support. 她小聲說了幾句支援的話。/ ‘What a fool I've been,’ he murmured softly.“我以前可真傻。”他輕聲嘀咕道。/ 'Night, night,' she murmured sleepily. “晚——安——。”她睡意十足地低聲說。/ ‘Mmm,’ she murmured appreciatively. 她讚賞地嗯了一聲。/ She heard him murmur something under his breath. 她聽見他低聲咕噥了幾句。/ He held her tight and murmured to her. 他緊緊摟住她，在她耳旁低語。<span class="definition">n. 1 [C] 低語、喃喃細語：</span>She answered in a faint murmur. 她低聲應答。/ They spoke in low murmurs. 他們低聲說著話。/ She gave a little murmur. 她低聲咕噥了一句。/ Murmurs of ‘Praise God’ went around the circle. 周圍的人群發出了“讚美主”的低語聲。/ the murmur of voices 竊竊私語聲 <span class="definition">2 [C]（也可用murmurings）情感的安靜表達，如嘟噥、咕噥：</span>a murmur of agreement, a murmur of approval, a murmur of complaint 表示同意的低語聲；表示贊同的低語聲；表示抱怨的低語聲 / He paid the extra cost without a murmur (= without complaining at all). 他一聲不吭地付了額外的花費。/ They did as they were told, without a murmur. 他們一聲不吭地照要求做了。/ polite murmurings of gratitude 有禮貌地連連低聲道謝 / He took the mug of coffee with a murmur of thanks. 他接過那杯咖啡，輕聲道了謝。/ The promise of some basic working rights draws murmurs of approval. 對一些基本工作權利的承諾引來一片低聲的讚許。/ Already there are murmurs of discontent. 已有一些不滿的怨言。/ a general murmur of assent 眾人表示贊同的低語聲 / He gave a little murmur of relief. 他輕輕地舒了一口氣。/ A murmur of excitement rippled through the audience. 觀眾一陣激動，發出竊竊私語聲。/ A murmur of amusement ran around the room. 輕輕的笑聲在屋子裡傳開了。
           
<span class="vocabulary">**mutter**</span> [ˈmʌtə(r)]
<span class="definition">vt.&vi. ~ (sth) (to sb/yourself) (about sth)（尤指因生氣、煩惱而）小聲嘀咕、嘟囔：</span>‘How dare she,’ he muttered under his breath. 他輕聲嘀咕道：“她怎麼敢。”/ 'God knows what's happening in that madman's mind,' she muttered. 她咕噥著：“天知道那個瘋子在想什麼。”/ She can hear the old woman muttering about consideration. 她能聽到那個老婦人小聲咕噥說考慮考慮。/ She just sat there muttering to herself. 她坐在那兒獨自唧唧咕咕的。/ I muttered something about needing to get back to work. 我嘀咕著說要接著幹活了。/ 'I don't need a drink,' she muttered through clenched teeth. 她咬牙嘟囔了一句：“我不喝。”/ Helen began muttering darkly about hospitals. 海倫開始嘟囔地講起醫院。/ A number of visitors were heard to mutter that it would not have happened at home. 有人聽到幾位參觀者嘟囔說在家就不會發生這種事。/ She heard him mutter an oath under his breath. 她聽見他低聲詛咒。/ He was muttering incoherently to himself. 他語無倫次地喃喃自語。<span class="definition">n. [usually sing.] 嘀咕、嘟囔、低語聲：</span>the soft mutter of voices 柔和的低語聲            
           
<span class="vocabulary">**mumble**</span> [ˈmʌmbl]
<span class="definition">vt.&vi. 低聲而口齒不清地說，如嘟噥：</span>I could hear him mumbling to himself. 我聽到他在喃喃自語。/ She mumbled an apology and left. 她嘟嘟囔囔地道了歉就走了。/ ‘Sorry,’ she mumbled. 她含含糊糊地說：“對不起。”/ Her grandmother mumbled in her sleep. 她祖母含混不清地說著夢話。/ He mumbled a few words. 他小聲咕噥了一句。/ I couldn't understand what he was mumbling about. 我不知道他在咕噥什麼。/ She mumbled something into her pillow. 她對著枕頭咕嚕了幾聲。/ George mumbled incoherently to himself. 喬治語無倫次地喃喃自語。<span class="definition">n. [usually sing.]（也可用mumbling[C, usually pl., U]）低聲而口齒不清地說出的話：</span>He spoke in a low mumble, as if to himself. 他自言自語般地嘟噥著。/ They tried to make sense of her mumblings. 他們試圖聽明白她在嘟噥些什麼。

<span class="vocabulary">**moan**</span> [məʊn; 美 moʊn]
<span class="definition">vt.&vi.（人）呻吟（通常表達不悅、痛苦或性快感）：</span>He moaned in despair. 他絕望地呻吟。/ She was still conscious and was moaning loudly with pain. 她神志仍然清醒，只是痛得大聲呻吟。/ The injured man was lying on the ground, moaning. 受傷的人躺在地上呻吟著。/ Tony moaned in his sleep and then turned over on his side. 託尼在睡夢中發出呻吟聲，然後翻了個身。/ ‘I might never see you again, ’ she moaned. 她呻吟著說：“我可能再也見不到你了。” / 'My head, my head,' he moaned. 'I can't see.' “我的頭，我的頭，”他呻吟著說，“我看不見了。” <span class="definition">n. [C] 呻吟聲：</span>a low moan of anguish 在痛苦中發出的低沉的呻吟聲 / a moan of pleasure 快活的呻吟 / A low moan of despair escaped her as she realized what had gone wrong. 她意識到是什麼事不對勁時，不由得發出了一聲絕望的低吟。/ He staggered about ten yards and fell down with a moan. 他搖搖晃晃地走了大約十碼遠，呻吟了一聲就倒下了。          

<span class="vocabulary">**groan**</span> [grəʊn; 美 groʊn]
<span class="definition">vt.&vi. ~ (at/with sth)（因為煩惱、痛苦或高興而）呻吟、哼哼：</span>Some of the patients were groaning with pain. 一些病人痛苦地呻吟著。/ to groan with pleasure 高興得直哼哼 / He lay on the floor groaning. 他躺在地板上呻吟。/ They glanced at the man on the floor, who began to groan. 他們瞥了一眼躺在地上的男人，那人現在開始呻吟起來。/ He groaned inwardly at the thought of spending another day in that place. 一想到要在那兒再待上一天，他就暗自叫苦。/ She groaned out loud in protest. 她大聲哼哼以示抗議。<span class="definition">vi. 不高興地低聲說，如咕噥：</span>‘It's a complete mess! ’ she groaned. “這簡直是一團糟！”她咕噥著說。/ 'My leg — I think it's broken,' Eric groaned. “我的腿——我想是斷了，”埃裡克咕噥著說。<span class="definition">n. [C]（人發出的）呻吟聲、咕噥聲：</span>She let out a groan of dismay. 她發出沮喪的嘆息聲。/ He let out a loud groan of frustration. 他沮喪地大聲哼了一聲。/ He stood up slowly with a groan of pain. 他痛苦地呻吟了一聲，慢慢地站起身來。/ a groan of annoyance 氣惱的哼哼聲 / He ignored her moans and groans. 他對她的呻吟充耳不聞。/ He fell to the floor with a groan. 他哼了一聲倒在地板上。
           
<span class="vocabulary">**grumble**</span> [ˈgrʌmbl]
<span class="definition">vt.&vi. ~ (at/to sb) (about/at sb/sth) 咕噥、嘟囔：</span>‘I'll just have to do it myself, ’ he grumbled. “我只好自己動手了。”他咕噥著說。/ They kept grumbling that they were cold. 他們不停地嘟囔著說冷。
           
<span class="vocabulary">**grunt**</span> [grʌnt]
<span class="definition">vt.&vi.（人）（尤指因為疼痛、煩惱或不感興趣）發出嘟噥聲：</span>He pulled harder on the rope, grunting with the effort. 他邊用力邊哼聲，使出更大的力氣拉繩子。/ When I told her what had happened she just grunted and turned back to her book. 我告訴她出了什麼事，她只哼了一聲就又看起書來了。/ The driver grunted, convinced that Michael was crazy. 司機嘟噥了一聲，認定邁克爾是發瘋了。/ He merely grunted at her and nodded his head. 他只對她咕噥了幾句，點了點頭。/ His father grunted at him as he left the room. 他離開房間時，父親對他咕噥了幾句。/ She asked him a question and he grunted in reply. 她問了他一個問題，他咕噥了幾聲算是回答了她。/ He grunted in pain. 他痛苦地咕噥著。/ She stirred the soup, grunting with satisfaction.她攪著湯，發出滿意的咕噥聲。/ Grunting and groaning, they heaved the wardrobe up the stairs. 他們吭哧吭哧地把衣櫥抬上了樓。<span class="definition">vt.&vi. 咕噥著說：</span>'Rubbish,' I grunted. “廢話，”我嘟噥了一聲。/ He grunted something about being late and rushed out. 他嘟噥著說要遲到了便匆匆跑了出去。<span class="definition">n. [C] 人的嘟噥聲、咕噥聲或哼哼聲：</span>to give a grunt of effort 發出費力的哼聲 / a grunt of pain 痛苦的呻吟 / little, slight, small, soft grunt 微弱的咕噥；輕聲的咕噥 / low grunt 低聲的咕噥 / He gave a non-committal grunt in reply. 他似是而非地咕噥著應了一聲。/ loud grunt 大聲的咕噥 / angry, annoyed, frustrated grunt 生氣的咕噥；厭煩的咕噥；沮喪的咕噥 / He lifted the heavy box with a grunt. 他哼了一聲搬起了那隻沉重的箱子。
           
<span class="vocabulary">**snort**</span> [snɔ:t; 美 snɔ:rt]
<span class="definition">vt.&vi. 人（表示氣憤或被逗樂）噴鼻息，哼：</span>He snorted with laughter. 他撲哧一聲笑了。/ He snorted loudly and shook his head. 他響亮地哼了一聲，搖了搖頭。/ She snorted in disgust. 她厭惡地哼了一聲。/ He snorted in disbelief. 他懷疑地哼了一聲。/ He snorted with derision. 他嘲笑地哼了一聲。/ He snorted indignantly and walked away. 他氣憤地哼了一聲，走開了。/ She snorted at his suggestion. 對於他的建議，她只哼了一聲。<span class="definition">vt.&vi.（表示輕蔑）哼著鼻子說：</span>‘You!’ he snorted contemptuously. “你！”他輕蔑地哼了一聲。/ 'Reports,' he snorted. 'Anyone can write reports.' “報告，”他哼著鼻子說，“誰都會寫。” <span class="definition">n. [C]（尤指表示氣憤或被逗樂的）噴鼻息，哼：</span>little, small snort 輕輕的哼聲 / loud snort 響亮的哼聲 / derisive snort 嘲弄的哼聲 / a snort of disgust 厭惡的哼聲 / a snort of laughter 撲哧一笑 / He gave a snort of contempt. 他不屑地哼了一聲。/ 'What a mess they made of it!' said Sam with a snort of derision. 薩姆諷刺地哼了一聲說“瞧他們弄得一團糟”。

<span class="vocabulary">**breathe**</span> [bri:ð] 
<span class="definition">vt.＆vi. [文學] 安靜地說，即輕聲說：</span>huskily, quietly breathe 沙啞地低語；輕聲說 / ‘I love you!’ she breathed softly. 她溫柔地低聲說：“我愛你！”

<span class="vocabulary">**mouth**</span> [maʊθ] 
<span class="definition">vt.＆vi. 用嘴唇不出聲地說：</span>to mouth a goodbye 只張嘴不出聲地說再見 / to mouth a few obscenities at him 朝他不出聲地罵幾句髒話
           
<span class="vocabulary">**undertone**</span> [ˈʌndətəʊn; 美 ˈʌndərtoʊn]
<span class="definition">n. [C] ~ (of sth)（說話的）低聲、悄聲：</span>'What d'you think?' she asked in an undertone. “你覺得怎麼樣？”她小聲地問道。/ Well-dressed clients were talking in polite undertones as they ate. 衣著光鮮的顧客們邊吃邊斯文地低聲聊著天。/ The couple at the next table were speaking in undertones. 鄰桌的夫婦在輕聲交談。
           
<span class="vocabulary">**hum**</span> [hʌm]
<span class="definition">exclamation. 用於躊躇時發出聲音，如嗯、呃：</span>Hum, I am sorry but I thought you were French. 嗯，很抱歉，我以為你是法國人。
