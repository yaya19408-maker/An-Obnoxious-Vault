☀ <span class="category">**傳聞 謠言：**</span>
<span class="vocabulary">**rumour**</span> [ˈru:mə(r)]
<span class="definition">n. [C, U] ~ (of/about sth) | ~ (that …) 謠言、傳聞：</span>to start a rumour, to spread a rumour 製造謠言；散佈謠言 / There are widespread rumours of job losses. 到處謠傳要裁員。/ There have been persistent rumours of quarrels within the movement. 運動團體內部存在爭執的傳聞始終未斷。/ Some malicious rumours are circulating about his past. 有人別有用心地散佈謠言，說他過去如何如何。/ I heard a rumour that they are getting married. 我聽到傳聞，說他們要結婚了。/ Many of the stories are based on rumour. 這些說法很多都是道聽途說。/ Rumour has it (= people say) that he was murdered. 有傳言說他被殺害了。/ Internet rumour 網上的謠言 / His lengthy absence will fuel rumours that he has been fired. 他長時間不來，會使大家更相信他已被解僱了。/ The actor confirmed rumours that he will be leaving the series. 那位演員證實了他將離開那部連續劇的傳言。/ The Chief Executive issued a statement to quash rumours of financial problems. 總裁釋出了一項宣告來平息有關財務問題的謠言。/ The rumour quickly swept the town. 謠言迅速傳遍了整個鎮子。/ rumour factory（英）, rumour mill 謠言工廠 / The Washington rumour mill suggests the money changed hands illegally. 華盛頓的傳言暗示那筆錢已經非法易手。/ The manager resigned suddenly amidst rumours of misconduct. 經歷在其失職傳聞纏身的情況下突然辭職了。/ There were persistent rumours of drug-taking among staff. 職員吸毒的傳言不絕於耳。/ Among the other employees, rumour has it that he was fired from his last job. 員工中傳言說，他上一份工作是被炒了魷魚的。/ There is no truth in the rumour that she is about to resign. 她要辭職的傳聞不實。<span class="definition">vi. be rumoured可指謠傳、傳言：</span>It's widely rumoured that she's getting promoted. 到處都在傳要提拔她了。/ He was rumoured to be involved in the crime. 有傳言說他捲入了這樁罪行。

<span class="vocabulary">**hearsay**</span> [ˈhɪəseɪ; 美 ˈhɪrseɪ]
<span class="definition">n. [U] 從別人那裡聽到的、未必真實的事情，即道聽途說、傳聞、傳言：</span>We can't make a decision based on hearsay and guesswork. 我們不能根據傳言和猜測作決定。/ hearsay evidence 傳聞的證據 / Much of what was reported to them was hearsay. 向他們彙報的訊息中很多都是道聽途說。/ Rumour, myth and hearsay obscure the truth after months of bloodshed. 殺戮持續了數月後，真相在謠言、謊話和傳聞的遮掩下變得模糊不清。/ Her judgements are based on hearsay rather than evidence. 她的判斷只是基於道聽途說，而非真憑實據。/ She discovered a world of parties and pleasure she had hitherto only known by hearsay. 她發現了一個以前只是略有耳聞的花花世界。/ They started to piece the story together from hearsay. 他們開始根據傳聞把事情的經過一點點拼湊起來。

<span class="vocabulary">**whisper**</span> ['wɪspə] 
<span class="definition">n. [C] 透過談論而被傳開的真假未知的新聞，即傳言，相當於rumour，不過不如rumour常規和常用：</span>I’ve heard whispers that he’s leaving. 我聽人說他要走。

<span class="vocabulary">**talk**</span> [tɔ:k] 
<span class="definition">n. [U] talk (of sth/of doing sth) / talk (that …) 傳言；謠言；流言飛語；（對未來可能發生的事件的）揣測。是一個簡單用詞：</span>There was talk in Washington of sending in troops. 華盛頓有派兵的謠傳。/ as newspaper talk 報紙上的謠言
           
<span class="vocabulary">**buzz**</span> [bʌz]
<span class="definition">n. [sing.] [非正式] the buzz可指傳聞、謠傳：</span>The movie, due out next summer, is already creating a buzz. 下一個夏季上映的這部電影已引發熱議。/ An intense buzz surrounded the event. 圍繞這件事議論紛紛。
 
<span class="vocabulary">**report**</span> [rɪ'pɔ:t] 
<span class="definition">vt. be reported用於表示某事已被陳述出來（而你並不知道此事是真是假），即據說，傳聞：</span>She is reported to earn over $1 million a year. 據傳她一年掙100多萬美元。/ It was reported that changes were being considered. 有傳言說改革措施正在醞釀之中。<span class="definition">n. [C]（真假未定的）傳聞。不太常用：</span>I don’t believe these reports of UFO sightings. 我不相信這些有關目擊不明飛行物的傳言。/ There are unconfirmed reports of a shooting in the capital. 有未經證實的傳言說在首都發生了槍擊事件。
           
<span class="vocabulary">**scandal**</span> [ˈskændl]
<span class="definition">n. 1 [C, U]（讓公眾震驚或憤怒的）道德或法律上的醜行或醜事，常翻譯為“醜聞”：</span>abuse, accounting, bribery, corporate, corruption, doping, drug（尤美）, drugs（尤英）, financial, political, sex, sexual scandal 虐待醜聞；會計造假醜聞；賄賂醜聞；公司醜聞；腐敗醜聞；興奮劑醜聞；毒品醜聞；金融醜聞；政治醜聞；性醜聞 / sex-abuse scandal 性虐待醜聞 / the prisoner-abuse scandal 虐囚醜聞 / a series of sex scandals 一系列性醜聞 / There have been calls for the resignation of the official involved in the sex scandal. 有呼聲要求捲入性醜聞的官員辭職。/ There has been no hint of scandal during his time in office. 他在任期間沒有任何醜聞。/ The scandal broke (= became known to the public) in May. 這樁醜聞是在五月份曝光的。/ The scandal broke on the front pages of all the papers the next day. 第二天所有報紙的頭版都披露了這一醜聞。/ financial scandals surrounding the government 困擾政府的金融醜聞 / scandal sheet (= a newspaper that publishes stories intended to shock the public) 刊登醜聞的小報 / He was imprisoned for his part in the bribery scandal. 他因牽涉賄賂醜聞而入獄。/ The apartment was paid for by the bank at the centre of the scandal. 這套公寓是由身處醜聞中心的那家銀行付款購買的。/ Until the story was published there had been no hint of scandal. 這宗醜聞在公開報道之前沒有露出半點兒跡象。/ In the wake of recent accounting scandals, new cases have come to light. 繼最近幾宗會計醜聞之後，又有新案件曝光。<span class="definition">2 [U] 對於醜行的談論、傳言或報道，即醜聞：</span>to spread scandal 散佈醜聞 / newspapers full of scandal 充斥醜聞的報紙 / He loved gossip and scandal. 他喜歡說三道四，散佈謠言。/ These mothers often abandoned their children because of fear of scandal. 這些母親經常會因為害怕流言蜚語而拋棄自己的孩子。
           
<span class="vocabulary">**dirt**</span> [dɜ:t; 美 dɜ:rt]
<span class="definition">n. [U] [非正式]（關於某人的）醜聞、流言蜚語：</span>Do you have any dirt on the new guy? 你知道新來的那個人的醜聞嗎？/ We need to get some dirt on her.（get尤為美式用法）我們得給她搞點兒醜聞。/ Steve was keen to get all the dirt he could on her. 史蒂夫一心想要蒐羅關於她的一切醜聞。/ He could dig up so much dirt on her. 他能挖出她的許多醜間。/ Both parties use computers to dig up dirt on their opponents. 雙方都用電腦蒐集對手的醜事。/ He dishes the dirt on his buddies. 他說自己夥伴的閒話。/ She just loves to dish the dirt(= tell people unkind/unpleasant things about sb). 她專愛說別人的壞話。
