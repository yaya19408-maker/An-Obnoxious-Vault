☀ <span class="category">**避開話題 轉移話題：**</span>
<span class="vocabulary">**tangent**</span> [ˈtændʒənt]
<span class="definition">n. [C] fly/go off at a ~ [英] [美為go off on a ~] [非正式] 突然轉換話題：</span>The conversation went off at a tangent. 交談突然偏離了正題。

<span class="vocabulary">**skirt**</span> [skə:t] 
<span class="definition">vt.＆vi. skirt (around/round) sth 避免談論某一話題（尤指因為困難或尷尬）：</span>He carefully skirted the issue of where they would live. 他小心地避開了他們將住在何處這個問題。/ She tactfully skirted around the subject of money. 她巧妙地避口不提錢的事。

<span class="vocabulary">**lead**</span> [li:d] 
<span class="definition">vt.（在討論或文章中的某個觀點或話題等）引導你到與之相關的另一個觀點或話題等：</span>I tried to lead the discussion back to the main issue. 我試圖把討論引回到主要問題上。/ Well, I think that leads me to the real point. 嗯，我想這把我引向要點。

<span class="vocabulary">**leave**</span> [li:v] 
<span class="definition">vt. 不再談論某一話題（而是轉而去談另一話題）：</span>I think we’d better leave the subject of nationalism. 我想我們最好不要再談論民族主義這個話題了。
           
<span class="vocabulary">**evade**</span> [ɪˈveɪd]
<span class="definition">vt. 迴避（談論某事）：</span>Her response was simply to evade the problem altogether. 她的回答就是乾脆迴避了整個問題。/ to evade answering a question 避而不答某一問題
                      
<span class="vocabulary">**veer**</span> [vɪə(r); 美 vɪr]
<span class="definition">vi.（交談）改變方向：</span>The debate veered away from the main topic of discussion. 爭論脫離了討論的主題。

<span class="vocabulary">**fend**</span> [fend]
<span class="definition">vt.&vi. fend off sb/sth 避開，迴避（難題、批評等）：</span>She managed to fend off questions about new tax increases. 她設法避開了關於新增賦稅的問題。/ He looked relaxed and determined as he fended off questions from the world's Press. 他迴避各國媒體的提問時顯得輕鬆而果決。
           
<span class="vocabulary">**hedge**</span> [hedʒ]
<span class="definition">vi. 避免正面回答；避免直接許諾（支援某個想法等）。如拐彎抹角：</span>Just answer ‘yes ’ or ‘no ’─and stop hedging. 就回答“是”或“否”——別再閃爍其詞了。/ They hedged in answering various questions about the operation. 他們閃爍其詞，不願回答有關此次行動的各種問題。/ 'I can't give you an answer now,' he hedged. “我現在不能回答你，”他閃避道。
           
<span class="vocabulary">**stray**</span> [streɪ]
<span class="definition">vi.（談話時）離題：</span>We seem to be straying from the main theme of the debate. 我們似乎是偏離了辯論的主題。/ The teachers rarely stray away from the approved textbook. 教師很少偏離規定的教科書的內容。

<span class="vocabulary">**off**</span> [ɔ:f, ɒf] 
<span class="definition">prep. 離開主題或故意避開話題：</span>We’re getting right off the subject. 我們完全離題了。/ Keep off the subject of politics. 避開政治話題。

<span class="vocabulary">**onto**</span> ['ɒntə] 
<span class="definition">prep.（談論過程中）轉向新的話題：</span>Let’s get onto more important matters. 咱們談些更重要的事吧。

☀ <span class="category">**支吾結巴 輕描淡寫：**</span>
<span class="vocabulary">**fence**</span> [fens] 
<span class="definition">vi. fence (with sb) 對某人講話時以聰明的方式來獲得談話優勢，如搪塞、支吾、迴避等：</span>瞭解即可
           
<span class="vocabulary">**prevaricate**</span> [prɪˈværɪkeɪt]
<span class="definition">vi. [正式] 避免直接給出問題的答案（以隱藏真相），如支吾其詞、搪塞、吞吞吐吐：</span>Stop prevaricating and come to the point. 別吞吞吐吐的，有話快說吧。/ British ministers continued to prevaricate. 英國的大臣們仍然閃爍其詞。
             
<span class="vocabulary">**falter**</span> [ˈfɔ:ltə(r)]
<span class="definition">vi. 嗓音不自信地說（如嗓音發顫、猶豫或暫停）：</span>His voice faltered as he began his speech. 他開始演講時說話結結巴巴。/ Her voice faltered and she had to stop a moment to control it. 她聲音發顫，只好稍作停頓以有所控制。         

<span class="vocabulary">**evasive**</span> [ɪˈveɪsɪv]
<span class="definition">adj. 不願給出明確回答的，即迴避提問的、含糊其辭的：</span>evasive comments, evasive replies 含糊其辭意見；含糊其辭的答覆 / Tessa was evasive about why she had not been at home that night. 特薩對她那天晚上不在家的原因避而不談。/ He was evasive about the circumstances of his first meeting with Stanley Dean. 他避而不談他和斯坦利·迪安第一次會面的情況。/ Direct questions would almost certainly result in evasive answers. 直接的提問得到的幾乎肯定會是含糊其詞的回答。
           
<span class="vocabulary">**stammer**</span> [ˈstæmə(r)]
<span class="definition">vt.&vi. 結結巴巴地說。相當於stutter：</span>Many children stammer but grow out of it. 很多小孩結巴，但長大就改過來了。/ ‘W-w-what? ’ he stammered. “什…什…什麼？”他結巴著說。/ She was barely able to stammer out a description of her attacker. 她只能勉強結結巴巴地說一說襲擊她的人是什麼模樣。/ 'Forgive me,' I stammered. “原諒我，”我期期艾艾地說。<span class="definition">n. [sing.] 結巴：</span>A speech-therapist cured his stammer. 一位言語矯治師治癒了他的結巴。/ bad（尤英）, nervous, severe stammer 嚴重結巴；緊張性結巴 / slight stammer 輕微結巴
          
<span class="vocabulary">**stutter**</span> [ˈstʌtə(r)]
<span class="definition">vt.&vi. 結巴；結巴地說。相當於stammer：</span>‘W-w-what?’ he stuttered. “什…什…什麼？”他結結巴巴地說。/ I managed to stutter a reply. 我結結巴巴，好不容易應了一句。/ I was trembling so hard, I thought I would stutter when I spoke. 我抖得厲害，感覺說話舌頭會打結。<span class="definition">n. [sing.] 結巴：</span>He had a terrible stutter. 他患嚴重結巴。/ He spoke with a pronounced stutter, which got worse when he was angry. 他說話明顯結巴，生氣時更為嚴重。
           
<span class="vocabulary">**stumble**</span> [ˈstʌmbl]
<span class="definition">vi. ~ (over/through sth) 不順暢地說話：</span>In her nervousness she stumbled over her words. 她因緊張說話結結巴巴的。/ his voice wavered and he stumbled over the words at one point 他聲音顫抖，一度說話磕磕巴巴的
           
<span class="vocabulary">**understatement**</span> [ˈʌndəsteɪtmənt; 美 -dərs-]
<span class="definition">n. 1 [C] 不充分的敘述，輕描淡寫的敘述：</span>To say we were pleased is an understatement (= we were extremely pleased). 說我們高興，那是輕描淡寫。/ ‘These figures are a bit disappointing. ’ ‘That's got to be the understatement of the year.’ “這些數字有點令人失望。” “那一定是本年度最保守的說法了。”/ To say I'm disappointed is an understatement. 說我失望那是說得太輕了。/ He was getting very hard to live with, and that's the understatement of the year. 說他很不好相處，那簡直是說得太客氣了。/ 'A little strange' is a gross understatement. "有一點兒奇怪"絕對是輕描淡寫。<span class="definition">2 [U] 輕描淡寫，低調說法：</span>typical English understatement 典型的英國式低調說法 / He always goes for subtlety and understatement in his movies. 他總是在自己的電影中運用細膩刻畫和淡化手法。/ He informed us with massive understatement that he was feeling disappointed. 他極力輕描淡寫地跟我們說他感到失望。/ To say he wasn't amused must be the understatement of the year. 說他沒有被逗樂肯定是本年度最委婉的說法。

周邊詞彙補充：
· impediment可指言語障礙（如口吃、結巴）；
· broken可指（非母語）說得緩慢且有很多錯誤的（如結結巴巴的）；不流利的。
