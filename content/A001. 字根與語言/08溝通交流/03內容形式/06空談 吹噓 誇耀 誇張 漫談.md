☀ <span class="category">**空談 漫談：**</span>
<span class="vocabulary">**empty**</span> ['emptɪ] 
<span class="definition">adj. 常用於名詞前，（某人的言語或行動）沒有任何意思的（即空洞的）；說話不算數的，相當於hollow：</span>empty words 空話 / an empty promise 兌現不了的承諾 / an empty gesture aimed at pleasing the crowds 旨在取悅觀眾的裝腔作勢

<span class="vocabulary">**windy**</span> ['wɪndɪ] 
<span class="definition">adj. [非正式，貶] 指講話誇誇其談的、廢話連篇依然講不清楚的：</span>瞭解即可

<span class="vocabulary">**talk**</span> [tɔ:k] 
<span class="definition">n. [U] [非正式]（沒有事實或實際行動支援的）空話；空談：</span>Don’t pay any attention to her--she’s all talk. 別聽她的，她只會動嘴皮子。/ The chairman’s boasts about future profits were just cheap talk. 董事長吹噓將來的利潤，那只是說說而已。/ For all their talk of equality, the boys ended up not doing any cooking. 男孩們張口閉口平等，但是他們最終還是沒做飯。
 
<span class="vocabulary">**ramble**</span> [ˈræmbl]
<span class="definition">vi. ~ (on) (about sb/sth) 漫談，尤指長時間東拉西扯：</span>He had lost track of what he was saying and began to ramble. 他忘了自己的話題，便開始瞎扯起來。/ What is she rambling on about now? 她現在又在東拉西扯些什麼呀？/ Sometimes she spoke sensibly; sometimes she rambled. 有時她講話很有條理，有時則講得漫無邊際。/ It would have been best written in a more concise way as it does tend to ramble. 它的行文確實有些信馬由韁，要是簡潔一些就好了。<span class="definition">n. [C] 東拉西扯的長篇大論：</span>She went into a long ramble about the evils of television. 她開始東拉西扯地大談電視的弊端。

☀ <span class="category">**吹噓 誇耀：**</span>
<span class="vocabulary">**boast**</span> [bəʊst; 美 boʊst]
<span class="definition">vt.&vi. ~ (about/of sth) 自誇；自吹自擂：</span>I don't want to boast, but I can actually speak six languages. 不是我吹噓，我確實能講六種語言。/ She is always boasting about how wonderful her children are. 她總是誇耀她的孩子們多麼出色。/ Carol boasted about her costume. 卡蘿爾炫耀著自己的穿著打扮。/ Sam boasted that she could beat anyone at poker. 薩姆吹噓說打撲克牌誰都贏不了她。/ He openly boasted of his skill as a burglar. 他公然炫耀他的盜竊手法。/ He openly boasted of his talents. 他當眾吹噓自己的才幹。/ Witnesses said Furci boasted that he took part in killing them. 幾位證人聲稱富爾奇曾吹噓自己參與了對他們的屠殺。<span class="definition">n. [C] [常貶] ~ (that…) 誇耀；誇口：</span>Despite his boasts that his children were brilliant, neither of them went to college. 儘管他誇讚他的兩個孩子聰明，他們卻都沒念上大學。/ It was her proud boast that she had never missed a day's work because of illness. 她所引以自豪的是她從未因病而耽誤過一天工作。/ empty, idle boast 空洞的誇耀；無聊的誇耀 / a boast he made about his achievements 他對自己成就的吹噓 / her boast of having seen all the countries of Africa 她吹噓自己去過非洲所有國家
           
<span class="vocabulary">**crow**</span> [krəʊ; 美 kroʊ]
<span class="definition">vi. [貶] ~ (about/over sth) （尤指在其他人不成功時）揚揚自得地誇口，自鳴得意：</span>He won't stop crowing about his victory. 他滔滔不絕地誇耀自己的勝利。/ Edwards is already crowing about his assured victory. 愛德華茲已經在為自己確定無疑的勝利自鳴得意了。/ The company hasn't much to crow about, with sales down compared with last year.公司的銷售額比去年下降了，沒有什麼可誇耀的。/ We've seen them all crowing that the movement is dead. 我們看到他們所有人都為那場運動的覆滅而洋洋自得。

<span class="vocabulary">**parade**</span> [pəˈreɪd]
<span class="definition">vt.&vi. ~ (sb/sth) as sth（使）冒充，偽裝，打扮成好或重要的樣子（雖然實際並非如此）, 也可稱為吹噓、誇耀：</span>The Chancellor will be able to parade his cut in interest rates as a small victory. 財政大臣就能把他的減息舉措當作一場小勝來誇耀了。/ She might have been paraded as a woman who was working hard to change her ways. 她可能已經被吹噓成一個靠努力工作改變命運的女人了。
                      
<span class="vocabulary">**trumpet**</span> [ˈtrʌmpɪt]
<span class="definition">vt. ~ sth (as sth) 自豪或熱情地公開談論某事物，如鼓吹、吹噓：</span>to trumpet sb's achievements 吹噓某人的成就 / Mark Morris, who is trumpeted as the dance talent of his generation 馬克·莫里斯，被吹捧為他那一代的舞蹈奇才

<span class="vocabulary">**aggrandize**</span> [ə'grændaɪz]
<span class="definition">vt. 使某人看起來比實際上更富有、更有權勢或更重要，即吹捧、吹噓：</span>At the dinner table, my father would go on and on, showing off, aggrandising himself. 我父親在餐桌上總是滔滔不絕地炫耀並吹噓自己。
           
<span class="vocabulary">**gush**</span> [gʌʃ]
<span class="definition">vt.&vi. [貶] 過分地表現對某人或某事物的感情（而看起來不真誠）：</span>He gushed about his love for his wife. 他吹噓自己如何愛妻子。
           
<span class="vocabulary">**puff**</span> [pʌf]
<span class="definition">n. [C]（美式也用puff piece）[非正式，常貶] 吹捧的文章（或講話）：</span>What is presented to the public as a critical evaluation is really an elaborate puff for him and his magazine. 公眾所看到的評論界的好評實際上是對他和他的雜誌的一種吹捧。

☀ <span class="category">**誇張 誇大：**</span>
<span class="vocabulary">**exaggerate**</span> [ɪgˈzædʒəreɪt]
<span class="definition">vt.&vi.（將情況、屬性、特徵等）誇張、誇大。主語一般是人：</span>He thinks I'm exaggerating. 他覺得我在誇大其詞。/ Don't exaggerate. 別誇張。/ The hotel was really filthy and I'm not exaggerating. 我不是誇張，這旅店真的很髒。/ He tends to exaggerate the difficulties. 他往往誇大困難。/ John does tend to exaggerate slightly. 約翰往往喜歡把事情略加誇大。/ These figures have been greatly exaggerated. 這些數字已被極度誇大了。/ The historical significance of these events can be easily exaggerated. 這些事件的歷史意義很容易被誇大。/ to artificially exaggerate 人為誇大 / to deliberately exaggerate 故意誇大 / It is difficult to exaggerate the importance of developing good study habits. 養成良好學習習慣的重要性怎麼強調都不過分。/ The allegations were highly exaggerated. 這些斷言大為言過其實。/ Demand for the product has been greatly exaggerated. 對這項產品的需求給過分誇大了。/ I'm sure he exaggerates his Irish accent (= tries to sound more Irish than he really is). 我肯定他把他的愛爾蘭口音故意說得很重。
                 
<span class="vocabulary">**exaggeration**</span> [ɪgˌzædʒəˈreɪʃn]
<span class="definition">n. [C, usually sing., U] 誇張、誇大、言過其實：</span>slight, gross, wild exaggeration 有點誇張；明顯的誇張；胡亂的誇張 / It would be an exaggeration to say I knew her well--I only met her twice. 說我非常瞭解她不免言過其實——我只見過她兩次。/ It's no exaggeration to say that most students have never read a complete Shakespeare play. 說大多數同學從未完整讀過一部莎士比亞戲劇一點也不誇張。/ It is no exaggeration to say that having a baby changes your life. 有了小孩會改變人生，這樣說並不誇張。/ He told his story simply and without exaggeration. 他簡單扼要、毫不誇張地講述了自己的故事。/ There was a degree of exaggeration in his description of events. 他對事件的描述有一定程度的每張。/ John is not usually given to exaggeration. 約翰通常不會誇張。/ Their results, even allowing for exaggeration, are impressive. 就算是有所誇大，他們的結果也是令人印象深刻的。/ There were, without exaggeration, hundreds of applications for the job. 不誇張地講，有幾百人申請這份工作。/ It would be a bit of an exaggeration to say that I'm desperate to leave. 說我渴望離開是有點兒誇張。
           
<span class="vocabulary">**magnify**</span> [ˈmægnɪfaɪ]
<span class="definition">vt. 誇大重要性或嚴重程度。相當於exaggerate：</span>They do not grasp the broad situation and spend their time magnifying ridiculous details. 他們不去把握大局，而是在細枝末節上花費時間大做文章。/ The daring of his exploits had been hugely magnified by constant telling. 他的英勇事蹟幾經傳頌已被極度誇大了。
           
<span class="vocabulary">**inflate**</span> [ɪnˈfleɪt]
<span class="definition">vi. 誇大（數額、效果等，以使某事看起來比實際上好，從而可以從中取利）：</span>They inflated clients' medical treatment to defraud insurance companies. 他們誇大客戶傷勢的嚴重性和治療情形以欺騙保險公司。
           
<span class="vocabulary">**romance**</span> [rəʊˈmæns; ˈrəʊmæns; 美 ˈroʊ-]
<span class="definition">vi. 渲染（某事，使其比原本的真實情況更有趣、更激動人心）：</span>瞭解即可
                      
<span class="vocabulary">**embroider**</span> [ɪmˈbrɔɪdə(r)]
<span class="definition">vt. 對（故事、敘述等）添枝加葉渲染（新增的細節資訊並非總是真實的）：</span>He told some lies and sometimes just embroidered the truth. 他說了些謊，有時只是對事實添油加醋了一番。/ She embroidered on this theme for about ten minutes. 她圍繞這個主題添枝加葉地說了大約10分鐘。
           
<span class="vocabulary">**caricature**</span> [ˈkærɪkətʃʊə(r); 美 -tʃər; -tʃʊr]
<span class="definition">n. 1 [C] 對一個人或事物的誇張的描述：</span>He had unfairly presented a caricature of my views. 他歪曲了我的觀點。/ Hall is angry at what he sees as a caricature of the training offered to modern-day social workers. 在霍爾看來,當代社會工作者所受的培訓被大肆誇大，他感到很氣憤。/ crude, grotesque caricature 粗俗的誇張描述；怪誕的誇張描述 / Most of his characters were mere caricatures. 他刻畫的人物大多極為誇張。/ The two stars have become crude caricatures of themselves. 這兩位明星已扭曲了自己的形象。<span class="definition">2 [U]（寫作的）誇張手法：</span>瞭解即可 <span class="definition">vt. [常用被動式] ~ sb/sth (as sth) 滑稽地描述：</span>She was unfairly caricatured as a dumb blonde. 她被不公正地醜化成了一個傻頭傻腦的金髮女郎。/ Her political career has been caricatured in headlines. 她的政治生涯在頭條新聞裡遭到誇張嘲弄。

<span class="vocabulary">**hysteria**</span> [hɪˈstɪəriə; 美 -ˈstɪr-]
<span class="definition">n. [U] [貶] 特別激動且誇張的行為方式，對某事的誇張反應：</span>the usual media hysteria that surrounds royal visits 媒體對於王室成員訪問的慣常的大肆渲染 / Some parts of the media are creating hysteria and exaggerating an important issue. 部分媒體制造混亂，大肆誇張某個重要的問題。/ public hysteria about the bombings 公眾對炸彈事件的過激反應 / public hysteria about/over AIDS 公眾對艾滋病談虎色變 / There is still so much hysteria surrounding the issue. 對這件事情仍有很多狂熱情緒。

<span class="vocabulary">**dramatic**</span> [drəˈmætɪk]
<span class="definition">adj. 戲劇般誇張的（以創造一種特殊的效果或吸引人們的注意力）：</span>I don't want to sound overly dramatic, but it changed my life. 我不想聽起來太誇張，但它的確改變了我的人生。
