☀ <span class="category">**合理、自然：**</span>
<span class="vocabulary">**reasonable**</span> ['ri:znəbl] 
<span class="definition">adj. 1 reasonable (to do sth) 公平的，實際且明智的，合理的。是一個常規用詞：</span>It is reasonable to assume that he knew beforehand that this would happen. 有理由認為他事先就知道會發生這樣的事。/ Be reasonable! We can’t work late every night. 要講道理呀！我們不能每天晚上都加班到很晚呀。/ reasonable person頭腦正常的人 / reasonable doubt 合理懷疑 <span class="definition">2（在某種情況中）可接受的或合適的：</span>a reasonable price 合理的價格 / to finish within a reasonable time 在合理的時間內完成 / This explanation is perfectly reasonable. 這種解釋完全合理。/ Her request sounded very reasonable. 她的要求聽起來非常合理。
             
<span class="vocabulary">**legitimate**</span> [lɪˈdʒɪtɪmət]
<span class="definition">adj. 有正當和可接受的理由的，即正當的、合理的：</span>a legitimate grievance 合乎情理的抱怨 / It seemed a perfectly legitimate question. 這似乎是完全合乎情理的問題。/ Politicians are legitimate targets for satire. 政客理所當然是諷刺的物件。/ That's a perfectly legitimate fear. 懷有這種恐懼完全在情理之中。/ The New York Times has a legitimate claim to be a national newspaper.《紐約時報》有理由稱自己是一份全國性報紙。          

<span class="vocabulary">**plausible**</span> [ˈplɔ:zəbl]
<span class="definition">adj.（藉口或解釋）有道理的、可能是真的：</span>Her story sounded perfectly plausible. 她的說辭聽起來言之有理。/ That explanation seems entirely plausible to me. 那個解釋在我看來完全說得通。/ The only plausible explanation is that he forgot. 唯一合理的解釋就是他忘掉了。/ He did not think it plausible that all the differences could be explained in this way. 他認為用這種方式解釋所有的區別似乎沒有道理。/ There was no way the story could be made to sound even remotely plausible. 這話再怎麼說都全然說不通。/ This view seems intuitively plausible and has a long history. 這種觀點憑直覺上覺得是合理的，而且有著悠久的歷史。
           
<span class="vocabulary">**justifiable**</span> [ˈdʒʌstɪfaɪəbl; ˌdʒʌstɪˈfaɪəbl]
<span class="definition">adj.（一個行動、情況、感情、主意）因為有正當理由而可接受的，如無可非議的、情有可原的：</span>justifiable pride 無可非議的自豪感 / The violence of the revolutionary years was justifiable on the grounds of political necessity. 出於政治需要，革命年代的暴力行為是情有可原的。/ the strong and justifiable desire of the Baltic States for independence 波羅的海諸國要求獨立的強烈而正當的願望 / We consider this action justifiable. 我們認為這一行動是正當的。/ commercially, economically justifiable 有正當商業理由的；有正當經濟理由的 / ethically, morally justifiable 合乎倫理；合乎道德 / The cutbacks are justifiable on the grounds of cost. 這些削減符合成本方面的考慮。/ The rule is justifiable on safety grounds. 從安全方面考慮，這條規定是合理的。
           
<span class="vocabulary">**justified**</span> [ˈdʒʌstɪfaɪd]
<span class="definition">adj. 1 ~ (in doing sth)（做某事）有正當理由的：</span>economically, ethically, financially, legally, morally, rationally, scientifically, theoretically justified經濟上合理的；合乎倫理的；法律上有理的；合乎道德的；理性的；合乎科學道理的；理論上正確的 / Can her actions be morally justified? 她的行為合乎道德原則嗎? / She felt fully justified in asking for a refund. 她覺得要求退款完全合理。/ He's absolutely justified in resigning. He was treated shamefully. 他完全有理由辭職。他受到了侮辱。<span class="definition">2（決定、行為或想法等）正當的，合理的，事出有因的：</span>In my opinion, the decision was wholly justified. 我認為，這個決定完全合理。/ a logical and easily justified decision 一項合乎邏輯又容易證明合理的決定 / His fears proved justified. 他之所以害怕，證明是有原因的。/ The suspicion proved amply justified. 事實證明這個懷疑非常合理。
           
<span class="vocabulary">**validity**</span> [vəˈlɪdəti]
<span class="definition">n. [U] 符合邏輯性、正確性：</span>We had doubts about the validity of their argument. 我們對他們的論點的正確性有過懷疑。/ Some people, of course, denied the validity of any such claim. 當然，一些人否定了此類說法的合理性。/ Don't you think that both views have equal validity? 難道你不認為這兩種觀點同樣都是合理的嗎?/ The theory has the face validity of being consistent with recent findings. 這一理論與最新的研究結果相一致，表面上是合理的。/ scientific, statistical validity 科學上的合理性；統計上的合理性 / His reputation lends a certain validity to the approach that it might not deserve. 他的名望賦予這一方法某種其本身可能並不具備的可信性。/ to assess the scientific validity of new treatments 評估新療法的科學效用 / The results are of doubtful validity. 這些結果的正確性值得懷疑。/ the theory's validity for parent-child relationships 這一理論在親子關係方面的正確性

<span class="vocabulary">**valid**</span> ['vælɪd] 
<span class="definition">adj.（爭論、評論、想法等）基於合理原因的（如基於邏輯或事實的）：</span>valid reasons, valid assumptions, valid arguments 合理的理由；合理的假設；合理的論證 / a logically valid deduction 符合邏輯的推論 / a valid set of moral principles 一套合理的道德準則 / He made a valid point. 他說得對。/ We accepted several different approaches as valid. 我們承認幾種不同的方法都很有道理。

<span class="vocabulary">**good**</span> [ɡʊd] 
<span class="definition">adj. 合情理的、有邏輯的，或（對正在被討論的內容來說）有充分根據的、有說服力的。是一個簡單用詞：</span>Thank you, good question. 謝謝，問題提得好。/ Yes, that’s a good point. 是的，那是個有說服力的論據。/ good reason 充分理由 / a good idea 好主意

<span class="vocabulary">**sound**</span> [saʊnd] 
<span class="definition">adj. 合理的。也較常用：</span>sound advice, sound proposal 合理的建議 / This is a sound practice. 這是一種正確的做法。/ a sound reason 正當的理由 / to be scientifically sound 科學上合理

<span class="vocabulary">**natural**</span> ['nætʃrəl] 
<span class="definition">adj. 符合人的理性的，即合理的、自然的：</span>natural law 自然規律 / It is only natural for children to play for a long time. 長時間玩耍對小孩子來說是很自然的。/ It’s only natural that she should feel upset. 她感到難過是再正常不過的。
        
<span class="vocabulary">**possible**</span> ['pɒsəbl] 
<span class="definition">adj.（在某種情況下）合理的或可接受的：</span>There are several possible explanations. 有幾種合理的解釋。

<span class="vocabulary">**rational**</span> [ˈræʃnəl]
<span class="definition">adj.（行為、思想等）基於理智（而非感情）的：</span>rational argument, rational choice 合理的論點；合理的選擇 / rational analysis, rational thought 有道理的分析；有道理的思考 / There is no rational explanation for his actions. 對他的所作所為無法做出合理的解釋。/ He's asking you to look at both sides of the case and come to a rational decision. 他是要求你看到問題的兩面後再做出理性的決定。/ Mary was able to short-circuit her stress response by keeping her thoughts calm and rational. 瑪麗能夠保持頭腦清醒理智，因而迅速做出反應。/ It all seemed quite rational to me. 在我看來這似乎全都合情合理。/ With children working from the age of ten, large families were economically rational. 孩子從十歲就開始工作，多要幾個孩子在經濟上考慮是合乎情理的。          
           
<span class="vocabulary">**sensible**</span> [ˈsensəbl]
<span class="definition">adj.（行為或決定）基於理性和經驗（而非感情）做出的，即明智的、合理的：</span>It might be sensible to get a solicitor. 也許應該請一個訴狀律師。/ The sensible thing is to leave them alone. 別去管他們才是明智的。/ I think the sensible thing would be to take a taxi home. 我想還是坐計程車回去的好。/ In the state I was in, this seemed a perfectly sensible remark. 根據我當時的情況，這麼說似乎完全合乎情理。/ Are you going out to search for it at this time of night? It seems hardly sensible. 你打算夜裡這個時候出去找嗎？這好像不那麼明智。/ I think that's a very sensible idea. 我看這個主意很妥當。/ This approach seems very sensible to me. 在我看來，這個方法似乎很明智。/ That advice sounds sensible enough. 那個建議聽起來相當合理。/ Say something sensible. 說點正經的。
                      
<span class="vocabulary">**sane**</span> [seɪn] 
<span class="definition">adj.（制度等）合理的：</span>a sane and safe energy policy 安全合理的能源政策 / the sane way to solve the problem 解決問題的合理方法

<span class="vocabulary">**understandable**</span> [ˌʌndəˈstændəbl; 美 -dərˈs-]
<span class="definition">adj.（行為、感情、反應等）看起來在某種情況中正常合理的，即可以理解的：</span>Their attitude is perfectly understandable. 他們的態度是完全可以理解的。/ His unhappiness was understandable. 他不高興是可以理解的。/ It was an understandable mistake to make. 那是一個情有可原的失誤。/ His frustration is somewhat understandable. 他的苦惱還算可以理解。/ It is certainly understandable that he would be so devoted to his home and family. 他對自己的家庭和家人會盡心盡力，這當然可以理解。
                      
<span class="vocabulary">**explicable**</span> [ɪkˈsplɪkəbl; ˈeksplɪkəbl]
<span class="definition">adj. [名詞前不可用] [正式] 可被理解的：</span>The older I grow, the stranger and less explicable the world appears to me. 年齡越大，世界對我而言就變得越陌生、越難理解。/ The sudden increase in sales is easily explicable.（easily尤為英式用法）銷售額的突然增長是很容易理解的。/ His actions are entirely explicable. 他的行為是完全可以理解的。

<span class="vocabulary">**logical**</span> [ˈlɒdʒɪkl; 美 ˈlɑ:dʒ-]
<span class="definition">adj. 1（行動、事件等）合乎情理的：</span>a logical thing to do in the circumstances 在那種環境下按理應做的事 / It was a logical conclusion from the child's point of view. 從小孩的觀點來看這是個合乎情理的結論。/ Connie suddenly struck her as a logical candidate. 她突然想到康妮是個合適人選。/ There was a logical explanation. 存在一個合理的解釋。/ It all sounds quite logical. 這一切似乎都頗合乎邏輯。<span class="definition">2 邏輯（上）的；（論證或推理方法）符合邏輯的：</span>The issue here is purely logical it has nothing to do with ethics. 這純粹是個邏輯問題，與倫理道德無關。/ Only when each logical step has been checked by other mathematicians will the proof be accepted. 只有每一邏輯步驟都被其他數學家驗證之後，證明才能成立。/ a logical argument 合乎邏輯的論證 / His arguments seemed perfectly logical. 他的論證似乎十分合乎邏輯。/ Computer programming needs someone with a logical mind. 計算機程式設計需要擅長邏輯思維的人。<span class="definition">3（結論或結果）邏輯上必然的，順理成章的：</span>If the climate gets drier, then the logical conclusion is that even more drought will occur. 如果氣候變得更乾燥，必然會出現更多旱情。/ This is the logical result of a long evolution in which we moved from working by the sweat of our brow and by muscle to industrial work and finally to knowledge work. 這是長期進化的必然結果：在這一過程中，我們首先從事汗流浹背的體力勞動，然後是工業勞動，最後發展到腦力勞動。
           
<span class="vocabulary">**logic**</span> [ˈlɒdʒɪk; 美 ˈlɑ:dʒɪk]
<span class="definition">n. 1 [U]（某類人或某個活動領域特有的）思維方式；解釋方法；邏輯：</span>The two parts of the plan were governed by the same logic. 計劃的兩個部分均受到了同一思維方式的制約。/ compelling, impeccable, inexorable logic 令人信服的推理；無懈可擊的邏輯推理；無法改變的推理 / faulty, flawed logic 錯誤的邏輯推理 / In their faulty logic, this is a great injustice. 按照他們的錯誤邏輯，這是嚴重的不公正。/ perverse, strange, twisted logic 荒謬的邏輯；奇怪的邏輯；反常的邏輯 / This is clearly a case of circular logic. 這顯然是迴圈推理。/ The plan had a simple logic to it. 該計劃包含著簡單的邏輯。/ He understood the basic logic of deterrence. 他理解威懾的基本邏輯。/ inherent, inner, internal, underlying logic 內在的邏輯 / The music has its own inner logic. 這音樂有其內在的邏輯。/ business, commercial, economic, political, scientific logic 經營邏輯；商業邏輯；經濟邏輯；政治邏輯；科學邏輯 / It's a stupid decision that completely defies logic. 這個決定很愚蠢，完全違背了邏輯。/ You can't use the same logic in dealing with children. 對孩子你不能用同樣的邏輯。/ They questioned the logic underlying his actions. 他們質疑他行為背後的邏輯。/ What's the logic behind this decision? 這一決定背後的邏輯是什麼? / There doesn't seem to be any logic in the move. 這一行動似乎毫無道理。/ The logic of this argument is very obscure. 這一論據的邏輯很費解。<span class="definition">2 [U, sing.] 道理，邏輯性或合理性：</span>Linking the proposals in a single package did have a certain logic. 把這些提議聯絡起來成為一攬子提議確有一定的道理。/ There is no logic to/in any of their claims. 他們的任何索賠都不具正當的理由。/ I don't follow the logic of your argument. 我不明白你的論點邏輯性何在。/ There would be no logic in upsetting the agreements. 把協議推翻在邏輯上站不住腳。
           
<span class="vocabulary">**clinical**</span> [ˈklɪnɪkl]
<span class="definition">adj.（思想、行為）非常符合邏輯的，不帶感情的：</span>All this questioning is so analytical and clinical--it kills romance. 整個詢問分析性和邏輯性太強，浪漫的氣息蕩然無存。

<span class="vocabulary">**well**</span> [wel] 
<span class="definition">adv. can/could/may/might well 有好的理由、合理地：</span>I can’t very well leave now. 我現在離開不太合適。

相關詞義延伸：
· reasonable還可指（價格）不是太貴的，即公道的。也可指還好但不是非常好的，不錯的，過得去的，還算好的；           
· logic還可指邏輯學。也可指計算機中的邏輯系統、操作規則。

周邊詞彙補充：
· justice可指公平（或合理）的品質，即正當性、公道、合理、公平合理；
· healthy可指正常的，合理的，（態度）明智的；
· understandable可指（行為、感情、反應等）看起來在某種情況中正常合理的，即可以理解的；
· realistic可指（目標或目的等）實際的、明智的、能夠實現的。

☀ <span class="category">**荒誕、怪誕的形容詞：**</span>
<span class="vocabulary">**ridiculous**</span> [rɪ'dɪkjələs] 
<span class="definition">adj. 非常愚蠢或不合理的，荒繆的，荒唐的，相當於absurd或ludicrous。但在口語中ridiculous遠比absurd常用：</span>I look ridiculous in this hat. 我戴這頂帽子看上去很可笑。/ Don’t be ridiculous! You can’t pay £50 for a T-shirt! 別犯傻了！怎麼能花50英鎊買一件T恤衫！/ a sense of the ridiculous（尤英）荒唐感

<span class="vocabulary">**absurd**</span> [əb'sə:d] 
<span class="definition">adj. 完全荒謬的，荒唐的；不合邏輯且不合常理的。相當於ridiculous：</span>That uniform makes him look absurd. 他穿著那種制服看起來怪模怪樣的。/ an absurd idea 荒唐的想法 / His sense of the absurd kept him from becoming too solemn. 他的荒誕感使他不會過於嚴肅。
           
<span class="vocabulary">**lunatic**</span> [ˈlu:nətɪk]
<span class="definition">adj. 荒唐可笑的：</span>lunatic ideas 荒謬的想法

<span class="vocabulary">**bizarre**</span> [bɪˈzɑ:(r)]
<span class="definition">adj. 極其古怪的：</span>bizarre situation, bizarre incident, bizarre story 稀奇古怪的局勢；稀奇古怪的事件；稀奇古怪的故事 / bizarre behaviour 古怪的行為 / The game was also notable for the bizarre behaviour of the team's manager. 這場比賽也因球隊主教練怪異的舉動而盡人皆知。/ You know, that book you lent me is really bizarre. 你知道嗎，你借給我的那本書真的很怪誕。/ I found the whole situation very bizarre. 我覺得整個情況很奇怪。/ It's a pretty bizarre movie. 這是一部頗為怪異的電影。/ He walked off in a most bizarre fashion. 他十分古怪地憤然離去。/ He made some totally bizarre comments. 他的一些評論十分荒誕。

<span class="vocabulary">**laughable**</span> [ˈlɑ:fəbl; 美 ˈlæf-]
<span class="definition">adj. 荒唐可笑的、不值得當真的：</span>The idea that TV shows like 'Dallas' or 'Dynasty' represent typical American life is laughable. 說《豪門恩怨》或《錦繡豪門》等電視劇是美國人生活的典型寫照是很滑稽的。/ He has denied suggestions that he hates all English people, claiming the allegations are 'laughable'. 他矢口否認那些有關他憎恨所有英國人的推測，聲稱這些指控是“荒唐的”。
           
<span class="vocabulary">**hysterical**</span> [hɪˈsterɪkl]
<span class="definition">adj. [非正式] 極其可笑的：</span>She seemed to find my situation absolutely hysterical. 她好像覺得我的處境極端可笑。/ Paul Mazursky was Master of Ceremonies, and he was pretty hysterical. 保羅·馬祖爾斯基擔任司儀，他簡直把人笑壞了。/ The whole episode was absolutely hysterical! 這一集從頭到尾好笑極了！

<span class="vocabulary">**ludicrous**</span> [ˈlu:dɪkrəs]
<span class="definition">adj. 不合理的；不能當真的：</span>a ludicrous suggestion 荒謬的建議 / It was ludicrous to think that the plan could succeed. 認為此計劃會取得成功是荒唐的。/ It was ludicrous to suggest that the visit could be kept secret. 認為此次訪問能夠保密是可笑的。/ It's a completely ludicrous idea. 這主意真是荒唐透頂。/ The whole idea is absolutely ludicrous! 整個想法荒唐至極！/ The plot was so simple, it was almost ludicrous. 情節簡單得近乎荒唐。

<span class="vocabulary">**nonsensical**</span> [nɒnˈsensɪkl; 美 nɑ:n-]
<span class="definition">adj. 荒謬的；無意義的；瞎說的：</span>It seemed to me that Sir Robert's arguments were nonsensical. 在我看來，羅伯特爵士的爭辯純屬瞎說。/ There were no nonsensical promises about reviving the economy. 復甦經濟的承諾是千真萬確的。
            
<span class="vocabulary">**irrational**</span> [ɪˈræʃənl]
<span class="definition">adj.（某人的感情或行為）不合邏輯的、沒有道理的：</span>an irrational fear 無端的恐懼 / an irrational fear of science 對科學的莫名恐懼 / He dismissed her fears as irrational. 他認為她的恐懼毫無道理而未加理睬。/ There was nothing inherently irrational in such a decision. 從根本上看，這樣的決定沒什麼不合理的地方。/ You're being irrational. 你不可理喻。
           
<span class="vocabulary">**illogical**</span> [ɪˈlɒdʒɪkl; 美 -ˈlɑ:dʒ-]
<span class="definition">adj. 不合邏輯的：</span>illogical behaviour, illogical arguments 乖戾的行為；不合邏輯的論點 / She has an illogical fear of insects. 她毫無道理地害怕昆蟲。/ It is illogical to oppose the repatriation of economic migrants. 反對把經濟移民遣送歸國是不合邏輯的。/ She may think your reaction is crazy or illogical, but she cannot deny what you are feeling. 她可能覺得你的反應瘋狂或者不合邏輯，但她不會否認你的感受。
           
<span class="vocabulary">**implausible**</span> [ɪmˈplɔ:zəbl]
<span class="definition">adj. 似乎不合情理的；不像真實的：</span>implausible claim, implausible idea, implausible theory 悖於情理的要求；說不通的思想；有悖情理的理論 / It was all highly implausible. 這毫無道理。/ It seems implausible that the projects would have gone ahead without her backing. 這些專案沒有得到她的支援就開始進行了，這似乎不合情理。/ These results might be considered implausible. 這些結果可能會令人難以相信。/ This idea is totally implausible. 這個想法完全不合情理。/ He gave a series of increasingly implausible excuses. 他給出一串藉口，一個比一個不可信。/ to be biologically implausible, to be psychologically implausible, to be scientifically implausible 不符合生物學的道理；不符合心理學的道理；不符合科學的道理
           
<span class="vocabulary">**unfounded**</span> [ʌnˈfaʊndɪd]
<span class="definition">adj.（謠言、信念、感情等）無事實根據的；沒有理由的：</span>unfounded rumours 無中生有的謠言 / The allegations were totally unfounded. 這些指控完全沒有事實根據。/ Speculation about a divorce proved totally unfounded. 有關離婚的猜測證實純屬無稽之談。/ These claims were completely unfounded. 這些要求完全沒有理由。/ The claim is manifestly unfounded.（manifestly為英式用法，法律用語）這個要求顯然毫無根據。

<span class="vocabulary">**unwarranted**</span> [ʌnˈwɒrəntɪd; 美 -ˈwɔ:r-; -ˈwɑ:r-]
<span class="definition">adj. [正式] 無正當理由的；不必要的：</span>Much of the criticism was totally unwarranted. 這種批評基本上是毫無道理的。/ Any attempt to discuss the issue of human rights was rejected as an unwarranted interference in the country's internal affairs. 任何討論人權問題的嘗試都會被以無端干預該國內政為由加以拒絕。/ He accused the police of using unwarranted brutality. 他指控警方無端施暴。/ The delay did seem unwarranted. 延期看來的確沒有道理。/ a wholly unwarranted smear campaign 完全無中生有的誹謗風潮
           
<span class="vocabulary">**groundless**</span> [ˈgraʊndləs]
<span class="definition">adj.（恐懼、指控、報道等）無理由的、無根據的：</span>groundless allegations 毫無根據的指控 / Our fears proved groundless. 我們的擔心證明是毫無道理的。/ Fears that the world was about to run out of fuel proved groundless. 事實證明對於世界燃料即將耗盡的擔憂毫無根據。/ A ministry official described the report as groundless. 一位部級官員稱這篇報道純屬子虛烏有。/ Our fears proved totally groundless. 事實證明，我們的擔心是完全沒有根據的。
                      
<span class="vocabulary">**unreasonable**</span> [ʌnˈri:znəbl]
<span class="definition">adj. 1（某人的行為）不合理的；不公平的；期望過高的：</span>The job was beginning to make unreasonable demands on his free time. 他的閒暇時間開始被工作過分地侵佔了。/ It would be unreasonable to expect somebody to come at such short notice. 要求人家隨傳隨到，太不近人情了吧。/ He was being totally unreasonable about it. 他在這件事上蠻不講理。/ She did not want to appear unreasonable. 她不想顯得不講道理。/ His demands were becoming more and more unreasonable. 他的要求變得越來越過分。/ I find her a little unreasonable at times. 我發現她有時有點兒不講道理。/ This clause in the contract was held unreasonable. 合同中的這個條款被判定不合理。<span class="definition">2（決策、行動、價格、數量等）不合理的：</span>unreasonable increases in the price of petrol 汽油價格的瘋漲 / The fees they charge are not unreasonable. 他們的收費還算合理。/ One in four consumers now say water prices are very unreasonable. 四分之一的消費者認為現在的水費很不合理。
           
<span class="vocabulary">**unprovoked**</span> [ˌʌnprəˈvəʊkt; 美 -ˈvoʊkt]
<span class="definition">adj.（尤指攻擊）無端發生的，非因被攻擊者的言行而起的：</span>an act of unprovoked aggression 無端的侵犯行為 / a totally unprovoked attack無端攻擊 / Her angry outburst was totally unprovoked. 她的暴跳如雷完全是無理取鬧。

<span class="vocabulary">**preposterous**</span> [prɪˈpɒstərəs; 美 -ˈpɑ:s-]
<span class="definition">adj. [正式] 完全不合理的，尤指讓人震驚或惱怒，即荒謬的：</span>These claims are absolutely preposterous! 這些要求簡直荒謬絕倫！/ their preposterous claim that they had unearthed a plot 他們無比愚蠢地聲稱自己發現了一個陰謀 / The whole idea was preposterous. 整個想法都荒唐透頂。/ preposterous suggestion 荒唐的建議
           
<span class="vocabulary">**grotesque**</span> [grəʊˈtesk; 美 groʊ-]
<span class="definition">adj. 怪誕的、荒唐的、荒謬的（這種荒誕通常讓人不悅）：</span>a grotesque distortion of the truth 對事實的荒誕歪曲 / It's grotesque to expect a person of her experience to work for so little money. 想讓她那樣有經驗的人為這點錢工作真是荒唐。/ the grotesque disparities between the wealthy few and nearly everyone else. 少數富人與幾乎所有其他人之間懸殊的貧富差距 / a country where grotesque abuses are taking place. 發生駭人聽聞的虐待事件的國家 / She has become a grotesque parody of her former elegant self. 她成為了以前那個舉止優雅的自己的怪誕模仿品. / grotesque caricature 荒誕的漫畫

<span class="vocabulary">**fantastic**</span> [fæn'tæstɪk] 
<span class="definition">adj. 也可用不太常用的fantastical，常用於名詞前，奇怪且表現出很多想象力的，如怪誕的、荒誕不經的，相當於weird。不太常用：</span>fantastic dreams of jungles 關於叢林的怪夢 / It may sound rather fantastic, but it’s the truth. 這聽起來可能相當離奇，但事實如此。

<span class="vocabulary">**crazy**</span> ['kreɪzɪ] 
<span class="definition">adj. [尤美，非正式] 不理智的，愚蠢的，可稱為瘋狂的：</span>She must be crazy to lend him money. 她把錢借給他，一定是瘋了。/ What a crazy idea! 這個想法真荒唐！/ I know it sounds crazy but it just might work. 我知道這事聽起來很蠢，然而也許就會奏效。

<span class="vocabulary">**silly**</span> ['sɪlɪ] 
<span class="definition">adj.（尤指像小孩一樣）愚蠢的，荒唐可笑的，冒傻氣的，相當於ridiculous。可看作是一個非正式用法：</span>a silly sense of humour 無聊的幽默感 / a silly game 無聊的遊戲 / a silly grin 憨笑 / I feel silly in these clothes. 穿上這些衣服，我覺得很可笑。

相關詞義延伸：
· preposterous還可指離奇古怪而愚蠢（或令人震驚）的。

周邊詞彙補充：

· circular可指（論點或理論）迴圈論證的（以一種觀點證明另一觀點，接著再用後一種觀點反過來去證明前一觀點）。

<span class="vocabulary">**absurd**</span> [əb'sə:d] 
<span class="definition">n. [sing.] the absurd 可指荒誕（或看起來荒誕）的事物：</span>He has a good sense of the absurd. 他對荒誕事物有較強的識別能力。

<span class="vocabulary">**joke**</span> [dӡəʊk] 
<span class="definition">n. [sing.] [非正式] 荒唐、惱人、可笑、不能得到嚴肅對待的一個人、事物或情況：</span>This latest pay offer is a joke. 最近這次提出的報酬簡直是開玩笑。
           
<span class="vocabulary">**nonsense**</span> [ˈnɒnsns; 美 ˈnɑ:nsens; -sns]
<span class="definition">n. [U, C] 被認為荒謬或錯誤的想法、言論或信仰，如謬論、胡扯、胡言亂語：</span>Reports that he has resigned are nonsense. 有關他已經辭職的報道是無稽之談。/ You're talking nonsense! 你在胡說八道！/ ‘I won't go. ’ ‘Nonsense! You must go!’ “我不想去。”“胡扯！你一定得去！”/ It's nonsense to say they don't care. 說他們不在意那是瞎扯。/ The idea is an economic nonsense. 這種觀點是經濟學上的謬論。/ Most orthodox doctors however dismiss this as complete nonsense. 但大多數傳統的醫生認為此說法完全是胡說八道。/ all that poetic nonsense about love 所有那些關於愛情的充滿詩情畫意的假話 / Most of his theories are arrant nonsense.（arrant為舊時用法）他的大多數理論都是胡說八道。/ You don't believe that superstitious nonsense, do you? 你不相信那些迷信的無稽之談，是吧？/ Don't talk nonsense! 別胡扯！/ Just stop this nonsense of refusing to talk to anybody. 不要再做這種拒絕與人說話的傻事了。/ What's all this nonsense about you giving up your job? 這些關於你要辭職的胡扯到底是怎麼回事？/ People are talking a lot of nonsense about him being the new Michael Jordan. 人們說他是新一代邁克爾·喬丹，淨是胡說八道。/ This decision makes nonsense of all our hard work. 這一決定使我們的全部辛勞完全白費。
          
<span class="vocabulary">**farce**</span> [fɑ:s; 美 fɑ:rs]
<span class="definition">n. 1 [U]（表演或寫作的一種風格）荒誕、滑稽：</span>The plot often borders on farce. 情節常常近乎荒誕。<span class="definition">2 [C] 不公平（或某活動組織得很糟糕）以致很荒唐的一種情況或一次事件，算是一種比喻意義上的鬧劇：</span>The trial was a complete farce. 這次審判完全是一場鬧劇。/ The elections have been reduced to a farce. 競選演變為一場鬧劇。/ The whole procedure has become a complete farce. 整個過程完全成了一場鬧劇。/ The debate degenerated into farce when opposing speakers started shouting at each other. 雙方辯手開始朝對方大喊，辯論成了一場鬧劇。           
           
<span class="vocabulary">**pantomime**</span> [ˈpæntəmaɪm]
<span class="definition">n. [C, usually sing.] [英]（常伴有很多混亂的）滑稽可笑的局面、鬧劇：</span>They were made welcome with the usual pantomime of exaggerated smiles and gestures. 他們受到了一如往常的歡迎，誇張的笑容和姿態如同一場鬧劇。/ The rights of every American to good government have been damaged by the pantomime on Capitol Hill. 每一位美國人享受良政的權利被國會山的鬧劇損害殆盡。

<span class="vocabulary">**antics**</span> [ˈæntɪks]

相關詞義延伸：
· silly還可指不適用的或不嚴肅的，如鬧著玩的。也可常用於向孩子指出其愚蠢行為，如傻孩子。
