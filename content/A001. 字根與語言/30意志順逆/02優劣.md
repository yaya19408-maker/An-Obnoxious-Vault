<span class="vocabulary">**advantage**</span> [əd'vɑːntɪdӡ] 
<span class="definition">n. 1 [C, U] advantage (over sb) 幫助自己（這個“自己”，可以是人，也可以是事物）並使自己比其他人更好（或更成功）的一個東西，可稱為優勢，有利條件，有利因素等：</span>Being tall gave him an advantage over the other players. 他個子高，比其他運動員有優勢。/ The new design gives us a clear advantage over our competitors. 新的設計使我們比競爭對手有明顯的優勢。/ competitive, educational, psychological, technological advantage 競爭優勢；教育優勢；心理優勢；技術優勢 / cost, price, speed, tax advantage 成本優勢；價格優勢；速度優勢；稅收優勢 <span class="definition">2 [C] 使某事物本身變得更好（或更有用）的一種品質，即優點：</span>A small car has the added advantage of being cheaper to run. 小型轎車還有一個優點是養護成本比較便宜。/ the advantages of living in a small town 在小城鎮居住的好處 / The bright lighting showed the jewels to their best advantage. 明亮的燈光將那些珠寶襯托得格外好看。
           
<span class="vocabulary">**superiority**</span> [su:ˌpɪəriˈɒrəti; sju:-; 美 su:ˌpɪriˈɔ:r-; -ˈɑ:r-]
<span class="definition">n. [U] ~ (in sth) / ~ (to/over sth/sb) 優越性、優勢性，尤指戰爭或衝突中具有的優勢或優越性：</span>military superiority 軍事上的優勢 / The US will need a three-to-one superiority in forces to be sure of a successful attack. 美國需要在軍力上具有三比一的優勢才能確保進攻勝利。/ We have air superiority. 我們有空中優勢。/ the enemy's attempts to establish air superiority 敵軍確立空中優勢的企圖 / to have naval superiority (= more ships/planes than the enemy) 有海上優勢 / biological, numerical, physical, technical, technological superiority 生理優勢；數量優勢；體力優勢；技術優勢；科技優勢 / We should make use of our superiority in numbers. 我們應該利用我們的數量優勢。/ the company's clear technological superiority over its rivals 這家公司相對於競爭對手的明顯技術優勢 / For a long time France enjoyed overwhelming superiority in this field. 在很長一段時間裡，法國在這個領域具有壓倒性優勢。/ Their team has had to acknowledge Australia's superiority in recent years. 近幾年他們隊不得不對澳大利亞隊俯首稱臣。/ inherent, innate, natural superiority 內在的優越性；天生的優越性；天然的優越性 / the superiority of this operating system 這種作業系統的優越性 / Driving the Jaguar gave him a feeling of effortless superiority. 開著捷豹汽車，他感到一種不費吹灰之力的優越性。
           
<span class="vocabulary">**supremacy**</span> [su:ˈpreməsi; British English also sju:-]
<span class="definition">n. [U] 優勢：</span>The company has established total supremacy over its rivals. 公司奠定了對競爭對手的絕對優勢。/ Coal gave the region industrial supremacy. 煤炭給這個地區帶來了工業上的優勢。/ In the United States Open final, Graf retained overall supremacy. 在美國網球公開賽決賽中，格拉芙始終保持優勢。

<span class="vocabulary">**strength**</span> [streŋθ] 
<span class="definition">n. [C] 優勢；優點；長處：</span>The ability to keep calm is one of her many strengths. 能夠保持冷靜是她的多項長處之一。/ the strengths and weaknesses of an argument 一個論點的有力之處與薄弱之處 / individual, personal, unique strength 個人的長處；獨一無二的優勢 / relative strength 相對優勢 / core, key strength 核心優勢；關鍵優勢
           
<span class="vocabulary">**merit**</span> [ˈmerɪt]
<span class="definition">n. 1 [U] [正式] 好的且值得讚揚、獎賞或欽佩的品質，如美德、優點：</span>She received a merit award for outstanding work. 她因為工作表現突出而獲得了優秀獎。/ Films are given a rating of one to five stars according to merit. 影片根據優劣按一到五星評級。/ Prizes are awarded entirely on merit. 獎項完全根據表現來頒發。/ There is lots of merit to the idea. 這個想法有許多優點。/ I can see no merit in excluding the child from school. 我看不出開除這個孩子的學籍有什麼好處。/ The winners are ranked in order of merit.（英）獲勝者按照成績排序。<span class="definition">2 [C, usually pl.] 值得讚揚、獎賞或欽佩的品特徵，如優點、長處：</span>They weighed up the relative merits of the four candidates. 他們對四名候選人各自的優點作了比較。/ We need to consider the relative merits of both makes of dishwasher. 我們需要權衡兩種品牌洗碗機相比較而言的優點。/ We need to assess the merits of both proposals before making our decision. 做出決定之前，我們需要對兩個提案的優缺點進行評估。/ I began to question the merits of these new technologies. 我開始質疑這些新技術的優點。/ They have been persuaded of the merits of peace. 他們被勸服，認識到了和平的好處。/ the technical merits of a film 一部電影技術上的長處

<span class="vocabulary">**plus**</span> [plʌs]

<span class="vocabulary">**edge**</span> [edӡ] 
<span class="definition">n. <span class="vocabulary">**virtue**</span> ['və:tju:] 
<span class="definition">n. [C, U] 一種吸引人的或有用的品質，如優點、長處、用處，相當於advantage，不如advantage常用（但比beauty常用）：</span>The plan has the virtue of simplicity. 這項計劃的優點是簡單明瞭。/ They could see no virtue in discussing it further. 他們看不到再討論下去有什麼用處。/ the virtues of the free market 自由市場的種種好處

<span class="vocabulary">**ground**</span> [ɡraʊnd]

<span class="vocabulary">**positive**</span> ['pɒzɪtɪv]

<span class="vocabulary">**beauty**</span> ['bju:tɪ] 
<span class="definition">n. [C] 令人愉悅的特徵，即好處，優點，相當於advantage。但遠不如advantage常用：</span>One of the beauties of living here is that it’s so peaceful. 在這裡生活的好處之一是安寧。/ The project will require very little work to start up; that’s the beauty of it. 這項工程幾乎不需要啟動工作，好就好在這裡。

<span class="vocabulary">**superior**</span> [sju:'pɪərɪə] 
<span class="definition">adj. superior (to sb/sth) 在品質上比其他…更好的（或更強於…的）：</span>Liverpool were clearly the superior team. 利物浦隊明顯更勝一籌。/ The enemy won because of their superior numbers. 敵人由於在人數上佔優而取勝。/ What is it that makes this technique superior? 這種技術的優越之處是什麼？/ genetically, intellectually superior 基因上佔優勢；智力上佔優勢

<span class="vocabulary">**strong**</span> [strɒŋ] 
<span class="definition">adj. 擅長於某事的：</span>It’s not my strong point. 那不是我的強項。/ Exports was the only strong point in the economy of our country. 出口曾是我國經濟中的唯一強項。/ The film has a very strong cast. 這部電影演員陣容很強大。

<span class="vocabulary">**plus**</span> [plʌs] 
<span class="definition">adj. 只作前置定語，用於描述你所認為的（某事物的）優點：</span>One of the hotel’s plus points is that it is very central. 那個旅館的優勢之一是它處於市中心。/ On the plus side, all the staff are enthusiastic. 好的方面是職員的工作熱忱都很高。

<span class="vocabulary">**advantage**</span> [əd'vɑːntɪdӡ]

<span class="vocabulary">**disadvantage**</span> [dɪsəd'vɑːntɪdʒ] 
<span class="definition">n. ☀ <span class="category">**劣勢的其它詞性：**</span>
<span class="vocabulary">**defective**</span> [dɪˈfektɪv]
<span class="definition">adj. 有缺點的、有缺陷的：</span>Her hearing was found to be slightly defective. 經檢查，她的聽力有點缺陷。/ Her vision/sight is severely defective. 她的視力有嚴重缺陷。/ to be mentally defective 精神缺陷 / defective goods 有缺陷的商品 / If the goods prove defective, the customer has the right to compensation. 如果貨品證明有缺陷，顧客有權索賠。/ Retailers can return defective merchandise. 零售商可以退回有缺陷的商品。
      
<span class="vocabulary">**deficient**</span> [dɪˈfɪʃnt]
<span class="definition">adj. [正式] 不夠好的，如有缺點的、有缺陷的：</span>to be intellectually deficient, to be mentally deficient 智力缺陷；心智不健全 / Deaf people are sometimes treated as being mentally deficient. 耳聾的人有時被看作智力不健全。/ deficient landing systems 存在缺陷的降落系統 / An educational system which fails to teach basic arithmetic is seriously deficient. 一個不教基礎算術知識的教育體制是有嚴重缺陷的。
         
<span class="vocabulary">**flawed**</span> [flɔ:d]
<span class="definition">adj. 有缺點的；有瑕疵的；被破壞的：</span>the book's flawed heroine 書中有弱點的女主角 / the unique beauty of a flawed object 有瑕疵物品的獨特美 / These tests were so seriously flawed as to render the results meaningless. 這些測試存在嚴重缺陷，致使結果變得毫無意義。/ a series of fatally flawed judgements 一系列有致命缺陷的判斷 / The study was methodologically flawed. 這項研究在方法上有缺陷。
           
<span class="vocabulary">**imperfect**</span> [ɪmˈpɜ:fɪkt; 美 -ˈpɜ:rf-]
<span class="definition">adj. 不完美的、有缺陷的：</span>We live in an imperfect world. 我們生活在一個並不完美的世界上。/ Our understanding of cancer remains imperfect. 我們對癌症的瞭解依然有待提高。/ an imperfect understanding of English 對英語的不透徹理解 / He spoke in very imperfect English. 他操著一口非常不標準的英語。/ All our sale items are slightly imperfect. 我們所有的廉價商品都稍有缺陷。/ These goods are slightly imperfect. 這些貨物略有瑕疵。/ What follows is an admittedly imperfect analogy. 下面是一個確實不算恰如其分的類比。

<span class="vocabulary">**weak**</span> [wi:k] 
<span class="definition">adj. weak point/spot 可指（一個人的性格、行為方面、論點、論據等的）易受攻擊或批評之處，或不擅長之處，即弱點；缺點；不足之處；軟肋：</span>The team’s weak points are in defence. 這個隊的弱點在防守上。/ Geography was my weak subject. 地理課是我的弱項。/ morally weak 道德上薄弱 / Do you think the prosecution made any particular errors, or did they just have a weak case? 你認為控方是真的出了紕漏，還是隻是證據不足？/ The essay was rather weak on detail. 這篇文章不夠細緻。

<span class="vocabulary">**minus**</span> ['maɪnəs] 
<span class="definition">adj. 使…顯得負面的（或不好的），或使其顯得缺乏吸引力：</span>What are the car’s minus points (= the disadvantages)? 這車的缺點是什麼？/ On the minus side, rented property is expensive and difficult to find. 不利因素是租房又貴又不好找。

<span class="vocabulary">**thin**</span> [θɪn] 
<span class="definition">adj. 局勢變得不利的：</span>The team is dangerously thin at wide receiver. 該（美式橄欖球）隊的外接手位置是個致命軟肋。/ You can spread yourself too thin, often leading to poor choices. 你可能會樣樣都抓，結果往往會樣樣抓瞎。/ His management team was stretched thin. 他的管理團隊管的事兒太多，力不從心。

<span class="vocabulary">**fault**</span> [fɔ:lt] 
<span class="definition">vt. 常與can和could連用於否定句，找出某人或某事物的一個缺點，相當於criticize：</span>Her colleagues could not fault her dedication to the job. 她的同事認為她的敬業精神是無可挑剔的。/ He had always been polite--she couldn’t fault him on that. 他總是彬彬有禮——她在這方面對他無可指摘。

相關詞義延伸：
· weakness還可指缺乏力量、權力、影響力或決心的，如軟弱、虛弱、疲軟、衰弱、懦弱等；
· fault還可指網球中的一次發球失誤。
