☀ <span class="category">**困難、麻煩、考驗、挑戰的名詞：**</span>
<span class="vocabulary">**difficulty**</span> ['dɪfɪkəltɪ] 
<span class="definition">n. 1 [C, usually pl., U] 一個問題；造成麻煩的一件事情或一種情況，如困難、難題等（相對於主體來說，是造成麻煩的一個客體）：</span>the difficulties of English syntax 英語句法的難點 / The bank is in difficulty/difficulties. 這家銀行處境艱難。/ economic, financial difficulty 經濟困難；財務困難 / practical, technical difficulty 實際困難；技術性難題 / learning difficulty 學習困難 / I’m having difficulty with the engine. 我的引擎出了問題。<span class="definition">2 [U] 做事（或理解）過程中的艱難、困難、費勁（是一種做事的狀態）；某事所涉及的努力或辛苦：</span>I had considerable difficulty (in) persuading her to leave. 我費了好大的勁兒說服她離開。/ He spoke slowly and with great difficulty. 他話說得很慢，而且很吃力。/ We had enormous difficulty in getting hold of the right equipment. 我們為找合適的裝置費了很大的勁兒。<span class="definition">3 [U] 某事的困難程度，即難度：</span>varying levels of difficulty 不同的難度 / questions of increasing difficulty 難度不斷增加的問題
                      
<span class="vocabulary">**killer**</span> [ˈkɪlə(r)]
<span class="definition">n. [C] [非正式] 極難的事：</span>The exam was a real killer. 這考試可真費勁。
           
<span class="vocabulary">**delicacy**</span> [ˈdelɪkəsi]
<span class="definition">n. [U] 棘手且某人極易被冒犯的一種情況：</span>I need to talk to you about a matter of some delicacy. 我需要與你談個有點棘手的問題。/ He sensed the delicacy of the situation. 他感覺到了形勢的微妙。

<span class="vocabulary">**enormity**</span> [ɪˈnɔ:məti; 美 ɪˈnɔ:rm-]
<span class="definition">n. [U] the ~ of sth（困難、任務、責任等的）艱鉅性：</span>He was anxious about the enormity of the task ahead. 他為眼前任務的艱鉅性而擔憂

<span class="vocabulary">**odds**</span> [ɒdz; 美 ɑ:dz]
<span class="definition">n. [pl.]（對於做某事或達成某事）不利的條件、掣肘的事情：</span>She struggled against terrible odds to overcome her illness. 她奮力與可怕的困境抗爭去戰勝疾病。/ She defied the odds to beat the clear favourite. 她克服困難擊敗了最有希望的奪冠者。/ The film is a heart-warming tale of triumph against the odds. 電影講述了一個克服重重困難獲得成功的感人故事。/ The odds against making a profit in this business are huge. 在這一行業要實現盈利有巨大的困難。/ Against all the odds, we managed to get through to the final. 我們克服重重困難進入了決賽。/ He will try hard to win, although he knows the odds are stacked against him. 儘管知道困難重重，他仍將盡力爭取獲勝。

<span class="vocabulary">**hardship**</span> ['hɑːdʃɪp] 
<span class="definition">n. [U, C]（由於沒有足夠的錢、食物、衣服等而）艱難且令人不快的一種情形，可稱為艱難、困苦、拮据等：</span>economic, personal, physical hardship 經濟困難；個人磨難；身體上的磨難 / They are living in genuine hardship. 他們確實生活困難。/ The cold was no real hardship to me. 我並不覺得冷到難熬的地步。
                      
<span class="vocabulary">**rigour**</span> [ˈrɪgə(r)]
<span class="definition">n. [pl.] the rigours of sth 條件的艱苦或嚴酷：</span>The plants were unable to withstand the rigours of a harsh winter. 這些植物經受不住嚴冬的考驗。/ The thick coat of the mountain goat protects it against the rigours of winter. 石山羊厚厚的皮毛可以抵禦冬天的嚴寒。/ He found the rigours of the tour too demanding. 他發現這次旅行非常艱苦。/ the rigours of childbirth 分娩的艱辛 / computers that are subjected to the rigours of the office environment 糟糕辦公環境中使用的電腦 / The town managed to escape the rigours of war. 這個城鎮設法逃過了戰爭浩劫。/ He quickly proved he could handle the rigours of the job. 他很快就證明了他能夠應對這個艱苦的工作。

<span class="vocabulary">**austerity**</span> [ɒˈsterəti; ɔ:ˈster-]
<span class="definition">n. 1 [U] 苦行；禁慾：</span>the austerity of the monks' life 僧侶的禁慾生活 <span class="definition">2 [C, usually pl.] 艱苦樸素的生活：</span>the austerities of wartime Europe 戰時歐洲的艱苦生活

<span class="vocabulary">**trouble**</span> ['trʌbl] 
<span class="definition">n. 1 [U, C] trouble (with sb/sth) 問題；憂慮；困難等；造成問題、憂慮或困難等的一種情況：</span>family, marital trouble 家庭問題；婚姻問題 / financial trouble 財務困境 / legal, political trouble 法律困境；政治困境 / boyfriend trouble 男朋友的煩惱 / We have trouble getting staff. 我們在招聘僱員方面有困難。/ Our troubles aren’t over yet. 我們的麻煩還沒有完呢。<span class="definition">2 [U] 困難（或危險）的處境；可能受到批評（或懲罰）的一種情形：</span>The company ran into trouble early on, when a major order was cancelled. 這家公司早些時候有一個大訂單被取消，因此陷入了困境。/ A yachtsman got into trouble off the coast and had to be rescued. 一個駕駛帆船的人在海上遇險需要救援。/ My brother was always getting me into trouble with my parents. 以前我弟弟經常連累我遭父母的責難。<span class="definition">3 [U] 一種憤怒或暴力的情形，如紛爭、騷亂等：</span>The police were expecting trouble after the match. 警方預料比賽後會有騷亂。/ If you’re not in by midnight, there’ll be trouble. 你要是半夜前不回家，我就讓你有好戲看。/ crowd trouble（尤英）人群騷亂 / There was trouble brewing among the workforce. 工人中間正醞釀著一場風暴。/ trouble spots 動亂地區 / trouble between the races 種族之間的紛爭 <span class="definition">4 [U] trouble (to sb) 額外的努力（或工作），可稱為麻煩，相當於bother：</span>I don’t want to make trouble for her. 我不想給她添麻煩。/ to save trouble 免去麻煩 / Making your own yogurt is more trouble than it’s worth. 自己做酸奶很麻煩，不值得。

<span class="vocabulary">**bother**</span> ['bɒðə] 
<span class="definition">n. [U] 麻煩或困難：</span>I don’t want to put you to any bother. 我不想給你添亂子。/ ‘Thanks for your help!’ ‘It was no bother.’ “多謝你的幫助！”“沒什麼。”/ Call them and save yourself the bother of going round. 給他們打個電話就免得你親自去了。/ I wouldn’t go to the bother of making the cakes myself. 我不會找麻煩自己做蛋糕的。/ He’s in a bit of bother with the police. 他跟警方有點兒麻煩。
                      
<span class="vocabulary">**inconvenience**</span> [ˌɪnkənˈvi:niəns]
<span class="definition">n. 1 [U] 不便、麻煩、困難：</span>We apologize for the delay and regret any inconvenience it may have caused. 我們對此次延誤以及因此有可能造成的所有不便表示道歉。/ I have already been put to considerable inconvenience. 我已經被添了相當的麻煩了。/ The rail strike is likely to cause considerable public inconvenience. 鐵路工人罷工很可能給公眾造成相當大的不便。/ I chose a different route to avoid the inconvenience of going through the town. 我選擇了另一條路線，以避開穿城而過的不便。/ I don't want to put you to any inconvenience. 我不想給你帶來任何不便。/ You could have picked me up at the airport and saved me the inconvenience of having to take the bus! 你本來可以去機場接我，省得我坐公共汽車這麼不方便!/ This store is closed today for staff training. We regret any inconvenience caused. 因員工培訓本店今日歇業，造成不便謹此致歉。/ Every effort will be made to minimize inconvenience to customers while work is in progress. 施工期間，會盡一切努力將給顧客造成的不便降到最低。<span class="definition">2 [C] 造成不便的人或事物：</span>I can put up with minor inconveniences. 我能忍受些小的不便。/ The practical inconveniences of long hair are negligible. 長髮在實際生活中產生的不便可以忽略不計。/ This isn't a mere inconvenience; it's dangerous. 這可不單單是不便，這是危險。

<span class="vocabulary">**hassle**</span> [ˈhæsl]
<span class="definition">n. [C, U] [非正式] 困難；麻煩：</span>Send them an email─it's a lot less hassle than phoning. 給他們發個電郵吧，這比打電話省事多了。/ I don't think it's worth the money or the hassle. 我認為不值得為此花費那麼多金錢和精力。/ Weddings are so much hassle that you need a good break afterwards. 婚禮真是件折騰人的事，辦完後得好好休息才行。/ They faced interminable legal hassles if they wanted to claim compensation. 如果他們要求賠償，就會面臨曠日持久的法律糾紛。/ I started to get all this hassle from my boss about increasing productivity. 老闆就提高生產率的問題開始煩我個沒完。/ He gave me so much hassle I decided it wasn't worth it. 他讓我煩不勝煩，我決定不費那個事了。/ It saves a lot of hassle if you buy them on the Internet. 網購可以減少很多麻煩。/ Camping holidays aren't really worth all the hassle. 野營度假麻煩多多，其實不值。/ I don't want the hassle of opening a new bank account. 我不想費事新開一個銀行賬戶。/ She got the computer set up with no hassle at all. 她沒有遇到一點兒麻煩就裝好了電腦。/ I've had so much hassle over this business. 這筆生意讓我遇到了那麼多的麻煩。/ Package tours take all the hassle out of travel arrangements. 包價旅遊能省去自己安排出遊的所有麻煩。
                      
<span class="vocabulary">**snag**</span> [snæg]
<span class="definition">n. [C]（尤指小的、隱藏的或意外的）問題、困難：</span>There is just one small snag─where is the money coming from? 只有一個小問題——錢從哪兒來？/ Let me know if you run into any snags. 要是遇到什麼麻煩就告訴我。/ A police clampdown on car thieves hit a snag when villains stole one of their cars. 警方打擊偷車賊的行動遭遇了一點小挫折：匪徒偷走了一輛警車。/ The school deals exclusively with children of high academic ability. There is a snag though, it costs £9,600 a year. 這所學校只招收學習能力強的孩子，不過還有個小問題——它每年的學費高達9,600 英鎊。/ A minor snag is that it's expensive. 一個小問題就是價格太高。/ There is just one snag—-his boss might not let him go. 只有一個問題，就是他的老闆可能不放他走。/ The only snag is the price.價格是唯一的障礙。/ Weve hit a technical snag—the printer isn't compatible with my PC. 我們遇到了一個技術問題∶印表機和我的個人電腦不相容。/ one of the snags in the plan 這個方案的問題之一 / I ran into a few snags on my thesis. 我的論文遇到了些麻煩。/ There is a snag to the job: you have to work at weekends. 這份工作的缺點是週末得上班。/ I suddenly saw a major snag with the whole idea. 我忽然發現整個想法存在一個大問題。

<span class="vocabulary">**woe**</span> [wəʊ; 美 woʊ]
<span class="definition">n. [pl] [舊時或幽默] woes 指某人的麻煩或問題、困難：</span>financial woes 財政困難 / Thanks for listening to my woes. 謝謝您聽我訴說不幸的遭遇。/ He did not tell his relatives and friends about his woes. 他沒有告訴親友自己的煩惱。
           
<span class="vocabulary">**tribulation**</span> [ˌtrɪbjuˈleɪʃn]
<span class="definition">n. [C, U] [文學或幽默] 巨大的麻煩、憂患、苦難、磨難、痛苦：</span>the tribulations of modern life 現代生活的苦惱 / the trials and tribulations of everyday life 日常生活的磨鍊與艱難

<span class="vocabulary">**test**</span> [test] 
<span class="definition">n. [C]（對實力等的）考驗：</span>It will be a major test of your patience. 這將是對你耐心的極大考驗。/ The calls for tax reform pose a severe test for the government. 稅改呼聲對政府構成了嚴峻的考驗。/ The pay dispute has really put her management skills to the test. 工資爭端使她的管理能力受到了極大考驗。/ political test 政治考驗 / to stand the test of time 經得起時間的考驗

<span class="vocabulary">**challenge**</span> ['tʃælɪndӡ] 
<span class="definition">n. 1 [C]（可以考驗某人的能力和技能的）一項新的或困難的任務，可稱為挑戰：</span>the biggest challenge of his acting career 他演藝生涯中的最大挑戰 / to face a challenge 面臨挑戰 / economic, environmental, intellectual, technical challenge 經濟上的挑戰；環境上的挑戰；智力上的挑戰；技術上的挑戰 <span class="definition">2 [C]（對某人所下的）挑戰書（以邀請其參加一場比賽、一場打鬥等）：</span>a title challenge 稱號挑戰賽；冠軍挑戰賽 / I accepted his challenge to a game of chess. 我接受他的挑戰，與他下一盤棋。/ a challenge from the other political party 另一政黨發出的挑戰

<span class="vocabulary">**mess**</span> [mes] 
<span class="definition">n. [C, usually sing.] 充滿問題或麻煩的一種處境（通常由於組織得欠佳或某人所犯下的一些錯誤），可稱為麻煩、困境、混亂：</span>economic, financial, emotional mess 經濟困境；財務上的混亂狀態；感情上的混亂狀態 / My life’s becoming a big mess. 我的生活正變為一團亂麻。/ I’ve really made a mess of things! 我真把事情搞糟了！/ The whole situation is a mess. 整個情形一團糟。/ Let’s try to sort out the mess. 我們來收拾一下殘局吧。
           
<span class="vocabulary">**palaver**</span> [pəˈlɑ:və(r); North American English also -ˈlæv-]
<span class="definition">n. [U, sing.] [英] [非正式]（尤指瑣事所引起的）很多麻煩、忙亂：</span>What's all the palaver about? 這些雞毛蒜皮的事到底是為什麼？/ What a palaver it is, trying to get a new visa! 申請新簽證真煩死人了！

<span class="vocabulary">**struggle**</span> ['strʌɡl] 
<span class="definition">n. <span class="vocabulary">**rub**</span> [rʌb]
<span class="definition">n. [sing.] [正式或幽默] the rub可指問題或困難：</span>The hotel is in the middle of nowhere and there lies the rub. We don't have a car. 難就難在旅館很偏遠，我們又沒有汽車。/ 'What do you want to write about?'. And there was the rub, because I didn't yet know. “你想寫什麼？”難就難在這兒，因為我還不知道寫什麼。
 
<span class="vocabulary">**upset**</span> [ʌp'set] 
<span class="definition">n. [U]（尤指意外的）出現一些問題或困難的情況：</span>The company has survived the recent upset in share prices. 這家公司撐過了最近股價的動盪。/ His health has not been improved by all the upset at home. 家中的紛亂使他的健康毫無起色。/ We had our first major upset when Rogers was taken off with a leg injury. 羅傑斯因腿傷被換下場，這是我們遭遇的第一個重大挫折。

<span class="vocabulary">**low**</span> [ləʊ] 
<span class="definition">n. [C]（某人的生活或職業生涯中的）艱難時期，即低谷：</span>a low in her life 她人生中的一個艱難時期 / the highs and lows of an actor’s life 演員生涯中的起起落落

<span class="vocabulary">**trial**</span> ['traɪəl] 
<span class="definition">n. [C] trial (to sb) 給某人造成困難或麻煩的經歷或人：</span>the trials and tribulations of married life 婚姻生活的考驗與磨鍊 / She was a sore trial to her family at times. 她有時讓家人傷透了腦筋。

<span class="vocabulary">**terror**</span> ['terə] 
<span class="definition">n. [C] [非正式] 給人造成麻煩（或難以控制）的一個人（通常是一個孩子）或一個動物：</span>Their kids are real little terrors. 他們的小孩都是十足的討厭鬼。
            
<span class="vocabulary">**menace**</span> [ˈmenəs]
<span class="definition">n. [C, usually sing.] [非正式] 引起麻煩的人或事物：</span>You're a menace to my privacy, Tom. 湯姆，你這人真討厭，居然侵犯我的隱私。/ As I have said earlier in this book, bad shoes are a menace. 正如我在本書前面說過的那樣，劣質的鞋子會很麻煩。          

<span class="vocabulary">**liability**</span> [ˌlaɪəˈbɪləti]
<span class="definition">n. [C, usually sing.] [非正式] 惹麻煩的人（或事）：</span>Since his injury, Jones has become more of a liability than an asset to the team. 瓊斯負傷以來，與其說他是全隊的骨幹倒不如說他已成為隊裡的累贅。/ As the president's prestige continues to fall, they're clearly beginning to consider him a liability. 隨著總統的聲望持續下跌，他們顯然開始視他為負累。/ What was once a vote catching policy, is now a political liability. 這一曾經吸引選票的政策，如今成了政治上的負累。

☀ <span class="category">**困境的名詞：**</span>
<span class="vocabulary">**dilemma**</span> [dɪ'lemə] 
<span class="definition">n. [C] 造成困難的一種處境（即困境），且在這種處境中常不得不在兩個同等重要的事情中做出一個非常艱難的選擇，可稱為進退兩難的窘境，相當於predicament：</span>moral dilemma 道德上左右為難 / social dilemma 社會的窘境 / political dilemma 政治上的窘境 / They were caught in a real dilemma. 他們陷入了進退兩難的境地。/ She faced a dilemma about whether to accept the offer or not. 她左右為難，不知道該不該接受這一提議。
           
<span class="vocabulary">**predicament**</span> [prɪˈdɪkəmənt]
<span class="definition">n. [C] 困境；尷尬的處境：</span>awful, difficult, dire, terrible, worse predicament 可怕的困境；尷尬的處境；悲慘的境況；更糟糕的處境 / Other companies are in an even worse predicament than ourselves. 其他公司的處境甚至比我們的更精糕。/ the team's dire financial predicament 這支團隊嚴重的財務困境 / When I was your age, I was in a similar predicament. 我在你這個年齡時處在相似的困境中。/ a portrayal of the human predicament 人類困境的寫照 / Many young people find themselves in this predicament. 許多年輕人都發現自己身處這一困境。/ How were we to escape this awful predicament? 我們該如何擺脫這一可怕的困境?/ She was searching for the right words to explain her predicament. 她正在尋找合適的詞語來解釋她的困境。/ Now I really was in a dire predicament. 現在我確實處境很悲慘。/ I'm in a terrible predicament. 我的處境十分尷尬。/ The decision will leave her in a peculiar predicament. 這個決定會讓她處於尷尬的境地。

<span class="vocabulary">**trap**</span> [træp]
