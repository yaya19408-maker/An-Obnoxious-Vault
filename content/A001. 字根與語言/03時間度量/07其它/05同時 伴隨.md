☀ <span class="category">**同時 伴隨：**</span>
<span class="vocabulary">**meanwhile**</span> ['mi:nwaɪl] 
<span class="definition">adv. 1（也可用非正式的meantime）在其他事情發生的期間，即同時，與此同時：</span>Bob spent fifteen months alone on his yacht. Ann, meanwhile, took care of the children on her own. 鮑勃獨自在他的遊艇上待了十五個月。在這段時間，安一個人照顧孩子。<span class="definition">2（也可用非正式的meantime）在兩個時間（或事件）之間的一段時間內，即期間：</span>The doctor will see you again next week. Meanwhile, you must rest as much as possible. 醫生下週還會給你看病。在此期間，你一定要儘可能多休息。<span class="definition">n. 1 [sing.] [英] for the meanwhile持續一小段時間，即一會兒，暫時：</span>We need some new curtains, but these will do for the meanwhile. 我們需要一些新的窗簾，但這些暫時還可以用。<span class="definition">2 [sing.] in the meanwhile在兩個時間（或事件）之間，即在此期間或與此同時：</span>I hope to go to medical school eventually. In the meanwhile, I am going to study chemistry. 我希望最終能上醫學院。這期間我打算學化學。

<span class="vocabulary">**together**</span> [tə'ɡeðə] 
<span class="definition">adv. 同時。不如meanwhile常用：</span>They both spoke together. 他們兩人同時發言。/ All together now : ‘Happy birthday to you …’（非正式用法）現在大家一齊唱：“祝你生日快樂…”
           
<span class="vocabulary">**coincide**</span> [ˌkəʊɪnˈsaɪd; 美 ˌkoʊ-]
<span class="definition">vi. ~ (with sth/sb)（兩件或更多的事情）同時發生：</span>It's a pity our trips to New York don't coincide. 真遺憾我們不能同一時間去紐約旅行。/ The strike was timed to coincide with the party conference. 那次罷工選擇在召開政黨大會的同一時間舉行。/ Her visit coincided exactly with a visit by the American president. 她恰好與美國總統同時來訪。/ The singer's arrival was timed to coincide with the opening of the festival. 特地安排這位歌手恰好在音樂節開幕時到達。
           
<span class="vocabulary">**coexist**</span> [ˌkəʊɪgˈzɪst; 美 ˌkoʊ-]
<span class="definition">vi. [正式] ~ (with sb/sth)（尤指和平地）在某時共存：</span>The illness frequently coexists with other chronic diseases. 這種病往往與其他慢性病同時存在。/ Different traditions coexist successfully side by side. 不同的傳統和諧地共存著。/ Pockets of affluence coexist with poverty. 少數富裕地區與貧困地區並存。/ Bankers and clockmakers have coexisted in the City for hundreds of years. 銀行家與鐘錶匠已經在倫敦城共同存在好幾百年了。/ Modern farming methods coexist with more traditional practices. 現代耕作方法與較傳統的耕作習慣並存。

<span class="vocabulary">**accompany**</span> [ə'kʌmpənɪ] 
<span class="definition">vt. 伴隨其他事而發生（或出現）：</span>strong winds accompanied by heavy rain 狂風夾著暴雨 / Each pack contains a book and accompanying CD. 每包內裝書一本，並附光碟一張。
           
<span class="vocabulary">**accompaniment**</span> [əˈkʌmpənimənt]
<span class="definition">n. [C] [正式] ~ (to sth) 伴隨某事而發生的事：</span>High blood pressure is a common accompaniment to this disease. 這種病通常伴隨有高血壓。/ The women's medical school opened in 1874, to the accompaniment of much ridicule. 女子醫學院在一片嘲笑聲中於 1874年創立。/ The sun was back, with its accompaniment of dust and flies. 太陽重新露了出來，灰塵和蒼蠅也隨之出現。
 
<span class="vocabulary">**attend**</span> [ə'tend] 
<span class="definition">vt. [正式] 與某事同時發生，即伴隨某事而發生：</span>She dislikes the loss of privacy that attends TV celebrity. 她不喜歡成為電視名人後隨之失去個人隱私。
          
<span class="vocabulary">**to**</span> [tu:] 
<span class="definition">prep. 伴隨或隨同（某事的發生）：</span>He left the stage to prolonged applause. 他在經久不息的掌聲中退下了舞臺。

<span class="vocabulary">**attendant**</span> [əˈtendənt]
<span class="definition">adj. [正式] [常用於名詞前] ~ (upon sth) 與剛發生的事緊密相連的，即隨之而來的：</span>attendant problems, attendant risks, attendant circumstances 隨之而來的問題；隨之而來的風險；隨之而來的情況 / We had all the usual problems attendant upon starting a new business. 我們遇到了創業時通常會出現的所有問題。/ Mr Branson's victory, and all the attendant publicity, were well deserved. 布蘭森先生的勝利，以及隨之而來的關注度，都是理所應當的。/ the risks attendant on the exploration of the unknown 探索未知所帶來的危險
           
<span class="vocabulary">**allied**</span> [əˈlaɪd,ˈælaɪd]
<span class="definition">adj. [正式] ~ (to/with sth)（兩個或兩個以上的事物）共存的；伴隨…發生的：</span>He possessed a raw energy allied to a feeling of something special. 他粗獷豪放，給人一種特殊的感覺。/ In this job you will need social skills allied with technical knowledge. 這項工作需要社交能力和專業知識。/ a disastrous rise in interest rates allied with a stock market slump 股市下跌的同時，利率卻在災難性地上漲
           
<span class="vocabulary">**incidental**</span> [ˌɪnsɪˈdentl]
<span class="definition">adj. 1 ~ (to sth) 附帶發生（且次要）的；（某事）非有意為之的：</span>The discovery was incidental to their main research. 這一發現是他們主要研究中的附帶收穫。/ incidental music (= music used with a play or a film/movie to give atmosphere) 配樂 / The playing of music proved to be incidental to the main business of the evening. 音樂演奏成了當晚唱主角的商業活動的陪襯。/ You may be able to get help with incidental expenses (= small costs that you get in connection with sth). 你可以付些錢找人幫忙。/ At the bottom of the bill, you will notice various incidental expenses such as faxes. 在賬單底部，你會發現有傳真等各項雜費。/ Information skills are not merely incidental to the curriculum but central to it. 資訊獲取技能絕不是課程中無關緊要的部分，而是核心內容。/ Any resemblance of a character in this book to a living person is purely incidental. 書中人物若與現實人物雷同，純屬巧合。<span class="definition">2 [術語] ~ to sth 作為某事的自然結果而發生的，如伴隨而來的、免不了的：</span>These risks are incidental to the work of a firefighter. 這些風險是擔任消防員不可避免的。/ It's just a risk incidental to the job. 這只不過是這項工作附帶的風險。<span class="definition">n. [usually pl.] 附帶的次要事情：</span>You'll need money for incidentals such as tips and taxis. 你將需要準備好付小費和乘計程車之類的雜項開銷。

<span class="vocabulary">**parallel**</span> ['pærəlel] 
<span class="definition">adj. 同時發生的：</span>parallel trends 並行發展的趨勢 <span class="definition">vt. 與…同時發生：</span>The rise in unemployment is paralleled by an increase in petty crime. 在失業率上升的同時，輕度犯罪也跟著增長。/ Often there are emotional reasons paralleling the financial ones. 經濟原因常常伴有情感原因。
           
<span class="vocabulary">**concurrent**</span> [kənˈkʌrənt; 美 -ˈkɜ:r-]
<span class="definition">adj. ~ (with sth) 同時存在或發生的，即並存的、同時發生的：</span>He was imprisoned for two concurrent terms of 30 months and 18 months. 他被判處30個月和18個月的監禁，合併執行。/ He will actually be serving three concurrent five-year sentences. 實際上，他將服3個5年徒刑，合併執行。/ Galerie St. Etienne is holding three concurrent exhibitions. 聖艾蒂安美術館同時舉辦3場展覽。
           
<span class="vocabulary">**simultaneous**</span> [ˌsɪmlˈteɪniəs; 美 ˌsaɪml-]
<span class="definition">adj. 與其他事同時發生（或同時被完成）的：</span>simultaneous translation, simultaneous interpreting 同聲傳譯 / The theatre will provide simultaneous translation in both English and Chinese. 劇院將提供英語和漢語的同聲翻譯。/ There will be simultaneous transmission of the concert on TV and radio. 電視和收音機將同步轉播這場音樂會。/ the simultaneous release of the book and the album 書和唱片專輯的同步發行 / simultaneous orgasm 同時達到的性高潮 / There were several simultaneous attacks by the rebels. 反叛者同時發動了幾起攻擊。

<span class="vocabulary">**alongside**</span> [ə'lɒŋ'saɪd] 
<span class="definition">prep. 與…同時：</span>Traditional beliefs still flourish alongside a modern urban lifestyle. 現代城市生活方式盛行的同時，傳統信念仍然大行其道。

<span class="vocabulary">**while**</span> [waɪl] 
<span class="definition">conj. 與（其他事件發生的時間）同時：</span>You can go swimming while I’m having lunch. 我吃午飯時你可以去游泳。/ shoes mended while you wait 在你等候的時候修的鞋

<span class="vocabulary">**with**</span> [wɪð] 
<span class="definition">prep. 隨著…而發生：</span>With the time passing by, ... 隨著時間的流逝，…

<span class="vocabulary">**as**</span> [æz] 
<span class="definition">conj. 當另一件事正在發生時，即隨著，表示一個時間段：</span>He sat watching her as she got ready. 他一直坐著看她準備停當。/ As she grew older she gained in confidence. 隨著年齡的增長她的信心增強了。
           
<span class="vocabulary">**synchronize**</span> [ˈsɪŋkrənaɪz]
<span class="definition">vt.&vi. ~ (sth) (with sth)（使）與某事同時發生；（使）與某事物以相同的速度移動：</span>The timing of the attack was synchronized with the ambassador’s arrival. 攻擊時間與大使抵達的時間同步。/ The sound track did not synchronize with the action. 聲跡與動作不同步。/ Synchronise the score with the film action. 配樂和電影情節進行同步合成。/ It was virtually impossible to synchronise our lives so as to take holidays and weekends together. 想讓我們的生活步調保持一致好一起去度假和過週末幾乎是不可能的事。/ Let's synchronize our watches (= make them show exactly the same time). 咱們對一下表吧。

相關詞義延伸：
· parallel還可指同時涉及若干項計算處理過程的，即並行的。
