☀ <span class="category">**比賽、競爭、對壘、交鋒的動詞：**</span>
<span class="vocabulary">**compete**</span> [kəm'pi:t] 
<span class="definition">vi. 1 compete (with/against sb) (for sth)（與…）競爭，對抗，爭奪。是一個基礎用詞，含義極廣：</span>to compete with the bank 與那家銀行競爭 / to compete for the contract 為得到那份合同而競爭 / Their products compete directly with ours. 他們的產品與我們的構成直接競爭。/ to compete against professional athletes 與職業運動員同臺競技 / The boy is competing for her attention. 那個男孩子正在她面前爭寵。/ Animals in the wild have to compete for food. 野生動物不得不為食物競爭。<span class="definition">2 compete (in sth) (against sb) 參加比賽或競爭：</span>I will be competing in the 100-metre race. 我將參加100米比賽。/ to compete actively in the global system 在全球體系中積極競爭

<span class="vocabulary">**fight**</span> [faɪt] 
<span class="definition">vt.＆vi. fight sb/sth (for sth) 參加競賽，競爭：</span>to fight an election 參加競選 / Regional monopolies were bitterly fought for. 為爭奪地方壟斷權發生了激烈的爭鬥。/ She’s fighting for a place in the national team. 她正努力爭取加入國家隊。/ The second half was fiercely fought, but neither side managed to score. 下半場比賽非常激烈，但雙方都沒有得分。<span class="definition">vi. 參加拳擊比賽：</span>Doctors fear he may never fight again. 醫生認為他可能再也不能重返拳壇了。

<span class="vocabulary">**struggle**</span> ['strʌɡl] 
<span class="definition">vi. struggle (with sb) (for sth)（尤指為得到某東西而）與某人競爭或爭辯：</span>rival leaders struggling for power 互相對立的領導人爭奪權力
            
<span class="vocabulary">**chase**</span> [tʃeɪs]
<span class="definition">vt. 努力去獲得或爭取…，例如金錢、工作或成功：</span>Too many people are chasing too few jobs nowadays. 如今有太多的人在角逐寥寥無幾的工作職位。/ There are too many schools chasing too few pupils. 太多的學校在爭奪太少的生源。/ In Wales, 14 people are chasing every job. 在威爾士，每份工作都有14個人在競爭。/ The team is chasing its first win in five games. 這支隊伍正全力爭取獲得五場比賽的首場勝利。          
           
<span class="vocabulary">**pursue**</span> [pəˈsju:; 美 pərˈsu:]
<span class="definition">vt. [正式] 追求一個興趣：</span>Which career do you wish to pursue? 你打算幹哪一行？/ She wishes to pursue a medical career. 她希望從事醫學工作。/ He was still doggedly pursuing his studies. 他堅持不懈地進行著自己的研究。/ It became harder for women married to diplomats to pursue their own interests. 與外交官結婚的女性要追求自己的愛好就變得更加困難了。/ the freedom to pursue her own interests 做她自己感興趣之事的自由 <span class="definition">vt. [正式] 付出努力（通常是長時間的努力）追求一個目標或結果：</span>to pursue a goal/an aim/an objective 追求（或實現）目標 / The implication seems to be that it is impossible to pursue economic reform and democracy simultaneously. 言外之意似乎是經濟改革和民主化程序是不可能同時進行的。/ He has aggressively pursued new business. 他積極開拓新業務。

<span class="vocabulary">**contend**</span> [kənˈtend]
<span class="definition">vi. ~ (for sth) 與某人競爭（以為了奪取…），即爭奪：</span>Three armed groups were contending for power. 三個武裝集團在爭奪權力。/ with 10 UK construction yards contending with rivals from Norway, Holland, Italy and Spain 10家英國建造廠與來自挪威、荷蘭、義大利和西班牙的對手展開競爭
           
<span class="vocabulary">**contest**</span> [kənˈtest]
<span class="definition">vt. 爭取贏得比賽：</span>a fiercely contested game 競爭十分激烈的比賽 / a closely contested regional flower show 各方爭奇鬥豔、難分高下的地區花卉展 <span class="definition">vt. 爭奪權力等：</span>Three candidates contested the leadership. 有三位候選人角逐領導權。/ He quickly won his party's nomination to contest the elections. 他很快獲得了本黨提名,角逐大選。/ The election was bitterly contested. 選舉競爭激烈。
           
<span class="vocabulary">**tussle**</span> [ˈtʌsl]
<span class="definition">vi.（尤指為了爭得某事物而）與…競爭：</span>Tom tussled for fourth place with Jerry. 湯姆和傑瑞為爭奪第四名展開了激烈較量。     
           
<span class="vocabulary">**jostle**</span> [ˈdʒɒsl; 美 ˈdʒɑ:sl]
<span class="definition">vi. ~ for sth 爭搶、爭奪（關注、位置、獎賞等）：</span>dozens of concerns jostling for your attention 有待你解決的諸多事情 / There is a wide spread of stories jostling for coverage on today's front pages. 有大量各種題材的報道爭著上今天報紙的頭版。/ Hordes of journalists jostled for position outside the conference hall. 大群記者在會場外爭搶位置。/ A motley collection of ornaments jostled for space on the crowded shelf. 一堆五花八門的裝飾品佔滿了擁擠的架子。/ the contenders who have been jostling for the top job 一直在爭奪這一高階職位的競爭者們
                      
<span class="vocabulary">**jockey**</span> [ˈdʒɒki; 美 ˈdʒɑ:ki]
<span class="definition">vi. ~ (for sth) 運用所有可能的方法來獲得對別人的優勢：</span>The runners jockeyed for position at the start. 賽跑選手力求搶佔起跑有利位置。/ The bands are constantly jockeying with each other for the number one spot. 這些樂隊經常相互耍手段以謀取榜首位置。/ The rival political parties are already jockeying for power. 相互競爭的政黨已經在耍各種手段爭權奪勢了。/ Already, both sides are jockeying to belittle the other side. 雙方已經在使出渾身解數貶低對方了。

<span class="vocabulary">**scramble**</span> [ˈskræmbl]
<span class="definition">vi. ~ (for sth) 為得到某東西而與別人爭搶：</span>Shoppers were scrambling to get the best bargains. 顧客爭先恐後地搶購最便宜的特價商品。/ More than three million fans are expected to scramble for tickets. 預計會有超過 300 萬的球迷搶購門票。/ Business is booming and foreigners are scrambling to invest. 商業繁榮，外國人爭相投資。
           
<span class="vocabulary">**snag**</span> [snæg]
<span class="definition">vt. 搶先抓住，搶先獲得：</span>I snagged a ride from Joe. 我截住喬的車搭了一段路。

<span class="vocabulary">**dispute**</span> [dɪˈspju:t]
<span class="definition">vt. 努力爭奪對某事物的控制權，或努力贏得某事物（如競爭）：</span>On the last lap three runners were disputing the lead. 在最後一圈，三名賽跑者在爭奪領先地位。
           
<span class="vocabulary">**duel**</span> [ˈdju:əl; 美 ˈdu:əl]
<span class="definition">vi. 爭鬥：</span>We duelled for two years and Peterson made the most of it, playing us off against each other. 我們爭鬥了兩年，彼得森充分利用這點挑撥我倆對陣。

<span class="vocabulary">**race**</span> [reɪs] 
<span class="definition">vt.＆vi. race (against) sb/sth 和…進行競爭；參與競爭：</span>The companies are racing to be the first to seize the market. 那些公司爭先恐後佔領市場。

<span class="vocabulary">**meet**</span> [mi:t] 
<span class="definition">vt.＆vi.（在比賽中與對手）遭遇；交鋒：</span>Smith and Jones met in last year’s final. 史密斯和瓊斯在去年的決賽中相遇。

<span class="vocabulary">**oppose**</span> [ə'pəʊz] 
<span class="definition">vt. 與某人競爭，對壘或角逐：</span>He intends to oppose the prime minister in the leadership election. 在領導選舉中，他欲與首相一決高下。

<span class="vocabulary">**bag**</span> [bæɡ] 
<span class="definition">vt. [英，非正式]（在別人做出聲稱以前）聲稱擁有…；在別人得到…以前而將其佔有，即搶佔：</span>to bag the seat 佔座位

相關詞義延伸：
· contest還可指正式反對一個決定或陳述，如爭辯、提出異議。

周邊詞彙補充：
· clash可指在體育比賽中廝殺、角逐；
· contend可指（尤指在爭論中）聲稱某事是真的，有時也可翻譯為“認為”；
· counter-attack可指（在戰爭、體育運動或爭論中）反攻、反擊。

<span class="vocabulary">**competition**</span> [kɒmpɪ'tɪʃn] 
<span class="definition">n. 1 [U] competition (between/with sb) / competition (for sth)（為某種資源而）競爭，角逐：</span>fair competition 公平競爭 / friendly competition 友好的競爭 / to fight off competition from overseas companies 擊退來自外國公司的競爭 / They won the order against fierce international competition. 他們在激烈的國際競爭中贏得了訂單。/ Fierce competition exists between schools in the area. 該地區的學校之間存在著激烈的競爭。<span class="definition">2 [C] 比賽，競賽：</span>a competition for the best chef 最佳廚師比賽 / a knockout competition 淘汰賽 / sporting（英）, sports（美）competition 體育比賽 / dancing, piano, surfing, photo competition 舞蹈比賽；鋼琴比賽；衝浪比賽；攝影比賽 / competition winner 比賽獲勝者
           
<span class="vocabulary">**contest**</span> [ˈkɒntest; 美 ˈkɑ:n-]
<span class="definition">n. 1 [C] 比賽、競賽：</span>beauty, song, talent, popularity, writing contest 選美比賽；歌詠比賽；才藝比賽；人氣比拼；寫作比賽 / athletic（尤美）, sporting（尤英）, sports（尤美） contest 田徑比賽；體育競賽 / global, international, local, national, regional contest 全球賽事；國際賽事；地方賽事；全國賽事；地區賽事 / to enter a contest, to win a contest, to lose a contest 參加比賽；贏得比賽；比賽失敗 / a contest of strength 實力的較量 / We have three major beauty contests a year. 我們每年舉辦三次大型的選美比賽。/ The contest was too one-sided to be exciting. 這場比賽太一邊倒，沒什麼意思。/ This contest is wide open: any of half a dozen teams could win it. 這場比賽勝負難料，其中六個隊都有機會獲勝。/ The contestants are eliminated one by one until the last two compete in a head-to-head contest. 參賽選手一個一個被淘汰，最後只剩下兩名選手一對一較量。/ Few contests in the recent history of British boxing have been as thrilling. 英國近年的拳擊比賽中很少有如此激動人心的。/ Jackson has injured his knee and is now out of the contest. 傑克遜膝蓋受傷，現已退出比賽。/ the contest between these two great boxers 這兩名偉大的拳擊手之間的比賽 / The New Zealanders are looking forward to future contests with South Africa. 紐西蘭人盼望著將來同南非進行更多比賽。<span class="definition">2 [C] ~ (for sth) 對控制權或權力的爭奪；選舉：</span>a contest for the leadership of the party 爭奪政黨的領導權 / a clear contest between church and state 教會與政府之間明顯的爭鬥 / A third candidate has entered the contest for the Republican nomination. 第三位候選人加入了角逐共和黨提名的行列。/ In the animal kingdom intruders usually lose contests over territories. 在動物王國裡，入侵者通常在領地爭奪中被擊敗。<span class="definition">3 [C] 競爭：</span>The other bidders for the contract complained that it had not been a fair contest. 其他競標人抱怨該合同的競標不公平。
           
<span class="vocabulary">**contention**</span> [kənˈtenʃn]

<span class="vocabulary">**rivalry**</span> [ˈraɪvlri]

<span class="vocabulary">**competitive**</span> [kəmˈpetətɪv]
<span class="definition">adj. 1 競爭性的：</span>competitive games, competitive sports 競技性的比賽；競爭性的體育運動 / Graduates have to fight for jobs in a highly competitive market. 畢業生不得不在競爭激烈的市場上奮力爭取找到工作。/ Only by keeping down costs will America maintain its competitive advantage over other countries. 只有透過控制成本，美國才能保持對其他國家的競爭優勢。/ Japan is a highly competitive market system. 日本實行競爭激烈的市場體制。<span class="definition">2 ~ (with sb/sth)（商品或服務）質優價廉的，即有競爭力的：</span>a shop selling clothes at competitive prices (= as low as any other shop) 在服裝價格上有競爭力的商店 / We need to work harder to remain competitive with other companies. 我們必須更加努力工作以保持對其他公司具有競爭力。/ to gain a competitive advantage over rival companies 佔有超越對手公司的競爭優勢 / Only those homes offered for sale at competitive prices will secure interest from serious purchasers. 只有那些價格優惠的住房才會引起真想買房的人的興趣。/ a travel company specializing in amazingly competitive rates for flights 專門提供極優惠航班折扣的旅遊公司 / skills training to make our industries more competitive in world markets 使我們各個行業在世界市場上更具競爭力的技能培訓 / Investment in research is needed to keep us competitive with other countries. 要保持與其他國家的競爭力，我們需要在研究方面投入資金。<span class="definition">3（人）努力競爭的、好勝的：</span>a very competitive person 競爭意識很強的人 / You have to be highly competitive to do well in sport these days. 如今你必須有強烈的競爭意識才能在體育運動中取得好成績。/ He has always been ambitious and fiercely competitive. 他一直都雄心勃勃，而且極其好勝。/ I'm a very competitive person and I was determined not be beaten. 我非常要強，下定決心不能輸給別人。

<span class="vocabulary">**compare**</span> [kəm'peə] 
<span class="definition">vi. compare with/to sb/sth與其他人或事物相似（無論其他人或事物是好還是糟），即與…同樣好或同樣糟：</span>This school compares with the best in the country. 這所學校可與全國最好的學校媲美。/ This house doesn’t compare with our previous one. 這房子比不上我們以前的。/ I’ve had some difficulties, but they were nothing compared to yours. 我遇到了一些困難，但與你的困難比起來就不算什麼了。/ Their prices compare favourably with those of their competitors. 他們的價格比競爭者的要優惠。/ These mountains do not compare with the Himalayas. 這些山不能與喜馬拉雅山相比。/ Few things compare with the joy of walking on a bright spring morning. 沒有什麼比在陽光明媚的春日早晨散步更愜意的了。<span class="definition">vt. compare A to B 相提並論：</span>The critics compared his work to that of Martin Amis. 評論家把他的作品和馬丁·埃米斯的相提並論。/ The golfer Tiger Woods is often compared to Jack Nicklaus. 人們常把高爾夫球手“老虎”伍茲與傑克·尼古拉斯相提並論。

<span class="vocabulary">**match**</span> [mætʃ] 
<span class="definition">vt. 1 與…一樣好，即相匹敵，和…不相上下：</span>The teams were evenly matched. 各隊的水平旗鼓相當。/ She found that his determination almost matched her own. 她發現他幾乎和她一樣堅決。<span class="definition">2 使等同於或優於：</span>The company was unable to match his current salary. 當時公司付不起和他現在相同的薪酬。/ We matched them in every department. 我們在各方面都比得上他們。/ Nothing quite matches the fine, subtle taste of this cheese. 這種乳酪細膩、精妙的味道無與倫比。/ No other rock band comes even close to matching their talent. 論才華，任何其他的搖滾樂隊都無法與他們相提並論。

<span class="vocabulary">**equal**</span> ['i:kwəl]
