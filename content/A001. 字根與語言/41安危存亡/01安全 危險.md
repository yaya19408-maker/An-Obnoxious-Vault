☀ <span class="category">**安全的形容詞：**</span>
<span class="vocabulary">**safe**</span> [seɪf] 
<span class="definition">adj. 1 safe (from sb/sth) 只作表語，指受到保護而不受任何傷害的，即處境安全的。強調在直面危險時，人身、財產等處於切實“安全”的一種狀態，不遭受實際的傷害或損失：</span>I don’t feel safe in the cave. 在這洞穴裡我感覺不安全。/ They were safe from attack. 他們不會受到攻擊。/ Keep your money safe by carrying it in an inside pocket. 為安全起見，要把錢放在內兜。<span class="definition">2 safe (to do sth) / safe (for sb) 不太可能對身體造成任何傷害的，即對身體安全無害的：</span>The food is not safe. 這食物不安全。/ an environmentally safe form of energy 一種對環境無害的能源 / a safe distance 安全距離 / The army experts made the bomb safe. 軍方專家排除了炸彈險情。<span class="definition">3 未受傷害、損害或丟失的：</span>The journalist in the war zone wrote to his wife and told her he was safe. 身處戰區的記者給自己的妻子寫信報平安。/ to have a safe trip home 回家一路平安 <span class="definition">4 指某個地方無危險的，或在此地人或物品不會丟失的：</span>I lived in a safe place when the war broke out. 戰爭爆發的時候我住在一個安全的地方。/ a safe village 安全的村莊 / Be sure your job is safe. 你要確保自己不丟工作。<span class="definition">5 safe (to do sth) 風險小或無風險的，不大會錯的，不致冒犯別人的：</span>safe investment, safe topic 無風險的投資；沒有忌諱的話題 / It is safe to say that the president will win more supporters. 可以肯定地說，總統將會贏得更多的支持者。/ better safe than sorry 穩妥總比後悔好

<span class="vocabulary">**safety**</span> ['seɪftɪ] 
<span class="definition">adj. 一般只用於名詞前。安全的，常指安全的特性或措施：</span>safety belt, safety rope, safety equipment, safety device 安全帶；安全繩；安全裝置；安全裝置 / A national insurance system provides a safety net for the very poor.（比喻用法）國家保險體系為最貧困者提供保障。/ safety glass, safety razor, safety seat 安全玻璃；安全剃刀；安全座椅 / safety tips, safety measures, safety procedures 安全小貼士；安全措施；安全程式 / safety laws, safety levels, safety standards 安全法規；安全級別；安全標準 / safety risk, safety awareness, safety check 安全風險；安全意識；安全檢查 / safety record, safety issues 安全記錄；安全問題

<span class="vocabulary">**secure**</span> [sɪ'kjʊə] 
<span class="definition">adj. 1 secure (against/from sth)不能被…影響或傷害的，即安全的，穩妥的。強調因預先做好了防禦措施而在將來的時間內即使遇到攻擊也會平安無事，因而讓人放心的：</span>Information must be stored so that it is secure from accidental deletion. 必須把資料儲存起來，這樣才不至於無意中刪除。/ economically, financially secure 經濟安全；金融安全 <span class="definition">2（建築物、房間或門等因有人把守或本身建造得比較牢固而）使人難以進入或離開的。反義詞為insecure：</span>Check that all windows and doors have been made as secure as possible. 看看是不是所有的門窗都關緊了。/ The house has been made secure against intruders. 這房子採取了防止入室盜竊的安全措施。/ a secure unit for child offenders 少年犯拘留病房

<span class="vocabulary">**sound**</span> [saʊnd] 
<span class="definition">adj. 狀況良好的，未受損的，未受傷的：</span>to be of sound mind 神智健全 / a financially sound company 一家財務狀況良好的公司 / The car is basically sound. 這輛車基本狀況良好。/ The house was structurally sound. 這棟房子結構完好無損。/ We arrived home safe and sound. 我們平安到家了。
           
<span class="vocabulary">**intact**</span> [ɪnˈtækt]
<span class="definition">adj. [名詞前不常用] 完好無損的：</span>Most of the house remains intact even after two hundred years. 雖然過了兩百年，這房子的大部分還保持完好。/ We found the tomb perfectly intact. 我們發現這座墳墓完好無損。/ The mill machinery is still intact. 磨坊裡的機器依然完好無損。/ Most of the cargo was left intact after the explosion. 大部分貨物在爆炸發生後都完好無損。/ The collection should be kept completely intact. 收藏品應當保持完整無缺。/ He emerged miraculously intact after the fight. 戰鬥之後他完好無損地回來了。/ The team returns largely intact to defend its title.（尤美）這支隊伍基本上是原班人馬參加冠軍衛冕之戰。/ He emerged from the trial with his reputation intact. 他受審獲釋，名譽絲毫未受損害。/ If the family unit is still intact, the patient frequently does very well. 如果家庭仍然完整，病人常常恢復得很好。
                      
<span class="vocabulary">**unscathed**</span> [ʌnˈskeɪðd]
<span class="definition">adj. [名詞前不可用]（在歷經危險後）未受傷的、未受損害的：</span>Tony emerged unscathed apart from a severely bruised finger. 託尼除了一根手指嚴重淤青外沒有受到其他傷害。/ The hostages emerged from their ordeal unscathed. 各人質歷盡磨難後安然生還。/ The children escaped unscathed. 這些孩子平安脫險。/ Amazingly, the driver waked away from the accident completely unscathed. 讓人吃驚的是，司機在車禍中毫髮無 / The company came through the crisis apparently unscathed. 公司挺過了危機，看來沒受什麼損失。/ East Los Angeles was left relatively unscathed by the riots. 相對來說，洛杉磯東部基本沒有受到暴亂的影響。

<span class="vocabulary">**benign**</span> [bɪˈnaɪn]
<span class="definition">adj.（物質或過程）無害的：</span>We're taking relatively benign medicines and we're turning them into poisons. 我們服的藥相對來說藥性溫和，但是我們卻在把它們變成毒藥。/ The effects of this chemical are fairly benign. 這種化學品的作用比較溫和。/ We are looking for an environmentally benign alternative to bleach. 我們正在尋找一種有利於環保的替代品用於漂白。
        
<span class="vocabulary">**harmless**</span> [ˈhɑ:mləs; 美 ˈhɑ:rm-]
<span class="definition">adj. ~ (to sb/sth)（某事物）無害的、（尤指對健康）不會導致損害的：</span>The bacteria are harmless to humans. 這些細菌對人無害。/ The substance is harmless to people. 這種物質對人無害。/ Industry has been working at developing harmless substitutes for these gases. 該行業一直在努力開發一些可以替代這些氣體的無害物質。/ This experiment was harmless to the animals. 這個試驗不會對動物造成傷害。/ a small and seemingly harmless creature 看似無害的小動物 / chemical wastes which have to be rendered harmless 必須進行無害化處理的化學廢料|

<span class="vocabulary">**innocuous**</span> [ɪˈnɒkjuəs; 美 ɪˈnɑ:k-]
<span class="definition">adj. [正式] 無害的；無危險的：</span>an innocuous substance 無害物質 / Both mushrooms look innocuous but are in fact deadly. 這兩種蘑菇看起來似乎都無害，實際上卻是致命的。/ The liquid looked fairly innocuous. 這種液體看上去沒什麼危險。

相關詞義延伸：
· safe還可指證據確鑿的；
· benign還可指（體內生長的腫瘤）良性的。

周邊詞彙補充：
· clean可指無有害或令人不悅的物質的；
· friendly可指對某人或某事物有幫助或無害的；
· innoxious可指無害的；無毒的。
 
☀ <span class="category">**安全的名詞：**</span>
<span class="vocabulary">**safety**</span> ['seɪftɪ] 
<span class="definition">n. 1 [U] 受到保護而免遭危險或傷害的狀態，即安全，平安。強調直面危險時的切實安全：</span>safety on aircraft 飛行安全 / The stairs are fitted with a handrail for safety. 為保證安全，樓梯上裝了扶手。/ Police fear for the safety of the missing children. 警方為失蹤兒童的安全擔憂。/ The police gave him protection for his own safety. 警方為他提供了人身安全保護。/ a place of safety 安全地點 <span class="definition">2 [U] 不危險的狀態，即安全性，無危險：</span>fire, food, nuclear safety 消防安全；食品安全；核安全 / road, traffic safety 道路安全；交通安全 / environmental, occupational, workplace safety 環境安全；職業安全；工作場所安全 / passenger, physical, public safety 旅客安全；人身安全；公共安全 / The seat is bolted in place for added safety. 座位用螺釘固定，以提高其安全性。<span class="definition">3 [U] 安全場所：</span>We managed to run to safety before the building collapsed. 我們在大樓坍塌之前設法跑到了安全地帶。/ She finally made it to the safety of her room. 她終於回到了自己的房間這個安全的地方。

<span class="vocabulary">**security**</span> [sɪ'kjʊərɪtɪ]

<span class="vocabulary">**hide**</span> [haɪd] 
<span class="definition">n. [sing.] [尤美，非正式] 用於指處於困境中的人的生命或安全：</span>All he’s worried about is his own hide. 他擔心的只是自己的生命安全。

☀ <span class="category">**危險、威脅的形容詞：**</span>
<span class="vocabulary">**dangerous**</span> ['deɪndʒərəs] 
<span class="definition">adj. dangerous (for sb) (to do sth) 很可能對某人或某事物造成傷害或損害的，即危險的。是一個常規用詞，極為常用：</span>dangerous road, dangerous illness, dangerous sport 危險的道路；危險的疾病；危險的體育運動 / The traffic here is very dangerous for children. 這裡的交通對孩子很危險。/ The prisoners who escaped are violent and dangerous. 這些逃犯殘暴而危險。/ The situation is highly dangerous. 形勢十分危急。
           
<span class="vocabulary">**risky**</span> [ˈrɪski]
<span class="definition">adj. 有（壞事發生的）風險的、有危險的：</span>Life as an aid worker can be a risky business (= dangerous). 救援人員的工作會有危險。/ a risky investment 有風險的投資 / It's far too risky to generalize from one set of results. 僅根據一組結果進行概括是十分不可靠的。/ Investing in airlines is a very risky business. 投資航空業風險很大。/ It's risky to assume that we know what voters will be thinking in a year's time. 自以為我們瞭解投票人在一年後的想法是很危險的行為。/ It all sounds highly risky to me. 對我來說，這一切聽起來太危險了。/ All business activities are inherently risky. 所有的商業活動本質上都有風險。

<span class="vocabulary">**treacherous**</span> [ˈtretʃərəs]

<span class="vocabulary">**ugly**</span> ['ʌɡlɪ] 
<span class="definition">adj.（事件、形勢等）令人不悅或危險的；涉及威脅或暴力的：</span>an ugly incident 危險事件 / There were ugly scenes in the streets last night as rioting continued. 昨晚暴亂持續之際，街上險象環生。/ A fight started and things got pretty ugly. 一場打鬥開始了，情況變得十分危險。
           
<span class="vocabulary">**nasty**</span> [ˈnɑ:sti; 美 ˈnæsti]

<span class="vocabulary">**virulent**</span> [ˈvɪrələnt; -rjəl-]

<span class="vocabulary">**mortal**</span> [ˈmɔ:tl; 美 ˈmɔ:rtl]

<span class="vocabulary">**lethal**</span> [ˈli:θl]
<span class="definition">adj. 1 可致死的、致命的：</span>a lethal dose of poison 毒藥的致死劑量 / a lethal dose of sleeping pills 劑量足以致命的安眠藥 / All these drugs are highly lethal.（lethal尤為美式用法）所有這些藥都是高致命性的。/ It was a potentially lethal mixture of drugs. 這種混合藥物可能會致命。/ chemicals lethal to fish and aquatic mammals 會殺死魚類和水生哺乳動物的致命化學品 / The pesticide is lethal to all insect life. 這種殺蟲劑可以殺死所有昆蟲。/ a lethal weapon 致命的武器 <span class="definition">2 [非正式] 危害極大的；毀滅性的：</span>You and that car--it's a lethal combination! 你和那輛車——真是一對殺手！/ High-powered cars are lethal weapons in the hands of inexperienced drivers. 馬力大的汽車到了缺乏經驗的司機手中便成了毀滅性的武器。/ Frost and wet are the lethal combination for plants. 霜雨天氣對植物危害極大。/ The closure of the factory dealt a lethal blow to the town. 那家工廠的關閉對這座城鎮是一個致命的打擊。

<span class="vocabulary">**suicidal**</span> [ˌsu:ɪˈsaɪdl; British English also ˌsju:ɪ-]

<span class="vocabulary">**intimidation**</span> [ɪnˌtɪmɪˈdeɪʃn]
<span class="definition">n. [U] 恐嚇；威脅：</span>the intimidation of witnesses 對目擊證人的恐嚇 / physical, psychological, verbal intimidation 人身脅迫；心理威脅；言語恐嚇 / voter intimidation 對選民的脅迫 / There were signs of voting fraud and voter intimidation. 有投票舞弊和脅迫選民的跡象。/ Workers were subjected to intimidation as they crossed the picket line.（be subjected to尤為英式用法）工人們在穿過糾察線時遭到了恐嚇。/ intimidation against trade unions 對工會的恐嚇 / Workers continue to enter the plant despite intimidation by mass pickets. 工人們不顧大批糾察的恐嚇，繼續進入工廠。/ fear of intimidation from paramilitary organizations 對來自準軍事組織的恐嚇的畏懼 / an act of intimidation 恐嚇行為 / a campaign of intimidation 恐嚇活動

<span class="vocabulary">**blackmail**</span> [ˈblækmeɪl]
<span class="definition">n. [U] 用威脅或讓對方感到內疚的方式來給對方施壓，讓對方去做對方本不願做的事，可稱為脅迫：</span>emotional, moral blackmail 情感上的脅迫；道德上的脅迫 / economic, nuclear, political blackmail 經濟敲詐；核訛詐；政治訛詐

周邊詞彙補充：           
· waters可指（不明朗的、未知的、困難的、危險的等）局面；
· minefield可指充滿潛在危險的形勢；
· pitfall可指危險或困難，尤指隱藏的或乍看上去不明顯的危險或困難，可稱為陷阱、隱患、圈套等；
· heroics可指（愚蠢或危險的）逞英雄行為；
· lever可指施壓的行為。

☀ <span class="category">**危險、威脅的動詞：**</span>
<span class="vocabulary">**endanger**</span> [ɪnˈdeɪndʒə(r)]
<span class="definition">vt. 使…處於危險之中：</span>Taking these drugs could seriously health your health. 服用這些藥可能嚴重危害你的健康。/ The health of our children is being endangered by exhaust fumes. 我們孩子們的健康正受到排放出的廢氣的損害。/ That one mistake seriously endangered the future of the company. 那一個失誤就嚴重地危及了公司的未來。/ They are accused of causing an explosion likely to endanger life. 他們被控引發了一起可能危及生命的爆炸事件。/ The debate could endanger the proposed peace talks. 這一爭論可能會危及提議進行的和平談判。/ endangered species such as lynx and wolf 猞猁、狼等瀕危物種 / The sea turtle is an endangered species (= it may soon no longer exist). 海龜是瀕危物種。/ 14%6 of primate species are highly endangered. 14% 的靈長類物種處於高度瀕危狀態。

<span class="vocabulary">**jeopardize**</span> [ˈdʒepədaɪz; 美 -pərd-]
<span class="definition">vt. [正式] 冒著…的危險，危及，危害（一種情況或活動）：</span>He would never do anything to jeopardize his career. 他決不會做任何有損於他職業生涯的事。/ He has jeopardized the future of his government. 他的行為已經危及到他這屆政府的未來。/ This scandal could seriously jeopardize his chances of being re-elected. 這樁醜聞可能會嚴重妨礙他再次當選。/ The talks may still be jeopardized by disputes. 會談仍有可能因存在爭端而破裂。/ Any delays could potentially jeopardize the company's ability to do business. 任何延誤都可能損害公司的經營能力。
                      
<span class="vocabulary">**threaten**</span> [ˈθretn]
<span class="definition">vt.&vi. ~ sb (with sth) 威脅、恐嚇某人：</span>They broke my windows and threatened me. 他們砸碎我的窗子並威脅我。/ The attacker threatened them with a gun. 襲擊者用槍威脅他們。/ He was threatened with dismissal if he continued to turn up late for work. 他受到警告，如果繼續上班遲到，就被辭退。/ The threatened strike has been called off. 揚言要進行的罷工被取消了。/ The hijackers threatened to kill one passenger every hour if their demands were not met. 劫機者要挾說，如果他們的要求得不到滿足，他們每過一小時就殺死一名乘客。/ He said army officers had threatened to destroy the town. 他說陸軍軍官們曾威脅要摧毀那座小鎮。/ He tied her up and threatened her with a six-inch knife. 他把她捆起來，拿著一把六英寸長的刀子威脅她。/ He says he was physically threatened in an attempt to get him to sign over his rights. 他說他曾受到人身威脅，要他簽字讓出權利。/ She had repeatedly threatened to commit suicide. 她多次揚言要自殺。/ I never felt threatened by him. 我從來沒有覺得受他威脅。<span class="definition">vt. 對…構成威脅，如危及、威脅到：</span>Pollution is threatening marine life. 汙染正危及海洋生物。/ Many species are now threatened with extinction. 許多物種目前有滅絕之虞。/ The newcomers directly threaten the livelihood of the established workers. 新來的工人直接威脅到老工人的飯碗。/ The unity of our society is threatened by troublesome and restless minorities. 我們社會的團結遭到了一小撮滋擾生事、不安分守己的群體的威脅。/ to threaten the stability of the whole area 威脅整個地區的穩定 / Our marriage was constantly threatened by his other women. 我們的婚姻不斷受到他的其他女人的威脅。/ Our way of life is gravely threatened. 我們的生活方式受到嚴重威脅。

<span class="vocabulary">**menace**</span> [ˈmenəs]
<span class="definition">vt. [正式] 對…構成危險，相當於threaten：</span>The forests are being menaced by major development projects. 大型開發專案正在危及森林。/ The European states retained a latent capability to menace Britain's own security. 歐洲國家仍具有威脅英國自身安全的潛在能力。<span class="definition">vt. [正式] 威脅某人：</span>She's being menaced by her sister's latest boyfriend. 她正受到姐姐最近一個男友的恐嚇。
                      
<span class="vocabulary">**intimidate**</span> [ɪnˈtɪmɪdeɪt]
<span class="definition">vt. ~ sb (into sth/into doing sth) 恐嚇；威脅：</span>Jones had set out to intimidate and dominate Paul. 瓊斯開始恐嚇並控制保羅。/ Attempts to intimidate people into voting for the governing party did not work. 脅迫人們為執政黨投票的企圖沒有成功。/ They were accused of intimidating people into voting for them. 他們被控脅迫選民投他們的票。/ She refused to be intimidated by their threats. 她沒有被他們的威脅嚇倒。/ She did not feel intimidated by him. 她並未覺得受到了他的威脅。/ psychologically intimidate 心理恐嚇 / Dissidents were physically intimidated, threatened, and harshly interrogated. 持不同政見者受到了人身恐嚇和威脅，並遭受了粗暴的審訊。/ The police had tried to intimidate him into signing a confession. 警方曾試圖脅迫他簽署認罪書。/ He was not a man to be easily intimidated. 他不是一個能被輕易嚇倒的人。

<span class="vocabulary">**blackmail**</span> [ˈblækmeɪl]
<span class="definition">vt. ~ sb (into doing sth) 透過威脅（或用手中掌握的關於別人的秘密）來勒索錢財或脅迫某人為你做某事：</span>She blackmailed him for years by threatening to tell the newspapers about their affair. 她以向報紙公開他們的戀情要挾了他很多年。/ The President said he wouldn't be blackmailed into agreeing to the terrorists' demands. 總統說他不會因受恐怖分子的威脅而答應他們的要求。/ He told her their affair would have to stop, because Jack Smith was blackmailing him. 他告訴她說他們的婚外情必須結束了，因為傑克·史密斯正在敲詐他。/ The government insisted that it would not be blackmailed by violence. 政府堅持說它不會接受暴力訛詐。/ Voters were effectively blackmailed into voting 'Yes'. 投票者實際上是被脅迫而投了“贊成”票。/ Don't let him emotionally blackmail you. 不要讓他在情感上脅迫你。/ She says she was virtually blackmailed into giving up her claim to the property. 她說自己實際上是受人脅迫而放棄了財產的要求。

相關詞義延伸：
· hazard還可表示向某人給出一個你知道有可能是錯誤的建議，即斗膽提出建議、做出無把握的猜測。也可表示冒…的風險；使處於危險。

周邊詞彙補充：
· trap可表示使自己的身體部位、衣服等緊緊地卡（或夾、纏、絆）在一個地方而不能動彈且有可能造成損傷或損害。也可表示強迫某人或某事物進入到其不能逃脫的某地或某種情況（尤指為了將其捕捉）；
· condemn可指正式說出（即宣告）使用…不夠安全。
