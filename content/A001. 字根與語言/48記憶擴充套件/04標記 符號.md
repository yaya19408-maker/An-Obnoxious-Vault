☀ <span class="category">**標記、印記、痕跡、跡象的名詞：**</span>
<span class="vocabulary">**mark**</span> [mɑːk] 
<span class="definition">n. 1 [C] 人或動物身上有助於人們對其進行識別的一個顯著的斑點或顏色區域：</span>a horse with a white mark on its head 頭上有塊白斑的馬 / Does he have any distinguishing marks? 他有什麼明顯的特徵嗎？<span class="definition">2 [C] 一種品質（或情感）的標誌或跡象：</span>Such coolness under pressure is the mark of a champion. 在壓力下仍能這樣保持冷靜，這就是冠軍相。/ The experience left a deep mark on her memory. 那段經歷在她的記憶中留下了深深的烙印。/ On the day of the funeral businesses remained closed as a mark of respect. 葬禮那天，各行業都停業以示敬意。/ Women are continuing to make their mark in business. 女性繼續在商界佔據一席之地。
           
<span class="vocabulary">**landmark**</span> [ˈlændmɑ:k; 美 -mɑ:rk]
<span class="definition">n. 1 [C] 地標（是一個顯眼的建築物或地理特徵，能幫助人們判斷自己所處的位置，或幫助人們判斷別的建築物的位置）：</span>The Ambassador Hotel is a Los Angeles landmark. 國賓大飯店是洛杉磯的地標性建築之一。/ The Empire State Building is a familiar landmark on the New York skyline. 帝國大廈是人們熟悉的紐約高樓大廈中的地標。/ After twenty years all the familiar landmarks had disappeared. 20年過去了，所有熟悉的地標都已蕩然無存。/ distinctive（尤英）, prominent landmark 顯眼的地表；突出的地標 / famous, well-known landmark 著名的地標 / city, local landmark 城市地標；當地地標 / architectural landmark 標誌性建築 / The building has been designated a historical landmark by the Commission. 大樓已經被委員會指定為歷史地標。/ landmark building 地標建築 / The residents are seeking landmark status for the building.（landmark status尤為美式用法）居民們正在為大樓爭取地標身份。/ The tower was once a landmark for ships. 那座塔曾經是指引航船的陸標。<span class="definition">2 [C] ~ (in sth) 抽象意義上的里程碑、轉折點（標誌著發展過程中的一個重要階段）：</span>The ceasefire was seen as a major landmark in the fight against terrorism. 停火協定被看作是與恐怖主義鬥爭的重要里程碑。/ a landmark ruling in the courts 具有里程碑意義的法庭裁決 / a landmark arms control treaty 里程碑式的軍備控制條約 / The baby was one of the big landmarks in our relationship. 孩子的出世是我們倆關係中的一個重要轉折點。/ The Russian Revolution represents a landmark in world history. 俄國革命是世界歷史上的一個里程碑。/ landmark decision, landmark legislation（美）, landmark ruling 劃時代的判決；里程碑式的立法；劃時代的裁決 / a landmark decision on gay marriage 關於同性婚姻的劃時代判決 / landmark case 標誌性判例 / landmark event 標誌性事件 / landmark achievement 標誌性成就 / landmark study 標誌性研究 / The piece is an important landmark in the history of music. 該作品是音樂史上一座重要的里程碑。<span class="definition">3 [C] [尤美] 有歷史意義（因而需要保護）的建築物（或遺址）：</span>historic landmark 有歷史意義的地標

<span class="vocabulary">**milestone**</span> [ˈmaɪlstəʊn; 美 -stoʊn]
<span class="definition">n. 1 [C]（也可用milepost[尤美]）（某事發展過程中的）一個極其重要的階段或事件，即里程碑、標誌性事件、轉折點：</span>He said the launch of the party represented a milestone in Zambian history. 他說該黨的創立是尚比亞歷史上的一個里程碑。/ Starting school is a milestone for both children and parents. 對於孩子和家長而言，開始上學是一件大事。/ another grim milestone in the war 戰爭中的另一嚴峻轉折點 / personal milestone 個人的里程碑 / career, development milestone 職業的里程碑；發展中的里程碑 / The company passed the £6 million milestone this year. 今年公司跨過了600萬英鎊的大關。/ It represents a major medical milestone. 這代表著醫學發展中的一個重要里程碑。/ The movie proved to be a milestone in the history of cinema. 事實證明這部影片是電影史上的一個里程碑。/ a milestone for Chinese technology 中國科技發展的一個里程碑 <span class="definition">2 [C]（路邊顯示到某地距離的）里程碑：</span>瞭解即可
           
<span class="vocabulary">**turning point**</span>
<span class="definition">n. [C] ~ (in sth)（事件或過程的）轉折點、轉機：</span>The promotion marked a turning point in her career. 這次提升標誌著她事業上的轉折點。/ It marks a great turning point in your lives. 這標誌著你生活中一個重大的轉折點。/ The vote yesterday appears to mark something of a turning point in the war. 昨天的投票似乎標誌著戰爭中的某個轉折點。/ The election represented a major turning point in American history. 這次選舉是美國歷史上一個重大的轉折。/ Hungary's opening of the border was a turning point for the refugees. 匈牙利開放邊境對難民來說是個轉機。/ This win could prove to be a historic turning point (= one that will be remembered）in the fortunes of the team. 這次勝利將可能是球隊命運的歷史性轉折點。/ political turning point 政治轉折點 / The industrial revolution was a major historical turning point. 工業革命是一個重要的歷史轉折點。/ In 2001 the world reached a turning point in its history. 2001年世界到達了一個歷史轉折點。/ The turning point came when reinforcements arrived from the south. 南方的增援部隊趕到，事態出現了轉機。/ The process of disarmament is at a crucial turning point. 裁軍程序正處於一個關鍵的轉折點。
           
<span class="vocabulary">**crossroads**</span> ['krɒsrəʊdz]  
<span class="definition">n. [sing.] 一個程序中的緊要關頭（在該關頭的選擇將決定下一步的走向）：</span>The company was clearly at a crossroads. 該公司顯然正處於緊要關頭。/ He had reached a crossroads in his career. 他的職業生涯發展到了一個十字路口。/ We are standing at an important crossroads in the history of our species. 我們正處於人類歷史上一個重要的轉折點。

<span class="vocabulary">**watershed**</span> [ˈwɔ:təʃed; 美 ˈwɔ:tərʃed; ˈwɑ:t-]
<span class="definition">n. [C] ~ (in sth) 比喻意義上的分水嶺，即標誌著重大變化的事件或時期，也可稱為轉折點：</span>historic, important, major watershed 歷史性的轉折點；重要分水嶺；主要分水嶺 / cultural, historical, political watershed 文化的分水嶺；歷史的轉折點；政治分水嶺 / Darwin's theory of evolution was a watershed dividing the old way of thinking from the new. 達爾文的進化論是劃分新舊思維方式的分水嶺。/ a watershed moment in recent music history 近代音樂中的轉折時刻 / The completion of this project was a watershed event in the company's history. 完成這個專案是公司歷史上的里程碑事件。/ a watershed year for Japan 對日本而言具有轉折意義的一年 / watershed project 具有轉折意義的專案 / watershed decision 具有轉折意義的決定 / The 19th century marked a watershed between the country's agricultural past and its industrial future. 19世紀是該國從農業國走向工業國的轉折點。/ The granting of the vote represented a watershed for the rights of women. 獲得選舉權標誌著女權運動的轉折點。/ With the strike, a historical watershed in the development of the trade union movement was reached. 這次罷工使工會運動發展到了一個歷史轉折點。/ The middle decades of the 19th century marked a watershed in Russia's history. 19世紀中葉標誌著俄國曆史的轉折點。/ The election of Mary Robinson in 1990 was a watershed in Irish politics. 1990年瑪麗·魯賓遜的當選是愛爾蘭政治上的一個轉折點。/ Tonight could prove to be a watershed for the international career of Barnes. 今晚將可能成為巴恩斯國際職業生涯的轉折點。

<span class="vocabulary">**fingerprint**</span> [ˈfɪŋgəprɪnt; 美 -gərp-]
<span class="definition">n. [C] 指紋；指印（通常用於警察識別罪犯）：</span>The detective discovered no fewer than 35 fingerprints. 那位偵探發現了至少35個指紋。/ his fingerprint on the murder weapon 他留在兇器上的指紋 / The police were able to obtain a set of fingerprints from the suspect. 警方能夠提取到疑犯留下的一套指紋。/ She was careful not to leave any fingerprints. 她小心翼翼以免留下指紋。/ The car is being examined for fingerprints. 正在檢查車上是否留下了指紋。/ The investigator questioned him and took his fingerprints. 審查員詢問了他並提取了他的指紋。/ The suspect's fingerprints have been matched with those found at the scene of the crime. 已將疑犯的指紋和犯罪現場找到的指紋進行比對。/ fingerprint reader, fingerprint scanner 指紋讀取器；指紋掃描器 / fingerprint analysis, fingerprint identification, fingerprint recognition 指紋分析；指紋辨別；指紋識別 / Many states have their own fingerprint databases. 許多州都有自己的指紋庫。
           
<span class="vocabulary">**footprint**</span> [ˈfʊtprɪnt]
<span class="definition">n. [usually pl.] 動物的腳印，或鞋子留下的足跡：</span>muddy footprints on the kitchen floor 廚房地板上的泥腳印 / a set of footprints in the sand 沙地裡的一串腳印 / The intruder had left some footprints in the snow. 闖入者在雪地裡留下了一些腳印。/ Her wet boots made footprints on the dusty floor. 她溼漉漉的靴子在佈滿灰塵的地上留下了腳印。/ The detective found fresh footprints in the mud next to the victim. 偵探在受害者旁邊的泥地上發現了新腳印。/ They had fled through the snow, and police had followed their footprints. 他們穿過雪地逃跑了，警察順著腳印追蹤。/ The wet footprints led back to the pool. 溼漉漉的腳印指回到水池邊。
           
<span class="vocabulary">**footstep**</span> [ˈfʊtstep]
<span class="definition">n. [usually pl.] 足跡：</span>footsteps in the snow 雪地上的足跡 / My father is extremely proud that I followed in his footsteps and became a doctor.（比喻用法）我的父親非常自豪，因為我繼承他的事業，成為了一名醫生。
 
<span class="vocabulary">**stamp**</span> [stæmp] 
<span class="definition">n. 1 [C] 印章印出來的印記：</span>visa stamps 簽證章 / to have stamp of approval（比喻用法）獲得批准 / Congress has given its stamp of approval to the budget. 國會已經批准該預算。<span class="definition">2 [sing.] [正式] stamp (of sth) 某種品質的或某個人的標記、痕跡、烙印、特徵：</span>the stamp of our personality 我們個性的印記 / the stamp of authority 權威的標誌 / to bear the stamp of Wall Street 帶有華爾街的印記 / to bear the stamp of truth 像是真實的 / President Reagan left an indelible stamp on the nation. 里根總統給這個國家留下了不可磨滅的烙印。/ His work bears the unmistakable stamp of genius. 他的作品清楚地展示出他的天才。
                      
<span class="vocabulary">**imprint**</span> [ɪmˈprɪnt]
<span class="definition">n. 1 [C] ~ (of sth) (in/on sth) 印記；壓印；痕跡：</span>the imprint of a foot in the sand 沙灘上的足跡 / Glaciation has left a permanent imprint on the landscape. 冰川作用在地貌上留下了永久的痕跡。/ Their products bear the imprint of Japanese design. 他們的產品帶有日本人設計的印記。<span class="definition">2 [usually sing.] [正式] ~ (of sth) (on sb/sth)（一個人或經歷對一個地方或形勢產生的）持久影響：</span>Both King and Gandhi were tremendously brave men whose unique form of courage left a lasting imprint on their nations' histories. 金和甘地都是勇敢無畏的人，他們獨一無二的勇氣在他們各自國家的歷史上留下了深遠影響。/ The city bears the imprint of Japanese investment. 該市帶有日本投資的深刻印記。/ The sinister atmosphere of the place left an indelible imprint on my memory. 這個地方兇險的氛圍讓我終生難忘。
 
<span class="vocabulary">**furrow**</span> [ˈfʌrəʊ; 美 ˈfɜ:roʊ]
<span class="definition">n. [C] 車轍：</span>Dirt bike trails crisscrossed the grassy furrows. 越野摩托車的輪跡縱橫交錯地佈滿條條草溝。
           
<span class="vocabulary">**rut**</span> [rʌt]
<span class="definition">n. [C] 車轍：</span>Our driver slowed up as we approached the ruts in the road. 當駛近路上的車轍時，我們的司機放慢了速度。/ the deep ruts left by the trucks' heavy wheels 卡車沉重的輪子留下的很深的車轍 / wheel rut 車轍 / wagon rut（尤美）載重馬車車轍
           
<span class="vocabulary">**groove**</span> [gru:v]
<span class="definition">n. [C] 車轍：</span>Their wheels left grooves in the ground. 他們的車輪在地面上留下了車轍印。

<span class="vocabulary">**track**</span> [træk] 
<span class="definition">n. [C, usually pl.] 指人、動物或車輛在行走時在地面上留下的痕跡：</span>animal, car, tyre/tire track 動物足跡；車轍；輪胎印 / Rabbits had left tracks in the snow. 兔子在雪地上留下了足跡。/ He had been careless, and had done little to cover his tracks.（比喻用法）他很粗心，沒怎麼隱匿自己的行蹤。
             
<span class="vocabulary">**trail**</span> [treɪl]
<span class="definition">n. 1 [C] 某人或某事物留下的一長串的痕跡、蹤跡：</span>a trail of blood 一連串血跡 / blood, smoke, vapour trail 血跡；煙跡；（飛機在高空飛行時產生的）凝結尾跡象 / muddy trail 泥痕 / wet trail 溼痕 / false trail 偽造的痕跡 / The couple laid a false trail to escape the paparazzi. 為了逃避狗仔隊，那對情侶偽造了蹤跡。/ The tourists left a trail of litter behind them. 遊客留下了一路的垃圾。/ Everywhere in the house was a sticky trail of orange juice. 家裡到處是潑灑的橙汁留下的黏糊糊的痕跡。/ The hurricane passed, leaving a trail of devastation in its wake. 颶風過後，滿目瘡痍。/ He left a trail of clues at the scenes of his crimes. 他在犯罪現場留下了一些蛛絲馬跡。/ They had to find the kidnappers before the trail went cold. 他們必須線上索變得模糊之前找到綁架者。<span class="definition">2 [C] 尤指打獵時跟蹤的足跡、蹤跡或氣味：</span>The hounds were following the fox's trail. 獵犬追蹤著狐狸的氣味。/ The dog had picked up the trail of a rabbit. 狗嗅到了兔子留下的氣味。/ The fox had crossed a stream, and the hounds lost the trail. 狐狸穿過了小溪，獵狗失去了它的臭跡。/ Ants follow a scent trail laid down previously. 螞蟻循著上次留下的臭跡爬。/ Fortunately the trail was still warm (= clear and easy to follow). 慶幸的是痕跡仍然清晰可循。         
           
<span class="vocabulary">**trace**</span> [treɪs] 
<span class="definition">n. 1 [C, U] 表明某人或某事物過去曾存在或出現的一個標記、物體或跡象，如遺蹟、痕跡、蹤跡：</span>archaeological, historical trace 考古遺蹟；歷史遺蹟 / indelible, permanent trace 抹不掉的印記；永久的印記 / memory trace（術語）記憶痕跡 / It's exciting to discover traces of earlier civilizations. 發現以前文明的遺蹟，真令人興奮。/ The local church has traces of fifteenth-century frescoes. 當地教堂仍留有 15 世紀壁畫的遺蹟。/ Little trace is left of how Stone Age people lived. 石器時代人類的生活狀況幾乎無跡可尋。/ Traces still remain of the old brewery. 老酒廠仍然有跡可尋。/ There's been no trace of my aunt and uncle. 沒有我姑姑、姑夫的蹤跡。/ Police searched the area but found no trace of the escaped prisoners. 警方搜尋了那一地區，但未發現越獄逃犯的任何蹤跡。/ e burglar had left several traces of his presence. 竊賊在現場留下了幾處痕跡。/ The search party had found no trace of the missing climbers. 搜救隊沒有找到失蹤登山者的蹤跡。/ Remove all traces of rust with a small wire brush. 用小鋼絲刷擦去所有鏽跡。/ Years of living in England had eliminated all trace of her American accent. 她因多年居住在英國，美國口音已蕩然無存。/ ‘No thanks,’ she said, with a trace of irritation in her voice. “不，謝了。”她說，聲音裡帶著一絲惱怒。/ without trace（英）, without a trace 無影無蹤 / The plane was lost without a trace over the Atlantic. 飛機在飛越大西洋上空時消失得無影無蹤。/ The ship had vanished without (a) trace. 那艘船消失得無影無蹤。/ The ship seems to have sunk without trace. 那艘船似乎已經沉沒，無跡可尋。<span class="definition">2 [sing.] sink without (a) ~（某人或某事物）迅速銷聲匿跡、迅速沉寂：</span>The Social Democratic Party has sunk without trace at these elections. 社會民主黨在這些選舉中已銷聲匿跡。/ Pop groups are like Olympic swimmers — they hit gold once, then they sink without trace. 流行樂隊就像奧林匹克游泳選手——拿到一次金牌後就無聲無息了。

<span class="vocabulary">**sign**</span> [saɪn]
<span class="definition">n. [C, U] ~ (of sth) | ~ (that…)（某事存在、發生或可能將發生的）徵兆、預兆、跡象：</span>Headaches may be a sign of stress. 頭痛可能是緊張的跡象。/ early signs of pregnancy 懷孕的早期徵兆 / The gloomy weather shows no sign of improving. 陰沉的天氣沒有絲毫轉晴的跡象。/ Her work is showing some signs of improvement. 她的工作出現了一些改進的跡象。/ There was no sign of life in the house. 那座房子沒有一點住人的跡象。/ a warning sign 預警徵兆 / He was silent. It was a good sign. 他沒有說話，這是個好兆頭。/ to point out the danger signs 指出危險徵兆 / The first signs of spring appeared. 飄來了第一縷春天的氣息。/ All the signs pointed to it being more than just a coincidence. 所有的跡象表明這不僅僅是一個巧合。/ He disappeared at the first sign of trouble. 一有麻煩的苗頭他就溜之大吉。/ There is no sign of John anywhere. 哪兒都沒有約翰的影子。
             
<span class="vocabulary">**warning**</span> [ˈwɔ:nɪŋ; 美 ˈwɔ:rn-]
<span class="definition">n. [C, U] 預兆（預示著某件壞事可能將要發生）：</span>The bridge collapsed without (any) warning. 那座橋在沒有任何先兆的情況下坍塌了。

<span class="vocabulary">**omen**</span> [ˈəʊmən; 美 ˈoʊ-]
<span class="definition">n. [C] ~ (for sth) 某事（可以是好事，也可以是壞事）發生的預兆、前兆、徵兆：</span>good omen 好兆頭 / bad, evil, ill omen 不樣之兆；凶兆 / They took the storm as a bad omen. 他們把那場暴風雨看作不詳的預兆。/ The smiling faces were a good omen for the future of the restaurant. 那些滿帶微笑的臉預示了這家餐廳美好的未來。/ an omen of death 死亡的徵兆 / The omens for their future success are not good. 他們未來成功的預兆不祥。/ Her appearance at this moment is an omen of disaster. 她在此刻出現預示著一場災難。/ Could this at last be a good omen for peace? 這是否終於可以視作和平的吉兆了？

<span class="vocabulary">**metaphor**</span> [ˈmetəfə(r)]
<span class="definition">n. [C, U] 象徵、標誌：</span>The divided family remains a powerful metaphor for a society that continued to tear itself apart. 破碎的家庭仍舊是一個繼續分崩離析的社會的有力象徵。

<span class="vocabulary">**pointer**</span> [ˈpɔɪntə(r)]
<span class="definition">n. <span class="vocabulary">**hint**</span> [hɪnt]
