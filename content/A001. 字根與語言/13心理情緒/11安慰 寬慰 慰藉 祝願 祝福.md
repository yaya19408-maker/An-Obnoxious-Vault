☀ <span class="category">**安慰、寬慰：**</span>
<span class="vocabulary">**comfort**</span> ['kʌmfət] 
<span class="definition">n. 1 [U]（心靈上的）安慰、寬慰或慰藉（不感到那麼多的痛苦、憂慮或不快）：</span>a few words of comfort 幾句安慰的話 / comfort food 安慰性食品 / spiritual comfort 精神慰藉 / The sought comfort in each other. 他們相互尋求慰藉。/ She found comfort in music. 她在音樂中找到了慰藉。/ We took great comfort from the fact that our savings were safe. 讓我們感到安慰的是我們的存款是安全的。<span class="definition">2 [sing.] 令（處於苦難、憂慮或不快中的）人感到安慰的人（或事物）：</span>The children have been a great comfort to me through all of this. 在我經歷這一切的日子裡，孩子們一直是我的一個巨大安慰。/ It’s a comfort to know that she is safe. 知道她安然無恙是件令人寬慰的事。<span class="definition">vt. 用仁慈和同情幫某人紓解擔憂和不快的心情，即安慰，寬慰，撫慰：</span>He is being comforted by family and friends. 家人和朋友們正在安慰他。/ She comforted herself with the thought that it would soon be spring. 她想到春天很快就要來臨，以此來寬慰自己。
           
<span class="vocabulary">**solace**</span> [ˈsɒləs; 美 ˈsɑ:ləs]
<span class="definition">n. 1 [U] [正式] 安慰、慰藉：</span>He sought solace in the whisky bottle. 他借酒澆愁。/ Henry was inclined to seek solace in drink. 亨利往往借酒消愁。/ She turned to Rob for solace. 她到羅布那兒尋求慰藉。/ I found solace in writing when my father died three years ago. 我父親三年前去世的時候，我從寫作中找到了安慰。/ spiritual solace 精神慰藉 / The news brought no solace to the grieving relatives. 這個訊息並未給悲痛的家屬帶來什麼安慰。/ Unable to leave his bed, he found some solace in reading about other people's travels. 他雖然不能下床，但是閱讀他人的遊記讓他得到了一些慰藉。/ They sought solace in religion from the harshness of their everyday lives. 他們日常生活很艱難，就在宗教中尋求慰藉。/ I take solace in the fact that he died in peace. 他死得很平靜，這一事實讓我聊以自慰。/ His career took a nosedive and he turned to drugs for solace. 他的職業生涯一落千丈，開始吸毒以尋求慰藉。<span class="definition">2 [sing.] [正式] 給人以安慰的人或事物：</span>His grandchildren were a solace in his old age. 他晚年從孫兒們身上得到安慰。/ She found the companionship of Marcia a solace. 她覺得馬西婭的陪伴是莫大的安慰。<span class="definition">vt. [文學] 安慰：</span>She smiled, as though solaced by the memory. 她笑了，彷彿在往事的回憶中得到了安慰。           
         
<span class="vocabulary">**reassurance**</span> [ˌri:əˈʃʊərəns; -ˈʃɔ:r-; 美 -ˈʃʊr-]
<span class="definition">n. 1 [U] ~ (that…)（讓人消除疑慮或擔憂的）保證或安慰：</span>to give/provide/offer reassurance 表明肯定的態度 / 'You really won't tell?' she asked, begging for reassurance. “你真的不說嗎？”她問道，乞求得到肯定答覆。/ She needed reassurance that she belonged somewhere. 她需要一種歸屬感讓她安心。/ Consumers need a little reassurance after all this bad publicity. 在這麼多的負面宣傳之後，消費者需要一點點安慰。/ She received little reassurance or sympathy. 她幾乎沒有得到安慰或同情。/ Continual calm reassurance should be given. 應不斷給予撫慰。/ He glanced at her seeking reassurance. 他看了她一眼，想得到安慰。/ She found reassurance in the high attendance at her lectures. 來聽她講座的人很多，這使她感到很欣慰。/ A system of beliefs can bring you reassurance at times of stress. 信仰可以在承受壓力時給人信心。/ He held onto her hand for reassurance. 他握住她的手以尋求安慰。/ I patted her shoulder in reassurance. 我拍拍她的肩以示安慰。/ The company tried to offer reassurance on the safety of its products. 該公司試圖讓人打消對其產品安全性的顧慮。/ A negative test result may give false reassurance. 檢查結果呈陰性可能會讓人放鬆警惕。<span class="definition">2 [C] ~ (that…) 讓人消除疑慮或擔憂的話語或行動，如保證：</span>We have been given reassurances that the water is safe to drink. 我們得到保證說這水適合飲用。/ We have had some reassurances from the council that the building will be saved. 市政會保證會保留那座建築，這使我們得到了些許安慰。/ reassurances that pesticides are not harmful 殺蟲劑沒有危害的保證 / reassurances from the researchers about their work 研究人員對他們工作的肯定 / She kept looking in the mirror despite my constant reassurances that her hair looked fine. 儘管我一直安慰她說頭髮看上去很好，她還是不停地照鏡子。|
 
<span class="vocabulary">**closure**</span> [ˈkləʊʒə(r); 美 ˈkloʊ-]
<span class="definition">n. [U]（因困境結束或事情得到妥善處理等的）寬慰；如釋重負：</span>The conviction of their son's murderer helped to give them a sense of closure. 謀殺兒子的兇手被判罪，讓他們得到了一些安慰。/ I asked McKeown if the reunion was meant to achieve closure. 我問麥基翁重聚是不是為了尋求解脫。/ People who lose family members need closure, not false hope. 失去家人的人們需要安慰，而不是虛假的希望。/ The conviction of their son' murderer gave them a sense of closure. 殺害他們兒子的兇手被定罪讓他們了卻了一樁心願。
           
<span class="vocabulary">**consolation**</span> [ˌkɒnsəˈleɪʃn; 美 ˌkɑ:n-]
<span class="definition">n. [U, C] 使感到安慰的人（或事）；安慰；慰藉。相當於comfort：</span>small consolation 小小的安慰 / a few words of consolation 幾句安慰的話 / If it's any consolation, she didn't get the job, either. 不知道這算不算得上安慰，她也沒有得到那份工作。/ The children were a great consolation to him when his wife died. 他妻子去世後，幾個孩子就是他極大的安慰。/ She had the consolation of coming second in her last race of the day. 使她感到慰藉的是，她在那天參加的最後一項賽跑比賽中獲得了第二名。/ She tends to seek consolation in food.她常在食物中尋求慰藉。/ He drew little consolation from this fact.（draw尤為英式用法）這個事實沒有給他帶來什麼安慰。/ When her mother died，she found consolation in her religious beliefs. 她母親去世後，她在宗教信仰中找到了安慰。/ Although we lost the game, we took some consolation from the fact that we played well. 儘管輸了比賽，但我們表現還不錯，這讓我們得到一些安慰。/ The next game will probably offer them the consolation of winning. 下一場比賽很可能會給他們帶來獲勝的安慰。/ This news was of little consolation to us. 這個訊息對我們來說沒有什麼安慰作用。/ If it's any consolation to you, the weather here is also awful. 這裡的天氣也很糟，不知這是不是對你來說算點兒安慰。/ When she lost her job, her only consolation was that she had some savings in the bank. 她失去工作後、唯一的安慰就是銀行裡還有些積蓄。

<span class="vocabulary">**console**</span> [kənˈsəʊl; 美 -ˈsoʊl]
<span class="definition">vt. ~ sb/yourself (with sth) 對不悅或失望的人給予安慰或同情，即安慰、撫慰：</span>Nothing could console him when his wife died. 他妻子去世後，什麼事情也不能使他感到寬慰。/ She put a consoling arm around his shoulders. 她摟住他肩膀以示安慰。/ Console yourself with the thought that you did your best. 你可以安慰自己的是你已經盡了最大的努力。/ I didn't like lying but I consoled myself that it was for a good cause. 我不願意撒謊，但我安慰自己那是出於好意。/ ‘Never mind, ’ Anne consoled her. “沒關係。”安妮安慰她說。/ 'Never mind, Ned,' he consoled me. “沒關係，內德。”他安慰我說。/ Often they cry, and I have to play the role of a mother, consoling them. 經常他們一哭，我就要充當母親的角色撫慰他們。
          
<span class="vocabulary">**soothe**</span> [su:ð]
<span class="definition">vt. 使某人由焦慮、煩惱等狀態變得更平靜，如安慰、撫慰：</span>The music soothed her for a while. 音樂讓她稍微安靜了一會兒。/ He would take her in his arms and soothe her. 他會把她攬在懷中撫慰她。/ It did not take long for the central bank to soothe investors' fears. 中央銀行很快便消除了投資者的擔憂。
           
<span class="vocabulary">**reassure**</span> [ˌri:əˈʃʊə(r); -ʃɔ:(r); 美 -ˈʃʊr]
<span class="definition">vt. ~ sb (about sth) 使某人安心、打消某人的恐懼或疑慮：</span>The doctor reassured him that there was nothing seriously wrong. 醫生安慰他說，沒什麼嚴重的病。/ I tried to reassure her, 'Don't worry about it. We won't let it happen again.' 我試圖使她安心，“不要擔心。我們不會讓它再次發生了。” / She just reassured me that everything was fine. 她剛剛讓我放心，說一切都好。/ He was constantly reassuring himself that he had acted for the best. 他不斷地安慰自己已經盡力做到最好了。/ The report will do much to reassure parents of children at the school. 這一報告會給該校學生的家長以莫大安慰。/ They tried to reassure the public about the safety of public transport. 他們試圖向公眾保證公共交通的安全性。/ She needed to be reassured of his love for her. 她需要證實他是愛她的。/ He reassured her with a pat on the arm. 他拍拍她的胳膊以示安慰。/ Kate nodded, but she didn't feel reassured. 凱特點了點頭，卻並未釋然。/ Often parents simply need reassuring that their children are happy at school. 家長往往只需要知道孩子在學校很快樂就放心了。
           
<span class="vocabulary">**comforting**</span> [ˈkʌmfətɪŋ; 美 -fərt-]
<span class="definition">adj. 令人感到安慰、欣慰或慰藉的：</span>her comforting words 她說的那些令人安慰的話 / She found his voice strangely comforting. 不知為何，她覺得他的聲音讓人感到欣慰。/ warm and comforting 溫暖並令人欣慰 / It's comforting to know that you'll be there. 知道你要去那裡令人感到欣慰。/ My mother had just died and I found the book very comforting. 我母親剛去世，我從這本書中獲取了不少慰藉。/ In the midst of his feelings of impotence, a comforting thought arrived. 正在他覺得很無奈的時候，一個令人欣慰的想法冒了出來。
           
<span class="vocabulary">**reassuring**</span> [ˌri:əˈʃʊərɪŋ; -ˈʃɔ:r-; 美 -ˈʃʊr-]
<span class="definition">adj.（某人的言語或行為）令人放心的、令人欣慰的（即令人的焦慮感減少的，或讓人對某事更加確定的）：</span>a reassuring smile 使人信心倍增的微笑 / It's reassuring (to know) that we've got the money if necessary. 我們有了應急的錢，這就不必擔心了。/ It was reassuring to hear John's familiar voice. 聽到約翰熟悉的聲音讓人放心。/ She gave me some reassuring news. 她告訴了我一些令我欣慰的訊息。/ Her comment--You can always try again if you fail--was hardly reassuring. 她那句"失敗後總可以再來"的話很難令人安心。
           
<span class="vocabulary">**soothing**</span> [su:ðɪŋ;美ˈsuðɪŋ]
<span class="definition">adj. 慰藉的；使人寬心的：</span>Her touch felt wonderfully soothing. 她的觸控令人感到很寬心。/ There was something quite soothing about her being there. 她人在那兒就讓人覺得非常安心。

<span class="vocabulary">**well**</span> [wel] 
<span class="definition">exclamation. 用於表達寬慰：</span>Well, thank goodness that’s over! 哎，謝天謝地，這事兒總算過去了！
     
周邊詞彙補充：
· mollify可表示使某人感到不再那麼憤怒，即撫慰、安撫；
· pacify可表示使憤怒或煩惱的人變得平靜。

☀ <span class="category">**祝願、祝福：**</span>
<span class="vocabulary">**wish**</span> [wɪʃ] 
<span class="definition">vt. 表示你希望某人快樂、幸運等，即祝願，祝：</span>I wished her a happy birthday. 我祝她生日快樂。/ Wish me luck! 祝我好運吧！<span class="definition">n. [pl.] wishes (for sth)（尤用於書信或賀卡中）表示你希望某人快樂、幸福或成功，即祝願，祝福：</span>With best wishes 祝好（如信中結尾語）/ Give my best wishes to Tom. 代我向湯姆致以良好的祝願。/ Every good wish for your future happiness together. 預祝你們將來幸福美滿。
