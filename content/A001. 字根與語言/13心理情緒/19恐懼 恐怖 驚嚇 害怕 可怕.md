☀ <span class="category">**可怕的、令人害怕的：**</span>
<span class="vocabulary">**dread**</span> [dred]
<span class="definition">adj. 可怕的、令人畏懼的：</span>a more effective national policy to combat this dread disease 對抗這可怕疾病的更有效的國家政策

<span class="vocabulary">**horrible**</span> ['hɒrəbl] 
<span class="definition">adj. 使人感到非常震驚或恐懼的，相當於terrible。略不如terrible常用：</span>horrible crime, horrible nightmare 駭人聽聞的罪行；可怕的噩夢 / a pretty horrible experience 一段相當恐怖的經歷 / It’s just horrible thinking about it. 想到這一點確實可怕。

<span class="vocabulary">**terrible**</span> ['terəbl] 
<span class="definition">adj. 非常令人不快的；使人感覺到非常不愉快、煩惱或害怕的。程度較重。是事物或事件給人的一種印象，而非人本身的一種心境：</span>terrible experience 令人極不愉快的經歷 / What terrible news! 多麼駭人聽聞的訊息！/ I’ve just had a terrible thought. 我剛剛產生了一個可怕的想法。/ He suddenly collapsed--it was simply terrible. 他突然昏倒了——簡直太可怕了。
                          
<span class="vocabulary">**dreadful**</span> [ˈdredfl]
<span class="definition">adj. [尤英] [常用於名詞前] 令人恐懼的：</span>a dreadful accident 可怕的事故       
           
<span class="vocabulary">**horror**</span> [ˈhɒrə(r); 美 ˈhɔ:r-; ˈhɑ:r-]
<span class="definition">adj.（經歷或事件）嚇人的：</span>a horror story about lost luggage while flying 乘飛機旅行時丟失行李的糟糕經歷 / They were trying to scare each other with horror stories about going to the dentist. 他們講述看牙醫的恐飾故事，互相嚇唬。

<span class="vocabulary">**appalling**</span> [əˈpɔ:lɪŋ]
<span class="definition">adj. 令人震驚的；令人驚駭的：</span>瞭解即可

<span class="vocabulary">**fearful**</span> [ˈfɪəfl; 美 ˈfɪrfl]
<span class="definition">adj. [正式] [只用於名詞前]（形勢）可怕的、嚇人的：</span>the fearful consequences which might flow from unilateral military moves 單方面軍事行動可能造成的可怕後果
           
<span class="vocabulary">**frightening**</span> [ˈfraɪtnɪŋ]
<span class="definition">adj. 讓人害怕的、可怕的：</span>frightening experience, frightening prospect, frightening thought 可怕的經歷；可怕的景象；可怕的想法 / It's frightening to think it could happen again. 想到此事可能再次發生就使人不寒而慄。/ The number of youngsters involved in crime is frightening. 年輕人犯罪的人數多得嚇人。/ The situation was getting frightening. 局勢變得可怕起來。/ What can we do to make the experience less frightening? 我們怎樣才能使這事兒不那麼嚇人呢?/ He found the responsibility rather frightening. 他覺得責任過重，讓人恐懼。/ This is extremely frightening for elderly people. 這對老年人來說極其可怕。/ It was all very frightening to a small boy. 這對一個小男孩來說非常可怕。

<span class="vocabulary">**awful**</span> ['ɔ:fl] 
<span class="definition">adj. 使人非常震驚的，即駭人聽聞的，可怕的，相當於terrible。不太常用：</span>the awful horrors of war 駭人聽聞的戰爭恐怖
           
<span class="vocabulary">**alarming**</span> [əˈlɑ:mɪŋ; 美 əˈlɑ:rm-]
<span class="definition">adj. 使人恐慌的、使人擔憂的：</span>an alarming increase in crime 犯罪活動駭人的增加 / The rainforests are disappearing at an alarming rate. 雨林正以驚人的速度消失。/ I find the prospect of being without work extremely alarming. 我預期可能會失業，覺得特別害怕。
           
<span class="vocabulary">**gruesome**</span> [ˈgru:səm]
<span class="definition">adj.（常與死亡或受傷相聯絡而）令人厭惡且恐懼的：</span>a gruesome murder 駭人聽聞的謀殺案 / gruesome pictures of dead bodies 恐怖的死人照片 / There has been a series of gruesome murders in the capital. 在首都發生了一連串讓人毛骨悚然的謀殺案。/ We spent a week in a gruesome apartment in Miami.（幽默用法）我們在邁阿密一套糟糕透頂的公寓裡住了一星期。

<span class="vocabulary">**sick**</span> [sɪk] 
<span class="definition">adj. [非正式]（笑話或故事等）令人毛骨悚然的，尤指其中還帶著幽默的：</span>a sick joke about a cat 有關貓的一個令人毛骨悚然的笑話 / That’s really sick. 那真夠嚇人的。
           
<span class="vocabulary">**creepy**</span> [ˈkri:pi]
<span class="definition">adj. [非正式] 令人毛骨悚然的；令人不寒而慄的：</span>a creepy ghost story 令人毛骨悚然的鬼故事 / It's kind of creepy down in the cellar! 地窖裡真令人有點不寒而慄！/ It can get pretty creepy in the cellar at night. 晚上地窖裡會變得非常恐怖。/ There were certain places that were really creepy at night. 有些地方晚上真是讓人不寒而慄。/ It feels a little creepy in here. 這裡有點兒令人毛骨悚然。/ Was she still married to that creepy guy, Dennis? 她和那個讓人毛骨悚然的傢伙丹尼斯還沒離婚嗎？
                      
<span class="vocabulary">**horrific**</span> [həˈrɪfɪk]
<span class="definition">adj.（攻擊、事故、傷勢等）極其可怕的：</span>Some of the scenes we witnessed were quite horrific. 我們親眼目睹的場面之中有些令人毛骨悚然。

<span class="vocabulary">**spooky**</span> [ˈspu:ki]
<span class="definition">adj. [非正式] 怪異嚇人的。相當於creepy：</span>a spooky old house 陰森森的老房子 / The whole place has a slightly spooky atmosphere. 整個地方的氣氛有點陰森可怖。/ I was just thinking about her when she phoned. Spooky! 我正在想她，她就來電話了。你說怪不怪！/ It got a little spooky when James started telling ghost stories. 詹姆斯開始講鬼故事的時候，有點兒讓人寒毛直豎了。

<span class="vocabulary">**weird**</span> [wɪəd; 美 wɪrd]
<span class="definition">adj. 怪異而神秘的、怪異而可怕的：</span>She began to make weird inhuman sounds. 她開始發出可怕的非人的聲音。           
           
<span class="vocabulary">**eerie**</span> [ˈɪəri; 美 ˈɪri]
<span class="definition">adj. 怪異、神秘且恐怖的：</span>I walked down the eerie dark path. 我走在那條漆黑恐怖的小路上。/ an eerie calm 靜得瘮人 / an eerie yellow light 神秘兮兮的黃燈 / I found the silence underwater really eerie. 我發覺水下的寂靜真令人恐怖。/ This place has a distinctly eerie atmosphere.（distinctly尤為英式用法）這個地方有一種明顯恐怖的氣氛。/ a silence so long that it was almost eerie 漫長得有些可怕的靜寂

<span class="vocabulary">**inhuman**</span> [ɪnˈhju:mən]
<span class="definition">adj. 非人類的、（似乎不是由人發出的因而）恐怖的、怪異可怕的：</span>There was a strange inhuman sound. 有一種不像人發出的奇怪聲音。/ inhuman shrieks that chilled my heart 讓我心驚膽寒的、異常恐怖的尖叫聲
           
<span class="vocabulary">**claustrophobic**</span> [ˌklɔ:strəˈfəʊbɪk; 美 -ˈfoʊ-]
<span class="definition">adj.（地方等）引起幽閉恐怖的：</span>the claustrophobic atmosphere of the room 房間裡的幽閉恐怖氣氛 / Crowds make me claustrophobic. 成群成群的人讓我感到幽閉恐怖。/ I find that building intensely claustrophobic. 我覺得那座建築物特別讓人有幽閉恐怖感。/ The atmosphere was somewhat claustrophobic. 氣氛有點兒幽閉恐怖。

周邊詞彙補充：
· threatening可指（行為）有威脅性的、表示恐嚇的；
· formidable可指（人、事物或情況）（由於太強大，或太令人印象深刻，或由於看似很困難而）可怕的（有時讓人尊敬的，此事可稱為令人敬畏的）。

☀ <span class="category">**恐懼、害怕的形容詞：**</span>
<span class="vocabulary">**afraid**</span> [ə'freɪd] 
<span class="definition">adj. afraid (of sb/sth) / afraid (of doing sth) / afraid (to do sth) 不用於名詞前，（由於認為可能會受傷或受苦而）害怕的，畏懼的。是人的一種心境：</span>Don’t be afraid. 別怕。/ Are you afraid of spiders? 你怕蜘蛛嗎？/ What has made you so deeply afraid of your boss? 是什麼讓你這麼害怕你的老闆？/ She is afraid of marriage. 她害怕婚姻。/ She was afraid to open the door. 她不敢開門。
           
<span class="vocabulary">**alarmed**</span> [əˈlɑ:md; 美 əˈlɑ:rmd]
<span class="definition">adj. [名詞前不可用] ~ (at/by sth)（對危險之事或壞事）害怕的：</span>She was alarmed at the prospect of travelling alone. 她一想到獨自旅行的情景就害怕。/ She began to grow alarmed when she realized how late it was. 她意識到天有多晚時，變得害怕起來。
           
<span class="vocabulary">**fearful**</span> [ˈfɪəfl; 美 ˈfɪrfl]
<span class="definition">adj. [正式] ~ (for sb) / ~ (of sth/of doing sth) / ~ (that)… 緊張且害怕的：</span>She was fearful that she would fail. 她生怕失敗。/ Bankers were fearful of a world banking crisis. 銀行家們害怕會發生一場全球銀行業危機。/ I had often been very fearful, very angry, and very isolated. 我以前經常感到非常恐懼，非常憤怒，非常孤獨。/ The experience had left her fearful and uncertain.這次經歷使她感到恐懼不安。/ He was fearful of every shadow. 每一處陰影都讓他害怕。
           
<span class="vocabulary">**frightened**</span> [ˈfraɪtnd]
<span class="definition">adj. ~ (of sth/of doing sth) / ~ (to do sth) / ~ (that…) afraid; feeling fear 害怕的；受驚嚇的：</span>a frightened child 受了驚嚇的小孩 / Don't be frightened. 別害怕。/ What are you frightened of? 你怕什麼？/ I'm frightened of walking home alone in the dark. 我害怕在黑夜單獨走路回家。/ She was frightened of flying. 她害怕坐飛機。/ He sounded frightened. 他聽起來受了驚。/ I'm too frightened to ask him now. 現在我嚇得不敢問他了。/ Miriam was too frightened to tell her family what had happened. 米麗婭姆太害怕了，不敢告訴家人發生了什麼事。/ She was frightened that the plane would crash. 她害怕飛機會墜毀。/ I'm frightened for him (= that he will be hurt, etc.). 我為他擔驚受怕。/ I'd never do that. I'd be frightened to death.（非正式）我絕不會幹。我會嚇死的。/ I got frightened when he lost his temper. 他大發脾氣，把我嚇壞了。/ I was nervous and frightened about the future. 我對將來感到不安和害怕。/ Most of us are frightened by our emotions. 我們中的大多數人對自己的情緒感到害怕。/ I'm rather frightened of dogs. 我有些怕狗。/ My friend is too frightened of my father to come to our house. 我的朋友太害怕我爸爸了，不敢到我家來。

<span class="vocabulary">**scared**</span> [skeəd; 美 skerd]
<span class="definition">adj. ~ (of sb/sth) / ~ (of doing sth) / ~ (to do sth) / ~ (that…) 害怕的、恐懼的、擔心的：</span>She is scared of going out alone. 她不敢一個人外出。/ I'm really scared of heights. 我非常恐高。/ I'm certainly not scared of him. 我當然不怕他。/ People are scared to use the buses late at night. 人們害怕在深夜乘坐公共汽車。/ I'm scared (that) I'm going to fall. 我擔心自己快掉下去了。/ I was scared that I might be sick. 我擔心自己可能病了。/ He was scared of letting us down. 他怕讓我們失望。/ The thieves got scared and ran away. 小偷害怕了，就跑了。/ a scared look 驚恐的表情 / I was scared to death (= very frightened). 我嚇得要死。/ I was too scared to move. 我嚇得動彈不得。/ We were scared stiff (= very frightened). 我們嚇得呆若木雞。/ He's scared stiff of the relationship breaking down. 他非常害怕關係破裂。/ They had their opponent running scared. 他們嚇得對手坐立不安。/ He wasn't sick, Sophie decided. He was just plain scared. 索菲斷定他沒病，只不過是受了驚嚇。/ It's only a little injection. It's nothing to be scared about. 只不過是打一小針而已，沒什麼好怕的。/ I'm feeling a little scared at the thought of the operation. 我一想到手術就有些害怕。
           
<span class="vocabulary">**terrified**</span> [ˈterɪfaɪd]
<span class="definition">adj. ~ (of sb/sth) / ~ (of doing sth) / ~ (that…) very frightened（內心）非常恐懼的、很害怕的：</span>He's terrified of spiders. 他非常害怕蜘蛛。/ She was absolutely terrified at the thought of jumping off the bridge. 她一想到從橋上跳下去就感到非常恐懼。/ He was terrified (that) he would fall. 他很害怕會跌倒。/ I'm terrified of losing you. 我真害怕失去你。/ She was terrified at the thought of being alone. 她一想到孤零零的獨自一人就驚恐不安。
           
<span class="vocabulary">**shaken**</span> [ˈʃeɪkən]
<span class="definition">adj. [名詞前不常用] 恐懼的：</span>The woman was left shaken by the attack. 這個女子受到襲擊嚇壞。

<span class="vocabulary">**shy**</span> [ʃaɪ] 
<span class="definition">adj. 1（動物）容易受到驚嚇且不願意靠近人的，即怕人的，膽小的：</span>The panda is a shy creature. 熊貓是一種膽小的動物。<span class="definition">2 shy of/about (doing) sth 不用於名詞前，害怕做某事的，或害怕捲入某事中的：</span>The band has never been shy of publicity. 這支樂隊一向不憚招搖。/ He disliked her and had never been shy of saying so. 他不喜歡她，而且從來不忌諱說出來。/ I was a little shy of them at first. 一開始我對他們有點兒戒心。
           
<span class="vocabulary">**apprehensive**</span> [ˌæprɪˈhensɪv]
<span class="definition">adj. ~ (about/of sth) / ~ (that…)（對可能發生的壞事）恐懼的：</span>He was rather apprehensive of failure. 他相當害怕失敗。

<span class="vocabulary">**yellow**</span> ['jeləʊ] 
<span class="definition">adj. [非正式，貶] 容易害怕的，即膽怯的，膽小的：</span>瞭解即可

<span class="vocabulary">**chicken**</span> ['tʃɪkɪn] 
<span class="definition">adj. [非正式] 不用於名詞前，膽小的、怯懦的：</span>Why are you so chicken? 你為什麼這麼膽小？
           
<span class="vocabulary">**startled**</span> [ˈstɑ:tld; 美 ˈstɑ:rtld]
<span class="definition">adj. 受驚嚇的：</span>She was momentarily startled by the sight before her. 她一時間被眼前的景象嚇了一跳。/ She was obviously a little startled at this idea. 她顯然對這個想法有點兒吃驚。/ I was startled by his sudden appearance in the doorway. 他突然出現在門口，嚇了我一大跳。/ She looked at him with startled eyes. 她用吃驚的目光看著他。/ He looked startled. 他顯得很驚訝。/ She jumped back like a startled rabbit. 她像受驚的兔子似的跳了回去。
           
<span class="vocabulary">**claustrophobic**</span> [ˌklɔ:strəˈfəʊbɪk; 美 -ˈfoʊ-]
<span class="definition">adj.（人）感覺幽閉恐懼的、患有幽閉恐怖症的：</span>I felt a little claustrophobic in the tiny room. 我在這個逼仄的房間裡感到有點兒幽閉恐怖。/ The churning, pressing crowds made her feel claustrophobic. 擠來擠去的密集人群使她感到一種透不過氣的恐懼。

周邊詞彙補充：
· funk可指（尤因處於自己無法控制的情形中而出現的）一種恐懼或憂慮的狀態。

☀ <span class="category">**恐懼、害怕的名詞：**</span>
<span class="vocabulary">**fear**</span> [fɪə] 
<span class="definition">n. [C, U] fear (of sb/sth) / fear (for sb/sth) / fear (that ...) 處於危險之中時、壞事可能發生時、嚇人的事發生時所具有的一種糟糕的感覺，即害怕，恐懼。是此義的常規用詞，極為常用：</span>(a) fear of the spiders 害怕蜘蛛 / The boy showed no fear. 這個男孩毫無懼色。/ He was shaking with fear. 他嚇得發抖。/ Public fears about the disease increased. 公眾對該疾病的恐懼越來越強烈。/ childhood fear 孩提時代的恐懼 / a fear of failure 害怕失敗
           
<span class="vocabulary">**dread**</span> [dred]
<span class="definition">n. [U, C, usually sing.]（對將要發生的壞事的）恐懼；令人恐懼的事物：</span>The prospect of growing old fills me with dread. 想到人會一天天老下去便使我充滿恐懼。/ She has an irrational dread of hospitals. 她莫名其妙地害怕醫院。/ The committee members live in dread of (= are always worried about) anything that may cause a scandal. 委員會成員整天提心吊膽，生怕有什麼事會引起流言飛語。/ My greatest dread is that my parents will find out. 我最擔心的就是父母會察覺出來。/ She thought with dread of the cold winters to come. 她害怕即將來臨的寒冷冬季。/ Her greatest dread was that she would lose her job. 她最怕的就是失去工作。/ the dread she felt at the thought of meeting him again 一想起要再見他她就感到的恐懼 / He lived in constant dread that one day he might be found out. 他一直生活在恐懼之中，擔心總有一天會被發現。/ Does the thought of flying fill you with dread? 想到飛行你就非常害怕嗎？/ After her shoplifting spree she lived in mortal dread of being found out. 在商店大肆行竊之後，她惶恐不安，害怕被人發現。/ her dread of discovery 她對真相敗露的恐懼 / a feeling/sense of dread 恐懼感
             
<span class="vocabulary">**fright**</span> [fraɪt]
<span class="definition">n. 1 [U]（尤指令人不快的事意外發生造成的）驚嚇的感覺：</span>She cried out in fright. 她被嚇哭了。/ He was shaking with fright. 他嚇得發抖。/ The steam pipes rattled suddenly, and Franklin uttered a shriek and jumped with fright. 蒸汽管突然發出咯咯的響聲，富蘭克林尖叫一聲，嚇得跳了起來。/ The birds smashed into the top of their cages in fright. 鳥兒驚恐地衝過去，撞到了籠子頂上。/ An untrained horse had taken fright at the sound of gunfire. 一匹未經訓練的馬聽到槍響受了驚。/ When costs soared, the studio took fright and recalled the company from Rome. 成本一路狂飆，製片廠受驚之餘撤回了羅馬的公司。/ He almost died of fright when the fish jumped out of the water. 魚從水中跳了出來，把他嚇壞了。/ They stood there, frozen with fright. 他們站在那兒，嚇呆了。/ The birds took fright at the sight of the cat and flew off. 鳥兒看到貓後嚇得飛走了。<span class="definition">2 [C] 嚇人或恐怖的經歷：</span>You gave me a fright jumping out at me like that. 你這樣跳起來撲向我，把我嚇了一大跳。/ I got the fright of my life. 我嚇得要命。/ The snake picked up its head and stuck out its tongue which gave everyone a fright. 蛇突然昂起頭，吐出芯子，每個人都嚇了一跳。/ The last time you had a real fright, you nearly crashed the car. 上一次你嚇得夠嗆，差點兒把車撞爛了。/ Leah got such a fright that she dropped the tray. 利婭嚇得失手掉了盤子。/ I had a terrible fright this morning when I saw you there. 早上看到你在那兒，我嚇了一大跳。/ You gave me the fright of my life, jumping out like that! 你這樣跳出來，把我嚇死了!         
                      
<span class="vocabulary">**horror**</span> [ˈhɒrə(r); 美 ˈhɔ:r-; ˈhɑ:r-]

<span class="definition">vi. 由於驚訝或害怕而突然且快速地移動，如突然一驚，相當於jump，不過不如jump常用：</span>The sudden noise made her start. 突如其來的聲音嚇了她一跳。

<span class="vocabulary">**quail**</span> [kweɪl]
<span class="definition">vi. [文學] ~ (at/before sb/sth) 感覺或顯出恐懼，如膽怯、畏縮：</span>The very words make many of us quail. 這些話讓我們許多人膽怯了。/ He told Naomi she was becoming just like Maya. Naomi quailed at the thought. 他告訴娜奧米，她正變得和馬婭一樣。一想到這裡，娜奧米便不寒而慄。  

<span class="vocabulary">**cower**</span> [ˈkaʊə(r)]
<span class="definition">vi. 因恐懼而蜷縮、退縮：</span>A gun went off and people cowered behind walls and under tables. 一聲槍響，人們縮到牆後或桌子底下躲起來。/ The hostages cowered in their seats. 人質們蜷縮在座位上。/ cat cower 貓蜷縮          
         
<span class="vocabulary">**flinch**</span> [flɪntʃ]
<span class="definition">vi. ~ (at sth) / ~ (away) 因恐懼而突然一動：</span>He flinched at the sight of the blood. 他一見到血就往後退。/ He met my gaze without flinching. 他毫不畏縮，跟我對視著。/ Murat had looked into the eyes of the firing squad without flinching. 穆拉特毫不退縮地直視著執行槍決的行刑隊員們的眼睛。/ She flinched inwardly as he took her hand. 他拉她的手時，她心裡縮了一下。/ She flinched visibly at the sight of the body. 她看到屍體時，明顯向後退了一下。/ She flinched away from him. 她後退避開他。
           
<span class="vocabulary">**recoil**</span> [rɪˈkɔɪl]
<span class="definition">vi. ~ (from sb/sth) / ~ (at sth) 身體因恐懼而從某處快速退縮：</span>He recoiled in horror at the sight of the corpse. 他一見到屍體就嚇得往後縮了。/ She felt him recoil from her, frightened. 她發覺他很害怕她，直往後縮。/ He recoiled a little at the sharpness in my voice. 聽到我尖利的嗓音，他往後縮了一下。/ As he leaned towards her she instinctively recoiled. 他向她靠近，她本能地往後退。/ For a moment I thought he was going to kiss me. I recoiled in horror. 我當時以為他要親我，所以嚇得直往後退。/ She recoiled from his touch. 她躲開他的觸控。<span class="definition">vi. ~ (from sth/from doing sth) / ~ (at sth) 對一個想法或一種情況作出強烈的恐懼的反應：</span>瞭解即可
           
<span class="vocabulary">**retreat**</span> [rɪˈtri:t]
<span class="definition">vi.（由於批評或環境變得惡劣）改變主意，如退縮：</span>The government had retreated from its pledge to reduce class sizes. 政府已經改變了縮小班級規模的承諾。/ He retreated in the face of strong opposition. 面對強烈的反對，他退卻了。

<span class="vocabulary">**shrink**</span> [ʃrɪŋk] 
<span class="definition">vi. 由於害怕或震驚而從某事物處退縮回來或從其移開，相當於cower：</span>She shrank back in terror. 她恐懼地往後退。/ He shrank back against the wall. 他向後退到了牆根。/ The child shrank from her touch. 那孩子躲開了她的觸控。

<span class="vocabulary">**tremble**</span> ['trembl] 
<span class="definition">vi. 非常擔心或恐懼：</span>I trembled at the thought of having to make a speech. 我一想到要做演講就發怵。/ He was still trembling in fear. 他仍然因恐懼而顫抖。

<span class="vocabulary">**rise**</span> [raɪz] 
<span class="definition">vi.（毛髮）豎起來，立起來：</span>The hair on the back of my neck rose when I heard the scream. 聽到那尖叫聲，我不禁毛骨悚然。 

<span class="vocabulary">**shy**</span> [ʃaɪ] 
<span class="definition">vi. shy (at sth)（尤指一匹馬）由於受到驚嚇而突然轉變方向：</span>Her horse shied violently at the noise. 這響聲把她的馬驚得猛然後退。

相關詞義延伸：
· flinch還可指因疼痛、驚訝等而突然一動，如後縮。

周邊詞彙補充：
· falter可指行走或行為沒有自信，如蹣跚、猶豫、畏縮；
· awe可指令某人敬畏。

<span class="vocabulary">**frighten**</span> ['fraɪtn] 
<span class="definition">vt.＆vi. 使某人突然感到害怕，如嚇唬：</span>She’s not easily frightened. 她不是輕易能嚇倒的。/ The prospect of war really frightens me. 戰爭的即將到來真的把我嚇壞了。/ Don’t creep around like that! You frightened me to death! 不要這樣躡手躡腳地到處走！你嚇死我了！/ I didn’t mean to frighten you. 我不是故意想嚇你的。

<span class="vocabulary">**scare**</span> [skeə] 
<span class="definition">vt. 嚇唬某人：</span>You scared me. 你嚇了我一跳。/ It scared me to think I was alone in the building. 想到樓裡只有我一個人，怪害怕的。/ His name scares off a lot of people. 他的名字嚇跑了許多人。/ You don’t scare me with your threats! 你那些威脅嚇唬不了我！/ The very thought of flying scares me stiff. 一想到要坐飛機我就嚇得發呆。
           
<span class="vocabulary">**startle**</span> [ˈstɑ:tl; 美 ˈstɑ:rtl]
<span class="definition">vt. 使某人突然受驚嚇（即驚訝的同時有些害怕）：</span>I didn't mean to startle you. 我不是存心要嚇唬你。/ The explosion startled the horse. 爆炸聲使馬受了驚。/ The telephone startled him. 電話鈴聲嚇了他一跳。/ I was startled by her question. 她的問題讓我大吃一驚。/ It startled me to find her sitting in my office. 我一進辦公室，發現她坐在裡面，把我嚇了一跳。
           
<span class="vocabulary">**freak**</span> [fri:k]
<span class="definition">vt.&vi. [非正式] ~ (sb) (out) 使某人恐懼：</span>Snakes really freak me out. 我一看見蛇便渾身發麻。/ I think our music freaks people out sometimes. 我想我們的音樂有時會嚇著大家。

<span class="vocabulary">**cow**</span> [kaʊ]

<span class="vocabulary">**snow**</span> [snəʊ] 
<span class="definition">vi. [美，非正式] 用花言巧語來唬住某人：</span>瞭解即可
           
<span class="vocabulary">**deter**</span> [dɪˈtɜ:(r)]
<span class="definition">vt.&vi. ~ sb (from sth/from doing sth) 透過使某人感到做某事過程中的困難或此事的糟糕結果，而使其不去做某事（或使其不再繼續做此事），即威懾、使望而卻步：</span>The present system does little to deter corporate crime. 目前的體制對於法人犯罪沒有什麼威懾作用。/ Will this harsher punishment effectively deter criminals? 這種更嚴厲的懲罰能有效震懾罪犯嗎？/ Supporters of the death penalty argue that it would deter criminals from carrying guns. 死刑的支持者認為它能阻嚇罪犯攜帶槍支。/ The alarm is usually sufficient to deter a would-be thief. 警報通常足以阻嚇一個想偷東西的人。/ Her words of warning would hardly deter him. 她的警告很難嚇住他。/ Arrests and jail sentences have done nothing to deter the protesters. 逮捕和監禁都沒能嚇倒抗議者。/ The skunk releases a pungent smell to deter the enemy. 臭鼬釋放出臭氣讓敵人望而卻步。/ Dogs can deter unwelcome intruders. 狗能阻嚇惡意闖入者。/ The lack of facilities in the town may deter the casual visitor. 鎮子缺乏便利設施，這嚇退了碰巧來參觀的人。/ The high price of the service could deter people from seeking advice. 這麼高的服務費可能使諮詢者望而卻步。
    
<span class="vocabulary">**terrify**</span> ['terɪfaɪ]

<span class="vocabulary">**chill**</span> [tʃɪl]
<span class="definition">vt. [文學] 嚇唬某人：</span>His words chilled her. 他的話使她不寒而慄。/ What he saw chilled his blood/chilled him to the bone. 他看到的情景使他毛骨悚然。/ There was a coldness in her that chilled him. 她身上透著一股冷漠，讓他不寒而慄。/ Some films chill you to the marrow of your bones. 有些電影讓人毛骨悚然。

<span class="vocabulary">**idle**</span> [ˈaɪdl]

<span class="vocabulary">**deterrent**</span> [dɪˈterənt; 美 -ˈtɜ:r-]
<span class="definition">n. 1 [C] ~ (to sb/sth) 起威懾或遏制作用的因素：</span>Hopefully his punishment will act as a deterrent to others. 對他的懲罰但願能起到殺一儆百的作用。/ They seriously believe that capital punishment is a deterrent. 他們真的相信死刑具有威懾作用。/ Defenders of the death penalty regard it as the ultimate deterrent.（ultimate尤為英式用法）支援死刑的人認為死刑是最具威懾力的刑罰。/ The tough new law should act as a deterrent. 這項嚴厲的新法將起到威懾作用。/ a punishment that will act as a deterrent to other offenders 用以威懾其他違法者的懲罰 / The bodies of executed criminals were hung on the city gates as a deterrent. 被處決的罪犯的屍體掛在城門上示眾。/ a deterrent against cheating 防止作弊行為的威攝手段 / There is no effective deterrent for these young criminals. 對這些少年罪犯並沒有有效的威懾手段。/ a deterrent to all but the most determined attacker 幾乎對所有人都具有威懾力的手段，最堅定的攻擊者除外 / They stressed the need for an independent nuclear deterrent. 他們強調擁有獨立核威懾能力的必要性。/ strategic deterrent（尤美）戰略威懾 <span class="definition">2 [C] 威懾性的武器：</span>a nuclear deterrent 核威懾武器 <span class="definition">adj. 威懾性的、嚇阻性的：</span>They were arguing about the deterrent effect of nuclear weapons. 他們在爭論核武器的威懾效果。/ Hopefully, that will have a deterrent effect on drug syndicates in the future. 希望這能在將來對販毒集團起到威懾作用。/ his belief in the deterrent value of capital punishment 他相信死刑的威懾力
